# Drakar och Demoner — *Ereb Altor: Spelledarbok* (Game Master's Book)

A detailed summary of the Swedish *Drakar och Demoner* (Dragons & Demons) campaign book for the world of **Ereb Altor**. The *Spelledarbok* (Game Master's book) describes twelve of the most interesting countries of the continent, plus shorter notes on several others. The companion *Spelarbok* covers stories and legends, the *Kampanjbok* covers the continent as a whole, and the *Äventyrsbok* contains two adventures set in Hynsolge and Erebos.

> **Setting "present day": the year 610 eO** (eO = "efter Odo", *after Odo*; fO = *före Odo*, before Odo). Several countries are written as standing on the brink of war "right now" (610 eO).
>
> **Recurring terms:** *Konfluxen* (the Conflux, 599–598 fO) = the cataclysm that destroyed the old empire **Jorpagna** and reshaped Altor. *Den Lysande Vägen* (the Shining Way) = the dominant western monotheistic church. *Svartfolk* = "black folk" (orcs, trolls, ogres, etc.). *Månsilver* = moonsilver. Stat abbreviations: STY=Strength, STO=Size, FYS=Constitution, SMI=Agility, INT=Intelligence, PSY=Psyche, KAR=Charisma, KP=hit points, ABS=armor absorption, FV/GC=skill chance, gm/sm=gold/silver coins, T=die (e.g. 3T6 = 3d6).

The twelve detailed countries: **Caddo, Cereval, Erebos, Felicien, Hynsolge, Jorduashur, Kardien, Klomellien, Nidland, Ransard, Trakorien, Zorakin.**

## 1. CADDO

A long volcanic island in the Copper Sea (Kopparhavet), inhabited entirely by the **dalker** (dalcoi, "island folk"). A prosperous trading democracy famous for its banking and archers.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~100,000
- **Demographics:** humans 97%, *ankor* (ducks/duckfolk) 3%
- **Capital:** Arno (~7,000)
- **Head of state:** none
- **Government:** council republic (37 cantons)
- **Exports:** ships, grain, wine, handicrafts
- **Imports:** timber, metals
- **Wealth:** rich
- **Army:** small standing army, very strong militia levy on invasion, strong navy
- **Religion:** a conservative branch of the Shining Way
- **Notes:** all magic forbidden within the realm; all dalker are colour-blind

**Geography.** A long island split lengthwise by the **Appobergen** (up to 3,000 m, many extinct volcanic cones). The mountains catch the sea winds, making the slopes fertile and well-watered. Most people live on the gently rolling north and south coastal plains (near overpopulated), growing wheat and olives. Only the mountains hold untouched wilderness.

**The dalker.** Tall, fair-haired, blue/grey-eyed, sun-tanned — and totally **colour-blind** (visitors find their garish colour combinations nauseating). Of unknown origin, unrelated to the *jorer*. Hard-working to the point of being exhaustingly energetic; superb craftsmen, merchants and shipbuilders. They prize equality — people are judged by conduct and office, not inherited rank, which annoys visiting nobles. Their language **dalkiska** is a unique isolate; few foreigners speak it.

**Government.** Born from the civil war *Ödesfejden* (104–111 eO). The island is divided into **37 cantons**, each with a *kantonförsamling* (assembly of all adult taxpayers, men and women, ~35% of the population). The **landsråd** (national council) of elected canton representatives controls foreign policy, the standing army, big projects, couriers and inter-canton disputes; its laws must be ratified by 2/3 of canton assemblies. Stable and popular for centuries; a military coup is essentially impossible. Tension exists between 12 merchant-city cantons and the rural cantons (farmers dominate the council but cities have strong self-rule).

**Trade & banking.** A trading nation dealing with all corners of Ereb (imports salt, timber, grain; exports metals, handicraft, leather and jewel work, grain). Caddo's **banks are the most trusted in all Ereb** because of political stability, the island's isolation/safety from raids, and the dalkers' reputation for honesty (with high interest). Leading banking families: the **Medicini** clan and the duck-house **Hugger**, who hold great sway over Copper Sea trade.

**Foreign policy.** Chronic rivalry with the other trading states, chiefly **Erebos**, over mainland trade. Friction with **Zorakin** over its conquest of *Fristaden* (which disrupted dwarf trade — Caddo and the dwarves later built the city **Ereno** further north). Currently drawn into conflict against **Felicien** (masked piracy), allied with **Berendien** and, reluctantly, **Erebos**.

**Military.** Three traditional parts: the *folkuppbåd* (general levy — every adult dalk man must own weapons & armour and train yearly; standardized arms: two throwing + one one-handed spear, or bow & battleaxe, plus shield), the *landsvakt* (professional patrol troops with battleaxe, composite bow, medium shield, mail or scale), and the *rikshär/riksflotta* (national army and fleet defending Caddo and its colonies). Weak in cavalry, but its archers are superb and prized as mercenaries.

**Religion.** Wholly dominated by the **Shining Way** (the only permitted religion). Magic is completely forbidden as contrary to the faith — though it is practiced in secret.

**History highlights.** 312 fO: a *jorisk* descendant, **Vasker Bruushane**, unites the dalker tribes and is made king. 102 eO: the Bruushane line dies out → civil war. 111 eO: warlord **Gerlo den Långe** ("the Tall") gathers the tribes at the **Council of the Long Noses** (*De Långa Näsornas Råd*); the **Arno Oath** (never again take up arms against kin) founds the republic. 200s eO: trade with the Österlanden makes Caddo a great power; it joins the Shining Way's crusades against **Morälvidyn**, beginning the **Great Schism** (see Zorakin/Kardien).

**Notable names:** Vasker Bruushane (founding king), Gerlo den Långe (republic's founder), banking houses **Medicini** and **Hugger**, the city **Ereno** (built with the dwarves).

## 2. CEREVAL

"The Dragons' Realm" — the land with the largest known concentration of dragons in Ereb, yet its people live in relative safety because the dragons are reined in by the mysterious **drakmästare** (dragon masters), and because of the semi-magical metal **månsilver** (moonsilver). Not a true state, but loose city-states, free heath-farmers and nomads all bound by the **Månlagen** (Moon Law).

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~80,000
- **Demographics:** 75% humans, 11% trolls, 14% svartfolk
- **Capital:** none
- **Head of state:** none
- **Government:** independent city-states with no obvious ruling class
- **Exports:** fish, meat, alchemical items and elixirs
- **Imports:** timber and metals
- **Wealth:** poor
- **Army:** no standing army but well-trained levy; nomads and trolls are very capable warriors
- **Religion:** several; extensive nature worship
- **Notes:** the dragon masters' Moon Law is supreme law for all cerevalier

**Geography.** From the **Cer-floden** in the north to the **Grynnerpasset** in the south, and from the **Cerevalbergen** in the west to **Masevabukten** in the east. From east to west: lowlands, forest, mountains. The lowland is split by the deep **Trollfjärden** (Troll Fjord) into **Dänslätten** (north) and **Lirslätten** (south).
- **Kustheden** (coastal heath): open grass/scrub, thin precious topsoil — destroying it is a grave crime punished with blood sacrifice (usually the offender). The three real towns/city-states sit here: **Cerevallis, Drakonia, Lirvella.**
- **Savannen/stenöknarna:** a dry ~500 m plateau with predators (wild dogs, cliff lizards, sand demons, jaguars) and prey (cliff rabbits, cereval antelopes, rhinos, **elefantfåglar** = elephant-birds).
- **Tarassusravinen:** a 20 km ravine of poisonous thornbushes with very ancient ruins.
- **Cer-skogen:** near-jungle of moss-covered ground, twilight even by day; home of the **trolls**, who cultivate forest ponds and pan for moonsilver.
- **Cerevalbergen:** high range (peaks over 6,000 m) where the dragons live and where moonsilver is mined; per legend formed when a star fell — the star's **heart** still lies under the roots, its shell now exists as moonsilver on the surface. The **drakmästare** dwell on the highest peaks.

**Månsilver (moonsilver).** A semi-magical metal found only in Cereval, in lumps in soil and rock; on the heath found while plowing, on the highland it glows faintly in moonlight, in the forest panned from streams. Each lump is matte bluish; sizes from a pea to multi-kilo blocks (now rare and pricey). Easily forged; tools of moonsilver make heavy labor trivial at a cost of **2–3 PSY points per hour** of use. Weapons of moonsilver are lighter, sharper, harder — **+1T2 KP extra damage**; armour pieces get **+1T3 ABS** at half the weight; both are nearly invisible in full moonlight (fully invisible if fully "charged"). Cost ~4–5× the steel equivalent.
- **Charging:** Heavy use weakens it (heavier, more brittle, dimmer); leave it in moonlight to recharge. **1 hour of moonlight = 3 hours of catalytic capacity.**
- **Outside Cereval** it slowly dies, turning blue-black and finally to dead powder with no magic — so it is worthless abroad. The Moon Law forbids exporting it; smugglers are killed or handed to the dragon masters. Within Cereval it's worth twice gold, but no one parts with it willingly (traded only by barter, fines or tax).

**The cerevalier (peoples).** Three human groups (~75% of the humanoid population): the city-state folk, the free heath-farmers/ranchers, and the nomads. Typically below medium height, dark-haired, light-skinned. Quiet, honest, peaceful — violence is rare because all respect the Moon Law; the main exception is the constant warring between city-states.
- **City-states:** craftsmen, merchants, scholars, outlaws; mostly barbarian-descended humans, plus small groups of dwarves, alver (elves), *ankor*, halflings in the three big cities. Outlaws may include people with svartfolk blood; full-blooded orcs and reptile-men are killed/driven off.
- **Free heath-farmers & ranchers:** tend the fragile soil almost religiously (destroying it = immediate execution); ranchers raise bison-like cattle and surgically implant **silver poison pouches** under the animals' skin so that a dragon that roasts one is poisoned. United on three things: care of the soil, the view of dragons, freedom from the city-states.
- **Nomads:** the original immigrants; live in clans of 30–60 adults under a chieftain of absolute power. Yearly clan-meetings for weddings/markets/trials (no fighting allowed). Keep tame antelopes and **elefantfåglar** (used as dragon-lookouts, mounts, and for eggs/down). Each clan has a moonsilver-smith and a shaman. The clan hunter carries a moonsilver sabre, a 5.5 m sidespiked pike, and a bola; camps are guarded by slings throwing two stones joined by a tarred rope (to entangle a dragon's wings).

**The trolls of Cereval.** Far more advanced than degenerate trolls elsewhere (likely from constant moonsilver contact); smaller and nimbler, higher intelligence and social behavior. Five kinds:
- **Skogstroll** (forest trolls): ~80% of all Cereval trolls; live in communities of 15–40 in caves; cultivate forest ponds, grow the white-green poisonous mushroom *trollhatt* (used as food, medicine, flour and a ceremonial drink), raise giant toads/water-snakes/spiders. Ruled by a härskartroll; use svartfolk slaves to handle fire (which trolls fear).
- **Härskartroll** (ruler trolls): the crown of trolls — larger, stronger, smarter; born of ordinary trolls, lavishly educated, then choose a "calling" (scholar, teacher, mage, warleader, or ruler). Rulers found new communities that grow until the ruler dies (rarely), then dissolve.
- **Krigartroll** (warrior trolls): bred by a dark-mage cult as a super-warrior race — huge and strong but low intelligence/magic; lured over to the forest trolls. Form the core of troll armies, led by a härskartroll warleader with skogstroll lieutenants.
- **Småtroll** (small trolls): a strange condition stops growth; tiny, playful, mischievous (smaller than gnomes/elves) with stronger magic; live for pranks, tormenting gnomes, elves, travelers and even dignified dragons (a humiliated dragon stays in a bad mood for years). Best handled with song, shiny objects and food.
- **Havstroll** (sea trolls): descend from småtroll that adapted to water; grow their whole lives, some sighted at ~20 m long; formidable in water. Since 408 eO **Trollfjärden** is forbidden to all human ships.
- **Grottroll** (cave trolls): a few genuine ones remain in the mountains, like elsewhere.

**Bortbytingar (changelings).** Human children swapped by troll magic for troll young, imprinted to look identical to a human child. They gain partial resistance to sun/fire/steel (but dislike it), grow larger/stronger, have a natural talent for magic and double-range dark-vision, but can never have children with humans. After ~100 years they gradually take on troll traits. Raised in upper-class human families they can make fine careers (warriors, craftsmen, mages); raised poor they often end up bullied and in the slums. Changelings raised by trolls are cherished, educated, trained in magic, and act as the trolls' loyal representatives (Jehennin observers, even university students at Drakonia).

**Other peoples & creatures.**
- **Svartfolk:** an underground orc realm at the northern tip of the Cerevalbergen (leaders are orcs; ogres and dark elves often as slaves). Since the Moon Council of 401 eO, all svartfolk except Cereval's trolls are **outlawed** and may be killed unpunished.
- **Drakmästarna (dragon masters):** humans call them this; the trolls call them roughly "the Damned/Forsaken People." Tall sooty-furred humanoids with long blond hair, pupil-less light-blue eyes, a prehensile gripping tail, and retractable claws as hard as dragon-claws. They live above the snowline among **white trees** that grow from the rock (criminals are transformed into these trees as punishment, meditating until their soul is purified and released). Communicate telepathically; immortal in material form but can be killed by violence (when one dies, four masters create a new body from their own flesh — their number is always constant). They keep and train the dragons.

**Drakmästare stats** — STY 3T6+10 (typ. 21, SB 1T4), STO 3T6+5 (16, KP 21), FYS 3T6+15 (26), SMI 4T6 (14), INT 4T6 (14), PSY 3T6+10 (21). Natural attacks: 2 claw-hands GC 65% 1T6; 1 claw-kick 60% 1T8; gripping tail 60%/60% 1T4; tail-throw 50% 1T8. Natural protection: 1-point fur; can only be hurt by dragon-bone weapons, magical steel weapons, and magic. Movement L14. Skills: as a learned man and mage; chief schools animism, elemental magic, symbolism; fights mainly with magic.

**The dragons.** Kept in great caves lower on the mountains; the young live together training in combat and magic under a master (the relationship is like a hunter and his hounds — voluntary on both sides). As a dragon nears maturity (~1,000–1,500 years) it seeks its own cave and hoard; most die in territorial fights. A few bond with their master and learn to carry him in flight; many remain too independent and eventually rebel. Cereval's dragons are **less long-lived but develop twice as fast** (halve all Monster Book dragon ages).

**Government — Månlagen (the Moon Law) & Måntinget (the Moon Council).** The Moon Law (also "Dragon Law") is supreme over all local law; its first purpose is that **no moonsilver leaves the world.** It regulates moonsilver (storage, charging, export, tax), conduct between individuals and communities, fines for breaches, and extradition of criminals to the dragon masters. A guest in a strange land performs a ceremony ("I follow the Law of the Moon and am bound by the Oath"); if accepted he gets safe passage, water and shelter (but not food) and must help defend the host if attacked. Breaking the Law usually means a dishonorable execution in which even friends join. Offenders may appeal to the **Måntinget**, held when the masters collect the moonsilver tax (every 1–2 years); a master reads minds and judges finally — losers are usually taken to the mountains, communities are heavily fined in moonsilver (the masters' way of reassembling the fallen star). To soften the harsh fines, the peoples formed **Jehennin**, an advisory council (non-binding). City-states are each fully independent, ruled by councils of the most successful merchants/craftsmen (always including a moonsilver-smith and a Moon-Law expert), led by a *borgmästare/byäldste/lagfrämste*.

**Religion.** Plays a small role. The dragon masters are revered (gods? immortals?). A moon goddess and druids are worshipped in most towns; dark temples are actively driven out. Trolls worship **Vandraren** (the Wanderer), represented in the sky by the constellation humans call **Orcherion the Warrior**; trolls believe they are reborn into the constellation, awaiting the return of a missing star. Nomads believe trolls are the souls of nomads eaten by dragons, passing through the forest in troll-shape before rebirth.

**History highlights (very long; key beats):**
- **Legend:** a star fell to Altor; its "forsaken people" were bound to matter and ordered to guard the star's heart — these became the drakmästare.
- **3600–1700 fO:** the **månalverna** (moon-elves, Cereval's elves) build a glittering high culture on moonsilver, driving off all rivals (orcs, dark elves, even dwarves).
- **1700–1100 fO:** the trolls rise to a high culture too; neither side can drive out the other.
- From **1600 fO** dragons gather; the drakmästare appear. Dragons (by day) + trolls (by night) wear down the proud month-elves. Around **1070–1040 fO** the last moon-elf falls; their last, most beautiful **hidden city** vanishes from the world — a lost paradise possibly still findable by adventurers (its traps and the dragons' size kept it from being plundered).
- **950–600 fO:** the dragon masters' consolidation; the blue halo appears on the peaks; the Moon Law is imposed.
- **599–598 fO:** the Third Conflux ravages even Cereval.
- **100 fO–287 eO** "The Great Disorder": humans and trolls war; the dragon masters systematically intervene; by 287 eO all human groups accept the Moon Law.
- **287–456 eO:** human cities arise — **Drakonia** founded 294 (university 304–312), **Cerevallis** 357, **Lirvella** 378. 408: Battle of Trollfjärden (Trollfjärden closed to humans forever). 456: **Jehennin** formed.
- **530–550 eO:** the era of **Dird Ligamester** (borgmästare of Cerevallis, later talman of Jehennin) — great orc/barbarian invasions, sieges of the cities, the relief of Drakonia by sea, conflict and reconciliation between coast cities, trolls and nomads over orc-looted moonsilver. 549: sea trolls destroy Lirvella's harbor.
- **604–610 eO:** huge moonsilver finds in Tarassusravinen (a 140 kg block in 604) spark a moonsilver rush; ancient moon-elf artifacts found (607) revive legends of the lost city; tension between trolls, nomads and city-states. **610 eO: "Now!"**

**Notable names:** **Dird Ligamester** (statesman of Cerevallis), the cities **Cerevallis, Drakonia** (with its university), **Lirvella**; the metal **månsilver**; the **drakmästare**; **Trollfjärden**, **Tarassusravinen**, the lost **month-elf city**; the councils **Jehennin** and **Måntinget**.

## 3. EREBOS

An island nation in the central Copper Sea — a pronounced trading state with a strong merchant fleet, ruled by the heads of its five greatest merchant houses. Caddo's great rival.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~125,000
- **Demographics:** humans 85%, *ankor* 15%
- **Capital:** Nohstril (~9,000)
- **Head of state:** (the Five Houses council)
- **Government:** plutocracy
- **Exports:** shellfish, gemstones, fabrics
- **Imports:** timber, grain
- **Wealth:** rich
- **Army:** small national army, plenty of mercenaries, no levy
- **Religion:** worship of **Fraschikel**, protector of all things; Shining Way strong
- **Notes:** ruled by a council of the leaders of the five largest merchant houses

**Geography.** Three rocky volcanic islands ~30 *mil* NE of Caddo, plus two more:
- **Beyural** (SW, most fertile, the realm's unofficial heart): most people, most towns, most harbors. Largest city **Gringul** — palaces and canals at the mouth of the river Elspet. Serfs raise **entorningar** (one-horned cattle), grow wine, *hersch* (a giant wheat) and *lullader* (small bitter stone-fruit used for spice and cooking oil). Remnant forests of *trill* and *järnbok* are protected. The **Bey-bergen** hold the only **solstenar** (sunstones) in Ereb — yellow gems that glow with their own warm light; very valuable, the foundation of Erebos's 400s-eO wealth; rumored (without proof) to ward dragons, svartfolk, demons, undead, magic and disease — and they do instill courage. Other Beyural towns: Jachindo, Behrill, Josch, Yrrtik.
- **Dakkilo** (smallest): essentially just the capital **Nohstril**; the rest is poor pasture for steppe-goats and long-wooled sheep (whose wool makes prized warm fabric, lately fashionable in Kardien/Felicien).
- **Targero** (largest, poorest): lively fishing; Targerans are bigger and tougher (most fleet soldiers are recruited here). Old **Jorpagna ruins** in its mountains draw treasure-hunters; the only Erebos island with svartfolk (orcs and trolls). Rumors of perytons, hydras, basilisks, even dragon-serpents (drakormar).
- **Tolokfe** ("the Cyclops' Isle"): shunned by all; per legend the cyclopes were cursed there in ancient times. No trees grow (so no escape by ship). Treacherous currents (Megalossundet, Gensnuporgraven) and perpetual fog keep it almost unexplored; the very few survivors return mad. One ~500-man invasion vanished entirely. The cyclopes live in vast cave-cities and raise steppe-goats; not evil, just hungry and wanting to be left alone.

**The erebosier.** Of *jorisk* descent (the original inhabitants, the **massiver**, were quickly driven into the mountains). Reputed lazy except where money is concerned. Short, hot-tempered, talkative; they admire wealth and skill in negotiation and bargaining, and value eloquence (skalds and bards prized) and learning. Individualists — judged by their own deeds, not ancestors. Speak **erebosiska** (an East-*jorisk* tongue, related to Hynsolgian/Berendian, mixed with the old *massi*); coastal fishermen often speak *nyjori*.

**Politics — the Five Houses.** For ~a century, Erebos has been ruled by the **Five Houses**, after the civil war of 512–514 when the old nobility tried to crush the merchants and lost. The five richest merchant houses formed the **Femhusrådet** (Council of Five Houses) in the new capital **Nohstril**; each house seats one representative; **all decisions must be unanimous (each has a veto)**, keeping a balance of power. They now control more than half the realm's resources; ~two dozen lesser houses remain. The old tribal chieftains now control only coastal fishing.

**Conflict.** Caddo and Erebos are arch-rivals (intrigue, diplomacy, sometimes violence over sea trade) — really house-vs-house, but it spills to national level. They now share a common enemy in **Felicien** (Felician pirates seizing Erebos/Caddo/Berendian ships); both Erebos and Caddo are rearming.

**Military.** A tax-funded national army & fleet, **plus each merchant house has its own fleet and army.** All forces are paid professional **mercenaries** — mobile and hard-hitting but smaller than e.g. Caddo's levy. Because everything must be sea-borne, forces are almost entirely heavy infantry and archers (cavalry rare); strong at marine operations and sieges, weak against mainland knight-armies in open field. The Five Houses prefer diplomacy to war (war is bad for business).

**Religion.** Chiefly **Fraschikel** — a survival from old Jorpagna: the blind goddess of justice who weighs good deeds against evil; also patron of all craftsmen and merchants, and a creator/death/sea goddess besides. Worship is casual (invoked when needed); small cloisters dot the islands. Very tolerant overall; the **Shining Way** is fairly strong but distrusted by merchants as "Caddo's long arm."

**History.** Little known before Jorpagna. The original **massiver** likely arrived after the second conflux. As Jorpagna expanded (700s fO) people settled Targero (then more fertile, a favorite haunt of mages). After the conflux the survivors fell into a Viking-like barbarism (ships raiding and trading); the scattered tribes united under a king ~tideräkning's start. Lacking rich farmland, no true feudalism arose — "nobility" were ship-controlling chieftains, gradually overtaken by trade-route-controlling families who grew ever richer.

**Notable names/places:** **Femhusrådet** (the Five Houses), capital **Nohstril**; cities **Gringul**, Jachindo, Behrill, Josch, Yrrtik, Thurbel, Klostro; islands **Beyural, Dakkilo, Targero, Tolokfe**; the goddess **Fraschikel**; **solstenar** (sunstones); the **massiver** (original people); the cyclopes of Tolokfe; the *entorning*, *hersch*, *lullad*.

## 4. FELICIEN

A proud, sea-loving slaving kingdom on the southern Tolan peninsula, with a powerful navy, an unusual matriarchal-leaning society, and a habit of state-sponsored piracy.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~170,000
- **Demographics:** humans 100%
- **Capital:** Tyros (~14,000)
- **Head of state:** King **Kuthegil Halvskägg** ("Half-beard")
- **Government:** monarchy with some popular rule
- **Exports:** handicrafts, gemstones, pearls, wine, stone
- **Imports:** timber, fabrics, metals, slaves
- **Wealth:** rich
- **Army:** large, very strong fleet; small, well-trained army
- **Religion:** worship of **Paridon** (sea god) and **Philas** (land god)
- **Notes:** ~half the population are slaves; great equality between the sexes

**Geography.** Southern Tolan peninsula, all land south of the **Erbulasbergen**. Split by the **Koalintviken** (Koalint Bay), whose three great cities sit along its shores. North half **Kritos**; south half **Pelenoshalvön**. Colonies: the slave-trading cities **Bylos** and **Caraidon** (in old Dermanien), the **Hakkai** island group off Soluna, and the **Gerdikk archipelago** (mussel/pearl fishing, centered on the isle **Kea**).

**The felicier.** Tall but slim, long black hair, dark eyes, sharp features that rarely show emotion; enduring, strong-willed, determined.

**Society & slaves.** **Women are the central figures** — by ancient tradition they do no hard physical labor, so they became politicians, bankers, craftsmen; the abundance of slaves frees them from housework too, so women are nearly as common as men in trade, crafts and the army. Men typically work as skippers, merchants, craftsmen, politicians or soldiers; almost no free felicier farms (that's for slaves). **Slaves are ~50% of the population**, in three groups: workers (majority — fields, fishing boats, galley slaves, helpers), servants (mostly women and eunuchs — households, child-rearing; loyal ones may be armed bodyguards with a permit), and **courtisaner** (young, beautiful entertainers/companions). Slaves are unpaid (but fed/housed), have no legal protection (equated with outlaws), yet killing one is risky (the owner is angered, good slaves are scarce). Most felicier treat slaves mildly (bad luck to do otherwise). Freed slaves often become paid servants (high status to keep a staff of these). **Elf slaves** are most prized (hardworking, obedient, long-lived) but only the richest afford them — fueling lasting enmity between elves and felicier. Several slave revolts have been crushed bloodily.

**Languages.** Two: the old, elaborate **kimzon** (official/upper-class, modified *jorisk* runes — felicier guard it jealously as their most distinctive cultural mark), and the common *jorisk* dialect **feliciska** (understood by Kardier/Klomellier/Zorakier, but its nautical/military terms are all from kimzon).

**Child-rearing.** Very strict (slaves may even discipline the children they raise). Age 5: sailing & running; age 7: arithmetic, reading aloud, writing; age 11: choose a future trade; trained to 20 (combat, crafts, seamanship, commerce, law, or rarely magic). At 20 considered an adult; those who can't support themselves must become farmers or fishermen.

**Technology.** Underdeveloped except war industry — high-quality weapons and armor (export forbidden, smuggled). **Shipbuilding** is a showpiece: sleek grey ships, fast and maneuverable, with massive steel rams. Excellent sculptors and winemakers; otherwise behind in farming/craft methods (which they won't admit).

**Government.** A king plus a **council of 15** (three from each of the five great regions: one military, one craft/merchant, one from the regional capital's council). Each of the 15 has one vote; **the king alone has four votes** — he passes a measure with the support of two regions, or one whole profession plus one extra vote. King's title is hereditary; he may levy trade/craft taxes.

**Trade.** One of the richest lands despite few natural resources — most felicier live by trade and craft around **Koalintviken** (home to some of Ereb's richest men). Natural resources: cedar (luxury furniture, ships); gemstones (yellow *oposer*, green *smalugger*, mined on south Pelenos — sometimes luring golden dragons from Cereval); inexhaustible white limestone & marble on north Pelenos (the famous white cities and sculptors); pearls in the eastern archipelago; and a new prized **purple dye** from the *purfurosnäcka* around Kea. Farming/fishing/mining in the south; fruit and grain in Kritos; **chyros** (a light sparkling wine) is the national drink.

**Foreign policy.** The king issues **letters of marque** (*friseglarbrev*) putting pirates in Felician service: privateers may plunder ships under certain flags (keeping 50% of the loot) under Felician protection — lucrative, and a tool to force neighbors into trade. Lately this has badly angered **Erebos** (whose merchants suffer); King **Kuthegil Halvskägg** denies the pirates are protected (indications say he lies). Erebos responded with armed escorts and by refusing to renew trade deals, turning instead to Felicien's arch-enemy **Berendien**. Military tension is rising — "a powder keg."

**Military.** Relies on its navy, split into the **marinkår** (marines, 2/5, trained on land and sea) and the **havskår** (sea corps, sea-warfare only). A galley: ~300 galley-slaves at 200 oars, 100 soldiers, 25 catapult-shooters, 25 crew, ~10 officers. War footing: marines ~50 battle-galleys, sea corps 75; peacetime 25 each. Ranks: kapten (one ship) → kommendör (5) → underamiral (a *flottilj* of 25 galleys: 2,500 soldiers, 7,500 galley-slaves) → amiral (a corps). The army (~2,000 in peace, spread across the land, ~500 always guarding the Erbulas passes) defends against Berendien and pirate raids and serves as police; 10 corps under a general, with överste/kapten/löjtnant ranks.

**Religion.** A sea-loving people. From the primal sea **Zykidos** rose two opposed gods: **Paridon** (the depths) and **Philas** (near the surface, who made land and sky, and is protector of all land, freshwater and animals). Angered, Paridon created **humans** — intelligent, to be superior to Philas's work (hence felicier deem humans superior to all other folk; Philas's later attempts made only halflings, elves, *ankor*, dwarves). **Paridon** is thus the chief god, worshipped in temples everywhere; a small offering is made before every sea voyage. **Philas** is mild and wise, worshipped mostly by vintners and hunters (priests are old farmers). Philas's daughter (by a cat) is **Felis**, goddess of darkness and mystery (alchemists, astrologers, soothsayers, thieves, some mages) — depicted as a beautiful dark woman who becomes a cat each night; cats are sacred, dark-skinned people under her protection. The felicier are named after Felis. The war god **Galmon** (a giant with a trident) was added during the 300s-eO Berendian wars; he takes those who die with his name on their lips to the **Halls of Honor** (Ärans Salar) — making felician soldiers fearless to the point of recklessness.

**History.** The warrior-king **Kiolomon Sjöfararen** ruled the **kimzoner** on the great isle **Garumos** off Akrogal, until driven out by the necromancer **Valadäön**. With three ships (under **Kalameikos**, hence "Kalameikos' children," the *Kalameikiden* saga) they reached **Tiron Cruvi** ("the Green Land") and the Copper Sea, settling Koalint Bay; building the white cities **Tyros den Gyllene** (the Golden, capital), **Sidon den Drömmande** (the Dreaming), **Elikon den Pärlskimrande** (the Pearl-shimmering). Named "felicier" after Felis. Rich trade (all but the elves, who foresaw their doom). Under **Cleriomon II** they expanded onto Pelenos and toward Soluna (until checked by brown *ankor*). **310 eO:** invaded by **Dermanien**; southern colonies fell, but the fleet stopped a full invasion; King **Kiomolon III** became a Dermanian vassal until 343, when a Dermanian general **Assarhaddon** was set as king — provoking a revolt that drove out Dermanien (felicier keeping the colonies Bylos and Caraidon). The **Berendian wars (344–411)** failed on land (felicier hate mountains) but won at sea — the **Battle of Selim (392)** crushed the Zorakin fleet (which had aided Berendien); peace 411 (unbroken since). Zorakin ceded the **Hakkai** islands (394). The **Sombatzian invasion (499 eO):** Felicien's new slave-market Sombatze armed its smuggled-in slaves and timed a fleet invasion with the worst slave revolt in Felician history (Tyros and Sidon besieged two months); King **Karmos** defeated the relief squadron and crushed the revolt — mass executions (rebels cast into the sea to Paridon), Sombatze paying huge reparations. Now King **Kuthegil Halvskägg** thinks the time ripe to dominate the Copper Sea (overpopulated land, strong army).

**Felician military structure (approx.):** Fleet ~20,000 men peacetime / 150,000 wartime (King & Grand Admiral) → Marine Corps and Sea Corps (each ~10,000/30,000 under an admiral) → flottiljer (10,000, underamiral) → kommendör (2,000) → kapten (400). Army under the King & General.

**Notable names:** King **Kuthegil Halvskägg**; founders **Kiolomon Sjöfararen**, **Kalameikos**, **Cleriomon II**, kings **Kiomolon III**, **Karmos**; the necromancer **Valadäön**; cities **Tyros, Sidon, Elikon**; gods **Paridon, Philas, Felis, Galmon**; the Battle of **Selim**; **Hakkai**, **Kea**, **Bylos**, **Caraidon**.

## 5. HYNSOLGE

A beautiful eastern land torn by a long civil war between the feudal nobility on one side and the king + common folk on the other. Patron god **Fingal**; the older **Parbager** pantheon strong in the countryside; a hidden demon-cult lies beneath it all.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~110,000
- **Demographics:** humans 100%
- **Capital:** Orkovia (~1,500)
- **Head of state:** King **Mefimor IV**
- **Government:** feudal monarchy
- **Exports:** livestock (breeding stallions), handicrafts
- **Imports:** metals, luxuries, fabrics, slaves
- **Wealth:** poor (because of the war)
- **Army:** small but strong king-loyal army; mostly mercenaries used in the civil war
- **Religion:** **Fingal** is the realm's patron; the **Parbag** cult is also strong; ancestor/spirit worship occurs
- **Notes:** a civil war is currently underway between the king and the nobility

**Geography.** Even, fine Copper Sea coasts (good for fishing/bathing, poor for shipping — except the major harbor **Fervidun** at Sirigon). Farmland inland (maize, sugar beet, some lemons); horse-breeding in the less fertile north. Bordered by mountains N & E, the Copper Sea W, Landori's forests NW, Krun S. Many deep rivers (good for riverboats) full of fish and **lobuster** (small dragon-shaped crayfish). Two named forests: **Hyllmarskogen** (rich game, the rare *bäckbjörn*) and the dreaded **Gormowacs skog** (dense, with the strangler-vine *stryparranka*, monstrous boars, blood-leeching tree-leeches, rumored monstrous cats). The civil war has devastated the countryside (burned farms, the flooded **Gridevandafloden**).

**Provinces (län) & their capitals/political state:** Baldor (Olséhem ~300; baron forced to join royalists); Kunhaga (Ghamborg ~500; royalist-held now); **Hamellien** (**Orkovia** ~1,500; ruled by King Mefimor IV); Jamarund (Sabarun ~400; baron besieged by rebels); Varpodia (Bolbosa ~300; baron besieged); **Sirigon** (**Fervidun** ~3,000; at war with the king); Brangoria (Gråborg ~1,250; political free-city since 538 eO); Mergilia (Bedorum ~500; under Abrahim Stenklo); Metipov (Orlangin ~250; forced into a pact with the New Army); Galdinor (Arbine ~300; under Abrahim Stenklo); Ansorvia (Kandor ~600; ruled by Abrahim Stenklo, new baron); Pendor (Wolkor — destroyed) and Olakia (Bemir — both washed away in the flood of 597).

**Cities.**
- **Orkovia** (capital, in the royal *län* Hamellien): built on the site where **Fingal Gorrond** defeated the blood-elf **Korumil Gormowac**'s orc army ~500 years ago. Ruled by King **Mefimor IV** (now sharing power with a loyal council of 20 merchants/priests). Loyal **Fingal-cult** stronghold; the **Parbag-cult** has weak hold. Circular wall; marble streets, glass windows, mosaics in the rich center, craft/merchant outer wards, no slum. Nearby the 60 m **Orchknölen** (where Fingal burned the fallen orcs for eight days) — now an execution site.
- **Fervidun** (Sirigon): founded by **Gordil Mauroc** in 412 fO, once the capital; now ruled by Sirigon's baron line and the **Angusia-cult**'s seat. The realm's only real harbor/trade city — so its feudal nobility profit hugely (other provinces pay high port fees; royalists barred). City guard = the **Angusia Order's Rättskipare** (justice based on social strata, favoring nobility); large slum.
- **Gråborg** (Brangoria): originally Brangoria's capital; in 538 baron **Djauruc Riikov** let the town buy its freedom (a contract making it politically neutral for 100 years). Now a craft city (famous Master Guldorf's silver jewelry, Master Klack-Soeren's footwear/toys), ruled by a ~10-person council under Hamellien's laws.

**The hynsolger.** Short (165–170 cm), lean, big feet, lightly tanned, black/dark-brown hair (men long, often braided, with big drooping mustaches), strong cheekbones. Country folk wear light durable work clothes; formal dress is the **tuxan** (black short-jacket suit with a wide red silk sash) for men, voluminous white/red dress for women. At heart still barbarians — hot-tempered, quick to arms; young men must pass manhood-trials (riding; spear, throwing-spear, shortbow, bola; unarmed combat; hunting). Originally spoke **prangti** (a gesture-heavy barbarian tongue, now nearly extinct, no writing); now speak **hynsolgiska** (a *jorisk* dialect close to Zorakian/Kardian). Very superstitious in the countryside (iron nails over doors, herb-pouches against ghosts; thunderstorms are evil omens — anyone caught outside is suspected of witchcraft or **Julsuian** worship; lynchings occur).

**The Uprising.** Centuries of growing noble tyranny → revolt. The common folk vent long-stored aggression against the nobility; the **king is on the people's side**, giving the rebellion moral weight; the imprisoned poet-hero **Agila Dubbelhand** is a rallying martyr.

**Politics.** The once-strong (2-century) monarchy has lost power to the feudal nobility; the king now effectively rules only his own *län* **Hamellien** (where he keeps legislative/judicial power, appoints officials, leads the military; taxes handled by a council of 20 learned men). Each of the other twelve *län* is ruled by a feudal **baron/friherre** (hereditary, eldest son inherits) with total power; under them ~10 **markiser** (collect taxes, raise warriors, may dub knights); barons hold up to a dozen knights. Several *län* (Brangoria, Mergilia, Ansorvia) have had serfdom since 381 eO. The **borgare** (burghers) exist only in Orkovia (and maybe Gråborg) — they back the Uprising; some favor a bloodless "silent revolution" (per author **Egilor Fjäderpenna**'s book *Den tysta revolutionen*).

**The priesthood's politics.** The **Fingal-cult** is wholly royalist (so unpopular with the nobility). The **Angusia** followers favor the feudal nobility and are funded by them (their **Justicier** advise barons on law/administration; strong in the north and Sirigon). Worshippers of **Samyyd, Grilovana, Reogil** are mostly common folk who lean royalist. **Julsuian** cultists are usually apolitical anarchists (big in Olakia and south Pendor after the flood). Other ancestor-cults stay neutral.

**Current war situation.** The Uprising has raged ~a decade and may be ending. King **Mefimor IV** has subdued Baldor and Kunhaga. Sirigon's wealthy *friherre* **Anargel Ståhlkänga** has hired orc and Felician mercenaries and holds off the king with siege engines. **Abrahim Stenklo** leads the **New Army** (the people's army) from Ansorvia (Mergilia just beaten; peace in Galdinor and Pendor, though bandits roam south Pendor); his New Army is weakened, awaiting foreign mercenaries; Brangoria is besieged, and Abrahim seems about to attack its baron **Rigurt Riikov**. The two strongest holdouts remain **Brangoria** and **Sirigon** (with the harbor Fervidun able to import mercenaries). If the royalists win, the king regains full power, abolishes hereditary nobility, and rewards the Uprising's champions with vacant *län* (a great chance for player-characters).

**Military.** The king's forces are led by his **seven generals (Mactubve)** — chiefly Fingal-loyal infantry plus cavalry. Feudal troops vary: Sirigon's army is mostly mercenaries (orcs, felicier) with siege engines; Brangoria's is all-human. The **New Army** (under Abrahim Stenklo) is peasants and common folk, mostly infantry, armed with everything from pitchforks to real weapons.

**Religion — the Fingal cult.** The realm's official patron is the deified hero **Fingal Orchnackare** ("Orc-cleaver" / Fingal Gorrond), god of military strength and the realm's unification (popular with royalists). Depicted as a tall, muscular, bearded man in mail with a huge greatsword, on a giant black mail-clad stallion. Symbol: a seven-pointed yellow star on dark blue (Fingal's star, which appeared at his death, autumn 142 eO). His clergy/temple-knights are anti-feudal, trained in both arms and learning; they swear to obey superiors, oppose the realm's fragmentation, and never flee from svartfolk.

**Religion — the Parbager (the Urgods).** Older (traced to ~900 fO), strong in the countryside. A divine family of nine, headed by **Anyak** (the Light-giver) and his wife **Angusia** (Warder of the Gates). Followers usually worship one god but honor the others (except Julsuian):
- **Anyak — the Light-giver:** god of light (strongest in east Hynsolge), friend of the sun/salamanders, linked to fire; symbol a yellow four-rayed sun; white-marble temples with an eternal flame; priests in white tunics, never head-coverings.
- **Angusia — Warder of the Gates:** Anyak's wife, goddess of wisdom and justice, guards the gates to **Bolgove**'s silent halls; symbol a golden key on a black hexagon. Her great temple **Pervusib-Angusia** centers the feudal-friendly **Angusia Order**, in three groups: **nologer** (study/administration), **justicer** (higher law), and **rättskipare** (~50 knights hunting criminals). Members may use force; fanatical about justice and the feudal order (the nobility's rule is god-given).
- **Ulvar — the Dark-bringer:** god of darkness, moon, night-cold; son of Anyak (not at odds with him — they share the day's hours). Small cult, no temples; many hang his symbol (a full moon) over their beds.
- **Grilovana — Queen of the Springs:** the Urfathers' youngest daughter, Ulvar's wife; weather-goddess of running water and sky, surrounded by birds; symbol a white dove on light blue; popular with animists/farmers/fishers; no temples or priesthood.
- **Samyyd — Mistress of the Meadows:** fertility goddess, very popular; small temples in every village (farmers offer crops monthly); symbol the circle (eternal rebirth); priestesses do charity/poor-relief.
- **Reogil — Lord of the Beasts:** god of forests and wild animals; his servants never kill for sport, offering a slain beast's heart to him; symbol a stag's head (priests wear antlered helms); followers wear no metal armor.
- **Bolgove — Mistress of Silence:** goddess of sleep, dream, silence, mystery and death; daughter of Ulvar and Grilovana; no known symbol (priestesses favor black); many know Spiritism and Illusionism.
- **Bolthar — the Axe-bearer:** war god, son of Samyyd and Reogil; followers prize strength/discipline/weapon-skill; favorite weapon the two-handed axe; wear no metal armor in battle; symbol a stylized two-handed axe.
- **Julsuian — Slave of Frivolity:** the family's black sheep — chaos, drugs, alcohol, sloth, carnal lusts (including deviant). Worshipped secretly; biggest in the underworld (prostitutes, smugglers, drug-dealers, addicts); symbol two linked rings with a threaded arrow, often tattooed.

**Stjärnläran (the Star Doctrine).** Preached from 421 by **Mustansir Omurtaag**; rooted among the nobility of Brangoria and Mergilia. Worships not a god but the universe and heavenly bodies; interested in magic, divination, alchemy, astrology, occult sciences. A secret society in four circles (Outer, Second, Third, Focal), each with ranks (laymen; soothsayers/astrologers/alchemists; novices/disciples/veterans; spiritists/necromancers/**Onaabys's servants**). The Outer circle (85%) provides income; the Second (10%) are brainwashed cultists; the Third (3%) are occult scholars; the **Focal circle** are the leaders, schooled in spiritism/necromancy, topped by "Onaabys's servant" (the high priest, taking orders from **Parikila**). The sect is behind the **kidnappings** of Parikila's victims.

**Shamans.** Older than even the Parbager cult; nearly gone, but many shamans live in **Olakia**, where the bandit-bands have revived tribal society and ancestor-worship.

**History highlights.**
- **~1350–1065 fO:** Krun grants Hynsolge to allied barbarian tribes; the **Krunska kriget** (Krun War, 1080); 1065 the hynsolger beat Krun at **Grivela** → the empire **Jorpagna** is founded. Wars with **Efaro**.
- **~900 fO:** Jorpagna conquers Hynsolge; the *jorisk* upper class flourishes, locals impoverished/enslaved; *jori* becomes the common tongue.
- **~800 fO:** province **Lasutyp** (later Nidland) made a sub-province of Hynsolge.
- **~750 fO:** Akrogal nomads on **hippogrifs** attack Krun; Hynsolge hides its great war-treasures.
- **599 fO:** the Third Conflux — a three-tailed **comet** (likely from the red moon **Raukhra**) strikes Hynsolge; plague kills 1/5, another 1/5 flee.
- **412 fO:** warrior **Gordil Mauroc** unites the realm, founds capital **Fervidun**; murdered 402 (by conspirators including his brother **Pirow Mauroc**) → oligarchy → collapse.
- **~50–124 eO:** orc raids; an orc army under blood-elf **Korumil Gormowac** invades; **Fingal Gorrond** ("Orchnackare") crushes it at the future Orkovia (124), is made king, picks twelve vassals; founds **Orkovia** (127). 139: Lasutyp joins voluntarily.
- **142 eO:** the childless king dies; astrologer **Elias den Vidreste** sees a new star; **Fingal is deified** as patron god.
- **312 eO:** weak king **Tanbul** loses power to the nobility; oppression of the people grows.
- **375–381 eO:** peasant revolt in Ansorvia/Mergilia (with dwarf aid) crushed; those provinces serf-bound.
- **419 eO:** "the Dwarves' March" toward Cereval; Hynsolge forced to stop them, weakening the king.
- **421 eO:** **Mustansir Omurtaag**, advisor to baron **Aurolf Järnnäve** of Brangoria, founds the **Star Doctrine** after a vision.
- **468 eO:** eastern province **Lasutyp** falls to **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** → becomes **Nidland**.
- **538 eO:** **Gråborg** buys its freedom (100-year neutrality), becomes a refuge for political fugitives.
- **543 eO:** Brangoria's nobles start slave-trading; slaves vanish mysteriously (numbers never grow…).
- **580–587 eO:** **Agila Dubbelhand** leads a small Mergilia revolt, is imprisoned; smuggles out poems that spread among the people ("The twisted horn we follow, the time of slavery is over").
- **596–606 eO:** flood in the SW; famines; the people besiege the nobles of Mergilia and Ansorvia → **the Hynsolge Uprising** begins (604).
- **604–610 eO:** Abrahim Stenklo (ennobled by Mefimor IV) leads the New Army; king declares war on Sirigon (606); Baldor, Kunhaga, Galdinor, north Pendor, Mergilia subdued; in Mergilia the rebels free **Agila Dubbelhand** (held hostage). **610 eO: NOW!**

**The comet's dark secret (GM info).** The comet from **Raukhra** (the Red Moon) radiates pure evil/chaos and caused the plagues; underwater most of the time, its power is dampened. Through it the demon **Onaabys — the Soul-eater** sent a vision to **Mustansir Omurtaag** (421), who founded the Star Doctrine. In 425 the Focal circle drained the comet crater, summoned Onaabys, and had him sire a child on Mustansir's wife (most cultists died in the resulting epidemic). The boy **Tervel** was sickly until they learned he must **devour one soul per week** to survive (his inheritance from Onaabys); he then grew explosively, fully adult in eight years, killed his stepfather, took his post as advisor to Brangoria's baron and the new name **Parikila** ("father-slayer"), and turned to dark magic (per some, inspired by **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern**). To get a victim each week without suspicion, Parikila had the baron let **Gråborg** buy its freedom (more people to kidnap) and start slave-trading — explaining the vanishing slaves.

**Notable names:** King **Mefimor IV**; **Fingal Gorrond/Orchnackare** (patron god); the blood-elf **Korumil Gormowac**; **Abrahim Stenklo** (New Army); the poet-martyr **Agila Dubbelhand**; *friherre* **Anargel Ståhlkänga** of Sirigon; baron **Rigurt Riikov** (Brangoria), **Djauruc Riikov**, **Boris Guldstenck**, **Aurolf Järnnäve**; founder **Gordil Mauroc** (& brother **Pirow**); astrologer **Elias den Vidreste**; author **Egilor Fjäderpenna**; **Mustansir Omurtaag** → **Parikila/Tervel**; the demon **Onaabys**; gods **Anyak, Angusia, Ulvar, Grilovana, Samyyd, Reogil, Bolgove, Bolthar, Julsuian**; places **Orkovia, Fervidun, Gråborg, Gormowacs skog, Hyllmarskogen, Orchknölen, Olakia**.

## 6. JORDUASHUR

A cold, willful Norse-style land of four jarldoms on a great northern peninsula, famous for its dragon-ships, the **Æsir** gods, dwarven neighbors, and a lost-paradise elf-forest. Settled ~150 years after Ibram Shahalat's circumnavigation.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~30,000
- **Demographics:** 50% humans, 7% dwarves, 10% elves, 33% black-/stonefolk
- **Capital:** Halle (~1,000)
- **Head of state:** **Elfhelm Korp**
- **Government:** union of four jarldoms
- **Exports:** salt, amber, metals, dwarven handicrafts
- **Imports:** luxury goods, fabrics, "plunder loot"
- **Wealth:** poor
- **Army:** none; unusually strong levy (up to 30% of the population)
- **Religion:** worship of **Æsir**, a family of gods
- **Notes:** the people are famed for their dragon-ships

**Geography.** A great peninsula N of Ransard and NW of Nargur. Only the coasts hold humans; the interior is wild orc/minotaur land or mysterious elf realms. West coast: jarldoms **Allnemark** and **Sigsdal**; north of them the highland **Ederls huvud** with the orc realm **Orxygg**; east of that the storm-lashed **Nordal** coast; and **Krakelid** on the Drakrännan coast. The deep **Kardskogen** forest holds at least one elf tribe. Travel is mostly by boat (almost no roads).

**The four jarldoms.** Effectively one realm but with no real king for ~150 years (since **Harald Krokig**, who founded Krakelid in Ibram Shahalat's day). The man seen abroad as king of Jorduashur — **Elfhelm Korp**, jarl of Allnemark — has no power over the others (locals regard their own jarl as king).
- **Allnemark** (southernmost, most populous, ~6,000): a string of ~200 farms down a fertile coast plus the city **Halle** (~1,000) and the **Svalöarna** islands (often seen as a fifth jarldom; home to some of Jorduashur's best sailors). Ruled by the young jarl **Sigurd Brandsvedd** from Halle. Halle has the realm's only multi-story wooden houses, the largest court, the most thieves and warriors — and **Allneborg**, the realm's main stone fortress on an islet, seat of the **storting** (great assembly) and the gilded **kungasal** (king's hall). Trade is protected by the **Hallefred** (Halle-peace): anyone bound for market under a solid-red banner is safe from attack (breaking it = outlaw for a year and a day). South, in the **Kardskogen**, lies the contested elf/orc border.
- **Sigsdal** (north of Allnemark, on the cold stormy **Sigsudd** peninsula, ~4,500): named for "victory" (won from orcs). Scattered fjord-farms; rich in minerals (many mines among men, dwarves, orcs and ogres — people sometimes pay tax to both a local orc chieftain and the jarl). Ruled by **Hjörleif Sköldklyve** from the fortified village **Tarkens Krök** (built on a clifftop by the river Tarken, nearly impregnable). Home to the **dwarf realm Kward'zak** (~1,000+ dwarves; cities **Kward'korzub** and the secret **Gerlwerd**); relations with the dwarves are good (and vital).
- **Nordal** (youngest jarldom, ~2,000+): a long, storm-lashed northern coast; formed ~25 years ago. People long for solitude yet bind tightly into large families; farms are far apart. Mostly they raid (barbarian lands, or south in Magilre/Klomellien). No real capital — only jarl **Håkon Smed**'s own farm **Härdsäkre**; the jarl has little power, and feuds between farms/families are common.
- **Krakelid** (last jarldom, ~3,000, capital-town ~500): founded by King **Harald Krokig** as an outpost in conquered barbarian provinces; site of the realm's second fortress. By the **Drakrännan** ("Dragon Channel," said to be the death-furrow of an enormous fallen dragon). Trades the area's great **rock-salt**; ruled (a loose hand) by **Ulf Stupa**, Harald Krokig's descendant; often raided by eastern barbarians (which unites the people). "Barbarians" = everything east of the border (Jordukerl-like folk and *jorisk* colonists).
- **Kardskogen:** elves in the SW ("Alvskogen," safe to travel), orcs/minotaurs/ogres elsewhere (avoid). All timber (for boats and houses) must come from here, traded from the elves (who forbid free logging); a dangerous business due to roaming 20–30-strong orc bands.

**The people (jordukerlar).** Large and powerful (avg. >180 cm), bearded and shaggy-maned against the cold. Distant kin to the barbarians of Barbia and Nargur, but taller and fairer. The key social unit is the **clan/kin (släkt)**, then the **family** (all who live on a farm, not only blood-kin); a farm of houses ("skåler" with turf/stone walls) often holds several households under the chief family. Speak **jordiska** (a barbarian tongue related to Ransardian).

**Government.** Four jarldoms, one realm, but no king (a king needs at least half the chieftains' votes; none has had them in ages). Each jarldom is independent under its hereditary **jarl** (now chosen in practice by the storting). The **storting** (in Halle's king's-hall, at least yearly) settles matters above a single jarl; any free person may bring an issue, even demand a jarl's removal (rarely effective). Each jarldom has its own **ting** (plus one on Svalöarna and a town-ting in Halle), led by a life-appointed law-man (often a blind/injured man); complex cases get a **visråd** of 8–12 men. Equality between free men and free women is nearly total. Below the jarls sit rich great-farmers (whose land others use), bound in a complex web of loyalties/alliances central to daily life.

**Trade.** Two main routes: **west** along the Fyrsneca current via Svalöarna to Trakorien (or further south — often selling the cargo at a Trakorian-island harbor); **east** with the primitive northern coast cultures (e.g. Barbia's horse-folk), occasionally all the way down Akrogal's rivers past Demontungan to Berendian/Felician harbors or south to Soluna (rare, dangerous, often bloody). Chief goods: **amber** (Orghin's coasts), **salt** (Krakelid), **metal** (Sigsdal). Two main ships: the **knarr** (robust, ~15 tons cargo, one mast, ~15–25 crew, slow) and the **drakskepp** (dragon-ship: shallow-draft, fast, flat-bottomed, war-suited, ~4–8 tons + up to ~50–60 passengers; carved gods in the prow — **Wödur**, **Elokir** — and a dragon/figurehead; named ships, decorated personally).

**Military.** No standing forces except the jarls' **hirds** (up to ~100 men sworn to blind loyalty in exchange for keep). But most of the population is battle-trained and can field a levy of up to **30%**.

Afterward, a long quiet period of yearly raids on Trakorien, Klomellien, Ransard and Kardien — until those grew into organized states; now trade often replaces raiding, though raids still happen.

## 7. KARDIEN

A feudal crusader kingdom on the Aidne peninsula, ruled exclusively by nobles of the **echther** people, devoted to the **Shining Way**, and bound to Zorakin by ancient alliance. (Detailed further in the module *Ivanhoe*.)

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~160,000
- **Demographics:** humans 90%, *ankor* 7%, halflings 2%, elf-folk 1%
- **Capital:** Ekeborg (~7,000)
- **Head of state:** **Vidar VI**
- **Government:** feudal monarchy
- **Exports:** timber, iron, copper, fur
- **Imports:** fabrics, salt, precious metals
- **Wealth:** poor
- **Army:** weak fleet, small army, strong knightly cavalry
- **Religion:** the Shining Way
- **Notes:** Kardien's noble class is extremely strong; described in detail in the campaign module *Ivanhoe*

**Geography.** On the **Aidne peninsula** around the towering **Aidne-bergen**; subtropical (like Spain/Italy). Split by the river **Stirpaz** (border with Zorakin): west lies the fertile **Divra-slätten** (wheat/rye, worked by serfs; the **Insakullarna** on **Faltraxnäs**); the broad slow **Gridefloden** is the key waterway linking villages to the cities **Arntuna** and **Ekeborg**. North of the mountains is **Inmark** (sparse, raided by mountain svartfolk) and the remote, poor north (duchies **Videlrike** and **Nordmark**) — weak royal control since the rise of the elf-realm **Goiana**, with svartfolk on **Antivin**'s north coast and a stone-age native people, the **ker-bosh**. The mountains hold cliff-goats, predators, svartfolk, and legends of cyclopes and giants. Forests are scarce (over-logged for ships): **Gladaskogen** in the west, **Brynhildurs skog** in the east (largely elf-inhabited, its trees protected from steel).

**Peoples.** The ruling **echther** (Divra; proud, suspicious of strangers, intensely honor-bound — would rather die than break their word; arrogant toward other folk; only echther can be nobles; nobles must marry within the echther). The **faltrakier** (Kardien's equivalent of Zorakin's legerdier — cheerful, good-natured farmers/fishers, a cowed people scattered in small villages, easy for nobles to control). Minorities: the **penterna** (Divra's natives, an autonomous province whose chieftains swear a special oath and rank as *underdrots*, paying only a yearly tribute to Ekeborg), the **ker-bosh** (Antivin's natives, left to themselves), the **svartfolk** (Aidne/Antivin, unsubdued), the **elf-folk** of Goiana's forests (~1,000, left in peace as the king's personal subjects), and a normal share of **halflings and ankor** (full members of Kardian society, mostly in the free cities).

**Language.** **Kardiska** — close to Zorakian but more like imperial *jori* (Zorakier find Kardier old-fashioned and stilted; Kardier find Zorakier sloppy).

**Government.** Feudal: an absolute king over **dukes (hertigar)**, **counts (grevar)** and **barons** — all echther only (giving the nobility much more power than in Zorakin); other folk may only be priests, burghers, farmers. Eldest son inherits estates undivided (creating many landless nobles who become mercenaries, order-knights or priests). Each vassal swears two oaths (to feudal lord and to king; the king's oath takes precedence). Castles/walls need royal permission (so even a rich city is defenceless before noble troops). Ten dukes form the **Rådet** (council) advising the king; barons are smaller and weaker than in Zorakin. Land was divided differently from Zorakin: each duke/count kept part of his fief as personal land; vassals pay only military service, not tax. **Ekenfara** is the king's personal domain (royal officials, non-hereditary; its income plus the free cities' fees make the king's treasury normally ≥5× the richest noble's — usually the dukes of **Satgherin** or **Ridfara**). The king's power is vast but rarely used (to preserve the balance — misusing it has ended "with a dagger between the shoulder blades"). Two kinds of cities: noble-owned (taxed, run by a *fogde*) and **free cities** (bought freedom + a royal charter to build walls and levy tolls; ruled by a council of guild *rådmän* sworn to the king).

**Military.** Noble retinues; peacetime soldier caps: King 500, Duke 100, Count 50, Baron 25, Free city 25, Exark 300, Tots-kerigass 50, Kerisgass 50, Bontisål 10 (church military orders not counted). Wartime adds conscripted peasants, mercenaries, and untrained city levies. The war-fleet is small and old; the **sun-knights'** (Shining Way order) warships would form the main fleet.

**Religion.** All Kardier devoutly follow the **Shining Way**; the capital **Ekeborg** (a softened form of "Echtherborg") holds the great **exark palace** from which the whole Shining Way church is governed (except Caddo). The church is nearly as mighty as the nobility.

**History.** The Conflux wrecked the *jorisk* culture (Kardien fared a bit better than the rest of Jorpagna, but all administration collapsed). 650 years of chaos followed before a new realm formed. A little civil war hit southern Kardien; the people gathered into villages; their leader **Valentin Echthe** died of illness soon after, but his followers became the **echther**, refusing dealings with other folk. Northern Kardien + Faltraxnäs was long a separate realm trying to expand, nominally part of the realm but uncontrolled — until the elf-realm **Goiana** declared independence in the east. Around then a strange malaise (some blame the **sorcerer of the Black Tower**) made all the rulers lose the will to rule ("in eternity's perspective it makes no difference"). With no one wanting to lead, the people offered the rule to the prosperous **echther**, who reluctantly accepted, dividing the realm (Zorakin-style) into duchies/counties for their own officials, locking down the faltrakier legally — and spreading the Shining Way to the north.

**Notable names:** King **Vidar VI**; founder **Valentin Echthe**; the echther chieftain **Vidar Odise** (whose 56-fO alliance with Zorakin's **King Valien** binds the two realms); the **sorcerer of the Black Tower**; capital **Ekeborg** (and its **exark palace**); cities **Arntuna**; the elf-realm **Goiana**; the **penterna** and **ker-bosh** peoples; the dukes of **Satgherin/Ridfara**; the royal domain **Ekenfara**.

## 8. KLOMELLIEN

A loosely joined union of eleven independent city-states on Ereb's west coast (Gryppa Bay); seven described. Named for the legendary hero **Klomul** and his giant turtles, the **klomuller**.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~125,000
- **Demographics:** 70% humans, 15% halflings, 8% *ankor*, 5% dwarves, 2% **vargmän** (wolf-men)
- **Capital:** none
- **Head of state:** none
- **Government:** union of eleven self-governing city-states
- **Exports:** timber, salt, iron, handicrafts, pearls
- **Imports:** fabrics, foodstuffs
- **Wealth:** varies between city-states
- **Army:** varies between city-states
- **Religion:** several, incl. the Shining Way
- **Notes:** the land consists of eleven independent states

**Geography.** On **Gryppas vik**, bounded east by the low **Melashebergen**. North is the bare plain **Ishna** (poor soil, thinly settled); east of it the hilly **Abzural**; along the eastern mountains the great forest **Ize**. Central **Lake Jaggarack** (steep cliffs) gathers the mountain rivers and drains via the deep river **Maure** to the sea. The coast is more fertile and dense — most city-states are here (trade, pearl/sea fishing, some farming).

**Klomuller.** Named for the hero **Klomul** (who hid under a giant water-turtle's shell from his enemies). These **giant turtles** are used as river-barge propulsion and for military purposes — the city-state **Triska**'s army sometimes mounts a superstructure on big klomuller as fighting platforms. (Older "north-klomuller" are calmer and bear towers more steadily.)

**The seven described city-states.**
- **Mercana** (the largest and most important, at the Maure's mouth bordering Magilre). Often confused with the city **Cvyrdo** across the river (which belongs to Magilre) — long ago the pirate **Gryppa Gultand** took the river-city and sold one half to Magilre's dwarves and the other to Klomellien; Mercana regards Cvyrdo as its slum. Ruled by a **Handelsråd** (trade council) of the nine richest merchant families, with two annually-elected **stadsmästare** (currently **Otte Cusmar** and **Haliban Nusemang**) checking each other. Its wealth is trade: goods flow on the Maure to Mercana and out to Trakorien/Magilre via the **Handelsvägskompaniet** (Trade-Road Company) reaching the all-trading city **O**. Tolls of 10% on bulk goods → a **varubrev** (goods-letter) listing the taxed wares (forgeries are common). **Religion:** mostly **Olem** ("the Lazy"), who supposedly created Altor out of boredom, then regretted it and ponders destroying it; worshippers beg his mercy and entertain him with monthly plays and song-contests at his temple's open stage. A few **Remuntra** priests live here. ~85% human; ~15% halflings/ankor (ankor in a walled quarter).
- **Hamur** (south, in the Melashebergen; ~the realm's 4th-largest city). Born from a fort guarding a silver vein found by the geologist **Niel Småhacka**. **Politics:** the ruler is chosen by a **death-match** of eight armed candidates on a round stage — last one alive is crowned for one year (the people name the victor, e.g. **Mantar Vassekniv**, **Brodwer Skallkrossare**, **Ghulon Blodkäft**; once an exhausted champion **Humnur Krossaryxa** fell to a teenage boy). **Trade:** silver/iron/copper from the mountains down rail-carts to the river Wemer; Mercana houses keep metal-buying offices here. **Religion:** worship of **Æsir** (under other names). Population ~60% humans, 30% dwarves, 10% **vargmän**; might makes right (only a ~50-man royal guard); all men over 14 must serve in the army; famed for craft/smithing (made the brass globes for the famed **kronolab in Tricilve**). Descended from barbarians (mixed with Jorduashur colonists).
- **Nya Arno** (oligarchy, ~4,500; Shining Way, Caddo branch; farming/fishing): founded by shipwrecked **Caddo** survivors, keeping Caddoan customs strictly; good ties with Caddo; too small to oppose the other states' magic-use.
- **Addiaska** (monarchy, ~10,000; Shining Way, Kardian branch; pearl-fishing): a young state founded on pearl-rich reefs; famous pearl-jewelry craft (miniatures carved from a single pearl); an absolute king.
- **Yolev** (monarchy, ~7,000; Shining Way, Kardian branch; fishing): founded first of all, by exiled Jorpagna fishermen; self-sufficient on fishing, neither imports nor exports, the only state at peace with its neighbors; superb sailors (many Mercana captains come from Yolev).
- **Triska** (oligarchy, ~20,000; Æsir worship; farming): aggressive; ruled by its three mightiest families **Cudda, Vildbete, Peffomir**; constant internal power struggles paused only to grab neighbors' land; the largest state by area; uses its army for plunder raids in lean years (and its klomull fighting-platforms).
- **Sanzas** (democracy, ~12,000; no religion; farming): founded by halflings fleeing svartfolk; grows fine tobacco (exported); a sprawling village-like town ruled by a mayor and a *byråd* (council elected every five years); the halflings endure constant insults from larger neighbors.

**Interesting places.**
- **Kvulmsjön** (Kvulm Lake): brown, sluggish, foul-smelling water beside the eastern mountains; called "the gods' drain"/"the anal of evil"; only svartfolk live nearby. The dark-elf tribe **De hårlösa** ("the Hairless") on its north shore fishes the rare **kvulmerkarp** (sold in cities as "Golden carp," a delicacy).
- **Dimmiga dalen** (the Misty Valley): in the southern mountains, under perpetual fog; almost no one who descends returns — "an excellent place to set an adventure."
- **Joraga:** ruined city on the isle **Diamor** in Lake Jaggarack — destroyed when a mage summoned a fire-demon from Chaos and lost control; the demon burned all of Diamor but couldn't leave (couldn't travel air or water) and finally died when nothing was left to burn. Sailors avoid it.

**History.** Settled late (long a svartfolk haunt). Jorpagna colonists settled the Copper Sea's north coast, then Magilre, and only ~620 fO crossed the mountains into Klomellien. The Third Conflux hit lightly; when Jorpagna fell, its Klomellien colonies became independent. Since then Klomellien has stayed obscure in Ereb's histories — too internally split and too out-of-the-way. Its main enemy is **Magilre** (eyeing its raw materials); good ties with **Kardien** (salt for food/metals). Its **salt** production gives broad modest prosperity (anyone can extract a few kilos of valuable salt in days).

**Notable names:** the legendary **Klomul** and the **klomuller** (giant turtles); pirate **Gryppa Gultand**; Mercana's **stadsmästare Otte Cusmar** & **Haliban Nusemang**; Hamur champions **Mantar Vassekniv, Brodwer Skallkrossare, Ghulon Blodkäft, Humnur Krossaryxa**; geologist **Niel Småhacka**; the explorer-writer **Habblar**; the god **Olem den Late**; Triska families **Cudda, Vildbete, Peffomir**; dark-elf tribe **De hårlösa**; places **Mercana, Hamur, Nya Arno, Addiaska, Yolev, Triska, Sanzas, Kvulmsjön, Dimmiga dalen, Joraga, the city O**.

## 9. NIDLAND

A walled magocratic dictatorship in easternmost Ereb, ruled with an iron hand by the dark-magician **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern**. Strongly Akrogal-influenced, sealed behind a Great Wall, and (according to rumor) arming for an apocalyptic crusade.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~240,000
- **Demographics:** humans 100%
- **Capital:** Khaanikel (~18,000)
- **Head of state:** **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** (the Arch-Magician)
- **Government:** magocratic dictatorship
- **Exports:** fabrics, precious metals, spices, handicrafts
- **Imports:** iron, slaves, weapons
- **Wealth:** poor
- **Army:** unknown — probably strong
- **Religion:** **kuzherismen** (a strongly racist monotheism)
- **Notes:** ruled with an iron hand by the dark-magician Vicotnik; foreigners not admitted without permission; magic forbidden; a wall surrounds the whole realm

**Geography.** Mostly a ~400 m highland between the **Grynnerbergen** and **Demontungan**. The land slopes steeply to the coast; the narrow coastal strips are richly cultivated with **alkhéri** (a grass whose ears mill to a green flour). The highland is meager (thin soil bound by shrubs and low trees), grazed by sheep and cliff-goats.

**The Great Wall (Den Stora Muren).** Runs along Nidland's entire border, even against the sea — 4 m high, 4 m thick, with 6 m towers every 50 m, built 502–541 (largely by convicts, criminals and bought slaves). Heavily fortified gates stand every 100th tower (every 5 km); it is said to be impossible to enter Nidland except through these day-and-night-guarded gates (though the wall is incomplete in places, especially at the coasts).

**The nidlänningar.** Tall, sun-tanned, black-haired; men wear big bushy full beards. Dress is uniform: very wide trousers (**puffader**), a cloth vest (**khresh**) or a long robe (**thogu**) over a light shirt, and the **burtan** headdress (a long cloth strip wound into a ball). Under Vicotnik the once-proud people have become mentally dulled and weak — they obey orders without thinking; whatever the Arch-Magician or his judges say is law. They speak **nidländska** (originally an Akrogal dialect, now so mixed with *jorisk* and barbarian tongues that other Akrogalers can't understand it).

**Government.** Vicotnik rules absolutely. Minor offenses → forced labor; serious crimes → the **Labraatriet**, the Arch-Magician's own experiment-chamber, where he and his pupils train their magic and seek new uses (horror-tales of bone-marrow turned to boiling lead, dissolved skeletons leaving a quivering lump, a head moved onto the belly, curses of trembling at sunlight); no one leaves without grave deformities or psychic damage, and such victims are then usually cast out and killed. **Vicotnik is arming for a great war** and has taken three steps to become the people's natural leader: (1) improved the lot of society's lowest (so they'll be good soldiers and feel indebted/loyal); (2) used spells to **erase the past from people's memories**, severing them from history so that Nidland is the only world they know; (3) used his harsh laws and dark-magic mind-reading to appear a "just leader," so that with kuzherism the people half-regard him as a god.

**Administration.** Below Vicotnik are **20 pupils** (dark-mages), called **domare (judges)** — they use mind-reading to decide guilt (civil cases follow an 800-chapter precedent book, *De åttahundra stegen*). Below them are **discipler** (50–100 promising young mages studying the innermost dark-magic secrets, hoping to become judges); failures become mountain hermits sworn never to use magic (Vicotnik ensures they keep the oath). Each town/village has an **åldersmäster** (usually the oldest man — he knows everyone, and his loss matters little) who answers for the community and has unofficial authority by any means short of breaking Vicotnik's laws.

**Crimes & punishments (sample).** Non-nidlänningar on Nidland soil (a permit costs ~10 gm/day): 1 month forced labor per illegal day + 10 gm/day fine (repeated if penniless). Killing a nidlänning: **Labraatriet**. Theft: 1 day forced labor × the goods' value in sm. Preaching any creed but Kuzher's: **Labraatriet**. Using magic: **Labraatriet**. Begging: forced conscription into the army.

**Trade.** Foreigners may not set foot on Nidland soil without a permit, so trade happens at great **floating bazaars** outside the harbors (giving good oversight of all imports/exports). Exports: silk fabrics, gold, silver. Imports: spices (Akrogal), timber (Barbia), iron and weapons (Grynnerbergen dwarves), horses (Hynsolge). The nidlänningar are known as honest traders who pay well for quality.

**Military.** Unknown strength, but much suggests vast armies are being gathered for **the Great Cleansing (Den Stora Reningen)**, a huge crusade across Ereb. When Vicotnik first came to Ereb (468 eO) he prophesied that the world had "a dozen dozen years" (144) to submit to him; with only two years left, no land has even spoken to him, and no one knows what lies behind the Great Wall.

**Religion — kuzherismen.** The only permitted religion, likely invented by Vicotnik to suit his ends (travelers report bits of it among various Akrogal cultures, suggesting he stitched it together). Core: the nidlänningar are the **Kuzhers** ("the One [God]'s chosen people"), destined to rule Ereb; all other peoples are inferior; with the One's prophet (Vicotnik) they shall crush all false faiths and reclaim their predestined dominion. Invented ancient history explains their fallen state (they repeatedly betrayed Kuzher for other gods), which gives many nidlänningar a shame that fuels fanaticism. The **elf-folk** are especially loathed — supposedly always thwarting the master-race, treacherous, demonic (dark-vision, immortality, sharp hearing; their oval pupils "prove" they were made by the same evil god who made snakes and wildcats). Ereb's peoples are to be enlightened by either **mission or war** (those who don't convert "chose" war).

**History.** The area was long part of Hynsolge but became cut off after the Dwarves' March (early 400s eO). In **468 eO** a short, stout man in a black-brown silk robe walked in from the east over Demontungan, calling himself **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** — wielding a mighty, evil Eastern magic unknown to Ereb's scholars. He quickly gained followers, drove out the weak local rulers, and ruled through pupils he taught the new magic, punishing the disobedient horribly. Exiled nobles gave the land the unflattering name **Nidland**. He banned the old Hynsolgian gods, introduced **kuzherismen**, sharpened all punishments (his 20 judges dispensing "justice"), barred foreigners, banned all magic (except his pupils'), doubled taxes on the rich and put the poor to paid forced labor. In **502** the **Great Wall** began (meant to turn all of Nidland into one fortress; labor shortages left it incomplete at the coasts). In the ~70 years since its completion, few reports have escaped the "stone curtain"; the most reliable say the Arch-Magician is preparing an enormous war — taken seriously by few, but watched with great unease.

**Notable names:** **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** (Arch-Magician, founder/ruler); his **20 domare** (judge-mages) and the **discipler**; capital **Khaanikel**; the **Stora Muren** (Great Wall); the **Labraatriet** (experiment-chamber); the planned **Stora Reningen** (Great Cleansing); the faith **kuzherismen** (the Kuzhers).

## 10. RANSARD

A kingdom widely (and unfairly) seen in the south as a barbarian realm of "evildoers" — aristocratic warriors bound by a strict honor-code, infamous above all for their **griffin cavalry**. Currently mobilizing for a final reckoning with Trakorien.

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~130,000
- **Demographics:** 85% humans, 15% svartfolk
- **Capital:** Kapalht (~4,500)
- **Head of state:** **Argald II** (the **vortiger**; the current ruler, **Argald den Yngre**)
- **Government:** clan-based monarchy
- **Exports:** rock-salt, metals, furs
- **Imports:** precious metals, grain, plunder-loot
- **Wealth:** normal
- **Army:** very strong; flying griffin-cavalry
- **Religion:** worship of **Æsir**, a family of gods
- **Notes:** ransarder are known for their ruthlessness in battle; they use long open ships at sea and flying griffins on land

**Geography.** Western Ereb, south of Jorduashur, north of the Ljusna peninsula. The core is the three Ransardian peninsulas — **Nordland, Ransland, Sydland** — plus the recently-subjected kingdoms **Timora** and **Alerien** (nominally independent under **vortiger**-kings of Ransardian blood). Between Nordland/Ransland/Timora lies **Gauland** (independent but paying tribute and the same governance). Bounded east by the **Kardbergen** (nearly 4,000 m), home to several strong orc tribes (e.g. **Röda månens orcher**, "Red Moon's orcs"). Below the mountains a partly-forested highland reaches the coast; much forest has been burned for farmland. The Kardbergen hold many **griffin nests** — long ago young griffins were tamed, and the ransarder built a powerful **griffin cavalry**.

**The ransarder.** Well above medium height (~180 cm), powerfully built, fair-skinned, often blue-eyed, heavily bearded. They weave thick wind-proof cloth dyed in bright colors; each clan has its own pattern (so you can tell a ransarder's clan at a glance). Everyday dress: white linen shirt, long-sleeved jacket, heavy hose, and a **merrlokh** (a long cloth wound round the body, loose on the left so the spare cloth can parry). For battle: a mail shirt and metal-reinforced leather limb-guards, an open conical helm with mail aventail. Favorite arms: a **seax** (between short- and broadsword) and a **tarque** (small round shield with a central grip — also offensive, used to "knock" an opponent like a boxer's jab), plus a long (2.5 m) spear (usable mounted, in melee, or thrown) and a long dagger. Some carry instead a huge two-handed sword, the **glamorg** (up to 2 m). Griffin-borne archers are uncommon but extremely accurate (their iron-hard bows pierce both shield and steel helm in one shot). Promising young warriors get intensive training (weapons, unarmed combat, field survival, drill, command).

**Values.** Shaped by bitter conflict into aristocratic warriors of high self-esteem, living by a strict honor-code (indecision is a great sin). Proud, arrogant, sometimes cruel and ruthless; prize physical strength, despise indecision and cowardice. They consider themselves superior to all other folk (other peoples are "uncivilized rabble"), especially contemptuous of *ankor*, halflings and svartfolk. They have nothing against elves, but a widespread belief that elf-blood grants immortality makes elves avoid them. Among themselves they are reasonable and just (an offender stands by his crime without complaint); fearless on the battlefield, often notching a stick for each foe slain. A ransarder with an inferiority complex usually leaves his clan to seek fortune abroad (hiding his origin — ransarder are not popular). They speak **ransardiska**, a west-Nargurian dialect close to *jordiska* (and to Timora/Alerien tongues).

**Government.** Life in **clans** of 50–500, very tightly bound; the eldest able man is chieftain (he may appoint another if he deems himself unworthy). Clans each hold a small territory; chieftains act like petty lords; there is no fixed rank among clans. Ransard is ruled by a king, the **vortiger** — war-leader, supreme judge, trade administrator and treasurer — who sits five years at a time, then must be re-approved by the chieftains (and names his own successor, usually his eldest son). The vortiger has absolute power; disobeying chieftains do so at their peril. The capital **Kapalht** and its surroundings always belong to the sitting vortiger.

**Laws.** Unusually enlightened and humane for Ereb (high self-esteem makes harsh penalties unnecessary) — "treat others as you wish to be treated." No torture of prisoners (a beaten foe is met with respect); slaves get tolerant if impersonal treatment. Clan **feuds** are common over disputed land or slain kin (a slain clansman must be avenged or the clan is dishonored), often settled by **holmgång** (duel) between the clans' best champions. Personal-honor crimes (rape, adultery, slander) are punished hard — usually death (if the offended kin decide) or exile (if the vortiger does).

**Trade.** Not really traders, though they pretend to be — in truth they **take what they need by force** (their coastal raids on Magilre, Trakorien, Klomellien, even Kardien are feared). Fast seaworthy ships range far for gold, silver, spices, fabrics, jewels. Griffins rarely come on raids (hard to carry by sea, big eaters), so raids stick to the coasts.

**Military.** Disciplined and methodical despite their love of battle. Favorite formation: the **haphener** — a six-man-wide wedge (25 men) with two flankers per side and archers behind the gap (the flankers, often with two-handers, keep the enemy from the wedge's sides; archers shoot through the gap, first at enemy horses). On contact the wedge spreads into a line; at ~15 men wide it opens to launch a new wedge.

**The griffins.** Ransard's mightiest allies, living in the Kardbergen. Their hard temperament lets ransarder tame griffins as mounts; nearly every self-respecting clan owns at least one. With lance-armed riders they are Ransard's greatest offensive weapon. A griffin bonds intimately with its tamer (unlikely to bear anyone else). In battle they wear a breastplate (some protection vs arrows) but rely mostly on quick evasions; riders have very high control skill (**FV up to ~20** is not unusual) and the griffins themselves are skilled flyers.

**Religion.** Worship **Æsir**, a god-family with very many members (see Jorduashur). They believe one is reborn in the gods' realm if slain in battle — hence their fearlessness.

**History highlights (long; key beats):**
- **900s fO:** barbarians arrive from the west; some go on to Jorduashur. **400s fO:** svartfolk (mainly orcs) reach the Kardbergen.
- **27 fO:** Ransard united under **Omric den Store** (the Great).
- **2 fO:** **Hanric Silvertunga** ("Silver-tongue"), Omric's son, weds Princess **Timrin of Belgoth**, with a treaty that Belgoth's crown falls to Hanric if its king dies heirless.
- **1 fO–10 eO:** the Great War of Freedom — Belgoth's chieftains revolt and are crushed (origin of many later feuds). **12 eO:** Belgoth absorbed into Ransard. **18–24 eO:** western Belgoth clans revolt; **Hanric** makes peace, forcing them to emigrate south of the Ljusna peninsula (founding a nominally-subject realm).
- **50 eO:** ransarder found a colony on the Trakorian isle **Saphyna**. **129:** the Brothers' War in Ransard.
- **176:** kingdom **Timolerien** founded east of Ransard by southern refugees.
- **181–191 (First Ransarder War):** the **klavyker** (Trakorians' forerunners) attack Saphyna; after years of positional war the Ransardian fleet is destroyed (187, Rubakins vik) and the colonists are crushed (191); the ruling clan **Omric** falls, clan **Amron** takes over.
- **204:** the theocracy **Palinor** is founded east of the Kardbergen by the fanatical **Gheril-sekten** (a front for "the Lord of Evil," **Gheeril**, behind a "good god" **Hjallbrand**). **210, 310, 410, 510:** Palinor launches **four crusades** against the "heathens," all beaten back (often with Ransardian help; the **Korsets brödraskap** founded in Alerien, 411, as defense).
- **277–287:** Ransard allies with Milacke and Magilre to crush the klavykiska riket; victory, then a falling-out over **Saphyna** drives both Magilre and Ransard back to the mainland.
- **370–385 (Second Ransarder War):** **Edorl den Unge** tries to retake Saphyna but splits his army; the Paratorna landing is beaten, Edorl falls, his brother **Margon Rådvis** organizes the retreat. **378:** Margon (clan Amron) deposed; **Teldoric** (clan Omric) becomes vortiger.
- **387–392:** Timolerien's king dies without naming an heir; the realm splits into **Timoria** and **Alerien** (Teldoric brokering a tense peace).
- **488:** **Argald Omric** ("to Gorm den Gamle") born, foretold to be Ransard's greatest vortiger.
- **497–507 (Third Ransarder War):** Trakorian troops land on Saphyna; vortiger **Gorm den Gamle** and the 11-year-old Argald sail to reinforce; Trakorian numbers prevail; Gorm gathers the survivors and sails home (507), pursued — half the fleeing ships sunk or burned.
- **508:** **the Millennium of the Griffins** begins as **Argald Omric** tames a king-griffin; titled **Griptämjare** (Griffin-tamer), he weds Princess **Eowen of Narim (Gauland)** and is crowned vortiger; many Kardbergen griffins are tamed.
- **510:** Palinor's fourth crusade is easily repelled with the new griffins; **Argald Griptämjare** personally slays Palinor's war-leader, the mage **Krarr**.
- **513:** **Joran Gyllene** (Argald's son) born; the **Vargavintern** (Wolf-winter) — ice-barbarians from Orghin invade from east and west; Ransard sends aid to poor **Narim**. **515:** Narim joins Ransard; **Nerol** (Joran's son) born.
- **532:** Argald Griptämjaren murdered by Trakorian spies; **Joran Gyllene** crowned. **540:** Timora invaded. **549:** Joran killed in an ambush before the Battle of Heron; **Nerol Omric** crowned, ends the Timorian war.
- **559:** **Caorm Omric** (Nerol's fourth son) born. **565–572:** the Red Moon orcs raid Narim repeatedly. **573:** Nerol's sons die one by one; Nerol grows paranoid and unpopular; Caorm travels Ereb learning foreign war-craft and becomes **Caorm Alvvän** (Elf-friend), blood-brother to a grey-elf companion.
- **582:** the chieftains depose Nerol; **Caorm Alvvän** returns and, with the griffins, crushes the rebels after a short bloody civil war. **583:** tentative contact with the Kardskogen elves. **584:** **Argald den Yngre** (Caorm's son) born.
- **599:** the theocracy **Alerien** attacks Narim; the Red Moon orcs (under a spell) ravage the Kardskogen elves; Ransard can't help (the Alerien army stands between them), and the elves break contact.
- **600–605:** Ransard counterattacks and conquers Alerien under **Caorm Alvvän** — who is murdered (605) by his Timorian mistress (again Trakorian fingerprints). **Argald den Yngre** swears at his coronation to avenge his father.
- **606–609:** Argald plots and mobilizes for a final confrontation with **Trakorien**; many young griffins are tamed. **610 eO: NOW!!!**

**Notable names:** vortiger **Argald den Yngre ("Argald II")** and his murdered father **Caorm Alvvän** (Elf-friend); founders/vortigers **Omric den Store, Hanric Silvertunga, Teldoric, Edorl den Unge, Margon Rådvis, Argald Griptämjare (Griptämjaren), Joran Gyllene, Nerol**; Princess **Timrin of Belgoth**, Princess **Eowen of Narim**; the mage **Krarr** of Palinor; capital **Kapalht**; the **griffins** of the **Kardbergen**; the **Röda månens orcher**; subject realms **Timora, Alerien, Gauland, Narim**; the rival theocracy **Palinor** (god **Gheeril**, the **Gheril-sekten**); weapons **seax, tarque, glamorg**, the **merrlokh**; the **haphener** formation.

## 11. TRAKORIEN

A loosely-held maritime empire of four islands, governed by an elected emperor and a great noble council (the **Bodåken**) divided into "limbs," reliant on mercenary armies, and home to many religions — including the dreaded contagious cult of **Remuntra**. (Detailed in the module *Trakorien*.)

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~160,000
- **Demographics:** 80% humans, 10% svartfolk, 10% *ankor* and halflings
- **Capital:** Tricilve (~9,000)
- **Head of state:** **Radassabar den Store** (the Great)
- **Government:** empire with a strong noble council, the **bodåken**
- **Exports:** metals, fabrics, farm produce, salt
- **Imports:** sulphur, timber, precious metals, spices, luxuries
- **Wealth:** rich
- **Army:** small but strong imperial army, strong fleet; mercenary forces are ~75% of the total army
- **Religion:** several

**Geography.** Four big islands, each distinct:
- **Palamux** (largest, most populous): the **Heliga Kishatet** (the Holy Kishate, god **Shamash**'s strongest seat); the **Fokalerslätten** (insanely hot from a mirroring wall around it; long salt-water irrigation has ruined much of the soil). The fierce **kargomiter** in the NW are feared by all; the rest of the island is shielded from them by the **Eniaken**, a wall of unknown fungus. In the south, the elf-realm **Gwondel Leigå** by the **Thimassergapet**; nearby the giant-folk **tolgulderna** guard the Fokalerslätten from the horrors of the far west.
- **Saphyna** ("Trakorien's granary"): the very fertile **Frimbolinerslätten**; the good-natured, simple **saphyner** farmers. Split N/S by the **Matagdebergen** (snowy, with evil svartfolk). The eastern cape **Tocme** holds the impassable **Tocmeskogen**. North of the mountains is pleasant **Korjulme** (fruit orchards). The whole western part is **Krindenland**, a feared zone of strange events — possibly Ereb Altor's seam against a parallel universe (causing violent overnight changes in geography); too unknown to describe.
- **Trinsmyra** (smallest, harshest): rocky, never fully subjugated; rich in tin/lead/copper/silver (shipped from **Vumbra**). Home to the **vargmän** of Vulferveden, the dancing **milacker**, the emotional **karker**, and the warlike rebellious **rungler** who ride **skolopoder** (centipede-like insectoids over the steep mountains); deeper caves hold goblins and other vermin.
- **Paratorna** (the main island): from its capital **Tricilve** the empire is ruled. The northern province **Ilibaurien** is enslaved to the **opianderkrydda** (a narcotic spice). **Kryfferskogen** (east) is choked by the thorny **kryffermåra** vine; only the dog-folk **skecker** (spotted skin) live there. **Skuggornas träsk** (the Swamp of Shadows, center) holds creatures that drive the toughest mad with psychic powers. The south is pleasant — golden grain and lush pasture.

**The trakorier.** Several peoples: **palamoxierna** (Palamux) and **paratornierna** (Paratorna, reputed proud to the grave) are the two largest; the **saphyner** (Saphyna) are good-natured and simple, constantly raided for their wealth. They generally speak **trakoriska**, a west-*jorisk* dialect.

**Government — three phases.**
1. **Feudal Lasemos** — proud noble houses, no king, each area a principality.
2. **The Klavykiska riket's empire** — founded (capital **Fontra Cilor**) by refugees from Lasemos's wars, choosing a life-emperor aided by a noble council, the **bodåken**.
3. **The Trakoriska riket's oligarchy** — after the **Kastykiska omvändelsen** (Kastyke Conversion), old morals were swept away (the thinker **Pisarus**: "Strike at everything! Crush the brittle, that only the durable may endure"; the profanet **Makulon Nihivelli** read this as "nothing is holy, only more or less expedient"). The form (emperor + great council **Bodåken**) is borrowed from old Palamux but is less rigid. The **emperor** is elected for life (the title is not hereditary — and his life may be short if discontent rises). From the council, smaller working groups called **limbes** (limbs; sing. *limba*) are detached, representing the many-armed **Kastyke**'s hands and feet:
   - **Mana Xifa** (Sword-hand) — defense
   - **Mana Friga** (Cold-hand) — law and justice
   - **Poda Arux** (Gold-foot) — finances
   - **Mana Extenda** (the Realm's Long Arm) — foreign policy
   - **Mana Phena** (Sea-hand) — shipping & fleet
   - **Poda Snica** (Fore-foot) — trade
   
   Sub-divisions are called **digeter** and **falaner** (fingers and toes). The emperor sits on every limba (unique insight) and has a **fivefold vote** in the Bodåken. There's also a folk-assembly of rich burghers with limited veto power over Bodåken decisions.

**Trade.** Lively island traffic (Magilre, Bzegusta, the city O); beyond, convoys guard against pirates/monsters/storms. The **sulphur convoy** sails to Marjura each spring/fall; ~three convoys a year go to Melukha, one (the **Kopparkonvojen**) continuing into the Copper Sea (Caddo, Erebos, etc.). Roughly every other year a dangerous, very profitable **"demontrade"** runs east through barbarian waters to Demontungan for eastern spices and Kamsun paper. Exports: metals, fabrics, farm produce, salt. Imports: sulphur, timber, precious metals, spices, luxuries.

**Military.** Composed of mercenary armies, hirds, *roteknektar*, folk-militia and private armies:
- **Legoarméer (mercenary armies):** hired out by a **kondottiär** (condottiere) who contracts to the best offer and pays his own men (who own their gear). Pros: the people stay productive; a disarmed populace rebels less. Cons: low loyalty (may switch sides if unpaid or over-endangered — though a disloyal kondottiär soon gets no contracts). Trakorien hires **many small armies** rather than a few big ones (so no kondottiär grows too powerful) and seeds them with spies from the feared secret police **Digeta Longa** (the law's long finger). Masterless mercenary armies can turn dangerous (raiding villages, ransoming towns; some kondottiärer have set themselves up as petty princes).
- **Hirder:** the realm's own professional troops under the Bodåken/emperor (used where mercenaries aren't trusted, e.g. capital defense); led by a **hirdman** (or **storhirdman**); only nobles from Paratorna/Palamux may be hirdmen.
- **Roteknektar:** local men maintained by a *rote* (group of households); keep village order, town-watch, tax-collection under the local *fogde*.
- **Folkmilis:** common folk quickly trained in emergencies (often used by kondottiärer to soak up the enemy's worst missiles/spells — a tactic called "black opening," considered dishonorable).
- **Privatarméer:** nobles with their own estates may keep a small private army (tax breaks if lent to the realm); often well-equipped but poorly trained and unpopular.
- **Flotta:** the war-fleet is mostly state-owned under **Mana Phena**, with kondottiär-type units common as privateers and pirate-hunters.

**Religion.** No single religion has ever dominated, so most are tolerated; the great theological debate is whether the world exists or is mere illusion. The main groups:
1. **Arketypiska gudar (archetypal gods)** — standard gods under various names, inherited from Jorpagna: **Enki** (Lord of Freshwater, wisdom, tradition), **Anxalis** (Queen of the Sea-depths, fickle — served by the **Ambaqua** order of wandering monks), and others: **Tiamat** (primal chaos), **Marduk** (storms/weather), **Ezgela** (clouds), **Luvena** (moon — unfairly tied to disease and creeping death; secret moon-cults of necromancy, human sacrifice and cannibalism, esp. on Palamux), **Ereshkigal** (death-realm), **Valliman & Drigel** (guardians of the sun), **Tigwalwan** (light, protector of the sick).
2. **Shamash** — god of truth; once a minor Jorpagna god, now central to a religious revival on Palamux after the salting of Moskorien/Fokale and the wars; a stretch of south Moskorien became the religious state **the Heliga Kishatet**. Priests teach the world is full of false images; the righteous keep to sincere faith, shun hypocrisy/vanity/seduction/lies; worship is ascetic (study, meditation, tithes of 10–30%). Symbols: circle, clear crystal, water-drop; brown-and-white robes. In Kishatet, travelers meet endless rules (no public laughter; mirrors, images and decorations are banned — confiscated or tarred over at the border).
3. **Inashtar** — originally from the NE ice-barbarians, long the special goddess of the **rungler** and all **Trinsmyra**; goddess of fire, war and unbridled lusts; depicted as a devastatingly beautiful armored woman amid wild flames (symbols: a hawk-headed dove, a battle-horn with harp-strings). Worshipped ecstatically (huge bonfires, wild music/dance, orgies, fighting-games); no organized church (all women are held to share **Inashavi**, part of the goddess — hence women's strong position on Trinsmyra). Splinter sects include the dreaded **RhabdoRana** ("the brotherhood of the striped frog"), assassins worshipping the demon **Ghumgakk** ("the Avenger with the Black Lips") — a professional murderer-guild terrorizing northern Ereb since ~150 eO; and the gentler cult of "the **little leather-fairy**," queen of the **astrilder** (tiny mostly-invisible love-fairies).
4. **Kastyke** — Paratorna's official patron, found among the native **skecker** as a huntress-goddess; her tenet: life is a struggle, never give up — **"Kastyke loves the one who loves himself!"** From the start the merchants made her cult their own (priests are really business-agents who rattle spirit-rattles to keep up appearances; many bought their priestly rights with gold).
5. **Remuntra** — the strangest and most-feared of all. Arose in **Krindenmaar** (Saphyna), where a tinker named **Klastobrest Dimvett** was seized by an irresistible calling to preach; he reached Lasemos (349 eO), gathered an inexplicably large following (including nobles and learned men), and sailed west "as a tribute to the god Remuntra" — half of Lasemos's fleet followed and none ever returned. Remuntra's only intelligible command: learn to distrust the world and all in it, so you can leave it laughing at life's end (an idea that questions itself, tangling the logic — "logic is a prison for the inner self"). A worshipper shaves the head to five tufts (one over each ear, forehead, nape, and one upright on the crown), wrapping the body in cloth hung with junk (rope-ends, fish-heads, a mug of mud, a broken wheel, a reed). All Trakorien dreads the cult (it spreads like a disease; never name the god). If Remuntra-worshippers appear, you give them what they want and get rid of them.
   - **GM info:** Remuntra-worship is not a real religion but a **contagious madness — a thought-parasite** that entered the world through the world-leaks in Krindenland; it spreads not by contact but by **thinking about it** (like a song stuck in your head). Whoever kills a Remuntra-worshipper is easily infected (by worrying about exactly this). The GM may call for a PSY roll in critical moments. It also appears in **Mercana** (Klomellien) and remote parts of Magilre.

**History highlights.** Begins with the Third Conflux (599–598 fO) and Jorpagna's fall. Jorpagna had just colonized **Lasemos** and **Laabne** on west Palamux. In the dark age (550–250 fO) the colonists warred constantly; mages seized **Laabne** (~380 fO, founding Trakorien's first academy in **Voag Aspede**, which drained Lasemos like a leech) until **Baskimer Brushane** overthrew the "grey eminences" (banning all magic in Lasemos). Around 290 fO, friendship with the **Gwondel Leigå** elves let colonists reach the **Moskoriska** plain; they named themselves **klavyker** ("from *klavys*, key" — they'd unlocked a promised land). The **Klavykiska krigen** (130–68 fO) over Moskorien forged a lasting alliance with the giant **tolgulderna**; the **Klavykiska riket** was born (68 fO). "Kristallens sekler" (50 fO–150 eO) saw failed efforts to cleanse **Kargom** of moon-worshippers, necromancy and svartfolk. Colonies spread to **Stegos, Paratorna**, **Bzegusta**, **Torilia**, and the city **O** (the holy **Kornella** lost O fighting Ransard; a revelation from **Anxalis** told the klavyker to stay on the islands — origin of Trakorian reluctance to settle the mainland). The **salting of Palamux** (irrigation leaving salt and silt) ruined Fokale/Moskorien — the palamoxer's great sorrow. The **First Ransarder War (180–200 eO)** ended in klavyker victory (Rubakins vik, 199). The "**galna milackerna**" of Trinsmyra (220–300) nearly overran Palamux (their fast **katremasser** burned the heavy fleet with **vildeld**) before being beaten back. The **Kastykiska omvändelsen (304 eO)** freed **Paratorna** from the weakened Klavykiska riket; the new nation **Trakorien** (from *traekor*, "to breathe freely") instituted **Kastyke** as patron. The **Heliga Kishatet** was founded (311). The **Second Ransarder War (370–385)** was repelled with the new Kishatet's help. Trakorien declared itself heir to the Klavykiska riket (the **Trakoriska riket**); cleared Paratorna of svartfolk by ~460; **conquered Ilibaurien (460)** without battle via the alchemist **Astrumal diFoldt**'s **opianderkrydda**; absorbed the old klavyker colonies. **Third Ransarder War (497–507)**: drove Ransard off Saphyna. The **Saphyna revolt (520–525)** used drug-zombified troops (**zombin**) that obeyed any order — including the enemy's; Trakorien won via the loud-voiced herald **Skrynk** ("Ylarnas afton"), then annexed all Saphyna. The **invasion of Kark (530)** broke south Trinsmyra (the ruling **Ålskallarna** fled into the unconquerable **Rung** mountains); Milacke kept its independence. The **occupation of Arhem on Marjura (595)** secured sulphur. **610 eO:** the **sulphur-winter** ends and the kingdom **Cruri** can emerge from the ice again — "how this will end remains to be seen."

**Notable names:** Emperor **Radassabar den Store**; thinkers **Pisarus** and **Makulon Nihivelli**; the mages of **Voag Aspede** and their conqueror **Baskimer Brushane**; the holy **Kornella**; the alchemist **Astrumal diFoldt** (opiander); the herald **Skrynk**; capital **Tricilve** (and its **kronolab**); the **Bodåken** and its **limbes**; the secret police **Digeta Longa**; cult-founder **Klastobrest Dimvett** (Remuntra); the assassin-sect **RhabdoRana** (demon **Ghumgakk**); gods **Shamash, Inashtar, Kastyke, Remuntra, Enki, Anxalis** (and the archetypals); peoples **palamoxier, paratornier, saphyner, kargomiter, tolgulder, milacker, karker, rungler, skecker, vargmän**; places **Palamux, Saphyna, Trinsmyra, Paratorna, Kishatet, Fokalerslätten, Krindenland, Gwondel Leigå, Tocmeskogen, Skuggornas träsk, Ilibaurien, Vumbra**.

## 12. ZORAKIN

A fairly prosperous feudal kingdom on the southern Aidne peninsula, descended from old Jorpagna, devoted to the **Shining Way**, with mighty knightly cavalry. The common folk have little say. (Detailed in the module *Ivanhoe*.)

**Country stats**
- **Population:** ~250,000
- **Demographics:** humans 90%, *ankor* 5%, elf-folk 4%, halflings 1%
- **Capital:** Pendon (~7,500)
- **Head of state:** **Valien VII Rättråde** ("the Just-counsel")
- **Government:** feudal monarchy
- **Exports:** grain, iron, timber, fur
- **Imports:** fabrics, salt, precious metals
- **Wealth:** normal
- **Army:** very strong knightly cavalry, weak fleet, strong church troops
- **Religion:** the Shining Way
- **Notes:** described in detail in the module *Ivanhoe*

**Geography.** Great regional contrasts: barren high-fell **Aidne-bergen** in the north, fertile plains rolling to the Copper Sea in the south; Kardien to the west; the **Dakkilobukten** inlets in the east. Two large peninsulas — **Grindanu** (west) and **Indar** (east) — joined by a narrow coastal strip; both are fertile, flat, fairly populous, and form "real" Zorakin. The north is too forested/mountainous for farming (people live by mining, hunting, forestry).
- **Grindanu:** ~half **Torilskogen** (once a bright leafy wood, now dark and evil since svartfolk came in the early 100s eO), ~half farmland; the cold fast **Jostefloden** (its only big river) ends in the great **Grimmaniträsket** marsh. ~Half of Zorakin lives here, more urban than Indar.
- **Indar:** fertile, flat, ¾ cultivated; the slow **Solaån** and **Granströmmen** (plus ditches) give the Copper Sea's biggest harvests; the over-exploited west turns to cattle-raising; windmills dot the windy plain. North of Indar, along the Aidne foot, lies the great **Nordbergaskogen** (mainly forest-elves, plus humans, satyrs, shy swan-maidens; the eastern part in the duchy **Salamora** is wholly elf-held, trading furs/herbs for being left alone).

**Peoples.** Two main, both of *jorisk* descent, ~¾ of the population (the "typical zorakier"):
- **Pennerna:** a *jorisk* folk of southern Grindanu, heirs of the pre-conflux inhabitants and of old Jorpagna culture. Speak a dialect close to original *joriska*. Since **Valien Rikesbyggare** ("Realm-builder") was himself a penner, they are over-represented among the nobility (most of Zorakin's upper class are penner). Traditionally warriors and merchants, more cultivated than most Aidne folk, with a unique sense for tactics/organization; deeply devoted to the Shining Way; *jorisk* artifacts and architecture have very high status.
- **Legerdierna:** the folk of Indar — pre-conflux farmers hit hard by the cataclysm. They first formed a kind of collective farming, then each farm became a tiny isolated realm (very vulnerable in the **lindskiarner** wars). After those wars (which methodically devastated Indar) each farmer rebuilt his own land, binding him ever more tightly to it — the basis of the **serfdom** that arose. Isolated and easy for the nobility (mostly of penner stock) to control totally; their farming is underdeveloped, saved only by extraordinarily fertile soil. A hard-cowed, very religious people (their priests are wholly under noble control), with a distinctive dialect (almost no short vowels).

**Government.** Headed by a king (hereditary since 156 eO — originally an elective kingdom). In practice ruled by the nobles who once received the land from the king. The population falls into three groups: ~¾ under a **baron → count → duke → king** chain; ~¼ "crown-folk" under a **markis** answering directly to the king; ~1/20 in **free cities** (a mayor + city council under the king). The **Rikets Råd** (Realm's Council) legislates/advises for the whole realm; the **ständerna** (estates) for all crown-land. The Shining Way church owns ~1/10 of the land (mostly gifted by dying barons for masses).

**The nobility.** At the **Stora Tinget** (Great Assembly) of 55 fO, King Valien rewarded the heavy cavalry who fought the lindskiarner: **riddare** (knights) became **barons** (a few km² each); better aides became **counts** (5–20 baronies); his ten closest men became **dukes** (3–5 counties). In their own fiefs nobles could make laws and tax — but a lord who starved his people through over-taxation would be stripped of his fief and declared "the basest and most outlawed of creatures." Fiefs were originally non-hereditary (the superior chose a successor), but by tradition the eldest son usually took over, and after 100 eO it became near-automatic. Each new lord swears to "aid with counsel and deed" (i.e., furnish a set number of armored warriors on demand) — the king could thus call on nearly 1,000 knights plus levies and mercenaries. As fiefs grew hereditary the king lost direct control; nobles took liberties, fought private wars, and increasingly bought out of muster with money rather than send troops (boosting mercenary use and shrinking knightly importance — by the 600s the king can count on only about half the realm's knights at muster). A baron has near-absolute power in his own fief (sets laws, levies taxes ~5–15 gold/year per farmer plus tolls on his bridges/mills/wine-presses/roads).

**Crown-lands & markiser.** King Valien set aside especially profitable baronies as **crown-land** (~1/5 of the realm's area), whose income funds the king. A baron could, for a large sum, buy free of his count and make his barony crown-land, becoming a **markis** (largely his own master, but swearing fealty to the king yearly).

**The free cities.** Of Zorakin's ~100 larger cities, ~20 have bought their freedom and become a kind of crown-land under special laws; largely self-governing under royal jurisdiction. Ruled by a **stadsråd** (an odd number, ~11–21, sitting for life, co-opting successors) headed by a **borgmästare** (a royal official); bishops (**bontisåler**) in diocesan capitals get a guaranteed council seat. Cities tax in money (not produce); ~20–30 gold/year on craftsmen/merchants, of which ~10 goes to the king. Some cities were bought free by a specific group (e.g. the church or a guild) and are dominated by it.

**Rikets Råd & the estates.** The **Rikets Råd** (14 members + the king) executes the king's decisions; each member heads an area (fleet, army, tolls, taxes, minting, etc.); the mightiest are the **drots** (head of the estates), the **kansler** (chancellor/finances), and the **marsk** (army). It may propose laws and advise; under weak kings it held great power, under strong kings none (the king need not heed it now). The **ständerna** coordinate all royal lands; all mayors and markiser may sit; meant to meet yearly to vote on proposals — but royal proposals no longer need their approval, so they are rarely called (only twice in the entire 300s). Originally all dukes/counts/barons sat in the estates and a majority of both Råd and estates was needed for royal measures — but the cunning **King Karolu XI** abolished that law and made Zorakin hereditary in 156 eO.

**Trade.** Not a trading state, but trades richly with the other Copper Sea lands. Exports: grain, iron, timber, fur. Imports: fabrics, salt, precious metals.

**Foreign policy.** Long a fairly strong realm (deterring attack). Fought in **Berendien's war against Felicien (370–394)** until its fleet was crushed and it sued for peace. Relations with **Caddo** strained ever since the **Great Schism** (trade blockades, crusades in Morälvidyn). Good, profitable relations with **Erebos** and, for six centuries, with **Kardien** (its sworn ally). **Berendien** is an old ally (good relations despite distance).

**Military.** Noble retinues (usually fortress garrisons), so offensive war needs conscripted peasants and mercenaries; city levies are untrained. Knights are very well-trained — heavy armor and strong war-stallions make them a formidable shock force. The war-fleet is small and old; the church's warships would be the main naval force.

**Religion.** The **Shining Way** is the only religion.

**History.** Marked by two great conflicts: the **lindskiarner wars** (50s fO) and the **Great Schism** (350s eO). From the 180s fO the **lindskiarner** (an aggressive man-like people) migrated in from the north, founding the city-state **Pharynx** in central Zorakin and conquering nearly all Grindanu, then (with a squadron of ogres) most of the north/west/center (peace 66 fO ceding it to them). **Valien Valiensson**, new leader of the southern Grindanu penner, sought aid from Caddo merchants and repeatedly from Kardien; with their eventual help the lindskiarner were barely beaten back. A pact was sworn between Valien and **Vidar Odise** (the Kardier leader) to aid each other forever; midsummer **55 fO** the kingdom of Zorakin was proclaimed, **Valien Valiensson** becoming **King Valien I**, and at the **Stora Tinget** the foremost warriors became dukes/counts/barons. **The Great Schism:** Odo's teachings won great response in Zorakin from year 0; by 278 a rift opened between the Shining Way's Caddo branch (magic is purely destructive, ban it) and the mainland branch (magic is neutral, its purpose set by the mage) — souring Zorakin–Caddo relations ever since.

**Notable names:** King **Valien VII Rättråde**; founder-king **Valien I (Valien Valiensson / Rikesbyggare)**; the Kardier ally **Vidar Odise**; the cunning **King Karolu XI** (made the realm hereditary, 156 eO); capital **Pendon**; the city-state **Pharynx** and the **lindskiarner**; the duchy **Salamora**; peoples **pennerna** and **legerdierna**; the offices **drots, kansler, marsk, markis, bontisål**; places **Grindanu, Indar, Torilskogen, Nordbergaskogen, Jostefloden, Grimmaniträsket**; the **Great Schism**.

## OTHER LANDS (Övriga länder)

Lands mentioned in passing or covered in other modules; given briefly here.

- **Berendien** — on the northern Tolan peninsula (north of Felicien, south of the great elf-forests **Landori**). A feudal monarchy like Zorakin/Kardien (king → grand dukes → counts…). A pronounced farming land (serfs growing wheat, barley, oats, rye; livestock only among richer great-farmers). Has fought several inconclusive wars with **Felicien**. Speaks **berendiska** (a *jorisk* tongue). Chief religion: worship of the **Unga Gudarna** (the Young Gods).
- **Jorpagna (the present)** — the central parts of the old empire have never recovered from the conflux (nearly 1,000 years on). Now fragmented into hundreds of tiny independent principalities whose intrigues block any central rule. Dominated by serf-farming; limited coastal fishing in the shadow of Erebos's huge fleet; a few miners share the mountains with dwarves.
- **Krun** — south of the Grynnerbergen in the far SE. An underdeveloped empire living on fishing in the freshwater sea **Golwynda**, slave-trading, and tolls on the Soluna–Akrogal–Ereb trade. Low farming, insufficient for the people. Devout worshippers of **Mullah** (regarded by many westerners as a corruption of **Etin**); church structure resembles the Shining Way's.
- **Magilre** — north of Klomellien. A feudal monarchy with a centuries-old ruling line; the nobility's power is very strong (the poor **magiller** are wholly dominated by the proud, diplomatic, charismatic nobles). Lives on trade with Trakorien/Kardien, fishing and farming; suffers a crippling lack of metals (forcing peace pacts with Trakorien and Klomellien). The Shining Way fully dominates. (Saying: to "talk someone out of their wits like a magiller"; a skilled demagogue is "a real magiller.")
- **Monturerna** — a small island group far west of Kardien's coast, settled in the 370s eO by Kardian dissenters. (To be detailed in a future product.)
- **Torshem** — wedged between the southern Nidabergen slopes and Landori's forests; mostly forest- and mountain-barbarians. Its one town, **Torsborg**, is an important hub for trade between the mountain dwarf-clans and Berendian merchants. (Detailed in the module *Torshem*.)
