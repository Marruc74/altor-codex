**A detailed summary of the *Drakar och Demoner Expert* adventure module**

## What the module is

Torshem is a **wilderness sandbox/campaign region** centred on the trading town of **Torsborg**. It gives the Game Master (SL = *spelledare*) maps, lore, and a set of ready-to-run adventures so they can build an ongoing campaign in the forests and mountains of central Ereb. It contains **one large adventure** (Kashim's Temple) plus **several smaller adventure hooks**, along with new rules content, gods, peoples, gazetteer, NPCs, and a monster appendix.

### GM presentation conventions
- Room/location descriptions are split into **italic text** (read aloud to players) and **non-italic text** (GM-only: hidden details, how to handle situations).

## Wilderness survival rules (Överlevnad i vildmarken)

Practical guidance the SL applies during travel:

- **Camp (Läger):** pitch tents in a circle with openings facing inward; camouflage them.
- **Campfire (Lägereld):** gives light/warmth, cooks food, keeps wild animals away — but attracts other creatures.
- **Watch (Vakt):** keep guards (esp. at night) in shifts of **2–4 hours**; early shifts are most popular (uninterrupted sleep). Expect 2–3 false alarms per night in forest.
- **Alarms (Larm):**
  - *Magic:* SIGILL combined with SPÖKRÖST, BLIXT, etc.; or pre-made alarm amulets hidden around camp.
  - *Traps:* tripwires that knock over a stone-filled jar, snares, pitfalls (time-consuming — only worth it for long stays).
- **Travel times (Tider):** Realistically ~**10 hours travel / 14 hours rest** per day. ≥8 h sleep, ~2 h watch, ≥1 h for meals; 5 min rest per hour. Most people sustain this for 2–3 days (maybe a week), not weeks on end — but adventurers aren't ordinary people.
- **Darkness:** travel impossible in forest at night (snags, hidden holes); torches risk forest fire. Possible on cleared roads/wide trails in summer. Summer = at least half the day is light; winter = only a few hours.
- **Hygiene:** for long camps, dig two pits (latrine + refuse) and fill them in when leaving.
- **Combat readiness (Stridsberedskap):** can't rest properly in armour. A mail hauberk (with padding) takes **≥20 min** to don with a helper; full plate **≥40 min**; a shield ~1 min. Marching all day in armour is impossible (max ~4 h/day in armour, or 1–2 h if also carrying a pack). Closed visorless helmets nearly suffocate; risk of heatstroke in sun. Good readiness = sleep/work with a dagger at your side and other gear within reach.

## New drugs & plants (Nya droger och växter)

### The Mennon flower (Mennonblomma)
- **Type:** Herb (viol/violet bloom); rare; temperate zone; deciduous forests; blooms in summer.
- **Useful part:** the **root** (one root = one dose of powder). Comes in yellow, black, or red.

|--------|--------|------|--------|------|-------|
| **Gult (Yellow) Mennonpulver** | Heals **1T6 KP** per dose | Yellow powder | Inhaled or in alcohol-free liquid | 1T10 min | Skills: Drug lore, Healing-herb lore |
| **Svart (Black) Mennonpulver** | Raises **PSY by 1T6** during effect | Black powder | Inhaled or in liquid | 1T10 min | Effect 60 min; aftereffects 30 min — dizziness, headache; movement −2, SMI & SMI-skills halved |
| **Rött (Red) Mennonpulver** *(POISON)* | Causes **total blindness** during effect | Red powder | Inhaled or in liquid | — | Effect 60 min (halved if poison roll succeeds); strength = user's FV-group +10 |

**Drug prices / availability (chans = chance an herbalist has it; ~1 herbalist per 1000 inhabitants):**
- Yellow root 200 sm; yellow powder 300 sm/dose
- Black root 200 sm; black powder 350 sm/dose
- Red root 400 sm; red powder 550 sm/dose (red is **illegal** — classed as poison; only buyable on the black market via a successful *Undre Världen* / Underworld roll)

## The gods (Gudar)

The book covers gods through their **doctrines and worshippers** (no stat blocks — PCs should never confront a god directly). Worshippers follow **levnadsregler** (rules of life), which function like the Expert rules' *Livsmål* (life goals): breaking them brings guilt that only a penance/atonement quest can lift.

### Wegil — "Ljusets Herre" (Lord of Light)
- A **good** god, worshipped mainly by humans (especially **jorer**); patron god of Torshem; from old **Jorpagna**.
- Depicted as a middle-aged, mighty bearded man with battle-club, longspear, and a short mithril mail shirt.
- Wants to spread **civilization, law and order**; eternal foe of **Kashim**.
- **Three worship organizations:** the *Church* (kyrkan — most widespread; priests in light-blue robes & dark-blue mantles), *Monasteries* (munkkloster — remote; monks in dark-blue robes), and *Knightly orders* (ordenssällskap — 40–50 knights per group, light-blue mantles/surcoats).
- **Rules of life:** help the weak and needy; avoid excessive violence/killing (self-defence and hunting OK); uphold law and civilization.
- **God-gifts (Gigant):** Fearlessness; Light (glowing staff + ability to cast BLIXT); Steel-gaze (hero ability Stålblick).

### Kashim — "Den Härskande Flamman / Livsherren"
- Seen as an **evil** god (not from Jorpagna; origin unknown), worshipped by humans.
- Depicted as a mighty creature with glowing red eyes, clawed hands, charred scaly skin, burning sword, bronze round-shield.
- **Doctrine — "the right of the strong":** a lasting society requires purging the **weak** (incl. **elves** and the disabled). Worship Kashim to gain strength and become one of the Strong; then "peace" via terror-balance. Light is an enemy until Kashim rules.
- Worshippers are unpopular; gather in **small secret societies**, wearing black robes with wine-red lining only at ceremonies; meet in remote temples/underground crypts.
- **Worship includes animal & human sacrifice:** victims brought to an altar with a Kashim statue, killed with a quick dagger thrust, blood collected in a bronze bowl and poured over statue/altar, body burned in fire-pits. Statues often adorned with stolen jewels.
- **Rules of life:** sacrifice at a temple ≥4×/year (one offering must be a non-believer); expose spies/traitors to the priesthood; avoid contact with Light and the Weak (or undergo strengthening rituals).
- **God-gifts (Gigant):** Fire protection; Fearlessness; Ghost protection; Darkness (a ~10 m sphere of darkness only the recipient can see within; only Wegil's "Light" gift dispels it).

## The region of Torshem (geography)

Torshem = the region around **Torsborg**, in the great forest belt of central Ereb. Torsborg sits on the **east side of Storsjön (the Great Lake)**; a hub for trade between the southern Copper Sea (Kopparhavet) lands and the dwarves of the **Nidabergen** mountains. Inhabitants live by trade, fishing, and farming.

### The Mountains (Nidabergen)
- Vast northern range, peaks several thousand metres, snow-capped year-round.
- **Nidapasset (the pass)** in the west: caravans cross in summer to trade textiles/metals for furs in **Nargur's forests**; snowbound and dangerous in winter.
- Rich ore and gemstone deposits on the pass's west side; **dwarf clan Kvizur** mines deep here.
- *Flora:* grass, heather, moss, lichen, dwarf birch, edible berries (blueberry, sloe, cloudberry).
- *Fauna:* mountain goats, deer, mountain sheep, wolves, **cave lions**, **cave bears**, lemmings, ermine, hares, rats, birds of prey; salmon-rich rivers.

### The Rivers
- Three originate in Nidabergen: **Gipul** (slow — navigable by canoe/raft, empties into Silversjön in the Old Forest, then south), **Hrid** & **Hrönn** (fast, narrow, winding, dangerous; empty into Storsjön).
- **Leipter** flows south from Storsjön — broad, deep, slow — navigable all the way to the sea (enables sailing upstream thanks to southerly winds).

### The Forests
Three great forest regions, part of an east–west continental forest belt:
- **Järnskogen** (Iron Forest) and **Gamla skogen** (Old Forest), separated by the Leipter.
- **Trollskogen** (Troll Forest) — a dark, hilly, deviant part of the Old Forest, covered in gnarled black pines and thorns; nearly trackless. A refuge for **svartfolk** (trolls, orcs, dark-elves, jättar/giants) — ~200–300 individuals. Rumoured immense treasure; no adventurer is known to have returned with proof.
- *Common forest trees:* beech and maple (also oak, larch); cathedral-like canopy, sparse undergrowth, easy travel.
- *Fauna:* great red deer, roe deer, **aurochs**, **woolly giant rhinoceros**, brown bears, wolves, lynx, wolverines, wild dogs, small game, many birds.

### Climate
Tempered inland climate (a bit warmer/drier than Sweden; like France/W. Germany). Forest dampens wind by one level. Mainly southerly winds. (Detailed precipitation/wind/stability tables given for spring/autumn, summer, winter — for use with Gigant.)

### Maps
- **GM's map of Torshem:** the full wilderness with all locations.
- **Players' map:** fewer locations (only those the PCs would know).

## History of Torshem (Torshems historia)

Dated in the **Dalkic reckoning** used across Ereb: **fO** = before Odo (the prophet of "the Shining Way"), **eO** = after Odo.

**Bronze Age / Howather arrival**
- **c. 1500 fO** — **Howatherna** arrive from the north; live at Stone-Age level.

**Jorpagna's era**
- **912 fO** — *Meeting at Grafferburg:* Emperor Thamsun I of Jorpagna summons the Nargur barbarian chiefs; their spokesman insults him; war begins.
- **c. 900–700 fO** — *War Time:* constant fighting; Jorpagna's disciplined phalanxes can't hold in the forests vs. barbarian ambushes; key battles around Nidapasset & Torshem.
- **600s fO** — *Bytiden (Village Time):* wars end through exhaustion; the weakened howather trade with Jorpagna (furs for metals/crafts), adopt writing (modified jori runes) and farming; villages grow.
- **599 fO** — **The Konflux (3rd Konflux):** a fateful astrological alignment. A swarm of billions of arm-long flesh-eating "köttbitare" insects from the south (each carrying a blue stone) halves central Ereb's population in weeks and destroys 4/5 of livestock. Jorpagna and howather civilization collapses — the "Mörkertiden" (Dark Time) begins.

**Dark Time (599 fO – ~0)**
- Surviving howather return to the forests, reject settled life, revert to Stone-Age tech (no mining know-how) but keep writing; live by hunting/forestry/fishing.

**After Odo (eO)**
- **100s–200s eO** — Jori settlers (landless farmers & out-competed craftsmen from **Berendien** and **Erebos** on the Copper Sea) immigrate; immigration stalls early 300s.
- **212–213 eO** — *Torsborg's Wall:* enmity over hunting/land; jorer clear forest for farmland; war 212; howather win in the forest but the **dwarves force a peace** through trade leverage. Borders for Torsborg's farmland fixed; the town's 400 inhabitants build a wall.
- **247 eO** — first town hall (Stadshuset) built by town-master Hoson.
- **327 eO** — first stone building: the **Wegil stone temple**.
- **335 eO** — *Karsakker attack:* a Nargur tribe invades via Nidapasset; howather + Torsborg unexpectedly **ally** and withstand a siege; dwarves strike the besiegers from behind. A turning point: enmity gives way to cooperation (symbolized by the 339 marriage of Lugaswitha & Svodryk).
- **300s eO** — *Arrival of the elves:* under animist magician **Eloaner**; they build **Santono** (a small animist academy) in southern Torshem.
- **487–488 eO** — *The Reser War (Reserkriget):* a well-organized horde of orcs, dark-elves and **resar (giants)** under warlord **Kaursch** besieges Torsborg; the city partly burns. Howather guerrilla war + dwarf cooperation starves the army; mutiny; Kaursch slain by his own. Mercenaries hired by merchant **Dan Vedort** from Erebos arrive by boat and break the siege; fleeing svartfolk massacred.
- **500s eO** — *New Village Time:* some howather clans settle again (semi-permanent, slash-and-burn). House **Vedort** grows at house **Sagbon's** expense.
- **590s eO** — *Arrival of the wolfmen:* the **Nékhi** clan migrates in, swears eternal friendship with the howather (oath 598).
- **603 eO** — **Laberkus Sagbon** succeeds his father Pemirad as town-master — a weak personality; some want the able **Vidar Vedort** instead.
- **609 eO** — *The Herse Feud (Herse-fejden):* the alcoholic herse **Gonoser** is deposed; he tries to murder his successor **Tagaswether** but is cut down by Tagaswether's brother **Tarosether**. The Gono-clan and Taros-clan become bitter enemies — seen as a very bad omen by the shamans.

## Peoples (Folkslag)

### Howather
- The region's **indigenous people**; related to the Nargur barbarians north of the Nidabergen; arrived ≥2000 years ago.
- Hard, austere life-philosophy; prize **bravery, loyalty, endurance, generosity, eloquence**; deeply loyal/hospitable to friends, cruel and vengeful to enemies.
- Often long blond hair, fair skin, blue/steel-grey eyes; medium height but powerfully built; tattoo arms/body to show pain-tolerance; men proud of beards. **Women are men's equals** and can become herse.
- Keep goats, sheep, strong hunting dogs; cattle rare; **horses essentially absent** (howather rarely ride).
- **Organization:** the **clan** (storfamilj) is the base unit, led by a **wagas** (respected elder of either sex). The whole tribe is led by a **herse** (chief — usually male, also war-leader). An annual tribal gathering judges the herse; if hunting/harvest failed he can be deposed.
- **Religion:** no developed religion — they worship **spirits** and fallen heroes; spirit-inhabited places are sacred (harming them = trial at the thing). Feasts feature the **völva** (female shaman) singing hero-songs; she is keeper of history, healer, seer, and adviser. Feared and respected.
- **Two groups:**
  - **Bergsklaner (mountain clans):** ~1000 people; stone houses; hunting + slash-and-burn; warlike, raid (but only worthy, equal foes); leather harness, cone-helm, large round shield; favourite weapons short-sword, throwing spear, short bow, hand-axe.
  - **Skogsklaner (forest clans):** ~4000 people; long single-room wooden houses with 1–4 hearths, palisaded; hunting/slash-and-burn + foraging; also fight only equal foes; leather/studded-leather harness, medium round shield, vendel-helm; favourite weapons hand-axe, longspear, short bow.
  - (Both make excellent player-characters; skill lists given.)
- **Language:** Howathiska (a Nargur tongue, rich in jori loanwords). **Forn-howathiska** (Old Howathic) survives only among shamans for ceremonies; counts as a separate language.

### Jorer
- All peoples descended from the old empire of **Jorpagna**, sharing **jori** as mother tongue (includes non-humans like **halvlängdsmän** / halflings). Not a single culture. Torshem's jorer came in the 100s–200s eO mainly from Berendien and Erebos.

### Dwarves (Dvärgar)
- The **Kvizur clan** in the village **Kvizur Kan** — originally from the **Grynnerbergen**, speaking **gryndur**; ~200 people in tunnels dug into the pass. Mine iron, copper, lead; smith superb tools/weapons/jewellery sold dear. Their craft trade is the economic lifeblood of Torsborg.

### Wolfmen (Vargmän / the Nékhi clan)
- Intelligent wolf-people; talk differently (different speech organs). Hunt all edible game with bow/spear/traps; never hunt intelligent creatures (taboo); waste nothing. Pack led by the strongest male (until too old). Strong **"we"-feeling, weak "I"-feeling** — loyal first to the pack. Sworn **eternal friendship with howather**. Seen as untrustworthy by humans (who misread their pack-loyalty); they in turn see humans as treacherous and selfish.

### Other forest folk
- **Harpyor (harpies):** nomadic bird-creatures; hunt and steal; cowardly, attack only at great advantage; love glittering things (may rob travellers).
- **Älvfolk (elf-kin):** scattered **dryader** in forests, **oreader** in mountains — shy, avoid mortals. The mighty animist magician **Eloaner** (a wood-elf) lives at **Santono**; respected even by trolls. Only ~100 elf-kin total in Torshem.

## Conflicts brewing under the surface

Torshem is currently peaceful but full of campaign-fuel tensions:
- **Traditions vs. new settlement:** the "new Village Time" splits howather — conservatives fear the spirits' wrath at permanent settlement. Dwarves back settlement (more food to buy); conservative howather are backed by jori farmers who fear howather wealth/power.
- **Trade rivalry:** Houses **Vedort** and **Sagbon** (both small Erebos houses) fight for the vital dwarf trade; dwarves play them off to avoid a monopoly. Espionage/sabotage simmer; open war would ruin both. → city intrigue adventures.
- **Farmland pressure:** Torsborg's 400-year-old farmland is depleting; overpopulation and growing landless ("torpare") class. Farmers want the 212-peace treaty revised for more land; howather (and, pragmatically, both merchant houses) refuse. Merchants instead urge the landless to leave.
- **The corpse-eaters (Likätarna):** unknown to all, they've taken the King's Barrows; people have been vanishing. Howather blame svartfolk; townsfolk wrongly blame the **wolfmen** → rising anti-wolfman racism in Torsborg. The first killing of a wolfman by a jorer will trigger a blood-money/blood-feud crisis that traps the howather (sworn to the Nékhi) between their allies and the townsfolk.

## The town of Torsborg

- Founded ~500 years ago; ~**800 inhabitants**; live by trade, fishing, farming. A typical, somewhat dirty wooden trading town (wooden streets, refuse in the streets, roaming pigs/dogs/rats).
- **Government:** ruled by a **stadsmästare** (town-master) — by ancient custom the eldest of family **Sagbon**. Laws are made by a monthly **council** (town-master + 1 rep each for free merchants [in practice house Vedort], craftsmen, and self-owning farmers; landless have no voice — not a democracy). A decision needs 3 of 4 councillors.
- **Law & order:**
  - **City watch (Stadsvakten):** 30 members; guard gates, patrol day & night; jail criminals until tried. Patrol = 4 watchmen + 1 officer. Gear: leather harness/greaves, cone-helm, dagger + short-sword (officers: short mail, metal greaves, winged cone-helm, dagger + broadsword); all carry medium shields with crossed-swords emblem.
  - **Harbour watch (Hamnvakt):** 15 members; one 10-man rowing boat.
  - **Gates** close 21:00, open 06:00; never opened mid-night except emergencies.
  - **Weapons ban:** all weapons (except knives) surrendered at the gate guardhouse and stored in the jail; returned on departure. Smiths may make/store weapons; buyers must immediately deposit them or leave.
  - **Magic ban:** practising any magic within the walls = arrest + punishment.
  - **Jail (Häkte):** stone, barred, ~20 capacity; base of the watch. Valuables can be stored (1% of value per week).
  - **Court (Domarrådet):** 3 men + 3 women chosen by the council; meets weekly; death sentences hanged publicly on the market square.
  - **Sentencing guidelines:**
    - *Minor crime* (pickpocketing, public brawling, vandalism, slander, weapon possession): flogging; repeat offenders lose the right hand.
    - *Medium crime* (armed robbery, grievous assault, grand theft, burglary, practising magic, illegal-drug possession): forced labour 1T4 months; death on repeat.
    - *Grave crime* (murder, arson, rape, robbery-murder, espionage, kidnapping): death.

### Shops & institutions in Torsborg
- **Vedorts hus** — Vidar Vedort's two-storey HQ + warehouses/quarters.
- **Sagbons hus** — by the town hall; run with an iron hand by Laberkus' sister **Elissa**.
- **Stadshus (town hall)** — stone, 2 floors; council chamber + town-master's office/residence; always guarded.
- **Häkte (jail)** — see above.
- **Örthandlare (herbalist)** — chance-based stock of herbs/drugs (new delivery ~monthly).
- **Handelsbod (general store)** — tools, containers, camping gear, sundries.
- **Skeppsbyggare (boatwright)** — small boats and canoes.
- **Wegils tempel** — the town's largest, impressive stone temple; priests always present.
- **Bokbinderi (bookbindery)** — binds books; can translate common languages (e.g. satenu = elvish, gryndur = dwarvish).
- **Marknadstorg (market square)** — morning market; food & drink, donkeys/mules/ponies/oxen; also beggars and thieves.
- **Blå draken (the Blue Dragon)** — the only inn for travellers (where PCs lodge); owner **Bern Armstarke**, a friendly ~60-year-old; known for good mead. Counts as a "Normal" inn.
- **Vapensmed (weaponsmith)** — all European weapons, armour, parts, shields.
- **Hästhandlare (horse trader)** — all horse types + saddles/saddlebags.

## Major adventure locations

### The Monastery (Munkklostret)
- NE of Torsborg in the Nidabergen — now **ruins**. The Wegil monks here (peaceful, prayer/meditation/copying) used to send reps to Torsborg festivals. About a month ago, monks spotted black-robed men heading to a cave; they followed and found a bronze door (grinning demon face) — the entrance to **Kashim's hidden temple**. They infiltrated via a secret stone door, were discovered, the monastery was burned, monks slaughtered or captured. Abbot **Karn Lendur**, mortally speared, left a parchment recounting events and a map to the temple, begging finders to rescue the captured monks (or at least deliver the parchment to the church).
- Now the ruin is haunted by the demon **Kanicaar-an-Cosh** (placed by the temple's servants to kill the curious). Key rooms: reception hall with 4 mutilated hanged corpses (travellers killed by the demon); monk cells (search for surviving small items — a 1T20 table of finds); prayer hall (broken Wegil statue; demon hides behind a pillar with its stolen treasure incl. spell scrolls TALA MED DÖD & KONTROLLERA LÄGRE ODÖD).

### Kashim's Temple (Kashims tempel) — the main dungeon
- NE of the monastery; originally built by Jorpagner during the Village Time, abandoned after the Konflux. **Eight years ago** the young necromancer **Kati Löm** rediscovered it, learned Kashim's worship from ancient books, settled in, grew powerful, and founded a **cult of ~50 fanatics**.
- **Hierarchy:** Kati Löm (priestess/leader) → 12 krigarpräster (war-priests), 10 of whom roam the world spreading the cult and return 4×/year to sacrifice; the other 2 are **vampires** — **Palimas Carnor** and **Dori Thalik** — her personal assistants (hunt nightly as bat/rat/smoke-cloud/wolf). The rest do daily chores.
- **Daily rhythm:** breakfast & work assignment in the morning; prayer/hymns in the sacrifice hall at night; ~monthly human or large-animal sacrifice; guests lodged in guest rooms. (Descriptions assume PCs explore at **night**; the SL adjusts for daytime — e.g. dining hall full by day, sacrifice hall full at night.) Doors are unlocked unless stated; locks are set into the doors (can't be smashed off).
- **Layout — Level 1 (selected rooms):**
  - **1. Port** — the locked bronze demon-door (lockpick SG 40; can't be smashed/axed/rammed).
  - **2. Collapsed tunnel** — hidden behind brush; ends at a stone door with an iron-ring handle (hard STY roll to open).
  - **3. Reception room** — a lectern with a locked **guest book** (in Old Howathic; needs FV B3 to read).
  - **4. Guard room** — 3 guards in studded leather; if killed, they carry the **key ring** opening all doors except 9, 10, 28–31 (held by the vampires / Kati Löm). Chest with oil, fire-kit, torches, rope, signal horn; 2 wine bottles ("Tyrs dugnad").
  - **5. Old guard rooms / 6. Guest room with secret door / 7. Guest rooms** — dusty, with chests of black robes etc. (impersonating servants is pointless — the few servants all recognize each other).
  - **14. Library** — extensive shelves (poetry/history/warfare/martial-arts/weapons/animal/elf-culture/Kashim ritual & hymn books, messages from other Kashim cults, magic-theory books). Certain books grant XP in **Historia, Kulturkännedom (elves), Kunskap om magi** after 6 days' study + INT roll (max 1–10 XP per book). Several magic/spell books were taken by Kati Löm to room 31.
  - **15. Dining hall / 16. Kitchen / 17. Storeroom** (food & drink stocks) — nothing of interest beyond supplies.
  - **18 & 19. Spiral stairs** to Level 2.
  - **20. Antechamber (Försal)** — grotesque demon-worship murals; cabinet with candles, incense, ritual bronze bowls, sacrificial knives, oil.
  - **21. Refuse room (Soprum)** — a 6 m pit of bubbling, foul dark-brown sludge (a mystery; predates Kati Löm; "consumes" all refuse).
  - **27. Sacrifice hall (Offersal)** — black Kashim statue on a platform; **Kati Löm** in red silk with bronze bowl & bloody dagger over a freshly sacrificed monk; black-robed servants singing. **Ten 6 m fire-pits** (fall = 2T6 damage + ongoing burn; two Klättra rolls to climb out). Two secret doors (W & E). If beaten, Kati flees via a secret door to room 29.
  - **28. Treasury** — locked (Kati's key; SG 20, 60 dmg); 3 chests: 750 km; 700 sm; 500 gm + jewellery worth 1400 sm.
  - **29. Anteroom / 30. Bedroom / 31. Storeroom** — Kati Löm's private quarters. Room 31 has a secret door → a long tunnel exiting ~4 km east of the main entrance; Kati flees this way with all her spellbooks (hence the empty chests) — likely to another Kashim temple, possibly returning for revenge.
- **Resolution:** free the monks, defeat/drive off the servants, escort the monks back to Torsborg. The church **praises** the PCs for crushing a secret Kashim cult but gives **no reward** ("virtue is its own reward") — PCs keep any treasure found.

### The King's Barrows (Kungshögarna)
- SE of Torsborg on open fields — three round grass-covered burial mounds with **magically sealed** stone doors. Old Howathic runes name those buried: **Mawasether, Melanowi, Kathenowi** + her husband and two children — great heroes of the Village Time (not actually royal; the name is folk legend). All hold treasure; never plundered (sealed doors + cunning traps). Sacred to the howather (visited at midsummer every 9th year by the völva; grave-robbing = death).
- **Now infested by special corpse-eaters (likätare).** Mawasether's mound (sample rooms): the door (FÖRSEGLA E4, SIGILL E4, PERMANENS E4, NEXUS E2 — only ÖPPNA can open it, with an effect-grade contest). Grave chambers hold skeletons + silver/gold coins, jewellery, and a magic **bronze vendel-helm** (MÖRKERSYN — night-vision when worn) and a magic **bronze broadsword** (FROST — activated by "Låt köldens runor träda fram!"; +4T3 cold damage when it penetrates armour; deactivated by the counter-phrase). Caves hold roaming corpse-eaters and victims (incl. the runaway couple — the dead youth was the missing woman's boyfriend).

### The Dark-elf Lair (Svartalfernas lya)
- Eastern Nidabergen — a tribe of ~50 dark-elves with a couple of **resar (giants)** and a handful of **svartnissar** (goblins), led by the ancient (73-year-old) **Svirk Långskägg** (mentally unstable — fits of amnesia/rage; the tribe takes this as spirit-possession and respects him more). They stay in caves by day, raid by night, ride tamed **ulvar (war-wolves)**, paint themselves/gear black (some add useless "magic" symbols).
- **Caves:** entrance with a wolf-rider mural; guard cave (5 wolves by day / 2 resar by night); two wolf caves (15 sleeping wolves each by day); goblin cave (10); supply cave; main dark-elf cave (35 sleeping by day; Svirk + 3–4 stay at night); resar cave (5/3; a sack of severed human/elf heads as "souvenirs"); **prisoner cave** holding siblings **Kåre & Måne Vermundsson** (children of Torsborg merchant Sigbjörn — possible reward); **treasure cave** (their gear + paints, black powder doses, spell scrolls FRAMMANA/SKICKA BORT UMBRA, ÅTERFINNA, STENVÄGG, **and Vlempi's spellbook** — i.e. the goal of adventure #4); emergency exit tunnel. Most cave entrances blocked by 60-BEP boulders.

### The Troll Lair (Trollens lya)
- Deep in the Troll Forest — orcs, resar, and trolls in hidden underground lairs (no adventurer is known to have returned alive). A small forest-troll tribe of 30 lives in a hill (two boulder-hidden entrances), led by the unusually large, ugly **Gomol Skalle** (cruel; tortures victims; sensitive about a facial scar from a fight with elves — enraged if it's mentioned). Trolls love jewellery (wear stolen necklaces/rings; tie victims' heads to their belts as trophies); hate dwarves, ducks, and halflings especially; eat people, elves, dwarves. Caves: entrance, sleepy guard, large main cave (29 trolls + Gomol; ~20 hunt at night), captive caves and loot caves.

### The Castle Ruin (Borgruinen)
- Between the Troll Forest and the Old Forest — the ruined castle of the wizard **Losether**, who vanished ~500 years ago during a magical experiment. Said to hold a hidden library. It is guarded by **30 animated skeletons** Losether created as permanent magical items (ANIMERA DÖD E5, SIGILL E5, PERMANENS E5, NEXUS E2 — they sense all living beings, never expire, guard only the keep). Skeleton combat notes: **no damage from thrusting weapons** (arrows, daggers, spears), **half from cutting weapons** (swords, axes), **full from crushing weapons** (clubs, hammers).
- Currently the orc **Argor Enöga** + 6 orcs occupy a tower (afraid of the skeletons in the main keep). Rooms include orc towers, a "rat tower" (50 starving rats), murals of skulls/runes/pentagram by Losether, a treasure secret-compartment (mostly emptied when Losether fled — copper ingots, silver goblet, candles, a magic **SYN E4** potion), trapped/weakened floor sections, and dungeon cells below (one with a chained skeleton prisoner).

### The Thing-place (Tingsplatsen — Thiwosgos)
- A sacred grassy hill where the howather hold their **annual thing (assembly)** at the spring equinox for 5 days. Empty (and respected/left alone) the rest of the year.
- **5-day procedure:** Day 1 attendance & jury election; Day 2 judging the herse (deposed if half vote against; declared outlaw if ¾ do); Day 3 law matters; Day 4 inter-clan disputes; Day 5 other matters + the völva sacrifices a deer to the spirits/ancestors.
- **Representation/law:** each clan represented by its eldest; decisions by simple majority; brevity in speech is prized (windbags get a bad name). Howather law is ancient (like medieval Swedish provincial laws), kept by the völva. **Three punishments:** fines, outlawry (anyone may kill the outlaw with impunity), death (hanging). Clan leaders judge internal cases; the thing handles inter-clan disputes and crimes against the whole people (e.g. desecrating sacred sites; land/spring ownership).
- **Sites:** the judges' ring (jury + völva), the **Altar** (sacred flat stone — oaths, eulogies, the closing sacrifice; where the 213 peace and the 598 wolfman-howather friendship were sworn), the herse's wooden throne, the speaker's stone.

### Santono
- The only large permanent elf-kin settlement in Torshem; founded 357 eO by **Eloaner** around a giant 50-m beech tree, with tree-houses ≥25 m up (reached by rope ladders; highly defensible). An **animist academy** teaching animism, botany, drug/healing-herb lore, first aid, healing, zoology. Residents work one day and study the next (even Eloaner). Usually 20–40 residents (~45% elves, ~45% humans, ~10% other — karkions, centaurs, metamorphs, etc.). No svartfolk (too barbaric) or dwarves (don't do animism). Functions best as a **safe haven** for healing, learning, or rest; all Torshem treats it with respect — or, for svartfolk, fear.
