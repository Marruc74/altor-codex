# Ereb Altor: Aidne — Reference Summary

A regional sourcebook for the Swedish RPG **Drakar och Demoner** (© RiotMinds AB), describing the **Aidne peninsula** in the **Kopparhavet** (Copper Sea) region of the world **Ereb Altor**. 98 pages, scanned. Designed for use with the *Expert* (EDD) and *Gigant* rules. The setting is subtropical, formed around the towering **Aidne Mountains**; climate/flora compared to Spain or Italy.

## 1. Geography

### Zorakin (kingdom)
- Two main peninsulas: **Grindanu** (west) and **Indar** (east), joined by the coastal duchy **Inmark**.
- North is mountainous/forested — economy here is mining, hunting, forestry; weak contact with the rest of the country.
- **Grindanu:** half farmland, half the dark **Torilskogen** forest. Once bright woodland; turned dark/evil after the *Svarta Hären* (black folk) were allowed in under King **Kruxhelm Armstarke** (early 100s e.O.).
- **Jostefloden** river → ends in the grim **Grimmaniträsket** swamp (Caddobukten). Border-river **Stirpaz** (shared by Zorakin & Kardien by ancient right).
- **Indar:** fertile, ~¾ cultivated; rivers **Solaån** and **Granströmmen**; windy plains covered in windmills that double as **semaphore signal towers** (warn of invasion, rain, tax collectors, press-gangs). Chief city **Indarsol** sits on a hill by the Solaån. People here are called **legerdier**.
- **Nordbergaskogen** forest (NE): wood elves, plus humans, satyrs, shy swan-maidens; eastern part (duchy **Salamora**) reserved entirely for elf folk.

### Kardien (kingdom)
- Also split. West of the Stirpaz: fertile **Divra plain** (wheat). Only hilly area = the **Insa-kullarna** on **Faltraxnäs**.
- **Gridefloden** = main trade artery (barges/coastal craft link Divra's villages to cities **Arntuna** and **Ekeborg**).
- North: **Aidne-bergen** cut down to the coast = **Inmark** (sparse, troll/orc-haunted). Fortified strip along the **Phalto** river.
- Far north = poor, semi-independent duchies **Videlrike** & **Nordmark** (~15% of population), under constant black-folk raids; orc tribes on Antivin's north coast outside Kardian control.
- Native stone-age people: the **ker-bosh** (pre-Jorpagna).
- **Inmarka-sjön** lake (fish-rich) — colonization blocked by aggressive orc tribes.

### Goiana (elf realm)
- Small independent realm between rivers **Goio** and **Firkax**; ~10,000 elf folk (mostly wood elves). Hilly oak/pine country. Formerly Kardian, now self-ruled, living in close harmony with nature.

## 2. Fauna (four zones) & Climate

### Fauna
- **Mountains:** cliff-goats, wolves, cave-lions/cave-bears, eagles; sparse orcs/giants/trolls; legends of cyclopes & giants; dragons occasionally nest from the sea.
- **Forests:** oak/pine/larch; deer, bison, boar; foxes, bears, wolves. Boar/deer hunting is a noble honor sport.
- **Swamps:** dominated by birds; hippos, crocodiles, snakes; reptile-men. **Unique: the Drinnen** — 75–100 cm, splayable hooves for marsh travel, black with silver/red scales, partial scale-armor, bladed triangular tail used in hunting; aggressive; lives in territorial pairs.
- **Plains:** mostly farmed; original antelope/horse/lion fauna survives only in remote **Antivin**.

### Climate
- Subtropical, **two seasons**:
  - **Winter** (early autumn → mid-spring): cool, 5–10°C, frequent rain.
  - **Summer:** hot, dry, 35–40°C midday, clear skies.
- *Gigant* climate stats: Stability 88240; Temperature: Subtropical. Wind/precipitation tables provided per season.

## 3. Neighbors

### Caddo (island)
- Volcanic island; spine = **Appo-bergen** (to 3,000 m, extinct cones); fertile coastal plains hold most of the population (wheat, olives — near overpopulated). Best shipbuilders in Ereb.
- People: the **Dalker** (tall, fair, blue/grey eyes; sunburned). Unique unrelated language (**dalkiska**) = major language barrier.
- **Government (unique):** born from the civil war **Odesfejden** (104–111 e.O.) and the **Arno-eden** (Arno oath). 37 **cantons**, each with a **canton assembly** of all taxpaying adults (men & women, ~35% of population). National **landsråd** (council of canton reps) handles foreign policy, standing army, big works, post, inter-canton disputes; national laws need ⅔ of canton assemblies. Stable, coup-proof. Tension: 12 trade-city cantons vs rural cantons.
- **Religion:** only the **Lysande Vägen** (Shining Path) allowed; magic forbidden (practiced only in secret).
- **Military:** folkuppbåd (levy) + landsvakt (professional patrol) + national army & navy. Weak in cavalry, superb **archers** (popular mercenaries); **best navy in the region**.

### Erebos (three islands)
- Caddo's great trade rival; rocky volcanic isles **Dakkilo, Targero, Beyural**. Fishing/seafaring; merchant culture (admires wealth & negotiation over the warrior ideal).
- **Government:** since civil war 512–514, ruled by the **Five Houses** (**Femhusrådet** in capital **Nohstril**); each house has a council seat; only **unanimous** decisions (each member has veto). ~2 dozen trade houses remain; the Five control >½ the country.
- **Religion:** Joric gods incl. the **Young Gods**; Lysande Vägen banned (seen as Caddo's tool); otherwise very tolerant.

### Morélvidyn
- Source of recurring "anka" (duck-folk) **pirate** raids on Kardien/Zorakin (see timeline).

## 4. Peoples (Folkslag)

- **Pamperna ("Hästfolket" / horse folk):** ~1,000 people, ~300 prized semi-wild horses (a fine one can fetch 20,000+ silver; they sell only 2–3/year and only "obedient" ones). Granted land by **King Valien** (50 f.O.) for 1 horse/year tribute; royal troops defend them. Distinct guttural language; eat only fish & grain; no known religion; live in villages of ~10 houses + stables.
- **Lindskiarnar (a.k.a. pharynkier):** mysterious telepathic warrior people; entered Zorakin ~200s f.O., built the dazzling fortress-city **Pharynx**, conquered widely on bronze (shunned iron) until defeated by **Valien Rikesbyggare** (wars ended 55 f.O.). Near-exterminated; ~100 survivors hidden in two secret mountain colonies. Masterful artisans (rivaling dwarves/elves). *Full description & stats in §15.*
- **Halflings & "ankor" (duck-folk):** farmers/traders/mercenaries. Accepted as equals in Zorakin; discriminated against (higher taxes) in Kardien → many live in free cities and the royal domain **Ekenfara**.
- **Svartfolk (black folk):** Torilskogen (trolls, orcs, dark elves, a few giants); reptile-men in Grimmaniträsket (poisoned arrows); svartnissar in Brynhildur's forest (E. Kardien). Mostly avoid contact; sometimes join **Svarta Tornet's** ("Black Tower") attacks.
- **Älvfolk (elf folk):** wood elves + rare swan-maidens in Nordbergaskogen (pay symbolic tribute of furs/timber to Chrymil & Luksilo) and Goiana.

## 5. Pendon — Capital of Zorakin

- **History:** founded **367 f.O.** as a monastery/lyceum by **Leon Inergryd** of the **Mandelorden** (scholar-monks of **Ingeril**, goddess of learning). Grew via its harbor into a major Copper Sea hub; capital when Zorakin was proclaimed (57 f.O.). By **610 e.O.**: ~7,000 people; 5th-largest Copper Sea city; largest in Zorakin & Kardien.
- **Government:** crownland (**kronomark**) — the king owns the land & all taxes. Day-to-day run by a **stadsmarskalk** (mayor) + city council + 6 city secretaries. Uniquely, Pendon citizens (since Ragnvald den Sköne) hold the right to propose new laws. The **muraskatt** = gate toll (~1 silver mark per traveler/animal); farmers & royal officials exempt.
- **Economy:** ~⅓ merchants, ⅓ craftsmen. Declining trade (after the lost *Sympatikriget* vs Felicien + rising black-folk activity), but still among the region's richest cities (with Casso, Pallin, Gringul). **Exports:** iron, timber, grain. **Imports:** textiles, salt, precious metals. (Book gives a worked grilny-textile trade example in guldmark.) Long list of craft trades (smiths, weavers, glassblowers, illuminators, bookbinders, etc.).
- **Education:** royal **library** (~10,000 volumes, oldest of its kind in the Copper Sea); the **Mandelorden lyceum**; ~200 scholars ("De lärdes skara") with ~800 students; subjects incl. history, languages, geography, magic, alchemy, medicine.
- **Defense:**
  - **City guard:** 400 men in 4 companies of 100 (serve 3 weeks of 4; ~300 on duty); ringmail + leather, conical helm, red surcoat with gold eagle; halberds + crossbows OR spear/shield/shortsword.
  - **Palace guard:** 200 elite (king's garde); half-plate, red cloaks/plumes, gold surcoat with red eagle; spear, round shield, broadsword; all mounted-capable + good bow/crossbow.
  - **Militia:** in theory all able men 20–45, but untrained for ~150 years.
  - **Weapons control:** weapons surrendered at gate weapon-houses; carrying requires a royal **vapenbrev** (buyable on the black market for ~100 silver/week). Low crime.
- **Layout:** stone/brick two-story buildings; castle on **Kastelludden** (former Mandeludden/lyceum, burned by pirate-ankor 55 f.O.). Long sandy shallows force cargo onto flat-bottomed barges (aids customs, hinders smuggling). The ~130 m **Kora-katedralen** at Kungstorget = largest Shining Path cathedral in Zorakin, **4th-largest in the world**; entrance faces **Arno** (Odo's birthplace).

## 6. Pendon's Castle
A detailed, **numbered room-by-room key** — ready-to-use adventure location. Covered rooms include guard quarters, knights' lodgings (each with the knight's arms on the door), the **jungfrukammare** (maidens' chamber, women-only, tapestries of Zorakin's history), the king's lavish **bedchamber** (marble-mosaic royal arms, carved "forest" walls, regalia in gold-fitted chests) and **study/förmak** (audiences by the fire, bookshelves of chronicles/law/religious texts), plus towers, kitchens/slaughter areas, and the rust-/weapon-chambers.

## 7. Zorakin Politics (selected)
- ~20 of Zorakin's ~100 larger cities are **free cities** (also crownland), largely self-governing under a city council (11–21 lifetime members) led by a royal-appointed mayor; corruption common; bishops (**bontisåler**) guaranteed seats in diocese capitals.
- Councils handle local laws, tariffs, the guard, public works, defense, building/business permits, festivities.

## 8. Goiana Specials

- **Enlil Talannen — the ensouled forest:** at the **Meditation in Golena** (429 e.O.), the elf **Telidor** met the spirit Enlil Talannen (driven from her home by necromancers). She bound herself to Goiana's people and forest for as long as Goiana exists. Powers:
  - **Omniscient observation** of all that happens in Goiana's forests.
  - **Communication** (whispers in the leaves) to those present at Golena; outsiders hear only wind.
  - **Magic aid:** animists from the meditation pay **half PSY** for spells and get **+2 CL**.
  - **PSY recovery:** can halve (or, if displeased, double) recovery time.
  - **Weather control** in Goiana; hostile armies always fight in the worst weather; can quench wildfires.
  - **Omens** for those who weren't at the meditation; near-immune to harm (can't be hexed; KONTROLLERA VÄXT fails in Goiana).
- **Silverlindar (silver-linden trees):** rare sacred tree (silver-grey bark), gift of **Tevatenu** (Mother Earth). Properties:
  - Elf meditating in a silverlind grove heals 1 KP/hour and recovers PSY 4× faster.
  - Leaves → healing paste (1 dose = 10 g; closes a wound in 1 hour; open wounds heal 2× faster).
  - Wood is highly enchantable: **+2 CL** for spells placed in silverlind items; makes excellent composite bows (STY-group 2).
  - Silverlind arrows treated by elf oilmakers are durable and wound normally without iron tips (prized by mages).

## 9. Names & Titles

### Commoners
- Common tongue is close to **old jori** (≈ Swedish); jori-flavored names (Styrbjörn, Kettil, Tryggheim, Björne).
- Almost everyone has a patronymic surname (Vilhjalmsson, Rikardsdotter). Master craftsmen with a master's letter are called **Mäster** + first name.
- For place names, the book suggests real maps of Norrland, Iceland, Faroe Islands, Gotland.

### Nobility
- **Knighting** in Zorakin/Kardien: requires outstanding combat skill + good conduct; only a knight can dub one, after consulting ≥5 other knights. Titles **not hereditary** but often buyable; knightly orders can confer it.
- A knight is **Riddar** + first name. A noble with a fief is title + family name or fief name.

### Titles of address
- King/Queen: **Ers Majestät**
- Prince/Princess: **Ers Kungliga Höghet**
- Duke: **Ers Nåd / Ers Hertigliga Höghet**
- Count: **Ers Grevliga Excellens**
- Baron/Marquis: **Ers Excellens**
- Other nobles: **Högvälborne**
- Knight + noble: e.g. "Baron Riddar Bartel"; order memberships placed after the full name.

### Noble inheritance
- Titles pass to eldest son → else eldest daughter's husband → else eldest brother's son / eldest brother's daughter's husband → else the noble's superior appoints a successor.

## 10. Calendar & Chronology (Tideräkning)

- Altorian year = 365.25 days; 12 months of 30–31 days. **2nd month = 28 days**, +1 leap day every 4th year honoring the first jori emperor **Tebertinu** (added to month 2 to keep him out of "unlucky" month 3).
- **Old (jori) month names** (12 time-gods): Persil, Gyrsag, Fateska, Iskamsi, Misiska, Minde, Melendo, Byrdo, Giren, Taragan, Nusin, Tannan.
- **New (church) month names** (folk name in parentheses): öppningsmånad (öppne), köldmånad (kalte), tömånad (töe), spiramånad (spire), groddmånad (grodde), solemånad (sole), växtemånad (växte), halvårsmånad (halve), skördemånad (skörde), vilemånad (vile), mörkemånad (mörke), Etinsmånad (Etinne).
- **Days of the week:** Mundesdag, Tyresdag, Mittvecke, Turesdag, Fridsdag, Larsdag, **Odosdag** (rest day; no physical work).

### Festivals (all church-linked)
- **Soldansen** — biggest annual festival, at summer solstice (longest day).
- **Ångerdagen** — autumn equinox; reflect on the year, repent sins.
- **Winter solstice** — boisterous celebration of the sun's return.
- **24th Etinsmånad** — Odo's birth (masses, gift-giving).
- **12th spiramånad** — Odo's death (a week's fast + mourning processions).
- **5th solemånad** (54 days later) — feast of Odo's reunion with his father.
- Each city/guild has a patron saint with its own Odosdag. Non-church feasts: New Year, weddings, harvests, inaugurations, jubilees.

## 11. Coins, Exchange, Measures & Weights

### Coinage
- Every country has copper, silver, gold coins. In Zorakin/Kardien only the royal mint may strike copper & silver; **gold may be minted by anyone**, so gold value varies widely (~30 types circulate).
  - Finest = **dvärgadaler** (up to 25 silver each); some guild/noble gold worth only 2–3 silver.
  - Zorakin **riksgulddaler** = 10 silver; Kardian **kardiermark** = 5 silver.
- **Money-changers** take ~10% commission. Each country's silver coin = 1 standard DoD silver in price lists (so list prices apply everywhere; Erebos is cheapest to buy in).

### Currency systems
- **Zorakin:** 10 koppardaler (kd) = 1 silverdaler (sd); 10 sd = 1 guldriksdaler (grd)
- **Kardien:** 10 kopparmark (kmk) = 1 silvermark (smk); 5 smk = 1 kardiermark (kam)
- **Caddo:** 12 kopparskillingar (ks) = 1 caddodaler (cd); 12 cd = 1 gulddaler (gd)
- **Erebos:** 20 örtugar (ör) = 1 silverkrona (sk); 20 sk = 1 riksdaler (rd)
- (Cross-exchange tables provided; rates differ by coin size/metal content. The rules are explicitly optional.)

### Measures & weights
- **Length:** 1 aln ≈ 65 cm (2/3 m) = 2 fot; 1 fot ≈ 30 cm = 12 tum; 1 tum ≈ 2.75 cm; 1 fjärdingsväg = 1,500 alnar ≈ 1,000 m.
- **Weight:** 1 skålpund ≈ 0.5 kg = 2 mark (250 g) ; 1 mark = 12 lod; 1 lod ≈ 20 g = 10 uns (2 g); 1 lispund = 10 skålpund (5 kg); 1 centner = 100 skålpund (50 kg).
- **Volume:** 1 kanna = 2 liter = 2 stop = 4 kvarter (0.5 L) = 16 jungfrur (12.5 cl); 1 tunna = 60 kannor (120 L).

## 12. Timeline (Tidslinje) — key dates

- **599 f.O.** — The **Third Konflux**: a cloud of carnivorous insects devastates Ereb; ~half of all inhabitants die; Zorakin's population halved; society collapses. Kardien fares better (remote); ~500 years of chaos follow.
- **599–~100 f.O.** — *Stabiliseringstiden*: scattered survivor settlements; black folk spread into mountains/forests; people gather into tribes. Early organizing efforts by the **echther** (Kardien) and **Mandelorden** (Zorakin).
- **599–595 f.O.** — Civil war in Kardien (general Jegher vs. advocate Dirivin); ended by **Valentin Echthe's** ruse. **590** — Echthe dies; followers isolate on Divra's cape.
- **~450s f.O.** — Indar's legerdier break up collective farms for private farms.
- **401 f.O.** — **Mandelorden** founded by Haakon Mandel (history research; gradually drops worship of Ingeril).
- **367 f.O.** — Lyceum of **Pendon** founded by Leon Inergryd.
- **340–180 f.O.** — Constant border feuds between Kardier and Zorakier across the Stirpaz.
- **~290 f.O.** — Gold found on southern Grindanu; Mandelorden secretly mines a fortune for 200 years; Grand Master **Aquahom** stores the treasure in the lyceum.
- **~200s f.O.** — **Lindskiarnar** arrive, build Pharynx; conquest wars follow.
- **55 f.O.** — Lindskiarner wars end; **Valien Rikesbyggare** triumphs; survivors hide.
- **~410 e.O.** — Wood elves settle Goiana's forests; **424** — Goiana founded as an elf realm; Kardien's King Beronrik deposed → **echther** reorganize Kardien, move capital Faltrax → **Ekeborg**, declare themselves nobility, introduce the Lysande Vägen in N. Kardien.
- **429 e.O.** — Meditation in **Golena**; pact with **Enlil Talannen**.
- **457 e.O.** — **Dance academy** founded in Goiana (Golena).
- **466–467 e.O.** — Zorakin's war with Morélvidyn; invasion crushed (mage **Lodekhiz**); peace, no territory lost.
- **498 e.O.** — Morélvidyn pirates raid Kardien to Arntuna; **King Vidar III** duels pirate chief **Illerest** — disarms him but is killed by a hidden crossbow; Illerest, shamed, kills himself with Vidar's sword.

- **536–540 e.O.** — Orc war on Goiana (backed by Svarta Tornet); orcs defeated.

- **591 e.O.** — Duke **Wilfred of Salamora** conquers Fristaden (but trade collapses instead of transferring).

## 13. Religion — the Lysande Vägen (Shining Path)

- Worships **Etin** (creator) via the prophet **Odo** (born in Arno). Scripture = the **Gyllene Boken** (Golden Book), written in **dalkiska** — so all clergy must learn it.
- **Doctrine:** spread the Lära ("Läran") by mission **or sword** (justifies crusades); destroy **undead** (an abomination); magic is the great sticking point:
  - **Caddo (orthodox):** ALL magic forbidden, incl. alchemy ("do not warp Etin's creation").
  - **Mainland:** magic is neutral; some spells are forbidden except against unbelievers — KONTROLLERA VARELSE, BLINDHET, EPILEPSI, FRUKTAN, MÖRKER, FÖRBANNA VAPEN, MÖRKERVÄG, ILLUSION, FATA MORGANA. **All necromancy forbidden** regardless of purpose.

### Hierarchy (Zorakin/Kardien)
- **Exarken** in Ekeborg (≈ Pope) + a **kuria** of 6 **tots-kerigasser** (church government).
- Each country = a **kerisåt** led by a **kerisgass** (≈ archbishop) → divided into **bontisåt** (dioceses) under a **bontisål** (bishop) → **församlingar** (parishes) under a **riark** (parish priest, often also a royal official; can buy out of celibacy).
- Financed by the **kyrkoskatt** (1/10 of total production); church + clergy tax-exempt → **enormously rich**, often luxurious (contradicting original ideals of poverty). Owns large estates.
- **Den Ljusa Handen** ("the Bright Hand") = the **Inquisition** (founded 314 by exark Zegedorn III; not on Caddo); monitors doctrine; **heretics are executed**. Has crushed popular protest movements.
- By law, kings of both countries must profess the Lysande Vägen; the exark always sits among royal advisors.

### Gudagåvor (divine gifts — Gigant religion rules)
- **Etin's** gifts: curse undead (halves their stats/move/skills for 1T4+2 days); immunity to normal hexes; **Solmakt** — conjure a blinding light-globe (petrifies trolls like sunlight; can fire BLIXT bolts; drains PSY); banish spirits/gastar via PSY contest.
- **Moder Jord's** gifts (green-clad, metal-free messengers): activate by 1 SR concentration + PSY roll (1 PSY each) — **+10 STY** (1T8+8 SR), **Sammansmältning** (merge with surroundings, only VARSEBLIVNING detects, 1T4+2 min), curse undead (as above). Cannot be used in dead/desecrated/artificial areas.

### Heresies (Kätterier)
- **Gaddism** — the Caddo view that magic is ungodly/destructive and must be banned (forbidden on the mainland since the great Schism).
- **Musism / illusister** — from Trakorien; the world is an illusion, all is random/chaotic, so pursue sensual pleasure. Banned by exark Pergon IV (403); illusists exiled to Trakorien → casts suspicion on the **illusionism** magic school.
- **De enkla** — believe Odo never wanted a church; everyone is equal before Etin; loudest critics of church luxury; watched closely by Ljusa Handen; many exiled.
- **Moder Jords kult** (Mother Earth) — elf folk + the **penter** of Faltraxnäs; she is **Tevatenu** (elves) / **Khallill** (penter), a formless Primal Force. No grand temples (worship at leaf-bowers/shore caves); 3 great festivals (spring, summer solstice, autumn equinox); destroying undead is a holy mission; no organized priesthood (chosen green-clad, metal-free messengers).

## 14. Orders (Ordnar)

### Monastic orders
- **Mandelorden** — founded 401 f.O. by Haakon Mandel; scholar-monks of **Ingeril**; mission = find & give Knowledge (research + teaching); ran lyceer (~20 at peak, 200s f.O.); only Knowledge brings lasting happiness. ~3,000 active members (more novices). Two ranks: **noviser** (still researching, do the physical work) and **Lärde** (teach). Funded by modest fees + donations.
- **2:a Orden till Odos minne (Odo-orden)** — founded 112 e.O. to plant mission monasteries around the Copper Sea; the project failed (driven off except on Aidne & Caddo); became independent; the **only strictly orthodox** branch (strict poverty, obedience, celibacy).

### Knightly orders
- General: charitable/martial branch of the Shining Path; bound by poverty & obedience vows but may keep good gear; loot is destroyed or given to the church; ideals = courtesy, honor, justice. Often heavy rules (no magic, wear order colors in battle, no alcohol/gambling, half of war booty to the order, never refuse a fair duel, show mercy). Privileges: free board/lodging, tax/toll exemption while serving, tried only by the order, reduced fees, free craftsman services, international support network.
- **Solriddarna (Sun Knights)** — the Shining Path's official order; founded in Ekeborg at the start of the Great Schism. **Only noble-born Kardier/Zorakier** may join. Protect the church & its shrines; the largest order. Vows of poverty/celibacy loosely kept (members are nobles). Organized in companies of 50–100 knights (each with 1–2 squires); entry fee ~1,000 silver buys lifelong upkeep. Led by exark + bontisåler; a **prior** ≈ colonel commands a company. Fight mainly mounted (lance & sword). **Black** cloaks/surcoats with a **gold sun**; ridderskap wear white belts/spurs; captains have black plumes + gold chains. **Hate the Order of St. Justin.** ~1,500 knights + 2,500 squires.
- **S:t Justins orden** — origin: **Justin från Metera**, a poor cobbler's apprentice (233 e.O.) led by a dream to take a sword from a crypt in Arno's church; the exark freed him on hearing his tale — the sword was **Odo's own**. Justin became a Sun Knight leader, hugely successful (sword **Gawalot**); died of old age 274, declared a saint; the first crusade left the next year. One of the strongest forces around the Copper Sea.

## 15. Lindskiarnar (race + game stats)

### Description
- 175–225 cm tall; yellow-white, smooth near-furry skin (softer than leather but tougher/more durable than cow-leather; shields from cold/wind). Up to **300 kg**; elephant-like feet; little finger drawn down like a second thumb (perfect grip). Very strong, quick, agile, wide-set eyes (bigger field of view than humans/dwarves/elves).
- **Telepathic** — fight in total silence with perfect coordination, no shouted orders; plans set before battle. Speak **jori** when they speak (source of the "human kin" myth).
- Superb warriors; fight in **square formations**, prefer spear or longsword + shield; almost never use missiles (rely on mass). Brilliant artists/craftsmen (rival dwarves' finest smithing & top elf art).
- **Weakness: iron.** No iron found in all of Pharynx; assumed magical origin. (See stats: iron contact drains PSY.)
- After the wars (ended 55 f.O.), ~100 survive in hidden valleys of W. Aidne-bergen and E. Snoarskogen; discoverers are enslaved/killed; mostly forgotten (often mistaken for resar/trolls). **PCs cannot be lindskiarnar after 55 f.O.** unless the whole party is.

## 16. Alchemy (Alkemi)

- Ereb Altor has different natural laws; magic flows through all things. **Magic** releases/reshapes that power (a spell = *besvärjelse*); **alchemy** harnesses substances' inherent power into an **elixir** from **ämnen** (ingredients). **Impossible:** turning base metals into precious metals.
- **Four ingredient roles:** **Basämne** (base/vehicle, usually a liquid), **Effektor** (decides the effect, e.g. eagle feathers → flight, orc-eye → darkvision), **Komponent** (controls activation, duration, max power = **Elixirstyrka / ES**), **Katalysator**.
- **Best raw materials** (strong magic): tissue of **dragons, elf folk, ancient trees (urträd)**, plus **mithril** and **diamond**.
- **Laboratory:** stone walls/floor (fire risk), big windows, good ventilation (toxic gases), ≥2 benches, shelves, recipe books, a big open hearth. Cost **1,000–2,000 gold** (cheaper where master glassblowers are common); upkeep **~100 silver/month** (glass breaks). Most alchemists learn glassblowing to make their own gear. Buyable ingredient cost (if unknown): **1T100+50 sm per ES per dose**.
- **Equipment list (examples):** alkohol, blåsbälg, E-kolv, fyrfat, fyrglas, glasburkar/-flaskor, glasstav, keramikkärl, kittel, kniv, kolvar, lins, mortel, mätglas, pincetter, pipett, prisma, provrör, retort, skalpell, smältdegel, tratt, trefot, tänger, vattenbalja, vaxljus, vedlår, våg & måttsats, öppen eld.
- **Failure table (by margin of failed skill roll):** ranges from half effect (1–2) → no effect + nausea + 1T4 KP (3–4) → bad taste + 1T6 KP + rest (5–6) → 1T10 KP + 1T10 h rest (7–8) → wrong/opposite effect + 1T6 KP (9–10) → **explosion**, 1T10 KP to all + lab destroyed (11–12) → deadly: phobia + 1T20 KP + unconscious 1T6 days (13–14) → **deadly poison STY 3T10**, destroys a sense + 1T20 KP + unconscious 2T10 days. (Applied to objects: reduces BV; at 0 the item dissolves.)

## 17. New Combat System (Nytt stridssystem)

- **Alternative damage system to EDD:** cross-reference the **attacker's weapon** (rows) with the **target's armor** (columns) to get a dice code, replacing the standard damage table. This surfaces each weapon type's strengths/weaknesses (e.g., a war-club is more effective vs ringmail than vs leather).
- **Armor columns:** Ingen rustning / Mjukt läder, tyg / Hårt läder / Ringbrynja / Plåt. ("Tyg" = padding/quilting, not ordinary clothes.) Weapon categories: **Eggvapen** (edged), **Krossvapen** (crushing), **Stickvapen** (piercing). Each has an **Infektionsrisk** value.
- **Armor is treated as incomplete pieces**, so you can (and should) **layer** armor. With multiple armors, use the dice combo giving the **least damage**, and subtract **2 per extra armor worn**.
  - *Example:* a broadsword vs plate-over-ringmail-over-padding → roll 1T20−17; under 16 = no damage.
- **Notes:**
  - Ringmail alone is fairly weak protection but lowers infection risk (wounds are more crush-type) — **except piercing weapons**, which drive broken rings into the wound (extra damage + infection).
  - **Plate** gives excellent protection (almost always worn over padding) — a dagger has only ~5% chance to hurt plate; this is why "1T20−14" (≈70% chance of no damage) is used instead of a flat 1T6.
  - **Stridsbonus** and mounted lance (horse's SB) add to normal damage regardless of armor.
  - **Infection:** worst from deep piercing wounds; for 0.5% risk, roll T100 → on 01, roll any die (even = infected). Dirty weapons triple the risk.
  - **Plate dents:** a hit doing >5 damage to plate makes a **buckla** (−1 to skills needing that body part to move freely; cumulative). Removed by a armorer or self with a successful Hantverk: smide roll (30 min/attempt).
  - Vs **wild animals** (no weapons), use the normal absorption system (or map an animal attack to a comparable weapon code, e.g. unicorn horn ≈ spear, horse kick ≈ flail).

## 18. Combat Skills (Stridsfärdigheter)

- **Tornerlans (jousting lance)** — Type STR, base SMI, cost 3, BCE 0. Develops the Lans skill. Blunt tourney lance does **2T6** (no SB; armor as normal). A hit on shield/chest/belly/head with a difference of ≥5 may **unhorse** the opponent (Tornerlans FV contest; both can be unseated; faller takes 2T6 fall damage, armor absorption halved). 50% chance the lance breaks on a hit. Successful rolls allow ringränning/stäkning/kvintanridning; **perfect** roll = applause + always unhorses; fumble = you fall off.
- **Stavkamp (staff fighting)** — Type STR, base SMI, cost 3, BCE 0. For the >man-height oak staff; slightly better than the Trästav skill. Can **attack and parry in the same SR**; staff does **2T6+SB**; a leg hit forces the (humanoid) target to make a SMI roll or fall. Popular with monks and travelers (an innocent walking-staff becomes a weapon).

## 20. Tournaments (Torneringar)

- Costly & lengthy: ≥2 months' planning (invitations, placards, heralds, menus, food, building lists, parade armor/garb).
- **Choose events** — at least one "peaceful" (ringränning, bågskytte, kvintanridning, tornej, stäkning) and one "martial" (strider till fots, **dyst** [joust], **bohord** [melee]). Usually 5–6 events; prizes for individual winners + overall.
- **Board & lodging** required for all knights (castle, nearby manors, or tents); breakfast, bread & wine by day, grand banquet at night. ~1 day per 30 entrants on a single track.
- **Costs:** heralds/criers/scribes 100 sm; food 30–50 sm/entrant/day; track & stands 300 sm; first prize 250 sm+; own parade armor 4,000–5,000 sm; clothes/banners ~1,000 sm. (Recouped by being invited to others' tournaments.)

## 21. Elemental Dances (Elementardanser) — Golena dance academy

- After the Golena Meditation, **Golena** became Goiana's learning center (academy formally founded **457 e.O.**, several hundred residents). Studies: nature-lore skills (Zoologi, Botanik), animism & harmonism (Gigant), dance & music.
- **Telidor** founded the dance college, inspired by his contact with Enlil Talannen — the idea that **elements (reality's components) dance together**, and a dancer can flow in harmony with them. Golena is the only place in Ereb teaching these. FV B5 earns the title **Dansmästare** (teaches; wears a flame-yellow silk master's robe).
- **Elementardanser** skill — Type särskild, base FYS, cost 12, BCE 0, Kategori B. **Not magic** (opposite approach to mages). Each of the **seven dances** is a separate skill. While dancing you can only move. Dance time by FV: B1 = FYS/2 min, B2 = FYS, B3 = FYS×2, B4 = FYS×3, B5 = FYS×4; then rest 120−(FYS×2) minutes.
  - From **B3**: protects against all damage that the matching elemental could do.
  - From **B4**: acts like ELEMENTARSKYDD.
  - From **B5**: acts like FRAMMANA/SKICKA BORT ELEMENTAR.
- **Vattendans (water dance)** — Telidor's first; lets you walk/dance on water. From B3 also FINNA VATTEN. Movement scales L1 (B1) → L5 (B5).
- **Elddans (fire dance)** — devised by master **Zeidon** (~440s) after fires ravaged Goiana's forests (war 403–425), to avoid fire damage. (Plus the other elemental dances; seven total.)
