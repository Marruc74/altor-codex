# Erebaltor: Barbia — *Siratsias vita formelbok* ("Siratsia's White Spellbook")

An adventure module for *Drakar och Demoner Expert*, written by Marcus Thorell, edited by Olle Sahlin, cover by Ken Kelly, published by Target Games / Äventyrsspel, Västerås 1989, set in the world of Ereb Altor. It needs the basic rules plus the Expert, Gigant (weather/mass-combat) and Monster books. It is two things at once: a **gazetteer** of the far-northern land of Barbia, and a long **main adventure** about stopping — or possibly helping — the immortal sorceress Siratsia. The single most distinctive design choice is that the GM secretly decides whether Siratsia is **evil or good**; the events play out almost identically either way, but the moral meaning of the ending flips completely. In his afterword the author calls Siratsia perhaps the most powerful and unusual NPC ever made for the game, and cites the film *Red Sonja* as an influence.

---

## The setting: Barbia

**The land.** Barbia is a large, cold, subarctic region in the extreme north of Ereb — isolated, easily forgotten, and steeped in hidden magic. Its people are freedom-loving nomadic barbarians who distrust the unknown and resist being ruled.

**History (in brief).** The world's dragons' great council long ago gave the dragon Alad-Arch a task before he went to sleep. Across the Stone and Bronze Ages, elves settled the enchanted Moonshine Forest, humans arrived as hunters and herders, and "svartfolk" (orcs, trolls and kin) warred endlessly from the highlands. A blue **Crystal Pillar** was discovered among the Wind Peaks; four **Priestesses of the Winds**, led by the mystic **Fadarim**, founded a temple around it and learned to read the winds — but only women can do so. When the priestesses died out, the last of them, Fadarim, built a golem guardian, took the **east-wind moonstone (pattern)** to her own grave, and so left the winds permanently "blind" to the east.

The southern empire **Jorpagna** rose and fell — devastated around 599 FO by an invasion of arm-long flesh-eating grasshoppers ("köttbitare"), which collapsed civilization into a stone-age "Dark Time." Centuries later emperors rebuilt the capital **Babor** and reoccupied the wind temple. Around 610 EO (the "present"), a just young emperor, **Yddris Yxhammer** ("Axe-hammer"), holds power but faces two wars: pirate raids from the Ardian Bay and a rebellion by the nomad coalition **Klan Tridor**. Tridor is secretly run by Yddris's banished enemy **Gehard Blodsgjute**, who has also seized the orc realm of **Edretch** through three half-orc magicians loyal to Siratsia. Mysterious "holes in nothing" have begun appearing in the northern wilds — Siratsia testing her ultimate spell.

**Peoples.** Highland north-barbarians (tall, blond, grey-eyed reindeer-herders and riders, shamanistic, blood-feuding); the traders and soldiers of Babor; scattered villagers (Nug, Sendul, Alder, Vesse, Utill); near-neanderthal cave folk in the Wind Peaks; and the warlike, near-unbeatable Habulon seafarers/pirates.

**Politics and the war.** Several merchant houses (Oliros, Felaroff, the honest old barbarian **Tarad**'s house, and the United Merchants under Memnum Iyi) jockey over legal and illegal trade. Klan Tridor claims all land north of the Wind Peaks; its three leaders are Gehard, **Relian** (who killed Yddris's father) and **Fridar** (who secretly plans to seize the throne himself). The plot's inciting event: adventurers plundered the joint grave-vault of the pirate Namik and emperor Les — a symbol of an old peace — reigniting the pirate war. Yddris offers a bounty for the grave-robbers, and Fadarim's priestesses, finding old legends, fear Siratsia still lives and recruit the player characters.

## The main adventure: *Siratsia's White Spellbook*

### Siratsia's backstory

Born 114 FO, the sole survivor of a wiped-out barbarian tribe, she was adopted as a baby by the hermit magician **Terendil**, who recognized her uncontrolled innate magic, named her Siratsia ("arch-priestess"), and trained her in mentalism and elementalism. She recorded everything in a **white spellbook** he gave her. Beautiful, brilliant and iron-willed, she surpassed him; when he died she grieved, planted a white rose on his grave, and went adventuring.

During those years she gathered companions and treasures, became betrothed to **prince Dejavin** (who gave her the necklace **Efert**), and lost him to war. In the ruins of fallen Jorpagna she found the demon-armband **Gharter** and a necromancer's spellbook, inside whose cover lay a strange **parchment** describing a ritual that banishes things into a *timeless dimension* where time stands still — leaving "nothing" behind. She turned it into a spell, and at 26 used it to **banish her own aging**, becoming eternally young, and resolved to use it to scour evil from a region — or the world — and build a personal paradise, setting herself against the gods.

She reanimated corpses as laborers, built her stone fortress **El-Glamor**, and learned to banish even living things. In 345 EO emperor Les attacked El-Glamor and "killed Siratsia" — actually a zombie-copy — sealing the dead under a blessed gold lid. The real Siratsia escaped. From the zombie-copy rose the vengeful wraith **Garam'Tuzach**. She then realized the winds can't perceive *illusions*, so disguised as "**Eloin**" she studied illusion magic in Babor, built a decoy illusion-fortress (**Dejavu**), and finally created her true home: **Skymningsros** ("Dusk Rose"), a near-real illusion powered by a lightning strike, which **teleports to a new location every midnight**, its energy bound to her necklace Efert. By 610 EO she is one month from completing 700 years of work; on **26 July**, Dusk Rose will return to its birthplace and a new era will begin. She has bewitched spies across the land.

### How it plays

The party are 4–6 powerful heroes recruited at **Fadarim's temple** by high priestess **Sien Nateli**, who warns the task will cost "blood, sweat and tears." They have ~42 days (they don't know the deadline at first). They study the temple's vast library for clues — a mix of truths, half-truths and lies hidden in cryptic verses and a cipher — which point them toward El-Glamor's ruins, the inn Ljunga äng, Terendil's house, Babor's academy (where "Eloin's" thank-you letter proves she survived), the cyclone of the dead east winds, and ultimately the moving fortress Dusk Rose. Two missing bounty-hunters, **Likon and Nadion**, can be rescued from Edretch's dungeons; they carry a map of possible fortress sites.

Siratsia's spies (notably the bewitched retired adventurer **Urban Stenskägge** and the traitor-priestess **Moirabia**) feed her information. Once discovered, she orders the orc-king **Jätterman** to besiege Fadarim with a svartfolk army; the winds give warning and fling attackers off the mountain. Siratsia herself may quietly read the party's minds, seize control of one of them, or visit disguised (e.g. as a wandering nun) offering "helpful" magic to win trust.

The goal is **not** to kill her (someone might find the book; she's easy to underestimate) but to **seize the white spellbook and destroy it** — and only **dragon-fire** can. So the party must reach the ancient dragon **Alad-Arch**, who has awaited this task since the dawn of time. An alternate solution is to destroy/recall the timeless dimension itself, which makes everything return — and her 700 banished years catch up with her, crumbling her to dust (this requires deadly astral-plane travel). A merciful fallback NPC, the mirror-mage **Tefurus Zaggzar**, can locate the fortress, but Siratsia bewitches him through his mirror so he betrays the party.

### The climax — Dusk Rose

The party must penetrate the teleporting fortress (its guardian is the demon-spirit **Letial**), survive its illusion-traps, and confront Siratsia in her tower as she reads the final ritual from the white spellbook. Whether they fight, talk, or are persuaded depends on the GM's secret choice of her alignment. They then bring the book to Alad-Arch, who asks if they have it; if yes, he incinerates it in yellow-orange fire that turns crackling blue-green, with an inhuman scream that echoes forever — "you have nothing more than honor to win."

### Alternate versions (the GM's toolkit)

The book offers many: a **good Siratsia** who only banished (not killed) her enemies and wrote the legends herself, giving a bittersweet ending where the heroes may have destroyed the world's one chance at an evil-free paradise ("How could evil swap roles with good?"); **Siratsia's guest** (she awaits a "prince of darkness" — a PC might impersonate him); **Fadarim's return** (teleport the ancient priestess out of her crystal grave to hold Siratsia in check); **the gods and Siratsia** (she's a self-made demigoddess who lulls both good and dark gods); and various outcomes if the heroes fail. Pointedly, there is **no treasure reward** — Yddris paying these near-equals would insult their honor; the reward is survival, experience, and moral reflection.

## Characters

**Siratsia** — The central figure; 726 years old but frozen at 26. Pale skin, almost unnaturally black hair (often in a page cut with a long thick tuft), large seductive black eyes that bend anyone to her will, porcelain-doll features, tall and slim, dressed in vivid high-quality silks. Normally wise, calm and soft-spoken; in a fight she becomes an intense, almost playful girl delighting in offensive spells, turning her tower into an inferno. She knows every spell, dislikes "the material," and keeps few but extremely powerful items. If her timeless dimension is destroyed she instantly ages 700 years. The GM may even have her be "not home" to spare the party.

**Sien Nateli** — Fadarim's high priestess, 31, black hair pinned with silver, white gown marked by three silver lines, an engraved open silver helmet. Wise, fair, a believer in shared power. A formidable mentalist (wind-control, weather-prediction, healing) and swordswoman. She recruits and guides the party and may lend out the temple's crystal amulets.

**Fadarim** — The legendary founder-priestess, now ~86 plus 1,230 years sealed in a crystal block in her grave atop the East Peak, holding the lost east-wind moonstone and thus knowing nearly everything. Potentially the equal or superior of Siratsia if freed.

**Terendil** — Siratsia's late adoptive father, a powerful magician; his journal (found in his ruined house) tells her origin in his own words.

**Tyclofon** — A bitter, lonely 292-year-old magician kept alive by life-extension magic; thin white hair, long cloud-like beard, deep-blue commanding eyes, black toga with copper-red embroidery. He rules from an ice-crystal throne atop the **cyclone of the dead east winds**, sees and hears via the winds, and resents the priestesses for excluding male priests. He witnessed Dusk Rose's creation and can only locate its birthplace.

**Tefurus Zaggzar ("the Mirror-man")** — A scatterbrained, kindly old astrologer-illusionist in a violet igloo-house with a violet rune-cloak and an ice-framed mirror. A merciful shortcut for the party — but Siratsia converts him through the mirror (his eyes turn black) and forces him to send them into the giant spider's grotto.

**Urban Stenskägge** — A burly, grey-bearded retired adventurer (once Gehard's brutal partner) who now advises young adventurers and rents rooms. Bewitched by Siratsia and made her local spymaster; he lured and captured the bounty-hunters and will try to do the same to the party, handing them to Edretch's orcs.

**Moirabia** — A bewitched Fadarim priestess and Siratsia's mole, tasked with blocking anyone researching Siratsia. A skilled thief-mentalist with a charged diadem; she'll exploit a siege to do Siratsia "dirty work" against the party.

**Kejsar Yddris av Dendor** — The 41-year-old warrior-emperor; quick, intelligent, dangerous in battle, said to be the most battle-capable emperor in Barbia's history. Like his late father Dendor he prefers compromise to violence, fights at the front, and has learned to trust no one. Spends ~60% of his time fighting Klan Tridor, 25% on the pirates, 15% in Babor.

**Medos** — Yddris's current best friend and ally, a nomad warlord famous for his huge two-handed war-axe, riding a giant horse. Undecided about submitting to the empire; Yddris intends to make him his right-hand man.

**Gehard Blodsgjute ("Blood-pourer")** — Once a big, strong adventurer (Urban's old partner), now thin, wiry, snobbish and consumed by bitterness and revenge. Usurped the throne when Dendor died (578 EO), ran "Barbia's reign of terror," was beaten and banished by Yddris (591), and has returned to secretly lead Klan Tridor. His son is **Azzarad**.

**Relian Dendorsbane** — Old battle-veteran, greying and arthritic, but a born strategist; one of Tridor's three leaders. Unlike the others he is sensitive and good-hearted and fights honorably at the front.

**Fridar** — The third Tridor leader; an officer who, unlike Relian, does not lead from the front. Secretly plans to kill Gehard and take the imperial title for himself.

**King Jätterman of Edretch** — A huge svartnisse (possibly with svartalv blood), the bewitched orc-king controlled by Siratsia's three half-orc magicians.

**Utangaz, Gyzel & Kirilir** — Those three half-orc magicians, installed in Edretch as Siratsia's puppeteers and loyal spies.

**Likon & Nadion** — The two missing bounty-hunters, ninja-like fighters in identical black armor (katana, throwing stars, karate). Lured via Urban into Edretch's cells, set for execution on 25 July; Nadion carries the map of likely fortress sites and will give it to the party.

**Reselves** — A serene, silver-blond bard in sky-blue, found in an annexed pocket-dimension that Siratsia turned into part of her fortress. He plays an enchanting flute at the triple-sunset and each night battles three colored solar-demons.

**Juggo & Almiak** — Optional companion NPCs. Juggo is a lone surviving warrior of a relief party sent to aid Fadarim; he takes orders literally. Almiak is a proud, strikingly beautiful 28-year-old scout-tracker in green, armed with an ornate magic dagger.

**Garam'Tuzach** and **Letial** — see *Creatures*.

## Places

**Fadarim's Temple** — A round granite fortress-temple perched on a ledge of one of the five Wind Peaks, reached by perilous serpentine paths and a swaying suspension bridge. Its octagonal-roofed central hall holds the glowing blue **Crystal Pillar**, which the priestesses use to read and steer the winds. Below lies a vast cavern with a bottomless underground lake (home of the sleeping Crystal Guardian) where the pillar continues underground into four wind-facing niches — the eastern one empty. The temple holds dormitories, meditation rooms, a steam-bath, a 200-metre stair to a dizzying lookout balcony above the clouds, secret passages, and a circular **library of ~50,000 ancient books** protected by an anti-magic shield — the one thing Siratsia won't attack magically, lest she reveal she still lives.

**Babor** — The granite capital, a confusing maze of spires and towers ringed by purple heather, its vulnerable harbor now guarded by war-galleys against pirates. Hireable guides; an academic library (open only recently) where, deep in a storeroom chest, the old *Encyclopedia Illusionia* hides Eloin's signed thank-you letter — proof Siratsia survived El-Glamor and a pointer to Dejavu.

**Ljunga äng** — A three-storey inn on the heather plains within sight of Babor, where the bounty-hunters were last seen being lured away by a black-armored knight.

**El-Glamor** — Siratsia's first fortress, now razed flat. A hidden gold lid covers the catacomb "**Well of the Dead**" where her 600 fallen warriors lie (many bricked into the walls). Inside: animated rotting skeletons that regrow if not fully dismembered, grave chambers, and the lair of the wraith Garam'Tuzach with its black marble altar and secret compartment.

**Dejavu** — A large hexagonal decoy fortress with a tall tower, built of an eerie blue-shimmering material at the source of the Vatnatra; a deliberate dead end to waste the party's time.

**Skymningsros (Dusk Rose)** — Siratsia's true home, a near-real illusion that teleports every midnight. White marble with gold scrollwork; a gauntlet of traps: an entry arch that heats metal weapons until they melt, illusory floating light-orbs that whisk people away via a LJUSVÄG spell to a false tower platform (whose taunting note dissolves the illusion and drops victims to a real fall), a throne room with the silk mantle and mithril crown she means to wear at her coronation, experiment halls, a memory-museum, and Reselves's annexed dimension.

**Reselves's Dimension** — A vast living forest under three torus-shaped suns (red, green, blue) whose combined light is white. At sunset a bewitching flute plays; beyond lies a lifeless stone landscape where, each night, a giant demon-castle disgorges three winged solar-demons that Reselves fights until dawn.

**Edretch** — The svartfolk cave-realm: multi-level natural tunnels reeking of orcs and trolls, with barracks, a guest-hall, a smithy worked by slaves, wolf-kennels, a great bronze alarm bell at the main gate (captained by **Bebek**), and the cells (21a) holding the doomed bounty-hunters.

**Terendil's House** — Siratsia's overgrown, decaying childhood home at the western edge of the Grenor forest (deep in dangerous Klan Tridor land). In the garden, on Terendil's grave, grows a **white rose** watered by grief-tears that should be dead but lives; whoever picks it is bound to it for life — let it die and they lose their soul. The teleport-door rooms inside disorient intruders.

**The Cyclone of the Dead East Winds** — Tyclofon's home: a permanent whirlwind of trapped east winds with his ice-crystal throne at the summit.

**Kettael** — A two-mile crater holding a blue lake and a crystal-white island ("the Moonstone") — the remnant of a fallen asteroid that is the source of the temple's wind-patterns. A **sphinx** guards it with three riddles; magicians chip pieces off for new patterns.

**Ghartov's Grotto** — A spider-webbed cave system (a former secret mithril mine) inhabited by giant spiders and, at its heart, the colossal spider Ghartov; the treasure room behind her holds coin, seven mithril ingots, scrolls, and the helmet **Genomskådaren**.

**The Soldemons' Castle / the lifeless stone landscape** — within Reselves's dimension (see above).

## Items & weapons

**The White Spellbook (*Siratsias vita formelbok*)** — Pure white, glowing palely when open and radiating grave-cold, with fine gold scrollwork on the cover. It contains everything about her "nothing spell," with the original parchment tucked at the back. Nearly indestructible — only Alad-Arch's fire can unmake it. It is itself a deadly enemy, sealed with NEXUS sigils that, e.g., fire lightning (BLIXT E3) at all living things within ten metres.

**Efert (necklace of magic)** — Several glittering silver chains set with diamonds around a beautiful ruby that, looked at closely, holds a tiny red rose. A gift from prince Dejavin, later enhanced. Grants thrice-daily teleport, invisibility and invulnerability, plus a constant "spell-quickness" letting Siratsia cast three quick spells per round. Crucially it also holds the **NEXUS that sustains Dusk Rose** — destroy the necklace and the fortress is destroyed too.

**Gharter (demon-armband)** — Gold, traced with an X-pattern, fused with a demon (Gharter) banished from chaos and once bound by the necromancer Tyllut, who died in the köttbitare invasion. Worn on Siratsia's right arm; because she poses as evil it grants her enormous boosts (roughly +23 STY, +20 PSY, plus STO/FYS/SMI/INT) and demonic regeneration, speed and strength-drain.

**Hosori (armband)** — A simple leather strap with four silver balls that she made herself; seals to her arm (FÖRSEGLA E5), its true power a steel-strike (STÅLSLAG E4) with NEXUS, plus the ability to turn invisible and backup escape spells (teleport, fire, KNÄCKA) for use if captured.

**Pary III (staff)** — Dark-brown wood with a gold ring at the top and a gold knob at the base (her third such staff; two were destroyed). It floats and parries three attacks per round, strikes for 3T3+3, paralyses and can knock down those it hits; BV 35, with NEXUS and FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3.

**Siratsia's elf-cloak (*alvkåpa*)** — A light-blue, silver-shimmering cloak won (92 FO) for saving elf pilgrims in the Moonshine Forest. Better than plate armor: it absorbs damage, makes her hard to hit, carries a NEXUS-powered KNÄCKA against svartfolk weapons, and outdoors boosts protection and camouflage while shifting color to match the forest.

**Beres's Flute** — A simple wooden flute (fetched for her by a vampire servant) that lowers the cost and raises the power of her harmonism spells and holds a reserve charge of psychic energy.

**Bardados (broadsword)** — A magical sword forged in the dwarves' first steel-forge; by 610 EO it pins a corpse to a wall in Dusk Rose's hidden treasure room, guarded by the undead **Sarvadas**, who reawakens if it's removed and can only be harmed by it. The blade can wound undead spirits and counts as enchanted (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E2 initially), though its bonus degrades with each hit on the undead. Notably, it deals **full** damage to the otherwise resistant wraith Garam'Tuzach.

**Genomskådaren ("the See-through")** — A tarnished silver helmet with gold inlay found in Ghartov's hoard; its wearer sees through all normal illusions.

**Reselves's swords** — The **Sunset Sword**, made of light and unparriable, called only at sundown; and the **Multicolored Sword**, used only at night to slay the three solar-demons (growing stronger as each falls).

**The Crystal Skull** — In Garam'Tuzach's altar compartment: a soot-blackened human skull that proves to be near-transparent crystal, with two flawless diamonds that fit as eyes; nearly priceless and useful as a magical "host" for a soul.

**The east-wind moonstone** — The pattern Fadarim removed to her grave; the reason the winds are blind to the east, and the object of an optional sub-adventure.

*(Generic gear — ordinary swords, spears, bows, shields, armor — uses standard rules and isn't catalogued here.)*

## Creatures

**Garam'Tuzach** — A terrible wraith risen from Siratsia's destroyed zombie-copy; a black shape darker than night in a tattered cloak, eyes burning with hatred of all living things. Wields a two-handed sword that does extra "unholy" damage to the living, can terrify and dominate with a steel-gaze, drains STY/PSY/FYS by touch, and shatters weapons that strike it. It takes only a third damage from magic and magic weapons, half from holy weapons — but **full** damage from the sword Bardados and from cold magic. In daylight it is badly weakened. It will not rest until Siratsia is punished.

**Letial — guardian of Dusk Rose** — A spirit-demon Siratsia summoned, as loyal as a familiar. Normally a **white panther with glowing red eyes**, he can also take the form of a great **white eagle** or a more human **white wraith**, all red-eyed. He senses souls and magic flows, phases through walls and floors, and has a mental link to Siratsia. He has three "shapes": kill one and he reforms in another; only when all three die is Letial dead. He stalks, observes and frightens intruders before alerting his mistress.

**The Crystal Guardian (Kristallväktaren)** — A mighty golem Fadarim carved from the base of the Crystal Pillar and sank into the bottomless temple lake. It rises from the water to destroy anyone who truly threatens the pillar — a formidable obstacle to anyone trying to topple it.

**Ghartov** — A colossal giant spider lairing in the old mithril-mine grotto, drooling white venom; she attacks without mercy but won't leave her caves unless forced. Lesser young giant spiders share the tunnels.

**The Kettael Sphinx** — Guardian of the crystal moonstone island, posing three riddles to those who approach.

**The Solar-Demons** — Three huge winged demons (red, green, blue) in Reselves's dimension; each night they steal the three suns and each night Reselves slays them, one color of sun rising as each falls.

**Alad-Arch** — The vast ancient **white dragon** of the eastern Wind Peaks, described as a frost-covered mountain that lifts into the sky, his scale-and-jewel armor glittering like ice crystals. He is not the rampaging kind; he has waited since the dawn of time for the god-given task of destroying the white spellbook, and sits on a hoard so immense even he seems small beside it. (A variant pits him against a **black dragon** Siratsia sends, for a dragon-versus-dragon battle.)

**Other beasts** — The wilds hold the sabre-toothed "kroksabelkatt," wolves, reindeer, mammoths and woolly rhinos; the historical flesh-eating grasshoppers ("köttbitare"); a petrified troll along the Lerån; and the usual svartfolk roster (standard/experienced/officer orcs, svartalver, svartnissar, trolls, war-wolves, zombies) with the orc captain **Bebek** wielding a two-handed axe.
