# Ereb Altor – Kampanjbok (Drakar och Demoner)

*Reference summary of the setting book for the continent of Ereb on the world of Altor.*
*Years are given as **fO** (före Odo / before Odo) and **eO** (efter Odo / after Odo). The "present day" of the book is year **610 eO**.*

> **Scope:** This book describes the continent **Ereb** and its surroundings — maps of language families, peoples, climate zones, trade routes and religions, plus history, gods, the calendar, languages, dark magic and heraldry. The GM (SL = Spelledare) is free to ignore or change anything.

## 1. History of Altor (Altors historia)

### Eras at a glance
| Era | Years | Key points |
|---|---|---|
| Förhistorien (Prehistory) | before ~9000 fO | Earlier doomed races; gods wiped out all intelligent life. Dragons are the only survivors of this age. |
| Stenåldern (Stone Age) | 9000–3500 fO | Neolithic-level; hunting, fishing, gathering; only local societies. |
| Bronsåldern (Bronze Age) | 3500–1500 fO | Three high cultures rise: **Melukha, Golwynda, Yndar**. |
| Järnåldern (Iron Age) | from 1500 fO | Dwarves smith iron in Grynnerbergen; iron secret spreads. |
| Kolonisationstiden (Colonization) | 1500–1080 fO | Colonies founded around the Copper Sea; Berendien & Erebos born. |
| Härarnas tid (Age of Armies) | 1080–950 fO | Power struggles around the Copper Sea. |
| Kejsartiden (Imperial Age) | 950–600 fO | The **Empire of Jorpagna** dominates central Ereb. |
| Den tredje konfluxen (Third Conflux) | 599–598 fO | Catastrophe; fall of Jorpagna and of Yndar. |
| Mörkertiden (Dark Age) | 599–1 fO | Civilization declines; the **Jori** language becomes common tongue. |
| Statsbildningarnas tid (Age of States) | 0–610 eO | New nations: Zorakin, Kardien, Berendien, Erebos, etc. |

### Prehistory & creation
- The world was once peopled by other races that "degenerated"; the gods agreed to **wipe out all intelligent life** and start over.
- Between **10,000–9,000 fO** many catastrophes left giant craters (e.g. **Kargomväggen** on Palamux in Trakorien), petrified forests and dried sea-beds.
- **Dragons** are the only surviving intelligent beings from prehistory.
- **Elves, humans and dwarves** were created around **9,000 fO**.

### The three Bronze Age high cultures
- **Melukhakulturen** – island of Melukha ("the blooming pleasure garden"). Humans **and elves** live in peaceful abundance. Worship **Moder Jord** (Mother Earth) and a pantheon of peaceful gods; developed music & architecture; adopted metal late (still trades for it). Always a **matriarchy**.
- **Golwyndakulturen** – around **Golwynda's freshwater sea**. Farming & trade culture (boom ~4000 fO). Legend of hero **Palofar** and water-god **Enki** (planted the divine grain instead of baking it → fields gave triple harvest). Mastered **bronze-casting**; founded **elementarmagin (elemental magic), symbolism and mentalism**; strong interest in mysticism, astrology and philosophy.
- **Yndarkulturen** – island of **Yndar**. Originally peaceful humans, then invaded by the **margyler** (from SE Akrogal). A cruel, proud, scholarly high culture; foundations of **mathematics, abstract writing, war-craft, torture — and of nekromantik (necromancy) & spiritism**.

### Other Bronze Age culture areas
- **Södra Akrogal** – steppe nomads who first domesticated animals (silky steppe-horses, war-spiders/*vargiller*, *enhornsbufflar*); spread herding to Golwynda; mounted nomads later devastate Ereb.
- **Efaro** – jungles of NW Farbanda; hunter/warrior cultures built on **slavery**; seafarers and craftsmen; magic was a form of **shamanism** that influenced many later schools.
- **Norra Erebs barbarer** – NE Ereb forests; organized alliances and large villages (200–300); worship **nature gods** plus manly ideals of strength & honor; great superstition and respect for elf-folk.

### Non-human races
- **Moder Jord** created all servant-races at once and spread them widely.
- **Skogsalver** (forest elves) most numerous; elf culture is contemplative/passive and never shaped history as much as humans/dwarves did. Forest-elf centre at **Landori** (between Nida-bergen and Berendien).
- **Grott- & silveralver** (cave/silver) live mostly underground; **gråalver** (grey elves) are seafarers crucial to **spreading bronze**; later worn down by conflict with humans.
- **Grynnerbergens dvärgkultur** – dwarf kingdom that farmed **klipptryffel** (an underground nutritious fungus). Mastered **copper-casting ~3700 fO, bronze ~3500 fO**; *vättar* (goblins) stole and sold the secret to humans at Golwynda.
- **Polarkulturerna**:
  - **Isherrarna** (Ice Lords) – north pole; beings of condensed energy, ~10 m tall blue shimmering anthropomorphs; feed only on polar energy. Worshipped by Orghin/Sanithsid ice-barbarians.
  - **Salamandrarna** (Salamanders) – south pole; reptilian energy-beings; telekinesis/levitation/telepathy. Worshipped by desert nomads, dinosauriderna on Samkarna, etc.
  - Their **eternal struggle** drives Altor's climate (ice spreads when Ice Lords win; heat when Salamanders win).
- **Drakarnas arkipelag** – remote islands where Altor's dragons gather ~once per millennium.
- **De rasslande rasernas arkipelag (Maminira me delema)** – giant intelligent **insects/insectoids**.

### Iron Age & Colonization
- Dwarves smithed **iron** in Grynnerbergen ~**1700 fO**; war-prince **Targald Karskfot** seized power at 60 and drove humans to the plains, but was captured and forced to reveal the iron-craft (dwarves kept their **alloy secrets**, e.g. legeringskonst, hidden).
- After 1500 fO colonists left the overpopulated high cultures:
  - **Furgia & Yndar** – Furgia fought margyler invasions (margyler held it until ~800 fO).
  - **Sombatze & Efaro** – mixed cultures of mysticism.
  - **Krun & Hynsolge** – Krun's "golden age" after defeating the dwarves; founded colonies along the Copper Sea's north coast (→ **Berendien, Erebos**). Allied with barbarians (granted them **Hynsolge**).
- **Krunska kriget** – Hynsolge vs Krun; **hynsolger** won decisively at **Grivela** (battle of **Jorpagna**, 1065 fO) → built capital **Jorpagna**, dominating Ereb until the 500s fO.
- **Skövlingen av Sombatze** – Efaro troops sacked Sombatze; chaos across N. Soluna to ~1000 fO.
- **Melukhas fall** – 1040 fO seafarers of **Morelvidyn** raided Sivoa then took Melukha; civil war ("nudelhärvan").

### Imperial Age (Kejsartiden 950–600 fO)
- **Kejsardömet Jorpagna** dominated central Ereb. At the **rådsmöte i Grafferburg (912 fO)** emperor **Thamsul** seized and executed the barbarian chiefs → long wars against forest-folk only deepened their hatred.
- Empire expanded **1025–710 fO** over today's Zorakin, Berendien, Felicien and west to Mirelien/Kardien. Never directly ruled **Trakorien** or **Caddo**. Today's common tongue descends from Jorpagna's language (**Jori**).
- **De akrogaliska nomaderna** (~750–600 fO) mastered hippogriffs; raided Jorpagna & Yndar.
- **Sanithsids upphöjelse** (600s) – dwarves crossed the ice; **isbarbarerna** on Orghin allied with Ice Lords; the Sanithsid ice-folk made a "holy alliance" (617 fO) and now ignore their neighbours.

### Third Conflux & Dark Age
- A **konflux** = a moment when the stars align ominously, triggering world-shaking events. **Den tredje konfluxen (599–598 fO)** was feared across Altor.
- **Jorpagnas undergång** – Summer **599 fO** a swarm of **köttbitare** (flesh-eating grasshoppers from the Samkarna marshes, via **Maminira me delema**) darkened the sky and halved central Ereb's population in weeks. The Empire collapsed permanently.
- **Den yndariska depressionen** – Yndar destroyed itself through reckless magic: the city of **Krau-ki** vanished and was moved by the gods to the island **Marjura**; a punitive expedition to **Slimpaku** unleashed the **slempukar** (creatures) that razed Kamsun and Yndar.
- **Mörkertiden (599–1 fO)** – writing and metalwork were lost in many places; slowly regions rebuilt; Jorpagna's tongue (**jori**) split into the common dialects spoken almost everywhere.

## 2. Beyond Ereb (Bortom Ereb)

### Samkarna (continent SW of Ereb)
- Wet, swampy, ever-rainier toward the south; teeming, deadly life.
- **Drakryggen** – Altor's highest mountain chain (peaks **>15,000 m**); blocks western winds, keeping Ormsjön/W. Soluna dry. Its only pass is **Geardons gap** (constant hurricane).
- **Morelvidyn** – N. coast; shifting pirate kingdoms; little respect for life; sells slaves from the interior; good but undisciplined sailors. Home also to **brunankor & svarta piratankor**.
- **Sivoa** – former forest-elf realm; elves still inhabit its noble-wood forests.
- **Melukha** – paradise island; people live well off hunting & nature, seek neither gold nor power; great hospitality; worship **"Gudinnan"** (a mother-figure, not the elves' Moder Jord) and household spirits; mummify and keep their dead at home; **matriarchal**, villages led by old women; war seen as a contemptible but necessary "men's" job.
- **Mosgilak** – ruled by **reptile-men** (more advanced than Ereb's lizard-folk); worship the south-pole salamanders; enslave & eat outsiders.
- **Paringuabukten** – fed by rivers Ulimen, Ghaldoil, Maur; seasonal blossom-nectar makes the bay sweet.
- **Morginnerskogen** – gigantic rainforest from Morelvidyn to Paringuabukten and Drakryggen, fading into the **Juronaträsken**.
- **Jurona & Blindhavet** – southern swamps (mangrove → giant ferns); huge amphibians/lizards; rumored hidden dinosaur-realms; fog-shrouded sea with man-eating algae.
- **Drakryggen & Krystalerna** – W. slopes hold isolated alien realms; the **Krystalerna** lakes are so clear the bottom is fully visible.

### Ormsjön (sea between Samkarna and Soluna)
- Weak winds; travel by twin-banked slave-galleys.
- **"Vägen bort"** – northern inlet; water still drinkable; flows strongly N→S, so sailing N requires a rare hard southerly (**RhabdoRhinn** wind, ~once in 5 years), when a **drug/poison/gem armada** sails up to **Krilloan**.
- **Faryngia / "Världsvalget"** – giant whirlpool from Geardons-gap winds; killer of careless sailors.
- **Nivralerna** – islands; trade-friendly kingdom **Niferland**; said to be "the world's edge."
- **Södra Ormsjön / Pokti** – patchwork of warring petty states.
- **Horyns gap, Maar Lacra & Amargoh** – southern outflow to deadly seas; **Maar Lacra** = "the Ocean of Tears" (no rain, no wind, murderous heat); the dead island **Amargoh** is home to titans who eat stone fossils (and the occasional human).

### Soluna
- **Efaro** – NW Soluna; old, proud, tragic; eight feuding tribes; sell rivals as slaves to Morelvidyn/Felicien; rich in ore and good soil. People are tall, dark, war-like and artistic.
- **Sombatze** – one of the original Golwynda culture-areas; survives by clever diplomacy; source of many original crops; ruled by rich landowners under an elected **stormogul**.
- **Traxilme** – once-blooming desert plains; rainbow-shifting magnetic terrain; poison gathered for deadly brews; ruins haunted by *gastar* and demons.
- **Solunas inre** – ominous ruins of vanished civilizations; forbidden poisonous plains.
- **Thelgul** – priest-state worshipping the heavenly bodies; ancient rival of Niferland.

### Golwyndahavet (the great freshwater sea)
- Between Ereb, Soluna and Akrogal; bulges upward as a "glass mountain" of water pressure; ~**250 m higher** than the Copper Sea (so no ship can sail between them). Three outflows:
  - **Raas Narram** – huge waterfall down to the Copper Sea (ruined giant lock-stair built by emperor Garhues of Krun).
  - **Demontungan** – water tunnels through the **stone bridge** linking Ereb and Akrogal, then jets out over Masevabukten; worshipped as the god **Okasu**.
  - **Grelgifallen** – lower but broader eastern falls; the island **Habete Grelge** (ancient magician-ruled principality) sits within them.

### De östra haven (the Eastern Seas)
- **Glashavet** – translucent razor-sharp reefs; nearly impassable.
- **Valgussjön** – vast unknown waters; islands of strange folk — titans, cyclops, sphinxes; reputed site of the **Drakarnas Arkipelag** (dragons' breeding grounds).
- **Yndar** – the "evil island," cradle of **necromancy**; cursed city **Krau-Ki** (moved by the gods to **Marjura**).

### Akrogal (continent east of Ereb)
- Mostly nomads & wandering hunters; little firm statehood.
- **Demontungan** (the stone bridge) – controlled by the **nidlänningar** on the west side; heavy caravan traffic; town **Bahaz** in Masevabukten.
- **Kalmurri** – ruler said to be the **moon-goddess's son**; at his death, holy troops seek his reincarnation (a white-eyed newborn); life swings with the moon's phases.
- **Furgia** – old realm of preserved knightly/chivalric ideals; capital **Ketoriam** at the mouth of the Harytis; archenemy of Kalmurri; target of "conversion" crusades from western Ereb.
- **Kamsun** – distant, nearly unknown; exports rare goods (e.g. **kamsunsk papir**, paper used for fine garments).
- **Vildmarkerna** – steppes & endless northern forests of nomad/hunter "indian-like" folk.

## 3. Altor's Universe (Altors universum)

- The accepted cosmology is the **Dykiska världsbilden** (proposed by Furgian astronomer **Dykos Barrha**, 612 fO).
- **Altor** is a perfect sphere at the center of the universe, diameter ~**1,000 mil** (≈ 4,000 km; cf. Earth ~1,250 mil).
- **North pole** = paralysing cold; **south pole** = murderous heat; **Ereb** lies just north of the equator with a mild temperate climate (year-mean ≈ ±0 °C). **Jih-pun** lies at the same latitude on the far side.
- Atmosphere thins with height: trouble breathing at ~15,000 m, near-impossible above ~20,000 m. The floating elf-island **Caranor** (silver-elf home) drifts in the upper air.

### The gods (Gudarna)
- Gods created Altor and play it as an eternal "chess game."
- **Förgänglighetens Lag** (Law of Transience): nothing on Altor may last forever. If anything eternal touched Altor's surface, the Law would break and the world end — so **gods cannot act directly**; they work through **halvgudar (demigods)**, **Gudagåvor** and **Sändebud**. Even the most evil gods uphold the Law (to keep an outlet for their evil).
- There are infinite gods; every faith worships partial images of the real ones. The eternal struggle is **Ljus (light/good)** vs **Mörker (cold/violence)**, both straining to claim as much of the planet as possible.

### Heavenly bodies (Himlakropparna)
Twelve observable, sphere-shaped bodies: **nine small moons, the sun, Raukhra, and the fixstjärnesfär**.
- **Luna / Lûnia** – largest moon, the only one visible year-round; ~24 h orbit; one dark side & one shining "face"; her light gives no warmth and is protected by the cold-gods.
- **De övriga månarna** – eight smaller moons (some only visible by telescope); several rotate in sync with Altor causing permanent eclipse-shadows; **three known "black holes"** (S. Soluna, far W. Västerhavet, easternmost Akrogal).
- **Solen** – the Life-giver; exactly **24 h** orbit, just outside Luna's; the prize fought over by heat-gods (push it north) and cold-gods (push it south).
- **Fixstjärnorna** – a sphere of black material studded with stars at the universe's edge; bits fall as **shooting stars**; great heroes are said to join them.
- **Raukhra** – the **evil** body; blood-red cold light; highly eccentric orbit nearing Altor a few times per millennium; a chaos-power (it nearly struck Altor during the Third Conflux as **Röda Varse**); worshipped only by dark-cultists.
- **Shakhora** – a hidden black moon, visible only to its cultists; thought to orbit just outside the sun.
- **Kometer** – fearful omens; never return.
- **Azuria (Blå Månen)** – a body said by sea-folk myth to have once given night-light; destroyed in the **First Conflux**.

## 5. Conflicts in Ereb (year 610 eO)

War is good for adventurers (mercenary work, plunder) but makes the land unsafe (no law, roaming brigands). The three main flashpoints:

### Feliciska piratkriget (Felician Pirate War)
- Felician shipowners buy royal **letters of marque** to legally raid "enemy" merchant ships; king **Kuthegil Halvskägg** issued ever more against Erebos vessels.
- Erebos now sails in **armed convoys**, an excuse to openly rearm.
- **Sides:** Felicien + Hynsolge **vs** Erebos + Berendien + Caddo.
- Wildcard: **Caddo** vs **Erebos** are bitter rivals; Caddo could switch sides at any time. Erebos's partners Kardien & Zorakin stay out (weak fleets).

### Ransarderröjet (The Ransard Threat)
- Aggressive **Ransard** seeks revenge on Trakorien; has annexed neighbour **Alerien** and arms for an offensive war.
- Few allies; superb, well-equipped army with flying **gripar (griffins)** that panic enemy cavalry/infantry (note: undisciplined troops –1, min 0.5; griffins make disciplined troops count as undisciplined).
- The **Palinor crusade** (every 100 years) is due in **610** — risk of a three-front war (W: Trakorien, N: alver, E: Palinor).
- Trakorien is poorly prepared (low readiness, untrained army) and may rely on mercenaries; can expect moral support from Copper-Sea lands. Alver dislike offensive war.

### Nidländska reningen (The Nidland Purge)
- **Nidland**, ruled by the evil magician **Vicotnik**, grows hostile; fanatic obedience to its leader; prophecy says Vicotnik's crusade ("a dozen-dozen years" after his arrival) sets out in **612**.
- Weakness: little iron/weapons in the land (probably bought from Akrogal).
- Likely target is **Cereval** (Vicotnik likely avoids the trolls of **Cer-skogen** and the **drakmästarna**). **Krun** (south of Nidland) has no readiness at all.
- Hynsolge is tangled in its own civil war.

## 6. Climate (Klimat)

### Five (six) climate zones
| Zone | Where | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Arktiskt** | Orghin, Sanithsid (far north) | Eternal biting cold, snow, ice; only ice-barbarians cope unaided. |
| **Subarktiskt** | Jorduashur, Barbia, Ereb–Orghin straits | Cold, harsh, windy; few snow-free months. |
| **Tempererat kustklimat** | Trakorien, Ransard | Cold wet windy winters, mild–sometimes hot summers. |
| **Tempererat inlandsklimat** | Dominant zone in Ereb | Yearly mean a few degrees above zero; moderate rain. |
| **Kopparhavsklimatet** | around the Copper Sea | Most pleasant: warm sunny summers, short mild winters (more rain/sleet than snow); ideal for shipping. |
| **Subtropiskt** | S. Soluna, Samkarna | Long dry periods + rains; year-mean ~+10 °C; cold nights. |

### Average temperature by month (°C)

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Arktiskt | -30 | -30 | -20 | -15 | -10 | -5 | ±0 | -3 | -7 | -10 | -15 | -25 |
| Subarktiskt | -20 | -20 | -15 | -5 | +5 | +7 | +10 | +7 | -3 | -7 | -10 | -15 |
| Temp. kust | -15 | -10 | -5 | ±0 | +7 | +10 | +15 | +10 | +3 | ±0 | -5 | -10 |
| Temp. inland | -10 | -10 | -5 | +5 | +15 | +17 | +20 | +17 | +7 | +3 | ±0 | -5 |

| Subtropiskt | +10 | +10 | +12 | +15 | +17 | +20 | +30 | +25 | +20 | +17 | +15 | +12 |

- **Daily temp:** roll **1T20–10**, apply to the table value. At month start/end, use the mean of the two months. Night drops 5–30 °C below.

### Precipitation grades (Nederbörd)
- **MN** Massiv, **KN** Kraftig, **LN** Lätt, **MU** Mulet, **VM** Växlande molnighet, **KL** Klart.
- Roll **1T6** (add +1 in winter) on the climate column. Below freezing → snow (2% chance of hail). If any precipitation falls in summer, **5% chance of thunder (åska)**.

### Wind grades (Vind), temp modifier
- **Storm (ST)** –10 · **Kuling (KU)** –5 · **Hård bris (HB)** 0 · **Normal bris (NB)** 0 · **Lätt bris (LB)** 0 · **Stiltje (SE)** +5.

### Weather effects (rules of thumb)
- No bow-fire in Regn, Hård bris, Kuling or Storm.
- Becalmed ships sit still; in stiltje subtract 5 (not 10) from the temp roll.
- In storm, a ship drifts; each hour an on-deck PC must pass a **Sjökunnighet** check or be washed overboard (unless below deck / lashed down).
- Temp **>+35 or <–25**: –10% to all skills needing body movement (–10% more per further 5°); negated by *besvärjelser* or extreme clothing.
- Precipitation **doubles CL** on Smyga/Gömma sig; Storm/Kuling/Kraftig/Massiv **halves CL** on Lyssna.

## 8. Calendar & Time (Tideräkning)

### Den Arcivalska tideräkningen
- Devised 212 eO by exark **Arcival** of Den Lysande Vägen; used wherever that faith is state religion (and a few others). The Ereb Altor box's timelines use it.
- **12 months × 30 days** + a 5-day **festeveckan** at year's end (celebrating Odo's birth & the new year).
- In use in: **Kardien, Zorakin, Klomellien, Magilre, Trakorien, Jorpagna, Berendien, Felicien, Hynsolge, Erebos, Caddo** (month names may differ locally but mean the same).
- **Ransard, Jorduashur, Barbia, Cereval** use a **moon-based** calendar; **Nidland** uses Vicotnik's; alver & dvärgar have their own.

## 10. Religion

The book details **Den Lysande Vägen** (state faith of **Zorakin, Kardien, Caddo, Berendien, Magilre** and much of **Klomellien**). Other faiths are sketched with their countries.

### Den Lysande Vägen (The Shining Way)
- **History:** ~600 years ago **Odo**, son of a potter in **Arno (Caddo)**, received a revelation from the sun-god **Etin** at age 27 and preached the Lysande Vägen. It spread across the Copper Sea; took firm root in **Zorakin & Kardien** in the 100s.
  - **Den stora Schismen (278):** dispute over whether magic was compatible with Odo's teaching. **281:** exarks of Arno and Ekeborg condemned each other; the church split into two rival organizations, weakening it.
- **Lära (doctrine):** **Etin** is the one true God, sun-god, all-seeing. The dead are judged by Etin: the best serve at his side; the worst go to **helvetet** of chaos/darkness; the middle endure a long purgatory.
- **Skapelsen:** Etin filled the void with light, made Altor and humans in his image; lesser god-beings can only **modify** creation.
- **Buden (the six commandments):**
  1. Etin är störst!
  2. Missbruka inte Läran!
  3. Hedra din familj!
  4. Begå icke grymma handlingar!
  5. Stjäl icke!
  6. Håll ditt ord!
- **Sedvänjor:** Odo's words are recorded in **Gyllene Boken** (written in **dalkiska**); symbol is the **sun**; greatest festival is **Soldansen** (summer solstice). Entry via the **Solceremonin**.
- **Organisation:**
  - Led by the **Exarken** (pope) in **Ekeborg**, with a curia of six **tots-kerîgasser**.
  - Hierarchy: **kerisât** (led by a **kerisgass**/archbishop) → **bontisât** (stift, led by a **bontisål**/bishop) → församling (parish, led by a **riark**).
  - **Finances:** funded by a 10% church tithe; church & clergy tax-exempt → **enormously rich** (lavish cathedrals, luxurious leaders) — which has eroded trust.
  - **Maktposition:** very strong in **Zorakin & Kardien** (kings & nobles are members); official in Erebos & Berendien but weaker. By law the four kings must profess the faith.
- **Den Ljusa Handen** – the church's **inquisition** (not on Caddo); instituted by exark **Zegedorn III, 314**; polices doctrine & hunts heresy.
- **Odöda & magi:** undead are an abomination to be destroyed; on **Caddo** all magic (incl. alchemy) is forbidden except for the god-gifted; on the mainland magic is "neutral." Spells deemed clearly anti-Lära (may only be used against the unfaithful): **Kontrollera varelse, Blindhet, Epilepsi, Fruktan, Mörker, Förbanna vapen, Mörkerväg, Illusion, Fata Morgana**; all **necromancy** is forbidden outright.

### Heresies (Kätterier) & dissenters
- **Caddism** – holds all magic ungodly and to be banned; forbidden on the mainland since the Schism.
- **Illusism** – from Trakorien; teaches the world is a grand illusion; condemned in Kardien (decree of exark **Pergon IV, 403**); illusionists exiled to Trakorien; magic school illusionism now viewed with suspicion.
- **De Enkla** – preach that no church should exist; live strictly by the Gyllene Boken; the loudest critics of church luxury (watched by Den Ljusa Handen).
- **Religioner map** legend: Den Lysande Vägens kyrka · Kerisgass-säte · Æsir-dyrkan · Naturreligioner.

## 11. Trade (Handel)

### Sea trade (Sjöhandel)
- Land transport is local only; almost all goods move by ship (one ship ≈ 500 pack-animals).
- A Copper-Sea crossing takes **≥1 month** (usually 1–1.5) and is risky (pirates, storms, reefs, raiders).
- **Closed sea-routes:** Golwynda's freshwater sea is **sealed** to shipping (too-strong currents; **Raas Narram** falls); same for the freshwater-sea ↔ Masevabukten (the **Demontungan** cuts the route). Trade thus happens at port-towns by the falls (e.g. **Stabul** by Raas Narram; **Ircront** in Nidland).
- **Great trade metropolises:** **Gringul** (Erebos), **Arno** (Caddo), **Tricilve** (Trakorien), **Mastika Agga** (Barbia, timber from Nargur), **Sterborg** (Kardien, but never controlled the trade due to its shallow harbour).
- **Tricilve** is the central hub between rich Trakorien and the Copper-Sea lands (everything can be bought/sold there).
- Most ships sail **inside Caddo** to dodge the Morelvidyn pirates (who let them pass if not over-plundered).
- **Restider:** roll **1T100–50** for % faster (positive) or delayed (negative). A travel-time table (in days) is given for 20 ports: Arno, Atrema, Cerevallis, Fervidun, Fristaden, Gringul, Halle, Jorgund, Kapahlt, Khaanîkel, Mastika Agga, Melukha, Mercana, Nohstril, Pendon, Stabul, Staden O, Sterborg, Tricilve, Tyros.

### Trade maps (legends)
- **Sjöhandelsrutter** (route frequency): Dagliga turer · >50/år · 10–50/år · 1–10/år. Named trades: **Nordtraden, Demontraden, Svaveltraden, Kopparkonvojen**.
- **Import centra** (what each land imports): Livsmedel, Metaller, Timmer, Tyger, Vapen, Slavar, "Plundringsbyte", Salt, Kryddor.
- **Produktion centra** (what each land produces): Spannmål, Fisk, Timmer, Köttprod., Pälsverk, Pärlor, Ädelstenar, Viner, Tyger, Skaldjur, Kryddor, Metaller, Salt, Hantverk, Vapen.

## 12. Languages (Språk)

### Major human-language families & branches
- **Jori** (from the old empire **Jorpagna**) — the largest family; mother of most tongues from Felicien to Trakorien. Three dialect groups:
  - **Västjori** – Kardien, Felicien, Zorakin, Magilre, Mereld, Bzegusta, parts of Klomellien/Trakorien (Saphyna/Klomellien/Magilre form a special **continental jori** group).
  - **Östjori** – Berendien, Hynsolge, the Erebos isles (Hynsolgska heavily influenced by **prangi**, the original barbarian tongue).
  - **Nyjori** – the old imperial heartland.
  - **Kejserlig jori** – the original imperial tongue, now used only by scholars & diplomats.
- **Erdir (Forntunga)** – ancient, now dead; survives in rituals & magic; written in a ~60-sign alphabet.
- **Barbarspråk (narguri)** – northern Ereb barbarian tongues, all from one root (**narguri**). Branches: **Sydnarguriska** (hamuriska, kaseni), **Tjugiska** (Orghin/Sanithsid ice-barbarians; gave rise to barboskin), **Barbiska**, **Västnarguriska** (jordiska, ransardiska, timoleriska), **Östnarguriska** (cerevaliska, prangi). Offshoot **howathiskan** (Torshem).
- **Dalkiska** – Caddo; very song-like with pitch contrasts (hard for outsiders; EDD FV B4/B5 cost double); the holy language of **Den Lysande Vägen**.
- **Dvärgiska språk (gryndur)** – many dwarf tongues; **gryndur** (orig. Grynner-bergen) raised to common dwarf speech; written in dwarven runes.
- **Drakspråket (dragon language)** – the **hardest** language; each word must be redefined to translate; dragons & humans can never fully understand each other; favored by magicians for expressing mighty magic. Holds the concept of a being's **"sanna namn" (preshashn)** — knowing/invoking it grants full power over that being.
- **Svartiska** – many orc/black-folk tongues; harsh, hard for humans (a dwarf finds it slightly easier); no native script (uses jori/dwarf runes).
- **Satenu (alviska)** – spoken by all elf-folk (**teni**); created by **Tevatenu** (Moder Jord); **no dialects**, very stable; soft, vowel-rich; ~30-sign ornate alphabet; popular among scholars for its constancy.
- **Ödlespråken** – primitive related tongues of reptile-men, insectoids, dinosauroids; many hiss-sounds; spoken by very limited groups.

### Writing systems (Skriftsystem)
| System | Signs | Used by |
|---|---|---|
| **Den joriska runraden** | 23 runes (devised ~1400-tal fO by *Harador*) | all jori-speaking folk & others |
| **Barbartecknen** | 20 signs (from 300s fO) | Ransard, Barbia, Jorduashur, Cereval, Nargur barbarians |
| **Alvtecknen / Satenu** | 24 signs (the narguriska/alviska alphabet) | elves & scholars across Ereb |
| Minor systems | — | dwarf runes; felician **kîmzon**; black-folk alphabets; Vicotnik's **nidländska** alphabet |

### Language-learning rules (Språkinlärning)
- Same **dialektgrupp**: a PC's CL/FV "carries over" — **half CL** (round down) to all other languages in the group (Tala & Läsa/Skriva). Expert (Drakar och Demoner Expert) version: **FV –2** in the same group.
- One group apart: **divide by three** (round down) / **FV –3**.
- Borrowed languages share kinship: **a quarter of CL** (round down) for Läsa/Skriva / **FV –4**.
- *Examples:* ransardiska 80% → automatic 20% in timoriska & aleriska; berendiska 75% → 37% in erebosiska & hynsolgska; feliciska 60% → 20% in klomelliska, saphynska, magillska.

## 13. Magic (Magi)

### Dark Magic (Mörkermagi)
- Introduced to Ereb by **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** (came from eastern/Akrogal lands **468 eO**, settled in **Nidland** south of Cereval); founded the magocracy and the **Akademin i Mörke**, ruling as **Storkejsare** with ~20 other dark mages (**domarna**); all other magic forbidden. His two mottos: *"Du skall dö!"* and *"Han skall dö!"*.
- All dark mages gain **mörkerseende**; they are night-creatures (bad reputation, "vampire" rumors).
- Their magic draws power from **Luna** (the moon): **stronger at full moon** (PSY doubles), **weaker at new moon** (PSY halves).
- **School-value → difficulty conversion** (for use under Grundreglerna): **1–4 → 1; 5–9 → 2; 10–14 → 3; 15+ → 4**.

### Dark-magic spells (Besvärjelser) — key entries
| Spell | Skolvärde | Effect (brief) |
|---|---|---|
| **Vicotniks onda öga** | 1 | "See" magical fields nearby; learn a magic's school & effect-grade (not through iron/thick walls). |
| **Vicotniks förbannelser** | varies | A curse the mage defines; higher effect-grade = stronger. Named examples: **Kheldrams** (KAR→25, opposite-sex viewers may be blinded), **Gælerûs** (KAR→1, viewers panic/flee), **Juttris svarta tunga** (E1, victim sick if eats non-meat), **Bluxtors** (E15, face erased — unrecognizable), **Froppas** (E10, weakened legs/bones), **Klostobers** (E2, water-terror, fears boats/bridges). |
| **Vicotniks farsot** | 5 | Inflict disease (E1 stomach-ill → E25 plague). |
| **Vicotniks mörka sannsyn** | 3 | Learn a magic item's origin/powers by effect-grade. |
| **Vicotniks själaförgörelse** | 18 | Read minds; erase a minor memory (vs PSY). |
| **Vicotniks oorganiska förgörelse** | 10 | Reduce inorganic matter to dust (volume by effect-grade; what it can destroy scales: stone → all). |
| **Vicotniks organiska förgörelse** | 12 | Wither & sever a body part over 10 days (stoppable with **HELA**). |
| **Vicotniks sinnesförgörelse** | 18 | Lower mental attributes (PSY/INT/KAR); also mind-reading/brainwash if drugged. |
| **Vicotniks vävnadsförgörelse** | 15 | Lower physical attributes (FYS/STY/SMI). |

### Magic nodes (Maginoder) & mage-storms (Magistormar)
- Magical power flows (**defluxer**) must hit certain matter to be usable; a magician "lights" them like a beam.
- Node strength depends on **defluxer concentration** + **node size**; effects: better spell success, less PSY cost, faster PSY recovery (in a *wrong*-type node, the opposite).
- Three node-types tied to environment:
  - **Topomagiska** – animism near sea-level; necromancy at heights/depths.
  - **Klimatomagiska** – cold-based elemental magic harder in warm climates (and vice-versa).
  - **Geomagiska** – mentalism easier where there's much rock/minerals underfoot.
- **Speldata:** node size **NSTO** = 2×T10 multiplied (1–100 → dm diameter). Node strength **NSTY** = 2T10 – NSTO (in m). To detect: be within NSTY m, PSY in the node's school higher than NSTY, then a hidden skill roll every 10 min.
- **Magistormar** – periodic magic "discharges" sweeping Ereb; detectable only via **Väderförutsägelse**; usually bring violent thunderstorms; spells cannot be cast while in their path; all magic items in the storm must resist (storm strength as active part) or be **avmagiserad** (de-magicked).
