# Magilre — Full Summary & GM Reference

> *"En äventyrsmodul till Ereb Altor"* — an adventure module for **Drakar och Demoner Expert**, set in the world of **Ereb Altor**. A country/region sourcebook describing the land of **Magilre** (centred on the canal-city **Moril Dilaz**) plus **three short adventures**. Published by Äventyrsspel (now RiotMinds AB), 1990.

> This is a high-level reference/prep summary. It describes structure, lore, plot beats, NPCs and maps — it does **not** reproduce the rules text, the keyed room descriptions, read-aloud boxes, or NPC stat blocks. You need the book to actually run it.

## 3. History ("Historia") — Timeline & Key Events

- **Original people:** Magilre's aboriginal population left no trace; the **Dyler** (see §5) were rolling across the peninsula in ancient times before being stopped.
- **Colonisation by Jorpagna:** Patrician families from **Grivela** with farming experience settled the land, bringing civilisation and a labour force; the Joric estate owners shaped society.
- **Breadbasket era:** Until Jorpagna's fall, Magilre supplied grain, dried fish and mussels to much of the world.
- **Dark interlude:** With imports (esp. metal) cut off, the land was poorly equipped to fend for itself — people briefly reverted to **stone tools**. Trade was eventually re-established.
- **Religious shift:** The revival of "Den lysande vägen" swept the region and **ousted the Kabrinzi religion** (see §6). Magillers have been loyal but indifferent temple-goers ever since.
- **Jorduashur pirates** raided Magilre's coasts in the past; relations are now mostly peaceful (occasional minor raids remain).
- **Loss of Bzegusta:** The island of **Bzegusta** was long counted part of Magilre. Klavykiska forces "liberated" it ca. **100–150 eO** in a bloody conquest. Its loss is the main reason Magilre keeps sending **mercenary troops** to the wars on the trakoric isles (the largest effort being against **Hiatus Neffro**, "the mad milacker," who tried to invade Saphyna).
- **Founding of the dynasty:** Duke **Ogilanz Ry'caril** had earlier fled to Bzegusta after publicly insulting the Klavykiska king **Hosin den förste** at a tournament (mocking his lack of virility) — a jab blamed for triggering the conquest. The Ry'caril line distinguished itself in the war; its head was offered the crown of "All Magilre."
- **151 eO:** **Capaz Ry'caril** is crowned **Capaz I**, vowing his line's foremost goal will be to one day **reclaim Bzegusta**. His descendants have ruled in unbroken line, producing celebrated "hero-kings."

## 4. Language & Character ("Språk" / "Sinnelag")

- **Language:** A continental **jorin** dialect; loan-words exist for things unique to Magilre. Outside influences: **Jorduasher** (fishing terms) and **Kardiska** (religious terms).
- **Disposition:** Magillers love to talk and are skilled demagogues and politicians. The phrase *"to talk someone out of their wits like a Magiller"* is well known. The celebrated orator **Eligin Portas** wrote the oft-quoted *"Saker jag sagt"* ("Things I've Said").
- **GM-only secret ("Enbart för spelledarens öron"):** The loan-words inherited from Magilre's original people are **actually magical**. When a Magiller addresses a non-Magiller in the local dialect, it grants a real bonus — **+1 to Övertala (Persuade) per level of the Tala magilliska skill**. The speaker must master the magilre dialect and the listener must understand it. Nobles, being better educated, are also affected.

## 5. Geography, Fauna & Peoples

### Geography ("Geografi")
- The land is famously **flat** farmland; fields are separated by thin woodland strips to stop the soil blowing away.
- **Villages** are the hubs of rural life, with farmland radiating out like wheel-spokes; most peasants work the land for the local baron ("dagsverken").
- Toward the interior, near the **Klomellien** border, the land rises into the soft hills and steep western foothills of the **Klomellerbergen** mountains.
- Tall multi-storey **castles/manors ("borgar")** are the only things breaking the flat horizon.
- **Efronzink** (*Efrons göl* / Efron's pool): a large swamp west of the last hills, resistant to all drainage and conquest; rich, dangerous wildlife. Home to the **Kabrinzi Seekers' refuge** and the temple ruin of Adventure 3.

### Fauna
- **Ziploditer** (native beasts), by size:
  - **Träskziploditer** (swamp) — gigantic; collapse under their own weight at ~200 years; vile-tasting flesh.
  - **Trögziploditer** (sluggish) — slow but immensely strong, gentle leaf-eaters; the ideal **draft animal** (used in wagon trains, "släptåg").
  - **Stäppziploditer** (steppe) — ≤90 kg, grass-eaters; sharp senses, fast; raid maize fields in herds of 10–15; farmers loathe them.
  - **Magillersnigeln** (the Magiller snail) — arm-long, shell-less; a prized **gourmet delicacy**; over-harvested.
- **Elefantiner:** two species of tiny, ferocious wild "elephants" (~1 m). One has ordinary feet; the **flat-footed** one has splayable toes for crossing the bogs of Efronzink.

### Peoples
- **Dylerna:** an ancient barbarian mountain folk; short, robust, very strong; masters of wilderness skills, weak in lore; emotionally volatile (rage, tears, wild mirth); fierce warriors. Pushed back into the **Klomellerbergen**; their old city **Jicon** (on the west coast) was taken by the Jorers and renamed **Moril Dilaz** — a grudge they have nursed for over a thousand years.
- **Folket (commoners):** typical trades — farmer, fisher, mussel-gatherer, ziplodit-driver, tramway maintenance worker, sailor, craftsman. Love multi-storey houses and lively chatter. Famous cuisine: oven-baked maize bread with pickled fillings & mussel sauce; roasted snail in seaweed.
- **Adeln (nobility):** descended from Grivela patrician families + retired generals; duchies/baronies are nominally royal but effectively **autonomous**. Nobles settle disputes with **words, not weapons** — whoever argues most convincingly wins by consensus in the **Council ("Rådet")**.
- **Kungaätten (royal dynasty):** the Ry'caril line (see §3); the king's personal power is limited, but his word carries great weight; openly defying his "well-meaning advice" is unpopular.

## 6. Government, Currency & Religion

### Politics ("Politik")
- **Administrative division:** **98 baronies**, of which **63** lie within **7 duchies**; the rest answer directly to the Crown.
  - **Galder** and **Bzegusta-i-öst** dominate south-coast mussel farming → richest region.
  - Smallest duchy by area is **Munzga** (the capital). Through tangled inheritance the rightful duke is the **king himself** — leading to a comical annual ceremony where the king swears loyalty to himself. Hence: *"as loyal as the Duke of Munzga."*
- **Domestic tension ("Inrikes"):** Nobles consider open violence against a fellow noble dishonourable, which restrains the court clique in **Munzga** from acting against the *nouveau-riche* "upstarts" of **Moril Dilaz** — resented because most national trade flows through Moril's harbour.

### Currency ("Magilres mynt")
- Well-developed banking, even a primitive **check system** (only nobles & rich merchants can "write a check").
- Coins are shaped like stylised wheat-ears, slang **"fogar."** Copper/silver/gold *fog* are **3× larger** than normal coins → **multiply listed prices by 3** when quoted in fogar.

### Religion
- **Den lysande vägen** dominates. High priest ("kerisgassen") **Juniz Duolik** sits in **Munzga** and directs all religious activity. It holds far less political power than in Zorakin/Kardien.
- Fishers & sailors widely worship the trakoric sea-goddess **Anxalis**. Foreign religions are tolerated (reluctantly) due to trade dependence — including the half-secret **Kabrinzi sect**, persecuted by both church and nobility.

### The Kabrinzi Order — full backstory (crucial for Adventure 3)
- **Founding (34 fO, in Kaibab / now Kabiona):** ~20 intellectuals — authors, scholars, magicians — formed a club, **Kabrinzi** (Joric for *"the greatness of thought"*). Seeking the "true and unassailable religion," they spent half a century in research, divination and travel to other worlds, arriving at a single god — **Kabrinzi** — who controls natural law and each being's fate. Their teachings were recorded in **De eviga sångernas bok** ("The Book of the Eternal Songs").
- **Zumlit Zaar:** an alcoholic, drug-and-magic-addled alchemist from **Krun**, recruited by mistake. Convinced the doctrine must spread, he **murdered the other founders**, burning them alive in a magical "firework" that left a crater still visible today. He then preached the doctrine himself (altering parts, esp. on mission), gathering many converts via sheer charisma. He **vanished without trace at age 65** on a missionary journey.
- **The robber-knights:** After Zumlit vanished (**4 fO**), a curia of priests founded a **knightly order** to spread the faith — which degenerated into an **ungodly robber band** burning fields and killing peasants in Kabrinzi's name, spreading the religion but ruining its reputation.
- **The three artifacts:** Three relics gave the church immense power (could convert a crowd with a single word): a **necklace/amulet ("halssmycke")**, a **short silver staff**, and a **glass flask of perpetually swirling vapours**. Kept under heavy guard in a cellar vault in Kaibab, used cautiously for fear of side-effects.
- **The Order's fall:** During the 100s eO, with Bzegusta conquered and Capaz I crowned, the rulers favoured "Den lysande vägen" as folk-religion. Bold nobles **broke into the vault, stole the three artifacts, and murdered the entire Kabrinzi leadership.** The order collapsed; temples were destroyed or left to rot. The artifacts were scattered and hidden, since no one knew how to use them.
- **The Seekers ("Sökarna") & their curse:** A remnant survived in an inaccessible part of **Efronzink**, determined to restore the faith by recovering the artifacts. Two faithful undergo a ceremony drinking holy water called **Mindusvinet**; if they fail to find all three relics within **20 years**, the **Sökarnas förbannelse** (Seekers' curse) kills them — but before dying they may name **two new Seekers**, each granted another 20 years. Every Kabrinzi adherent vows to aid the Seekers. **Two of the three artifacts have already been recovered; the third has just been located** — the hook for Adventure 3.
- **Three present-day branches (~3,000 members total):**
  1. **Munkarna (Monks)** — secluded in the mountains near Klomellien; prayer, ritual objects, self-sufficiency.
  2. **De underjordiska (the Underground)** — the largest group (~half); ordinary folk of all trades; secret, rare meetings.
  3. **De vandrande riddarna (the Wandering Knights)** — armoured messengers/spies/enforcers; no heraldry; their one tell: **all ride pure white horses.**

## 7. Moril Dilaz — City Gazetteer

- **Origins:** Founded ~2,000 years ago by the **Dyler** as **Jicon**, on the only break in the West Sea cliffs. The marshy ground made it slowly sink; after ~100 years it was unliveable, just as **Joric settlers** arrived with legions and drove the Dyler out. Subsidence later stabilised and the Jorers rebuilt it as **Moril Dilaz**.
- **The canal city:** Water flows between the buildings; people travel by **boat**. Houses of stone/brick with tile roofs; **terraces** serve as walkways, linked by small stone/wood bridges (larger gaps have ornate pillared bridges). Common transport: short rowboats and **dolphin-drawn taxi-boats**. The water is unusually clear — on sunny days you can glimpse the **sunken old city** below.
- **The volcano:** A small but **active volcano** sits just under the bay's surface offshore — central to Adventure 1.

### Four Districts
1. **Gamla Jicon** — middle-class centre; craftsmen, bakers, boat-builders, officials; decent inns; lower-end mystics (alchemists, astrologers).
2. **Hamnen (Harbour)** — quays, trade offices, customs house, warehouses, cheap sailors' dives ("hamnsjapp"); the only district with real streets; reeks of fish, beer and tar.
3. **Gunsten** — exclusive district by the duke's palace; sedan chairs, private guards, **toll to enter**; home to rich merchants, nobles, bankers, the **town hall** and the **Stora utropningstorget** (where a crier announces the duke's news twice daily).
4. **Fattigkvarteren (Poor Quarters)** — the city's "backside"; worn, unheated, crime & prostitution; crews the great **fishing fleet**.

### City Services
- **Ordningsmakt (Watch):** 300 uniformed soldiers in 5–7-man patrols; well-paid, hard to bribe; patrol only Hamnen & Gamla Jicon (Gunsten has the duke's guard; the poor quarters have none).
- **Renhållning (Sanitation):** organised only in Gunsten; elsewhere refuse goes into the water (which stays clean thanks to flow + fish).
- **Transport:** dolphin taxi-boats (≤1 silverfog); sedan chairs (pricier); owning a boat is a rite of passage.
- **Tolls ("Tullar"):** silverfog to enter Gunsten; **20 silverfogar/day** harbour fee for visiting ships.

### Notable Places ("Bemärkta platser")
- **Biblioteket** — grand marble library; history/politics (much in imperial jori & satenu), geography, sailing, fishing; paper impregnated against damp gives it a peculiar smell; five elderly librarians + translators. Good place to research Dyler, storm-waves, **Rhys**, etc.
- **Palatset** — Duke **Marcaril**'s palace on an island in Gunsten; meant to be 7 storeys but only 4 built; **no cellar**; haunted attic — the ghost of master builder **Ruvik Gruvgarne**, who fell from the roof two days before the inauguration.
- **Mestraz' handelsbod** — sells anything legal; also owns the big Hamnen warehouses; motto: *if Mestraz can't get it, it's not worth owning.*
- **Gyllene gåsen** — the city's foremost gourmet tavern (prices to match).
- **Stora sjökon** — cozy Gamla Jicon inn run by **Veron Kerkez**; recently "haunted" by a pale figure at night. (The truth: Veron *himself*, under a curse laid by a guest he once served an undercooked maize dumpling — he sleepwalks, powders himself white with flour, wanders the inn and takes a dip in the canal, then wakes baffled that his bedclothes are always wet.) A light, self-contained side-mystery.
- **Grå havskatten** — the magicians'/scholars' tavern; unusually open shop-talk; gateway to **Borkon Symbolisten** (Magilre's top symbolist mage); anything said here has a **20% chance of reaching the Dyler.**
- **The legend of Rhys:** Long ago, sorcerers lived under the bay, mistaken for gods. The Dyler drove off all but the young **Rhys**, who slowly sank their city in revenge. When the Jorers came they made a pact: leave Rhys in peace, and he will **protect the city from any great threat**. Commoners therefore view magicians very positively. Rhys is the built-in **fail-safe rescuer** for Adventure 1.

## 8. The Three Adventures

### Adventure 1 — "Dylernas invasion" (a.k.a. "Sanningen" / The Truth)
- **Setup:** After a couple of weeks in Moril Dilaz, the PCs are visited one night by a **masked, sword-bearing nobleman**, an anonymous envoy of **Duke Marcaril Marguz**, burgomaster of Moril Dilaz. He warns that the **Dyler** — original founders of the city — plan to return and destroy it, and hires the PCs to investigate.
- **The real plot ("Sanningen"):** Only a small group of Dyler is actually in the city; the rest are coming from the Klomellerbergen. They are led not by a warlord but by a respected merchant, **Miril Gereid**, fronting a conspiracy in Munzga — the **"Konsortiet för Morils förstörelse"** (the Consortium for Moril's Destruction) — that wants Moril Dilaz **ruined** out of commercial rivalry. The plan: artificially trigger an early eruption of the **offshore volcano**, sending a destructive wave to **flood the city**, then send in fighters amid the chaos.
- **Exotic means:** Miril has knowledge of the **raugoner** (insectoid floating sea-colonies) and through them the sea-beasts **harebir**. He hires a **magician (Borkon Symbolisten)** and a **pirate** to lure a harebir — using a giant covered amphora-like **bait** — to smash the volcano at the right spot. Codeword/sigil: **HETSA**. The raugoner's reward: free run of the city's remains.
- **The PCs as targets:** The plotters realise the PCs are the duke's counter-move and try to **lure them into a trap (see "Fällan").**
- **Key scenes / locations:**
  - **Hos Abiniz Matematikern** (At Abiniz the Mathematician's) — Abiniz was hired to compute the eruption's effects; he holds key information and will try to flee; round-by-round (SR) behaviour and stats are detailed.
  - **Miril Gereids hus** — his luxurious home (servants, two boats always ready); he is out only at night; keeps no incriminating evidence at home.
  - A **conspiracy meeting** the PCs can eavesdrop on (cast: Miril Gereid, Borkon, Liebon, Rondolf, Minis) — includes the giant lure and a threat to any "blabbermouth" ("…otherwise you'll be shark-food").
  - **Fällan (The Trap)** — a **shark-man ("hajman")** posing as a wounded sea-captain lures the PCs onto the water; he transforms and several shark-men ambush the boat. Water-combat penalties apply (e.g. swords/axes −10 FV, spears −5; bows/throwing weapons only from the boat). Shark-men are cowardly and flee when hurt.
- **Side hooks:** mangled corpses in the canals (the work of the shark-men, mistaken for natural sharks); the "ghost" at **Stora sjökon** (cursed, sleepwalking Veron Kerkez); the genuine ghost of master builder **Ruvik Gruvgarne** in the **palace attic**.
- **Fail-safe:** if the players can't stop it any other way, **Rhys** rises to save the city; they may also seek him out directly.

### Adventure 2 — "Intermezzo i Gabrika"
- **Setting:** The southern **Barony of Gabrika** on the Gabanzga peninsula — grain, mussels, a coastal oak/beech wood; poorer than the rest of Magilre.
- **Backstory:** A bloody succession feud among old **Baron Torton's** three sons left the line tangled; the current ruler is **Baron Castorin**.
- **The situation:** Castorin is **chronically (and largely imaginarily) ill** and bedridden ~80% of the time, pouring the barony's finances into healers and mages. His most successful healer, **Sartibug Kroppsvrängaren** ("the Body-twister"), now effectively **runs the barony**, sidelining the baron's former right-hand man, **Algotis Ferizan**. Discontent has produced a **resistance movement**.
- **Structure — three "Spelintroduktioner" (entry points):**
  - **2:** The sidelined advisor **Algotis** approaches the PCs.
  - **3:** At the inn **Alzia och Ziploditen** in **Estra**, the PCs are taken to the cellar HQ of the resistance, led by **Asterion** (code-named **"Svanen," the Swan**), who tasks them with **assassinating Baron Castorin**, offering maps of the keep and all the group's resources. Refusal/hesitation → captured and locked in the hideout's dungeons.
- **The squeeze:** The PCs end up with **two opposing commissions**, each patron wanting the other killed. They can pick a side (a Lawful PC may back the baron, an Anarchist the people), play both, or **trick both and walk away rich**. Outcomes branch widely — including the PCs killing everyone and **becoming rulers of Gabrika** (a fine seed for civil war).
- **Maps:** **Gabrikas Värn** (the baron's keep — ground floor with Chapel & Guard Room, upper floor rooms 5–11) and **Motståndsrörelsens gömställe** (the resistance hideout — rooms 1–9, a secret door, and an **underground river**).

### Adventure 3 — "Det heliga gudavinet" (The Holy God-Wine)
- **Hook:** In a Moril Dilaz tavern the PCs meet **Silazion Gyllensvärd**, a knight of the old **Kabrinzi order**, and his companion **Magot Magul** (a tonsured, wise monk who handles the group's ~750 sm funds). They hire the PCs to help recover a lost holy artifact — an **amulet ("halssmycke") called Det heliga gudavinet** — from a crypt beneath a ruined Kabrinzi temple a few days' travel into **Efronzink**.
- **The twist (GM intro):** The two are **Sökare (Seekers)** under the **Seekers' curse**; they are dying. The amulet is the **last of the three artifacts**. The PCs are likely to be **bound into the Seekers' fate** before the end.
- **The journey & temple:** Wagon + tramway + a final stretch on foot. The **Tempelruinen** sits on a cliff ledge reached by a narrow rock-cut stair; almost wholly collapsed, with a ruined tower and an old **well**.
- **The Crypt ("Kryptan"):** A sealed crypt now infested by **insectoids**. **Three ways in:** the distant **secret passage ("lönngången")**, the **well ("brunnen")**, and the **tower ("tornet")**. The spell **JORDVÄG** (Earth-path) can help (all PCs must know it); **LJUS** (Light) extends the 5 m vision sphere.
- **The prize:** The amulet was once worn by a statue; the **insectoid queen** took a liking to it and now wears it in **room 11**. The insectoids are **not evil** — they defend the queen but don't kill for sport. **Cunning beats violence** for getting the amulet. (Insectoid stats reference the Monster book.)

## 9. NPC Roster (Appendix)

Named NPCs are given full Drakar och Demoner stats in the appendix (Grundegenskaper STY/SMI/KAR/STO/INT/KP/FYS/PSY/SB, Skydd, Stridsfärdigheter, Språk, Övriga färdigheter, plus background). Key figures by role:

**Moril Dilaz / Adventure 1**
- **Marcaril Marguz** — Duke & burgomaster of Moril Dilaz; sends the masked envoy.
- **Miril Gereid** (~45) — outwardly a respected merchant from an influential Munzga family, running the local trade office; secretly the brains and front-man of the conspiracy. He actually *likes* Moril Dilaz, but the Consortium needs an insider. A consummate businessman and skilled actor; he originated the volcano plan. Travels with 2+ bodyguards.
- **Borkon Symbolisten** (~52, quarter-dyl) — reclusive, unfriendly master **symbolist** mage; the best in his field in Magilre. Shunned by the magicians' guild and self-conscious about the dyl traits visible in his face — which is partly why he agreed to help his "kinsmen" take the city. Hired to lure the harebir.
- **Abiniz "Matematikern"** — the mathematician hired to calculate the eruption's effects; holds key clues and tries to flee when confronted.
- **Aloxin hoo'Blafiner** (dyl, ~23) — the Dyler agent found in Abiniz's house; taciturn and intensely loyal. Knows where the Dyler HQ is, but **not** that Miril leads them, nor who Borkon is.
- **Rondolf Tandlöse** (~53) — a limping, peacock-feathered ex-pirate captain from the legendary pirate isle **Mytos**, on the run after betraying powerful people. Hired by Miril to drive a harebir into the bay; cares only about the gold, not the plot.
- **Hebrix, Birixor & Kadarax** — three mature **shark-men ("hajmän")** working as assassins for the Dyler; they spring the "Fällan" trap, lurking in the water by night to pass for sharks.
- **Rhys** — ancient sorcerer dwelling on the bay floor among dolphins & sea-elves; the city's mystical protector and Adventure 1 fail-safe (keep him aloof — not a party leader/errand-boy).
- **Ruvik Gruvgarne** — ghost of the palace's master builder; haunts the **palace attic** (he fell from the roof two days before the inauguration, having never been allowed to finish the building as designed).
- **Veron Kerkez** (~38) — genial host of the **Stora sjökon** inn; the inn's "ghost" is actually him, sleepwalking under a guest's curse.
- **Mestraz** — the city's great merchant/fixer ("if Mestraz can't get it, it's not worth owning").
- **Juniz Duolik** — high priest ("kerisgassen") of Den lysande vägen, in Munzga.

**Gabrika / Adventure 2**
- **Baron Castorin** — ~47; the "imaginarily ill," bedridden, distrustful ruler of Gabrika.
- **Sartibug Kroppsvrängaren** — middle-aged healer; de facto ruler of the barony.
- **Algotis Ferizan** — Castorin's sidelined former right-hand man; vain, ingratiating; rides a white horse.
- **Asterion ("Svanen")** (~58) — leader of the Gabrika resistance; a failed merchant/sailor who returned to settle in Estra. He believes in the cause but secretly intends to seize the barony for **himself**; he runs the group as a hidden dictator (only Alzia is wary of him). He wears a **magic ring of truth-hearing** (SANNHÖRSEL via NEXUS) that tells him whether statements are true or false — though it misfires roughly 1 time in 4 and never reveals the *whole* truth, so PCs lying to him is risky but not hopeless.
- **Alzia** — ~37; ex-treasure-hunter of Efronzink (abandoned by cowardly companions after a snakebite); now runs the inn **Alzia och Ziploditen** in **Estra**; hosts the resistance; tough, practical, wants to finish her old Efronzink expedition.
- **Veroniz** — Alzia's partner at the inn; daughter of one of Alzia's old companions.

**Kabrinzi / Adventure 3**
- **Silazion Gyllensvärd** — Kabrinzi knight; a dying Seeker.
- **Magot Magul** — Silazion's monkish companion; talkative, pragmatic, handles the money.
- **Zumlit Zaar** — ~500 years old via the drug **sarsilu**; the order's catastrophic "founder"; now a wretched addict living in a crypt; tied to reptile-men and insectoids near Efrons göl.
- **The insectoid queen** — wears the amulet in crypt room 11.

## 13. New Game Material (Appendix)
Besides the NPCs, the appendix introduces setting-specific rules content (described here in summary — see the book for the actual mechanics):

- **Sarsilu (new plant/drug):** Made from the dried yellow petals of the *sarsilu* flower. On first use it sharply boosts the user's body and mind and extends life by about a month per dose. It is mildly habit-forming at first but becomes powerfully addictive; long-term use steadily degrades the user until, in the final stage, the body wastes away — grey, wrinkled skin, failing muscles, lost teeth, eyes clouding over. This is the drug keeping the ancient **Zumlit Zaar** alive.
- **LUFTBUBBLA (new spell — "Air Bubble"):** Conjures a breathable air bubble around a person underwater that can move them about; it bursts if it breaks the surface. A natural fit for the canals of Moril Dilaz and the flooded approaches in the adventures.
- **HETSA (new symbol/spell):** A compulsion symbol that drives a bewitched creature to hurl itself at the symbol with full force, ignoring danger — the mechanism by which the conspirators steer the harebir into the volcano in Adventure 1. (Recall: "HETSA" is also the conspirators' code-word.)
- **Harebir (new creature):** A huge, frog-like deep-sea beast with powerful swim-fins, white skin and hard, spiked horn-plates. It almost never surfaces; on the rare occasions it does, its magical discharges spawn sudden, inexplicable **storms**. Central to Adventure 1.

## 14. Quick-Reference: Who Wants What

**Adventure 1 — the conspiracy at a glance**
- **Konsortiet för Morils förstörelse** (Munzga commercial interests) *wants* Moril Dilaz destroyed → funds the plot.
- **Miril Gereid** fronts it inside the city, steering the **Dyler** (who want revenge for ancient Jicon).
- **Borkon Symbolisten** (mage) + **Rondolf Tandlöse** (pirate) are hired to lure a **harebir** into the **volcano** using the **HETSA** symbol.
- **Abiniz** did the maths; **Aloxin** is the Dyler footman; **Hebrix/Birixor/Kadarax** (shark-men) handle wet-work, incl. the trap.
- Opposing them: the **PCs** (hired by Duke **Marcaril Marguz**), with **Rhys** as the last-ditch saviour of the city.

**Adventure 2 — the Gabrika factions**
- **Baron Castorin** — the (imaginarily) sick ruler; the target.
- **Sartibug Kroppsvrängaren** — his healer and *de facto* ruler; wants to keep his grip.
- **Algotis Ferizan** — the sidelined ex-advisor; wants back in (and Asterion gone).
- **Asterion "Svanen"** — resistance leader; publicly wants reform, privately wants the barony for himself.
- **Alzia** & **Veroniz** — host the resistance at the inn in **Estra**; Alzia distrusts Asterion.
- The PCs are caught between two patrons who each want the other dead — and can back either, both, or neither.

**Adventure 3 — the Kabrinzi quest at a glance**
- **Silazion Gyllensvärd** (knight) + **Magot Magul** (monk) are dying **Seekers** seeking the last of the three artifacts, the amulet **Det heliga gudavinet**.
- It lies in the **crypt** beneath **Tempelruinen** in **Efronzink**, now worn by the **insectoid queen** (rm 11).
- The PCs are likely to be drawn into the **Seekers' curse** themselves before the end.
