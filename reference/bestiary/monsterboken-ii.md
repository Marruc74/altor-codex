# Monsterboken II — Summary & Reference Sheet

A scanned bestiary (monster manual) for the Swedish tabletop RPG **Drakar och Demoner** ("Dragons and Demons"). This is a full structural + content summary with quick‑use lists. Game terms are kept in Swedish with English glosses, since stat blocks use the Swedish abbreviations.

**Origin (from the foreword):** During spring 1986 the publisher's magazine *Sinkadus* ran a contest inviting readers to submit monsters. Roughly 500 creatures were received. Submissions copied from existing fiction (e.g. balrogs, triffids, nazgûl) were rejected for legal/originality reasons; crossbreeds (half‑elves, orch‑giants, etc.) were rejected as unoriginal. The selected ~80+ creatures fill this book.

## 3. Stat‑block format (every creature uses this template)

Each entry has flavour text (appearance, habits, habitat) plus a boxed stat card containing:

- **Namn** — name (often with a mock‑Latin species name, e.g. *Hjortid = Palaeanthropus cerviformus*)
- **Hemvist** — habitat (numeric codes, see §5)
- **Vanlighet** — frequency / rarity (see §4)
- **Antal** — number appearing (dice expression, e.g. `1 eller 4T6`)
- **Grundegenskap / Typvärde** — attribute roll / typical value (see §6)
- **Naturliga vapen / GC / Skada** — natural weapons / base hit chance % / damage
- **Naturligt skydd** — natural armour (points absorbed each hit)
- **Förflyttning** — movement rate and mode (see §7)
- **Färdigheter & förmågor** — skills and special abilities

> Note: **T** = tärning (die). So `4T6+14` means 4d6 + 14. **EDD:** flags rules that require *Expert Drakar och Demoner*.

## 8. Rules content in the introduction (pp. 5–14)

- **Intelligent vs. unintelligent creatures.** Intelligent creatures have variable INT; unintelligent ones have a fixed INT (usually 5) used mainly on the resistance table (representing cunning, not reasoning). Their skills depend on profession/race (intelligent) or activity such as hunting/grazing (unintelligent).
- **Unintelligent creatures' base chance** with natural weapons: `STY + SMI + (2T4 × 5)`; the `2T4 × 5` term may be replaced by its average, **25**.
- **Combat skill by experience** (multiplier applied to base chance):

  | Stridserfarenhet (experience) | SVF (weapon skill) | NVF (natural‑weapon skill) |
  |---|---|---|
  | Oerfaren — Inexperienced | ×1 | ×1 |
  | Erfaren — Experienced | ×4 | ×2 |
  | Mycket erfaren — Very experienced | ×7 | ×4 |
  | Veteran | ×10 | ×5 |

  (SVF = stridsvapenfärdigheter; NVF = naturliga vapenfärdigheter.)
- **Naturliga vapen** table: standard natural weapons (claws, bite, etc.) and their combat effects; damage bonus (SB) is **not** included in each weapon's listed damage.
- **Träffområden (hit locations, EDD).** Body divided into regions; how much each takes depends on Total KP (= average of STO + FYS). Creatures with Total KP ≤ 7, formless creatures (e.g. giant amoebas), and highly variable ones (hydra, stryparranka) have **no** hit‑location breakdown. Tables are provided per body plan: reptiles/amphibians, fish & whales, drakorm/serpent, dragon, insects, spiders, octopus (bläckfisk). Spiders/insects don't lose Total KP from leg hits.
- **Flygregler (flight rules):** an expanded version of the simple flight guidelines from *Monsterboken I*.
- **EDD note:** sections beginning "EDD" require the Expert rules; the book is still usable without them.
- **GM guidance:** most intelligent creatures aren't automatically hostile; animals rarely attack humans except when starving or defending young; aggressive animals can sometimes be bribed with meat.
