# SYMBOLER — Sinkadus #35 (April 1992)

**Full title:** SYMBOLER — En grafisk handledning av Thomas Allvin
*("SYMBOLS — A graphic guide by Thomas Allvin")*

**Source:** Sinkadus #35, April 1992 (Target Games / Äventyrsspel), pages 6–8.
**System:** Material for the Swedish RPG *Drakar och Demoner* (Dragons & Demons).
**Illustrations:** Gabriel Sandberg, 1992.

---

## Overview / Author's introduction

The article is a collection of magical symbols, signs and ideograms that a magician (*magiker*) in *Drakar och Demoner* can use — painted, carved, hewn, or hastily drawn in the sand. The author's framing points:

- Magic's world is the world of possibility; the lucky ones are those who master it.
- A good adventure should feature at least one character schooled in the magical rituals or a magical object — but magic is not only waving arms and reciting strange chants, nor does it end with magical items.
- The graphic/symbolic side of magic is too often forgotten or pushed aside; only a small number of "symbolists" master the art of writing magical symbols.
- What follows is a collection of symbols and signs presented as **common knowledge among all magicians**.
- Although most look simple, **not just anyone can draw them effectively** — a magician must inscribe the sign under concentration and then speak the power-word (*kraftord*) needed to make it work.

**Important game-mechanics note from the author:**
- The symbols are given **no specific game-mechanical effect**.
- No PSY consumption (or similar) is defined.
- This is a small "handbook for magicians" and is **not meant to intrude on the high-regarded school of symbolism**.
- The GM (*spelledare*) decides what effect, if any, each sign has — and may change it from time to time.
- Intended as flavour ("krydda"), not as an absolute, powerful tool in players' hands.
- A number of the symbols are meant to be usable **only by powerful magicians** and exist mostly to create magical atmosphere.

## The 30 Symbols (full list)

> Numbering follows the article (1–30). Each entry summarizes the symbol's meaning and lore.

1. **Spiritus clarus ("the pure")**
   - Ancient symbol denoting the pure, clear and spiritual; called *"spiritus clarus"* by the Emperor's tongue (kejserliga jorin).
   - Rarely has any effect on its own, but used as an **international sign between magicians** to mark holy groves, healing springs, magazines/storehouses, and the like.
   - Also used as a **recognition sign among the kafriler monks** on the island Mereld, south of Trakorien.

2. **"Keep and preserve" (Behåll och bevara)**
   - Keywords are *keep* and *preserve*.
   - Carved into jars/jugs or other containers when one wants the contents to **stay in good condition especially long**.
   - Originally a rune stemming from the barbarians in/around Nargur's forests, where it is used freely by shamans and druids.

3. **Quinta essentia (the fifth element)**
   - Concept from the Golwyndian era, when elements were thought to number five: earth, water, fire, air, and **ether (eter)**.
   - Symbolizes ether, which was considered necessary for all life.
   - Used by magicians who **research the structure of the universe**, and a must for dimension-travellers and other magical pioneers.
   - A learned faction in Arkhaz studies only quinta essentia and its secrets; they use the symbol to try to **make contact with other worlds**.
   - *(See Sinkadus #30 for more on ether.)*

4. **The classic pentagram (det klassiska pentagrammet)**
   - Known to everyone.
   - A perfect pentagram can supposedly be produced by marking the wandering-star **Durval's** position on an astrological star-chart five years in a row on the same date, then drawing lines between the points.
   - Hence pentagrams are often associated with Durval; many astrologers constantly observe that star's movements as they're considered very important.
   - Besides summoning powerful demons and other high-magic, the pentagram is used in many other, more toned-down contexts. A small mountain-dwelling mystic with a house far in the wilderness might draw it on the door as **protection against trolls and evil spirits (oknytt)**.
   - Also used as a plain **recognition mark** by several occult circles worldwide.

5. **The reversed (upside-down) pentagram (vänt upp-och-ner)**
   - Witnesses to the powers of darkness being active/afoot.
   - Considered the symbol of **Prince Shaitan**, Etin's foremost enemy (the Demon Prince).
   - Lures forth the henchmen of evil.
   - The inverted pentagram is the "righteous-turned" sign's opposite pole, with equally great powers; but only when one paints the pentagram on a horizontal surface can the barrier between worlds be broken, demons summoned, or dimension-travel initiated.

6. **The infinity cross / endless cross (oändlighetskorset)**
   - Denotes something **without end and eternal**, in contrast to all else on Altor.
   - A magically charged infinity cross acts as a "wave-breaker" and creates unrest in the world-flow / the quintessence, because it counteracts the law of transience (*förgänglighetens lag*) — meaning it says nothing can be eternal.
   - Therefore the infinity cross **should be used with caution**.
   - *(See "Ereb Altor" for more on the law of transience.)*

7. **The polygon / many-cornered sign (denna månghörning)**
   - Created from two squares; symbolizes **strong fortifications**.
   - Carved on the gate of strong castles to **protect them against enemies**.
   - Said to stem from Akrogal.

8. **Creation's octogram (skapelsens oktogram)**
   - Is the **A and O (alpha and omega)** for magicians who, despite the gods' warnings, try to create their own beings or substances.
   - The octogram is imprinted in the consciousness of every living being; the magician who fully masters it will become **very powerful**.
   - Inscribed in a dwelling, it's also said to be a valuable **protection against tomtar and oknytt** (house-spirits/gremlins).

9. **The sign for Time (tiden)**
   - This curious phenomenon — that **Gadirm Drakmästare** wanted as the eighth element.
   - Correct use of the sign supposedly allows one to **manipulate the flow of time within limited areas**.

10. **The unpleasant symbol (denna obehagliga symbol)**
    - Stands for the ultimate measuring-rod, **inexorable powers**, the limitation of unreal/material structures.
    - Can be used when inscribing **curses** or other attempts to shape people's destinies (livsöden).
    - Often linked with the **krunkian philosopher Ptelemos (435–527)**, who constantly shook his clenched fist at Fate and Time's inexorable course. Hence sometimes called *"Ptelemos' lament" (Ptelemos' klagan)*.
    - Drawn with two cross-strokes, the symbol stands for **death and the merciless reaper (lie-man / Death)**. Can be carved on persons, houses or other things the magician wants struck by plague, slaughter, or death.
    - Both symbols (10 and 11) often appear together in connection with plague, famine and other catastrophes; then painted with **white chalk on the doors of the afflicted**.

11. **The sign of expansion (expansion)**
    - Stands for **expansion**, society, institutionalized religion, authority and governance.
    - Almost always drawn into law-books, but also into statutes/ordinances for trading houses, guilds (skrån) and societies (gillen), and is believed to **bring luck**.
    - In Trakorien, for example, used by certain **imperialistic factions** working for expansion and extended borders.

12. **The sign of putrefactio / decay (denna symbol)**
    - Often used by **necromancers** and other dubious people to denote *putrefactio*, the **process of rotting/decomposition**.
    - Can be used to **speed up** the breakdown of organic matter into dust and mould.
    - Also used to **counteract symbol nr 2** (the "keep and preserve" sign).

13. **The crescent moon (månskäran)**
    - Symbolizes **insight, the subconscious, emotional life, and the personal**.
    - Magicians can **meditate** over this sign (drawn/painted) to gain mental balance, or perhaps to get new ideas when imagination runs dry.

14. **The powerful thunder sign (detta kraftfulla tecken)**
    - **Invokes the thunder god**, primarily his weapon the lightning bolt (blixten).
    - Said that magicians who got lodging at a farm overnight, then carved this sign on the house wall before disappearing — during the night a sudden thunderstorm appeared and struck the farm with lightning, burning it down to the ground.

15. **The fertility / life-seed sign (detta tecken)**
    - Said to have come to Ereb all the way from **Jih-Pun**, on the other side of the Western Sea (Västerhavet).
    - Shows the **seed of life (livsfröet)**, or the universal sprout/germ (universumsgrodd) sprouting in empty space.
    - Used to help **women who never became pregnant** to have a child, to make trees and crops grow faster, etc.

16. **The spiral of potential power (denna symbol)**
    - Strongly connected to the foregoing (15) and shows the **seed of life rotating in the spiral of potential power**.
    - Can be carved on an **infant's cradle** so the child gets a rich and grand life ahead.
    - *(Continues:)* Said also to appear as a **birthmark** on children who, during their adult lives, accomplish great upheavals and change their world in radical ways.

17. **An all-round occult sign (allroundtecken)**
    - A bit of an **all-purpose sign** for occult needs.
    - Denotes **death, rebirth, deeds in the hidden and deep, and the subconscious processes**.
    - Per old scriptures the symbol is said to "**watch over those who work underground / subterraneously**".
    - Much squabbling has occurred in learned circles over whether this refers to people who literally work under earth (mine-workers and dwarves) or to persons such as thieves, spies, assassins (lönnmördare) and others who are figuratively "underground".

18. **Matter over spirit (materiens symbol)**
    - In this sign, **matter's symbol is placed over the spiritual**, thereby suppressing it.
    - The symbol creates **chaos, confusion and other disharmonious states**.
    - Often linked with the chaotic celestial body **Raukhra**, and figures most often in scriptures dealing with the **third conflux (tredje konfluxen)**.

19. **Spirit over matter (raka motsatsen till nr 18)**
    - Stands in **direct opposition to no. 18**.
    - Here the **spirit rules over matter** so that balance and harmony — the quintessence — arises.

20. **Perz — the sign of secrets (denna märkliga tecken, benämns perz)**
    - This curious sign, called **perz**, stands for **secrets and mysteries**.
    - Can be imprinted in a script that the magician wishes to **keep secret**, making the writing distorted and seemingly hard to grasp for an unwanted reader.
    - Perz can also **conceal secret doors** or otherwise befuddle people's minds to preserve secrets.

21. **The sign of flows / currents (tecknet betecknar flöden)**
    - Denotes **flows**, most often power-flows (kraftflöden); used among other things in **conjuration gatherings** to mark how psychic forces should be channeled into various forms.
    - Can also mean **"melt"** — said to have been used by the magician **Rimbald** when he led his followers across the frozen river at Jorduashur after stealing Hakvin's treasure chest. He carved the sign on the river's ice so it melted and broke under the pursuers' feet.

22. **The anti-magic sign (motverka och skingra)**
    - This sign attempts to **counteract and disperse the magical power-flows** that arise when a conjuration is cast — a kind of **anti-magic in miniature**, among other things.
    - Note that the sign counteracts **all conjurations** cast in the area, even those originating from the sign's own caster.
    - The symbol's **power-barrier diminishes with distance**.

23. **The spirit-tamer's sign (andebetvingaren)**
    - The "**spirit-tamer (andebetvingaren)**" usually uses this symbol.
    - Used as **protection against ghosts (gastar)**, specters and other undead spirits.
    - The famed magician **Perokytalar**, who supports himself as a wandering exorcist (andeutdrivare), has this sign **embroidered on his midnight-black cloak** as extra protection against spiteful wraiths (argsinta vålnader).

24. **The ideogram of the Seer's thousand eyes (detta ideogram)**
    - Correctly applied, this ideogram is considered to be one of the **Seer's (Skådaren) thousand eyes**. The Seer is a demigod from **golwyndian mythology**.
    - A mighty magician who has drawn the ideogram on, e.g., a wall, can from a completely different place **concentrate and "see"** what happens as if standing with his back to that wall.

25. **Kossel's staff (Kossels stav)**
    - This sign is usually called **Kossel's staff**, after the legendary arch-magician, and denotes **elevated power (upphöjd makt)**.
    - Power-hungry warlords (härskare) tend to keep a **beautifully painted staff on their throne**.

26. **The staff of the healing arts (läkekonstens stav)**
    - **Speeds up healing processes** and recovery/convalescence.
    - Used by magicians, it is said one can even **cure deadly diseases and heal severed/mutilated body parts**.

27. **The four solstice/equinox signs (dessa fyra tecken)**
    - These four signs stand, in turn and order, for the **winter solstice, spring equinox, summer solstice, and autumn equinox**.
    - Found on the famed **calendar rune-staffs (kalenderrunstavarna)**, now kept in **Entika**.
    - Correct use of the signs at the respective time-point can **release powerful magical charges**.

28. **The alchemical sign (alkemiska konsten)**
    - Stands for the **alchemical art** and shows the **sun, the four elements' triangles, and the all-encompassing circle of knowledge**.
    - Among skilled alchemists, this symbol can be found **painted in the bottom of glass vessels, retorts (kolvar) and the like**.

29. **Life, rebirth and transmigration (liv, pånyttfödelse och själavandring)**
    - **Life, rebirth and the transmigration of souls** are what attract the magician who uses this symbol.
    - It has the **staff of life** inside it, inside an **enclosing circle, crowned by the sun**.
    - A magician may have the symbol **carved on his grave** together with other mighty signs to **secure a good afterlife**.

30. **The sign on "the philosopher's stone" (de vises sten)**
    - This symbol can be **beheld carved into "the philosopher's stone" (lapis philosophorum / de vises sten)**, which stands erect on the road between **Smyrrha and Arkhaz**.
    - Like the other signs carved around it, **no living magician or learned person knows what it is for**.
    - The philosopher's stone is **still visited by magicians from near and far** to crack the riddle and gain a share of the ancient ones' mighty magic.

## Notable named lore (people, places, beings)

**People / magicians**
- **Thomas Allvin** — author of the article.
- **Ptelemos (435–527)** — krunkian philosopher who shook his fist at Fate; symbol #10 named after him.
- **Rimbald** — magician who melted river ice with sign #21 (Hakvin's treasure / Jorduashur).
- **Perokytalar** — wandering exorcist (andeutdrivare) with sign #23 on his cloak.
- **Kossel** — legendary arch-magician (sign #25, "Kossel's staff").
- **Gadirm Drakmästare** — wanted Time as the eighth element (sign #9).
- **Hakvin** — owner of the stolen treasure chest (sign #21 legend).

**Beings / deities**
- **Prince Shaitan** — the Demon Prince; Etin's foremost enemy (sign #5).
- **Etin** — implied opposing power to Shaitan.
- **The Seer (Skådaren)** — demigod of golwyndian mythology (sign #24).
- **Thunder god** — invoked via sign #14.

**Places**
- **Mereld** — island south of Trakorien (kafriler monks, sign #1).
- **Nargur's forests** — origin of sign #2 (shamans/druids).
- **Arkhaz** — faction studying quinta essentia (sign #3); on the road with the philosopher's stone (#30).
- **Akrogal** — origin of sign #7.
- **Trakorien** — imperialistic factions use sign #11.
- **Jih-Pun** — across the Western Sea; origin of sign #15.
- **Jorduashur** — site of Rimbald's ice-melting (sign #21).
- **Entika** — keeps the calendar rune-staffs (sign #27).
- **Smyrrha** — the philosopher's stone stands on the road between Smyrrha and Arkhaz (#30).
- **Raukhra** — chaotic celestial body linked to sign #18.
- **Durval** — wandering star linked to the pentagram (#4).

**Concepts / cosmology**
- **Quinta essentia / ether** — the contested fifth (or eighth) element.
- **The law of transience (förgänglighetens lag)** — that nothing is eternal (sign #6 defies it).
- **The third conflux (tredje konfluxen)** — context for sign #18.
- **Putrefactio** — the rotting process (sign #12).
- **The philosopher's stone (lapis philosophorum / de vises sten)** — ancient unsolved magic (#30).

**Cross-references mentioned in the text**
- *Sinkadus #30* — for more on ether (sign #3).
- *"Ereb Altor"* — for more on the law of transience (sign #6).
