# Kaos Väktare — Reference Summary

*Drakar och Demoner* supplement, Target Games / Äventyrsspel, 1993. Construction: Magnus Seter, Johannes Nesser, Michael Stenmark. A **rules and campaign module about demons** — it is **not standalone** and requires the *Drakar och Demoner* (1991 edition) core rules.

This summary preserves Swedish game terms (spell names, attributes, abilities) since they're what you'll reference at the table, with explanations in English. Attributes: STY (strength), STO (size), FYS (constitution), SMI (agility), INT, PSY, KAR (charisma), KP (hit points), SB (damage bonus), FV (skill value), CL (effective chance), SR (combat round), Sx/S = Skicklighetsvärde-based duration/range, R = ritual, E = effektgrad.

## 1. The Multiverse (Multiversum)

**Origin.** The universe began as chaos — evil, dark forces and terrible winds. Chaos eventually birthed **light** as its opposite. Around the light the **higher powers (de högre makterna)** created new worlds and beings. To stop chaos reaching the light, they (working among the titans) raised the **dimension walls (dimensionsmurarna)**, dividing creation into infinite separated worlds — the **Multiverse**. As an outer shield they created **Demonicum**, a ring of the outermost worlds bordering chaos, populated by **demons** set there to guard the Multiverse.

**Structure.** Light radiates from an unknown center. Inner worlds = pure, spirit-beings, ruled by higher powers. The farther from light, the more physical and the more chaos intrudes. The outermost frontline against chaos is **Demonicum**, defended by demonic guardians and their servant demons.

**Why the walls also trapped demons.** Beyond stopping chaos, the walls were meant to keep the higher powers' own creations (the demons) from migrating inward and overthrowing the order — a fear they'd grow mightier than their makers.

**Travel between worlds = magic only.** Either open a direct portal (very dangerous) or, far more common, **"walk the grey path"** via the astral plane: **De Grå hallarna (The Grey Halls)**.

### De Grå hallarna (The Grey Halls)
- Stepping through a portal strips your physical body; only your **astral form** (a grey, colorless mirror of you) remains.
- A closed portal appears as a **locked ancient iron gate** with runes, beside a stone plate bearing the rune of the world you left.
- Endless grey pillared halls full of statues and memories. Without knowledge you lose direction and may **forget your physical form forever** (or until you find a new body).
- **Astral traces:** all beings leave fading astral trails, trackable by demon hunters.

**Travel rules:** Best aid is the skill **Kunskap om Multiversum**, ideally with knowledge of the target world. Astral maps (e.g. *Isghuls astralkarta*) or a *lintropisk pendel* reduce wandering time by 1 hour each (use only one).

**Dangers — lost beings:** Demons, magicians, adventurers who lost direction wander the halls. They'll **help or attack** depending on alignment, trying to slip through a portal to regain a body — or **possess** the first body they find if they've forgotten their own.

**Detection / combat risk:** Opening a portal to the Halls = **1-in-20** chance of detection. Risk **+1 per hour** spent (max 5), then **−1 per hour** down to 0.

**Combat in the Grey Halls:**
- No physical matter. You keep PSY, KAR, INT and a grey shadow of your entry-form.
- Skills based on INT/KAR/PSY keep their values; **all other skills become PSY-based** (Hoppa, Rida, Smyga, Slagsmål, Akrobatik, etc.).
- **Damage is in PSY, not KP. Size is irrelevant.**
- Physical spells convert to mental versions (MENTAL ELD, MENTAL BLIXT, MENTAL SPLITTRING), dealing PSY damage.
- Weapon damage depends entirely on **attacker's PSY** (see table). Parrying with weapon/shield absorbs damage per the same PSY scale.
- **Reaching 0 PSY = death:** astral form dissolves, soul doomed to wander forever.

**PSY → Damage table (Grey Halls):**

| PSY | Skada | PSY | Skada |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–10 | 1T2 | 23–26 | 1T10 |
| 11–14 | 1T4 | 27–30 | 2T6 |
| 15–18 | 1T6 | 31–34 | 3T6 |
| 19–22 | 1T8 | … | … |

**Direct portals to other worlds:** Possible but only for master magicians. High failure risk (may open onto a wrong, more dangerous world) and the spell weakens the caster. Upside: avoids the Grey Halls' dangers.

## 2. Demonicum & Demon Society

**Demonicum** = the collective name for the infinite worlds farthest from light, populated by demons, forming the outer wall against chaos. Constant war rages between invading chaos armies and demon hordes — and between demons themselves.

**Demons' nature.** Created to resist chaos's corrupting force; given a higher consciousness and great mental power. Consequently **independent, scrupleless, amoral** — "false beasts who betray their word" unless bound by higher powers or cosmic laws. They feel no compassion. Their core trait is **aggression**, plus **demonic abilities** suited to fighting chaos, and an ever-growing **hunger for power**. Subject to summoning rituals, they can be torn to other worlds and made to materialize there.

### Demon hierarchy (highest → lowest)
1. **Väktarna (Guardians)** — the first created; generals against chaos; rival the higher powers. Hundreds exist; the three mightiest are Nehcrom, Bemoth, Caliban. Cannot be summoned against their will, cannot be statted (immeasurable power).
2. **Paschorna (Paschas)** — demon-warlords directly under guardians; the basis of our world's demon legends; the mightiest demons that visit our world; can change size at will in Demonicum.
3. **Krigardemoner (War demons)** — officers commanding hordes of younger demons; harassed by paschas; **the most common demons in the DoD world**.
4. **Demonträlar (Demon thralls)** — the foot-soldier backbone of demon armies; rarely larger than a human; survivors eventually become war demons.
5. **Demonetter (Demonettes)** — lowest; weak, often formless, hand-high; treated like animals; used for menial chores.

**Demonhoven (The demon courts):** rigid hierarchy held by iron hand. Lesser demons compete for guardians' favor. Structural changes (e.g. a ruler's death) are violent and chaotic.

### Notable worlds
| World | Ruler / Theme |
|---|---|
| **Caboon** (cliff **Khla**) | Nehcrom — devastation, war |
| **Leithicor** (palace **Toroth**) | Bemoth — wildlands, beasts |
| **Calibandium** (fortress **Methucalem**) | Caliban — madness |
| **Inferno** | Prince Zahmet — realm of the dead; invaded by chaos (see *DoD Monster*) |
| **Gehenna** | Fire demons (elddemoner); oxygen-less, flares on contact with our air; source of "demoneld" |
| **Niflhem** | Guardian **Tauftamir** (Nehcrom ally); 5 worlds round a dead star; ice demons |
| **Libricum** | Knowledge demons (kunskapsdemoner); giant libraries of all Multiverse knowledge |

### Races' views on demons
- **Människor (Humans):** First opened portals to Demonicum; hit hardest. See demons as evil's henchmen; demonologers feared/hunted. Some low and some high cultures built demon-cults; demons always eventually destroyed their masters.
- **Alver (Elves):** Demons = soulless monsters; foremost demon-hunters/tamers. Elf demonologers exist but are purely evil and build demon-warrior armies; shunned by their own.
- **Dvärgar (Dwarves):** Instinctive aversion to all magic; judge demons by behavior (destroy if a threat, else ignore). Very few demonologers (only out of greed). Closed archives make rumors unverifiable.
- **Halvlängdsmän (Halflings):** Enormous fear; demons as bogeymen. Essentially no demonologers; would pay hunters rather than face demons.
- **Ankor (Ducks):** Magic-skilled but **hatred (not fear)** — verges on berserk rage. Mighty demons enjoy ravaging duck towns. Lone duck demon-hunters are exiled but respected; all trained by humans/elves.
- **Svartfolk (Dark folk):** Most accepting — close to demons in nature. Lower demons are heroes/role models; mighty ones worshipped as gods. Lack patience to become demonologers; those who do are usually controlled by a demon.

## 3. The Three Guardians

### NEHCROM — Den behornade (The Horned One)
- **Role:** Outermost guardian; first line against chaos. Power from violence, plunder, death, bloodshed; openly revolts against the higher powers and rules his realm as a god.
- **Appearance:** Vast humanoid 3× human height; sunken eyes, predator jaws, two temple horns, many growths, red-brown scarred skin; wears black/blood-red armor.
- **Realm:** **Caboon** — the cliff **Khla**, a black rock in chaotic space, built into an endless fortress (the Black Fortress); throne of crushed enemy skulls. War-fleet at *Khlas hamnar*; legendary weapon smithies forging besjälade vapen and war machines.
- **Enemies/Friends:** Sworn war with **Bemoth** (centuries-long). Caliban is an enemy who nonetheless needs the chaos Nehcrom makes. **Svartfolk** worship him; followers are warriors, assassins, executioners.
- **Org:** Hordes under field-lords and **black priests (svarta präster)** who wear his symbol and make far-reaching pacts.
- **Symbol:** A horned skull, black on red.

### BEMOTH — Bestarnas furste (Prince of Beasts)
- **Role:** Second guardian; power over wild nature forces and beasts. Dual nature — endlessly cruel yet sometimes mild; **always keeps his word to allies**. The **only guardian still loyal to the original task** — hunts chaos through the Multiverse.
- **Appearance:** Vast humanoid that drops to all fours; **stag-like antlers** (unlike most demons' growths); shaggy mane, tail, predator features.
- **Realm:** **Leithicor** — untamed wildlands and ancient forests under an eternal moon. No palace; his seat is a stone altar in a monolith circle called **Toroth**, which roams worlds with the moon's phases.
- **War with Nehcrom:** Nehcrom invaded Leithicor and won many worlds, but Bemoth's wild hordes (sweeping from sky and earth) shattered his armies. Leithicor stands unconquered; the war continues.
- **Friends/Enemies:** Nehcrom = death-enemy. Caliban = neither friend nor open enemy. Respected/worshipped by nature peoples, shamans, druids; elves don't see him as a direct threat.
- **Org:** No standing armies — hordes form and scatter. Few cults; servants are shunned by their own kin.
- **Symbol:** A grinning, stag-horned predator skull; colors black and blue.

### CALIBAN — Den eviga vinden (The Eternal Wind)
- **Role:** Third guardian; feeds on mortals' sick minds and drives. Madness was his weapon against chaos — but it defeated him; now he is **king of madness**, spreading insanity to claim all worlds and avenge his curse.
- **Appearance:** Vast humanoid, human proportions, **no horns** (unique); long sinewy body, deep-blue skin covered in shifting tattoos/painted symbols, always glistening with fragrant oils.
- **Realm:** **Calibandium**, fortress **Methucalem** — endless festival halls of madmen, fools, raving monsters; absurd parody-laws; the borg constantly shifts shape/size; only the insane navigate it.
- **Friends/Enemies:** Recurring war with Nehcrom. Avoids open battle with Bemoth (beasts resist madness). Servants/cults hunted by order-keepers everywhere.
- **Org/Worship:** Hordes of mad warriors, berserkers, mad demonettes. Popular among lustful/weak-willed humans, decadent aristocrats; cults in nearly all human societies and even some dwarf ones.

## 4. Creating a Demon (procedure)

Steps: **1 Rang → 2 Ursprung & yrke → 3 Grundegenskaper → 4 Färdigheter → 5 Magiska förmågor → 6 Demoniska förmågor.** Each step can be rolled or chosen.

### Step 1 — Rang (1T20)
| 1T20 | Rank |
|---|---|
| 1–2 | Demonett |
| 3–9 | Demonträl |
| 10–19 | Krigardemon |
| 20 | Pascha |
*(Demonettes are very rarely worth binding.)*

### Step 2 — Ursprung & Yrke (1T6)
| 1T6 | World | Modifiers | Yrke (1T6) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Khla** (Nehcrom) | +4 STY, +4 FYS; humanoid, bestial horned heads, dark iron armor, grotesque 2-h weapons | 1 Lärd man; 2–4 Krigare; 5 Lönnmördare; 6 Riddare |
| 2 | **Leithicor** (Bemoth) | +4 SMI, +4 FYS, −4 INT; beasts, often 4-footed, claws/horns | Krigare only |
| 3 | **Calibandium** (Caliban) | +4 PSY; any form, gaudy clothes, all insane | 1 Krigare; 2–4 Bard; 5 Lärd man; 6 Magiker |
| 4 | **Gehenna** | dragon-like, fire-wrapped; deals (PSY+STO)/4 on touch; double dmg from cold/water | 1 Lärd man; 2–5 Krigare; 6 Magiker |
| 5 | **Niflhem** | beautiful human/elf of ice; deals (PSY+STO)/4 on touch; double dmg from heat/fire | 1 Lärd man; 2–5 Bard; 6 Magiker |
| 6 | **Libricum** | +8 INT; dark-robed monks, hidden faces | 1–4 Lärd man; 5–6 Magiker |

### Step 3 — Grundegenskaper (rank statblocks)

| | Demonett | Demonträl | Krigardemon | Pascha |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Lägre förmågor | — | 1T6 | 2T6 | 2T6 |
| Högre förmågor | — | — | 1T6 | 2T6 |
| Magiska förmågor | 1T3 (Minimagi only) | 1T3 | 1T3 | 1T6 |
| Skräckslå | —/5 (grp 10+) | —/10 | 5/15 | 10/15 |
| STY | 4 | 12 | 26 | 54 |
| STO | 4 | 9 | 21 | 45 |
| FYS | 6 | 13 | 26 | 54 |
| SMI | 8 | 14 | 26 | 54 |
| INT | 4 | 8 | 16 | 36 |
| PSY | 6 | 12 | 26 | 54 |
| KAR | 1 | 5 | 15 | 25 |
| KP | 5 | 11 | 24 | 50 |
| SB | — | — | 1T6 | +3T6 |
| Förflyttning | L8 | L11 | L22 | L30 |
| Naturligt skydd | 0 | 2 | 6 | 12 |

All demons automatically have **Skräckslå**. Default temperament if rolled: egoistic, treacherous, violent.

### Step 4 — Färdigheter
- Start with **8 skills**. FV = (governing attribute + INT) / 3.
- Extra skill points = the demon's **INT**. 1 point buys a new skill (at the formula value) **or** raises an existing skill by 1 step (can exceed INT).

### Step 5 — Magiska förmågor
- Work like spells; FV = **(PSY + INT) / 3**. GM picks schools to fit origin. A demon **cannot** have a magical ability whose Skolvärde exceeds its PSY. Demonettes get **Minimagi only**.

### Step 6 — Demoniska förmågor
- Number per rank (above). Roll on the tables, or design to taste.

### Playing a demon (GM notes)
- A summoned demon is **furious** — usually tries to provoke the magician into sending it back or breaking protections so it can take revenge.
- It **obeys orders literally or in their widest sense** (e.g. "fetch a plate" → year-long quest for the king's plate).
- It **never tells the useful truth** unless it benefits it; speaks in riddles, half-truths, omissions.
- **By rank:** demonettes/young demons are rebellious, inventive, deadly mischievous; high-rank demons are confident, threatening, condescending.

## 6. Demon Combat (Demonstrid)

**Intel first.** Hire a demonolog/demonkunskapare. With **Kunskap om demoner** (after seeing the demon, hearing its usual name, or a detailed description) you can learn things at these difference values (a big library gives +1 to +6):

| Info | Difference value |
|---|---|
| Approximate power / rank | 3 |
| Usual name | 0 |
| Home world | 2–4 |
| Strengths (key skills/attributes/abilities) | 6–10 |
| Weaknesses (vulnerable weapon/magic/element) | 8–12 |
| Strategy (berserk? passive? finesse?) | 6–12 |
| **True name** | roll must be **perfect** |

Cost: **1,000 sm** per piece learned via a hired demon-knower (**500 sm** even on failure).

**Preparations:** ranged weapons vs. melee-only demons; ANTIMAGI + ready SKINGRA vs. magic users; armor-piercing arrows vs. heavy armor; cold magic vs. fire demons.

**Tactics:** Demons are single-minded; rely on demonic abilities/aggression. **Far more durable** than normal beings — will trade wounds to land a killing blow (like berserkers, but fully in control).

**Before/Attack:** Often open with disarming conversation to lull foes, then strike mid-sentence with Skräckslå + abilities, **targeting magicians/scholars first**, then the rest.

**Weapons:** Natural claws/teeth, else heavy crushing weapons (two-handed flails, spiked clubs, war hammers, morning stars). Mighty demons own demonic weapons.

### New professions
- **DEMONOLOG** (*Magiker*; INT 16, PSY 16): full-time demon specialist; **Yrkesförmåga** = instinctively sense demonic presence (range PSY m, CL = PSY; like a Känna magi specific to demons/Demonologi). - **DEMONJÄGARE** (*Utbygdsjägare*; FYS 16, SMI 12, INT 12, PSY 14): swore to hunt/destroy demons; **Yrkesförmåga** = +5 on all Skräcktabellen rolls **and** +2 FV in Spåra. Tattoos that shimmer dark-blue to blood-red fetter the demon.

*The three new professions are sub-professions under a "main profession" and keep that profession's ability too (e.g. Demonkrigare always has the Krigare ability).*

### Pentagram mechanics
- **Right-turned (rättvänt) pentagram** draws demons IN and traps/weakens them: demon's PSY counts 1 lower per B-FV while inside; the circle's edge is a wall of **STY = B-FV (1–5)** to beat with PSY.
- **Inverted (omvänt) pentagram** pushes demons OUT: STY = B-FV (1–5) to be overcome with the demon's STY to enter.
- **Strengthen temporarily** by sacrificing PSY (raises STY by that amount for 1 SR), or use alchemy (below). A plain pentagram stops only very weak demons.

## 9. Rituals, Protection Circles & Tattoos

**Rituals** gather power into symbols/points and release it together. Missing rekvisita = CL penalty (up to the spell's full Skolvärde if all missing). Time: **1T4 hours per effektgrad**. For very mighty targets, the school value is doubled (**SVx2**) — e.g. *driva bort demon (R)* SV 15 cast as a 1-SR-prepare/1-SR-cast spell has SV 30.

**Three elementary protection circles** (need Pentagramritning + the matching spell):
1. **Skyddsslöjan** — hides everything inside (pattern: an eye in an inverted pentagram).
2. **Skyddssfären** — blocks demons from entering/affecting those inside (inverted pentagram + central triangle).
3. **Kraftsfären** — empowers those inside (inverted pentagram + 8 power-signs).

**General rules:** every protection circle contains an inverted pentagram; nest circles (roll each separately, outermost protects inner); **one broken line = circle fails.**

**Pentagrammets fängelse (prison pentagram / demoncirkel):** like a protection circle but uses a **right-turned** pentagram (keeps the demon in). Strengthen via *fängsla demon* or *skapa demongissel* (a chain/rope binding the demon, always adorned with three right-turned pentagrams).

## 11. Demonology (procedure & failure)

**True name (sant namn):** every demon has one (given by the light to control them); reveal it in the demon's presence and it falls under your control; it cannot harm someone who knows it. **+20 CL** to summon that demon. **Usual name (vanligt namn):** required to summon at all; found via Kunskap om demoner, Minimagi *Finna demonnamn* (within 10 m), or old books.

**Procedure for summoning:**
1. Draw protection circles / prison pentagram (Pentagramritning; fängsla demon, skyddssfär/-slöja, kraftsfär, skapa demongissel).
2. Summon the demon (nedkalla demon); take control (Kunskap om demoner).
3. Control it or trap it (kontrollera demon / fängsla demon).
4. Bind or form it (binda demon / forma demon).
5. Optionally add sigill + permanens to its form.

**All demonologers must know to FV >14:** SKYDDSSFÄR, KONTROLLERA DEMON, FÄNGSLA DEMON.

Two branches: **demonbetvingare** (traditional controllers, exploit demons) vs. **demonbesvärjare** (drive away demons, hunt demonologers).

**Misslyckandetabell (1T10):**
1. Demon absorbs the magic → **double all attributes**; tries to break out and kill the magician.
2. Accompanied by 1T6 lower-rank demons (demonettes by 1T6 siblings).
3. Demon slips loose and returns to Demonicum.
4. A magic storm; devastates the room, steals 1T6 PSY before dying after 1T10 SR.
5. Demon materializes outside all protection circles.
6. Demon tries to possess the demonolog.
7. Demon tries to possess someone/something nearby.
8. Demon returns home but uses *skapa kommunikationsportal* to seize the demonolog's subconscious.
9. Wrong demon appears.
10. Nothing happens.

**Fummel (botch):** summons a demon **one rank higher** than intended + 1T6 nearest-lower companions, **outside** all circles; also roll Snedtändningstabellen.

## 13. Possession & Pacts (Demoniskt inflytande)

**Besatthet:** A freed demon-spirit can possess a body (beat owner's PSY); failure costs it 1T6 temp PSY (so odds drop with each try). Possessing objects is easy but rarely wanted (limited mobility).

**Att bli besatt:** The owner's soul remains and controls the body better than the intruder. Full demon control costs **5 temp PSY/SR** (beat owner's PSY; owner retakes control each SR). Demons prefer to **weaken the owner's resistance with offered advantages** in exchange for offerings.

### Fördelar (advantages a demon can grant)
- **Demoniska/magiska förmågor** — mostly mental ones; distort the host; usually drain 1 PSY/min after activation.
- **Besvärjelser** — a spell the demon knows (raises FV to demon's if higher; drains PSY normally).
- **Färdigheter** — a skill (raises FV to demon's if higher; drains temp PSY per use).
- **Grundegenskapsökning** — +1T6 to an attribute (max = demon's value); costs 3 temp PSY, lasts 1T20 SR; **never PSY**.
- **Undsättning** — emergency rescue via the demon's abilities (used to leverage the host into deeper pacts).

**Fjättra demonen** (via *binda demon*): per effektgrad the demonolog picks one advantage; risk = if the demon beat the host's PSY at binding, host rolls 1T10 + (advantages) on the nackdel table.

### Gåvor & uppdrag
Advantages always demand counter-service to corrupt the host. Each advantage taken = a roll on the disadvantage table, **+ total advantages received**. Tasks modify the next roll: short demonic ritual **+1**; killing on order **+2**; worse deeds **+3 or more** (cumulative).

**Heroes:** −1 to the disadvantage roll per every other total HP earned.
