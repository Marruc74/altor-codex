# *Oraklets fyra ögon* ("The Oracle's Four Eyes")

A campaign module for **Drakar och Demoner Expert**, set in the world of **Ereb Altor** / the **Trakorien** archipelago. Written by **Erik Granström** (edited by Johan Anglemark, cover by Greg Hildebrandt), published 1990. It is a direct sequel to the module **Svavelvinter** and the second installment in a larger campaign about "the fifth conflux." The original text is in Swedish; this is a full English-language summary.

---

## Background (recap of *Svavelvinter*)

In year 595 eO the Trakorian realm occupied the western part of the bleak northern island of **Marjura** to control its valuable sulphur deposits, building a penal labor camp there. In the winter of 610–611 eO the player characters (the adventurers) traveled to Marjura to wipe out the **Shagulit sect**, a band of fanatical necromancers. They met dwarves, giants, and rebels; encountered the rheumatic dragon **Blatifagus** (whose tail-tip had been cut off by the knight Arn Dunkelbrink); and were led by the native **kvurer** folk to **King Ottar's** forgotten grave in the region **Cruri**, where they found ancient powerful weapons — including the two-handed sword **Ebharing**. Disturbing the grave woke the long-dead King Ottar, who reanimated his entire dead people.

The adventurers sank the Shagulits' iron tower into lava, but then discovered the open grave of **Shagul**, the sect's founder, supposedly dead 50 years. Shagul revealed himself alive and ageless, allied with King Ottar and the demon Ebharing. When an angry adventurer beheaded him, an identical **clone** stepped out of a hidden alcove, mocking them — Shagul cannot be killed by ordinary weapons because he exists as multiple clones. The party was forced to flee, vowing revenge.

---

## Central premise

A prophecy called **Vox Ranzina** has been completed at the holy living mountain **Ranz**. It foretells the **fifth conflux** — a fated point in time and space that will shape the world's future. Shagul, himself an astrologer, believes the conflux will occur on Marjura, which he controls via Ottar's undead army, and wants it for himself (to bring the demon Ebharing fully into the world). To hide the conflux's timing, he has ordered the assassin guild **RhabdoRana** to systematically murder every other astrologer in Trakorien.

Crucially, a verse hints Shagul will be killed by **someone who has seen his true face** — i.e., the player characters from Svavelvinter. So he has also ordered RhabdoRana to track down and kill all foreigners who visited Marjura in 610 eO. Meanwhile the monks of Ranz are searching for those same Marjura-visitors, because hidden verses of the prophecy are meant for them.

The hidden verses describe **four "eyes"** — four important seer-creatures who must be saved from Shagul and his assassins so the conflux doesn't fall into his hands. The campaign is a race against RhabdoRana to find and rescue these four oracles.

---

## How the campaign opens

The party is resting at a farm in Trakorien (spring 613 eO). A wandering Ranziner monk, **Bodonius**, arrives — but he is actually impersonated by a RhabdoRana assassin who tests the party by showing them a forged copy of the sword Ebharing (only people who saw the real one will recognize it, exposing them as targets). The **real** Bodonius then bursts in, wounded; the impostor flees or is killed. The party must rush to Ranz, where the dying order-master **Peatro Petralba** (poisoned by an assassin) reveals the secret verses and charges them with saving the four eyes, then sends them fleeing through a secret passage. He directs them to seek help from the troll **Manghalde** in the Shadow Swamp, and to take the sorceress **Didra** along. The four sub-adventures can be tackled in any order.

## The four eyes (sub-adventures)

### The White Eye — Raoga
The white-furred wolf-woman **Raoga** is a seer at the court of the wolf-men in **Wulferveden** forest on Trinsmyra. She was captured by hunters from **Gatves klippa** (Gatve's Rock), an islet ruled by twin magicians **Kamalkus** and **Fundibera**. The party must infiltrate the wolf-men's society (with its elaborate dominance etiquette), then free Raoga from the twins' citadel (full of magic mirrors, a teleporting tower-model, and a gas-trap hall) before 60 vengeful wolf-warriors arrive on rafts and massacre the island. Tragically, Raoga has gone mad — but her insane singing in "the golden key's mode" is itself the key to "Store Stenfar," who appears in the next module.

### The Blue Eye — the Blue Child
In the drug-ravaged city of **Skuger** (Ilibaurien), a temple holds a **floating cliff** suspended by the invisible fire-spirit (efrit) **Basenanji**, chained by a silver thread. Atop it lives **the Blue Child**, worshipped in "the cult of the innocent one," tended by the simple-minded girl **Innocentia**. The Blue Child is secretly a "pirate clone" of Shagul, grown from his severed hand by Gobrugda — its existence prevents Shagul from making new clones. The party must perform the temple's ascension ritual (smearing themselves in honey so the efrit lets them pass) to reach and extract the child. Cutting the silver chain frees Basenanji, with consequences ranging from harmless to the destruction of Skuger.

### The Red Eye — Nastigast
Deep in **Hoskas klyfta** (a canyon modeled on the Grand Canyon) in **Björkrike** stands the **Kungsklippan** (King's Cliff), home to a bloody oracle cult of the **norsar** people who sacrifice humans. The actual oracle is **Nastigast**, an immensely fat mathematical genius kept by two evil flying beast-creatures (onaquier), **Bhe-Yell and Ghu-Tha**, who feed on sacrificial hearts. A deadly labyrinth full of electrical and magnetic traps guards him; a stone model boat outside encodes the safe path. The whole region is plagued by magnetic cliffs, "water bodies," cascade-lightning, and an iron taboo. Nastigast cannot be moved on foot — the only way to extract him is to trap his soul in Didra's crystal pendant and release it into another body.

### The Green Eye — Melealina
The dark-haired elf astrologer **Melealina** lives in a giant flower-oak in a valley deep within **Tocmerskogen** (on Saphyna), surrounded by berry-bushes ("järnek"). She is protected by ~250 invisible elves and a unicorn. The forest contains a blood-sucking-seed peril, the centaur **Kestur** guarding soldiers transformed into juniper bushes 400 years ago, an evil sentient "black primordial mountain," and the lonely mountain-troll **Grimhild** who adopts and over-protects (and accidentally kills) "children." Melealina already knows everything about the party and their mission — she is the safest and most indispensable of the four eyes.

## Major characters (NPCs and creatures)

**Shagul** — the antagonist; a master of necromancy, mentalism, and shagulit body-control, able to create exact clones of himself. Born in Tricilve, raised by a magician in Fontra Cilor; founded the Shagulit sect. Exists as four clones at the campaign's start; one must always sit at the "earth-blood spring" in Ottar's grave to power his extended psyche. He has secretly infiltrated and now controls RhabdoRana telepathically. He cannot be psychically harmed unless all living clones are within a spell's range. The module recommends a hidden player run Shagul, submitting moves between sessions. STY 20, INT 22, PSY 40.

**Peatro Petralba** — title (not a name) of the Ranziner order's leader, "father of the white cliff." A thin old man with a white beard, radiating priestly aura. Murdered the night the party arrives at Ranz; his ghost later visits his allies to guide the party.

**Bodonius / Kinquarras** — the patient, near-photographic-memory Ranziner monk who recruits the party. If he dies, the monk Kinquarras (identical stats) replaces him. STY 8, INT 16, PSY 17.

**Goba da Grummi** — a "mashmashu" (high castrato priest), one of the mightiest Kastyke priests and superior to da Kaelve in **Digeta Longa** (the secret police). Obese, lavishly dressed, intelligent and scheming; never travels under his own power.

**Lugal Praanz da Kaelve** — a noble master-fencer from Tricilve, now a high officer in Digeta Longa. Missing his right ear; ambidextrous; fights with two weapons. His deep secret: a birthmark proving he has "skecker blood," unthinkable for his rank. The module suggests a second hidden player run him. SMI/KAR 17, Sabelfäktning 26.

**Furstessan Didra Damagi av Krimbydh** — sinuous, panther-like princess covered in glowing tattoos, granddaughter of Elmesum. She carries her grandmother's vengeful ghost in her right ear. Skilled in magic; armed with a magic wave-bladed dagger, hooked bolas, and a soul-prison diamond pendant (currently imprisoning her uncle Belibak). A paralysis-symbol tattooed on her thigh enchants any male who sees it. She is also a weapon against Shagul, who still secretly loves her resemblance to Elmesum.

**Elmesum** — the princess Shagul kidnapped 50 years ago for company in his grave; died when it opened, but her ghost lingers in Didra's ear, urging revenge.

**Gobrugda / Giafrak (alias "Manghalde")** — the last surviving Shagulit patriarch, who survived Shagul's poisoning betrayal, severed and preserved the poisoning clone's hand (which became the Blue Child), and swore vengeance. Now slowly rotting alive (the sect's life-sustaining rituals are gone), he hides in the Shadow Swamp worshipping Enki and fate, attended by the horn-demon Adgurbog and undead reptile-men. He can aid the party in many ways. PSY 24, Nekromanti 22.

**Horndemonen Adgurbog** — Gobrugda's mighty horn-demon bodyguard, telepathically linked to him. STY 26, FYS 22.

**Gozcana da Skugre** — elder of the diFolt order in Ilibaurien; Gobrugda's guest, refusing to give up the Blue Child; can dose intruders with weird drugs (Magnolin, Prosserblad).

**Onda bettet (Aoya)** — the aging wolf-queen of Wulferveden, with a vilddjur's sixth sense for danger (Upptäcka fara 25). Pierced right ear.

**Raoga** — the white albino wolf-seer, now insane from "remuntra madness"; her singing is a magic key.

**Kamalkus** — magician twin on Gatves klippa; spent 150 years stranded in the acidic "Gombitakle" slime-jungles, leaving him scarred and secretive. His "sculptures" are actually people he petrified with a mirror. Wears the **Pandora-orb** talisman. INT 18, PSY 24.

**Fundibera** — Kamalkus's twin; uses FATA MORGANA to constantly change her appearance into beautiful forms; loves illusions and lavish balls. PSY 23.

**Salmello** — a male courtesan, Fundibera's lover/toy on Gatves klippa, whom Kamalkus plans to petrify.

**Kondottiären Cymba** — cruel mercenary from Skuger, drug-addicted, now guarding the Blue Child's temple. Disgraced after brutal reprisals in Milacke triggered an uprising.

**Basenanji** — the giant invisible efrit holding up Skuger's floating cliff, bound by a silver thread; wants only freedom to return to Valgus's fire-lake.

**The Blue Child** — Shagul's "pirate clone" (see Blue Eye). On the cliff it is a happy blue 7-year-old; removed, it becomes a tormented blue dwarf with Shagul's knowledge, a "dampening" power (greying colors, neutralizing heat/cold/emotions), and devotion to Tiamat/oblivion.

**Kondottiären Moliax** — jolly, cautious sutar merchant-captain who ships supplies to the Malurangu mission; his all-wood ship **Barkmannen II** avoids the magnetic Ferrofager islands.

**Godael** — 60-year-old high priestess running the merelder mission station at Malurangu; experienced animist (HELA 18).

**Bhrullist** — noble björkebening, ninth son of the sutar chieftain; a devout but dim light-god worshipper who has vowed to kill the Kungsklippan oracle, carrying holy jade weapons, a magic shield, and a strength-mask (+10 STY).

**Sigisnar av Godlar** — boorish exiled Lasemos knight commanding the border fort Lom Koranna; drunken, loud, but a formidable warrior.

**Nastigast** — the fat kargomite math genius (the Red Eye); absolute number-memory and infallible eye for measurement, but no practical or combat skills and can barely walk or speak. INT 26.

**Bhe-Yell and Ghu-Tha** — the two evil, clever onaquier running the Kungsklippan cult; feed on sacrificial hearts; if one dies the other flees forever. Both wield necromancy.

**Kentauren Kestur** — good-natured grey-and-red centaur who has dutifully guarded his bush-transformed comrades since 191 eO; speaks only elvish; can talk with birds.

**Stöveljunkare Gaddagormgald** — the sole surviving Klavykian soldier-turned-juniper-bush; stubborn, unwashed old-school warrior who joins the party out of gratitude if revived.

**Bergstrollet Grimhild** — ancient, tragic mountain-troll on Tocme; not evil but deranged by her son's death, she "adopts" and lethally over-protects humanoids. Tremendous strength (STY 65, KP 53), unerring smell-tracking (Spåra 19). The way past her is to show her her son's skeleton.

**Jättekorparna Alame and Elux** — Grimhild's two giant, centuries-old talking ravens; cowardly but useful scouts.

**Älvorna Pipo, Piso, Sipo, Siso** — four elf siblings (P = male, S = female) who may join the party; invisible, telepathic, cannot lie but unreliable; armed with sleep-arrows.

**Enhörningen Kornalbon** — Melealina's loyal unicorn; heals 1T4 KP per person/day, detects enemies telepathically within 1 km.

**Melealina** — the elf astrologer (the Green Eye); born during a solar eclipse, giving her sight of hidden things; can teleport a whole party, communicate with nature, and is nearly unharmable by anything of this world. The campaign's true astrologer and indispensable guide. Animism 20.

**Demonen Shaggira** — a psyche-parasite Shagul allied with; possesses victims (draining 1T4 PSY/week), used as a spy; can suck out a brain through the ear.

**Horndemonen Lugahs** — Shagul's own horn-demon (same stats as Adgurbog), stationed at the earth-blood spring.

## Key places

**Berget Ranz** — the holy living mountain by Malossi Lake; the Ranziner monks' home. Features the village, four funnel-shaped monk pavilions, holy caves, the white cliff containing a giant fossil (Peatro Petralba lives in its skull), a garden under a copper grate, the "Chapel of the True Word" (where the prophecy book was kept), and a secret passage to a hidden boat ("Vågslukaren").

**Skuggornas träsk (Shadow Swamp)** — Gobrugda's hidden island lair, reached by navigating between stone cairns; defended by reptile-zombies, spider-vines, sharpened stakes; contains a "mandala chamber" (a portal that can scatter a soul into the multiverse).

**Wulferveden** — the wolf-men's man-hostile forest on Trinsmyra.

**Gatves klippa** — the twins' islet with its fishing village, Fundibera's locked ballroom, and the green-towered citadel (work-room, private chamber, gas-trap hall, museum of magic mirrors, hidden treasure vault with ~70,000+ gm of wealth).

**Skuger** — the drug-ruined city in Ilibaurien (geographically modeled on Visby), dominated by the opiander drug trade; the temple complex with its floating cliff, ascension platform, and the Blue Child's cloud-illusion sleeping chamber.

**Björkrike / Hoskas klyfta** — the björkebening homeland; the Grand-Canyon-like canyon with the trading ground, fork in the road (giants throw stones by day), fort Lom Koranna, and the **Kungsklippan** trap-labyrinth and underground lake.

**Malurangu** — the merelder mission island with its light-shard relic (which heals and powers priest magic).

**Tocmerskogen** — the dangerous forest on Saphyna with the bloodsucking mispel-seeds, the centaur's glade, the evil black primordial mountain, Grimhild's walled garden, the elves' forest, and **Melealina's island** with her giant flower-oak (containing dining branch, leaf-hall, sleeping chamber, the "Astrarium" star-model dome, and lookout).

## Notable items, weapons, and magical artifacts

**Ebharing** — the two-handed sword possessed by the demon Ebharing; central to Shagul's plans. RhabdoRana carries cast-iron **forgeries** to test suspects who recognize the real one.

**The four magic mirrors (Gatves klippa)**
1. A "mirror-world" mirror that summons a hostile, hateful mirror-double of the viewer.
2. A **gorgon mirror** that petrifies anyone who looks into it motionless (Kamalkus uses it on victims he wants to "preserve").
3. An animist "x-ray" mirror that strips away layers (skin, muscle, etc.) for medical viewing or seeing through walls.
4. A **teleportation mirror** whose range scales with the viewer's PSY.

**The tower-models (Gatves klippa)** — magic devices whose silver figurines teleport people between matching rooms of the citadel.

**Pandoraklotet & Nekromorbus** — Kamalkus's black orb talisman; if unscrewed, golden butterflies fly out and awaken **Nekromorbus**, a perfect, indestructible living green-black statue-warrior (one of thirteen at the Etemenanki temple) that relentlessly hunts whoever opened the orb. It can only be stopped by destroying its buried red meteorite "heart" or one of the other twelve statues.

**Didra's gear** — the soul-prison diamond pendant, the magic wave-bladed dagger (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN), the hooked bolas, and the paralysis-symbol tattoo.

**Bhrullist's holy weapons** — jade-headed spear and morning star (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN), a bronze shield with jade constellation inlays (ANTIMAGI + SKYDD), bone armor, electricity-insulating boots, and the **strength-mask** (+10 STY).

**Peatron's brass bell** — given to the party; Gobrugda's horn-demon recognizes it from 8 km away, guiding them through the swamp.

**Melealina's gifts** — exorcism incense sticks, a birch-bark vessel that draws food/drink from trees, magic acorns that grow into giant oaks overnight, and bags of crystal-dust that animate plants into walls/snares.

**Fafalder's mantles (Skuger temple)** — red robes with a gold hourglass that let the wearer see the invisible efrit Basenanji.

**Carried over from *Svavelvinter*** — Rirba's mirror, the sword/sabre **Sturmendrang**, and Grafficanus's demon-staff, which Gobrugda may have recovered.

## RhabdoRana (the assassins)

"The brotherhood of the striped frog," an order serving the vengeance-demon **Ghumgakk** (per the myth of Inashtar's descent into the underworld). ~150 members across Ereb in cells of 4–8, bound by a primitive collective telepathic consciousness (giving each member doubled PSY to resist mental coercion). Members blacken the inside of their lower lip in homage to Ghumgakk. Shagul secretly rules them by infiltrating their collective mind.

Their techniques include the "hammer-blow" (a silent, motionless knockout punch), contortionist escape, hypnosis, and a chameleon-like ability to move their eyes independently. Their murder methods are elaborate: a disassembling scythe, garrote wire, fire-ants ("fenixmyror"), trained musteloner, red tree-scorpions, strangler-spores, **strober-spiders** (that induce epileptic fits and burrow into the nose), and **splinter-emerald** (crushed crystal that grows in the bloodstream to mimic a natural death).

---

## Digeta Longa (the secret police)

The Trakorian realm's feared security service. Da Kaelve is investigating links between the Marjura crisis, RhabdoRana, and Vox Ranzina. The module suggests a hidden player run this faction too (creating a three-way tug-of-war: party vs. Shagul vs. the state). Da Kaelve commands ten spies, an imperial safe-conduct letter, the torture-fortress at Fam Kvalvi (with its own library, four loyal mages, and the protected court astrologer Miraklor), but answers to Goba da Grummi.

---

## Political backdrop and the war

While the party hunts the oracles, the realm slides into the **Fourth Ransarder War**. The prophecy and Shagul's machinations feed escalating tensions. On 26/6 (613 eO) Trakorien is attacked simultaneously by **Kishatet**, **Ransard**, and **Trinsmyra**. Cities fall (Vumbra, Gudiena, Soblak, Fontra Cilor); Trinsmyra declares independence; the fanatical Shar Kishati **Manzonla** seizes power and proclaims a holy war.

The climax ("Stegos's enlightenment," 30/8): the centuries-old curse over the dark island **Stegos** ends, and a vast white-sailed fleet appears, ruled by **Arn Dunkelbrink** (whose face was burned off by Blatifagus) — fulfilling Vox Ranzina. Kishatet collapses overnight, and the four great Trakorian islands end up each under a different power, none able to dominate the others.

---

## The ending

The module ends once the party has brought the surviving oracles to safety — with Gobrugda in the Shadow Swamp, on Melealina's island, or to King Arn Dunkelbrink's newly-awakened Stegos (where the oracles become royal advisors and the heroes may be ennobled). The fate of the conflux — and what happens if only some oracles are saved — is left for the next module. Melealina, with Nastigast's calculations, will determine what must be done next.

Each rescued character has a defined role going forward:

- **Raoga's** song is a key to "Store Stenfar."
- **The Blue Child** keeps Shagul from cloning, and can grey the red earth-blood's power.
- **Didra** is bait for the lovestruck Shagul.
- **Nastigast** calculates the stars' positions for Melealina to interpret.
- **Melealina** is the campaign's indispensable astrologer.
- **Bodonius/Kinquarras**'s listening skill is needed for "Store Stenfar."
- **Gobrugda**'s ability to travel between worlds remains valuable.
