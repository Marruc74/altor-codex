# NOVASTENEN
### An adventure from *Äventyrspaket 2* for *Drakar & Demoner* (TAMB Äventyrsspel, 1987)
*Designer: Andreas Englund. A puzzle- and dungeon-focused adventure about recovering a stolen magic ruby.*

---

## Overview

The player characters arrive at the village **Utmir** after a brutal trek through the Shadow Forest (Skuggskogen). At the inn they are drawn into a hunt for a stolen artifact, the ruby **Novastenen**. They are fed three competing rumours about where it is, only one of which is true, and they must follow the trail through a series of locations until they reach the wizard who actually has the stone.

The adventure can be run for the basic *Drakar & Demoner* rules or with the Expert rules (EDD passages). It emphasises thinking over fighting, though the climactic dungeons are dangerous.

---

## The Plot in Detail

### Opening at Utmir

Utmir is a tiny settlement (about ten farms plus a smithy, trading post, poorhouse, mill, and an altar to the mountain-god **Org**), wedged between Skuggskogen to the west and Orgskog to the east. The stream **Lirbäck** runs through it.

At the inn **Stopet**, the party is served by the innkeeper **Bork**, who has to fetch a fresh beer-barrel — buying time for an old man by the fire, **Glockmar**, to tell them a legend.

### The Legend of Salagir

Glockmar recounts how, at the dawn of time, the gods waged war and mortals lived in terror. His ancestor **Salagir** stumbled upon a secret council of the evil gods and overheard their plan to ambush the good gods. He warned the high god **Org** on the holy mountain **Stjärnpiken**. Org sprang a counter-trap on the great plain, defeating the evil gods, and raised a great cliff there to mark the victory — the very cliff on which **baron Dargomor's** castle now stands. As thanks, Salagir was given **Novastenen**, an eight-sided ruby that grants great magic only to Salagir and his bloodline. If the stone is lost, all magic becomes impossible for the family until it is recovered.

Bork then identifies the old man: Glockmar, a magician who lost his powers when **baron Dargomor stole Novastenen**, though Glockmar himself believes the ghost of king **Tortumkan** took it. Glockmar habitually asks strangers to recover it.

### The Three Trails (only one is true)

Glockmar offers a rich reward (see *Novastenen's Power*) and gives **trail #1**: the ghost of Tortumkan carried the stone to its **grave mound** in the fallen kingdom of **Sur**, three days' march north.

After Glockmar leaves, a child gives **trail #2**: the dragon **Gasp** has the stone in its lair in Gaspepass — supposedly heard from dwarf warriors.

If the party presses Bork, he gives **trail #3** (the truth): Dargomor took the stone to his castle two days south, near the village **Siron**. Bork writes a sealed letter of recommendation to his brother **Norg**, a resistance member at the inn **Springaren** who can get them into the castle.

If the party asks around the village, the three versions are evenly spread among the inhabitants.

### The Truth

Bork's version is correct. Glockmar was **drugged with a powerful poison**; a normal human would have died, but because Glockmar is not human (see below), he merely hallucinated wildly and came to believe Tortumkan's ghost was the thief. The northern grave and the dragon's lair are **dead ends** — though both can be lucrative side-treks.

### The Real Path

Following the southern trail, the party reaches Dargomor's castle and (with Norg's help, via a secret path) infiltrates it. They learn the ruby seems to be on Dargomor's sword or in his treasury — but in his bedchamber they find only a **decoy ruby on a dagger**, planted to mislead thieves.

The way forward is to force a confession from Dargomor at swordpoint, or to be captured. Either way, events steer the party toward the **siege subplot**: baron **Gilgarm** besieges Dargomor's castle. Dargomor sends the party through enemy lines to fetch his ally **baron Lilvar**; alternatively the party may side with Gilgarm. Whichever side wins, Dargomor is eventually made to talk.

### The Revelation

Dargomor confesses he does **not** have Novastenen: a month before the party first arrived, the wizard **Dingheimul** acquired it and took it to his caves in **Orgbergen** to master its power. What *Dargomor* doesn't know is that his own physician **Domiral** secretly conspired with Dingheimul and stole the stone for him.

### The Climax — Dingheimul's Caves

The party travels to Orgbergen (the *Bergsvägen* route) and assaults Dingheimul's two-level cave complex, guarded by the half-giant **Khan**, animated skeletons, a chained demon, a magnetite trap, crocodiles, dark elves, and a network of one-way teleporting mirrors. The copper ring from Domiral's room lets the party bypass most guardians peacefully. They can free the captive elf-princess **Galdimiriar** for help and a reward.

In the laboratory (**room 20**) they confront Dingheimul, who reads their minds, casts **KOPIA** (creating illusory duplicates of himself), and attacks with a petrifying basilisk-bone dagger. Novastenen sits under a glass dome; lifting it triggers a **BLIXT E1** as the experiment short-circuits.

### Resolution

When Dingheimul dies, the mountain collapses; the party has ten minutes to escape. If they brought Galdimiriar, she rewards them with magic silver rings (one each) before vanishing into a silver oak in Orgskog. Returning to Utmir, they hand the stone to Glockmar, who fastens it to a chain and is transformed — growing taller and younger, radiating power. He grants them **one wish** from the *Novastenen's Power* table, then vanishes.

The designer notes the setting has campaign potential: an unvisited Tortumkan's grave, adventures through Skuggskogen, or a dwarf invasion from the east.

## Locations

### Utmir
Frontier village between Skuggskogen and Orgskog. Home to the inn Stopet, where the adventure begins.

### Tortumkan's Grave (false trail, optional treasure dungeon)
Reached via the **Road North**, past the narrow **Gaspepass** and into the ruined kingdom of **Sur**. A 9 m tall, 45 m wide grass mound with an overgrown side entrance. A trap-laden tomb:
- **Room 1** — Three spring-loaded swords jut from the wall (triggered by a floor plate); three dead grave-robbers lie beneath them.
- **Room 2** — A 1×1 m trapdoor over a 6 m pit; cannot be disarmed, only jumped. Closes when unweighted.
- **Room 3** — Floor plates fire paired diagonal arrows (1T4) from wall holes; 10 plates, each must be disarmed individually, and triggered pairs can't be dodged.
- **Room 4** — A steel-wire net trips an iron grate (4T6 if it falls; can be held at STO 25 for someone to crawl under).
- **Room 5** — Dead-end with a red warning inscription on a movable stone slab (STO 20); moving it opens room 6.
- **Room 6** — Servants' chamber: 10 chests with embalmed people, an embalmed dog with a gold collar (100 sm), a mummified horse in a silk blanket (50 sm).
- **Room 7** — **Queen Laminor's** chamber: a marble sarcophagus (lid STO 10) and caskets holding royal clothes (500 sm), 10 gemstones (500 sm), 5 gold jewels (1200 sm), perfume (50 sm), gilded toiletries (200 sm), etc. The sarcophagus rotates to reveal a hole down to room 8.
- **Room 8** — **King Tortumkan's** chamber. Gold-inscribed sarcophagus warded with **BLIXT E3** (triggers if the STO 10 lid is disturbed). Contents: gold crown (500 sm), king's mantle (100 sm), full armour, a **diamond-studded two-handed sword (4000 sm)**, 200 gm, fine clothes (300 sm), **diamond-studded boots (800 sm)**, and books on Sur's history.

> *Novastenen is not here.*

### Gasp's Lair (false trail)
Reached via the **Drakstig**, a narrow path winding 20 km up to a high plateau in Gaspepass.
- **Room 2** — A vast smoky hall where the dragon **Gasp** sleeps on a hoard of **4,602 gm and 20,883 sm**. Careful movement won't wake him; noise will.
- **Room 3** — A small grotto holding the skeleton and **ghost of the knight Deonor** (see below), plus the magic sword **Glim**.

> *Novastenen is not here either.*

### Dargomor's Castle (true trail)
On a 30 m cliff above the village **Siron**. 200×200 m walls 5 m high, with a 9 m tower at each corner, patrolled. A secret western path (found via Finna Dolda Ting, or shown by Norg) leads up the wall.
- **Outbuildings:** four towers (3 floors each), barracks (two dormitories of 81 beds), a stable (40 stalls).
- **Main building, Floor 1:** entry hall (1), armoury (2 & 3), a torch-lit guard room with four soldiers and a stair to the cellar (4), a grand marble **banquet hall** with two 25 m tables (5), a pool hall with crystal rainwater and a nymph fresco (6), a sauna with silk towels (7), **Domiral's reception room** with the hidden letter to Dingheimul (8), a **chemistry store** with labelled elixirs (9), stairwell (10), kitchen (11), storeroom (12), servants' quarters (13).
- **Floor 1 rooms 14–19** — staff bedrooms (kitchen maids, chambermaids, servants, farmhands, housekeeper, head cook/steward), small cash and jewellery in cupboards.
- **Throne room (20)** — Dargomor waits here, feigning sleep, to spring the ambush: 40 soldiers led by his sons pour out of rooms 21 and 22.
- **Floor 2:** **temple of the evil goddess Ariera** with 20 ambush soldiers under son **Sigvird** (21); a **library** of ~2000 books with 20 ambush soldiers under son **Digor** (22); the banquet-hall balustrade (23); roof stairwell (24); sons' bedchambers (25 Sigvird, 26 Digor — the latter holding a golden dagger and 200 sm); **Nirmila's** (Dargomor's wife) chamber with 2000 sm in jewellery (27); **Dargomor's bedchamber** with the **decoy ruby dagger**, a gilded helmet (500 sm), 100 sm (28); guest rooms (29–33); a mystic corridor depicting Ariera's life (34); **Domiral's chamber** with the five rings, one of them the real **copper pass-ring** (35); Domiral's lecture hall (36).
- **Cellar:** guard room with the party's confiscated gear when imprisoned (37); cell corridor (38); **dungeon cells** 39–43 (party held in 39/40, Norg and resistance in 41/42); **Dargomor's treasury** (44) — 10,000 sm, gemstones and jewels (5,000 sm), silver statues, gilded shields and armour, a gold scepter and crown, etc. (20,000 sm), behind an unpickable steel door.

### Siron
Village of ~50 farms below the castle, with the inn **Springaren** where Norg lives and (later) Gilgarm makes his headquarters.

### Dingheimul's Caves (climax dungeon, Orgbergen)
Reached via the **Bergsvägen** (through Orgskog, across Orgsflod by ferry, then mountain paths).

**Level I:**
- **Room 1** — Guarded by the half-giant **Khan**; the copper ring buys passage and the hint to "take the right passage."
- **Room 2** — Reliefs of skeletons; three are animated guardians, pacified by the right sign (the ring).
- **Room 3** — **Magnetite room**: entering from room 2 triggers a magnetic field that pins anyone wearing metal to the walls (freeing metal = resistance 5×STO). Safe if entered from room 4.
- **Room 4** — A **chained demon** (5 m chain) blocks the way unless the copper ring is shown.
- **Room 5** — A magic mirror that *receives* people teleported from room 21 (one-way).
- **Room 6** — Khan's giant-sized living quarters.
- **Room 7** — A rock ridge over pitch-black water holding **four crocodiles**; four dark elves rush in to push intruders in (the ring makes them escort you instead, to room 10).
- **Rooms 8, 9** — Guard passage and den (68 sm in a casket).
- **Room 10** — A magic mirror that teleports you to room 11 (one-way).

**Level II:**
- **Room 11** — Arrival mirror from room 10.
- **Room 12** — **Heim** (Dingheimul's brother) acts as gatekeeper with two wolves, **Hugg** and **Bit**; the ring opens the door to room 13.
- **Rooms 13, 18** — Decorated corridors; doors labelled "chemicals" (19) and "private" (20).
- **Room 14** — A chained "captive princess" who is actually a **likätare (corpse-eater)** that lures and bites victims.
- **Room 15** — Dingheimul's dining/bedroom; the food contains a PSY-restoring elixir.
- **Room 16** — Kitchen where the captive elf-princess **Galdimiriar** works; she helps the party (unless they show the ring, which silences her).
- **Room 17** — Food store.
- **Room 19** — Chemistry store with four labelled (permanent-effect) magic potions.
- **Room 20** — **Laboratory**: the final confrontation with **Dingheimul**; **Novastenen** sits under a buzzing glass dome (lifting it = BLIXT E1).
- **Room 21** — A magic mirror that teleports you to room 5 (one-way).

## Characters

### Glockmar
A weakened **demigod**. The loss of his power-source, Novastenen, forced him to remain in human form. Kind, honest, and stuck in a bad situation. With the stone restored he is transformed and grants the party a wish.
*Stats: STY 10, STO 10, FYS 8, SMI 15, INT 18, PSY 1 (rises to 21 while touching Novastenen), KAR 11, KP 9.*

### Bork
Honest innkeeper of Stopet in Utmir; gives the true trail and a sealed letter to his brother Norg.

### Norg
Bork's brother, innkeeper of Springaren in Siron and natural leader of the **resistance** against Dargomor. Clever, principled, willing to risk his life for justice. He shows the party the secret path in exchange for a promise to kill Dargomor.
*Stats: STY 12, STO 11, FYS 13, SMI 12, INT 12, PSY 16, KAR 13, KP 12; Dagger 60% (1T4+1).*

### Baron Dargomor
Greedy, power-hungry tyrant, likely megalomaniac; overrates his own skill as a ruler. Bleeds the people of Siron dry. Holds only a decoy ruby and never realises the real one is gone.
*Stats: STY 16, STO 16, FYS 16, SMI 15, INT 14, PSY 12, KAR 13, KP 16; Battle-sword 90% (1T10+1), medium shield 80%, full armour 8p.*

### Sigvird & Digor
Dargomor's sons, each leading an ambush band (Sigvird in the Ariera temple, Digor in the library), in full armour.
*Sigvird: battle-sword 90% (1T10+1). Digor: kroksabel 70% (1T8+2).*

### Nirmila
Dargomor's wife. Wakes and screams for the guards if disturbed.

### Domiral
Dargomor's physician, who secretly **conspired with Dingheimul** and stole Novastenen for him. Few scruples. Keeps five rings, of which the **copper one** is the genuine pass for Dingheimul's caves.
*Spells: ANTIMAGI 60%, BLIXT 60%, HELA 80%, LYFT 60%, TANKEÖVERFÖRING 60%, RING 60%.*

### Baron Gilgarm
A fair but hard-handed neighbouring lord, liked by his peasants. Besieges Dargomor to end his cruel rule. Honours his promises.
*Stats: STY 13, STO 18, FYS 14, SMI 14, INT 14, PSY 14, KAR 15, KP 16; battle-sword 90% (1T10+1), medium shield 90%, scale armour 5p.*

### Baron Lilvar
Dargomor's distant ally; mustered to break the siege but arrives to find Gilgarm already gone.

### Gasp (dragon)
Greedy, egotistical, contemptuous of weaker creatures — typical of dragons. Sleeps on a great hoard; does *not* have Novastenen.
*Stats: STY 100, STO 100, FYS 40, SMI 10, INT 12, PSY 24, SB 6T6, KP 70; scale hide 8p.*

### Deonor (ghost)
Spirit of a self-proclaimed great dragon-slayer who was actually a braggart, now forgotten. Trapped and starved by Gasp. Begs the party to slay Gasp with his magic sword **Glim** so his soul can rest; attacks if refused.
*Stats: STO 11, FYS 0, SMI 24, INT 11, PSY 18; fear attack.*

### Khan
Half-giant son of Dingheimul and the giantess **Eskel**; guards the cave entrance to the death unless shown the copper ring.
*Stats: STY 25, STO 25, FYS 25, SMI 13, INT 10, PSY 9, KAR 5, SB 1T6, KP 25; great wooden club 65% (2T8).*

### Heim
Dingheimul's insecure younger brother and gatekeeper, emotionally manipulated by Dingheimul into loyalty. Keeps two wolves (Hugg and Bit).
*Spells: ANTIMAGI 70%, BLIXT 70%, TANKEÖVERFÖRING 70%, TELEPORTERA 70%.*

### Galdimiriar
A kind forest-elf princess from Orgskog, captured by Dingheimul; a magic **silver ring** around her neck compels her obedience (removing it while he lives kills her). She helps the party and asks to be taken home; rewards them with magic rings afterward.
*Spells: AVBILD 50%, HELA 50%, OSYNLIGHET 50%.*

### Dingheimul
The principal villain: a power-hungry, knowledge-thirsty, selfish wizard whose goal is to master Novastenen and become a demigod himself.
*Spells: ANTIMAGI 70%, AVBILD 90%, ENERGISTRÅLE 90%, HELA 60%, KOPIA 90%, MÖRKER 80%, SKINGRA 80%, TANKEÖVERFÖRING 70%, VARSEBLI 75%. Wields a basilisk-bone dagger (50%) that petrifies anything it wounds.*

### Notable wandering NPCs (road encounters)
- **Limir & Dirlian** — shy elves from Orgskog (carrying 15 / 20 sm).
- **Gorga & Dargol** — hungry trolls, north road.
- **Sergo, Litar, Timon, Sul** — ambushing robbers (south road).
- **Rex & Exod** — wandering knights itching for a duel (full armour, broadswords).
- **Tob, Sigul, Baskion, Sira, Urgul** — a jester troupe with a fortune-teller whose prophecy (1T100) can hint at the truth.
- **Kob, Kol, Sirk, Dohor, Lomho** — dwarf prospectors near Lilvar's castle, who warn of robbers, mysterious rider-patrols, and a greedy dragon.
- **Sog & Dog** — trolls hunting in Orgskog.
- A **mountain giant** and a **chimera** guarding the Bergsvägen (the chimera relents if shown the copper ring).

## Items, Weapons & Magic (notable)

### Novastenen
An **eight-sided ruby** that grants powerful magic, but only to **Glockmar** (the demigod descended from Salagir). Useless to anyone else, which is why Dargomor and Dingheimul fail to control it. See its powers below.

### Glim (magic sword)
In Gasp's lair, beside Deonor's ghost. Contains **FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E4** (always active, powered by NEXUS so it doesn't drain PSY) and **BLIXT E3** (no NEXUS) that discharges when the blade wounds a *living* creature — but never the wielder.

### The decoy ruby dagger
In Dargomor's bedchamber (room 28): a diamond-studded dagger set with an **eight-sided red ruby** — a deliberate fake to fool thieves.

### Castle elixirs (Dargomor's chemistry store, room 9)
Each jar holds two doses (0.1 BEP): **Healing** (HELA E1), **Cleaner** (antidote, −1T6 to poison strength), **Slumber** (sleep 24−STO hours), **Strong** (+1T6 STY for 15 min), **Pep** (+10% to all skills for 10 SR).

### Dingheimul's permanent potions (cave store, room 19)
Risky permanent-effect brews: **Dumstark** (−1 INT, +1 STY), **Bedragare** (+4 KAR), **Lille Snille** (+2 INT, −1 STY & STO), **Magiker** (+4 PSY, −1 to all other base attributes).

### The copper pass-ring
One of five rings in Domiral's chamber; the genuine token that pacifies Khan, the skeletons, the chained demon, the dark elves, Heim, and the Bergsvägen chimera. Domiral never reveals which ring is correct.

### KOPIA (spell)
Range: personal · Duration: 10 SR · Difficulty: 4 · (Illusionist spell, Expert rules). Creates one indistinguishable, immaterial copy of the caster per effect level. Copies mimic the caster and cannot be harmed or harm anything; the only "tell" is that they show no damage. Broken by SKINGRING, revealed by SYN. The caster may shift bodies (1 PSY, half a round's movement, full concentration).

### Novastenen's Power (Glockmar's reward table)
While Glockmar holds the stone, the party may request **one** of:

| Effect | CL | PSY |
|---|---|---|
| Permanently raise/lower a base attribute by 1T6 (one attempt per creature) | 10% | −5 |
| Heal all lost KP (one attempt per creature per week) | 20% | −4 |
| Summon a demon (one attempt per day) | 30% | −3 |
| Raise/lower a person's skill by 20% (one attempt per creature) | 40% | −2 |
| Cast E1 of *any* spell (Glockmar has access to all spells via the stone) | 70% | −1 |
