## Part I — The Roleplaying Campaign

*Drakar och Demoner* campaign finale by **Erik Granström**. Fifth and final installment of the Konflux/Trakorien cycle, following *Svavelvinter*, *Trakorien*, *Oraklets fyra ögon* and *Kristalltjuren*. This document covers the **roleplaying adventure** — the linear story the player characters (the "äventyrare") live through. The parallel strategic war game *Slaget om Clusta Noba* is covered in Part II below.

> The book frames itself with two booklets sharing one table of contents: the player-facing adventure (summarised here) and the Game Master's booklet for the strategic battle (*Spelledarhäftet*). The win condition for the whole campaign is brutally simple: **whoever is closest to the fifth conflux wins.** Everything else is the difficult road to get there.

---

### Background / Where the story stands

The campaign so far: the Trakorian Empire occupied the bleak northern island **Marjura** in 595 eO for its sulphur swamps. There the heroes clashed with the necromancer sect of **Shagul**, eventually sinking the cult's iron tower into lava — only for Shagul himself to rise from his grave and claim he had been behind everything. By accident the party also woke an 800-year-dormant Marjurian kingdom, the **Crurer**, who draw power from a wound in the earth called the **jordblodkälla** (earthblood spring) and are now led by the undead **King Ottar**, whom Shagul controls through magic. Ottar wields a sword possessed by the demon **Ebharing**, which Shagul intends to unleash whole into the world (Altor).

The prophecy **Vox Ranzina** (from *Oraklets fyra ögon*) foretells the fifth conflux — a fixed point in time and space — and says Shagul, who exists as several clones, must be killed by someone who has seen his true face. It also named four beings who must be kept from Shagul's assassin guild **RhabdoRana**: the elf-woman **Melealina** (charged by the gods to pilot the conflux safely), the wolf-woman **Rirba/Raoga**, the mathematician **Nastigast**, and the **blue child** (a pirate clone of Shagul). Meanwhile war broke out in Trakorien (Ransard + the holy state Kishatet vs. the empire), until the newly-woken island of **Stegos** under King **Arn Dunkelbrink** changed the balance.

As this adventure opens, the heroes have returned from the model-world Bythos with **Store Stenfar** (a god-hero now in the form of a giant crystal seed) and the divine **rainbow pen** (regnbågspennan). Melealina has rejoined them aboard the living grey-elf ship **Bladverk** and announced that the fifth conflux will occur on the tiny holm **Clusta Noba** near Marjura — but she fears it has become **split into two points** (one on Clusta Noba, one in the Trakorian Sea), which would be a catastrophe.

### Structure of the adventure

The module runs in roughly chronological chapters:

1. **Mot Isakra!** (To Isakra) — the sea voyage and Melealina's council.
2. **Äventyr i Isakra** (Adventure in Isakra) — recover the multimandala, gather lore and allies.
3. **Mot Clusta Noba!** (To Clusta Noba) — the race north.
4. **Äventyr på Clusta Noba** — establish a base, locate the conflux, plant and grow Store Stenfar.
5. **Jorddragaren** (The Earth-dragger) — descend underground to free the titan and begin Marjura's journey.
6. **Staden under isen** (The City Under the Ice) — Krau-Ki, retrieve Rirba's items and the sword Blåbite.
7. **Damgambit** (Queen's Gambit) — Melealina's sacrifice to rid Altor of Ebharing via a kaklun.
8. **Jordblodkällan** (The Earthblood Spring) — infiltrate Fari-Nhata and quench the spring.
9. **Konfluxen** (The Conflux) — the final race and showdown; eight possible endings.

The four great evils the heroes must undo (per Melealina's council): **heal the earthblood wound**, **return Ottar's undead to the grave**, **banish the demon Ebharing**, and **kill/neutralise Shagul's clones** — all while **protecting the conflux**.

### Plot walkthrough

#### 1. To Isakra
Bladverk sails from the fishing village **Frakudd** toward the city **Isakra**, carrying the crystal seed of Store Stenfar lashed to the deck under living leaf-mats.

- **Melealina's council** lays out the goals above and introduces the **raugoner** (insectoid pilgrims on an instinctive holy errand tied to the conflux).
- The party must reach **Isakra's Etemenanki temple** to find the **multimandala** — the gods' woven "map" of the world's northern reaches, which has hung there 1,000 years — and repair it with the rainbow pen, because Melealina believes a flaw in the map explains the conflux's split.
- **Intermezzo — the dragon Blatifagus:** the dragon (working for Arn Dunkelbrink, searching for Melealina) attacks Bladverk. The cachalot **Grono** drags him underwater; the wounded dragon later dive-bombs, tears the crystal seed loose, and flies off — but the seed *speaks to him in the High Tongue*, offering him a new true name (**Nåchoberudh**, "to shed one's skin") if he gathers a host of dragons and returns to serve Store Stenfar for 300 years. Blatifagus accepts and abandons the seed on a beach for the party to recover.
- En route, barbarians of **Manovga/Lumaria/Crachz**, pirates, or the warring fleets may be encountered. A Ranzinermunk in the party will weep with joy near the seed and become the first priest of a reborn stone-god religion (and a conduit for Store Stenfar's hints).

#### 2. Adventure in Isakra
The reclusive **vävarsekt** (weaver sect) who guard the multimandala have fled in terror: the tapestry began *unravelling itself*, and they understand that what happens to the weave happens to the world. They hide in the fish-shack of Primamus's brother **Snodegald** in the timber town.

Ways to find the sect and the map:
- Ask **Syster Dobcharel** at the temple (she has the centuries-old sect register), consult the **temple dwarves** under **mäster Gulser**, or have Melealina **meditate near the rainbow pen** for cryptic clues ("Our house is cold. Below us roars the sea.").
- Once found, the rainbow pen instantly convinces the weavers; they repair the weave using metal instruments (never touching it by hand). Three weavers (**Primamus**, **Mannohar**, **Trydulne**) and the mandala will travel on to Clusta Noba.

**Other Isakra business (Piondermunkarnas library):**
- Maps of Clusta Noba, Isakra and Marjura; a **portolan** of the old sulphur route (eases the sea voyage).
- A **konfluximetric letter** revealing that **one hour of time = six metres of distance** to a conflux — vital for the finale.
- The **Geomagern's notes & map**, showing Clusta Noba lies at Marjura's south tip and that the whole island has rotated ~60° counter-clockwise over 200 years (used to locate the earth-dragger's lair).
- The **Jorddragarhymnen** (cosmology of the furianer/tektoner — the titans whose shields *are* the world) and an **imprint of a temple foundation stone** in mirror-written **Ithilgroms tunga** giving riddle-clues to reaching the earth-dragger ("a clang of ore... the salt of the tongue... a high road, not a hollow road... the power of a fire-kettle...").

**Side-quests in Isakra:**
- **Free the cachalot Grono:** lured into the harbour by sirens' song, trapped behind the great chain (lodfallet). Solution: drive him off with the worst sound a whale can hear — **Baron Angantyr Bälgnäsa's singing** — while removing the chain and fleeing the mob.
- **Acquire vildeld** (wild-fire / Greek-fire-like liquid) for naval defence — but asking draws spy-suspicion and the attention of **RhabdoRana** (whose Rung cell, led by **Desaba**, has broken from Shagul and may **ally** with the party).
- **Recruit the surveyor Gulser** (best done via RhabdoRana's "offer he can't refuse").
- Meet **Arn Dunkelbrink** (he loves Melealina and has come alone seeking her), and contend with rival forces (Trakorians under Praanz da Kaelve, Ransarders under Trugdan Järnbuk) who may already be in town.

#### 3. To Clusta Noba
A potential transport leg, but **six groups** race for the holm (see the strategic document). Hazards by season: **pirates** (spring/autumn), **icebergs & sea-serpents** (winter — Grono wards them off), **cyclones & ocean-whirlpools** (summer — Bladverk and Arn Dunkelbrink are charmed against disaster). Arrival times are rolled per group; the order of arrival shapes everything that follows.

#### 4. Adventure on Clusta Noba
Goals: **secure a base**, **locate the conflux precisely**, **plant Store Stenfar and make it grow**, and **enter and defend it**.

- **Base options:** camp on Marjura (risky — Crurer sense the living), camp in Clusta Noba's mountains, or — best — win the trust of the island's monk-schools.
- **Locate the conflux:** Melealina meditates (one site/day) to triangulate the distance; **Gulser's** survey halves the error. If the party saved **Nastigast**, they can use the hex-map of candidate sites; otherwise the search is slow.
- **Plant Store Stenfar:** the seed need not be buried. It **grows only** to the wolf-woman **Raoga's** singing or to the tones of the **gold key/flute from Krau-Ki**.
- **Growth of the castle:** governed by a growth value **S** (modified by: Raoga keeps singing inside +1; **Kmeard** dies +1; **Ebharing** leaves Altor +1; castle edge within 30 m of the conflux +1; Crurer dominate the conflux hex −1; Landakennings lagoband reaching Palamux/Stegos −3). The castle grows spherically (up to 70 m radius); if it reaches the conflux grotto it encloses and secures the conflux. **Any undead/Shagul/Ebharing entering before the conflux kills the castle instantly.**

**Locations on Clusta Noba:**
- **Byn Clusta Noba** (the village) — mostly abandoned; the inn **"Svinhugg"** run by **Hildur Ornetand**; the smith **Kuveste**; the senile **Guzier da Ollach** and the fallen druid **Perrima**.
- **Moggers gård** (the duck merchants Asimund & Gizmo Mogger), **Ödegård** (haunted; the simpleton boy **Zymald/Måsen** with his imaginary protector "Mangarux"), **Hertigens gods** (the self-styled "Duke of Clusta Noba" and the wretched Klomellian skald **Arvor**).
- **Kyrias högre tacticum** (Trakorian officer school; ~30 soldiers; commander **Kyrias**, leader-value 5), **Felikernas kloster** (cat-monks, 60 strong, masters of stealth raids), **Hypherernas kloster** (mental-discipline monks who grow a defensive **Eniaken** spore-fungus; perfect couriers; strictly neutral).
- **Crurernas läger** (~800 vandöda + 300 odöda building the stone bridge), **Noriman Bävergälls fädernesgård** (where Rirba makes contact), geyser fields, and the gas-powered **fyrtorn** (lighthouse) tended by **Heria**.
- **Munk-trust (FP):** a "trust score" mechanic; high FP wins a protected base. The monks follow the warrior code **Banzikan** (its three ideals: the oak that walls off its rot, the seed that gathers itself in a guarded chamber, the stone resting on the riverbed).
- **Intermezzo:** during the island's rotation a low tide lets the party run out to **Kalmanzerrevet** to fetch a black pearl from a giant murex — a quick side-adventure.

#### 5. The Earth-dragger (Jorddragaren)
The undead witch **Rirba** of Cruri contacts the party (via the hunter **Noriman Bävergäll**, whose brother she has taken hostage). She loathes Shagul and wants to free her cursed people into true death — and to take revenge.

- **Rirba's bargain:** she explains what the multimandala truly is and that it must be carried underground to free the titan **Jagernatha**, who will then merge the two conflux points. In return she wants help fetching items from Krau-Ki and quenching the earthblood spring — plus a secret future favour, sealed by an oath (psychologically binding only; the invoked gods have long lost interest).
- She raises her ancestor, the dead witch-master **Etorgo**, in his crypt to learn the route's riddles — but human speech there lets **Shagul overhear everything**.
- She gives the party a **rosa snäck-puderdosa** (a paired shell-mirror) to communicate during the "hour of the swine" (03:00–04:00).
- **Descent (the brass diving-bell):** down a shaft past boiling water (drained by removing a blue contaminant thread from the mandala), through the **Tjurarnas rum** (bull-tongue puzzle with crushing traps and the dying, telepathy-granting jelly creature), the **glashallen** (skeleton hat-stands; wear a leather hood with **red** glass to survive the labyrinth's blinding light), and the geometric **labyrint** to the **inre öga**.
- **Freeing Jagernatha:** the magical circle (E7) that hypnotises the titan is dissolved by touching it with the multimandala; the mandala is placed on the altar, transforms into a living landscape map, and the titan lurches awake. The chamber cracks, the mandala plunges, and **lava erupts** — a real-time race back up the shaft, possibly drawing lots over who must be sacrificed. This begins the countdown: the conflux now occurs **30 days + 2T20 hours** later.
- During the descent the undead weaver-founder **Shivran** wakes and murders weavers in the night.

#### 6. The City Under the Ice (Krau-Ki)
Rirba sends the party to her old **magitornium** in the frozen Crurian city of **Krau-Ki**, now thawing out of the glacier, to fetch three magic items:
- a **life-giving bronze urn** (keeps anything inside unchanged forever — needed to hold living earthblood);
- a small **gold flute shaped like a key** (a music key/altklav — opens locks, can make Store Stenfar grow);
- an **ivory staff with a blue serpent** that "dampens extremes" (it kept her wine from freezing).

Complications: the city was emptied by the monster **Kmeard** (the cold-being who *is* the glacier Kmorda), and Rirba's own forgotten wards block the way.

- **Routes in:** Ottar's people are melting a smoky tunnel; better is the secret way known to the **Kvurer** ice-folk (allies from *Svavelvinter*) down through the ice-lake's hidden outflow.
- **Inside the ice-cave city:** an ice-luge ravine crossed via a frozen Kvurer warrior's skates; a frozen warrior holding the **black star-shaped throwing weapon** (unfailingly lethal — kills any living being, including Kmeard, Melealina or a Shagul clone; against a kaklun it kills only one component); the pleasure-park where **Kmeard** lies frozen in the pond (pull his brow-horn to defeat him; the druid **Perrima's** presence near-paralyses him).
- **The sword Blåbite** lies *inside Kmeard* and is released at his death — initially as mighty as Ebharing (effect 26), but immediately begins to weaken. It is the only weapon stronger than Ebharing once the stone bridge is built; whoever bears the island's mightiest weapon is auto-accepted by Store Stenfar.
- **Rirba's tower puzzles:** an acid-cleaned rune in the vineyard pool reveals the **C-key/altklav** sigil; the gold flute opens the FÖRSEGLA-sealed door; the bronze urn hides among spoiled food; a combination-lock of figures (king, queen, warrior, beasts, horse — turned per southern-hemisphere directions) opens the burnt pergament chamber.
- When Crurer melt through, the cave roof collapses and floods; escape is via the river toward Kvurernas dal.

#### 7. Damgambit (Queen's Gambit)
A **parallel adventure** that, if it succeeds, destroys **Ebharing** and one Shagul clone. Melealina senses a **kaklun** (a unique, fused immortal made of hundreds of beings — so grotesque that looking at one drives mortals permanently insane) in a crater on northern Marjura, mistaken by **Remuntra** cultists for their god.

- Melealina sends the party with a sealed package to the kaklun and a sapling of Bladverk to plant in the crater, plus a **potion** (the only safe way to "see" the kaklun — it sends the drinker's ether-body out of their comatose body for 1T10+10 hours).
- The package secretly contains **Melealina's severed left ring-toe** — bait to confirm the kaklun's passionate interest in *merging* with her. (The wizard **Kamalkus** may appear and offer to carry the message himself, vanishing forever.)
- **Melealina's plan:** she will teleport to the crater sapling, merge with the kaklun while *dominating* it (becoming a kaklun in her own form), then let herself be captured by Shagul and "sacrificed" to Ebharing.
- **The sacrifice scene:** Shagul drives Ebharing into Melealina's chest — and the demon recoils in horror as her dying voice declares she is both Melealina *and* the kaklun in her flesh, offering Ebharing a choice: join them, or leave forever. Ebharing tears free, drags the Shagul clone away to eternal torment, and **abandons Altor**. Melealina frees the party, bids farewell (sending her love to Arn Dunkelbrink), and steps through a mirror, gone.

#### 8. The Earthblood Spring (Jordblodkällan / Fari-Nhata)
The undead court has rebuilt its city **Fari-Nhata** ("the Fly's Intoxication") in the **Stenfingerskrevan** ravine — the very chasm where Ottar's grave was found in *Svavelvinter*. At its centre, in a bull's-head grotto, is the **jordblodkälla**, kept open by imported fly-larvae (kråkbromsar).

- **Two ways in:** the heavily guarded ravine mouth, or **Shagul's secret passage** (a deadly gauntlet — a sluice-gate puzzle solved with a slowly dissolving salt-sack, an airless suffocating library that is a decoy of three endlessly copied books, a treasure chamber, guard-rooms, and the imprisoned reparation dwarves).
- **Quench the spring** with the blue child's touch, the **ivory staff**, or the hilt of **Blåbite** — the blood turns to grey stone and the wound heals forever. The naiad **Inadrel** rises from the blood to stop them and fights to the death.
- **King Ottar** can be met in the festsal; a speaker of Ithilgroms tunga (or wielding the senile dagger **Demens**) may reach him. If Ottar drinks from the spring his mind clears; **Inadrel** advises him to dominate the conflux, but he may instead choose the peace of the grave and help the party.
- **The last earthblood:** quenching the spring outright instantly destroys the **blodsbröder** (Ottar, Rirba, the five generals) and turns all vandöda into ordinary odöda. But several beings (the party, Rirba, **Keriaka** the raugon female, Shagul) covet a last flask of living earthblood: the party to pour it into the sea (so the undead follow it down), Rirba to drink at the conflux, Keriaka to seed a new insectoid race.

#### 9. The Conflux
Back to Clusta Noba for the climax in the **conflux grotto** (a steam-filled cave 30 m underground, reached via a ravine the titan's lurch opened; the exact spot is "two o'clock" between the cave's edge and centre).

- **Who wins?** The conflux is a four-fold point (3 space + 1 time). At T=0 the being **closest** wins. Crucially, **time counts backward**: one hour = six metres. So someone who sat on the exact spot 10 minutes earlier (≈1 m) can beat someone standing 2 m away at T=0. The victor must still possess intelligence ≥ the threshold (not dead/undead). Only **Nastigast** (and Melealina, if she worked with him) grasps the time-math.
- **Split vs. united conflux:** if the titan was freed within 20 days of arrival, the conflux unites on Clusta Noba; otherwise it stays split and triggers a thousand-year war.
- **Specials:** the conflux master gains five-fold power and fate-bound invulnerability; the two black guardian statues of Store Stenfar bring a **loyalty medallion**; and **Blatifagus returns** with a host of dragons (count scales with S) — unless Store Stenfar died.
- **Eight possible endings** (detailed in the strategic document) depending on who dominates: **Arn Dunkelbrink** (the happy ending), a **player character**, **Shagul/Ebharing** (mask-plague apocalypse), **Rirba**, **Keriaka** (insect swarm rebirth — repeating the third conflux), **Manzonla** (Ransard), **Praanz da Kaelve** (Trakorien), or **King Ottar & Inadrel**.

The grand finale also plays out as Marjura rams into Trakorien (see strategic document): **Tricilve** drowns, **Stegos** is reached, and the campaign closes.

### Characters (NPCs)

**Allies & the party's circle**
- **Melealina** — elf-woman, divine pilot of the fifth conflux; an oracle who can sense world-foreign beings across great distances and teleport to known trees. Beloved of Arn Dunkelbrink. Sacrifices herself by merging with a kaklun to banish Ebharing, then leaves Altor.
- **Aldrun (Aldrun the helmswoman)** — captain of the living elf-ship Bladverk.
- **Bodonius / Kinquarras of Ranz** — a Ranzinermunk who can study the library and interpret Store Stenfar; falls weeping before the crystal seed.
- **Keriaka** — a **raugon** female (insectoid) on the conflux pilgrimage, woken from torpor; ill-tempered, instinct-driven, amoral. Secretly schemes to be impregnated by a male PC, gather earthblood, and lay eggs in the conflux grotto to birth a new devouring swarm.
- **Didra Damagi**, **Desaba** (RhabdoRana cell leader who may ally) and his daughter **Dahobny**, the surveyor **Gulser**, the weavers **Primamus/Mannohar/Trydulne**, and Remuntra cultists **Tåtitta, syster Ystre, Pullepot**.
- **Arn Dunkelbrink** — King of Stegos, the prophesied future ruler; loves Melealina; charmed against sea-disasters; the "happy ending" candidate.
- **Perrima** — once a Kmorda-druid, now fallen to drugs and prostitution on Clusta Noba; her presence near-paralyses Kmeard.
- **Kvurer** & their merchant **Umma** — primitive ice-folk; potential guides into Krau-Ki.
- **Kamalkus** — melancholy wizard who once lived with a kaklun; may carry the party's message and vanish.

**Antagonists**
- **Shagul** — the archvillain necromancer, existing as several increasingly independent **clones**; controls Ottar; allied (warily) with Ransard and the cannibal barbarian isles; detects nearby magic. Must be killed by one who has seen his true face (per Vox Ranzina). Created a fake **Ghumgakk** (using the weaker demon **Faglang**) to keep RhabdoRana.
- **Ebharing** — demon bound in King Ottar's two-handed sword; obsessed with drinking Melealina's blood; if he dominates the conflux he manifests as a controllable mask-plague. Banished in Damgambit.
- **King Ottar** — undead ("vandöd") king of Cruri, one of the seven **blodsbröder** tied to the earthblood spring; dim and melancholy under Shagul's drugs until he drinks from the spring.
- **Queen Ottilia** — Ottar's grieving undead queen; hates Rirba; reveals the voodoo doll Ottar made of Rirba's hair.
- **Rirba (a.k.a. Raoga, the wolf/white-eye)** — Ottar's ancient witch-master, creator of **Landakennings lagoband**; one of the blodsbröder; double-crosses everyone; her singing makes Store Stenfar grow. Wants the last earthblood for herself.
- **Inadrel** — the naiad of the earthblood spring; advises Ottar to conquer; fights any who would quench the spring.
- **Etorgo** — Rirba's long-dead witch-master grandfather, raised to give the descent-riddles.
- **Shivran** — undead founder of the weaver sect; murders weavers in the night during the descent.
- **Kmeard / Kmorda** — the horned cold-being that drove the Crurer from Krau-Ki and *is* the glacier; immune to ordinary weapons and magic; guards the sword Blåbite within itself.
- The five Crurer generals (**Cerafox, Galtryn, Peregald, Trus, Hurubam**) and the horn-demon **Luggash/Lugash**.

**Minor Clusta Noba folk:** Hildur Ornetand, Kuveste, Guzier da Ollach, Zymald "Måsen", the "Duke," Arvor, Asimund & Gizmo Mogger, Heria, Kyrias.

### Places

- **Bladverk** — the living grey-elf chebeck; can submerge, dampen collisions, and feed ~20 from its fruiting branches; carries the crystal seed.
- **Isakra** (formerly Esach-Rha/Esakra) — Trakorien's northernmost city, founded by Crurer colonists; whaling/fishing/trade hub; home of the **Etemenanki** all-temple on the north harbour mountain. Notable spots: the harbour mouth between the petrified-giantess cliffs **Koljärnsystrarna**; the great chain & **lodfallet**; **Yotta** castle and the Etemenanker knights; the **Rakmakolonnaden**; **Lilla världen** (pilgrim quarter); the dwarf grottoes; the weavers' hideout.
- **Etemenanki temple** — open to all faiths, run by ~80 Etemenanker knights; rules forbid locked doors and imprisoning intelligent beings; holds the multimandala in the weavers' chapel.
- **Clusta Noba** — the small holm where the conflux falls; village, three monk-schools, Crurer camp, lighthouse, geyser fields. (Its real-world model, per the author, is **Storön** in the mouth of the Ångermanälven.)
- **Jorddragarens klyfta** — the shaft and labyrinth down to Jagernatha's inner eye.
- **Krau-Ki** — the frozen Crurian city under the glacier, on a white limestone cliff; Rirba's magitornium, gardens, pleasure-park, guard-halls, execution grounds, guild workshops.
- **Fari-Nhata** — the rebuilt undead city in **Stenfingerskrevan**; the burning graveyard, market of the mad, audience hall, the **stenkista** execution device, the earthblood grotto, the festsal, and Shagul's clifftop näste.
- **Marjura** — the cursed northern island, dragged by Jagernatha; its movement reshapes the map and ends with it ramming Trakorien.

### Items, weapons & artifacts

- **Store Stenfar** — a god-hero in the form of a giant golden-glowing crystal **seed** that grows into a black, near-impregnable living castle (animated wall-demons and reliefs, a guest-book that governs who is "accepted," a stone-face room where Ranzinermunks read its slow messages). Protects the conflux.
- **Multimandala** — the gods' woven map of the world's reality; repairing/installing it controls geography; freeing it from the magic circle wakes Jagernatha.
- **Regnbågspennan** (rainbow pen) — divine implement, now a shuttle with rainbow thread; what is drawn with it becomes real; recognises and repairs the mandala.
- **Ebharing** — demon-possessed two-handed sword; can kill even otherworldly beings; effect 26.
- **Blåbite** — sword hidden inside Kmeard; as mighty as Ebharing at first (effect 26) but weakens over time; can quench the earthblood spring and grants Store Stenfar's acceptance.
- **The black throwing star** (snowflake-shaped) — unfailingly lethal to any single living being.
- **The bronze urn** (life-giving; keeps contents — e.g. living earthblood — unchanged), the **gold flute/key** (opens locks, grows Store Stenfar), the **ivory "dampening" staff** (quenches the spring) — Rirba's three items from Krau-Ki.
- **The shell puderdosa / paired mirror** — communicate during the hour of the swine.
- **The blomstereken acorns** — eaten at midnight, teleport the bearer back to the flowering oak (dangerous if it lets undead into Store Stenfar).
- **The senile dagger Demens** — reads/translates Ithilgroms tunga; an ambassador to Ottar.
- **Ottar's crown & the Cruri gold** — hidden in Shagul's passage; the voodoo doll of Rirba's hair (commands her).
- **Vildeld** — water-igniting incendiary liquid, sought for naval defence.
- **Ezers huvud** — a holy relic with limited power (no effect on Ebharing or the technically-not-dead Crurer).

## Part II — Slaget om Clusta Noba (The Strategic War)

*Drakar och Demoner: Den Femte Konfluxen* by **Erik Granström**. Alongside the roleplaying campaign (Part I above), the book includes a parallel **strategic war game**, *Slaget om Clusta Noba* ("The Battle for Clusta Noba"), with its own Game Master's booklet (*Spelledarhäftet*), troop markers, and a hexagon map (the *flexagon* map). This document covers that military/strategic layer: the great powers racing to dominate the fifth conflux, their fleets and commanders, the geographic upheaval, and the eight possible victors.

> **How it's played:** a proper strategy game with troop markers moved on the hex-map, running in parallel with the roleplaying. Up to three players (plus optionally a "Shagul player") run the competing armies; ideally played alongside the RP adventure — by the same or different people — so everyone knows **who** is **where**, and **when**, which can decide the adventure itself. The 100-odd markers (blue = Trakorians, red = Ransarders, green = Stegoser, yellow = Crurer) are on the back of the booklet.

---

### The contest

Many forces converge on the tiny holm **Clusta Noba** to dominate the fifth conflux. Victory in the whole campaign goes to **whoever is closest to the conflux** when it occurs. The strategic game is the contest between the great powers to get their champion to that exact point in space — and, subtly, in time.

#### The six racing groups (out of Isakra)
Counting from the day **Bladverk** leaves Frakudd as Day 0, the groups depart Isakra and arrive at Clusta Noba at staggered, partly random times:

1. **The heroes** + their sworn allies aboard **Bladverk** (arrive ~1T6+27 days after leaving Isakra; immune to icebergs, no wind effects).
2. **Trakorian fleet** under **Lugal Praanz da Kaelve** (reach Isakra after 1T8+6 days; know the route, −3% to all hazards, more with the portolan).
3. **Ransard fleet** under **Amiral Trugdan Järnbuk** (reach Isakra after 1T10+10 days).
4. **Stegos fleet** under **Amiral Naramsi av den östra klyftan** (reach Isakra after 1T6+20 days).
5. **King Arn Dunkelbrink** of Stegos, travelling alone in a small boat (reach Isakra after 1T4+6 days; providentially spared every disaster).
6. **King Ottar's undead armies** (the Crurer), advancing by stone bridge rather than by sea.

If a faster convoy overtakes a slower one, there's a 50% chance they sight each other at sea; whether they make contact depends on who is seeking it.

#### Sea hazards (per season)
- **Spring/autumn — pirates:** 10% per week; pirates have 2–4 ships. Bladverk can evade (fog/submerge); Arn Dunkelbrink simply boards and takes over any pirate ship, adding it to the Stegos flag.
- **Winter — icebergs** (15%/week; lead scout-ship takes hull damage; Bladverk unharmed) **and sea-serpents** (25%/week; Grono wards them off Bladverk; convoys lose ~10 soldiers per encounter, with a chance a giant serpent drags a whole kavarell under).
- **Summer — cyclones** (20%/week) and **ocean-whirlpools** (15%/week; can sink the lead ship). Bladverk and Arn Dunkelbrink avoid these.

Trakorians always subtract from hazard rolls (they know the sulphur route); the **portolan** from Isakra helps everyone.

#### Deployment on Clusta Noba
Good anchorages and strategic land-hexes are marked on the GM's simplified hex-map (with "w" and one/two stars) and listed in the portolan. Troops can't land directly from ocean ships — they need boats or must swim. The first force usually anchors in the bay off the village and takes the two-star sites; the next takes the south tip / one-star sites; a third or weak force stays offshore. **Crurer** push to take the whole island north-to-south once they force the **Hypherer** monks' fungus wall. The heroes can't win militarily even with Stegos backing — their path to victory is to **quietly find the conflux and plant Store Stenfar** before anyone else.

A **loyalty table** governs faction attitudes (Trakorier, Stegoser, Munkar etc.) toward the player characters — ranging from "fred if outmatched" through neutral/avvaktande to "vänner" or "efterspanade" (wanted). Stegoser turn friendly once Arn Dunkelbrink arrives.

---

### The great powers & their commanders

**Trakorien (the Empire)** — blue markers. Led at sea by **Lugal Praanz da Kaelve**, an honest nobleman who personally offers at the temple's war-god chapels and tries to "sweep the trail clean" by seizing any useful documents in Isakra. Knows the route well. Elite, arrogant soldiers. If given proof of Shagul's birthplace (the destruction of his "cradle"), **Digeta Longa** will grant the heroes unlimited resources in exchange for loyalty unto death. Other Trakorian figures include **Amiral Tasseluka de Zafelasi**.

**Ransard** — red markers. Fleet under **Amiral Trugdan Järnbuk**, who won't risk landing his hot-headed soldiers and goes ashore only with a small bodyguard (2 mages + 15 fighters). Allied with the holy state **Kishatet**; may ally with Shagul. Their scholar **Manzonla** (a former Krystalokrat) spends all his time in the Piondermunkar library. Ransard's core interest is opening trade routes to the Copper Sea.

**Stegos** — green markers. The newly-woken island whose fleet beat Kishatet. King **Arn Dunkelbrink** left alone to find Melealina, ordering his adjutant **Naramsi av den östra klyftan** to send a fleet to Marjura. Internal strife (the faction that never accepted the foreign king, now backed by old Kishatet advisors) nearly tips into civil war; Naramsi and the loyalists seize the harbour and sail off. Stegos lies dangerously near the *second*, paradoxical conflux point in the Trakorian Sea — which is why Melealina wants Arn to send his fleet there.

**Shagul & the Crurer (Chrachz alliance)** — yellow markers (Crurer). Shagul, the necromancer archvillain, effectively commands Cruri's undead armies though Ottar's **five generals** take orders only from the king's own mouth. Shagul's strategic situation:
- He keeps clones and forces in Trakorien because the **second conflux point** there is (by definition) impossible and unexplained.
- **RhabdoRana** is decimated; a clone leads the remnants behind a fake **Ghumgakk** (the weaker demon **Faglang**).
- The cannibal/necromancer isles **Lumaria, Manovga, Chrachz** north of Palamux have pledged ~500 men + a couple of ships per week — usable immediately against Clusta Noba, no bridge needed.
- He warily entertains a **Ransard alliance** but wants the conflux for himself, so he won't sabotage their fleet (that would only draw attention to the conflux).
- His clones have grown independent of the spring; the one nearest the spring (or carrying fresh earthblood) has full power. He detects nearby magic, Rirba's archaic style especially, and senses when **Blåbite** is drawn.

**The conflux master's reinforcements** — the dragon host: when Store Stenfar grew, the dragon **Blatifagus** was promised a new true name (**Nåchoberudh**) to gather a host of dragons near **Yndar** and return for 300 years of service. At the conflux he arrives with **5 + (S × 1T4)** dragons (S = Store Stenfar's growth value) — an unstoppable formation — **unless** Store Stenfar has died or never grew, in which case the dragons turn home.

---

### The undead engine: Landakennings lagoband

The strategic strength (and weakness) of the Crurer rests on the spell **Landakennings lagoband**, devised by **Rirba** drawing power from the bottomless **jordblodkälla**:

- It binds magic to land **unbroken by water**, keeping the Crurer "alive" as **vandöda**. Lose contact with land (lifted >1 m, or carried over water) and a vandöd instantly collapses into an ordinary **odöd** — and can never become vandöd again. This is why they must build a **stone bridge** to Clusta Noba rather than ship troops over.
- Anyone who **dies** within its reach rises (50%) as an odöd after 1T4 minutes; half of destroyed undead troops re-rise after 1T4 hours; half of slain human troops rise as odöda loyal to Cruri.
- It conducts through **stone, earth, and ice over them** — but **not** flowing water, wood, or organic material. Bodies thrown in the sea are beyond it.
- It only ends when the spring is **quenched**. Quench it with no earthblood saved and all vandöda become wandering odöda (then despatchable normally). Save living earthblood (in Keriaka's pouch or Rirba's urn) and the spell persists until that blood is gone; pour the last into the sea and all the undead follow it down forever.
- The **seven blodsbröder** — King **Ottar**, witch **Rirba**, and the five generals **Cerafox, Galtryn, Peregald, Trus, Hurubam** — are directly fused to the spring; destroy one and 1T4 hours later it re-forms from the spring. They are immune to magic and (in Stenfingerskrevan) hurt only by magic weapons. They instinctively sense and prioritise hunting the last earthblood above all else.

**Typical vandöd Crurer:** mummified, leathery, weapon-vulnerable like the living, immune to psychic effects, sensing living beings by heartbeat (50 m for alert patrols, 3 m otherwise — so they detect the invisible but not the magically silenced). They fight without fear or enthusiasm, believing themselves alive.

---

### Marjura's movement (the great strategic event)

Freeing **Jagernatha** turns the whole island into a moving game-board piece. The titan's task is to drag Marjura (with Clusta Noba) south and slot it into Trakorien so the two conflux points coincide:

- **T=0, the lurch:** the bergplatta jerps a couple of metres west; the ground cracks; at least three ravines open on Clusta Noba (one near the conflux, opening the route to its grotto).
- **T=0–7 h, the rotation:** the titan turns the whole plate ~60° clockwise so Clusta Noba faces due south. Sea water is sucked from the bay (−10 m extreme low tide); ships strand; a **temporary land bridge** to Marjura lets Crurer stream across (rolled hourly) — extending Landakennings lagoband to Clusta Noba and waking extra odöda there — but the water returns before they mobilise serious numbers.
- **T=7 h to ~4 weeks, the voyage:** the titan makes ~6 knots south; the bay floods to +20 m, drowning unprepared troops, lifting anchors and wrecking ships (per a 1T10 table). No ship may leave or reach Marjura/Clusta Noba while it moves — **fleets that didn't arrive in time can't take part.** As Marjura travels it **sheds pieces** (Krau-Ki and the kaklun's crater may fall away).
- **T≈4 weeks, impact:** Marjura rams Stegos and slots against Moskorien/Naströl like a puzzle piece (HOXOH ends up by a lake, silencing the eternal music "Det Gambla Hafvet"). A 50 m bow-wave sweeps across the Trakorian Sea. Two small islands, **Myxa** and **Mandruska**, are crushed under.
- **Tricilve's death:** ten hours after impact a colossal flood-wave reaches the Trakorian capital **Tricilve** and obliterates it — fulfilling Vox Ranzina's prophecy of the doom of Shagul's birth-city.

If the titan was *not* freed within 20 days of the heroes' arrival, the conflux stays **split** (one point on Marjura, one in the Trakorian Sea), and the two victors wage an unwinnable thousand-year war that ruins northwestern Ereb.

---

### Winning the conflux (the strategic victory condition)

The conflux is a **four-fold point**: three space coordinates (NS, OV, depth — it lies 30 m down, so D=0 there) and one of time (T). The general distance is √(NS² + OV² + D² + T²), where **T in hours × 6 = metres**. Practical upshot: **time counts only backward** — someone who occupied the exact spot earlier can still be "closest." Two examples the book gives:

- Whoever passed the grotto **five days ago** (120 h = 720 m) was closer than someone standing **1,000 m away** at T=0.
- **Keriaka** sitting on the exact spot **ten minutes** before the conflux (=1 m) beats a hero standing **2 m** away at the moment it falls — and her eggs hatch.

Constraints: the winner must still have full intelligence at T=0 (no dead/undead/odöd). Only **Nastigast** (and Melealina, if she worked with him) understands the time-math; Trakorian and Ransard astrologers are worse and can only narrow to the right hex (3T100 m error), leaving the heroes a chance to slip in and plant Store Stenfar on the true spot.

**The conflux grotto** itself: ~150 m from the surface point a ravine opened at the lurch; descend ~40 m to 40 °C sulphur water, wade ~50 m, then climb to the grotto. The exact spot is "two o'clock" between edge and centre. A raugon male lies in ambush on the ledge if Keriaka is present; a final battle here is the natural climax.

---

### The eight possible endings (who dominates)

1. **Arn Dunkelbrink (Stegos)** — the favourite and the "happy ending." Starlight restores his looks; Stegos repents; Moskorien, old Kishatet and Lasemos join; Trakorien throws in the towel; dragons burn the Ransard ships. He secures regional peace, frees Trinsmyra and Paratorna, annexes Saphyna with free trade, rewards the heroes with land and titles. Years later he abdicates and roams the Multiverse seeking Melealina (and can be met playing chess with Death in Bythos).
2. **A player character** — open-ended, an outsider unforeseen by prophecy; with Store Stenfar's and the dragons' loyalty the PC becomes a power but probably not a unifier. (A dominating PC earns ~150+ HP; helpers 50–100.)
3. **Shagul & Ebharing** — the nightmare. Ebharing forces fully into Altor as an incurable, disfiguring **mask-plague** (maskpest); Shagul controls who is infected and extorts power over all Trakorien. His personal powers quintuple and he becomes divinely inviolable for 500 years; Store Stenfar dies; the dragons turn home; all undead become his. "An misery of miseries" — wait for the sixth conflux.
4. **Rirba of Cruri** — drinking her urn of earthblood at the zero point, she reverts to a living, blue-haired beauty, five-fold stronger (school value 30, PSY 60). All Landakenning undead become hers; she can raise 100,000 dead on Palamux and Stegos and — power now flowing from her, not the land — send them roving. A realm of darkness with empty neighbours.
5. **Keriaka (the raugon female)** — repeats the third conflux. Her earthblood-fed eggs hatch into billions of tiny insects that devour the paralysed males, then her, then everything within a radius growing 3 km/day, swelling to dove-sized red beetles; after two weeks of terror the swarm flies south, eats down through Ereb, and founds a new society in the Samkar jungles.
6. **Manzonla (Ransard)** — the ex-Krystalokrat is chosen by the god **Shamash** as tool for a future religious state; gains charisma, divine miracles, and Arn-class invulnerability. Marjura runs *over* Stegos (which vanishes from Altor entirely). Paratorna's Trakorians get no mercy — slaughtered; Palamux and Trinsmyra become the god-state's core in alliance with Ransard.
7. **Praanz da Kaelve (Trakorien)** — grows in the fate-stars' light; dragons arrive and drive off the last Ransarders. His beloved Tricilve is gone, but a hasty noble council makes him the new emperor; he founds a new capital, **Trinoba**, at the conflux point and rules justly — a moral uplift for Trakorien. He crushes the Ransard fleet (dragons burning their hippogriffs), forces a harsh peace, offers Trinsmyra self-rule under tribute, annexes Stegos and Palamux, and amnesties the heroes. Arn Dunkelbrink declines a governorship and leaves to seek Melealina.
8. **King Ottar & Inadrel** — only possible if the spring survives. Marjura's collision opens fresh earthblood; Landakennings lagoband spreads to the other islands, raising hundreds of thousands of dead loyal to Cruri; the Crurer regain living-level intelligence and learn to store earthblood in vessels, freeing them to take to the seas. Store Stenfar dies, dragons turn home, Trakorier and Ransarder flee and pay tribute. Fari-Nhata becomes the undead realm's capital with Inadrel worshipped as its mother; Rirba is walled up forever for her treachery.

**Conflux specials (any winner):** five-fold personal power, fate-bound invulnerability (à la Arn/Kamalkus — at the price of becoming a stranger everywhere); the two black guardian statues bring Store Stenfar's **loyalty medallion** (its bearer decides who the castle accepts, and can summon the statues as Nekromorbus-class fighters); the dragon host arrives; and "the gods' sledgehammer" — a leftover **meteor** strikes the winner's chief rival where it hurts most (e.g. a council of Shagul clones).

### How the strategic game feeds the roleplaying

- **Timing is everything:** which fleet reaches Clusta Noba first decides who holds the anchorages and whether the heroes can find a base, and the loyalty table shifts with Arn Dunkelbrink's arrival.
- **Couriers:** the Hypherer monks can move couriers undetectably through any enemy camp — a decisive intelligence asset for whichever army they aid.
- **The bridge & the tide:** the Crurer stone bridge completes exactly 7 days after the heroes arrive, but once the titan moves and the water rises it must be extended another 14 days — and any vandöda already on Clusta Noba turn to ordinary odöda when the link breaks.
- **The hunt for the last earthblood** becomes a multi-party chase (heroes vs. Rirba vs. Keriaka vs. Shagul vs. the blodsbröder) that can spill into open battle on the way back to the conflux.
- **The conflux grotto showdown** is where the strategic race and the roleplaying climax meet.
