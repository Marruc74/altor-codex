# Kopparhavets Kapare — Summary & Reference

> A supplement for the Swedish tabletop RPG **Drakar och Demoner** (Dragons & Demons), set in the **Kopparhavet** (Copper Sea) region of the world **Ereb Altor**.
> Subtitle: *"Sjöfartsregler och kampanj"* — **Seafaring rules and campaign.**
> © RiotMinds AB (originally published 1991).

---

## At a Glance

- **Type:** Naval/seafaring rules expansion + campaign material for *Drakar och Demoner* (DoD).
- **Theme:** Pirates, privateers, sea trade, naval battles, sea magic, and intrigue across the Copper Sea.
- **Core offering:** A complete **ship-to-ship combat system** (playable both as roleplay and as a board/miniatures game), new professions and skills, a sea-magic school, a detailed setting, a bestiary of sea creatures, ready-to-use NPCs, adventure hooks, a full campaign, plus ship deck plans and maps.

## 1. Professions (Yrken)

"Sjöfarare" (seafarer) is treated as a broad profession group with many specialized sub-professions. All share the seafarer's base requirements (around **FYS 12, SMI 12**) and can pick seafarer profession abilities plus the new skills.

| Profession | Requirement | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Hantverkare** (Craftsman) | Hantverk B4 | Specialists: sail-maker, carpenter, ship's smith, cooper, doctor, rope-maker, siege engineer; auto FV 19 in *Knowledge of Ship*. Pay ~15–30 sm/day. |
| **Kapten** (Captain) | KAR 12, INT 12; Segla 18, Navigera 15, Sjökunnighet 15, Havsgeografi 10 | "The ship's god." Has warrior + seafarer abilities; commanding voice raises crew's morale by +5. New PC captain starts ~150 BP, min. middle-aged. |
| **Lots/Navigatör** (Pilot/Navigator) | INT 14; Navigera 15, Havsgeografi 18, Geografi 10 | Indispensable on long voyages; reads the difficult charts/logbooks. Gets 2 extra skills (e.g., speak/read foreign languages → 14 profession skills total). |
| **Marinsoldat** (Marine) | STY 14; Sjökunnighet 10 | A full warrior adapted to sea conditions; may add Sjökunnighet, Knopar (knots), Änterhake. Cannot take Berserk/martial-arts skills. |
| **Matros/Gast** (Sailor/crew) | none | The all-round deckhand; handy and resourceful. Pay ~10 sm/day; pirate share 1–5% of loot. |
| **Pirat** (Pirate) | STY 12; Sjökunnighet 10, one weapon skill 10 | Jack-of-all-trades sailor + warrior. May add Disarm, Berserk, Draw Weapon, Two Weapons. Cannot take Navigera. Pay = 1–5% of loot. |
| **Roddare** (Rower) | STY 12, STO 12 | Free man or galley slave; must endure storms/combat without giving up. Galley-slave PCs can "sell" 1 PSY for 5 BP (max 3 times). |
| **Trumslagare** (Drummer) | STO 17; Spela trumma 15, Piska 10 | Galley's central figure; drumming gives orders and can produce bard-like harmonist spell effects (Doubt, Numbing, Paralysis, Fear, Fury, etc.) for 10 minutes. |
| **Underofficer** (Petty officer) | KAR 12; Segla 5, Sjökunnighet 15 | Responsible for an area (a mast, a deck, war machines, a crew); as captain +3 to morale. New PC ~135 BP. |
| **Vindvävare** (Wind weaver) | INT 12, PSY 14; Havsgeografi 10, Spå väder 10, Vindväveri 1, Vindkontroll 1 | A type of sea-mage / weather-mage; learns animism spells (Summon Lightning, Weather Prediction, Wind Control, Fog, Weather Control) and the Vindväveri skill instead of the Animism school. Cannot pick spells freely at start. |

## 2. New Skills & Seafarer Special Abilities

**New skills (Nya färdigheter)** include:
- **Bordning** (Boarding) — primary skill (base SMI); combination of Hoppa (Jump) + Klättra (Climb); used to leap between lashed ships or swing across on a rope (Änterhake = grappling hook).
- Plus skills referenced throughout: **Sjökunnighet** (seamanship), **Navigera** (navigate), **Havsgeografi** (sea-geography), **Knopar** (knots), **Spå väder** (weather forecasting), **Segla** (sail), **Änterhake** (grappling hook), **Vindväveri** (wind-weaving).

**Hero/seafarer special abilities** (replace some standard hero-table results), e.g.:
- **Segelhasning** — slide down from the mast by driving a knife through a sail.
- **Kölrullning** — automatically roll clear of the keel when keel-hauled (avoid crush/cut damage).
- **Mycket uppmärksam** — as lookout/pilot, always spot a grounding in time.
- **Sjömansgång** — never lose balance on a ship, even in a storm.
- **Vädervän** — foresee weather; rough one-week forecasts.
- **Hjältebordning** — always succeed at boarding rolls.
- **Hjältefett** — permanent blubber layer; float indefinitely, +2 KP in the belly.

## 3. Naval Combat Rules (Sjöstridsregler)

A full system usable as **roleplay** *or* as a **standalone board/miniatures game**. Combat is split into two scales:
- **Sjöstrid** (ship combat) — moving markers and firing war machines.
- **Bordning** (boarding) — fighting on deck, using troop markers; runs on a different time scale (other combat pauses).

### The Ship Sheet (Skeppsformuläret) records:
- **Namn** (name), **Fartygstyp** (ship type: galley, longship, cog…), **Tillhörighet** (allegiance: nation, state, pirate fleet, trading house).
- **Rörelsepoäng** (movement points per wind bearing; R = oars, S = sail).
- **Manöverförmåga** (max turns per combat round).
- **Skrovpoäng** (hull points — damage before sinking).
- **Riggpoäng** (rigging points — sail damage; reduces movement).
- **Bestyckning** (armament & its attack value).
- **Roddpoäng** (rowing points — rowers + oars; can be reduced by killing rowers/breaking oars).
- **Besättning** (minimum crew to move the ship).
- **Trupp** (troop markers carried, with attack/defense values), **Ramm** (ram & its value), **Övrigt**, **Loggboken** (log: planned moves & loaded ammo type).

### Combat round (Stridsrunda ≈ 1 minute = 12 "normal" SR) phases:
1. **Vindfasen** — Wind phase (determine wind direction/strength).
2. **Orderfasen** — Order phase (all actions written/declared simultaneously; resolved together).
3. **Förflyttningsfasen** — Movement phase.
4. **Projektilfasen** — Projectile/missile phase.
5. **Bordningsfasen** — Boarding phase.

Also covered: **Reparationer** (repairs), **Omladdning** (reloading), **Effekt av skador** (damage effects), **Detaljerad bordning** (detailed boarding), **Att rollspela ett sjöslag** (roleplaying a sea battle), **Att enbart använda brädspelet** (pure board-game play, with point-buy fleets).

## 5. Ships (Fartyg)

- **Grundläggande nautisk terminologi** — an extensive nautical glossary (akter, för, bogspröt, köl, mesanmast, kryssmast, råsegel, latinsegel, gaffelsegel, bordläggning/klink & kravell, barlast, kastell, kabyss, kajuta, galjonsfigur, märs, stormast, rigg, skrov, ramm, etc.).
- Sections: **Fartygstyper** (ship types), **Fartygstabeller** (stat tables), **Flaggor** (flags), **Krigsmaskiner** (war machines), **Flottor och fartyg** (fleets & vessels).

### Ship types (Fartygstyper)
- **Goldiner** — legendary vessels of the grey-elves (the sea's nomads); the **world's largest ship type** (4–6 masts, can exceed ~100 m), incredibly fast under sail but too unwieldy for the Copper Sea's tight, shallow waters. Often armed with corpions and mighty arbalests.
- **Chebecker** (Xebec) — small, shallow, 2–3 masts with big lateen sails; can mount rams; favored by **pirates** raiding merchants near port. Common in Morëlvidyn, Sivoa, and Krun.
- **Galeasser** (Galleass) — large, heavy galleys (2–4 masts) built to carry **heavy war machines**; less nimble than galleys but extremely powerful; backbone of Felicien's war fleet. Known as **galbaloner** in Trakorien.
- **Galeoner** (Galleon) — a smaller, slimmer, faster development of the carrack; no large aftercastle; very popular as a fast **pirate ship** despite light armament.
- **Galärer** (Galleys) — among the most common warships; light and oar-driven, wind-independent. Two kinds: **light galleys** (single big square sail, rowers double as soldiers) and **heavy galleys** (reinforced bow ram, war machines, ballistae on a fortified superstructure). A staple of the Felecian marine and the Krunska imperial fleet.
- **Svartfolkens galärer** (Dark-folk galleys) — usually captured ships rebuilt for boarding (orcs are poor sailors); cluttered with weapon platforms and war machines, making them clumsy but powerful.
- **Muletor** — favored by pirates from Samkarna's northern coasts; complicated rig so only arbalests fit aboard, but excellent sailing qualities; common as merchant in the Ormsjön where contrary winds make big sailing ships impractical.
- **Karacker** (Carrack) — large 3–4-masted square-rigger, now mainly a **warship**; tall fore/aftercastles, deck covered against boarding, sides lined with arbalests/ballistae firing through gun-ports. Common in western Copper Sea war fleets.
- **Karaveller** (Caravel) — common merchant ship; lateen-rigged on all three masts, no castles, flat stern; the big trading houses own whole fleets. Somewhat old-fashioned but not yet outcompeted by the galleon.
- **Kavareller** — a Trakorian variant of the caravel; longer, with a fourth square-rigged mast forward.
- **Koggar** (Cog) — small ship with large cargo capacity; one mast with square sail plus an aftercastle; used as a coaster, mainly in the western Copper Sea and northern Ereb.
- **Långskepp** (Longship) — flat-bottomed, very shallow; built for long-grounded coves, river systems, and being dragged across narrow necks of land; excellent for merchants reaching remote waterways. The northern barbarians who first reached the Copper Sea combined trade and raiding in these; some of the Copper Sea's black ankor clans use them exclusively. Carry arbalests and small ballistae rather than big engines.
- **Kyrakh** — the **floating fleet-cities** of the dark folk ("sea-harriers"); huge rafts of lashed-together vessels with metal-reinforced ramparts, built so orcs need not stay under the sun unnecessarily; self-sufficient communities with a central lagoon serving as a harbor from which galleys raid. Survive even very heavy weather.

## 6. Races & Nations (Raser)

Different races relate differently to the sea; some get seasick or can't swim, affecting seafaring.

**Races at sea:** Människor (Humans — by far the most common seafarers), Svartfolk (orcs/dark folk), Alver/Älvfolk (elves), Ankor (the DoD "duck-folk"), Dvärgar (dwarves).

**The human nations of Kopparhavet (key powers):**
- **Felicien (Felicier)** — Rich, expansive, aggressive kingdom; **probably the largest single fleet** in the region; issues **kaparbrev** (letters of marque) to privateers who may seize ships not flying a flag Felicien has a treaty with. The central antagonist power.
- **Erebos (Erebosier)** — Copper Sea's wealthiest nation by trade income; capital **Nohstril**; largest fleet by ship count; powerful trading houses on **Dakkilo**; massive fishing fleet. In constant feud with **Caddo**.
- **Caddo / Dalker** — Island of Caddo; the region's most stable power; famed **banking** (Erebs' foremost banks); lucrative trade with dwarves; exports wine & grain. Allied with **Berendien** against Felecian pirates.
- **Zorakin (Zorakier)** — Once a rising sea power, broken by Felicien's fleet; now mostly small coastal-defense ships; the Church keeps a few heavy vessels.
- **Kardia (Kardier)** — Minor maritime role; trades north and with Zorakin, Caddo, Erebos, and southern lands.
- **Berendien (Berendier)** — sailors not among the most famous (mostly uses other nations' ships); has a small merchant fleet and a military fleet that took a hard beating in the opening battles of the Felecian Pirate War.
- **Krun (Krunier)** — long traded only east of Golwynda's freshwater sea; now its distinctive ships (complex rigging, ornate decoration) sail the Copper Sea, almost always in **convoys** of 3–4 merchants escorted by 2–3 fast armed sailing-galleys. ~20 Krunska ships in the region, recognizable by **golden sails with a red crescent**.
- Other named states/regions: **Montagor** (notorious pirate-trade city), **Krilloan**, **Soluna**, **Samkarna**, **Dermanien**, **Hakkai** (island group).

### Non-human races at sea
- **Svartfolk** (Orcs / dark folk) — generally dislike water, but those living at sea are fully adapted and never get seasick; they mostly keep to their floating cities (**kyrakher**) outside the Copper Sea.
- **Alver / Älvfolk** (Elves) — fully adapted to the water world.
  - **Gråalver** (Grey-elves) — the **guardians of the sea**, living in harmony with nature aboard the enormous **goldiner** and floating cities. Superb shipwrights and sailors; they have kept the orcs from expanding and dominating the seas. They sometimes leave the Copper Sea entirely and return from voyages to lands and seas no humans have heard of; they very rarely come ashore and avoid contact with other races.
  - **Havsalver** (Sea-elves) — water-dwelling elves.
- **Ankor** (Duck-folk) — a major presence at sea; the **black ankor** clans (e.g., crewing longships) and the **Sextetterna** crime syndicate are central to the setting. City-states **Ankorath** and **Dunderin**.
- **Dvärgar** (Dwarves) — covered for completeness; chief trade partner of Caddo.

## 7. Seafarer Types (Sjöfarare)

Catalog of who sails the Copper Sea:
- **Kapare** (Privateers) — state-sanctioned via *kaparbrev*; well-equipped, near-military discipline; home ports in protected harbors; may only attack ships at feud with their sponsor. Many trading houses buy "lejdebrev" for safe passage.
- **Kustpirater** (Coastal pirates) — small bands (10–20), prey on slow coastal craft and lone merchants; hide in secluded coves.
- **Flodpirater** (River pirates) — operate on the busy rivers using flat-bottomed prams; leave no witnesses.
- **Vrakplundrare** (Wreckers) — lure ships aground with false lights in storms, then plunder; despised even by other pirates.
- **Nordfolk** (Northmen) — hardy folk of the northern Erebs; traders, explorers, and raiders; feared **berserkers**.
- **Sjörövare** (True sea-pirates) — homeless, often ex-mutineers; attack any ship; sell loot in shady ports like **Montagor** and **Krilloan**; bury treasure in defended hideouts.
- **Slavhandlare** (Slavers) — vast slave ships running captives (from Soluna/Samkarna) to Felicien and Krun.
- Plus **Piratbekämpare** (pirate hunters), **Svartfolk till havs** (dark folk at sea), **Handelshus och rederier** (trading houses & shipping companies), and **Upptäcktsresande** (explorers).

## 9. Sea Voyages (Sjöresor)

Tools for running travel between ports:
- **Navigation** rules, **Sjökort** (sea charts), and a **Restidstabell** (travel-time table, in days) connecting the major ports: Ankorath, Arno, Atrema, Dunderin, Fristaden, Gringul, Jorgund, Karakh-Khun, Montagor, Nohstril, Pendon, Darition, Sterborg, Tyros.
- **Slumpmässiga händelser** (random events) and **Mötestabeller** (encounter tables) for events at sea.

### Hurricane table (Orkantabell, roll 2T10) — sample outcomes
- **2:** ship's treasure washed overboard.
- **3–7:** nothing decisive happens.
- **8:** lightning shatters a mast.
- **9:** ship takes on water; movement points drop for 1T6 hours.
- **10:** a great wave sweeps the deck (crew must roll SMI or be washed overboard).
- **11:** sails torn; movement lost until new sails are set.
- **12 / 13:** rudder torn loose / all masts gone — ship drifts with the wind.
- **14:** war machines smashed by the waves.
- **15:** the captain is incapacitated (sjösjuk) — no maneuvers until recovered.
- **16–19:** rowers thrown off rhythm, leaks, oars torn off, cargo shifts (heavy list).
- **20:** **the ship capsizes.**

### Seasickness (Sjösjuka)
Each race has a sensitivity value vs. weather; on a failed roll (1T20) a character becomes seasick, with knock-on effects on performance. Some races (e.g., svartnissar) struggle to adapt and spend long stretches at the rail.

## 10. History (Historia)

Setting timeline structured around the calendar epoch **Odo** (year 0 = Odo's birth), measured in **fO** (before Odo) and **EO** (after Odo).

**Before Odo (Före Odo):**
- Early on, civilized peoples lacked the skill to build long-voyage ships.
- ~1400s fO: seafaring develops; trade and sea transport flourish around Kopparhavet.
- 1500–1000 fO: the Copper Sea's structure forms; empires rise and fall (e.g., **Jorpagna**).
- 598 fO: the **third conflux** shatters development; ~600 years of chaos and minimal trade follow.
- Around year 0: seafaring blossoms again.

**After Odo (Efter Odo) — selected events:**
- The **Kîmzon** peoples arrive; the young state of **Felicien** is founded and quickly becomes a leading power.
- Erebos and Zorakin rise as sea nations; slave markets grow in Felicien.
- **196:** First crusade sets sail from Kardia toward **Efaro**; **199:** key city **Kwoske** besieged and taken after seven months.
- **~200:** Caddo begins its rise as a sea & trade power; Erebos and Caddo become chief rivals; **Montagor** grows as a pirate-trade city; Felicien plants colonies on Solunas' north coast.
- **204:** A maelstrom forms between Solunas and Samkarna, cutting off Ormsjön traffic; city of **Krilloan** founded on Samkarna's east coast.
- **212:** Northern Efaro under Kardic/Zorakic crusader control; Caddo & Felicien profit from the new market.
- **275:** Second crusade sails from Pendon to retake lost lands; the great Caddo–Zorakin schism stalls it.
- **287:** Kardia begins a third crusade (target Morëlvidyn); Zorakin's king is slain.
- **292:** A Kardic fleet invades Caddo; Caddo pays reparations and loses much of its fleet in a magical storm.
- **310:** Felicien falls and becomes a vassal of **Dermanien**; Caddo & Erebos become the sea's dominant powers.

## 11. Cities (Städer)

### Montagor — "the Copper Sea's pearl" (pirate free-city)
- Built on small islands of the **Hakkai** archipelago, much of it pile-driven onto the seabed; founded **174 eO** when a Zorakian expedition discovered freshwater springs there and built a harbor/fleet base. It served as a base for the four crusades (196–353 eO).
- Its perfect mid-sea position turned it into the ideal trade hub; merchants met to swap cargoes "halfway," sparing long voyages. An exotic, charismatic port of canals and vaulted bridges.
- **The Pirate Society (Piratsamfundet):** As wealth grew, pirates flocked in. After Hakkai was bought free from the Felecian crown in **576 eO** and Montagor declared independent, the pirate captain **Dorovich Rauto "Blodskägg" (Bloodbeard)** negotiated full citizenship for the pirates in exchange for duties — not attacking ships tied to the city's trade, and defending the city if attacked (the city even issues its own **letters of marque**). This founded pirate-city Montagor.
- **Year 392 eO:** the Zorakian fleet was crushed by Felicien at the **sea-battle of Selim**, forcing Zorakin to cede Hakkai.
- **The Academy (Akademin):** schools of swordmastery (mästeri), war-magic (krigsmagi), and wind-weaving (vindväveri); students attend ~5 hrs/day plus library study; term fee ~6,500 sm (≈50 XP). Idle students are nicknamed the *"ack-ackerna."*
- **The Harbor (Hamnen):** one of the Copper Sea's largest and busiest; piers extend into the basin forming a labyrinth with the islands' canals; harbor tax ~5 sm/day at the quay, 2 sm/day at anchor (not for locals or pirate-owned ships). Rough by night — shanghaiing and brawls are common.
- **Ankornas pir (the Ducks' Pier):** the liveliest quarter, centered on the ankor community and ruled in part by the **"Svarta ankklanen"** ("Black Duck Brotherhood"); a teeming bazaar of exotic goods at bargain prices.
- **Öarna (the Isles):** the most picturesque, canal-laced district — small docks and boathouses by day; a smugglers' haunt by night where the city's notorious shanghaiings happen.

### Darition — Felecien's southern naval outpost
- A major Felecian war port; the gathering point for the huge galley fleet. Its harbor basin is protected by the treacherous **"Daritionlabyrinten."** In the campaign, the Felecian fleet's elite (32 galleasses of the *havskåren*) musters here.

### Karakh-Khun
- A third detailed city in the supplement (with its own map and locations).

## 12. People & Creatures (Personer och varelser)

Ready-to-use NPCs and a **bestiary** of *Sjöodjur och andra havsvarelser* (sea monsters and other sea creatures). Notable named characters include:

- **Grimbad** — a brutal orch pirate captain. ~STY 23, KP 19, KAR 17, STO 21; profession Seafarer (Captain); key skills Navigera 6, Övertala (Persuade) 18, Stridsslága 21. Immune to fear and radiates courage. Bounties on his head: 3,500 sm from the Sextetterna (the ankor crime syndicate); 10 gold + 40 jugs of wine from House Dunfjat of Montagor.
- **Den döende hinden** ("The Dying Hind") — a **ghost ship** that appears in dense fog flying a black plague flag; legend tells of **Kapten Död** (Captain Death), a gambler cursed with eternal life who now sails plague-stricken in all waters. Any corpse drifting near the ship is hoisted aboard and animated as undead crew. The whole crew can be destroyed — **except the captain himself**, who always reforms aboard.
- **Kapten Död** (Captain Death) — undead captain (~180 cm of rotted flesh and black skin) in a fine wine-red velvet outfit and tall leather boots, wielding a black two-handed sword. ~STY 16, KP 8, INT/SMI/STO ~15; key skills Bordning 18, Tvåhandssvärd 18, Dolk 22, Segla B4.

### Bestiary — Sea creatures (Sjöodjur)
Most sea life is peaceful, but the civilized races can be attacked while traveling.
- **Dödsfisk** ("Death-fish") — common shoaling predator fish (40–300 at once); ~STY 10, KP 8, bite (1T4, FV 15); fast swimmer (S23). Swarm the survivors of wrecks.
- **Alegni** — a very rare, harmless mammal that became sea-dwelling; 10–15 m long, lives on seaweed (tång) in vast kelp areas, travels in small pods over huge distances. Sailors consider sighting one a good omen. ~STY 24, KP 32, claws (1T6) and bite (1T6), natural armor 3.
- **Rammval** ("Ram-whale") — a distant relative of toothed whales / orca; looks like a giant orca whose markings have changed, with a cartilage outgrowth on the spine (the "ram"). Charges at high speed to gore prey — and small ships — repeatedly. **Huge:** ~STY 80, KP 72, STO 75; ram (3T6, FV 12), bite (1T6, FV 15); Spot Danger 14, Swim B5, Navigate 19; natural armor 4; movement S25. Hunts giant squid and sea-serpents.
- **Sjöormar** (Sea-serpents) — a whole family of specialized predators found in all waters, from polar cold to tropical currents; can grow aggressive when disturbed (especially in hunting/mating season). Two common kinds:
  - **Eldorm** ("Fire-serpent") — the best-known; long feeler-tendrils ("spröt") along its sides emit a powerful **electric pulse** to stun or kill prey, even setting objects alight or charring flesh. Up to **30–40 m**. ~STY 40, KP 30, STO 40; bite (1T8, FV 15), constrict (1T6, FV 12), electric tendril (1T10, FV 8); natural armor 4; movement S28.
  - **Stormorm** ("Storm-serpent") — the **largest** sea-serpent (up to **45–50 m**); a powerful body makes it an unbeatable swimmer; surfaces in heavy seas (hence the name), disorienting fishermen. Relatively peaceable unless provoked. ~STY 40, KP 40, STO 50; bite (1T8, FV 15), constrict (1T6, FV 12); natural armor 4; movement S32.
- **Raugoner** — a race of **sea-dwelling insectoids** that devour driftwood and ship-debris indiscriminately. They build their own floating, discus-shaped **nests** at sea from saliva-bonded driftwood and wreckage, riddled with passages and chambers and steerable via "sea-tulip" appendages on the underside. Four castes per colony: **queens/honor** (4–7, lay the eggs, can live ~150 years), **warriors/krigare**, **swimmers/simmare** (~20, ~as smart as a house pet, gather building material from the deep), and **workers/arbetare**. The grey-elves sometimes trade with raugon colonies out of curiosity; lore claims raugoner descend from grey-elf tales, communicate via pure sound and scent (antenna signals), and have a script of notched bones — likely the same writing found on the islands the "rattling races" call **Maminira me delema**.

## 13. Adventure Hooks (Intriger)

A toolbox of campaign seeds (combinable):
- **Handelsresande** — life as travelling merchants; secure trade deals, deliver goods on time.
- **Sjörövare** — playing pirates living "for the day," hunted by warships and bounty-priced.
- **Shanghajning** — PCs drugged and press-ganged onto a ship; possibly marooned after a wreck.
- **Myteri** — the PCs' ship faces a mutiny; join it or fight it.
- **Skattkarta** — the party gets a pirate treasure map; complications over who else knows.
- **Piratkrig** — the PCs get pulled into a full-blown pirate war.
- **Kaparbrev** — the PCs buy a letter of marque and turn to (legal) raiding.
- **Underliga laster** — carrying strange/illegal cargo (embargoed goods, weapons, political fugitives, mercenaries), e.g.:
  - **Lik i lasten** ("Corpse in the cargo") — a captain is hired by cloaked strangers to smuggle heavy sealed crates aboard at night; the cargo is sinister (variants: political body-disposal, a demon-worshipping cult needing the body for a sacrifice, or **six vampires** fleeing their old hunting grounds — with an undead vampire-hunter potentially in pursuit).
  - **Val-/monsterfiske** — joining fishermen hunting whales or a fearsome sea-monster.
  - **Upptäcktsresa** — a long voyage of discovery that can frame a whole campaign, taking the heroes beyond all known borders.

## 14. Main Campaign — *Det Feleciska Piratkriget* (The Felecian Pirate War)

A campaign-scale backdrop (sized like the published "Den nidländska reningen"), meant to be elaborated freely by the GM.

**Setup (year EO 615, high summer):**
- The long-anticipated conflict between **Felicien** and its Copper Sea rivals breaks out.
- After privateer attacks escalate, Felicien's war fleet strikes three **Berendien** west-coast harbor cities: **Atrema, Entika, and Ventimiglia.**
- The mighty **galleasses** and galleys crush moored enemy ships (including vessels of several other Copper Sea nations) in surprise attacks; cities are burned and looted; **Ventimiglia** suffers a civilian massacre.
- The assault is both a direct attack on Berendien and a gross provocation against many states whose ships were sunk indiscriminately — devastating trade under the new Caddo–Berendien–Erebos treaties.

**The build-up (Upptakten):**
- **Erebos** (in Nohstril) reacts hardest; its **Femhusrådet** (Council of Five Houses) mobilizes economically and pushes for a joint command/admiral.
- A fragile alliance, **"Den trehövdade alliansen"** (The Three-Headed Alliance), forms under Erebosian admiral **Eyndvind**, joining **Erebos, Caddo, and Berendien**; Zorakin lends a few warships; envoys are even sent to the **ankor** (duck-folk) of Dunderin and Ankorath. Harbor defenses are reinforced with war machines and bought sling-machines from Zorakin.
- **Montagor** stays diplomatically neutral via non-aggression pacts, freeing its pirate captains to swoop on whichever side's fleeing wreckage promises the most loot.
- The **Ankor** city-states (Ankorath & Dunderin) decline formal alliance but secretly fear Felecian expansion; the **Sextetterna** (ankor crime syndicate) quietly declares "war" on Felicien, sending pirate fleets to sabotage and demoralize, secretly backed by Montagor's ducks.
- **Felicien** gathers a huge galley fleet (**over 500 ships**) at and around the port city of **Darition**; the rest guards Felecian waters and stations at Koalintviken.

**The current situation (Nuläget):**
- The Three-Headed Alliance launches a surprise dawn assault on the Felecian fleet outside **Darition** — but the fleet is too well-organized to be caught unready.
- Hidden by mist, Erebosian/Caddo galleasses rain incendiary sling-bolts into the harbor basin; the dense Felecian formation spreads fire fast.
- Felecian ships then ram the attackers and lure them at speed into a **labyrinth** of harbor defenses (the treacherous "Daritionlabyrinten"), where the assault stalls — ships jam together in the southern channel while heavy galleasses meet the three galleys driven into the northern channel.

This sets up an open-ended naval war the GM and players can carry forward.
