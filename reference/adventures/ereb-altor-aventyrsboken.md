# Ereb Altor: Äventyrsboken — Reference Summary

The Ereb Altor Adventure Book, a collection of two linked adventures for 3-6
player characters, written chiefly for Expert Drakar och Demoner. The first is
meant to be run before the second, and both sit in the Copper Sea (Kopparhavet)
region, tied to Hynsolge and Erebos. Original-language source notes for table
reference; not a reproduction.

---

## 1. Enhörningen och Drakormen — The Unicorn and the Dragon-Serpent

The player characters, short of coin, take the mercenary's shilling in any
Copper Sea port and ship to Hynsolge to fight for the rebel New Army of Abrahim
Stenklo against Baron Rigurt Riikov of Brangoria. The civil war hides a darker
plot: the baron's adviser Parikila Omurtaag is a half-demon two centuries old,
son of the demon Onaabys, who leads the astrologer cult called the Star Lore.
The cult means to carry a fallen comet (the Red Stone, fallen in Gormowac's
forest during the third conflux) to a hidden temple beneath Gråborg, work a
ritual, and teleport the stone and Parikila away to Soluna. The heroes pick up
the thread at the inn the Dead Rooster, follow a sealed message under the
baron's dragon-serpent seal to a possessed priestess and a graveyard ritual, and
trace poisoned wells, sabotaged beer-casks and a buried comet to the underground
temple. The rising is decided in the lemon groves between Gråborg and the
baron's castle, the rebel white-unicorn banner against the baron's red
dragon-serpent.

- **People:** Abrahim Stenklo (rebel leader, baron of Ansorvia), Parikila
  Omurtaag / Tervel (half-demon adviser), Baron Rigurt Riikov of Brangoria,
  Eb-Bolthar (old axe-priest sent to stop Parikila), Aldred Stenwaard (adventurer
  and secret werewolf), Salah Guldhiärta (his spell-casting beloved), Serter and
  Agila Dubbelhand, Otho, Morkil "the Rooster" Kregebo, Maurelin (possessed
  priestess), Dergvol Myhene (thieves' ring), Ordril Togvane, Mabel Sober,
  Helper Adrewol, Hantvir Slägga, Angus Grimtand, Korumil Gormowac (blood-elf),
  Boris Guldstenck, the demon Onaabys.
- **Places:** Hynsolge, Gråborg, Brangoria, Galdinor, Gormowac's forest, Soluna,
  the Dead Rooster inn, the Anyak / Augusta / Fingal / Bolgove temples.
- **Items:** Kred-Bar-Onaabys (Parikila's soul-drinking sword), the Red Stone
  (the comet), portin (the well poison), the elixir recipe.

## 2. The Oathbreakers of Erebos

In the port of Nohstril the heroes talk the ruined merchant Erbolsus Ödvinsson
down from a bridge. His cargo of dwarf-forged swords, traded from the Nida
mountains and promised to a Zorakin duke, never reached port; his insurer denied
the security fee; his office was burned, his son kidnapped, his wife and other
son gone. He begs the heroes to clear his name. The truth: the trading house of
Hinkare, one of the Five Houses that rule Erebos, sold his whole cargo to enemy
Felicien, then forced the surety-man Steinar Rödnäbb to burn the proof and sent
the toughs Lannos and Erwin to torch the office and take the boy. Erbolsus's own
ship, the Sea Swallow, rides in harbour repainted as the Wave-Cleaver. The heroes
gather proof, seize the youngest brother Valentin Hinkare, and let Erbolsus
denounce the house in the city square, after which the rival House of Pestglade
moves to cast the Hinkares out of Erebos.

- **People:** Erbolsus Ödvinsson, Valentin and Jürgen Hinkare, Steinar Rödnäbb
  (duck surety-man), Lannos and Erwin (harbour toughs), Derdil Lintott (clerk),
  Nithard Silverskiöld, the slaves Bhismall and Abdhull.
- **Places:** Erebos, Nohstril, the river Wolp, the Pelenos headland.
- **Houses & ships:** the House of Hinkare, the House of Pestglade, the Council
  of Five Houses, the ship Sea Swallow (renamed Wave-Cleaver), Felicien.
