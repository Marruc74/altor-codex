# Spindelkonungens Pyramid — GM Reference

*An adventure for **Drakar & Demoner** (Dragons & Demons). Includes the bonus adventure **Skelettbyns hemlighet** ("The Skeleton Village's Secret").*

- **Publisher / Copyright:** TAMB Äventyrsspel HB, 1983–1984
- **Pyramid adventure by:** Anders Johansson (tips: Eva Kingsepp, Mikael Kuniholm; editing: Johan Arve, Fredrik Malmberg; art: Nils Gulliksson, Klas Berndal)
- **Bonus adventure by:** Roger Undhagen (previously published in *Sinkadus* nr 1)
- **Party size:** 4–6 weak or 2–3 strong characters (both adventures)
- **Requires:** the *Drakar & Demoner* core rules — this is not a standalone game

> Summary written in English; all proper nouns, stat blocks, formulas, and game terms are kept in the original Swedish so it stays usable at the table.

## Quick-reference legend

**Stats:** STY = Styrka (Strength) · STO = Storlek (Size) · INT = Intelligens · SMI = Smidighet (Agility) · FYS = Fysik (Constitution) · PSY = Psyke

**Combat & body:** SR = Stridsrunda (combat round) · KP / Kroppspoäng = hit points · AP / Absorptionspoäng = armor absorption · Pansar = armor · Parering/Parad = parry · Skada = damage · Vapen = weapon · Förflyttning/Fart = movement · Förmågor = abilities

**Dice:** 1T6 = d6, 1T8 = d8, 1T100 / T100 = d100, etc.

**Currency:** km (kopparmynt, copper) < sm (silvermynt, silver) < gm (guldmynt, gold); 1 gm = 10 sm = 100 km. *sp = silverpenningar* (used in the bonus adventure). **BEP** = weight/carry unit; 200 gold coins ≈ 1 BEP, a chess set ≈ 4 BEP.

**Skills/formulas referenced:** KLÄTTRA (Climb), HOPPA (Jump), SIMMA (Swim), FINNA DOLDA TING (Find Hidden Things), PUTSA (Polish), GRUVARBETE (Mining), HELA (Heal), MÖRKER (Darkness), BLIXT (Lightning), FÖRTROLLA VAPEN (Enchant Weapon), SKINGRING (Dispel), Skattkunskap (Treasure Lore), Historia.

**Room write-up format used throughout:** *Överblick* (overview, with dimensions L×W×H), *Dolda ting* (hidden things), *Skatter* (treasure), *Bestar* (creatures), *SL* (GM notes).

# ADVENTURE 1 — Spindelkonungens Pyramid

## Legend of the Spider King (Arach)

- The Spider King was a magician and later king under the name **Arach**, who united nearly all desert tribes to attack Detwach. His raids in all four directions terrified the region; his bodyguard rode enormous spiders into battle.
- At the peak of his power he died suddenly (a magical experiment, per the back cover). He was buried in a gigantic pyramid near **Cerand**, guarded by countless spiders and demons.
- His empire collapsed in a bloody civil war. The pyramid has stood untouched ~400 years, casting a dark shadow over the caravan road to Faar.
- **Location:** ~1 *mil* (≈10 km) southeast of the caravan city Cerand, at the edge of the vast, dangerous, nomad-infested **Toorra** desert (a link between Detwach, Gwarch, and distant Faar). The pyramid sits ~200 m off the caravan road; terrain is open, flat, ~40 °C in shade.

## Player information (the hook)

- In Cerand the party is hired by an old man, **Assar Besvärjaren** ("Assar the Conjurer"), to retrieve **three jade statues** hidden in the pyramid.
- **Pay (in advance):** 500 gm + a ring giving **+20 to SIMMA**.
- His three tips:
  1. A communication shaft runs through the whole pyramid.
  2. There are several **false** treasure chambers.
  3. The last guardian of the treasure is, by legend, the giant spider **Lepera**.
- **Optional starting aid:** if the party are beginners/suspicious, Assar can give the strongest warrior a healing drink (1 SR to drink, works as a 1st-grade HELA).

## Entering the pyramid

- Each tier is 3 m high. A wide staircase on the south side leads up, flanked near the top by two statues. To get in, the party must either climb the tiers (roll **KLÄTTRA**; failure = 1T6 damage) or take the stairs.
- The adventure proper begins at **Room 1 (The Top)** once the party reaches the 7th tier.
- **It's pitch-dark underground** — a torch reveals ~5 m at best. Track lighting and *who holds the torch*. Spider King's servants are mostly dumb and aggressive; iGunther's treasure-hunters are a relatively peaceful bunch.

## Pyramid rooms (1–20)

### 1. Toppen (The Top)
- **Överblick:** Top tier 12×12 m; the 8th tier is a 6×6×3 m box. Two statues of naked women with vile bronze insect-heads flank the stairs. A polished **stone disc** (door) is set in the wall ahead.
- **Dolda ting:** The disc is practically indestructible. Above it sits a verdigris copper plate with **three copper spiders** and a verse in Old Toorranian (read on **INT×5** on T100; +20 if learned):
  > *"Sist vet inte vart / Letar runt i fingrars tal. / En vill till gudar upp / Andren vill in i bergets skal."*
- **Lock solution:** turn the **lower** spider **ten** turns ("fingers' number"), press the **middle** one **in** ("into the mountain's shell"), turn the **first** one **up** ("to the gods up"). The disc sinks silently; a rank stench escapes.
- **Bestar:** Two giant bird-spiders attack while the party works the lock. If the party isn't on guard, they get a free first SR.
  - **SPINN** — STY 10 STO 22 INT 1 / SMI 14 FYS 5 PSY 11 · Bett 65% / 1T6+2
  - **DELL** — STY 9 STO 19 INT 1 / SMI 15 FYS 5 PSY 12 · Bett 70% / 1T6+2
  - Both: skin absorbs 3, Förflyttning 14 m/SR, **HOPPA 85%**. On a hit, poison also strikes (always at 13 on the resistance table vs target's FYS); if it penetrates, target loses **1T4 SMI**, regained at only 1/week of rest.
- **SL:** Help baffled players with an *idé-kast*. Shock them with the spiders, give them a round, have fun. If one spider dies, let the other flee.

### 2. Kammaren (The Chamber)
- **Överblick:** 5×5×2½ m, dark, **large hole in the floor**, two staircases down at the north end, whole room sooty.
- **Dolda ting:** A brightly polished **pole** rises ~1½ m out of the hole (pole-riding, see Room 6); it ends in **Room 20**. The hole looks bottomless — anyone who tumbles in dies. Down the stairs: wooden doors, plus scenes from the Spider King's life painted on walls/floor (under all the soot!).
- **Bestar / Skatter:** None.

### 3. Labyrinten (The Labyrinth)
- **Överblick:** A pitch-black, stinking tunnel system.
- **Dolda ting:** Ceiling 2–4 m, uneven walls. All doors are unlocked but **spring shut automatically** unless held. The passage to **Room 10** slopes steeply down, ≤110 cm tall at the top. The door to the spiral staircase is **walled-over/hidden** — needs a **FINNA DOLDA TING** roll to find the handle.
- **Bestar:** A dead dark elf lies near the spiral stair (just died of poison).
- **Skatter:** That dark elf carries **10 kopparmynt**.
- **SL:** Use the self-closing doors to stress and confuse the party.

### 4. Tombolan (The Spinning Room)
- **Överblick:** Empty room, 3×2×2 m.
- **Dolda ting:** When anyone steps in, the room spins like a tombola wheel. Each rotation: anyone failing **SMI×5** takes **1T8**. After **1T6** rotations it stops and a sniggering voice says *"– Ta en lott!!"* ("Take a lottery ticket!!"). Ten minutes before it can spin again.
- **Bestar / Skatter:** None.
- **SL:** Pure comedy/annoyance. Describe dented armor, etc.

### 5. Fälla (Trap)
- **Överblick:** Corridor.
- **Dolda ting:** A trapdoor triggered by the **second** person to step on it. Failing **SMI** on 1T100 → fall into **Room 6** below, **1T6** damage (3½ m drop).
- **SL:** The faller is also attacked by **Puutch**. Corridor is very cramped; if the faller carried the only light, the corridor goes pitch-black.

### 6. Schaktets väktare (The Shaft's Guardian)
- **Överblick:** 6×4×3 m, connected to the shaft from Room 2. A pile of money in one corner; something terrifying in another.
- **Dolda ting:** None. The fireman's pole passes in the shaft. Trap 5 ends here.
- **Bestar — Puutch:** A vile spider/human hybrid bound by the Spider King to polish the pole daily; bloodthirsty, hates discussion. If players pole-ride past, he grabs **rider #2 and all after** and punishes them.
  - **PUUTCH** — STY 15 STO 15 INT 6 SMI 29 FYS 19 PSY 12 · Klor 45% / 1T6 (**3 attacks/SR**)
  - Pansar 4 (skin), Förflyttning 16 m/SR, **KLÄTTRA 85%, PUTSA 100%**. A special enchantment makes him hard to hit — **all hits against him −15%**. He can't move more than **6 m from the pole**.
- **Skatter:** Polishing rags + an economy-pack of Ansgarsuperglans polish, plus:
  1. **30 gm, 135 sm, 3 km**
  2. A ring (30 sm) and a diadem (~350 sm)
  3. Three amphorae of **LaDannervin** (legendary wine; a learned PC recognizes it)
  4. A strong healing drink (heals **1T6+2**)
- **SL:** Not fully dark — light seeps down the shaft.

### 7. Mumiekammaren (The Mummy Chamber)
- **Överblick:** 3×2×2 m, two benches, a chest, a sarcophagus leaning on the wall.
- **Dolda ting:** Chest is locked (difficulty **22**), withstands **40** damage. Holds three glass bowls + jewelry; if it takes >3 blows to open, the bowls break. The sarcophagus holds a **mummy**.
- **Bestar:** The mummy lurches out menacingly but **cannot harm anyone**; the first hit pulverizes it, and a faint voice says *"– Tack, ni har befriat mig från spindelkonungens förbannelse"* ("Thank you, you've freed me from the Spider King's curse").
- **Skatter:** Glass bowls 15 sm each; in the chest two gold rings (60 sm each) and three silver chains (80 sm each).
- **SL:** Another fake scare — roll the mummy's "attacks," then reveal it's harmless.

### 8. Fälla (Trap)
- **Överblick:** Corridor.
- **Dolda ting:** A knee-high cord (nearly invisible) triggers a brief shower over the **first** person — liquid is odorless and colorless.
- **SL:** They'll find out what it was in **Room 10**.

### 9. Fälla (Trap)
- **Överblick:** The usual corridor.
- **Dolda ting:** Three spiders in the high ceiling drop on the first person/group to pass under.
- **Bestar:** Three medium human-faced spiders, roaring, fight to the death. First attack targets the **largest** PC, with **+20%** the first SR.
  - **SPINDEL 1** — STY 5 STO 6 INT 2 / SMI 15 FYS 5 PSY 10 · Bett 50% / 2T4
  - **SPINDEL 2** — STY 6 STO 7 INT 1 / SMI 14 FYS 7 PSY 14 · Bett 45% / 2T4
  - **SPINDEL 3** — STY 5 STO 4 INT 1 / SMI 12 FYS 5 PSY 9 · Bett 60% / 2T4
  - All: 2 points skin, Förflyttning 10 m/SR.
- **Skatter:** Each wears a gold necklace worth **20 sm**.

### 10. Skattkammaren (False Treasure Chamber)
- **Överblick:** 3×4×2½ m, clean white-marble room, an enormous pile of money in a corner.
- **Dolda ting:** The "money pile" is a **gelé/jelly monster** hidden by illusion. Door to Room 11 locked (difficulty **18**).
- **Bestar — Gelemonster:** Green plasma blob, attacks the first to approach.
  - **GELEMONSTER** — STO 12 FYS 12 INT 0 SMI 3 PSY 30 · Vapen: Speciell
  - Pansar 6, Fart 6 m/SR, **Illusion 95%**. PCs failing **SMI×5** are sucked in: **2T6/SR** until they take 5 damage or it dies. Non-metal gear destroyed in 1 SR; metal armor loses 1 AP (permanent)/SR; weapons −1 damage/SR; valuables gone in 2 SR.
- **SL:** Hungry but very dumb. **Whoever got sprayed in Trap 8 smells delicious to it** and gets **+10** to their SMI escape roll.

### 11. Kapellet (The Chapel)
- **Överblick:** 6×7½×4 m marble. Purple tapestries (gobelänger) on both short walls; the **north** one bears a golden spider.
- **Dolda ting:** A debris heap at the north end: three empty wooden coffins, purple cloth, a mummified cat. Door to Room 13 locked (difficulty **18**).
- **Bestar — Spöket Ninea ("Ninea the fair"):** A beautiful ghost who begs *"– Hjälp mig, Ninea den fagra, att kräva hämnd på den fasansfulle spindelkonungen!"* She dodges questions and insists the party **tear down the north tapestry** (true: it reveals the Spider King's treasure). Doing so **opens the hidden doors to Rooms 12 & 14** — and the Room-12 skeletons + Ninea then attack.
  - **NINEA DEN FAGRA (Spöke)** — STO 9 SMI 22 INT 16 PSY 26 · Vapen: Speciell. Attacks and tries to *skräckslå* (terrify) per the rules; targets the most wizard-like PC first.
- **Skatter:** Tapestries worth **1300 silverpenningar** (if you can sell them).
- **SL:** A language-skilled PC with a good idé-kast realizes **"Ninea" means "spider" in Old Toorranian**.

### 12. Benkammaren (The Bone Chamber)
- **Överblick:** A hidden cavity behind a sliding wall in Room 11, just big enough for three skeletons.
- **Bestar:** Three rowdy skeletons rush out as soon as the sliding wall moves.
  - **SKELETT 1** — STY 7 STO 7 INT 0 / SMI 15 FYS 0 PSY 1 · Handyxa 55% / 1T6+2 · Medelstor Sköld parering 60%
  - **SKELETT 2** — STY 7 STO 6 INT 0 / SMI 13 FYS 0 PSY 1 · Handyxa 65% / 1T6+2 · Medelstor Sköld parering 75%
  - **SKELETT 3** — STY 5 STO 3 INT 0 / SMI 13 FYS 0 PSY 1 · Handyxa 50% / 1T6+2 · Medelstor Sköld parering 55%
  - All: Förflyttning 10 m/SR, no armor, attack immediately.
- **SL:** The leader (#3) is, as a joke, a **duck skeleton** (ankskelett).

### 13. Fälla (Trap)
- **Överblick:** 3×3×2½ m marble room; a skeleton in a heap on the floor. Door from the staircase is unlocked.
- **Dolda ting:** The floor is **cardboard/papier-mâché**. The first to step on it falls through (unless **SMI** on 1T100). Two meters down is a meter-deep pool of green slime; to climb out, roll **SMI×4** on 1T100.
- **Bestar — Träsck (demon):** Woman-shaped with red eyes, blue-green scaly skin, crocodile jaws; loves human flesh.
  - **TRÄSCK (demon)** — STY 17 STO 17 INT 8 SMI 14 FYS 21 PSY 38 · Käft 50% / 2T6
  - Pansar 2 (shell), Fart 6 m/SR, casts **HELA 85%** freely on herself.

### 14. Den siste väktaren (The Last Guardian)
- **Överblick:** ~13×4×4 m, irregular, hewn from rock, terrible stench; ~1 m lower than Room 11.
- **Dolda ting:** A very narrow passage begins at the far end (leads to Room 15).
- **Bestar — Lepera (giant spider):** The vilest thing the party will ever see. Can climb up into Room 11. Waits calmly until the skeletons and Ninea have acted before moving.
  - **LEPERA (stor spindel)** — STY 33 STO 40 INT 4 SMI 14 FYS 20 PSY 22
    - Riv & Slit 60% / 2T6+2 · parering 60%
    - Riv & Bit 50% / 2T6+2 · parering 50%
  - Skin absorbs 4, Förflyttning 15 m/SR, **both attacks each SR**, casts **SKINGRING 65%**.
- **SL:** Lepera will give the party a beating — but it's the **last** round they'll get.

### 15. Kryptan (The Crypt — the real tomb)
- **Överblick:** A long, unpleasant tunnel ending in a small low chamber (2×3×1 m) full of treasure.
- **Dolda ting:** The Spider King is buried here. Floor = black marble slab reading **"Arach konung"** in gold. A matching passage opens opposite the one the party entered by.
- **Skatter:**
  - ~**1200 guldpenningar**
  - A **magic arrow** with permanent 3rd-grade **FÖRTROLLA VAPEN** (15% it breaks after each use)
  - A chess set (schack)
  - Eight human skulls
  - **The three statues Assar Besvärjaren sought** (very valuable!)
  - A keg of pepper (expensive in these times!)
  - A jewel worth ~**1000 silverpenningar**
- **SL:** Treasure is **heavy** (200 gm = 1 BEP; chess set = 4 BEP, etc.). The opposite passage leads to the pyramid's **exterior, tier 3** — hidden behind a stone disc, impossible to find from outside.

## iGunther's Treasure Hunters (the dig crew)

A well-organized, unusually loyal band of **dark elves (svartalfer) and trolls**, working for **King Tachmog the Cruel** of the Toorra mountains. They correctly suspect the treasure lies **under Room 19** and just want to be left alone — they fight only if attacked and gladly **negotiate**. In a crisis, **iGunther blows up Room 15 and escapes**; Tachmog pays ransom for captured members (experts are scarce). They are spread across **Rooms 17–20** (most in 19); **Room 17 always has a guard** who hears break-in attempts at the staircase door. All see in the dark — no lamps needed. (Note: the trolls don't dominate the dark elves; if anything, the reverse.)

### 16. Vaktrum (Guard Room)
- **Överblick:** 3×3×3 m marble; a bench and three stools. **Stairs from Rooms 3 & 13 end here.** Door to the staircase locked (difficulty **22**); door to Room 17 unlocked. Three dice on the bench.
- **Bestar:** At least one guard.
- **Skatter:** If more than one guard, **4T6 km** on the table.
- **SL:** The dark elves play dice if there's more than one.

### 17. Förråd (Storage)
- **Överblick:** 9×4×3 m marble, full of junk. Doors to 18, 19, 16 unlocked; the shaft from 2/3 passes by with a **rope ladder rigged down to Room 20**. Junk = tools, blankets, clothes, water jug, three barrels of meat.
- **Bestar:** Possible dark elves.

### 18. Armborstrummet (Crossbow Room)
- **Överblick:** 3×3×3 m marble; an **enormous crossbow** aimed at the door to Room 19.
- **Dolda ting:** **60%** to hit anyone in the Room-19 doorway; **4T6** damage; **1 hour to reload**.
- **SL:** It's iGunther's defense against anything emerging from the grave.

### 19. Utgrävningen (The Excavation)
- **Överblick:** 4×4×3 m marble; floor broken up, tools everywhere.
- **Bestar:** Most dark elves + **both trolls**.
- **SL:** The crypt (Room 15) is **2 m below**; iGunther's crew has ~1 m left to dig. Here also is iGunther's **eld- och dunderbomb** (fire-and-thunder bomb): **12T8** to everyone in Room 15, plus collapses ≥2 tons of ceiling — and iGunther will use it callously.

### 20. Lavakammaren (The Lava Chamber)
- **Överblick:** Large irregular chamber, high ceiling. Two features: an **enormous spider cadaver (STO 130)** and a **smoking lava flow**.
- **Dolda ting:** The pole-ladder shaft ends here (pole bored deep into the ground). iGunther's communication tunnel also starts here.
- **SL:** Lava = **instant death**. The tunnel runs south ~**3.3 mil (33 km)** to Tachmog's stronghold; narrow, ceiling rarely >1 m, full of guards and traps — **firmly dissuade** any PC who wants to crawl through (it doesn't work).

# ADVENTURE 2 — Skelettbyns hemlighet
*("The Skeleton Village's Secret")*

Fully standalone; no special setting requirements. Two extra terms used: **SLP** = Spelledarens Personer (GM's NPCs); **AP** = Absorptionspoäng.

## The village & its history

The village sits at the end of a long, lush valley, four days' march from the nearest town. Founded by ~fifty settlers.

| Year | Event |
|---|---|
| 0 | Site found; first houses built. |
| 3 | Grown to 22 buildings, 125 inhabitants; prosperous. |
| 13 | Decided to build a church to honor their god (see *Guden*). |
| 15 | Church finished; first mass held. |
| 89 | Dark elves attack to burn/plunder the village; a **knight in shining armor** bearing the god's symbol drives them off but **falls by the village wall**. Villagers bury him with all his gear in a **secret chamber under the church** ("Den Namnlöse Riddaren" — the Nameless Knight). |
| 187 | A mighty man in the east sends a servant of death — a **Svartkonstnär (necromancer)** — to scout the area, trailed by a creature of death. |
| 188 | Disguised as an adventurer, the necromancer learns of the Nameless Knight's grave and suspects a large church treasure. He sends **20 skeletons** to ravage the village. The **village priest** confronts him at the altar and helps blast him with a huge **BLIXT**, razing the western wall and killing **both** necromancer and priest. The village is ruined except the church; only the necromancer's **skeletons** remain. The **priest's spirit** lingers to find someone to cleanse the village. |

**The hook:** the priest's spirit seeks out the adventurers, tells the story, and in return reveals where to find the Nameless Knight's grave.

## Skeleton patrol schedule (the four village patrol skeletons)

They follow a fixed loop precisely. Start counting time **T** when the party gets the map and is at **Start**. Keep tracking time and skeleton position throughout.

| Time | Position |
|---|---|
| T (start) | Northeastern well |
| T+1 min (12 SR) | Fork on the larger west road |
| T+2 min (24 SR) | Manor ruin (Herrgårdsruinen, north) |
| T+4 min (48 SR) | Still at Manor ruin (wait until here) |
| T+5 min (60 SR) | Back at the road fork |
| T+7 min (84 SR) | Fork by the two small square ruins; **split into two pairs** (SW road / E road) |
| T+8 min (96 SR) | Reunite between the hills; then split again, one pair per side of the hill |
| T+10 min (120 SR) | Southern well |
| T+12 min (144 SR) | Leave southern well (wait until here) |
| T+15 min (180 SR) | Northern well — full loop ≈ 15 min |

If they spot an intruder they pursue relentlessly and try to annihilate them. All slain (or apparently lifeless) bodies are dragged to the nearest well and dropped in.

**Patrol skeleton stats** (all: PSY-1, Förflyttning 10 m/SR):

- **Patrullskelett 1** — STY-6 STO-9 FYS-0 SMI-14 INT-0 · Enhandsspjut 60% / 1T6+1 · Liten sköld 55% (8 AP) · Ringbrynja 4 AP · **KP 5**
- **Patrullskelett 2** — STY-6 STO-8 FYS-0 SMI-15 INT-0 · Enhandsspjut 65% / 1T6+1 · Liten sköld 50% (8 AP) · Ringbrynja 4 AP · **KP 4**
- **Patrullskelett 3** — STY-6 STO-8 FYS-0 SMI-16 INT-0 · Bredsvärd 60% / 1T8+1 · Medelstor sköld 60% (12 AP) · Ringbrynja 4 AP · **KP 4**
- **Patrullskelett 4** — STY-6 STO-4 FYS-0 SMI-17 INT-0 · Tungt Armborst 65% / 2T6+2 · Ringbrynja 4 AP · **KP 2**

## Village rooms (1–12)

### 1. Kyrkan — markplanet (The Church, ground floor)
- **Överblick (outside):** 26×16×8 m, stone with a copper tower roof now light-green. **West side razed**, blocked by rubble. South entrance = two unlocked wooden doors with rusty iron fittings; the god's symbol in gilded copper sits above them. North entrance = a single wooden door, **locked (difficulty 18)**. All windows except the west ones are intact, depicting saints/heroes.
- **Överblick (inside):** A hall (22×14×4.5 m) with two rows of six benches up to a half-meter podium; seven stone pillars; three steps to a marble **altar** flanked by **two black-clad skeletons** with battle-swords and shields. The god's symbol is on the altar front. A torn red-and-black two-part drapery hangs behind the altar; a similar one in the NE corner.
- **Dolda ting:** Turn the god-symbol **90° east** to release two pins and slide the **altar west**, revealing the secret descent. Behind the two-part drapery: a boarded wooden door; behind the other drapery: an unlocked wooden door up to the **eastern bell tower**. **Room 5** (staircase room) is under the altar.
- **Bestar — Kyrkovakten (4 skeletons):** Attack anyone within **5 m** of the altar; fight to destruction; pursue fleeing intruders, dragging the slain to the south well (22 m deep, only 0.8 m wide). All PSY-1, Förflyttning 10 m/SR, Ringbrynja 4 AP, Stor sköld 16 AP:
  - **Nordvästra** — STY-6 STO-8 SMI-16 · Slagsvärd 55% / 1T10+1 · Sköld parering 60% · KP 4
  - **Nordöstra** — STY-6 STO-6 SMI-14 · Slagsvärd 50% / 1T10 · Sköld 55% · KP 3
  - **Sydvästra** — STY-6 STO-9 SMI-17 · Slagsvärd 65% / 1T10+1 · Sköld 60% · KP 5
  - **Sydöstra** — STY-7 STO-9 SMI-16 · Slagsvärd 70% / 1T10+1 · Sköld 65% · KP 5

### 2. Östra klocktornet (Eastern Bell Tower)
- **Överblick:** Unlocked wooden door; ~1.5 m spiral stair, slippery with wet moss. Ends in a 3.5×3.5×2.5 m room, empty but for the rope to the ceiling bronze bell; a small glass window marks each compass direction.
- **Dolda ting / Skatter:** None — though someone could ring the bell (dull clang), and the bell weighs 80 kg.
- **SL:** If the bell rings, decide whether anyone else reacts.

### 3. Arbetsrummet (Office)
- **Överblick:** 6×6×2.5 m, full of smashed furniture: a bookshelf (~100 books), two chairs, a writing desk, a small table, a small statue, broken vase glass; a small open fireplace in the NW corner.
- **Dolda ting:** Most books are admin ledgers (harvests, bookkeeping); the rest cover religion, legends, foreign languages, botany. (Exact book contents = GM's choice.)

### 4. Sovrummet (Bedroom)
- **Överblick:** 6×6×2.5 m, a window centered on each wall; NW corner has a fireplace + cozy armchair; an overturned bed against the east wall; a nightstand under the north window; an opened, hacked-up chest in the SW corner.
- **Dolda ting:** Previous looters missed a **secret compartment in the chest lid** — a small leather purse with **22 small white/green/blue/red/yellow gemstones**.
- **Skatter:** Gems total ~**800 silverpenningar**.

### 5. Trapprummet (Staircase Room)
- **Överblick:** A narrow, wet, slippery stair down to a square room with passages in all directions except north, where a locked, iron-reinforced door blocks the way (**difficulty 20**). Bricked walls. *(Margin note in original: an enchanted broadsword may hang over the passages.)*
- **Dolda ting:** Push the topmost stair-step left to release pins and slide the **altar above** westward.
- **Bestar:** Roll **1T100** — **01–20** = encounter with two patrolling catacomb skeletons; **21–00** = no encounter.
- **Katakombens väktare** (black-clad but smaller; recognize their own; fight to the end; haul the dead to the well):
  - **Skelett 1** — STY-5 STO-4 FYS-0 SMI-14 INT-0 PSY-1 · Bredsvärd 50% / 1T8+1 · Liten sköld 60% (8 AP) · Ringbrynja 4 AP · KP 2
  - **Skelett 2** — STY-4 STO-3 FYS-0 SMI-17 INT-0 PSY-1 · Kortsvärd 45% / 1T6+1 · Liten sköld 50% (8 AP) · Ringbrynja 4 AP · KP 2

### 6. Gångarna i underjorden (The Underworld Passages)
- **Överblick:** ~1.8 m wide and tall, bricked, slimy wet moss.
- **Bestar:** **Two patrolling skeletons** wandering randomly (unpredictable). Every 5 minutes roll 1T100 vs the Room-5 encounter table.
- **SL:** Impossible to surprise these skeletons.

### 7. Balsameringskammaren (Embalming Chamber)
- **Överblick:** L-shaped, 8×6×1.8 m, with a large stone altar in the middle, a stone book-holder in the NE corner, rusty engraved iron plates along the south and east walls, and a wooden door in the north.
- **Dolda ting:** Each iron plate is a gravestone for a priest **walled in** behind it. **Eight plates, only seven inscribed** (the eighth is blank). North wooden door is unlocked.
- **Bestar:** See Room 5 (patrol skeletons).
- **SL:** Former priests were embalmed and walled in to give themselves to their god.

### 8. Förrådsutrymme (Storage)
- **Överblick:** Small closet, 2×2×1.8 m, full of embalming material. Nothing else.

### 9. Pelarfällan (The Pillar Trap)
- **Överblick:** A 6 m corridor (2 m wide, 5 m deep), three tall stone pillars 2 m apart, a **locked iron-fitted wooden door (difficulty 25)** at the far end. On the floor: two armored skeletons + four half-rotted bodies, swords, shields, scattered gear.
- **Dolda ting:** Examining the **third (farthest) pillar without standing on it** reveals the trap; placing >50 kg on it makes the trap undetectable. When triggered:
  1. The south door slams shut violently — anyone in the way takes **2T6** and is shoved into the staircase room.
  2. All three pillars shoot into the ceiling, crushing anyone on them. Roll 1T100: **01–10** stays put → takes **1T10+1T8+1T6+1T4+4** then falls; **11–00** falls off.
  3. **20 sharp spears** shoot up 2 m from the floor (holes only found by feeling the floor). Roll **1T8**:

  | 1T8 | Spears hit | Damage |
  |---|---|---|
  | 1–2 | none | — |
  | 3–4 | 1 | 2T4 |
  | 5–6 | 2 | 4T4 |
  | 7 | 3 | 6T4 |
  | 8 | 4 | 8T4 |

  4. Everything resets; the trap can fire again.
- **Skatter:** On the floor: **9 km, 34 sm, 12 gm**.

### 10. Förrummet (Antechamber)
- **Överblick:** Four ~2 m walls forming a half-circle; the north rock wall has collapsed (5×3×1.5 m).
- **Dolda ting:** In the SE wall, scrape away the two lowest loose stones + the four directly above them to break through into the next room.
- **SL:** Let players talk through *how* to do it rather than rolling — it flows naturally and creative players enjoy it more.

### 11. Den Namnlöse Riddarens grav (The Nameless Knight's Grave)
- **Överblick:** 6×4×2 m bricked room. A stone altar in the center bears a **skeleton**. On the walls: a **silver shield** (god's symbol in gold, north), a **glimmering bastard sword** (east), a **shining silver knight's armor** (god's symbol in gold, south), and a **horn** (west).
- **Dolda ting:** The skeleton is **213 cm** long; otherwise nothing.
- **Skatter:**
  - **Shield:** medium-sized, silver-plated, god's symbol in gold — worth ~150 gm
  - **Sword:** ornate bastard sword with gems and gold inlay — worth ~120 gm
  - **Armor:** silver-plated — worth ~8000 sp; **absorbs 7 AP**, weighs only **6 BEP**
  - **Horn (the only magic item):** brought from another world/time. When blown, the enchantment affects **everyone who hears it within 120 m** (not just the usual five targets). The needed power is **drawn from the blower**.

### 12. Skattkammaren (Treasure Chamber)
- **Överblick:** The entry door is solid iron, **locked (difficulty 50)**. Circular room with a domed ceiling (6×3 m); a large wooden, gold-inlaid chest in the center (chest difficulty **13**).
- **Skatter:** The chest holds **4345 sm and 367 gm**.

## The God — Eledain (optional)

The author leaves the god deliberately generic; if you have no deity of your own, use **Eledain**, who represents **light, piety, magnanimity, life, creation, and luck**. His many worshippers include all sorts. The knightly order **Den Evigt Skinande Stjärnans Brödraskap** ("Brotherhood of the Eternally Shining Star") wears shining armor with Eledain's symbol on the chest; they **never draw weapons first** and only as a last resort. **Symbol:** an eight-pointed star (compass-rose style).

## Back-cover blurb (in brief)
400 years ago the spider king **Arach** died in a magical experiment; his grieving bodyguard built a great pyramid on the caravan road between Detwach and distant Faar and buried him with his treasures. His nomad alliance collapsed in the ensuing power struggle, but fear of Arach and the legend of his resurrection endured. Now the old man **Assar Besvärjaren**, seeking three magic statues from Arach's grave-treasure, has paid a group of adventurers to brave the pyramid — *and you are one of them.* The booklet also contains **Skelettbyns hemlighet**, set in a village held by an evil wizard's servants — but what drives the guardians to defend it against all intruders?
