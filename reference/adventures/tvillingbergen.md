## Premise (back-cover hook)

> *Can you find the wizards Kobbler and Swarzard's hidden treasure?*
> An exciting adventure starting from the small village of Vindpina. The players meet fearsome dangers and clever traps on their way toward the now-dead wizards' treasure — a treasure said to surpass almost anything.

---

## 1. Adventure Start (Äventyrets start)

### GM prep advice (printed intro)
- This adventure is written **for the Game Master**. If you are a player, stop reading immediately, for your own enjoyment.
- The GM should read the **whole adventure carefully — preferably several times** — so you don't have to sit and flip pages and search during play.
- *"There's nothing as dull for the players as a GM who keeps interrupting to sit quietly and read to themselves."*
- You don't need it all memorized, but read closely enough that you truly understand **what** is said and **where** it's said.

### Setting
**Vindpina** — a small, blustery village ("windy hole").
- ~**5 days' march** from **Sarnath (Gamla Sarnath / Old Sarnath)**.
- ~**3 days' march** from **Tvillingbergen** (the Twin Mountains).
- Sits on the river **Laxforsen**; the village holds the **only ford** across it.

**Village makeup:** smithy, inn/tavern, church, general store, stable, stonemason/undertaker (*stenhuggare/begravningsentreprenör*), weaver, miller, poorhouse (*fattighus*), and about ten farms.

### Opening scene — at the inn "Säckpipan"
*(The inn is named "The Bagpipe" because the wind drags and whistles through its roof beams.)*

1. It's just turned dusk and the wind is picking up hard outside. Inside the inn it's **warm and pleasant**, and that's where the players are.
2. Innkeeper **Talgar** has just brought out several tankards of beer and now wants payment — **2 kö per person**.
3. The door opens: in come the **smith** and the **miller**, good friends, laughing and chatting, each ordering a beer. Talgar and the smith look very alike — **in fact they are brothers**. After getting their beer they sit at a table next to the players (it's a small inn; nearly all tables are beside the players').
4. A while later the door opens again. An **old man** enters. Spotting the players, he lights up with a **toothless grin**. He goes to the innkeeper for a whispering conversation, then comes to the players' table and asks to sit.
5. The old man is **talkative**. He chatters about the village, says his name is **Kie**, and that he lives at the **poorhouse on the edge of the village**.
6. After a short silence, Kie asks whether the players are *"a new band of those adventurers"* out to try to find the wizards **Swarzard and Kobbler's treasure**. If the players say they've never heard of any such treasure or wizards, Kie offers to tell the history of Tvillingbergen — **if they buy him a beer.** Then he begins.

## 2. Background History (Bakgrundshistoria) — what Kie tells

- **~Half a century ago**, troubles began at the mountains **Simenov** and **Menov**. Two mighty mages held strongholds, one on each mountain. They were constantly at war, each wanting dominion over the peninsula the mountains sit on. They sent out groups of trolls and other dark beings to try to kill one another, destroy each other's castles, or otherwise cause grief. **To finance the raids, they plundered the countryside and surrounding villages.**
- Over the years neither gained any lasting advantage.
- **~40 years ago**, the sons were born: **Flamroll** (son of Swarzard) and **Lokfaste** (son of Kobbler). They were raised, naturally, in the same spirit as their fathers.
- With the years, **Swarzard and Kobbler withdrew** and left the contest for dominion to the sons. But neither son cared to end his days like his father; instead they **met in secret and forged common plans**, deciding to make a deal and **join forces** ("slå sina påsar ihop").
- **One dark night under a waning moon, the sons murdered their own fathers.** They buried them in a **burial mound midway between the mountains**. Right afterward they took their armies and **marched north** to terrorize the honest peasant population.

## 3. Rumors (Rykten)

### Transition
- When Kie finishes, he drinks the last of his beer and says he must get back to the poorhouse. If the players want to buy him more beer, he's **easily persuaded** — but he **knows nothing more** about Tvillingbergen or the wizards.
- Once Kie has left, the **smith and miller come over** and say what Kie tells must be taken with a pinch of salt — *he'll say almost anything for a free beer.* Still, there may be **a grain of truth**, because plenty of rumors circulate in the village.
- **SL goal:** The point is to make the players genuinely curious — to get them to go around the village, talk to people, and pick up some rumors.

### The 12 rumors circulating
**Rumors marked (FALSE) are deliberate false leads — the players don't know which.**

| # | Rumor | Status |
|---|-------|--------|
| 1 | Tvillingbergen lies on a peninsula by the sea, **3 days' march (on foot) south** of the village. | True |
| 2 | The sons took precautions against grave robbers and **installed an alarm system** that runs to one of the mountain castles. | True |
| 3 | Flamroll has **left a group of dark elves (svartalfer)** in his castle to watch over his and Lokfaste's area.* | True* |
| 4 | Flamroll and Lokfaste left **valuables and other things** they couldn't or wouldn't take with them in the burial mound for safekeeping. | True |
| 5 | **Traps** were installed in the mound so grave robbers won't get out alive. | True |
| 6 | The mound is built on **more than one level**. | True |
| 7 | There is a **back way in/out** of the mound that is well hidden and very hard to find. | True |
| 8 | The riches are hidden **under a loose stone** in the large burial mound. | **FALSE** |
| 9 | Flamroll and Lokfaste are **on their way back** to the peninsula to check everything is in order. | **FALSE** |
| 10 | It's said the **gods themselves** have taken on guarding the grave until the sons return. | **FALSE** |
| 11 | A **gang of reptile men** makes the area unsafe. | True |
| 12 | **No one** who has gone south to investigate the mound has ever returned. | True |

- **SL:** When marked **F** it means a false lead (the players naturally don't know this).
- **\*Footnote (rumor 3):** This is in itself true, but the dark elves **abandoned the castle long ago**, since it was frequently visited by hungry trolls and reptile men.

## 4. The Journey to the Mound (Resan till gravkumlet)

On the journey the players may run into the hungry beasts living in the wilderness.
**Roll 1T6 twice per day** (once for day, once for night) and consult the table.
- A **dash** means nothing happens.
- **If a number comes up twice, the second (and any later) occurrence counts as a dash** (nothing happens).

| 1T6 | Encounter |
|-----|-----------|
| 1 | — (nothing happens) |
| 2 | — (nothing happens) |
| 3 | **2 Dwarves (dvärgar).** Gorin and Drego, brothers; they won't attack unless provoked. If the players are lost, the dwarves can point the right way (**costs 10 sp**). They're not interested in helping otherwise. **Gorin carries 17 sp, Drego 26 sp.** |
| 4 | **4 Trolls.** Haven't eaten in two days; out hunting — with luck they might catch a human or two… |
| 5 | **2 Will-o'-the-Wisps (Lyktgubbar).** Named **Mist** and **Fog**; they spot the players either at dusk or at dawn. (See §9.) |
| 6 | **3 Reptile Men (Reptilmän).** All brothers; they've been to **Dypölen** visiting their cousins. On the way home they encountered and killed a deer, which they carry tied to a pole between them. |

## 5. Arriving at the Mound (Framme vid gravkumlet)

- The burial mound is a **stone cairn (stenröse): 3 m high, 20 m in diameter.**
- Climbing the cairn, you see at the top a **stone slab, 2×2 m (STO 21).**
- The slab bears an inscription in hard-to-read letters (**roll *Läs och Skrivkunnighet* / Reading & Writing**):
  > *"Här vilar Swarzard och Kobbler, men knappast i frid"*
  > ("Here rest Swarzard and Kobbler, but hardly in peace.")
- To remove the slab → roll on the **resistance table (motståndstabellen)**.
- When the slab is off, a **closed, damp draft of wind** rises toward the adventurers. Straight down is a **hole 1.5×1.5 m, about 3 m deep**, with a **ladder** fastened on one side.
- **The ladder's three lowest rungs are rotten — STY 4** (roll against the character's STO). If the ladder breaks, the character **falls and takes 1T6 damage**; only leather and cloth (armor) protect.
- *(The booklet includes a cross-section diagram of the cairn, hole, ladder and the stairs below.)*

## 6. Inside the Mound (I kumlet)

Below the ladder, a **staircase winds a further 9 m down**. The staircase is **1 m wide**, so the players must travel **single file** — it's too narrow to walk more than one abreast.

### Dungeon layout / map notes
*(From the two floor-plan maps in the booklet.)*
- **Upper level** holds the entrance stairs and **Rooms 1–9** in an interconnected cluster, organized around the **U-shaped Corridor (Room 3)**.
- **Room 4** appears as **multiple small snake chambers** reached via hidden floor hatches off the corridor.
- **Rooms 1 and 2** are the guard rooms flanking the entry corridor.
- **Rooms 5 (Chapel) and 6 (Storage)** are the two large rooms; **Room 7** (ghost) sits below/adjacent.
- **Room 8** (antechamber/winch) connects via the winch mechanism to **Room 9** (Lion Chamber) and overlooks **Room 11** through a grille.
- **Room 10** is the reptile men's water passage, linking the sea, Room 9, and the Room 12 trap.
- **Room 11** (mummies) hides the spiral stair down to **Room 12 (Treasure Chamber)** — the lowest point.

---

### 6.1 — Guard Room (Vaktrum) — Room 1
- **Overview (Överblick):** Square room containing 2 skeletons.
- **Examination (Granskning):** 3×3×2.5 m.
- **Contains (Består):** 2 skeletons — **A** and **B**.
- **SL:** When the players advance up the corridor, the door to Room 1 opens and the skeleton(s) rush out to defend the mound.

### 6.2 — Guard Room (Vaktrum) — Room 2
- **Overview:** Square room containing two seemingly dead humans; **3 attacking skeletons.**
- **Examination:** 3×3×2.5 m. The bodies are badly decomposed — they are **former adventurers** killed by the skeletons. Loot on the corpses: **bastard sword, small shield, hard-waxed leather (hårdvaxat läder)**, and **25 sp / 40 kö** respectively.
- **Contains:** 3 skeletons — **C, D, E**.
- **SL:** These three wait to attack until the players go into Room 2 — **but preferably they try to attack the players in the back** as they head down the corridor.

### 6.3 — Corridor (Korridor) — Room 3
- **Overview:** U-shaped corridor.
- **Examination:** For dimensions, see the map.
- **Hidden things (Dolda ting):** **3 stone slabs in the floor, 0.5×0.5 m.** These open secret hatches in the floor down to the **Room 4** chambers.
- **SL:** Demand that the players state **exactly where they walk.** If a person steps on a trapped square, **secretly roll the character's Luck (Lyckokast)**; on a failed roll, one of the hatches **silently opens** into one of the Room 4 chambers. **One stone can open only one room.**

### 6.4 — Snake Chambers (Ormkammare) — Room 4
- **Overview:** Small, low room containing snakes.
- **Examination:** 2×2×1 m. **5 snakes.** If you succeed at **Zoology (Zoologi)**, you know they're venomous. *(The "Zoologi" note is handwritten in the margin replacing the printed skill.)*
- **Hidden things:** The hatches to the snake chambers.
- **Contains:** In **each** chamber there are **5 venomous snakes.**
- **Poison rules:** If a bite really hurts a player, there's a chance the player is **also poisoned.** Each snake's poison does **3T6 damage**. The poison's effect must **overcome the victim's damage points on the resistance table**; if it succeeds, it adds full damage to the victim — otherwise **half effect**.
- **SL:** Once someone triggers the trap, the snakes slowly slither out of their holes over **3 turns (slingrar)**. If the players retrace the same way they came in, they'll find the snakes spread out across the floor.
- **[Margin note — snake stats]:** STY 5 · STO 2 · FYS 8 · SMI 20 · INT 4 · PSY 17 · Damage 1T4 · Poison 3T6 (notation "[L8]").

### 6.5 — Chapel (Kapell) — Room 5
- **Overview:** Large room with benches, an altar with candles. Between the candles stands a small **crystal ball on a pedestal**; the wall behind the altar is covered by a drapery.
- **Examination:** 6×9×2.5 m. **10 benches** facing an altar **2×1×1 m.**
  - The **candlesticks** on the altar are **silver, worth 40 sp each.**
  - The candles aren't cheap wax — they're **real stearin candles, worth 10 sp each.**
  - **If anyone touches the crystal ball, it disappears.**
- **Treasures (Skatter):** See examination.
- **SL (the alarm trap):** The crystal ball holds a **4th-degree FÄLLA** and a **4th-degree TELEPORTERA.** Touching it **teleports the ball to Lokfaste's castle**, where it was *meant* to make the dark elves sound the alarm — **but they have since moved out**, so the alarm comes to nothing. (See §7; the ball can later be found on a pedestal there.)

### 6.6 — Storage Room (Förvaringsrum) — Room 6
- **Overview:** Large room filled with things — goods, paintings, tools, furniture — **all in complete disorder.**
- **Examination:** 6×9×2.5 m. **Examining this room takes twice as long as usual.** The room holds the grave goods the two wizards meant to bring into the realm of death:
  - 2 mummified horses
  - 2 mummified dogs
  - **2 mummified men (slaves)**
  - 16 urns with various crops/grains
  - 2 sets of all kinds of tools
  - 2 paintings (one of **Kobler**, one of **Swarzard**)
  - 2 tapestries (gobelänger)
  - 2 wine flasks (vinflaskor)
  - 2 large shields
  - 2 chain mails (kedjebrynjor)
  - **1 flask** (special — see below)
- **Hidden things:** The flask is **magic** and has a **FRAMMANA/SKICKA BORT UNDIN 3** (Summon/Send Away Undine 3) placed in it. *(This is the intended escape item for the final flood trap — see §8.)*
- **Treasures:** Everything listed above (except the magic flask) is worth **3000 sp** total. The **magic flask itself is worth 2500 sp.**
- **SL:** Granting/examining this room takes **twice the usual time.**
- **[Margin note — mummy stats]:** STY 66 · STO 14 · FYS 0 · SMI 7 · INT 14 · PSY 13 · KAR 1 · Fists 1T3 · Kick 1T6 (notation "50/2").

### 6.7 — Burial Chamber (Gravkammare) — Room 7
- **Overview:** A larger room containing **two sarcophagi.**
- **Examination:** 6×6×2.5 m. The sarcophagi are large — over **2 m long and almost ½ m wide.**
- **Hidden:** The **far sarcophagus has a loose bottom**, discoverable once the lid is removed.
- **Contains:** A **ghost (spöke)** dwells in the **nearer** sarcophagus.
- **SL:** If players open the sarcophagus, the **ghost attacks at once.** If they open the sarcophagus, the ghost gets out through the sarcophagus it rests in and attacks. The ghost attacks as described in the rulebook — i.e. **KRA vs KRA on the resistance table.** *(Margin note: "see p. 60 in the rulebook (black…)".)*

### 6.8 — Antechamber to the Burial Vault (Förkammare till gravvalvet) — Room 8
- **Overview:** Oblong room; the far part of the **left long wall** is covered by a **portcullis/trap grille (fällgaller)**; on the **far wall** sits a **winch handle (vinschhandtag).**
- **Examination:** 3×6×2.5 m. Through the trap grille you can see a burial chamber: **2 sarcophagi surrounded by four large pillars.** If the players say they specifically examine the winch handle, let them discover the **little turning handle (vridhandtag)** sticking out of the wall just below the winch handle.
- **The mechanism / puzzle:**
  - In its current setting, the turning handle is turned **toward the long wall without the grille.** If players try to hoist the grille with the winch, **the secret door to Room 9 opens instead.**
  - If the **turning handle's position is changed**, the winch will then **open the trap grille** normally.
  - **Brute force:** Should someone try to lift the grille by hand, it has **STO 30.**
- **Hidden things:** The door to **Room 9** from this side can be opened **only with the winch.**

### 6.9 — Lion Chamber (Lejonkammaren) — Room 9
- **Overview:** Small room containing various items, plus **four reptile warriors** who look quite menacing.
- **Examination:** 3×3×2.5 m. In one corner lie **3 lion skeletons (lejonskelett).**
- In the **far-left corner** hangs a **drapery**, concealing the **hole in the wall** the reptile men made.
- **Contains:** 4 reptile warriors — **Shhass, Flll, Zapp, Viss.**
- **Treasures:**
  - **Zapp** has a **ring of OSYNLIGHET-2 (Invisibility 2).**
  - **Viss** has a **healing drink** that heals **2T6 damage.**
  - They have **260 sp total** among them.
- **SL:** If the players try to examine the room, the reptile men attack — likewise if the players enter via the water route. **Zapp likes to use his ring** if he thinks he hears suspicious sounds. They **absolutely** don't want their secret passage revealed. (They found this room by chance, following the secret water channel from the sea, and made it their dwelling, bringing all their belongings.)

### 6.10 — Reptile Warriors' Secret Entrance (Reptilkrigarnas hemliga ingång) — Room 10
- **Overview:** Half the room is a **narrow passage with irregular walls**; the other half is **water.**
- **Examination:** 2×6×1.8 m. Evidently the reptile men dug the narrow passage themselves.
- The **water is 1 m deep.** The channel disappears out through the **right short wall**, through a **hole below the waterline.**
- **SL:** The channel **feeds the Room 12 trap with water** — though this cannot be discovered from here.
- It's hard to escape to the sea this way: the tunnel runs **entirely underwater**, **unless** the person is carried by a **2nd–3rd degree UNDIN** (degree depending on the person's STO).

### 6.11 — Burial Chamber (Gravkammaren) — Room 11
- **Overview:** A large room containing **two sarcophagi and four large pillars.** *(The booklet illustration shows the two sarcophagi labeled **KOBBLER** and **SWARZARD**, with items strewn around them.)*
- **Examination:** 6×9×2.5 m. The sarcophagi contain the **two mummies — Swarzard and Kobbler.**
- **Hidden things:** A **hidden door in the right, far pillar.** It opens **inward**, is **self-closing**, and has **no handle on the inside.** Behind it is a **spiral staircase descending 3 m**, leading to the corridor that runs to the treasure chamber.
- **Contains:** 2 mummies — **Swarzard and Kobbler.**
- **SL (the twist):** If the players open the sarcophagi **one at a time**, they must **fight the mummies.** If they open **both** sarcophagi, the **mummies will fight each other** instead.

### 6.12 — Treasure Chamber (Skattkammaren) — Room 12
- **Overview:** A large square room, with the **door set a bit out into a corridor.**
- **Examination:** 6×6×2.5 m **+ a 1×1×2.5 m alcove** (by the door).
  - In the **middle** stands a **large wooden chest (träkista).** It is **impossible to move** (fastened in the floor).
  - On the **opposite wall** hangs a **large hall mirror (hallspegel).**
  - The wooden chest's lid has **no lock** — only **two "hinges" (gångjärn) made of leather strips.**
  - **Inside the wooden chest** sits an **iron chest (järnkista)** that matches the wooden chest exactly in **width** but is **40 cm shorter.** Peering into the side gaps reveals **handles fastened to the iron chest.**
- **The silver plate on the lid** is engraved with the warning verse:
  > *För fosterland och kung*
  > *Fast kistan är tung*
  > *Om Du försöker lyfta skatten*
  > *Du dig över huvudet tager vatten.*

  ("For fatherland and king / Though the chest is heavy / If you try to lift the treasure / You'll take water over your head.")
- Just below the plate hangs a **key fastened on a thin silver thread.**
- **Hidden things:** The trap can be discovered **only** by **breaking or dismantling the wooden chest** — never from outside.
- **SL:** If anyone tries to **lift the iron chest out of the wooden chest, the trap triggers.** It cannot be detected from the outside. The wooden chest can't be moved (fixed in the floor).

**THE TRAP — when triggered:**
- In the **1×1×2.5 m alcove (by the door)**, a **stone block (STO 45)** falls from the ceiling and **blocks the door.**
- From the hole, **water gushes in from Room 10.**
- **The whole room, floor to ceiling, fills with water in 10 minutes.**

**Treasures in the chamber:**
- **Hall mirror** — coated with silver inlays — **worth 450 sp.**
- **Swarzard's magic spiked club / mace (spikklubba)** — contains **FÖRÄNDRA 4** and **ILLUSION 4.** Chance to activate it: **Lyckokast (Luck roll).**
- **The demon sword *Dziggetai*** — houses a **demon (INT 10, KRA 7).** The demon can cast **BLIXT (Lightning), VÄSSA/FÖRSLÖA (Sharpen/Dull), KONTROLLERA (Control), LJUS/MÖRKER (Light/Dark), and LYFT (Lift)** — **all at 50%.**
  - To take control of the demon: win a **KRA vs KRA** roll on the resistance table. Whoever wins keeps control. **Control lasts until the person dies.**
  - If the owner is knocked **unconscious**, the demon can break free by succeeding at its **own KRA vs KRA** against the owner's KRA.
- **Diamond-set goblet (bägare)** — **worth 1200 sp.**
- **4 jewels** at **200 sp each.**
- **20 gt** and **14 sp.**

## 7. The Wizards' Castles (Trollkarlarnas borgar)

- Should the players decide to investigate the castles on **Simenov** and **Menov**, they'll find **both castles in ruins.** Some of the dark elves who were to guard the mound have moved away; the rest **became troll food.**
- The only notable find: a **pedestal in a tower room of Lokfaste's castle.** If the players have already triggered the Chapel crystal ball's **FÄLLA**, they will find the **crystal ball placed on that pedestal** (it teleported there).

---

## 8. Conclusion (Avslutning) — Escaping the Flood Trap

There are at least **two ways** (three, counting the variant) to escape a triggered trap (the treasure room):
1. If the players **strip off heavier armor and swim** with light packing, they can move **as fast as the room fills**, up to **Room 10.**
2. If the players found the **magic flask with the undine**, the undine can carry **one player per SR** up to **Room 10.**
3. *(If only one player remains and that one has the undine, the undine can carry the player all the way out through the **water tunnel** to the sea.)*

> This is the payoff of the §6.6 flask: parties who picked it up have a guaranteed escape route.

## 9. Will-o'-the-Wisps (Lyktgubbar) — Bestiary

Small, intelligent creatures living in **marshes and bogs.** Because they're so weak, they've turned to **magic to survive**: with spells they lure humans or animals **down into wells, lakes, or off cliffs** to knock them unconscious or kill them, then **fetch the bodies** and carry them home to their **earthen den (jordkula)** — usually located **under a deciduous tree** — and make **stew of the meat.**

**Stats:**

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| STY | 2T4 |
| KRO | 2T6+6 |
| STO | 1T4+4 |
| INT | 3T6+6 |
| KRA | 5T6 |
| SKI | 3T6 |
| KAR | 2T4 |
| Movement (Förflyttning) | 24 m/SR |
| Armor (Bepansring) | none (ingen) |

- **Spells (Trollformler):** ILLUSION 60% · DIMMA 60% · OSYNLIGHET 60% · LJUS/MÖRKER 60%
- **Skills (Färdigheter):** Sneak (Smyga) 75% · Hide (Gömma Sig) 70% · Find Hidden (Finna Dolt) 50%
- **Group size:** usually **1T4+1** working together.

**Typical maneuver:**
At dusk or dawn, the wisps let the victims be **swallowed in DIMMA (fog)**, then one of them conjures an **ILLUSION** of a **weak, moving lantern**, luring the victims to follow and investigate. They maneuver both ILLUSION and DIMMA so the victims **trample into a deep hole or fall off a cliff.**

**Loot hook & warning:**
Wisps are also **childishly delighted by magical items**; their dens often contain a magic item. But forcing a wisp to reveal where it lives can be a **costly experience** — wisps are **cunning as foxes** and not seldom have set **traps around or inside their dens.**

## 10. New Spells (Nya besvärjelser)

### 10.1 — DIMMA (Fog)
- **Range (Räckvidd):** 60 m
- **Duration (Varaktighet):** 15 min
- The spell creates **fog**, placeable **anywhere outdoors**, **3×3×3 m** in size.
- **Visibility by degree:** A 1st-degree DIMMA gives **1 m** sight. Each degree beyond the first can **either add 3 m to a chosen direction (ledd)** or **reduce the sight further.** A 2nd-degree DIMMA gives **½ m** sight, a 3rd-degree **⅓ m**, and so on.
- The fog can be **dissolved whenever the caster wishes**, or if a **SKINGRING (Dispel)** overcomes the spell's degree.
- While the mage **concentrates**, they have **full control** over the fog and can move it freely. If the wizard is forced to **fight or concentrate on something else, the fog stays in place.**

### 10.2 — FÄLLA (Trap)
- **Duration (Varaktighet):** Special
- **Range (Räckvidd):** Touch (Beröring)
- FÄLLA creates a **magic trap.** It can be placed on a **door, box, window, book, floor slab, or the like.** It lasts **until triggered** — by someone/something opening the door, lifting the box, opening the book, stepping on the slab, smashing the window, or equivalent.
- **Detection:** **Cannot** be found with **Finna Dolt (Find Hidden)** — but **can** be found with **SYN (Sight/Vision)** or **VARSEBLIVNING (Perception)** (if the trap is specified).
- **Removal:** **Cannot** be removed with **Desarmera Fällor (Disarm Traps)** — **only with SKINGRING (Dispel).**
- **Mechanism:** A triggered FÄLLA **does nothing by itself.** To get any effect, you must have **embedded a second spell** in the FÄLLA when it was cast. That second spell takes effect when the FÄLLA triggers and then lasts its **normal duration.** Its **degree may not exceed** the degree of the FÄLLA. If the spell should affect something specific, **what and how must be specified at creation.**
  - **Example:** A **MINSKA 6 (Reduce/Shrink 6)** in a **FÄLLA 6**, placed on a door, would **reduce 6 STO points** when the door opens.
  - If the embedded spell normally requires a **KRA vs KRA** roll on the resistance table, then **3× the degree of FÄLLA** must overcome the victim's KRA on the resistance table.
