# JERAZ
### An adventure from *Äventyrspaket 2* for *Drakar & Demoner* (TAMB Äventyrsspel, 1987)
*Designer: Mats Blomqvist. A combat-heavy ruin crawl set in a hot, dry peninsula, centred on a demon-haunted ruined castle.*

---

## Overview

The party comes to the small village **Kharan** and is drawn — by rumour, by reward, or by a rival expedition — to the ruins of **Asiki**, the hunting castle of the long-dead mage-noble **Aram Jeraz**, where a botched demon-summoning 500 years ago left a half-open "gate to Chaos." Getting there means crossing a hostile landscape (heat, thirst, wolfmen, a monk colony that hides a curse, and a toll-taking ogre family), and the ruin itself spawns demons until the party destroys the crystal that powers the gate.

The adventure is described as relatively easy to *play* but demanding that the characters be **very well-equipped for combat**. Runs under the basic rules or with the Expert rules (EDD passages).

---

## Background & History

The region is a hot, dry peninsula of hills and low mountains, dotted with wild olive, fig and pine, and prone to rare violent rainstorms.

About **500 years ago** the area was ruled by the noble **family Jeraz**, who held several castles including the remote hunting lodge **Asiki**. The family's last head, **Aram Jeraz**, abandoned the warrior tradition of his ancestors for the study of magic. Eventually he attempted to summon a single demon — and, overreaching his skill, summoned a **whole horde** instead. The demons murdered everyone at the castle and nearly everyone within a day's march before returning whence they came. The land recovered, but a **half-open gate to Chaos** remains in the ruins, slowly leaking an evil influence.

## The Plot in Detail

### Kharan and the Hook

The party arrives at **Kharan**, a poor village of smallholders and shepherds clustered around the inn **"Röda Hästen" (The Red Horse)**. Through rumours (see below), the hunter **Zareth**, the old mercenary **Soran**, or the rival noble **Asterion**, the characters learn of the cursed ruin of Asiki on the high plains and its supposed treasures.

### The Rival Expedition

**Asterion**, a young nobleman descended from an *illegitimate* son of Aram Jeraz, has mounted his own expedition to Asiki — partly to clear his ancestor's name of the "black sorcerer" slander, partly for the riches. He travels with a magician (**Elissa**) and two soldiers (**Malchion**, **Gotarzes**). The GM can deploy this group flexibly: as ambushers, as rivals who arrived first, as rescuers (or rescue targets) who create a debt of gratitude, as a group to merge with a weak party, or simply as an information source. The party may equally choose to ambush *them*.

### The Journey

To reach Asiki the party crosses the high plains and a river. The chief environmental threats are **heat and thirst** (see rules below). On the way they may meet:
- The **wolfmen** flock on the high plains — wary but not automatically hostile;
- The collapsed **bridge** (must swim or find the ford);
- The **monk colony** in a hidden valley — outwardly idyllic, secretly cursed;
- The **ford**, guarded by a toll-taking ogre family in a cave.

### The Monk Colony's Secret

Six monks live by meditation, goat-keeping and herb-gathering. In truth **all of them are werewolves**: a younger monk drank from Asiki's well two and a half months ago, transformed at the next full moon, and infected the rest. They are wracked with guilt over the hunter, sheep and travellers they have since killed. The elder **Archelaus** tries to send guests away as the full moon nears, lying badly; only if the party seems responsible and sympathetic will he confess. Curing them earns lifelong gratitude. (Whether it is full moon during the visit is the GM's call.)

### The Ford and the Ogres

Crossing without swimming means using the ford — held by the **resar** (ogre/giant) family **Glub, Zlika**, and their son **Tsuk**. Three or fewer travellers are attacked outright; four or more are charged a "toll" (1 gm / 12 sm / 97 km per head, *plus* at least 18 STO-points of living creature to eat). They eat almost anything except insectoids and undead, refuse all haggling, target ranged fighters first, and flee from harmful magic.

### Asiki

Within 3 km of Asiki the land sickens: grass turns bluish, small animals are deformed, and near the castle nothing grows. Above ground only metre-high wall stubs remain. A staircase leads down into the **black-stone cellar**, where **demons periodically materialise** (20% present on arrival, 20% per five minutes thereafter) at one of three points. The only way to stop their appearing is to **smash the 30 cm crystal pyramid in room 5** — which also traps any currently-present demons by stopping their *disappearance*. Touching the pyramid burns (1T6); it shatters after 7 points of damage.

### Resolution

There is no single scripted ending: the party loots the ruin, survives or banishes the demons, possibly destroys the pyramid, and may settle (or be settled by) the question of Asterion, the werewolf monks, and the petrified statues. The designers leave the aftermath — including the fate of the curse and any debts of gratitude — open for the GM to develop into further adventure.

## Locations

### Kharan (the village)
- **1. Inn "Röda Hästen"** — two-storey main building, stable and outhouse; ten small upstairs rooms. Owned by the unflappable middle-aged couple **Sartos** and **Nania**. (Price list includes shared room 3 sm, private room 6 sm, lamb stew 2 sm, mug of wine 1 sm, etc.)
- **2. Soran's house** — home of the retired mercenary **Soran**, a teller of tall tales and a good source of rumours (especially over wine).
- **3–10. Ordinary houses** — typical villagers, no special abilities.
- **11. Zareth's house** — the village's only full-time hunter, the one person who knows the high plains; can be hired as a guide with a good persuasion roll and decent pay.
- **12. Well.**

### High Plains, Bridge, River
The high plains are the wolfmen's hunting ground. The once-grand **stone bridge** collapsed about a year ago (a severed horse skeleton lies at the far abutment); crossing now means swimming or the ford.

### The Monk Colony
Hidden valley with **1a–1c** monk huts, **2** Archelaus's hut (a chest with the common purse: 33 sm, 204 km), **3** an empty/dilapidated hut, **4** a goat enclosure (37 goats), **5** a storehouse (goat cheese, dried meat, dates, nuts), **6** a latrine, **7** a footbridge.

### The Ford & the Resar's Cave
- **1. Outside the cave** — a junk pile holding usable gear (two throwing spears, a kroksabel, a short sword, a battle axe, a pickaxe, a lantern, two saddles) and, at the map's cross-mark, a **buried leather sack** with 61 gm, 133 sm and **three rubies (220 / 130 / 110 sm)**.
- **2. Storeroom** — a half-eaten donkey and a sack of rancid lark pastries.
- **3. Tsuk's room** — a heap of blankets hiding three sausages.
- **4. Glub & Zlika's room** — two filthy beds; a large jar (304 km) and a small jar (21 gm, 13 sm, 3 km, and a **map** matching the overview map).
- **5. Tsuk's "playroom"** — a 2.2 m winding chamber strewn with junk (two daggers, a battle-club, bones, a dead young hyena, etc.); nothing of value.

### Asiki — Above Ground
- **A. Skeleton** — a giant's skeleton with a rusted ~3 m two-handed sword and a **gold neck-chain (400 sm)**.
- **B. Well** — water here is cursed: drinking it makes you a **werewolf** at the next full moon (a demon dumped a rotted werewolf corpse into it decades ago).
- **C. Almond grove** — trees that move on their own; **all almonds are poisonous** (strength 6).
- **D. Large bush** — dark-green leaves, blue flowers whose stench keeps animals 15 m away.
- **E. Staircase** — 3 m wide, down into the cellar.
- **F. Statue** — a marble satyr missing its right arm and left horn.

### Asiki — Underground (cellar)
Black stone; 5 m ceilings in rooms 1, 4, 5 and 3 m in 2, 3, 6, 7. Demons may appear in any room ("Varies").
- **1. Stair room** — a dozen skeletons in rusted armour; one wears a **gold ring (50 sm)**.
- **2. Wine cellar** — ~20 barrels (only sour dregs that sicken drinkers) but **three bottles of fine white wine (40 sm each)** hidden behind the east-wall barrels.
- **3. Food cellar** — collapsed shelves; nothing of value.
- **4. Antechamber** — under a loose stone by the torch-holder, a casket of bribes the guard-commander collected over a year: **3 gm, 31 sm, 79 km**.
- **5. Magic room** — the **crystal pyramid** that governs the demons (destroy to seal the gate).
- **6. Library** — burnt and torn books, plus three salvageable ones: a *Handbook of Rhetoric* (+10% Persuade after a week's study), a *Teratological Encyclopedia vol. XV* (on the corpse-eater/likätare), and *A Guide to Demonology vol. IV* (how to address demons). Under a shelf, an **ivory nymph statuette** — magic, dances on command; worth 300 sm.
- **7. Statue room** — eight life-sized statues (an old staff-bearer, a winged panther-man, an armoured warrior, a noblewoman, a giant slag-swordsman, a minotaur with a pole-axe, a huntress, a reptile-man). All are old enemies of Aram Jeraz, **petrified by his tame basilisk**; each is worth 200 sm but weighs hundreds of kg. *(Expert rules: restorable with HÄV FÖRSTENING.)*

## Characters

### Asterion (rival noble)
A 24-year-old, descended from Aram Jeraz's illegitimate son, leading an expedition to clear his ancestor's name and claim the riches. Idealistic, generous, friendly — but ambitious and proud of his birth.
*STY 14 · STO 12 · FYS 15 · SMI 15 · INT 15 · PSY 12 · KAR 16 · KP 14. Scale armour (abs 5), small shield (90%). Kroksabel 85% (1T8+2), composite bow 65% (1T10+1). Skills incl. Ride 85%, Heraldry 55%, First Aid 60%.*

### Elissa (Asterion's magician)
A 27-year-old, dark-skinned, dressed entirely in black; cynical, misanthropic, suspicious, devoted to magic — and secretly fond of Asterion. Carries a black mantle that acts as a **PSY "battery"** (PSY 20) she can draw on and recharge. Owns a rare grammar of spell-notation (worth ~150 sm).
*STY 8 · STO 10 · FYS 12 · SMI 15 · INT 18 · PSY 20 · KP 11. Spells: ANTIMAGI 60%, BLIXT 80%, FÖRSEGLA 60%, LJUS 85%, SKINGRA 80%, VARSEBLIVNING 75%, ÖPPNA 65%.*

### Malchion (soldier)
Powerfully built, dark, big-bearded, 25. Very loyal to his employer; brave and stingy.
*STY 17 · STO 13 · FYS 15 · SMI 13 · INT 11 · PSY 10 · KAR 12 · KP 14. Scale armour 5p, small shield 80%. Battle-axe 85% (1T10+2), composite bow 80% (1T10+1).*

### Gotarzes (soldier)
Sinewy, scarred, with long dirty hair. Ruthless and foolhardy, but very afraid of magic and magicians.
*STY 13 · STO 10 · FYS 12 · SMI 14 · INT 12 · PSY 11 · KAR 10 · KP 11. Ringmail 4p, small shield 70%. Kroksabel 80% (1T8+2), composite bow 80% (1T10+1), dagger 50%.*
*(All four ride dromedaries, with two pack-dromedaries carrying eight days' provisions for four, water-skins, arrows, torches, a pickaxe, sacks and cooking gear.)*

### Aram Jeraz
The long-dead mage-noble whose botched summoning created the demon gate. Appears only as backstory and as the subject of the petrified statues and the curse.

### Sartos & Nania
The unflappable middle-aged couple who own the Red Horse inn; ~30 years in the trade, hard to surprise, neither talkative nor especially informative.
*Sartos: STY 14, KP 10, club 50%. Nania: KAR 14, KP 11, dagger 40%.*

### Soran
Retired mercenary and inveterate teller of tall tales; a useful (and more honest, off-topic) source of rumours, best loosened with wine.
*STY 13, KP 10; kroksabel 85% (1T8+2), small shield 80%, throwing spear 75%.*

### Zareth
The village's only full-time hunter and the one person with real knowledge of the high plains (he's been as far as the bridge, but doesn't know it fell, nor that the other hunters are wolfmen). Quiet and laconic; hireable as a guide.
*STY 12, FYS 16, KP 13; short bow 80% (1T6+1), hand-axe 50%, leather 2p.*

### Gr'wf (wolfman chieftain)
Leader of the high-plains wolfman flock (14 adults, 6 young) — descendants of guardians the Jeraz family once hired. Muscular and scarred, wearing a lion-tooth necklace. The wolfmen speak no human tongue; communication is by sign. They flee when losing and prefer their bows.
*STY 16 · STO 12 · FYS 17 · SMI 15 · INT 13 · PSY 11 · KAR 13 · KP 15. Short bow 90% (1T6+1), short sword 65%, skin 1p + leather 2p. (Typical wolfman: short bow 85%, club 55%, movement L10.)*

### Glub, Zlika & Tsuk (the resar/ogres)
A toll-taking ogre family at the ford. **Glub** (the loud, haggle-hating patriarch) and **Zlika** are awake by day; their dim, cruel son **Tsuk** torments small animals. They attack small groups, demand toll from large ones, and flee harmful magic.
*Glub: STY 36, STO 27, KP 22, SB 2T6, great club 65% (2T8), hide 1p. Zlika: STY 33, STO 23, KP 18, SB 1T10, great club 55% (2T8). Tsuk: STY 24, STO 19, INT 4, KP 17, SB 1T6, great club 35% (2T8).*

### Archelaus & the monks (werewolves)
**Archelaus** is the sinewy, ~40-year-old monk-elder who knows some local history and confesses the curse only to a trustworthy party. He and the five other monks are **werewolves**, deeply guilt-ridden.
*Archelaus (human): STY 10, FYS 16, INT 15, KP 13, staff 90% (1T8), skin 3p. (As wolf: STY 30, FYS 24, SMI 24, KP 22, bite 1T8.) Typical monk: staff 75%; as wolf STY 33, FYS 20, KP 22, bite 1T8.*

## The Demons of Asiki

Spawned by the lingering gate. Four are statted; the GM may create more. Each has "Varies" for treasure and may appear in any cellar room.

### Geen'r
Black-and-yellow scaled, three red eyes, a tail that splits in two; condescending, sows division then attacks.
*STY 36 · STO 36 · FYS 24 · SMI 13 · INT 15 · PSY 30 · SB 2T6 · KP 30. Scales 5p. 2 claws 90% (1T6), 1 bite 80% (1T8). Speaks common 75%, Persuade 50%. Movement L16. Regeneration 1 KP/SR.*

### Gazchak
A powerful human-like body topped by **six tentacles instead of a head**; nearly 5 m long (clumsy indoors). Attacks anything that reads as alive — statues, people, animals.
*STY 53 · STO 53 · FYS 21 · SMI 12 · INT 5 · PSY 34 · SB 4T6 · KP 37. Fur/scales 2p. 2 hands 75% (1T3). Movement L14. Regeneration 2 KP/SR; Invisibility (undercut by its loud clomping and hissing).*

### Kainaa
A woman's torso (long black hair, four arms, a smooth blank where the face should be) on a black-scaled serpent body; ambush predator.
*STY 22 · STO 22 · FYS 20 · SMI 17 · INT 13 · PSY 23 · SB 1T6 · KP 21. Hide/scales 2p. 4 hands 65% (1T3). Hide 80%, Sneak 60%, Detect Danger 55%. Movement L12. Telepathy, Terror-strike, Life-drain (used heavily).*

### Te'on
A gorilla-like body in long white fur with a **hyena head and glowing blue eyes**; poses as a cursed human and tries to acquire magic items.
*STY 27 · STO 27 · FYS 19 · SMI 12 · INT 14 · PSY 25 · SB 1T10 · KP 23. Fur 5p. 2 claws 80% (1T6), 1 bite 70% (1T10). Speaks common 80%, Persuade 55%. Movement L14. Hypnosis, Telepathy.*

## Items, Weapons & Magic (notable)

- **The crystal pyramid (room 5)** — the demon-gate's controller; destroy it (7 damage, 1T6 burn to touch) to stop demons spawning (and trap any present).
- **Ivory nymph statuette (library)** — magic; dances on command; worth 300 sm.
- **Cursed well water (B)** — induces werewolf transformation at the next full moon.
- **Poisonous almonds (C)** — poison strength 6.
- **Petrified statues (room 7)** — eight enemies of Aram Jeraz turned to stone by his tame basilisk; restorable via HÄV FÖRSTENING (Expert rules).
- **Three salvageable library books** — *Handbook of Rhetoric* (+10% Persuade after a week's study), *Teratological Encyclopedia vol. XV* (corpse-eater lore), *Guide to Demonology vol. IV* (addressing demons).
- **Elissa's PSY-battery mantle** — stores PSY 20 to draw on and recharge (Expert rules: the LADDNING spell).

### Rules: Heat & Thirst (a major "enemy")
A human needs ≥3 L water/day (minotaurs 5 L; small folk 2 L; horses 10–15 L; camels/dromedaries can go a week dry). Without enough water, **STY and FYS** drop by 1 (day 1), 1 (day 2), 2 (day 3), then 4 per day; recovery is a day's rest with plentiful fluids per 2 points; at 1 you collapse, at 0 you die. **Heatstroke**: strenuous daytime effort forces a roll where FYS must beat a factor of 1 + armour absorption; failure means fainting 1T10+5 minutes and resting 1T3 hours.

### New creatures referenced (shared appendix)
- **Vargman (Wolfman)** — muscular, wolf-headed, stone-age-level pack hunters of unintelligent prey.
- **Varulv (Werewolf)** — an intelligent creature with lycanthropy; transforms at moonrise (2 SR) into a powerful wolf; spread by bite (1 point of bite damage infects); takes half damage from ordinary fire and natural weapons, but normal from silver, magic, and suffocation; in wolf form STY/FYS/SMI rise and INT/KAR fall.
- **Basilisk** — rooster-headed lizard whose gaze (and whose bone fragments) petrify living creatures; only referenced, not encountered.
- **KOPIA (spell)** — see the Novastenen file; creates immaterial duplicates of the caster.
