# Äventyrspaket 1 — Detailed Summary

*"Adventure Pack 1"* — a bundle of two short scenarios for the Swedish
roleplaying game **Drakar och Demoner**: **Svart Duell** ("Black Duel") and
**Skönheten och Odjuren** ("Beauty and the Beasts").

> This is an original summary written for reference. It paraphrases the
> booklet's content and does not reproduce its text. *Drakar och Demoner* is
> © RiotMinds AB (this product originally © 1986 TAMB Äventyrsspel HB).

---

## Publication details

- **Type:** A pack of two short adventures, each playable in roughly one
  evening, built for the basic *Drakar och Demoner* rules but usable with the
  Expert rules too. Not standalone — you need the core game. (Art. nr. 01-401.)
- **Published:** 1986, TAMB Äventyrsspel HB, Stockholm. Printed in Västerås.
- **Svart Duell** — design: Paul Schnackenburg, Henrik Becker (assistants
  Anders Johansson, Daniel Håkansson).
- **Skönheten och Odjuren** — design: Magnus Anå, Anders Blixt (assistant
  Henrik Larsson).
- **Editing:** Anders Blixt. **Interior art:** Stefan Kayat, Nils Gulliksson.
  **Maps:** Stefan Thulin, Göran Eriksson. **Cover:** Oliviera Berni.
- The two scenarios have opposite tones: *Skönheten och Odjuren* is aimed at
  beginners and rewards thinking and avoiding violence; *Svart Duell* needs more
  experienced characters and at least one mage in the party.

A note on game-stat abbreviations (Drakar och Demoner): **STY** = Strength,
**STO** = Size, **FYS** = Constitution, **SMI** = Agility, **INT** =
Intelligence, **PSY** = Psyche, **KAR** = Charisma, **SB** = damage bonus,
**KP** = body/hit points, **GC/FV** = skill chance, **BV/Abs** = weapon
durability/absorption, **E** = effect grade of a spell, **NEXUS** = an
enchantment that lets a magic item draw power from nature instead of the user.

# Adventure 1 — Svart Duell ("Black Duel")

## The Plot

**Background.** Syrta Bay (Syrtabukten) was once a quiet region beyond
civilization, ignored by the king's officials because it was so hard to reach.
That peace ended when the magician **Telach** appeared and began ravaging the
area with orcs and giants. Local orcs flocked to him, and he soon conquered the
whole region, extorting treasure from the villages and burning those that
refused to pay. His headquarters is an ancient black tower on the small island
of **Syrtan**, built in unknown antiquity by a mighty mage and sitting atop an
underground cave system. The only force that could threaten Telach is a tribe of
**cave elves** in the nearby forests; they once attacked but failed, partly
because the tower's original builder laid a curse over the island that halves the
STY and SMI of any elf-folk who land on it.

**The hook.** Traveling through a forest, the player characters are met by the
elves, who explain that the PCs must reach Syrtan and kill Telach before he
gathers enough warriors to crush them. Most of Telach's troops are off raiding
to the south, so only a few defenders remain. A small group might slip in
unnoticed where the elves' army cannot. The reward is modest — whatever the PCs
can loot (with exceptions) plus the elves' lasting friendship — but the elves
specifically want back four rings and a book, **"Vishetens stora bok" (The Great
Book of Wisdom)**, stolen from them, returned undamaged. The elves lend the PCs a
small sailboat (holds six).

**Getting there.** The roughly 8-hour sea crossing risks sharks (they swarm
anyone who falls overboard) and a treacherous reef ring; the lookout must spot
the only safe passage into the bay by the village or the boat runs aground. The
island itself is about 8 × 4 km, sparsely vegetated, with random encounters
(orc patrols, wandering skeletons, giants, and a once-only sighting of a distant
dragon en route to a dragon-gathering).

**The catch behind the tower.** The black tower is actually a sentient,
malevolent artifact that has corrupted three power-hungry humans over the
centuries — Telach being the latest. A few hours before the PCs arrive, Telach
botched a ritual to summon a demon from Chaos; the **demon Whaisszg** broke free
of its binding, forcing Telach to flee into the caves beneath the tower. The
tower's own power keeps the demon caged inside the building — it cannot leave.
So the adventurers walk into a stronghold where the master is hiding underground
and a furious demon is trapped on the upper floors.

**The climax.** The PCs make their way through the tower and down into Telach's
cave-stronghold for the final "duel." Telach fights until he loses half his hit
points, then flees through a secret passage to the harbor and sails off (raising
the alarm so the corridors fill with guards), unless he judges he can win, in
which case he rallies his orcs and giants and attacks again. If the PCs kill both
Telach and the demon and report back, an elven envoy of the goddess Mother Earth
works out how to break the tower's evil soul, and the elves' blessing causes the
earth itself to blow the tower apart. If the PCs instead keep the tower as their
own base, its evil will slowly corrupt them — a hook for a later story.

## Places (Svart Duell)

### The island of Syrtan (locations A–H)

- **A. The Reef (Revet)** — sharp reefs ringing the island, patrolled by sharks;
  the only safe passage leads to the village bay.
- **B. The Village (Byn)** — a circular ~50 m ruined fishing village behind a
  burned palisade, with 26 keyed spots. It is haunted: transparent, soundless
  shadows of fleeing, dying villagers play out their massacre, and two **giant
  ghosts (resespöken)** ambush intruders at the square. Among the corpse-filled
  houses are scattered coins, weapons, two silver candlesticks (120 sm each,
  hidden in a garden shed), and the village well (no. 20) stuffed with rotting
  bodies. **Burying the dead** earns the villagers' god's blessing: +10% to all
  skills while the character stays on the island. An attentive player notices the
  attack looks recent even though Telach has held the island for years — a
  deliberate puzzle (Telach's curse keeps the ruin "fresh," reeking and haunted).
- **C. The Path (Stigen)** — branching trails across the island; nothing special.
- **D. The Cliff (Klippan)** — sheer sea-cliffs full of nesting gulls that
  attack climbers (a 1d8 hazard table). A hidden cave entrance (**D1**) leads to
  a smaller cave (**D2**) holding the skeleton of one of Telach's deserters.
- **E. The Orcs' Guardhouse (Vakthuset)** — a windowless, black, flat-roofed
  blockhouse with arrow-slits, two rooftop guard posts, and a magically sealed
  iron-bound gate (a 75-KP *Försegla*). Garrison: five orcs and a giant, on a
  rotating watch schedule. Inside are a kitchen/bunkroom (**E1**) and a big
  gaming room (**E2**) where off-duty orcs play dice and cards (2d10 km hidden on
  each bench). The guards spot daytime arrivals and march on the village.
- **F. The River (Ån) and the Sphinx** — a stream crossed by a wooden bridge
  guarded by a **sphinx** (Telach's friend) that poses a riddle (answer: *an
  egg*); answer wrong and she attacks (she flees if losing). The clear water hides
  her past victims' treasures, divable in pieces, including a silver brooch, silver
  goblets, jewels, coins, and an enchanted **throwing dagger (Förtrolla Vapen E3)**.
- **G. The Tower (Telachs torn)** — a five-sided black cone with a sawn-off top
  and a hidden entrance (–40% to find). Five floors:
  - **G1 Corridor; G2 Library** (Telach's travel chronicles in an ancient tongue;
    five sellable books worth 50 sm each).
  - **G3 Altar Room** — deliberately disorienting (mirrors make it asymmetric;
    a FYS×5 roll to avoid nausea); the stone altar hides a passage to the caves;
    the mirrors are worth 375/1875 sm (damaged/undamaged).
  - **G4 Bedchamber, G5 Dining Room** — both smashed by the demon; G5's pantry
    hides stairs up.
  - **G6 Star Room** — a gold-and-silver planetarium (a valuable but bolted-down
    75-BEP astrologer's globe); a hidden door leads up.
  - **G7 The Demon's Lair** — a pentagram room with five scented incense burners,
    where the **demon Whaisszg** waits; each of the five walls stores 5 PSY that
    Telach can draw on for one bound spell (Fire, Image, Darkness, Energy-beam,
    Protection), recharging 1/day. A silver dagger (300 sm) lies on a table; a
    ladder leads to the roof.
  - **G8 The Roof** — holds a **flying carpet** (*Lyft 7* + *Nexus*, worth ~3000 sm
    to someone who knows what it is) — a handy escape from the demon.

### Telach's underground stronghold (the cave system, rooms H1–H20)

- **H1 Passage from the Tower** — torch-lit; a hidden trapdoor (–40% to find)
  drops the unwary into an underground river that washes them out to sea.
- **H2 Telach's Passage** — ends at a richly decorated **bronze gate** with no
  handle or keyhole, opened only by speaking the password **"Faugnir"** (written
  in near-invisible letters in the ancient tongue); a wrong reading misleads.
  Speaking the word also activates the skeletons in H5.
- **H3 Giant Guards' Room** — home of the giant bodyguards **Ghashan** (money
  hidden in the wall) and **Tahg** (an art-lover with two paintings worth 112 and
  105 sm; the GM may make one worth 1000 sm). **H4** is an identical, empty
  giant-room (its occupants are out patrolling).
- **H5 The Undeads' Passage** — six niches holding skeletons in black armor with a
  red sun on the chest, activated by the "Faugnir" password.
- **H6 Telach's Chamber** — where Telach is found; he greets visitors politely
  while secretly casting *Skydd*, then teleports his sword to hand and attacks.
  His bookshelf holds the quest items and his magic gear (see below).
- **H7 Telach's Bedroom** — a hidden door (sealed with *Försegla 2*) leads to his
  secret escape passage, which holds his hidden coin (96 gm, 582 sm) if he hasn't
  fled with it.
- **H8 The Stinking Passage** — a fish-reeking dark corridor to H9.
- **H9 The Underground Lake** — home to an **alligator**, one of Telach's pets.
- **H10 The Underground River** — the fast outflow that carries trapdoor victims
  out to sea.
- **H11 Kitchen; H12 Latrines; H13–H18 Orc bedrooms** — mostly mundane; **H17**
  holds two sleeping orc brothers, **Ork and Tork**.
- **H19 The Storeroom** — stolen goods, weapon-metal, supplies (locked, 50 KP);
  ten amphorae of wine (50 sm each) hidden among the junk; backs onto Telach's
  escape route (the hidden door can't be opened from this side).
- **H20 The Harbor** — a water-filled cave with a quay, supply crates, and two
  ships; four orc stevedores load the boats. Telach's escape point.

## Characters & Creatures (Svart Duell)

- **Telach** — the villain: once an ordinary magician, corrupted by the tower into
  a power-hungry conqueror (STY 17, INT 18, PSY 24, KP 13). No armor — he relies on
  his *Skydd* spell. Wields the mithril two-handed sword **"Månbladet" (the
  Moon-blade)**, made by the long-dead mage Gamat, which he can teleport to his
  hand at will. Spells include *Försegla, Mörker, Antimagi, Avbild, Eld, Skydd,
  Energistråle*; the five tower walls feed several of these. Highly skilled and
  meant to be played as a cunning, slippery opponent who flees a losing fight.
- **The demon Whaisszg** — a kangaroo-shaped Chaos demon with cat feet, predator
  teeth, bat ears, and horn-plated hide (STY 40, KP 32, 8-point hide). Bite plus a
  poisoned tail-stinger; the demonic gift of Speed. Trapped inside the tower; it
  fights to the death but cannot leave the building.
- **The sphinx** — lion-bodied, eagle-winged, with a woman's fanged head and a
  mania for riddles; a friend of Telach who guards the river bridge.
- **The cave elves** — the PCs' patrons, who want their stolen rings and Book of
  Wisdom back; their goddess Mother Earth's envoy can break the tower's curse.
- **Giant bodyguards Ghashan and Tahg** (H3) and the **guardhouse garrison** —
  named orcs (Olb, Sadh, Omer, Torsehk, Kaerk, Jishnak the watch-chief) plus the
  giant **Grum**, each individually statted.
- **Orc patrols** — roaming groups (named members include Braschet the patrol
  leader, Lillklink, Gorm, Chack — who has a sleeping-poison hook for a hand —
  Gursch and Vrash) ordered to capture suspicious strangers for Telach.
- **Wandering giants** — Storslägga, Huggkubbe (one-legged, aggressive) and the
  big Brumme, on the same capture orders.
- **Two giant ghosts (resespöken)** in the village and **black-armored skeletons**
  with a red-sun device, plus the **alligator** and **sharks** as creature hazards.

## Notable items (Svart Duell, beyond ordinary gear)

- **"Vishetens stora bok" (Great Book of Wisdom)** and **four rings** — the elves'
  quest items, found in Telach's chamber (H6); the rings aren't magical but are
  holy to the tribe, to be returned untouched.
- **Telach's gear (H6):** the mithril sword *Månbladet*; a **silver diadem**
  (*Antimagi 2* + *Nexus*); a **key** with *Försegla 4*; a **crystal ball**
  (*Ljus 5*, activates when the user thinks of friends in need); a small **wooden
  pegasus statuette** (*Hela E1*); a **ring** of *Öka 3*.
- **The flying carpet** on the tower roof (*Lyft 7* + *Nexus*).
- **The enchanted throwing dagger** (*Förtrolla Vapen E3*) in the sphinx's river.
- Sundry sellable loot: the planetarium globe, the altar-room mirrors, silver
  candlesticks, the wine amphorae, and the bronze gate (too heavy to move).

# Adventure 2 — Skönheten och Odjuren ("Beauty and the Beasts")

## The Plot

**Background (timeline).** Centuries ago, Karl Svärd founded the **Karl Order
(Karlsorden)**, dedicated to the god **Eiron** and sworn to fight darkness. The
order built **Karlsklostret**, a wooden monastery on the shore of the clear
mountain lake **Anja**, which contains an island called **Anjas Öga (Anja's
Eye)**. The monastery's prior, **Edvard Eskilsson**, was brother to the local
lord, **baron Eskil Eskilsson** of the Pansarnäve ("Armored-fist") clan. About
60 years before "now," a svartfolk army invaded; baron Eskil crushed one orc
band and seized its war-booty, which he gave to his brother the prior, who hid it
in an **underground temple beneath Anja's Eye**. The baron then died trying to
break out to reach the king's army. The monastery was stormed and burned. The
captured prior offered to lead the orc commander to the treasure, but once down
in the temple he spoke a secret **"Word"** that extinguished the holy altar fire
and opened concealed channels, flooding the temple and drowning himself and the
orc leader to protect the hoard. A few brothers escaped; the monastery was never
rebuilt. One survivor, **brother Franz**, still lives, very old, in the village
of Eskilsby.

**The hook.** The PCs come into possession of **"The Document" (Dokumentet)** —
an excerpt from the monastery's chronicle, found e.g. among the remains of a
bandit-attacked caravan. Using Heraldry and History they can locate where the
Pansarnäve clan ruled and trek to the little community of **Eskilsby**, at the
foot of the Pansarnäve fortress **Eriksborg** (where the current lord, baron
Eskil Eriksson, resides). Supplies and mounts are scarce and expensive here.

**The guide.** Only **brother Franz** knows where the monastery stood. If the PCs
win his trust, he'll come along — lured by a last look at his childhood home —
though he's physically frail and a bit dotty. He knows the temple's altar fire
must be relit to drain the water, but little about its layout, and the war-booty
is unknown to him.

**The bandits.** Lake Anja lies a week's march into the wilderness; the ruined
monastery on its shore is now occupied by a small, sympathetic **bandit gang**
led by the ex-soldier **Rickard Hökvinge** (who deserted rather than help raze
his home village). The bandits assume the PCs are the baron's agents and will
first try to scare them off (using their giant member), then fight — but they
*won't* fight to the death and will switch to forest guerrilla tactics if
pressed. Crucially, the PCs can **negotiate**: convince the bandits their errand
is against the baron and the gang may cooperate for an equal share, which spares
the PCs a fight against opponents who know the terrain. (This is the adventure's
"thinking beats violence" theme.)

**The flooded temple.** The treasure sits in the water-filled temple under Anja's
Eye, reached by a spiral stair down into a flooded shaft. The central puzzle is
**draining the water**, which can be done at least three ways: relight the holy
altar fire (room 3) so the inflow channels seal (~15 min to drain); use *Stenvägg*
to plug the inflow tunnel found with *Varseblivning*; or have a dwarf dig a new
lake outlet (~84 man-hours, lowering the lake ~5 m over ~12 hours). With the
Expert rules, *Vattenandning* plus a summoned *Sylph* can carry fire to the
altar. The altar fire, once lit, can only be put out by speaking the lost "Word"
— so it burns over water, powered by Eiron.

**The temple's guardian and the climax.** When any intelligent non-svartfolk
enters the temple hall (room 3), the **ghost of prior Edvard Eskilsson**
materializes by the altar, gesturing for the fire to be relit and barring anyone
from leaving until it is (he can no longer speak well). Relighting the fire frees
his tortured soul — and he blesses everyone present with a permanent +5% to one
weapon skill. (Caution: if frail brother Franz suffers Edvard's fright-attack,
there's a 50% chance he dies of a heart attack.) Once the water recedes, the PCs
can reach the vaults holding the war-booty.

## Places (Skönheten och Odjuren)

- **Eskilsby** — a ~250-person village at the foot of **Eriksborg** (baron Eskil
  Eriksson's fortress, ~50 cavalry). Home of brother Franz; little to buy, with
  expensive mounts (light horse 500 sm, pony 300 sm, donkey 250 sm).
- **Lake Anja and Anjas Öga** — the lake (a week into the wilds) with the central
  island; its hidden outflow surfaces at a spring (källsprånget). The shoreline
  brush grew up after the lake sank when the monastery fell.
- **The monastery ruins (Klosterruinen)** — demolished buildings inside a decayed
  palisade, now the bandit gang's base.
- **The Temple on Anja's Eye** — a round surface building (7 m across) whose
  spiral stair (**room 1, Nedgång**) descends into the flooded temple. Keyed
  rooms below:
  - **2. Antechamber (Försal)** — three orc skeletons; minor coin.
  - **3. Temple Hall (Tempelsalen)** — the bronze altar (holy to Eiron, eerily
    un-silted), reliefs of Eiron's and Karl's myths, four skeletons including the
    prior **Edvard Eskilsson** and the orc leader; the altar must be relit; the
    prior's ghost appears here. Loot: a mithril **boot-dagger (Förtrolla Vapen E3,
    Nexus)** and the prior's exquisite ~800 sm mithril dagger-sheath (heraldically
    marked — unsellable locally; the baron will want it back).
  - **4. Dining Hall (Måltidssal)** — water-logged furniture; clear a path in
    ~10 min.
  - **5. Vestry (Skrudkammare)** — muddy gold-thread (150 sm); a hidden door (+25%
    to find) to rooms behind.
  - **6. Hall** — a bronze-fitted oak door (200 KP) on the south wall and a flood
    crayfish; **7. Hall** with stairs down; **8. Hall** with a half-collapsing
    flooded passage; **9. Hall** linking to the room-1 secret door.
  - **10. Guardian Hall (Väktarsalen)** — lit by enchanted ceiling stones; long
    green vines cover the floor — a dormant **strangle-vine (stryparranka)** that
    "wakes" ~30 minutes after the door opens (half strength at 30 min, full after
    an hour) and ambushes the careless.
  - **11. Antechamber (Försal)** — 300-KP bronze-fitted oak doors.
  - **12. Great Security Vault (Stora säkerhetsvalv)** — baron Eskil's war-booty:
    6000 km, 3000 sm, 200 gm in (rotting) sacks; two chests of jewels (worth 1400
    and 1900 gm); and three magic items (see below).
  - **13. Little Security Vault (Lilla säkerhetsvalv)** — the monastery's holy
    silver vessels: seven pitchers (1000 sm each), seven serving-dishes (1200 sm
    each), 49 chalices (150 sm each). A Karl-brother in the party will insist
    these belong to the order and must not be taken.
  - **14–17. Library (Biblioteket)** — book-scrolls all ruined by age/damp.
  - **18. Secret Treasure Chamber (Hemliga skattkammaren)** — shelves of objects;
    contents left to the GM to fill in.

## Characters & Creatures (Skönheten och Odjuren)

- **Brother Franz** — the frail, ~80-year-old monk and sole living guide to the
  monastery (PSY 18; skilled in Botany, Reading/Writing; weak in body and
  somewhat confused). Knows relighting the altar fire drains the temple, but
  little else; at risk from the ghost's fright-attack.
- **Edvard Eskilsson's ghost** — the former prior's tortured spirit, bound to the
  temple (SMI 30, PSY 32, a Fright-attack), who forces and then helps visitors
  relight the altar and is freed (blessing the party) when they do.
- **The bandit gang** — morally grey highwaymen who avoid killing locals:
  - **Rickard Hökvinge** — the leader, a former soldier turned outlaw, a good and
    well-liked commander (STY 15, FYS 18, INT 18; battle-axe and large shield,
    metal mail).
  - **Silverfot, Nejlika, Panther** — followers; Nejlika is a crack woods-woman
    (crossbow). 
  - **Krannish** — an unusually well-mannered **giant** who joined the gang for
    company.
  - Their combined treasure (25 gm, 203 sm, 623 km, three *Hela E2* potions) sits
    in a chest in the ruins, which they'll grab if forced to flee.
- **The strangle-vine (stryparranka)** in room 10 — a man-eating plant (STY 32,
  KP 28, eight grasping tentacles) lurking dormant after the long flooding.
- Plus a **flood crayfish** and the room-3 **orc skeletons** as minor hazards.

## Notable items (Skönheten och Odjuren, beyond ordinary gear)

- **The war-booty** (room 12): bulk coin, two caskets of jewels (1400 + 1900 gm).
- **Three magic items** (room 12 chest): a **gold arm-band** (*Öka E3*, no Nexus,
  command-phrase activated); a **mithril short sword** (*Förtrolla Vapen E3*, no
  Nexus, command-phrase); and a **silver rod** (*Frammana Sylf E2*, no Nexus,
  command-phrase).
- **The mithril boot-dagger** (*Förtrolla Vapen E3*, Nexus) on the orc leader's
  skeleton (room 3), and the prior's heraldically-marked **mithril dagger-sheath**
  (~800 sm, unsellable locally).
- **The monastery's holy silver vessels** (room 13) — claimable only if no
  Karl-brother objects.
- The bandits' chest (coins + three *Hela E2* potions) and the GM-defined hoard in
  room 18.

## Bestiary appendix (creatures detailed in the booklet for the GM)

The pack includes stat profiles for the non-standard creatures it uses: the
**Rese** (giant — a big, fierce, not-very-bright relative of orcs); the
**Stryparranka** (a man-eating, magically-created strangle-vine); the **Sfinx**
(riddling lion/eagle/woman hybrid); and the **Haj** (shark), **Alligator**, plus
the demon, ghosts, skeletons and various orcs/giants statted within each
adventure.
