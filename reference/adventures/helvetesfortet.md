# Helvetesfortet ("The Hell Fort") — Complete Summary

**Den Nidländska Reningen, Part 1 of 3** — an adventure module for *Drakar och Demoner Expert*, set in the world of Ereb Altor. Published by Target Games / Äventyrsspel, 1991. Requires the Expert rules and the Ereb Altor box.

*Credits:* Lead design Johan Sjöberg; editing Henrik Strandberg; cover M. Harrison; maps Ann-Sophie Qvarnström; interior art Gabriel Sandberg; production Nils Gulliksson. Foreword written in Lidingö, November 1990.

This is the free-standing first chapter of a trilogy in which the player characters (PCs) struggle against the dreaded archmage Vicotnik. The designer notes that while the module can be played "hack-and-slay," it rewards thought, and that the players are **fated heroes called by the gods** — meaning several plot beats are deliberately railroaded, and the Game Master (GM) is told to nudge the PCs back on track if they stray, because later installments and the prophecies depend on it.

---

## Setting Background

### Hynsolge
A long coastal country on the western side of the Krunish peninsula in the southeast of the continent Ereb Altor, bordered by seas on multiple sides. It is divided into **thirteen counties (län)**, separated by natural borders. The **Hynsolgians** are short (165–170 cm), lean, dark-haired and dark-eyed, sunburnt, hot-tempered and quick to reach for weapons. The climate is warm, occasionally unbearably so.

It borders Krun and Nidland to the south, Cereval (the dragon-masters' realm) to the northeast, and Landoris' elf-woods to the northwest. Grynnerbergen mountains form the eastern boundary; two great forests lie within — Hyllmarskogen (east) and Gormowac's forest (north).

### The Civil War (Inbördeskriget)
Since the year **604 eO**, Hynsolge has been wracked by civil war (the adventure plays out in the winter between **612 and 613 eO**). It began over a harvest failure: when the nobility refused to help the starving peasants, the peasants rose in revolt. King **Mefimor IV** fights on the peasants' side. The rebel main force is the **Nya Armén ("New Army")**, led by **Abrahim Stenklo**, an almost-forty-year-old warrior. The war's outcome long looked uncertain, but most now believe the people will win, as the last real power on the nobility's side is **Anargel Stålkänga, baron of Sirigon**, who still holds out with orcish and Felician mercenaries. The countryside has been ravaged and villages burned, so even victory will leave the land scarred.

### Nidland
Lies on the eastern part of the Krunish peninsula, connected to the continent of Akrogal by a thin land-bridge. For over 100 years it has been entirely walled off from the world by a great wall, the **Stora Muren ("the Great Wall")**. Population **1.2 million, all human** — tall, sunburnt, strongly built, with thick full beards; men wear light, airy clothes and large turbans. The capital is **Khaaníkel**. The Evler plain in the south holds the Ümerhít highlands, whose mountains are rich in precious metals.

### Vicotnik the Archmage (Ärkemagikern)
Since the year **468 eO**, a man named **Vicotnik** has ruled Nidland as dictator. Beneath him serve **twelve disciples**, who like him command the fearsome **dark magic (mörkermagi)**, whose effects are largely unknown. ("Vicotnik" means "the Archtroll-man" in East-Hynsolgian, the root of the Nidlandish tongue.)

### The Nidlandish Cleansing (Den Nidländska Reningen)
When Vicotnik arrived in Nidland in 468 (then still part of Hynsolge), he proclaimed a prophecy: that in **"a dozen dozen years"** (a dozen dozen = 144 years, i.e. the year **612**) he would launch a crusade against the rest of the world — the *Nidländska Reningen* — to "cleanse" it. Recent years have indeed seen military buildup in Nidland despite the land's scarcity of iron for forging weapons. Yet as 612 nears its end, no crusade has appeared, and it seems the Cleansing has failed to materialize.

**The crucial twist:** Akrogalian/Nidlandish years are **three days longer** than ordinary years — the sun crosses the sky **368 times**, not 365. This means only about **143 years** have passed in Nidland while 144 have passed in the rest of Ereb. The Cleansing has *not* failed — it is **imminent**. The PCs' mission is to discover this and, ultimately, to disrupt it.

## The Plot

### 1. The Tavern (Silverspjutet, Bedorum)
The PCs are mercenaries who, about a week ago, were dismissed from the Nya Armén along with their fellow soldiers (the local situation no longer being deemed to need hired blades). They gather upstairs at the tavern **Silverspjutet ("The Silver Spear")** in **Bedorum**, capital of Mergilia county, at the invitation of **Argwin**, a big red-bearded man who hosts a final farewell feast (he failed to convince the Nya Armén to pay for it, but trusts everyone has coin enough). A company of Dalkic archers drinks downstairs.

Through tavern conversation the PCs are drawn into the plot. The Dalks gossip about the war and about Nidland; one recalls his village priest predicting Vicotnik's "cleansing" was due "this very year," and others recall an old man muttering "when a dozen dozen years come, the end comes for you all." A mercenary then scoffs that **Vicotnik is a bluff**, pointing at **Kharman** — a turban-wearing Akrogalian in their own company — as proof. This exchange should lead the PCs to the key insight: the prophecy spoke in **"Akrogalian years,"** which are longer, so the reckoning is different than assumed. The GM is instructed to make sure the players latch onto this thread — otherwise there is no adventure. They agree to meet the next day at 11:00 to investigate.

Meanwhile **Kharman** — secretly **Achatoth's spy**, his cover now blown — grows nervous, slips out, and rides off toward Nidland to report. (If the PCs follow him they find only that he leaves; if they search his room later they find clues, detailed below.)

### 2. Gathering Information in Bedorum
The locals can point the PCs to several sources: the **Anyak temple**, **old man Otho**, the merchant **Tegan Gyllenkedja**, and the **town hall (Rådhuset)**. From these they can piece together (a) the three-day-longer Akrogalian year, and (b) the existence of **Helvetesfortet ("the Hell Fort")**, a fortress on the Nidlandish side of Grynnerbergen.

At the **Rådhuset**, the lone available councilman **Ulbar** explains he cannot lend soldiers — the King has strictly forbidden the Bedorum garrison from leaving the town — but offers a loan of **500 silver (sm)** and suggests the PCs sneak into Nidland to gather intelligence on troop numbers and equipment. He recommends a guide: **Ehrman Wall**, who has good contacts with the dwarves.

### 3. The Journey to Ehrman Wall
The PCs travel about **two and a half days** to **Ehrman Wall's valley (Ehrman Walls dal)** on the Hynsolgian highland. Ehrman is a reclusive trapper-guide who refuses to lead anyone into Nidland *willingly*. If pressed with money he will take them (≥600 sm; reducible to ~200 sm if they explain the true cause and appeal to him), on the condition that he only guides them to the border, not into Nidland itself. He proposes the route through **Tarat-khôn**, the dwarven realm, which extends under Grynnerbergen and beneath the Great Wall, allowing a hidden entry. He advises bringing torches and leaving the horses in his valley.

### 4. Encounters En Route (Möten)
The party crosses four regions: Mergilia's ravaged countryside, Hyllmarskogen, Grynnerbergen, and finally Nidland. Possible encounters include couriers, jobless mercenaries, refugees, beggars, orphans, robber bands. Three encounters are **obligatory** for the plot:

- **Hyllmarskogen — Rescuing Talyros (obligatory).** A small robber band camps here. More importantly, the PCs hear combat and find the elf-prince **Talyros** defending himself and his wounded **pegasus, Enulisor**, against **15 svartalfer (dark elves)**. The PCs must intervene; after a short fight the dark elves flee. Talyros heals all the PCs' wounds, gives each a **berry from the elf-glass-birch** that grants darkvision for several hours, and pledges a vital boon: **"Enulisor can hear your cry of distress from six days' march (16 mil) away. Call, and you shall be fetched."** (This is the only escape from the finale.) Looting the dead dark elves yields 27 km, 13 sm, 5 gm, and jewels worth 41 gm.

- **Grynnerbergen — The Oracle (obligatory).** A violent storm forces the party into a cave with a "mystical pull." Inside sits a masked, skin-wrapped humanoid who greets them as the chosen and recites the prophecy:
  > *"One day a Cleansing shall come / When the Punisher / Through the unknowing ones' arrival / To the Abode of Death / Gives the Cleansing Ones power."*
  
  The figure then flares with light and vanishes, leaving the prophecy hanging in fiery script. (This foreshadows the climax: the PCs are "the unknowing ones," Helvetesfortet is "the Abode of Death.")

- **Grynnerbergen also imposes a special magic rule** (mineral-rich rock disrupts magic): all spells cast at **one effect grade lower** (minimum 1), **cost +1 PSY**, **double the risk of misfire (snedtändning)** with **+2 to the misfire table**, and the spell **JORDVÄG ("earth-path") is useless**.

### 5. Tarat-khôn — Turned Away at the North Gate
Tarat-khôn consists of the capital Konrith-thar and five smaller cities (one, **Ghan-toth**, has been abandoned). Almost all dwarves are warriors or miners. Only the **Norra Porten ("North Gate")**, also called **Ghan-meth**, matters here.

**Backstory:** In 609 the Hynsolgian nobleman **Pariklias Måånsköld** attacked Tarat-khôn to seize weapons and ore. The dwarves repelled him but, frightened, sealed all their gates and stopped selling ore and weapons. This enraged **Vicotnik** (their biggest customer), who in revenge helped a svartfolk tribe colonize the dwarves' old abandoned mines (Ghan-toth) to supply himself. The dwarves kept only the North Gate open, for contact with remaining friends.

The garrison, normally 50 warriors, is currently only **15 plus the commandant Gidmir Hållfaste and his 4-man bodyguard** (due to the splintered state of Hynsolge). Their gear: white tabards bearing a **four-pointed light-blue star with a black half-circle**, black double-axes, conical brass-ornamented helms, knee-length ringmail.

No matter what reasons the PCs give, commandant **Gidmir Hållfaste** — embittered toward humans since Måånsköld's attack — **refuses passage** and ejects them, blindfolded. The PCs *cannot* force through: even if they somehow defeated every dwarf, the inner gate (to room 14) is unopenable, requiring a hidden mithril key *and* a password known only to Gidmir, who would die before revealing it. The gate resists ÖPPNA and JORDVÄG.

**Gidmir or Ehrman then suggests an alternative:** the abandoned dwarf-city **Ghan-toth**, three days south, whose mines may also pass under the Great Wall. Neither realizes Ghan-toth is now a **svartfolk colony**. Ehrman will guide them there but **will not enter** through the gates.

### 6. Through Ghan-toth
The PCs must slip through Ghan-toth largely unseen — open combat would draw overwhelming numbers. The city is best crossed by night (no orcs in the mines then) or by day (city nearly empty). They pass the gate guard, traverse the dwarf-scale city (2 m-wide streets, sustained stench requiring FYS rolls, dim light from surface shafts), slip through the smiths' quarter, and descend into the **mines**.

### 7. Ma'tho Fherengi's Burial Vault (obligatory)
In the mines the PCs reach a large hall. Crossing it, they are attacked by **wolf-riding orcs (1T6+10 of them)** and flee onto a **runaway, brakeless ore-wagon** down a slope. The wagon crashes through a barrier and the PCs tumble down a shaft into an **underground lake**, finding themselves in an ancient burial complex — **Ma'tho Fherengi's grave vault**.

The vault belongs to **Ma'tho Fherengi**, the last ruler of a humanoid race that predates elves, dwarves, and humans. Here the PCs can recover the two great treasures: the **Demonspiran ("Demon Sceptre")** and the sword **Fherengi Fanor**, both held by Ma'tho's mummy. Notable rooms and hazards:

- **The Tunnel (1):** gold-clad walls, gold-armored statues, an inscription on an unknown-language door (no visible handle).
- **The Spider Trap (2):** an iron grate would once have trapped intruders for a giant spider — but the spider died long ago; its desiccated, harmless husk remains, and the grate can be lifted.
- **Room of the Four Ways (3):** octagonal, with doors inscribed (in ancient script, readable with Forntunga B5 + INT roll) "The Last Fherengi," "His Second," "His First."
- **The wives' chambers (4 & 5):** the two queens' tombs, richly furnished with gold and silver grave goods (e.g. arm-bands and rings worth 500 gm; balsamed corpse with silver mask worth 400 sm, silver jewelry worth 750 sm). The women are of the same extinct human-like race.
- **The Pillar Hall (6):** a green-stone statue of Ma'tho in a chariot drawn by two *bathur*; **Living Armor** guardians flank a hard-to-open door (**STY 35** on the resistance table).
- **The Trapdoor (7):** a tilting floor-section; failing a Hard SMI roll drops you into a hole, sealing a grate and releasing **four skeletons**.
- **Antechamber (8):** statues of servants/craftsmen; a **secret door (−5 to Find Hidden)** with a nonsense "eternal curse" inscription leads to the treasure room.
- **The Treasure Room (9):** a gilded cabinet (gold breastplate, gold-and-horn helmet on a gold stand), and a locked chest (**SG 50**) holding **25 gold ingots of 3 kg each**.
- **The Gas Trap (10):** a corridor with a relief swinging a sword and a strangely-shaped sceptre; stepping on the marked floor triggers poison gas from floor-level holes (~3 SR until dangerous; 2T6 KP damage). Escape by pressing the **relief's right eye** (stops the gas), then exit through the wall ahead — the door back to room 8 locks.
- **Ma'tho Fherengi's burial chamber (11):** the gilded room with the sarcophagus; opening it reveals the **mummy** wielding **Fherengi Fanor** in its right hand and holding the **Demonspiran** in its left.

### 8. Out via the Gate toward the Great Wall (Porten mot Stora Muren)
A back exit guarded by **3 soldiers + 1 rese (giant)** (haskushdal-clan orcs who'd rather surrender than fight; the giant fights until the orcs give up). A heavy bar (**STY 35**) must be lifted. Beyond lies the Hynsolgian side of the Great Wall.

### 9. Into Nidland — Over the Wall
The PCs emerge into daylight, the Wall some 300 m off. **The Wall:** 7 m high, watchtowers every 100 m (10 m tall), larger fortified gates every 5 km, total garrison **50,000**. Built 502–541 by slaves and convicts. **Every other tower has human guards (3 soldiers each); the alternating towers have magical guardians (a *Väktare* in bird form).** Guard shifts rotate monthly; a patrol passes weekly to resupply. Coastal stretches toward Cereval and Krun (where the Wall doesn't reach) use signal-fire chains that relay alarms quickly to Khaaníkel.

The PCs must **climb over** (difficult without a grappling hook). Crossing near a magic-guarded tower triggers alarm unless they have both **SINNESMASK** and **OSYNLIGHET** active (min E4) or land **SKINGRA E15** on the Väktare; a manned tower is even riskier.

**Kharman's Return:** As the PCs approach, they hear hoofbeats; **Kharman** rides up, makes a strangled call, throws up a rope, and climbs over the Wall. The dangling rope is pulled up after ~1 minute — the PCs must act fast to use it.

### 10. The Capture (Tillfångatagandet) — fate-mandated
The crossing triggers the alarm. **The PCs *must* be captured** — this is required for the plot, so the GM should make it swift. A patrol of about **50 men under an überstekrigare (very capable officer)** surrounds them. Resistance gets them knocked unconscious (or killed). They are stripped to their clothes and bound in coarse **hand-, foot-, and neck-irons that block all magic use**. The officer hints that "**My lord, the Archdisciple Achatoth, has been expecting you.**" They are marched under heavy guard to **Helvetesfortet**.

### 11. Helvetesfortet ("The Hell Fort")
A massive fortress built entirely of black stone, set in a Grynnerbergen ravine on the Nidlandish side. Corpses of humans, animals and other creatures hang from its walls; tortured screams echo from laboratories. It has **four gates** (one large, three small) and **eight towers** (each tower larger than the entire castle at Fervidun). It garrisons close to **10,000 men** (it looks like 100,000), with a vast war-camp around it, siege engines, and tall horses. Its supreme commander is **Achatoth, 5th disciple of Vicotnik**. It is only one of several mobilization sites for the coming Cleansing.

The PCs are thrown into a cell, all gear confiscated (except clothes). **No map is provided** — instead the GM uses random tables: **Table A** (corridor/passage layout), **Table B** (rooms below ground), **Table C** (rooms above ground), generating guardrooms, torture chambers, prison-holes, barracks, armories, etc.

### 12. The Meeting with Achatoth (climax)
The next day the PCs are brought to **Achatoth's chamber** atop the tallest tower — octagonal, nearly 40 m across, with a **black circle absorbing all light** on the floor, a podium, and the PCs' confiscated gear on a small table. Achatoth has the guards unshackle the PCs and dismisses them (a whip lashes anyone who acts up; anyone attempting a spell chokes instantly).

Achatoth reveals the truth: **the PCs themselves are "the unknowing ones"** of the prophecy. They are to be **sacrificed at moonrise that very night**, when the stars are favorable, so that **Vicotnik and his disciples — "the Cleansing Ones" — gain the power needed to launch the Cleansing.** He declares: *"my name is Achatoth the Punisher, and I shall be the one who gives my lords the power they need for the Nidlandish Cleansing!"* and laughs triumphantly.

### 13. The Escape — the Demonspiran
At this moment, the PC who **first touched the Demonspiran** in the vault sees it begin to **glow red** and hears it telepathically:
> *"I am the Guardian of the Gate. Tell me when all hope is out and you need His help."*

(Achatoth, meanwhile, perceives the world slowing around him as the sceptre's power stirs — but the PCs can speak of it freely without him understanding.) If the PCs **grab and invoke the sceptre**, the imprisoned **Bestraffaren ("the Punisher")** within it is freed, instantly resolves their predicament, and tells them to flee. **This same chance recurs at the execution that night** if they miss it now (when their gear is again laid in the same place and Achatoth begins the Death Ritual). If they pass up *both* chances, "then it's pretty much over."

### 14. The Homeward Flight (Hemresan)
Ten minutes after the Bestraffaren is freed, it breaks fully out of its prison and assumes its **true form** — an enormous **25 m tall, coal-black, horned, reptilian-tailed demon** — in an explosion that **annihilates everything within a 300 m radius**. It sends the PCs a telepathic message of thanks (*"I am the Punisher. I thank you human creatures for freeing me from my prison. Return now to your home dimension; this is no longer a fitting place for you."*) and departs **inward toward Nidland to aid Vicotnik** (the thread continues in Part 2).

**The only way to survive the blast** is to call **Enulisor** (Talyros's boon). The pegasus arrives regardless of distance (hearing within 16 mil), bringing other pegasi, and flies the PCs to safety as everything goes black. The adventure ends — "an exciting continuation follows in the next part!"

## Key Locations

### Bedorum
Capital of Mergilia county, ~500 inhabitants, mostly on the western Grynner plateau. War-scarred, still showing ruins of burned villages. Conquered for the rebel cause by Abrahim Stenklo himself (who slew the previous lord, Boris Guldstenck, his father's murderer). A market town where one can buy almost anything, and famous for its beer. Ruled by a council: the lord, the burgomasters, and academic/administrative office-holders. Surrounded by a **5 m town wall**.

Numbered locations on the town map include:
- **(1) Borgen (the castle):** on a central hill, garrisoned by ~200 Nya Armén soldiers; residence of **Edward Silversporre**, the appointed lord of Mergilia and one of Stenklo's closest, most capable men; also houses the town jail.
- **(2) Rådhuset (Town Hall):** Bedorum's largest building, granite ground floor with two wooden upper floors; seat of nearly all academic and administrative offices. (See plot — councilman Ulbar.)
- **(3, 8, 17) Breweries:** two small and one large; source of Bedorum's renowned light beer.
- **(5) Anyaktemplet:** see below.
- **(11) Tegan Gyllenkedja's general store / Diversehandlare:** see Tegan below.
- **(18) Bank:** a depository for money and valuables; if robbed, obliged to repay a percentage to customers.
- **(22) Finsmed (Fine-smith):** a smith specializing in silver- and goldwork (skill B4).
- **(23) Silverspjutet, (6) Natt och Dag, (15) Stopet:** the three taverns.
- **(27) Värdshus Kitteln:** Bedorum's only inn.
- **(32) Lärd man (Learned man):** well-versed in many fields; knows of Helvetesfortet and the Akrogalian year (an alternative info source to the temple).
- Other numbered trade buildings: bakers (12, 7), glazier (16), stables (5 — *Hyrstall*), midwife/herbalist (9 — *Jordemor*), master builder (14), tailor (26), butcher (20), smiths (19, 34), school (13), shoemaker (10), town wall (33), town guard barracks (25), weaponsmith (21), armorer (31), cooper (28), weaver (24).

### Anyaktemplet (the Anyak Temple), Bedorum
Consecrated to **Anyak, god of light** (worshipped by the *Parbagerna*). The upper floor is built of green-veined white marble; the lower floor of timber. The whole temple is ringed by a dense **3 m iron-oak hedge** overgrown with **white claw-roses**, nearly impenetrable without being torn to shreds; a corner garden holds a half-dozen goats and grows fruit, vegetables and herbs. Currently staffed by one **high priest, three priests, and eleven novices**, robed in white tunics embroidered with Anyak's symbol (a golden sun with four long curving rays), shaven-headed, never wearing headgear.

The high priest is **Pirocton** (a short, fat man). The library (bokrullsamling) can be researched for a fee (15 sm per person per day) or freely if the PCs win the priests' trust. Pirocton knows Vicotnik came to Nidland in 468 and that the Cleansing was prophesied for "a dozen dozen" years = 612. The bookroll-priest **Bhûl-ten** can translate texts (and can identify Kharman's coins as Nidlandish with 99% certainty, though he can't read the script around their rims). Relevant excerpts the PCs can find:
- *"Prophecies and Premonitions" by Andres Halffot* — the full text of Vicotnik's prophecy.
- *"Akrogal, a Different Continent" by Cator Fidul* — establishes the 368-day Akrogalian year. (GM note: only ~143 years have passed in Nidland to Ereb's 144; the Cleansing is very possibly imminent.)
- *"My Life as a Dwarf" by Findar Tekel* — an eyewitness account of **Helvetesfortet** seen from the highlands during a dwarven patrol into Nidland: a gigantic black fortress in a valley filled with screams, legions of black-clad soldiers, animals trained as cruelly as men, walls crowned with dead animal and human bodies; described by his dwarven guide as "an evil dwelling, Hell's forecourt, where the Dark Magic rules." (Hints where to strike.)

### Gubben Otho (Old Man Otho), Bedorum
Lives in house 32, southern part of town; nearly 60, white-haired, hunched, strongly senile, constantly shaking; cared for by his 17-year-old granddaughter. He once saw the Great Wall from the inside in his youth, alongside "old Ehrman Wall," but can't remember where; advises asking Ehrman. (A useful device for the GM to feed the PCs missed clues.)

### Ehrman Wall's Valley (Ehrman Walls dal)
A small valley on the Hynsolgian highland, ~2.5 days from Bedorum, holding a turf-roofed timber cabin. Map locations:
- **A — The Cabin:** two rooms. The large room has a table, chairs, fireplace, cookware shelf; trophies on the walls (a bear's head, an orcish kroksabel, and a **mithril face-mask** souvenir from the dwarves); animal-fur rugs. The small room is Ehrman's bedroom (bed, small table, wax candle in a wooden holder). A loose floor-section (50×50 cm) hides a chest containing **Ehrman's savings: 105 km, 64 sm, 21 gm, plus three semiprecious stones worth 30 sm each, and three mithril ingots (200 g each, worth 200 sm each)**. The chest is padlocked (−10 to pick).
- **B — Outhouse (avträde).**
- **C — Root cellar (jordkällare).**
- **D — A grove** where Ehrman stretches and tans hides.

### Tarat-khôn — North Gate (Ghan-meth)
Detailed earlier. Map rooms (page 42): **(1) Entré** — 3 m-high hall; statues of dwarf-warriors with two-handed axes line the walls; a 50 cm-deep niche with an inscription; a hidden door (−10) at "1a" leads to corridor 2b. **(2a-b) Corridors** with eight niches; levers in each niche's right wall open firing-slits onto the entry hall (or, in the innermost niche, drop the statue to reveal a staircase). **(3) The Stairs** — a ~70 m sloping gangway that branches; chains with handles hang at both doors (ring a bell within). **(4) The Well** — 140 m deep, ice-cold pure water; dwarves practice fighting in the surrounding hall. **(5) The Prison** — five cells with grated doors; currently no prisoners (or, if PCs are aggressive, they're placed here with two guards). **(6) The Great Hall** — vaulted, black-and-red floor, a throne on a podium; the audience hall where PCs meet Gidmir. **(7) Residences** — fifteen four-man rooms (currently 14 dwarves; personal effects in locked chests, SG 10). **(8a-c) Bodyguard's quarters** — for Gidmir's 4 personal guards; ceremonial gear including **silver beard-nets**, a storeroom (locked, −15) with halberds, gold/silver candelabra, Akrogalian camel-hair rugs. **(9a-e) Commandant's rooms** — office with desk and three chests, bedroom with broad bed, small storeroom, private water-closet. **(10) Workshop** — forge, anvil, work-tables; broken items repaired here. **(11) Storeroom** — chairs, tools, rope, planks, two crossbows on stands. **(12) Kitchen.** **(13) Food store** — clay jars of beer and grain, salted meat, dried fruit, cheeses. **(14) The Inner Gate** — copper-ornamented steel double-doors leading deeper into Tarat-khôn; opened by a **two-decimeter mithril key** (hidden under the floor plates, −15) **plus a secret password** known only to Gidmir; resists ÖPPNA and JORDVÄG. **The PCs cannot get through here.**

### Ghan-toth — The Abandoned Dwarf-City (now a svartfolk colony)
Once Tarat-khôn's third-largest, blossoming city; abandoned during the great emigration to Nidabergen, its entrances sealed, mined with traps, and magically hidden by the dwarves. After the dwarves cut off the world in 609, Vicotnik (deprived of ore) helped the orc-leader **Gartach** subdue a small tribe of orcs, dark elves, and svartnissar; **Achatoth broke the dwarven magic** holding the gates, and when the svartfolk searched the city they sprang many traps, burying half of it (and many svartfolk). The orcs and giants took the city as their dwelling; the dark elves were relegated to the mines.

**Special rule:** Grynnerbergen's mineral-rich rock disrupts magic (as above) — spells one grade lower (min 1), +1 PSY, double misfire risk +2 on the table, JORDVÄG useless.

#### Ghan-toth's Inhabitants
- **The Orcs:** 790 adult males, 470 females, 275 young. Led by **Gartach**, with **twelve underchieftains** each leading a sub-tribe of ~150. One underchieftain, **Yrttugh**, is Gartach's deputy and leads the **Blue Sword Guard**. Females mind young and craft (sewing, weaving, ceramics).
- **The Blue Sword Guard (Blå Svärdets Garde):** 30 of the most experienced, powerful, intelligent warriors, hand-picked by Gartach and Yrttugh, sworn to personal loyalty (willing to kill their own kin on command); always combat-ready, used only in emergencies or special tasks. Named for Gartach's sword (see below) and bearing its symbol on **crimson coats over black shields**; **shaved heads**, and before battle they rinse their mouths with ink from the cave-cuttlefish to dye their teeth **coal-black**. Armed with kroksablar and short bows (fired even from wolf-back). Six **resar (giants)** currently serve in the Guard, led by Yrttugh.
- **The Partjertopp Clan:** 220 men and women; smaller and weaker than the others but the most skilled crafters, so exempted from mine-work — they forge weapons in the old dwarven smithies up in the city, near the council-square, and help keep order. Clan-mark: **three white eagle-claws joined at the "leg."** Underchieftain: **Fraslak.**
- **The Haskushdal Orcs:** 175, the smallest and most agile; ill-suited to heavy mine-work, so they serve as **guards, patrols, and hunters**. #### Ghan-toth's Mines (Gruvor)
Pitch-black; orcs and dark elves carry torches. Constant distant hammering from the smithy. **The Smithy (must be passed to reach the mines)** and **Ma'tho Fherengi's vault** are the obligatory features.

**The Smithy (Smedjan):** constant work; ≥30 svartfolk; the Control Patrol passes 3–4×/day. **(1) The Blast Furnace (Masugnen)** — a smoking apparatus (recognizable to a dwarf/smith as a masugn, built atop the dwarves' own furnace which was buried in the cave-in); the iron rails for ore-wagons run from here; the ringing steel comes from room 2. **(2) The Smithy proper** — deafening clang, thick red-tinged smoke from three furnaces; eight orc smiths work around the clock forging Ghan-toth's ore into weapons/armor for themselves and Vicotnik; so absorbed they won't notice PCs sneaking through. **(3) Storeroom** — racks for iron-ingots; currently **117 iron-ingots (järntackor).** **(4) Cave-in (Ras)** — a huge collapse blocking this part; two wagon-tracks vanish under it (this route once led up to the destroyed city quarter and out to the Great Wall gate; now one must go via the mines); climbing the ras risks 2T10 damage on a failed roll.

**The Mine (Gruvan):** a vast warren of passages; some have wagon-tracks, often blocked by collapses; benches here and there for rest. After long abandonment, **three kinds of mushroom** grow: **Belrondsvamp** (grey-blue, lightly spiked foot — usable as a drug), **Gruvsopp** (the common one, ~10 cm with a big brown cap — very good cooked, raw makes you queasy), **Tafrisvamp** (all-black cap and foot, like a champignon — exquisite but **dyes the tongue black**). PCs with **Grottorientering (Cave-orientation)** add their highest skill-margin to the roll; the svartnisse Bartolom or a captured orc can also help.

If they rescue the svartnisse, he thanks them in broken Hynsolgian, introduces himself as **Bartolom**, and begs to follow "the mighty warriors" on their adventure.

## Key Items & Weapons

### Demonspiran / Portens Väktare ("The Demon Sceptre / Guardian of the Gate")
**Backstory:** Long before humans, elves and dwarves saw the light of day, there lived a people who much resembled today's humans, in close relation to their gods. The realm's mightiest magician was struck by **hubris**, came to believe himself a god, and tried to reach the gods' dwelling. As punishment the gods sent the **Bestraffaren ("the Punisher")** to destroy him — but the magician was prepared: he **ensnared the Punisher within one of the Twin-Sceptres (Tvillingstavarna)** and cursed it, so that the Punisher would remain imprisoned, bound to perform a **single (1) command** for the realm's ruler when called, and only then be freed.

The sceptre passed down the ruling line; when the last of that line died, it was buried with him (Ma'tho Fherengi). The Demonspiran is a **50 cm staff of unknown material**, covered in mighty troll-runes of a long-forgotten kind, plus a readable inscription the PCs can decipher as **"Guardian of the Gate."** A being especially close to a "good" god (e.g. a messenger of Mother Earth) may sense that it radiates evil.

**In play:** Over its long captivity the Punisher has weakened its prison enough that only a touch is needed to begin its release. When someone **touches the Demonspiran**, it slowly transforms (over **20 SR**) into a faintly human-like, ~**190 cm grey-black creature with burning eyes** and an unpleasant staring gaze, which announces: *"I am the Guardian of the Gate. Tell me when all hope is out and you need His help,"* then reverts to sceptre-form. Later, when in dire need, it **telepathically offers help to the PC who first touched it**; if accepted, it **doubles in size, helps the PCs solve their problem**, and tells them to flee as far as possible — for **after 10 minutes it breaks free entirely** and assumes its **true form** in a colossal explosion **annihilating everything within 300 m**. From the blast rises a **25 m coal-black demon with great buck-horns and a reptilian tail** — the true Bestraffaren — which thanks the PCs telepathically and departs into Nidland to aid Vicotnik (continuing into Part 2).

**Limitations:** It **cannot be given an explicit, specific order** (e.g. "Kill Vicotnik!" or "help us now!" won't work). **GM guidance:** the sceptre is the scripted escape device, so the GM should **prevent premature use** — e.g. by having an inner voice say "all hope is not yet out" and otherwise blocking the PCs if they try to use it too early or for the wrong purpose.

### Fherengi Fanor
A magic **broadsword (slagsvärd)** that passed down the Fherengi line. When Ma'tho died young and heirless, he was buried with it, as it was deemed bearable only by a **true Fherengi**. A superbly forged bronze blade (**BV 17, damage 1T10+2**), the blade rune-covered in a long-lost tongue, the hilt crowned with a **bird-of-prey head in gold**. Forged by the first Fherengi's court-magician on his own initiative, intended so that **all who ruled after him would rule exactly as he had** — so it was magically imbued with his own way of **thinking and acting**. It is enchanted with **FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3 plus NEXUS and PERMANENS**.

**The Curse:** A PC who begins wielding Fherengi Fanor grows, **week by week, increasingly arrogant, estranged from the world, and self-absorbed**, coming to consider themselves superior to others (best simulated by having the player roll up a new PC while the GM takes over the old one). The wielder also becomes **morbidly attached** to the sword. The process can be **stopped with a Hard PSY roll, made during the first week**. If the wielder later loses the sword, they must **search until they find it again**, losing **1 PSY per week** in the meantime; **if PSY reaches zero, the character dies**. Lost PSY is regained at a rate of 1 per week once the sword is refound.
