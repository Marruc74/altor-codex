# *Rösten från Forntiden* ("The Voice from the Ancient Times")

A standalone adventure module for **Expert Drakar och Demoner**, by **Torsten Alm & Lars Hellström** (cover by Alan Craddock; interior art by Nils Gulliksson & Stefan Thulin), published 1986 by TAMB Äventyrsspel HB, Stockholm. Unlike *Oraklets fyra ögon*, it is deliberately **not tied to any particular game world** — the GM can drop it into any campaign. It is built for 4–6 experienced characters and assumes at least one magician in the group; it requires the Expert rules and the Monster Book to run. The original text is in Swedish; this is a full English-language summary.

---

## Background

About 400 years ago the land had no king, and powerful families schemed for the throne. The two strongest were the **de Klochard** and **Zeen** families. The de Klochards' pretender, **sir Valland de Klochard**, was close to winning popular support. The Zeen patriarch, **Aldorg Zeen**, decided to murder him and take the crown himself. Suspecting this, Valland stole the royal regalia — crown, scepter, and crown-gold — and hid them, then set off to meet his father sir Havellan de Klochard.

He never arrived. Aldorg's men ambushed and killed him, burying the body in a forest glade. Enraged that the regalia were now lost, Aldorg used a **magic curse-staff** (crafted by a mighty necromancer) to doom Valland's soul to wander the earth as a wraith until the end of time, then broke the staff into three pieces.

For centuries Valland's wraith existence was tolerable — he watched Aldorg fail as king, forced to raise taxes to replace the lost regalia (a new crown, scepter, and crown-gold), saw kings come and go, and idly frightened off anyone who tried to use his burial ground while he spent most of his 400-year life philosophizing.

Now, 400+ years later, the gentlemanly ghost is weary and wants eternal rest. He has worked out that **reassembling the three staff-pieces at the exact spot where he died** will break the curse — but as a ghost bound to his grave-site, he cannot fetch them himself.

---

## The premise / opening scene

The characters are out adventuring and make camp in a grassy forest glade. After supper, a shimmering figure — a fair-haired man in light-blue robes, about thirty — appears at the tree line. He introduces himself as **sir Valland de Klochard's wraith**, recounts his story, and reveals that the glade where they're sitting is the exact place he was killed. He offers them the hidden **king's treasure** in exchange for retrieving the three staff-pieces, which are scattered across three locations:

- **The Kings' Grave-Temple** — a walled complex 3–4 days' ride *east* of the city Faltrax.
- **Castle El Nhassiv** — built by Aldorg's son Brotvig, now orc-held, 5–6 days *northwest* of Faltrax.
- **A castle on Falkön** — built by Aldorg's eldest son Haftenghast and still inhabited by his Zeen descendants, a week's sail out to sea.

To pinpoint the exact locations, a character must succeed at both **Historia** and **Geografi**; failing that, they must pay a learned man for the directions. The three sub-adventures can be tackled in any order. Once all three pieces are gathered and joined at the glade, the wraith is freed in a thunderous storm, leaving behind an **indestructible staff** (it absorbed all the magic that bound him) as a memento.

## The three sub-adventures

### 1. Castle El Nhassiv (the orc-held castle)

The party hires a guide for the journey through a war-ravaged former forest, now patrolled by warring **orcs** and **elves/dark elves**. Random encounters include a dark-elf patrol (who attack on sight if a party member is an elf), forest-elf challengers wary of anyone heading to El Nhassiv, a curious brown bear, a glade strewn with fresh elf/dark-elf corpses, and a scorpion in the camp.

The castle sits on a hill at the edge of a mountain massif, in barren, desolate country. It was taken from the human Zeen family ~100 years ago and now houses **three orcs and 21 dark elves** under the orc leader **Orta**, who rules with an iron hand because losing the castle means a fate worse than death. The orcs don't realize a valuable staff-piece is hidden somewhere inside.

**The orcs' plan:** On the party's first arrival most of the garrison is away raiding; only **Lorrf** (Orta's deputy) and ten dark elves remain. If the guards spot the party before they get inside, they raise the alarm and defend with missile weapons; if the party gets in undetected, Lorrf sets an ambush in a room or corridor (dark elves attack first; Lorrf hangs back and joins only if it's going well). If the party is clearly winning, Lorrf flees. While the party searches, the **raiding party returns** (Orta, Bagmol, and dark elves 11–21). Realizing something is wrong, and being poor tacticians, the orcs launch a stereotyped **night assault**: two ladders (fetched from a nearby village) are raised against the west wall, dark elves storm up first, orcs follow, and they try to kill everything in their path — never fleeing.

**The 30-room castle** is a vandalized ruin. Highlights:
- Defaced family gallery (room 11), a "day room" where orcs smash things to vent (12), a toppled-altar chapel (14), and a library of fiction and travelogues (13).
- Living quarters with minor loot: a poisoned carafe of orc-brew (5), Lorrf's room with a chest containing a HELA E2 flask (16), Bagmol's room with his club (17), and **Orta's room (19)** with a poison-needle-trapped treasure sack (143 sm, 13 gm, an 875-sm ruby, and a HELA E3 flask).
- A dungeon (26) with five starved family skeletons, a treasure cave (**23**) holding stolen wealth (276 sm, 46 gm, three 500-sm opals, 300 sm of silverware) guarded by wall-launched knives, and two watchtowers (29, 30).
- **The staff-piece is in room 27**, an untouched throne-room: lifting the cushion of the throne-like chair reveals a secret compartment holding a cloth roll with the staff-piece inside.

### 2. The castle on Falkön

A seven-day voyage with random encounters: thick fog (penalizing the captain's Navigation), a swarm of flying **forgyor** attacking the deck, the **pirate ambush** at sea, a storm forcing a 12-hour delay, a drunk captain, and a floating bottle-message from a prisoner ("I, Belgandar Seline, am a prisoner on Falkön... free me, help me overthrow Fulrach").

The island holds a castle run by the magician brothers **Fulrach** and **Chameeth Zeen**, who hijack ships and brainwash or experiment on their crews. On approach the party may spot a tower with smoke, beached boats, a tormented scream, the sunken lifeboat "Jungfru Maheer," a large green sea-monster, and human skeletons on the seabed. Island encounters: a harmless goat flock (the magicians' livestock), the magicians' graveyard guarded by three fresh zombies, and a gathering storm.

The two magicians roam the castle (the GM rolls each session for each one's location). Key rooms:
- A **hall** with a guard who can summon the magicians; the kitchen with a loyal, dangerous **cook**; an old library used as guards' quarters; a **dining room** whose black candles inflict Angel-of-Death nightmares; two watchtowers with horn-blowing guards.
- A **laboratory** of 23 mystery flasks (mostly poison; possible HELA E3 / ANTIMAGI E2 / HÖRSEL E4 / FLYGA E4) and the **magicians' library** with study-boosting tomes.
- A **torture chamber (13)** with the torturer **Alghaan Gharhd** and his magic axe.
- The **treasure chamber (14)** — door marked "Entry Forbidden," rigged with a Fear power-symbol, lock SG 50 — holding rubies, a 2,000-sm necklace, and a black-wood casket containing the **staff-piece**.
- The dungeon cells (15a–e): the surviving tortured sailor **Karish Nerek**, a **blue-plague** corpse (a deadly contagion if looted), a hanged prisoner, and two recently dead men.
- A magic-sealed **tower top (18)**, reachable only by an incense-and-pentagram teleport, where the captured magician **Belgandar Seline** is held (an ally if freed — but rescuers risk becoming trapped/starved if they brought no spare incense).

### 3. The Kings' Grave-Temple

A walled necropolis (4-meter stone wall topped with razor-sharp metal spikes, entered via a single rusty iron grate) 4 days east of Faltrax. Encounters en route: a too-good-to-be-true fast horse for sale, a pack of hungry hyenas, a thunderclap that spooks the horses, a blessing-granting begging monk (±4 on the next important roll), and a lamed horse.

Inside are **three grave-temples** — the oldest, the half-old, and the new — each a gold-adorned building of bronze-coffin'd royal statues with grave-curse inscriptions. Disturbing certain chests is dangerous: chest C of the oldest temple ("Kung Fizzbraun IV") unleashes a roaring **ghost**, and chest F of the half-old temple ("Kung Contreric, Befriaren") releases a sickening poison gas.

**The staff-piece is in the "new" grave-temple, chest H**, which belongs to **King Aldorg Zeen himself** — the very man who cursed Valland. His remarkably preserved (explicitly non-vampiric) mummy lies with **a wooden staff driven through its heart**, with no trace of blood: that staff is the third piece.

## Major characters

**Sir Valland de Klochard (ghost)** — the courteous gentleman-wraith and quest-giver; SMI 26, PSY 32, INT 16, STO 12. His only "weapon" is a fear attack. Played with humor and self-irony; his life-goals were Chivalry and Law & Order.

**Orta** — beloved-but-hard orc leader of El Nhassiv, 24, 192 cm, 100+ kg, wielding a great double-axe (STY 22, KP 15). Sends his dark elves and other orcs in first while he watches from a distance, joining the fight only if things go badly.

**Lorrf** — Orta's deputy of ~3 years, runs the castle when Orta raids; uses a kroksabel and light crossbow (STY 19, SMI 15, KP 12); flees if the fight turns against him.

**Bagmol** — youngest orc (18), small (157 cm, 56 kg) but clever (INT 16); compensates with quick thinking. Keeps a great wooden club in room 17.

**Dark elves (svartalfer) 1–21** — the El Nhassiv garrison, armed with shortswords, throwing spears, and small bows.

**Argol Thengel** — a worn-out former court jester (60+, white hair, big blue eyes) met on the road home; a talkative information source who hints at the "haunted" Grave-Temple. High social/performance skills (Sjunga & Spela 20, Överklasstil 20, Upptäcka Fara 20).

**Fulrach Zeen** — master of Falkön; 58, tall, thin, black-haired, coal-black squinty eyes, obsessed with seizing the throne via a coup using brainwashed soldiers and undead. A powerful necromancer/mentalist (PSY 29; Nekromanti 19, Mentalism 17) who always carries a large spellbook. Does not know the staff-piece is in his own vault.

**Chameeth Zeen** — Fulrach's younger brother, treated like a kid by him; a fairly skilled magician (PSY 27, INT 18; Symbolism 19, illusion/control-type spells), carries his own spellbook.

**Alghaan Gharhd** — Falkön's torturer; over 2 m, bear-strong but dim (INT 6); sickly in love with his torture tools. Won't attack unless threatened. Carries a magic **executioner's axe**.

**Belgandar Seline** — kindly old captured magician (PSY 26, INT 17) imprisoned in Falkön's sealed tower-top; an ally if freed. Has no staff, so memorizes no spells; his lost spellbook sits in the castle library.

**Karish Nerek** — emaciated, tortured sailor in a Falkön cell, the only one of his shipwrecked crew to resist Fulrach's magic; his KAR and KP are reduced from his ordeal.

**Londuine Camaar** — beautiful young wife of the harbor's warehouse manager, secretly the pirate Migroun's lover and fence for stolen goods; runs the inn "Vågtoppen" (KAR 18, high Bluff/Övertala).

**Borune Camaar** — the harbor's skilled, overweight warehouse manager, oblivious to his wife's affair (high Administration, Köpslå, Värdesätta).

**Piratkapten Migroun** — 33, careful and "fairly smart" pirate leader; motto "better to flee than fight badly." Wields a **magic shortsword** (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E2 + PERMANENS).

**The pirate crew (skurkarna)** — Alron, Mernaz, Svatz, Bridd, Garth; only 2–3 join a land robbery, all join a sea attack.

**The hired captain & sailors** — a reliable, experienced sea-dog (one of several named candidates, each with a reaction modifier) plus 3–6 loyal sailors who follow the highest-KAR character if the captain is disabled.

## Notable places

**Faltrax harbor** — the launch point for Falkön, with a detailed map of inns, warehouses, and homes: the pricey "Det Gyllene Ankaret," the dive "Sjöbjörnen," the full inn "Trossen," and the pirate-linked "Vågtoppen," plus the warehouse, the manager's office and home, captains' residences, and sailors' quarters. Migroun's pirate gang preys on careless, visibly-rich parties here (chances of a night robbery scale with how rich the party seems).

**Castle El Nhassiv** — the ruined orc-held border castle (30 rooms across inner/outer courtyards, living quarters, chapel, library, dungeon, treasure cave, secret stairs, and two watchtowers).

**The castle on Falkön** — Fulrach's island fortress (hall, kitchen, two libraries, laboratory, torture chamber, magic-sealed tower-top prison, treasure vault, and dungeon cells), with a partly-collapsed tower from a botched demolition spell.

**The Kings' Grave-Temple** — a spiked-wall necropolis of three royal mausoleums full of statued bronze coffins and grave-curses.

## Notable items, weapons, and treasures

**The three staff-pieces** — the broken curse-staff; reassembled at the death-glade, they free Valland and leave behind an indestructible staff.

**The king's treasure (the reward)** — buried under a great oak 613 double-paces east of Faltrax's town gate: a gem-set gold crown, a gem-set gold scepter, and 6,425 gm. The crown and scepter are royal property — sell them on the black market (~8,000 gm crown, ~4,500 gm scepter) at great legal risk, or return them to the king for 6,000 gm and a possible knighthood.

**Magic weapons:**
- Migroun's **shortsword** — FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E2 + PERMANENS.
- **Silver dagger** in chest C of the oldest Grave-Temple — FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E4, PERMANENS, no NEXUS.
- **Broadsword** in chest B of the half-old Grave-Temple — FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E8, PERMANENS, no NEXUS (drains 8 PSY per use; disastrous for low-PSY characters).
- Alghaan's **executioner's axe (Bödelsyxa)** — FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3, no NEXUS/PERMANENS.

**Magic potions** — HELA E2 (Lorrf's chest, El Nhassiv), HELA E3 (Orta's hoard; plus a possible one from Falkön's lab), and Falkön's lab roster (HELA E3, ANTIMAGI E2, HÖRSEL E4, FLYGA E4).

**Books (Falkön's magic library)** — study tomes that grant skill bonuses: *Det magiska pentagrammet* (+2 Kunskap om Magi), *Häxkonst* (+1), *Livsgnistan* (+1), *Läkekunskap & Droger* (+3 Läkekunskap, +2 Första Hjälpen, +3 Drogkunskap), and *Voodooritualen* (+3 to the Voodooritual spell for trained necromancers). Plus fiction (*Tio små Svartalfer*, *Tolls fördömda män*) and **Belgandar Seline's spellbook**.

**Other treasures** — gemstones and coin caches in the orc treasure cave and Orta's poison-needle-trapped hoard (El Nhassiv); rubies, a 2,000-sm necklace, and the staff-casket in Fulrach's vault; a 1,465-sm necklace (chest D, half-old temple) and topazes in the snake nest of the Grave-Temple; and the pirates' hidden opals and gold at sea.

---

## New creature

**Forgyor** — deadly silver-scaled, bright-blue-winged predatory deep-sea fish that usually live alone in the deep but gather to hunt, using enlarged belly-fins as wings (fast but poor maneuverability). STY ~7, Bett 1T6, 3-point scale armor, flight movement F24/S20. They swarm the party's ship during the voyage to Falkön.

---

## The ending

After reassembling the staff at the glade, two thunderclaps sound, lightning strikes from the wraith into both ends of the staff, a whirlwind rises, and a final bolt from the sky destroys the ghost — leaving silence and an **icy, indestructible staff**. The party then digs up the buried regalia (gem-set gold crown, gold scepter, and 6,425 gm). The back cover frames the whole adventure as the ghost's plea: ***"Låt mig dö!"*** ("Let me die!").
