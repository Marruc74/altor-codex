## 2. Premise & Series Recap

The campaign is set in the waters **west of Ereb Altor**. The PCs follow the fugitive cult-leader **Luciano** through a black-magic portal and end up in **Inferno** (Hell), where the whole hidden plot of the series finally comes together.

**Part 1 — *Handelsfursten* ("The Merchant Prince"), island of Pandarea**
- PCs hired by trading house **DiMercio** to infiltrate rival house **Machiavellio**.
- DiMercio massacred the Machiavellio family and framed the PCs.
- Survivor **Lucretia Machiavellio** used the **Sword of Truth (Sanningens svärd)** to learn the truth, then turned the PCs against DiMercio.
- The trail led to the volcanic island **Vulkanön** and the assassin clan **Brödraskapet** ("the Brotherhood").

**Part 2 — *Tempelherren* ("The Temple Lord"), islands of Agriea & Materé**
- A dark cult had infiltrated the priesthood of the god **Iaos**.
- Cult/temple lord **Luciano Lupino della Peredio** and the undead dragon **Arraquwâin**.
- PCs slew the dragon in its cave; **Luciano fled through a black-magic portal**.

**Part 3 — *Demonprinsen* begins**
- PCs leap through the portal after Luciano, into Inferno.
- They carry the **Sword of Truth** and serve **"Den glömda guden"** ("The Forgotten God").
- **If the group never played Parts 1–2:** the priesthood of Iaos hires or pressures them through the portal instead.

## 3. The Big Picture (GM eyes only)

Two demon-princes — **Tårg** (Circle of Shadows) and **Malkûth** (Circle of Chaos) — have warred for eons using mortal agents and proxies in Altor. **The PCs have been Tårg's unwitting pawns the entire series.**

- *Handelsfursten*: the trading houses were the princes' tools (Brotherhood/pirates = Malkûth's side).
- *Tempelherren*: Malkûth secretly ran the death-cult through his servant **Luciano**, a demon of the chaotic **acconater** race who only pretended to obey the dragon.
- *Demonprinsen*: Tårg manipulates the PCs into **killing Malkûth**, so Tårg can seize the Circle of Chaos and install his heir **Tårgkrizt** as a puppet ruler.

The PCs return to Altor "older and hopefully wiser."

## 4. Setting: Inferno

Inferno is the infinite plane of the damned: **nine circles** connected by **Avgrunden** ("the Abyss"), a vast cleft of fire and ice. A recurring framing device quotes an eyewitness manuscript by the sinner-poet **Tando Galiheri** (year 285).

- **Supreme ruler:** Prince **Shaitan**, in a black Citadel in the 9th (inner) circle.
- His soul is said to be bound inside **"Domens stenstod"** (the Court's black stone pillar) at the bottom of the Abyss — destroy it and Inferno is annihilated.
- **Nine judges** (corruptible, especially by Belfegor) judge incoming souls.

### The Nine Circles (top → bottom)

| # | Circle | Character | Prince |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Limbo** | Grey shadow border-world | Nahema |
| 2 | **Köldens krets** (Cold) | Icy wastes | Boral |
| 3 | **Vilddjurets krets** (Beast) | Steaming jungles, monsters | Lilith (princess) |
| 4 | **Skuggornas krets** (Shadows) | Grey dust desert, 2 black suns, 35–50 °C | **Tårg** ★ |
| 5 | **Farsoternas krets** (Plagues) | Disease marsh | Belfegor |
| 6 | **Krigens krets** (Wars) | Endless armies, none may die | Asmodeus (the War-Lord) |
| 7 | **Smärtornas krets** (Pains) | Torture machines | Astarot |
| 8 | **Kaos krets** (Chaos) | Formless flux, no laws, the Vortex | **Malkûth** ★ |
| 9 | **Den inre kretsen** (Inner) | Shaitan; sub-realms Caina, Antenora, Ptolemea, Giudecca | Shaitan |

★ = the circles central to this adventure.

### Magic Rules in Inferno

- **Demon magic** does not work for PCs.
- **Animism:** half strength / half range; costs **2 PSY per effect grade**.
- **Necromancy:** double power, half PSY cost, double range/time.
- (Old-rules note) **Hela** half effect; **Mörker** double effect.
- **Near the Vortex (Chaos):** double PSY-point cost; elemental magic double effect; illusions go wild and may become real; **all failed spells misfire (snedtändning)**; *Känna magi* fails entirely.
- **Corruption:** Inferno pushes PCs toward evil — the GM may rule that jokingly-stated evil acts actually happen. **The Sword-of-Truth bearer is immune to fear and corruption.**

### Demons (general)

- Hermaphroditic; change form and grow with power.
- Can breed across races and with humans.
- Abandon their young.

## 5. The Three Regions Visited

### Avgrunden — The Abyss (not a circle)
- A cleft in the iron mountains (**Järnbergen**), ruled by Prince **Antraxhus** from the citadel **Aiunus** (just beneath Limbo) plus 7 sub-citadels.
- Climate ~35 °C, sulfurous.
- **Smärtornas bro** ("Bridge of Pains") = a km-long bridge of bone and steel over the sea of fire; **death-scarabs (dödskeruber)** escort souls downward.
- **People:** the **baahl** — a war/plunder race: rainbow-shimmering skin, black eyes, double-jointed limbs, grow with power; live in *khraal* forts in clans, no families.
- **Intrigue:** Overcommander **Behmoth** conspires with Astarot against Antraxhus.

### Skuggornas krets — Circle of Shadows
- City of **Mirâsch** = km-high four-sided black monoliths (*gomrath*); Prince Tårg's palace is the central cube.
- **Inhabitants:** **pazuzu** (fly-masters), **nagan** (snake-demons), **gruzer** (tall ash-colored, melancholy servant race).
- Entire culture is death-obsessed and melancholic; a ritual **wind-death** in sand-shafts produces holy bones.
- **Politics:** Tårg is an upstart who overthrew the former prince **Baalcepher** a few hundred years ago and broke away from Asmodeus; rival **Exxatha** is gathering troops.

### Kaos krets — Circle of Chaos
- No fixed geography — everything flows.
- **The Vortex:** a red-light maelstrom at the center, the **source of all magic**; induces insanity.
- Prince **Malkûth** (legend: a mortal mage who tamed the Vortex) rules from the iron-satellite palace **Xathanu**; a refined culture of assassination and intrigue.
- **Inhabitants:** **belmôner** (pale, half-transparent skin, white eyes, amoeba-like) who ride **krazzt** (giant praying-mantis mounts).
- **Plot:** Malkûth is plotting to expand into Tårg's Shadow-planet — the PCs will uncover this.

## 6. Episode-by-Episode Walkthrough

### Ep. 1 — Ned i underjorden ("Down into the Underworld")
- PCs jump through the portal in Arraquwâin's cave → fall into Avgrunden.
- They land on an icy edge near citadel Aiunus. **PSY < ½ roll or break down in dread** (Sword-bearer immune).
- A vast **baahl army** marches past toward Aiunus (ignore the PCs unless provoked). Near the cleft it grows hot — **metal armor deals 1T4 damage/round**.
- PCs follow the army to Aiunus (a 3-day march).

### Ep. 2 — Aiunus
- Labyrinthine bone-and-basalt citadel of Antraxhus; the magic walls are fed by the suffering of walled-in workers.
- No inns, no open trade. Statues of Antraxhus everywhere.
- Baahl speak "baahl-ish," but most also know an Erebish tongue.
- PCs must hide valuables and act subservient.

### Ep. 3 — En oväntad inbjudan ("An Unexpected Invitation")
- A noble demon's retinue drags the PCs to a party "Till Orchos" (speaks broken *västjori*).
- Venue: 10 large + 100 small halls; drinks that knock you flat.
- PCs meet a black-clad human, **Lord Videric**, with 4 bodyguards.
- Videric hates Luciano & Malkûth (ancient enemies). He offers info on **how to leave Inferno** in exchange for **killing Luciano**, and lends two bodyguards, **Leon & Beran**, who know Luciano's tower.

### Ep. 4 — Lucianos torn ("Luciano's Tower")
- Tower on the Abyss edge. Bluff your way in, or fight **20 guards** (5 on patrol / 10 in guardroom; fight to the death).
- **Luciano has already left** (gone to Malkûth).
- Videric returns, reveals that Prince **Tårg** (Shadow circle) is Luciano's enemy, and offers to **teleport the PCs to the Shadow circle** in exchange for the tower as payment.
- He casts the spell: ash circle + soot mark on each forehead + incantation *"Om mane tarfas ufar sin, afar dasin, tar fas, in."*

**Tower layout** (see §9 for the map):

- **Ground floor:** 1 Entrance (bleeding metal stair, 2 winged-demon statues, copper door) · 2 Hall (paintings that reach out) · 3 Stairwell · 4 Guardroom (10 guards) · 5 Servants' room · 6 Stairwell down.
- **Cellar:** 7 Well · 8–10 Storage (incl. salted dwarf-heads).
- **1st floor:** 11 Corridor · 12 Drawing room (luxury) · 13 Five guest-rooms.
- **2nd floor:** 15 Stairwell · 16 Banquet hall (gold floor, silver mirror-walls, sapphire pillars, silver throne-block w/ gold throne).
- **3rd floor:** 17 Stairwell · 18 Valet's room (demon valet **MmmnRizkt**) · 19 Armory (locked cabinet = Luciano's demon-weapon & demon-armor) · 20 Library · 21 Study · 22 Bedchamber.
- **4th floor:** 23 Stairwell · 24 Torture chamber · 25 Thirteen prison cells (~40 chained slaves/demons/baahl).

### Ep. 5 — Skuggornas fångar ("Prisoners of the Shadows")
- PCs arrive in the grey dust desert; PSY rolls vs despair.
- They encounter **belmôner riding krazzt**.
- PCs rescue a small pazuzu demon from a gruzer-guarded ambush — he is **Tårgkrizt**, Prince Tårg's favorite child and heir.
- Malkûth's belmôner hunt them; run an exciting **chase** (the PCs must not get badly hurt).
- PCs reach **Mirâsch**, the monolith city.

### Ep. 6 — Moboliternas stad ("City of the Monoliths")
- Tårgkrizt cuts each PC's hand and adds his **black-green blood** as a recognition-mark to pass the cube's protective cuneiform spell.
- He leads them through the death-cult palace to meet **Prince Tårg** on his throne (a huge facet-eyed fly-demon).
- Tårg tells a misleading story and makes the PCs guests for 3–4 days.
- They meet **Korasch** (court necromancer, near-human, black-skinned, goat-horns, friendly, has traveled in Altor).
- **Tårg's briefing:** claims Malkûth (and Luciano of the "acconater" race) is trying to seize Altor and break the cosmic balance. He haggles for the PCs' souls (a bluff), drops to "20 years' service," and finally just **lends** them weapons & armor *"forged of my own blood — only a prince's blood can destroy a prince."*
  - **Only the best fighters get the gear** — none for priests, mages, or thieves.
- He assigns war-leader **Nimloth**.

### Ep. 7 — Planning + Mellan världarna ("Between the Worlds")
- Nimloth → strategist **Akaron** (apparent human, actually a damned soul recruited by Tårg).
- **The plan:** A direct portal would be detected by Malkûth. Instead, gradual **"phasing"** between adjacent spheres along Vortex power-lines, starting from Mirâsch's main shaft and descending **Plague → War → Pain → Chaos**.
- Take only the flying **Quetzal Legion** (2000 winged-lizard cavalry; no foot troops).
- **Journey:** disease swamp (Plague) → crucifixion-plain war (War, "Asmodeus' cursed hell") → torture-corridor (Pain; **SMI rolls or crash = 1T4**) → into Chaos. Magic-sensitive PCs black out from the Vortex but stay strapped into their saddles.

### Ep. 8 — Vortex
- Akaron opened the last portal too near the Vortex; the forces scatter and the PCs lose Nimloth & Akaron.
- They land in a floating, broken **ruin-city** near the Vortex; their demon-gear glows like a beacon.
- They meet **Mara**, a human art-thief from a parallel (Moorish / early-Islamic analogue) universe; she recognizes the prince-blood gear and offers to lead them to **Kirian** (Malkûth's commander, who secretly wants the throne).
- **Travel:** she shrinks her magic steel-bird; through "Achars/Arachs skog" (a shifting plant-labyrinth) and "Mandelbrots väg" (fractal grass land-bridges) to Xathanu's far side / Kirian's war-camp.
- **Kirian:** Tårg's quetzal legion (Nimloth) is already battling Malkûth's krazzt-riders. Kirian welcomes the PCs, then phases them via a secret hollow-way to a **spiral-sigil chamber** — a hidden back-entrance he built into Xathanu. PCs walk the spiral (**PSY roll, 1 min/attempt**) → teleport into Malkûth's throne hall.

### Ep. 9 — Malkûths sista strid ("Malkûth's Last Battle") — Climax
- **Layout (see §9):** 1a–1d advisors' chambers (PCs arrive in 1a, Kirian's) · 2 Corridors · 3 Balustrade overlooking the hall (4 stairs down, 3 m) · 4 Throne hall (transparent floor over the Vortex) · 5 Throne (pulsing green crystal on gold).
- **Present:** Malkûth (appears as a dark-haired, bearded man of ~40), Luciano Lupino, 3 black-cloaked advisors.
- Malkûth attacks the PCs alone (a formal challenge); Luciano joins after hesitation.
- **Killing Malkûth** (only prince-blood gear can) shatters the throne and darkens the Vortex.
- Then **Tårg** arrives with Kirian and an enlarged **Tårgkrizt**; Kirian kills the 3 advisors; **Tårgkrizt is enthroned as new ruler of Chaos.**
- Tårgkrizt thanks the PCs, magically dissolves the borrowed gear, and releases their service-bond.
- **Akaron** escorts the PCs back via a smoke-cloud to Arraquwâin's cave (the portal is now gone) and answers their questions, explaining the whole Tårg-vs-Malkûth game.

### Epilogue
- **Reward:** Arraquwâin's dragon-treasure, left in the cave.
- Honors and medals from the priesthood and from Lucretia Machiavellio.
- The series is concluded.

## 7. Magic Items

### Demon-weapon & Demon-armor rules
Demon-weapons and demon-armor are **semi-intelligent objects** that a demon has imbued with part of its soul. They have their own **STY / SMI / INT / PSY**.

- **Per 10 STY:** weapon **+1T6 damage** / armor **+3 absorb**.
- **Per 10 SMI:** weapon **+5% attack & parry** / armor **+5% parry** to the bearer.
- **Per 5 INT:** the object knows one spell to **E1**.
- The object casts using **its own PSY** (regains 1 PSY/hour), only if the bearer wishes.
- **Only the creator-demon can use the powers** (others are attacked by the item) — unless its INT > 50 **and** it gives explicit permission.

### Luciano's demon short-sword (Lucianos demonsvärd)
- Black-crystal short-sword, silver hilt.
- **STY 30 / SMI 20 / INT 50 / PSY 73.**
- **+3T6 damage, +10% hit & parry.**
- 10 effect-grades of spells: **Frost E4, Blixt E4, Teleportation E1, Mörker E1.**

### Luciano's demon-armor (Lucianos demonrustning)
- Black-crystal, jeweled full-armor.
- **STY 30 / SMI 10 / INT 40 / PSY 37.**
- Absorbs **9 more** than full-armor, **+5% parry.**
- 8 effect-grades: **Antimagi E4, Osynlighet E2, Eld E2.**

### Tårg's lent demon-weapon (Tårgs demonvapen)
- Type per player's choice (sword / axe / hammer / spear). Coarse black iron, cuneiform script.
- **STY 30 / SMI 30 / INT 60 / PSY 40.**
- **+3T6 damage, +15% attack & parry.**
- 12 effect-grades: **Frost E4, Motståndskraft E4, Skingra E4.**
- *(Tårg's personal weapons are 10× mightier than these loaners.)*

### Tårg's lent demon-armor (Tårgs demonrustningar)
- Black-iron ring-mail.
- **STY 20 / SMI 10 / INT 50 / PSY 50.**
- Absorbs **+6** over normal ring-mail, **+5% parry.**
- 10 effect-grades: **Eld E3, Motståndskraft E5, Mörker E2.**

## 10. Bestiary (Varelser)

*Generic stat blocks introduced by the module. Format as above; "Värde" = typical value of the listed die.*

### Baahl — Abyss war-race
- **8T6 (28) · 6T6 (21) · 8T6 (28) · 3T6 (11) · 2T6 (7) · 2T6+1 (8) · -1T6 (-4) · KP 25 · SB +1T6**
- **Natural weapons:** 2 claws 50% / 1T6; 1 bite 40% / 1T8. Hide 4.
- Vaguely humanlike but taller/stronger; rainbow-shimmering black skin, black eyes; claw-nails and predator fangs; **two joints per limb, knees bent backward.**
- **Personality:** life revolves around power and combat — gained through battle, assassination, and treachery.

### Belmôner — Chaos amoeba-folk
- **5T6 (18) · 6T6 (21) · 4T6 (14) · 3T6 (11) · 4T6 (14) · 3T6 (11) · 1T6 (4) · KP 18 · SB +1T4**
- **Demonic abilities:** Teleportera, Kroppsförändring.
- Resemble humans but with **half-transparent skin, white dead eyes, pastel-shimmering hair.** Born of the Vortex; closely related to chaos; bodies tend to lose structure and flow into amoeba-blobs.
- **Sexless; reproduce by division** — the original's identity is destroyed and two new belmôner are created; no family/kin ties.

## 13. Cosmology & Theology of Inferno

For GMs who want to play the setting straight, the module supplies a full metaphysical frame.

- **What Inferno is:** A separate plane bound to the mortal world, where damned souls suffer eternal punishment. Some faiths hold the torment is only *temporary* — that the souls will eventually be freed in a new age.
- **Scale & structure:** Infinite, divided into nine circles linked by the Abyss. Each circle contains countless **spheres**, some as vast as all of Altor, connected by maelstroms and portals. The Abyss threads through them all; no one understands how a single cleft can join infinite worlds — Inferno simply defies logical structure.
- **Origin debate:** Was Inferno created alongside Altor, or did it arise *afterward* as a consequence of human evil? Some even claim **Altor is merely a shadow of Inferno**. The dominant view (held by *Den Lysande Vägen*, "The Shining Way") is that Altor sits *between* Inferno and Paradis as their opposing counterpart, and that Inferno's supreme prince is ultimately subject to the god **Etin**.
- **Official ideology:** Shaitan is the primordial source of all power and evil; life-affirming gods like Etin were created only as opposing forces. Therefore **evil is "natural" and goodness is a false mask hiding selfish motives.** Might makes right — Shaitan rules by superior strength, and there are no protected hereditary dynasties.
- **The judges:** Nine supposedly incorruptible judges at the Court's pillar assign souls to their torments — yet they take bribes. **Belfegor** is the most skilled at acquiring tormented souls through corruption, and some souls vanish en route to the pillar and end up in other hands.
- **The framing device:** Recurring epigraph-quotes are drawn from a manuscript attributed to the sinner-poet **Tando Galiheri** (300s), said to have been smuggled out from the battlefield of Abydion. Use them as atmospheric chapter openers.

### How demons work (expanded)
- Most demons are grotesquely large, with tusks, claws, and natural weapons — **unless** they carry inherent magic to compensate (which tends to make them smaller and more human-like; cf. Kirian, Malkûth).
- All are hermaphroditic and can reproduce alone. Born small and vulnerable, they are abandoned early, grow explosively in their first weeks, and keep transforming as they gain power.
- Demons that fail to assert themselves never grow past "puppy size" and are often eaten in infancy.
- Sufficiently powerful demons spontaneously develop shape-shifting. **Shaitan** can take any form; his natural state is a vast formless mass of limbs, tusks, claws, and tentacles.
- Cross-breeding (between demon races, and with humans and other creatures) produces a bewildering variety of forms — varying numbers of limbs, eyes, and heads.

## 14. The Power-Game (Maktspelet), by Region

Inferno's politics are a perpetual war of all against all. Useful background for improvising NPC motives.

### Avgrunden / Antraxhus
- Antraxhus constantly raises armies to seize unguarded spheres, regarding the Abyss as the **origin of all Inferno** and therefore his by right.
- He keeps **at least 20 million warriors** under arms at all times; when there's no one to conquer, he sets them fighting each other.
- He **rents his legions** to other princes, and sells **passage rights** through the Abyss (the key route for attacking other circles) in exchange for military gain.
- Treaties with Astarot and Malkûth aimed at taking the Cold and Beast circles; lately his attention has shifted to **Asmodeus**, who keeps hurling waves of the damned against the Abyss.
- His overcommander **Behmoth** leads the armies but secretly conspires with **Astarot** to overthrow him.

### Skuggornas krets / Tårg
- Tårg is a **parvenu**: he rose from war-leader under Asmodeus only a few centuries ago after an unexpectedly successful campaign, then broke away to rule the Shadow circle. His authority is **not** secure.
- Demons are only ~**5%** of the Shadow population; the most common are **pazuzu** (fly-masters). **Gruzerna** make up roughly **a fifth** — tall, ash-colored, part-reptile/part-insect, the realm's labor force and servants.
- The defining trait is **melancholy about transience** rather than bloodlust. The damned are pitied rather than tormented for sport; pazuzu stand silently in the wind-storms watching prisoners haul stone.
- **Ritual wind-death:** in Tårg's palace, gruzer and pazuzu let themselves be killed by artificially channeled storm-winds in sand-shafts, leaving bleached bones used in the court death-cult.

### Kaos krets / Malkûth
- No fixed geography — stone blocks, creatures, water-spheres, and whole worlds flow together in an endless maelstrom; landscapes can shift from solid ground to bottomless chasm. The only stable landmark is the **Iron Mountains** in the northeast (split by the Abyss — the sole safe passage in and out).
- Movement requires supernatural means: teleportation, winged serpents and dragons, or the princes' own reality-warping.
- The **chaos-princes' palaces travel** within the maelstroms. They come from all over Inferno and other worlds, drawing those who chafe at the strength-worship of the other circles. Official Shaitan-worship has little hold here, and **new demon religions are emerging**.
- ~**One third** of Kaos' population are demons; the rest are mostly **belmôner**.
- **Malkûth's method:** despite millennia of rule and overwhelming magic, he governs by **refined court diplomacy**, not tyranny — imposing no rules and demanding no allegiance, letting nobles indulge in assassination and intrigue. He secretly harbors ambitions **beyond** Kaos (the Shadow-planet scheme the PCs uncover).

## 15. Scene-Running Notes & Atmosphere

Beats and sensory cues pulled from the text, to help run each set-piece.

- **Arrival in the Abyss:** ice-covered stone and cliffs; the Iron Mountains looming behind, a flaming chasm ahead (the gap from mountains to edge varies between ~½ and ~2 miles). Patches of ice burn with eerie blue fire; air swings between scorching and freezing.
- **The baahl army:** thousands in heavy black armor with cleaving-knives and axes, lizard-cavalry, tattered standards; it drags chained captives (small red demons, human warriors) and hundreds of severed heads on spears. If the PCs stay still, it largely ignores them — the odd acid-spit or half-hearted swipe. Near the cleft, the heat, steam, and ozone make hair stand on end; **metal armor deals 1T4/round.**
- **Aiunus streets:** a maze across hundreds of levels under gothic vaults; paved avenues, half-frozen-mud alleys; demons, baahl, beggars, thieves, enslaved humans; corpse-pits swarming with rats. Covered squares like dark halls, dominated by statues of the many-limbed, tusked, tentacled **Antraxhus**. No marketplace — only furtive deals in shadow. Some areas radiate such evil the PCs get heart palpitations and cold sweats. Baahl bodies visibly shift and they erupt into causeless fights.
- **The Orchos party:** gothic vaults, crystal chandeliers, tapestries, divans; small demons bearing bizarre food and colored drinks; a discordant minuet to off-key piping. The drinks knock PCs flat (the demons find this hilarious). Deeper halls grow wilder — half the guests passed out, demons hauling people into dark corners, bloody brawls, corpses floating in pools. Amid it, the startled human **Videric** and his four bodyguards.
- **Luciano's tower approach:** ~1 hour through alleys and stairs to the city's edge; the tower is bone and steel, with **insane laughter** echoing out. Inside: a black metal stair dripping blood, winged-demon statues, a copper double-door; hall paintings that reach for the PCs; a torture chamber where Luciano spends his leisure; thirteen cells of ~40 chained beings forced to watch the torture.
- **The phase-journey (Ep. 7):** Akaron flies ahead wreathed in blue light, singing a long monotone; reality melts around him.
  1. **Plague swamp:** humid, stinking, mosquito-clouds, whipping plants; trees blur, small human figures struggle in mud and vanish.
  2. **War plain:** a trampled road lined with thousands crucified on crosses and stakes; a vast army assembling; arrows whistle, some quetzals shriek and fall.
  3. **Pain corridor:** extreme heat, stench, screams; dying humans spiked to walls; a torture-machine hall — **SMI roll or crash for 1T4** (twice).
  4. **Into Kaos:** colors explode, mounts panic and bolt; magic-sensitives black out from the Vortex but stay strapped in.
- **The floating ruin-city (Ep. 8):** the panicked quetzal lands on a moss-covered stone that proves to be the **cheek of a colossal half-buried statue.** Broken blocks, bridges, and houses drift and slowly orbit; a weak wind pulls everything toward the Vortex (dropped objects drift off and vanish into it). A riderless **silver bird** cries from a rooftop → stairs down into a ruined banquet hall lit by a glass globe, where **Mara** is cutting paintings from frames.
- **Arach's forest & the Mandelbrot paths:** a shifting plant-labyrinth (good spot for optional encounters — small demons, giant spiders, carnivorous plants, flying lizards), opening onto spiraling grass land-bridges in a **fractal** pattern crossed on foot with ropes. Mara name-drops the Mandelbrot set. Xathanu's jagged silhouette looms against the red Vortex.
- **Kirian's war-camp:** brightly colored tents on a hill behind the krazzt-riders; battlefield wreckage, supply wagons, field hospitals; four belmôner guards challenge the party; Kirian sits outside a red tent watching the battle, hammer in hand.
- **The spiral lock (Ep. 9):** Kirian seals the pursuit behind a collapse; a small chamber holds a floor-spiral of fist-sized stones — his back-door into Malkûth's chamber, which **he cannot use himself** (Malkûth's wards). Walk it without stopping: **PSY roll**, limbs grow heavy and vision dims; each failure costs a minute before retrying. At the center, sudden release → materialize in the throne hall.

### GM pacing cautions
- The module repeatedly warns: **don't let the PCs take too much damage before the finale** — they must be in fighting shape for Malkûth. Keep the Vortex chase and optional encounters tense but non-lethal.
- During the Mirâsch stay, a **gruz-physician** visits the wounded — foul salve, incomprehensible chants, full healing — a convenient way to top the party up before the descent.
- Treat Tårg's and (later) Tårgkrizt's power as absolute: if PCs pick a fight with a prince, they simply die.
