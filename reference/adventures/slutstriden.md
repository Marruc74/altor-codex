# Den Nidländska Reningen 3: Slutstriden — Summary

*The Nidland Purge 3: The Final Battle* — the concluding part of a Swedish adventure trilogy for the RPG **Drakar och Demoner**, set in the world of **Ereb Altor**. Written by Johan Sjöberg and published by Äventyrsspel in 1991. It is a GM (SL = *spelledare*) campaign module.

---

## The story so far (Parts 1 & 2)

### Part 1 – *Helvetesfortet* (The Hell Fort)
The player characters (PCs), just discharged as mercenaries in Bedorum, learn that the archmage **Vicotnik** is preparing a great invasion ("the Nidland Purge"). They are sent to spy on and sabotage **Helvetesfortet**, a fortress in the Grynnerbergen mountains. En route they rescue the elf-prince **Talyros** and his pegasus **Enulisor**, and are guided by the dwarf-friend **Ehrman Wall** through abandoned dwarven mines. There they fall into a buried tomb and recover a ~50 cm staff inscribed *"Portens Väktare"* (the Gate's Guardian), a being that promises help "when all hope is lost." They are captured and taken into Helvetesfortet, then rescued by Enulisor as the fort explodes. The explosion frees the **Onde Bestraffaren** (Evil Punisher), an ancient demigod who allies with Vicotnik.

### Part 2 – *Melindors återkomst* (Melindor's Return)
Waking in Landori's elven forests after three months, the PCs learn Vicotnik has conquered Krun, Hynsolge and parts of Cereval. They travel to Berendien to help its sick king, are framed as Nidland spies by the false advisor **Merkato**, and are told by the deposed advisor **Hagras** that the only hope is the exiled princess **Melindor**. They rescue Melindor from Fervidun, return to find King Frekvand murdered, and pursue Merkato — who is revealed to be **Achatoth**, the Hell Fort's commander thought dead. After a climactic battle, Melindor is crowned. The book repeatedly hints the victory may not be final.

## The plot of *Slutstriden*

### The beginning – Landori
A month later, Talyros again visits the PCs (now embedded with the Berendien resistance), saying his father, elf-king **Pargal**, has gathered Ereb's heroes in his tree-castle (Trädslottet) because a way to destroy Vicotnik has been found. He tells them to pass through Landori's magical protective girdle at a thousand-year-old oak using the phrase *"Landoria salinara oasria son."* He then transforms into a dove to leave — and is killed in mid-air by a mysterious **black bird of prey**, which the PCs cannot harm or stop (it is magically shielded by its master). Passing through the girdle, the PCs are spotted by this same raptor, meaning the secret passage is now compromised.

Reaching Trädslottet, they are met by Talyros's grieving brother **Lilarion**, then taken to Pargal in his garden. Pargal explains the plan: tomorrow all the heroes will be teleported to **Yndar**, the necromancers' homeland, to retrieve the **Tvillingstaven** (Twin Staff) from the mountain **Hertach**. The staff can summon the **Gode Bestraffaren** (Good Punisher) — but only at **Densel**, the buried city of mages, which has enough magical potential to free the Good Punisher from its prison and counter the Evil one Vicotnik now wields.

That night the castle is attacked. In the central **Blommornas sal** (Flowers' Hall), about 20 elf-mages led by Pargal begin the teleportation ritual, but Pargal is struck down by a bolt of darkness and the black raptor (revealing a humanoid form) sabotages the magic. Demonic claws drag the heroes out of the magical maelstrom — only the PCs reach the correct destination; everyone else, including Lilarion, is scattered or lost. Lilarion leaves the PCs a small bag containing **four doses of healing salve**, a note, and a **white coral horn** (to summon Enulisor near Landori).

### Yndar
The PCs land in a dead, blood-red-skied wasteland. Yndar was once home to a high culture (the magyler) that delved too deep into magic; its city Krau-ki vanished and was teleported to Marjura. Now it is inhabited by necromancers who worship the imprisoned god **Hertach**, whose petrified arm juts from a mountain. To reach Hertach the PCs must:

- Cross a 5 km-deep **lava ravine** (random encounters: eruptions, lava-lizards, sandstorms, skeletons, leather-birds).
- Navigate a randomly-generated **cave system** (a two-headed wolf guards a bridge; magical gates require ÖPPNA spells).
- Traverse a **petrified forest** stalked by leather-birds and night-cats.

At Hertach they pass through seven arches into the mountain's chambers (warriors' hall with 5 animated statues, the high priest's quarters), then climb to **Hertachs arm** at the summit. There the **Twin Staff** is encased in a 2 m crystal staff held in a giant clawed hand. The high priest **Daechetuen** summons the staff's guardian, the serpent **Wuuhg**. Freeing the staff (via SKINGRA, KNÄCKA, a perfect magic-weapon hit, or a dwarf calculating the breaking point) triggers a magical whirlwind that sucks the PCs toward Densel — but drops them short, in a Nidland war camp.

### Into the desert (*In i elden*)
The PCs land inside an officers' tent and must escape the camp; once discovered, they flee into **Kabsahs sandöken** (Kabsah's sand desert), facing mirages, nomads, sand-dragons and red vultures. After ~2 days they meet **Aborghin**, a seemingly kindly hermit-mage who takes them to his rock dwelling, heals them, and helps them research **Densel's** location in his library (texts hinting it lies buried in the "golden sea" — the desert — of what was once called Lasutyp/Nidland). Aborghin is secretly the disgraced dark-mage **Gründulf**, who plans to lead the PCs to Densel and kill them to seize the Twin Staff.

### Densel
The buried city, founded by **Jachul**, is being excavated by a Nidland expedition led by the **Onde Bestraffaren**, his disciple **Rashar**, dark riders (*mörkerriddare*), soldiers and slaves. The PCs must reach **Världens mitt – Citadellet** (the central pyramid). With the Twin Staff, a hidden pentagram opens a way down into a sequence of four magical labyrinths:

1. **First labyrinth:** physical trials — animated stone warriors, a color-coded floor puzzle, a mirror room, and a lightning-shooting statue holding a cross-key needed to proceed.
2. **Second labyrinth:** trap-filled corridors — trapdoors, skeletons, sealing walls with sliding-tile puzzles, living armor, spike traps — ending in a room of statue-copies of the PCs.
3. **Third labyrinth:** an illusory grotto that materializes the PCs' old enemies (e.g. Echotun and Achatoth) from their thoughts; defeating them requires seeing through the illusions (INT vs. difficulty 20).
4. **Fourth labyrinth:** 36 shifting rooms rolled at random — a pillar hall, a riddle-asking ferryman, an archery puzzle, a burning room, an upside-down room, a dark room.

The labyrinths lead to **Stormarnas sal** (the Storms' Hall, Densel's heart), reached by a ~6 km descending stair. Here **Aborghin/Gründulf launches his attack**; once defeated, the PCs perform the ritual depicted in the hall's prophetic murals (forming a star with the staff-bearer at center) and summon the **Good Punisher**. The Evil Punisher crashes through the ceiling and the two titans battle — the Good one impales the Evil with its single horn, banishing it. The Good Punisher then gives the PCs a **sword** (the Twin Staff transformed) and cryptically instructs them to remove "what is rotten in noblest blood… an evil's claw-hand," before magically transporting them to Hynsolge.

### Hynsolge
The PCs wake near Bedorum (where Part 1 began) with the sword beside them. They realize the "evil's claw-hand" is **Melindor's right hand**, through which Vicotnik controls her. Traveling to Bedorum (meeting old friends Riorik and Argwin, and an apparition of the now-dead Pargal's soul), they infiltrate her heavily fortified castle. They must cut off her right hand without killing anyone on their own side. When the hand is severed, a fresh hand grows back, the enchantment breaks, Melindor is freed (laughing and weeping with joy), and the sword reverts to the Twin Staff. Melindor and Hagras send the PCs to confront Vicotnik directly while they lead the allied armies.

### Toward Vicotnik (*Mot Vicotnik*)
Summoned by the **white coral horn**, Enulisor and his pegasi carry the PCs into Nidland. They are captured by a flying-lizard air-galley, escape, sneak toward the capital **Khaanikel**, are captured again, and are brought before Vicotnik in his tower.

### The final battle
Vicotnik reveals himself — first as a small, unimposing man, then as the **black raptor** that killed Talyros, then resembling the statue of **Jachul** in Densel (they are one and the same: the ancient power-hungry being **Grahë**). As he prepares a spell, the **Good Punisher** darkens the moon (negating Vicotnik's magic) and grants the PCs superhuman strength to break their shackles. Touching the Twin Staff turns it into the magic sword again. The PCs must defeat Vicotnik and his guards unaided. Victorious, they fly out on Enulisor's pegasi through a magic portal above the battlefield, where the Nidland armies are routed and the enslaved silver-elves freed.

### Epilogue
The allies celebrate; weeks later all Nidland armies are reported defeated and the bewitched populace freed. The book closes — as always — by asking whether evil is *truly* gone, "and where it will appear next time."

## Major characters

**Vicotnik / Grahë / Jachul / Margyl** — The archvillain, age 12,456. One of the first beings on Altor, a shapeshifter who has hungered for divine status since the dawn of time. As **Grahë** he served the high culture of Hongria and provoked the gods, who sent the original Punisher against him; he ensnared and cursed it in the **Demonspiran** (Demon Spire) and created its opposite, **Ljusets spira** (the Twin Staff). Punished repeatedly, he became **Jachul** (founder of Densel, buried 5,000 years for hubris), then **Margyl** in Akrogal, then developed dark magic on Yndar, and finally — after learning from the master **Treh'al Kash** (whom he murdered) — became **Vicotnik** ("Arch-troll-man"). Now massively weakened (perhaps a tenth of his former power), he appears as a small, round, stubbled man. Mage of all schools; wields the two-handed sword **Hytrang**. When killed, he dissolves into a slimy pool.

**Pargal** — Elf-king of Landori, age 4,271. Tall, slender, snow-white-haired, with cat-pupiled blue eyes. A former great hunter turned philosopher-mage (animist), living in Trädslottet with his life-partner Ilaine. Largely responsible for the tree-castle's construction. Killed during the assault on Trädslottet; his soul later appears to the PCs.

**Talyros** — Pargal's son and the PCs' friend; killed in dove-form by the black raptor at the adventure's start, his death triggering the plot.

**Lilarion** — Talyros's brother, who guides the PCs through Trädslottet and is lost in the failed teleportation.

**Melindor** — Princess/resistance leader, age 32. Once warm and a flute-player, now cold and egotistical because she is under Vicotnik's control via a mark burned into her right hand. An excellent archer and swordswoman, skilled in tactics. Her secret plan is to lead the allied army into a catastrophic ambush. Freed when the PCs sever her hand. Rides the stallion Faerth.

**Hagras Rådgivaren** — Former advisor and ally, now corrupted by Vicotnik. Tall, bearded, calm chess-player turned occasionally violent; a Mentalist/elementalist mage. Knows Melindor's plans only in rough outline.

**Aborghin / Gründulf** — Age 65; a failed, banished dark-mage of Khaanikel's Mörkerakademin. Small (~145 cm), gaunt, frog-eyed, beardless, disarmingly friendly. Poses as a "good" anti-Vicotnik hermit to befriend the PCs, intending to kill them and take the Twin Staff at Densel. He startles when he sees Jachul's statue (which resembles Vicotnik). Skilled in drugs, herbs, poisons, dark magic and necromancy.

**Rashar** — Age 54; the expedition's chief disciple at Densel, second only to the Evil Punisher. Large, powerful, black-bearded, dominant, devout. A dark-mage harmed by sunlight; carries an unbreakable Tirahn-crystal staff.

**Daechetuen** — Age 87; Hertach's high priest. A hunched, blind (yet sighted), scarred, pale necromancer who hates all living things and sacrificed his own servants. Carries the cursed ceremonial broadsword **Eungür**.

**Enulisor** — A great pegasus, age 1,095, for 950 years Talyros's companion. Highly intelligent, telepathic within 25 m, with extraordinary hearing (he hears the coral horn from vast distances). Eldest of the Grynnerbergen pegasi; may choose to bond with a PC afterward.

**The Bestraffaren (Punisher)** — Originally **Thiri**, a being from another dimension where everything needs a light and dark counterpart. Its "outer self" was bound to Altor as the Punisher; ensnared by the mage **Fhênesh** in the Demon Spire and freed by the PCs in Part 1, merging into the **Evil Punisher** that serves Vicotnik. The **Good Punisher** (its opposite) is summoned via the Twin Staff and, by banishing the Evil one, becomes indirectly bound to Altor — which is why it can intervene one last time in the finale. In essence a god, usually appearing as a stately man in chain-mail.

## Creatures (notable)

- **Wuuhg** — The Twin Staff's guardian, placed in Hertach by the mage K'hadukil Hjurte. A huge black-scaled, horned serpent (STO 80, KP 65) whose red-yellow eyes can paralyze/hypnotize; fights until all its body-points are gone, then retreats.
- **Lavaödla (lava-lizard)** — Heat-resistant predator up to 3 m, red-scaled, slow, living in the ravine's lava.
- **Läderfågel (leather-bird)** — Featherless, leather-skinned birds with ~3 m wingspans; cowardly, attacking in large flocks with grating shrieks.
- **Nattkatt (night-cat)** — Large, coal-black, razor-fanged/clawed predator of the petrified forest; pupil-less yellow eyes; cubs can be tamed with difficulty.
- **Statykrigare / labyrinth statues / living armor** — Magical stone or enchanted combatants in Hertach and Densel; living armor falls apart at 0 KP and can then be worn.
- **Whrithagerna** — Vicotnik's personal bodyguards.
- The **mörkerriddare** at Densel — Khaner, Trusth and Pheth, with dragon-, bull- and beautiful-woman-shaped helmets.
- Also: Helvetehundar (hell-hounds), sand-dragons, flying-lizard riders, and the enslaved silver-elves.

## Key places

- **Landori / Trädslottet** — Pargal's living tree-castle, hidden in the protected elven forest; contains the central Blommornas sal and Pargal's mirror-tarn garden (where he watches and records events across Altor).
- **Yndar** — Dead necromancer homeland with a lava ravine, cave network, petrified forest, and **Hertach**, the petrified-god mountain housing the Twin Staff.
- **Kabsahs sandöken** — The Nidland desert where Aborghin lives in a rock dwelling (entrance, hall, kitchen, living room, bedroom, garden oasis, library, and a hidden laboratory of forbidden experiments).
- **Densel** — The buried city of mages (overview includes a 100 m statue of Jachul, an observatory, a deliberately leaning tower, a monolith, and the expedition's headquarters). Its center is the **Citadellet** pyramid leading down through the four labyrinths to **Stormarnas sal**, whose walls bear Fate's prophetic murals.
- **Bedorum** — Allied war-capital; its hilltop castle (detailed lower/upper/loft floors) houses Melindor's suite, Hagras's tower workshop, barracks, prison tower and machine room.
- **Khaanikel** — Nidland's capital, site of Vicotnik's palace and tower where the final battle occurs.
- The book also maps southeastern Ereb Altor, Nidland/Hynsolge/Krun, and Pargal's garden.

## Significant items & weapons

- **Tvillingstaven / Ljusets spira (the Twin Staff)** — ~50 cm, light metal, intricately engraved, crowned with golden wings. One of Altor's mightiest magical objects and the only thing that can save the world. Its sole powers: it is constantly drawn toward its opposite (the Evil Punisher, summoned by the Demon Spire), and it can summon the **Good Punisher**. Transforms into a magical sword (effectively FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E4, +4 to hit and damage, no PSY cost) when wielded by a PC.
- **Hytrang** — Vicotnik's two-handed sword (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E6, PERMANENS, NEXUS); his clothing also carries SKYDD E10.
- **Eungür** — Daechetuen's gold-gleaming ceremonial broadsword (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3); cursed so its bearer becomes progressively withdrawn and headstrong, and "good" beings sense an evil aura around it.
- **Pargal's gift bag** — Four healing-salve doses (3T6 KP at full dose, 1T6 at half), a note, and the **white coral horn** that summons Enulisor.
- **Rashar's Tirahn-crystal staff** — unbreakable, LADDNING E5.
- **Daechetuen's and Hagras's charged mage-staffs** — the latter with NEDKALLA ÅSKVIGG E4 (Call Down Thunderbolt).
- **The cross-key** — found in the first labyrinth, required to open the way deeper in.
- Background lore items: the **Demonspiran** (Demon Spire) imprisoning the Evil Punisher.
