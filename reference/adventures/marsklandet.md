# MARSKLANDET
### A campaign module for *Expert Drakar & Demoner* (TAMB Äventyrsspel, 1988), set in Ereb-Altor

*Original concept by Christer Regebro (for the first "blue box" DoD); reworked for Expert rules and the Ereb-Altor world by Anders Blixt, Ann-Sophie Qvarnström, Ewiz Ehrsson, Stefan Thulin and others. Cover by Jim Holloway. This was, at the time, the thickest Expert adventure module the publisher had released — and unusually, most of it is played from a canoe.*

> **A note on this summary.** Marsklandet is a *sandbox*: a delta of ~80 islands the party explores in almost any order, held together by one through-line (the hunt for the Crystal Orb) plus a secondary quest and many optional island events. Because the background, travel rules, inhabitants and quests interlock across every location, this is written as a single document rather than one file per location. The numeric stat blocks for creatures and NPCs are printed in a separate *Karthäftet* (map booklet) that is **not** part of this PDF, so creatures are described qualitatively here.

## Setting & Background

**Marsklandet** ("the Marshland") is the swampy delta of the river **Orquin**, in the far north of **Kardien**, about 450 km due north of the city **Faltrax** (the setting of the adventure *Rösten från forntiden*). The terrain is a maze of open water, vast reed-beds, impassable mud-flats and about **80 islands**. It has been invaded many times — by elves, dark elves, an aboriginal people and modern humans — and has often harboured pirates who shelter in its un-navigable channels. Today Kardien's kings have little real control; the region is nominally Kardish but in practice lawless, and people fend for themselves. Many frightening stories circulate (some deliberately spread to keep the curious away from treasure caches), and boats still vanish, especially off the main channel.

Climate: southern temperate, summers to ~+30 °C, winters to ~+5 °C (the marsh has never frozen). Mostly westerly sea winds, but inside the marsh the islands damp the wind and waves, so canoes are easy to handle. It's often cloudy and rainy. Summer is the best season to adventure, because in winter the ~4 °C water is "literally paralysing" — a person dropped in it is almost instantly incapacitated.

The common tongue is **jori** (represented by Swedish; archaic/formal forms are shown as Latin). Other languages spoken in the marsh include **svartiska** (dark-elf), **älviska** (elvish), the reptile-man tongue, and **ker-bosh** (the aboriginal language). Literacy is rare — essentially only Viandar's temple, the magic academy, and adult elves can read; the illiterate can't even interpret maps, so they give directions verbally.

Money is rarely used (mostly barter); a large gold coin called the **gulden** circulates here, worth two normal gold coins.

### Travelling the marsh (rules in brief)
You move by flat-bottomed rowboat or canoe; sailing is pointless (weak, unreliable winds). A **träskbåt** (marsh-boat) is 9 m long, 3 m wide, draws only 30 cm, weighs ~120 BEP, carries ~300 BEP, has oars, poles, lockable bow/stern lockers and a rear tent — bought in **San-Bu-Nim** (~12 km upriver) for 1200 sm. Canoes (Canadian-style; no kayaks) are also sold there. Boat/canoe theft is punished by being thrown into deep water with your feet tied to a stone. Navigation uses the **Orientering** skill, not Navigering. Combat from a canoe is heavily penalised (e.g. −6 to CL for two-handed weapons, −8 for chain weapons); reptile-men try to capsize canoes to fight in the water. A cork belt (0.5 BEP, 20 sm) keeps a STO-20 creature afloat; ringmail makes you sink like a stone. Useful skills here: Fiska, Kanot, Orientering, Simma, Träsköverlevnad, Karate (for fighting in water), the elvish/dark-elf languages, Zoology; and animism/elemental/mentalism spells suited to water and weather.

### Standard islands
Most small islands are unkeyed "standard islands." Six map sketches are reused; the GM rolls or picks one, then can add terrain features from a **Variationstabell** (big rock, cave, spring, ruined hut, ovenstone, hollow tree, thorn thickets, mud pit, edible-berry bushes, animal den, etc.) and rolls for random encounters (day/night) and huntable game (waterfowl, rabbit, deer, boar, water buffalo, hippo, marsh-bear). They make good "bases" to rest at.

## The Inhabitants & Threats

**Pirates.** A band of ten in a fast rowboat (chief, helmsman, two archers, six rowers). They pose as the "customs master of North Marsk," demanding 100 sm per person plus 10% of any treasure for a 3-month "toll" receipt — refusal means a fight (lose, and they take the boat and everything but your clothes). The chief can be bribed if his crew doesn't see; show him wine, food, medicine, furs, small jewellery — but show him *too much* wealth and his greed takes over. They come from a sea-pirate village outside the river mouth and know the marsh's geography but avoid inhabited spots.

**Kardier (humans).** The only permanent village is the **Handelsstationen** (trading station); they fish, farm and trade with the aboriginals, and hate/war with dark elves and reptile-men.

**Urbefolkningen / ker-bosh (aboriginals).** Sand-skinned, fair curly-haired nomadic hunter-fisher tribes with stone-age technology, predating the elves; nobody (not even elves) knows their origin. They trade hides, dried meat, snake/crocodile skin and plant-drugs (and prized snake/spider antivenoms) via an elaborate silent-barter ritual (lay goods out, withdraw 30 m, take turns; honour-bound never to steal). They regard the whole north-west marsh as taboo, so they know nothing of Kristallön or Viandar's temple, and avoid the magic academy out of fear of Dermatt.

**Svartalfer (dark elves)** — a tribe of ~80, living in caves/rocky islands, raiding others for food and slaves, travelling by canoe. They hate sunlight (−2 CL by day). Among them are **storalfer** (big dark elves): bigger, stronger, sneakier, sun-tolerant, used as daytime guards and feared by ordinary dark elves. Night raids try to cut throats while victims sleep; reinforcements arrive in waves.

**Reptilmän (reptile-men).** 5–8 nomadic hunting bands of 2T10, excellent swimmers and water-fighters (they fight with claws and fangs in water, spear+shield on land), warm-blooded (active even in cold seasons), at perpetual war with the aboriginals. They treat all humanoids as prey; ambush from the water and capsize boats.

**Alver (elves).** A reclusive, music-devoted tribe (see *Alvön*) — 1000+ years old, respected and feared by humans.

**Najader (naiads)** and **karkioner** — see the lagoon side-event.

**Dvärgar (dwarves)** — a small expedition far from home (see *The Dwarves*).

**The cult of Viandar** — fanatical priests of an evil crocodile/water goddess (see *Viandar's Temple*).

**Animals & monsters.** Babian (baboon troops with sentries), **drinn** (a deceptively cute but vicious territorial marsh predator with scaled armour and a razor tail-tip), flodhäst (hippo, capsizes boats), deer, **krokodil** (drags prey underwater), **Nidingen** (a possibly-legendary 10–15 m fish/sea-serpent/mosasaur lake-monster — the GM decides if it's real; best used to terrify rather than fight), snakes (venomous, constrictor, spitting), **slemvarelse** (a 4 m "moss-patch" slime that engulfs and digests prey; only cut out with a blade; its juices permanently corrode gear), giant bird-spiders, **träskbjörn** (giant marsh slap-bear), **träskvarg** (pack wolves), **vampyrfladdermus** (eagle-sized vampire bats whose numbing, anticoagulant saliva makes victims bleed 1T4/SR), and water buffalo.

## The Main Quest — "Grunduppdraget"

The party's reason to come is to retrieve a **Kristallkula** (Crystal Orb) of immense power, on behalf of the old animist mage **Serego**.

**Two start hooks.** (1) An animist PC who once studied under Serego gets a letter from him. (2) An elf (or other elf-folk) PC is approached by **Hanaita**, an elf in the service of Moder Jord, who asks them in the goddess's name to help Serego.

**Serego's tale.** As a young apprentice he learned of a crystal orb whose power exceeds even *Dytars Hjärta*. He has spent his life finding it and learning to control it. It rests, unprotected, somewhere in Marsklandet — on a ruined island that was once home to a secret **order of silversmiths** (elves who broke from their tribe, later joined by dwarves for mining and humans for service/guarding). The order achieved unmatched magical silverwork and great wealth; refusing to share its secrets, it made powerful enemies who eventually overthrew the friendly king garrisoning the island and wiped out the order with **träskpest** (marsh-plague). The attackers tore down the castle but never reached the order's innermost rooms (magically protected) or the plague killed them first. The order's last member escaped to seek help, was murdered, but lived long enough to pass fragments of the order's secrets to a mage — whose apprentice was Serego.

**The deal.** 1000 sm now, 2000 on return with the orb, plus three months' training in marsh skills (via the fisherman Fodder). Serego wants the orb to *study and then destroy* it — in the wrong hands it would be catastrophic. The party may keep everything else they find (including mithril). He gives them an **old, partly-wrong map** and a **magic candle** (charged with a VARSEBLI variant: lit, it makes any magic-charged metal within 15 m glow). He warns that part of his notes about the orb were stolen.

**The Crystal Orb's danger.** Touching it bare-handed forces an immediate *hard PSY roll* or you become its will-less slave. If the party holds it more than four weeks, each member must pass a hard PSY roll **every day** from day 28 or be enslaved; an enslaved person does only what others tell them, freed only when the orb is put back in its casket. If the whole party is enslaved they sit motionless until they starve or someone finds them (e.g. Fodder, who comes looking). The orb's actual powers are deliberately left undefined (like Tolkien's One Ring — more menacing for being mysterious).

**Getting started.** The party travels to the fisherman **Fodder** in the upriver village **San-Bu-Nim**, who trains them (Kanot 19, Fiske 19, Simma B5, Träsköverlevnad 18, Orientering 18), warns them to wear cork belts and not ringmail aboard, and lends them three canoes (to be returned) plus ~100 sm of fishing gear.

## The Secondary Quest — the sea-serpent brew

While dealing with aboriginals, the party may meet a **magicians' expedition** carrying an injured high priestess in a litter. If they share a meal, the priestess **Seep-Hath-Ka'aa** (high priestess of the *good* snake-god **Ka'aa**) offers a job. To keep her powers she must offer **sjöormsbrygd** (sea-serpent brew) to Ka'aa once a year; it's brewed from sea-serpent spawn only by rare creatures called **karkioner**, and one is rumoured to live in the marsh. Injured (by priests from "a strange temple" — Viandar's), her people can't search. She asks the party to seek **najader** (naiads, distant elf-kin) who live where karkions do.

**Terms.** Pay 1600 sm per flask (negotiable up to 3200), or 200 sm even on honest failure. Each PC gives a **hair** as a pledge; selling the brew to anyone else triggers Ka'aa's curse — skin turns to green scales and KAR crashes to 1 (reversed when they finally sell to her, though she'll dock 400 sm per cursed PC). She cannot be coerced; attacking her paralyses the attacker automatically.

## Keyed Locations

### Magiakademin (the Magic Academy)
A fortified former pirate keep (earlier a lord's castle) on an island, now a school run by the magician **Dermatt**, who teaches mentalism and elemental spells to boy apprentices (currently 5 younger, 9 older). Dermatt is secretive about why the school is so remote; the locals (whose rumours he himself spreads) believe him an unpredictable, immortal wizard who turns people into toads. He gives information on the marsh and can write a letter of introduction to the elves. The keep is mapped in detail (entry hall with murder-holes, kitchen/hall, study chambers, dorms, a **laboratory** with valuable alchemy ingredients, a **library** of 400 books and 50 maps — one map shows buried treasure on **Vargön** — plus magic scrolls and demon-lore tomes; cells, food stores).

**Optional demon-attack adventure.** If the party stays the night, a dabbling older student, **Nerri**, secretly summons a **demon from Kaos** using a forbidden necromancy book, loses control, and is killed; the demon wounds Dermatt (who staggers in and collapses, perhaps dead). The boys panic and look to the party to lead. The library yields aids (scrolls — FRAMMANA SALAMANDER E4, FRAMMANA GNOM E5, KONTROLLERA REPTIL E3 — and two demon-lore books, *Libris Chaosium* and *Annales Victoriae*, explaining demon abilities and how to trap demons with pentagrams of metal wire, salamander-fire, or gnomes). The GM is told to tune the demon's power (e.g. give it Invisibility, Teleportation, Hypnosis or shape-change for an *Alien*/*The Thing*-style horror) so the party has a chance.

### Alvön (Elf Island)
A large wooded island approached via several beaches (Rärrön, Gårdsön, Bergsviken, Skogsviken, Bäckviken). The elf village sits in **giant trees**, with tree-houses 25–35 m up reached by rope ladders (the elves can drop a whole tree-house in pieces if besieged). The tribe — 35 adults, 3 children, all related, hundreds of years old — is **devoted to music** (everyone plays or sings, with FV B4–B5 in a music skill; flutes and harps over drums). Guests who stay get an extraordinary musical evening; a PC with FV B4+ in a music skill can perform and win favour.

The elves are extra-wary now: dark elves are raiding, and a **young elf woman has been kidnapped** — a clue (a torn **dwarf boot**) points at dwarves, but this is a *false lead*. (The real kidnappers are the **trading station** humans — see below.) They offer **70 gulden** to return her, **35** for useful information, and give knowledge of every location plus gifts (canas cakes; a crocodile-repellent powder). Their tree-house is mapped (great hall with bows/spears, kitchen with running water piped from the canopy, music room with silver flutes, bedrooms, library, workshop, guest rooms).

### Handelsstationen (the Trading Station)
The only permanent human village — a palisaded compound of huts behind an earth wall, with a watchtower. The chief is unusually cold and wants the party gone by dark; **hut 3 is forbidden**. The huts cover a smithy, weaver, leather-workers, the chief's home, a tavern, and a shrine to the fertility goddess **Tafkol** (tended by a shaman).

**The village's secret.** The chief's only child (an 8-year-old girl) was hit in the back by a (probably poisoned) dark-elf arrow and is dying. Out of desperation the villagers rowed to Alvön, panicked, and **kidnapped the elf girl** (the very one the elves are missing), hoping she could heal the child. The terrified elf girl — who speaks only elvish and was raised on warnings about temples that sacrifice children — believes she is in such a temple awaiting sacrifice, and refuses to cooperate. If the party heals the child themselves (or persuades/enables the elf girl to do it — she is a skilled healer, 70% to halt the poison with gathered herbs), the chief is hugely grateful (gifts worth ~2000 sm, the best hut for the night). Reuniting the elf girl with Alvön collects the elves' reward — the module's clearest example of how the two threads link.

### Svartnissarnas torn (the Dark-Gnomes' Tower) — "Svartfolksstaden"
A cursed round stone tower (24 m across, 14 m high, **no door** — the only entrance was a now-collapsed drawbridge from a support tower, ~8 m to the roof battlements). A **crocodile-filled moat** (5 crocs) rings it. A **curse** disguises every treasure native to the tower as worthless junk (wooden mugs that are silver, "stones" that are gulden, etc.) — the disguise breaks only once an item is carried *outside the wall*. It's now home to a tribe of **svartnissar** (dark-gnomes: smaller, weaker cousins of the dark elves, often enslaved by them) — 15 men, 13 women, 20 children — who know all the tower's vents and ambush intruders. A passing **svartalf expedition** (12 svartalfer + 4 storalfer) camps on the north shore. The tower is mapped over three floors, roof and cellar, full of disguised loot, venomous spiders/scorpions, a magic silver **spinning top** (inscribed "spin me when afraid and sad"; heals 1 KP to all, worth ~5000 sm), a magic **shield** (SKYDD E5), a nasty **teleport trap** on the cellar stairs (sends metal gear to a separate room, and the person to the roof or a cell), and the svartnisse **temple** (a statue with brass crown and **ruby eyes**, 1000 sm each).

### Viandars tempel (the Temple Island)
A white marble temple with a gilt roof, sacred to **Viandar**, an evil crocodile/water goddess who grants her priests power over marsh creatures, long life and protection — in exchange for **human and animal sacrifice**. They now abduct anyone who can't defend themselves. Night-time **panthers** guard the grounds. Visitors are welcomed warmly, ushered into a guest hall, and offered **drugged wine**; those who drink are bound and taken to a prep room, then sacrificed at dawn (forced to touch the idol, which paralyses them and drains 1 PSY/hour while gold needles cost 1 KP/hour until they die). The temple is mapped in full (public hall with a swinging altar over a secret stair; kitchen and pantry where confiscated gear is hidden; the temple knight's room with a **mithril ringmail**; the inner **Helgedomen** — a green-onyx crocodile idol with **ruby eyes** and a great living-looking **crystal** on a white-gold chain, warded by PARALYSERING + PSY-drain, with six side-statues that fire **BLIXT E2**; the high priest's, priests' and novices' rooms — the latter holding an **elf belt** from an elf youth they killed years ago — plus a bell-drop trap and poisoned-pin door traps). Run by the fanatical, half-mad high priest **Leokop Wigorn** and the brutal temple knight **Mypold Safre**, with two priests (Olders, Annaus), four squires and seven novices. They will not let the party leave alive — but won't pursue far off-island (they daren't weaken the temple's defences against dark-elf/reptile-man raids).

### Kristallön (the Crystal Island) — the climax
The site of the orb. Erosion has split it into the small **Nordön** and larger **Sydön**. There are **two surviving entrances** to the order's underground temple — via the **NW tower well** in the castle ruin on Sydön, and via the **old fisherman's-hut well** on Nordön — plus a third (a mine shaft) now collapsed and lethal. The evil magician **Kang-Szrit**, with a **rese** (ogre) and **nine svartalfer**, is **digging a new tunnel** in (the normal entrances are magically protected); he is the one who **stole Serego's notes**, because he wants the orb too. (Scale the dark elves to ~50% more than the party's number.)

The **borgruinen** (Sydön castle ruin) sits on a height; its SE-tower cellar holds a spike-pit trap, the old kitchen, and the **mine shaft where Kang-Szrit's crew digs** — he'll offer a treacherous "partnership" (he gets the orb and half of everything) before betraying the party. The **NW-tower well** (35 m, with a spiral of footholds and one-way mechanical hatches that originally tested the order's novices) is the proper magical way down, guarded by **constrictor snakes** in the water.

Below is the **order's temple** — an elaborate gauntlet of *trials* (originally to test novices' honesty, humility and intelligence) and *traps*: mirror-bright **mithril corridors** that fire six bolts (2T4+3 each) if you bump the walls; spike-pit floors; an illusory tomb of "**UNDER**" with a teleport that punishes greed/rudeness (sending offenders, stripped of gear, back to a locked fish-shed or the NW tower); a poison-gas chemical room; an illusory bottomless spike-pit (really only 1 m, breakable with SKINGRA E3); a labyrinth with a humility test (a dwarf-statue that bows to the un-greedy) and a greed trap (an illusory gold room that locks you in until you pay "thrice your coin-hunger"); and a **flooding water-staircase** triggered by removing the wrong crystal. To reach the orb you must gather **four talismans**: the **chain** (room 20, mithril), the **carpet/matta** (room 22), the **crystal sphere** (room 31, magic rock-crystal), and a **magic candle** (room 19 or Serego's). With them, sealed mithril doors open to **room 42**, where the **Crystal Orb** sits inside a silver casket with a steel inner shell (plus a second empty silver casket worth ~2700 sm, and a steel/mithril casket of element-magic scrolls — the casket alone worth up to 20,000 sm if its mithril is recognised). Lifting the orb out risks enslavement (see the main quest). A hidden stair under the lower temple altar leads through a 300 m underwater tunnel to Nordön (rooms 43–49), itself a chain of interlocked flooding-trap chambers and a disguised-ladder well in the fisherman's hut.

The module stresses there is **no guaranteed success**: even skilled parties may fail to penetrate Kristallön and return empty-handed — and an honourable failure may beat death.

## Side Islands & Random Events ("Andra äventyr")

### Tigerön (Tiger Island)
Two man-eating tigers, descendants of beasts set to guard a long-since-looted royal grave. Hungry, they attack landing parties (focusing on a horse if present). Loot in the grass: 4T100 sm and 1T4 gold chains.

### Drakön (Dragon Island)
A burnt-out pirate keep on a hill, destroyed ~200 years ago by a young dragon. Inside the hill, a tunnel and old cellars lead to the lair of the dragon **Kréurm** (hatched 1547 years ago in the volcano Eldtinn; can no longer fly — a necromancer withered his left wing with FÖRTVINA EXTREMITET — and hides it). Sour, antisocial and hungry, he wants to *eat* the party (so avoids his fire-breath), lures with fair words, and fights to the death in his lair (but can't swim, so won't follow into water). The tunnels hold dead treasure-hunters' caches (gulden, a gold bowl/ring, a magic bronze **throwing axe** with TELEPORTERING E1 + PERMANENS + NEXUS that returns to the thrower on a miss) and a room of 17 starved women, servants and children with silver mugs and jewellery. Kréurm's hoard: **507 gulden, 2,367 gm, 360,530 sm, 37 silver ingots, a great diamond (114,000 sm), 36 gems, 16 gold rings, a gold crown (56,000 sm)** — plus, if slain, dragon hide (50 BEP), 96 teeth (625 sm each) and 20 claws (150 sm each). The diamond and crown are dwarf clan heirlooms (see the Dwarves).

### Vargön (Wolf Island)
21 marsh-wolves and a 500-year-old guardian **ghost** (PSY 12, STO 10) over a buried treasure (the cache marked on the academy library map). Digging deep enough uncovers a skeleton, the ghost's fear attacks, then loot: a bronze **kroksabel** in a silver scabbard (FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E1 + PERMANENS, active when drawn), gulden and gems, and a deeper copper chest of 82 gulden, 1140 sm and five gem rings. The GM may optionally add the werewolf **Svakor** (a once-ordinary shepherd, bitten by a werewolf, who killed his own family in wolf-form and fled here, mad; the wolves treat him as pack-leader) — a dangerous monster to be built to fit the party.

### The Naiad Lagoon
A shallow lagoon with a tiny holm, home to the naiads **Ereli** and **Eelin** (beautiful, bound to their place; each in an underwater hut, with a controlled seal and **crocodile "guard-dogs"**). Ereli's dagger is mithril; their pouches hold drugs. They barter — uninterested in money, they want rare herbs, beautiful songs/poems, and fine craft (especially small bone instruments). A handsome, high-KAR elf or human man may be invited by Eelin to spend the night in her underwater hut (she makes him able to breathe there); on a hard KAR roll she grants him permanent freshwater-breathing for life. They sell drugs (celli, duragos, canas, sarassos, smaugifonia) and waterproofing fluid, and — only if the party has the secondary quest — sell **karkion information** (where/when/how/what, at escalating BYP cost): a karkion lives nearby (a hidden, magically concealed home); he can be met only at night meditating on the holm; you approach by sitting beside him and offering, in **elvish**, to tell a fascinating story; karkions love knowledge and despise haggling.

**The karkion Gandimon Dyhn Jamboki­var.** ~60% chance he appears at night. Engage him correctly (a good listener, no commerce) and by dawn he gives a flask of **nine litres of sjöormsbrygd**, then leaves for good. Mention *buying* it and he indignantly refuses ("catch your own sea-serpent spawn — only 57 years till the next batch!"). Sea-serpents come to the marsh every 165 years; the brew induces religious ecstasy and divine communion (with feverish side-effects), cures hangovers, and — used daily for three months — grants immunity to alcohol, but after six months stops the digestion and kills the user. The karkion can also correct the party's map and gives wry opinions on every location.

### The Dwarves
A grim event on a standard island: five dwarves — **Train** (leader), **Borin, Noin, Dovin, Nim** — far from home and miserable in the wet (dwarves at sea suffer halved STY/SMI). Train, drunk at a feast, swore a binding oath (*bragelöfte*) to slay the dragon **Kréurm**, who robbed his clan 108 years ago. They're hunting the dragon with **Nim's "Drakdödaren"** — a ballista firing a great spear (5T6) or a 30-bolt shotgun-spread (2T6+2 each), with 30 special **FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3 + SIGILL** bolts reserved for the dragon. **Dovin** carries drugs (incl. snakebite antidote). They trade information (especially about dragons) and may propose a joint hunt (treasure split evenly, extra share to whoever lands the killing blow). They covet the **diamond and gold crown** from Kréurm's hoard (clan heirlooms — Train will write a 15,000 gm promissory note for them); refuse to deal and the dwarves become implacable enemies who will take them by force. (They're also the source of the false "dwarf boot" clue at Alvön — they did *not* take the elf girl.)

### Fjärden (the Firth)
On open water the party finds a dead, naked elf-like woman floating, an arrow between her ribs bearing **Viandar's** symbol. She is a **svanmö (swan-maiden)**, **Serina**, a hermit drug-brewer shot in swan-form by a temple hunter who mistook her for game. Her waterproof pouch holds a papyrus **book** (elvish): six pages of non-magical drug/poison/antidote recipes (5 Drogkunskap XP per page), eight elvish **poems** (worth 16 BYP each to the naiads — one is about "Alenoin/dawn-wing"), a zoology section (drinn and vampire-bat habits, speculation on whether the Nidingen is real, and a sketch of **Kréurm with a withered left wing**), and a diary note: she saw a **karkion near Alvön** ~3 years ago. As she's examined, **four reptile-men** attack from below to capsize the boats; they retreat if two are downed.

## Characters (named NPCs)

- **Serego** — the very old animist mage at the academy who hires the party to find and destroy the Crystal Orb. Reveres nature; offers modest pay and hopes they act for the cause, not coin.
- **Hanaita** — an elf in Moder Jord's service; the alternate quest-giver for an elf PC.
- **Fodder** — the fisherman in San-Bu-Nim who trains and equips the party and lends the canoes; later may come searching if they vanish.
- **Seep-Hath-Ka'aa** — high priestess of the good snake-god **Ka'aa**; gives the secondary quest (sea-serpent brew); honourable but enforces her hair-pledge with a scaling curse.
- **Dermatt** — reclusive mentalism/elemental magician running the academy for boy apprentices; cultivates a fearsome mystique; helpful and informative if respected. (Students include **Nerri**, who summons the demon, and **Stepan**, the rooftop watch-boy.)
- **Gandimon Dyhn Jamboki­var** — the karkion; ancient, proud, knowledge-loving, hates commerce; source of the sea-serpent brew.
- **Ereli & Eelin** — the two naiads of the lagoon; barterers and (for Eelin) a possible romance/boon.
- **Train, Borin, Noin, Dovin, Nim** — the dwarf dragon-hunting expedition; oath-bound, prickly, dangerous if cheated; Dovin = drugs, Nim = the Drakdödaren ballista.
- **Serina** — the dead swan-maiden of the Firth, whose book ties several threads together.
- **Leokop Wigorn** — bald, ~50, fanatical, half-mad high priest of Viandar; commands the temple.
- **Mypold Safre** — Viandar's big, brutal, skilled temple knight; runs the temple's defence and gives no quarter.
- **Kang-Szrit** — the rival magician digging into Kristallön with an ogre (rese) and dark elves; stole Serego's notes; offers a treacherous alliance, then betrays.
- **Kréurm** — the 1547-year-old, flightless (withered-wing) dragon of Drakön; sour, hungry, fights to the death.
- **Svakor** — (optional) the mad shepherd-turned-werewolf pack-leader of Vargön.

## Notable Items, Weapons & Magic

- **The Crystal Orb (Kristallkulan)** — the goal: an orb of undefined, immense power that enslaves anyone who touches it bare-handed (hard PSY roll) or holds it too long; kept in a steel-lined silver casket in Kristallön's room 42.
- **The four talismans** to reach the orb — the mithril **chain** (room 20), the **carpet** woven with metal thread (room 22), the magic **crystal sphere** (room 31), and a **magic candle** (room 19 or Serego's, which makes magic metal within 15 m glow).
- **Mithril everywhere on Kristallön** — corridor plating, doors, caskets, a chain; immensely valuable but takes weeks and heavy tools to pry loose.
- **Magic bronze throwing axe** (Drakön) — TELEPORTERING E1 + PERMANENS + NEXUS; returns to the thrower on a miss.
- **Bronze kroksabel** (Vargön) — FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E1 + PERMANENS, active when drawn.
- **Silver spinning top** (Svartnisse tower) — "spin me when afraid and sad"; heals 1 KP to all in the room, ~5000 sm.
- **Magic shield** (Svartnisse tower) — SKYDD E5 + PERMANENS.
- **Mithril ringmail** (Viandar's temple knight's room); **mithril dagger** (the naiad Ereli's).
- **Drakdödaren** — the dwarves' ballista (great spear 5T6, or 30-bolt spread 2T6+2 each), with 30 dragon-specific FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E3 + SIGILL bolts.
- **Drugs** traded by aboriginals, naiads and dwarves (celli, duragos, canas, sarassos, smaugifonia, stridsdryck, stjärnedryck, svartblod) and antivenoms; **sjöormsbrygd** (the secondary quest's prize). The academy and temple stores hold HELA elixirs and poisons.
- Magic books/scrolls in the academy library (demon-summoning and demon-trapping lore; the Vargön treasure map) and Kristallön's element-magic scrolls.
