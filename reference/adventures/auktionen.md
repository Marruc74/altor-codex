# AUKTIONEN ("The Auction")

*An adventure by **Ola Nilsson** for **Drakar och Demoner** (Dragons & Demons)*
Published in **Sinkadus #36**, June 1992 (Target Games / Äventyrsspel), pp. 26–32.

> Swedish RPG terms are kept and glossed in English. Game shorthand used in the text:
> **FV** = skill value · **KP** = hit points · **PSY** = psyche · **STO** = strength · **FYS** = constitution ·
> **SR** = combat round · **sm** = silver coins · **gm** = gold coins · **BV** = breaking value ·
> **skräckslå X/Y** = a "fright roll" on the horror table · **CL/E1** = casting level / effect degree.

---

## 1. Premise & Setup

- Set in a **large, crime-ridden city** with a seedy urban feel.
- The party gets pulled into a **power struggle between a demon-worshipping murder cult and the city's assassins' guild**.
- The supplement *Tjuvar och lönnmördare* ("Thieves & Assassins") **helps but is not required**.
- **Required hook:** at least one PC must be **carrying an important item they cannot or will not give up** — in this scenario, a **magical object lent by their master**, with a warning never to lose it.

---

## 2. The Two Factions

### Drakens sällskap — "The Dragon's Society" (the cult)
A secret demon cult hiding behind a respectable charitable order. Organized in **three circles**:

| Circle | Who | What they know / are |
|---|---|---|
| **1st (inner)** | Council of 5 "masters": 4 rich merchants + the demonologist **Prenikaft** | The real cult; performs the rituals. Prenikaft has contact with the demon prince **Azhkhurath** |
| **2nd** | ~12 brainwashed young men sworn as assassins | Do anything for the cult, even die; don't know the 1st circle's rituals |
| **3rd (outer)** | "Ordinary" rich, influential men (e.g. the fat merchant **Barbana di Lautmaz**) | Dupes; think it's a charitable order |

- **Symbol:** a **black dragon**. Members bear a small dragon tattoo somewhere on the body; the demonologist is tattooed head-to-toe with a giant dragon.
- Funds public charity through proxies to keep up appearances.

### Lönnmördarna — the Assassins' Guild
- Small but highly effective guild that sells its services to the highest bidder.
- **Hierarchy:** a legendary hidden **Master** → **3 teachers** (master-assassins; only 2 are currently in the city, 1 at a remote training camp) → **older students / "throat-cutters"** (10 men, 3 women) → **~20 novices** (about half at the camp).
- Existed **12 years**; works smoothly with the thieves' guild and "Kharynos."
- About a year ago a **mystery cult appeared and started committing brutal ritual murders** the guild didn't do — wrecking the guild's reputation. The guild is now hunting these "competitors."

## 3. Act-by-Act Structure

### Act I — Arrival & the Robbery
1. **Till staden / Hjälpsamme herrn ("The Helpful Gentleman"):** The party can't find lodging. A well-dressed young man, **"Gilimart Verandes"** (really **Kalidak Ab-Mar**), offers to lead them to a cheap inn run by his cousin, *Den vinglande näktergalen* ("The Wobbling Nightingale").
2. **The ambush:** There is no inn. He leads them into a rough quarter; a gang of **flåbusar (thugs)** jumps them. Gilimart flees.
   - GM should **steer the fight so the PCs are knocked out without serious harm**.
   - Thugs take **bags/packs but deliberately leave pockets & purses**, then **fence the loot at the Arkadum auction house** without checking if it's magical.
   - **Thug tactics:** at least double the party's numbers; **Slå medvetslös (Knock Unconscious) 15**; surprise the party and auto-win initiative on the 2nd SR via local knowledge; they're in a hurry.
3. **Oväntad hjälp ("Unexpected Help"):** A woman, **Mealin**, helps and brings them to the small boarding house she runs with her husband **Ernandhiz**.
   - Ernandhiz is a reformed member of **Kharynos** (a criminal org exiled when "Vicotnik" took power). He knows their goods can be recovered at the **Arkadum auction house** but won't say how he knows.
4. **Ordningsmakten ("The Law"):** Useless. The city watch is **corrupt** and even frequents the auction house; storming it is pointless without hard evidence.

### Act II — The Auction (*Första, andra, dunk!* = "Going once, twice, sold!")
- A few nights later, the cult holds an auction of "foreign goods" at **Arkadum**, a fence-house run by the **thieves' guild** (kept legally unconnected to crime).
- **Atmosphere & rules of the room:**
  - Auctioneer + armed gladiator-like assistants on a raised podium; a drape hides the lots.
  - **Many "Ormtungor" (Snake-tongues)** salted through the crowd; everyone is armed. **Violence is strongly discouraged** — Ernandhiz warns against it.
  - Bidding is silent/whispered: a finger, a glance, a twitch, a raised hand, or rare shouts. Winner's name/nickname/appearance is noted; goods + payment collected afterward at a counter.
- **Skill cues:**
  - **Upptäcka fara (Detect Danger):** an ugly, menacing mood hangs over the room.
  - **Finna dolda ting / Lyssna, CL –10:** the auctioneer sells off an **old coffer** — the one robbed from the party — for **50 gm**.
  - **Undre världen (Underworld):** most goods are stolen; the PC recognizes bandits, pirates, and rogues.
- **GM option:** run the full auction; let them bid on books, art, magic trinkets, "a pig in a poke," etc. — and **cheat them of some cash**.

### Act III — Reclaiming the Coffer
- **Uthämtning ("Pickup"):** Four counters; "richest first" queue (~1 hour wait). To get their own stolen gear back they must also **buy back the old coffer (50 gm too much)** or get nothing and be thrown out.
- **Den fete ("The Fat One"):** The grotesquely fat, wheezing, saliva-spraying **Barbana di Lautmaz** accosts them, desperate to buy the coffer — offering **100 sm, then 100 gm** for a "worthless" box.
  - He pulls out a purse holding **far more** than 100 gm → PCs should grow suspicious and **keep the coffer**.
  - Give players ~½ minute; if they refuse, he tries to grab it, is easily shoved off, and is left red-faced and cursing.
  - Why: Barbana was tasked to buy the dead magician's coffer at auction but **missed it due to ill health**, and panicked on seeing the PCs with it.

## 4. The Coffer (*Kofferten*)

Old and worn; a simple lock (easy to pick or break). Belonged to the dead necromancer **Khaltem**. Contains magical curiosities, junk — and the item the cult wants:

- **Carved pipe of precious wood** — magic; auto-lights the tobacco and is self-cleaning.
- **Brass telescope (tubkikare)** — see clearly at **150 m**; weighs **3 kg**.
- **Brass whistle** — emits a sound no human hears; **50% chance 1T6 dogs respond**.
- **Erakkart's Forbidden Black Book** — leather-bound; symbol of distorted bones + a sneering skull.
  - Gives a **necromancer 10 XP after 4 weeks of intensive study**.
  - **Anyone else** must **skräckslå 5/5** just from leafing through the dreadful images.
  - Said to exist in only **4 copies**; sellable to the right buyer for **200 gm**.
- **Leather pouch (drawstring)** — **32 bone bits** from various creatures; once belonged to an orc shaman-priest (a *benman*). Orcs consider bone bits holy.

---

## 5. The Artifact — the Demonic Dagger

- **Appearance:** flame-shaped blade of black-shimmering metal; polished black-wood haft shaped like a **claw clutching a black pearl**; inlaid runes that **glow red in moonlight**.
- **Curse / binding:** the **first PC to touch it** forms a magic link with the demon inside; failing that, it bonds with the **lowest-PSY** PC, who is compulsively drawn to touch it. **Only a living possessor can be "chosen."** Its previous owner (the necromancer) was chosen before the cult could complete the ritual.
- **Purpose:** during a long ritual the dagger is **driven into the chosen one's chest**, and the **demon prince's child grows inside the victim's body** — making the dagger a vessel to unleash the demon's offspring onto **Altor** as its deputy.
- **Skill cues:**
  - **Upptäcka fara / Känna magi:** it is evil and brimming with mystic power.
  - **Demonologi (runes):** roughly *"the black soul-eater's spawn shall rule for eternity."*

### Nightmares (*Mardrömmar*)
The chosen PC suffers dreams; **skräckslå 5/–** after each. He wakes cold-sweated, thirsty, and hazy on details.

1. **Night of the choosing** — A misty black forest; a hissing **black dragon with glowing red eyes** breathes fire and hunts him through falling trees; he feels the heat.
2. **After visiting the merchant** — He lies on a cold stone altar; five faceless, half-real men surround him; the altar warms, he can't move, and one raises a **black dagger and stabs it into his chest**.
3. **After the training camp** — Standing waist-deep in dark, stinking water in a tunnel; he hides from robed men in procession; **yellow slit-pupiled eyes** watch and approach as the water rises behind a grating.

## 6. Investigation (*Efterforskning*)

PCs can dig for info on the dagger. Each source needs a successful **Övertala (Persuade) / Muta (Bribe) / Undre världen (Underworld)** roll where relevant.

**Thieves' Guild / Kharynos** (Ernandhiz brokers it; **1T20 sm per fact**):
- The coffer was stolen by the master-thief **Skuggyngel**.
- Rumor: Skuggyngel **lost his mind** and left on a long pilgrimage.
- The coffer's owner was **killed by Skuggyngel in his tower**.

**Magicians' Guild** (**1T10 gm per fact**):
- The dagger is magical, loaded with **unknown** magic.
- It radiates a **demonic presence**.
- A mage can translate the runes (see §5).

**Learned Man** (**1T10 gm per fact**):
- It's a **ritual** dagger, probably **ancient**.
- Surely **several hundred years old**.
- **Antique value ~300 gm** to the right buyer.

**Weaponsmith** (**1T10 sm per fact**):
- **Master-forged**.
- **Not made for combat**.

## 7. The Honest Buyer — Jargaf (*Ett erbjudande*)

- A 3rd-circle cultist, the wealthy ~50-year-old merchant **Jargaf Sauder** (fabrics, spices, slaves; caravans, ~12 trade ships, ~100 employees), is told to **buy the dagger**.
- His envoy, **Liesa Karrak** (a woman in her twenties), invites the PCs to his **walled palace** outside town and even offers to stay as a "hostage" for paranoid players. **No harm is intended.**
- **GM note:** rumors confirm Jargaf is **genuinely honest and generous** — keeps his word, gives to temples, orphanages, and soup kitchens.

### The Palace Visit (*Jargafs palats*)
- Three-story main house + two wings; 3 m stone wall, ~12 elite guards + dogs; wife, sons, ~12 servants, 2 concubines.
- Lavish hospitality: tour of house, garden, wine cellar, guardroom, bathhouse/sauna; guests may help themselves; a **3–4 hour feast** ("food first, business later").
- **Weapon room** (upper floor, with side rooms): **30+ weapons** on walls/ceiling; one side room holds **100+ daggers/knives**. PCs may handle them freely; Jargaf relaxes with just two guards.
  - **Känna magi:** ~a dozen weapons are magical (low values, e.g. **FÖRTROLLA VAPEN E1 without NEXUS**).
  - **Vapenkunskap / Vapenfärdighet / Smide:** all top-grade, deadly, decorated, several master-smithed — a collector's dream.

### Negotiations (*Förhandlingar*)
- Jargaf knows they have the dagger and will pay almost anything — e.g. **~2000 gm, a weapon, or a (low-degree) magic item**.
- Whatever happens, he stays friendly and even gives a parting gift.
- **GM note — if the PCs do something stupid (e.g. attack him):** they'll likely kill him, but he flees if pressed and the guards assume the PCs are merely guests. Consequences:
  - His **family hires bounty-hunters (prisjägare)**;
  - the **city watch actively hunts them**, as does the **Dragon's Society**.

## 8. If Jargaf Fails to Buy It

### Theft attempt (*Stöldförsök*)
Three cultists shadow the party during a quiet between-adventures period (provisioning, training, partying, job-hunting), mapping their routines, then strike when the players grow careless.
- **Upptäcka fara / Skugga:** an uncanny feeling of being followed/watched.
- **Finna dolda ting:** a **tattooed man in baggy grey hooded clothes** is staring at them.

### Daggers in the Night (*Dolkar i natten*)
- A **nighttime ambush**, breaking in via roof/floor/walls (walls cut with tools days ahead).
- PCs are **auto-surprised**; **1T3 SR** to wake and grab weapons (only **1 SR** if a PC explicitly sleeps with their sword and someone kept watch → not surprised).
- **Attackers:** **4 cultists** in only trousers, dragon tattoos visible, a **bound dragon-dagger in each hand**, **drugged and tongues cut out** so they can't talk. A **5th cultist waits outside** to grab the dagger and flee to the temple.
- **GM note:** rig the scene so the cult **gets the dagger** — repeat until they do.

---

## 9. Interlude (*Mellanspel*) — happens "off-screen"

- The cult tries the ritual but botches it: the merchant **Kirisval** (5th 1st-circle member) is used and **transformed over two agonizing months into a demon-hybrid**, which **escapes into the sewers** — which is why the temple can now **only be reached via the sewers**.
- The assassins close in; the cult **massacres the guild's hideout**, leaving only the **Master** alive. He shadows the cult to the temple.
- A **vision** reveals the true "chosen one" is a PC; the Master is sent to bring that PC to the temple **unharmed** for a new ritual.
- **Pacing:** GM's call — at least a week if the PCs are being heavily shadowed.

---

## 10. The Assault & the Master

### The Assault (*Överfallet*) — night
- A cultist lures the PCs (or the chosen one alone) into a violent quarter (theft + chase, a shady "job," a paid courtesan, etc.).
- **Attackers:** **10+ cultists**, of whom **4 wield dragon-daggers** and **2 are fire-spitters** (glass flasks + prepared torches).
- Make it look hopeless at first — then the **Master** wades in, throwing daggers and flanking; together they should beat the cultists.

### The Master (*Mästaren*)
- Dressed all in black, face veiled, only eyes showing; a **dozen daggers** in straps across his body.
- He explains the cult threat and the assassins' guild, then takes the PCs to a **remote training camp**, offering **protection, training with the guild, and a shot at revenge**.
- **Stats:** treat as a **hjältelönnmördare (hero-assassin)**.

---

## 11. Training (*Träning*)

A hidden camp at the edge of civilization. The Master leads all training.

**Daily schedule:**
- Wake at dawn → ~1 hr jogging warm-up → breakfast
- Weapon training (morning) → light midday meal → meditation / psychic training
- Twilight meal → special-skill training (a few hours)
- Every block includes **≥10 min meditation**. Training is brutal; PCs end exhausted.
- Skills drawn from **Tjuvar och Lönnmördare** and **Krigarens handbok** (the Warrior's Handbook).

**Food:** good and rich, herbed for effect — healing, stamina, smithing, and learning boosts; minor side effects (temporary impotence, mild thinning, morning nausea — nothing serious).

**Advancement rule:** each day, each PC rolls a **base-attribute check** for the attribute behind a skill; **success = +1 XP**. This is fast FV growth justified by master tutelage + drugs + meditation, **but no extra bonus XP is given after the adventure.**

### Training events (*Händelser under träningen*)
- **Sparring** vs a throat-cutter or teacher (no damage logged except from fumbles); at the end they may fight the **Master himself** if they wish.
- **Hide-and-seek:** find the Master in the forest while he tries to knock out one PC at a time.
- **Trap gauntlet:** snares, pits, dart-traps, falling logs — get through the mined area.
- **Bear hunt:** subdue an angry old battle-bear (kill or lure it away — it loves honey).
- **Mock attacks:** the Master stages harmless "kills" — grass-blade garrote, harmless poison in food, a "knife" that's a leaf, surprise strikes, etc.

---

## 12. The Counterattack (*Till motattack*)

- After **4 weeks** the cult locates the camp; the Master kills a spy and the attack must go in **before the cult acts**.
- **Strike team:** master-assassins, a teacher, some throat-cutters, and the PCs. **Novices block the streets** outside while the rest hit fast, **mid-night, all in black.**

---

## 13. The Temple (*Templet*)

Three layers: a **false façade**, a **decoy public temple**, then **down through the sewers** to the **real demon temple**.

### Upper (decoy) temple — in the craftsmen's quarter
- Run by genuine-but-clueless 3rd-circle priests/warriors/high-priests; openly charitable.
- Always open; thieves/bandits "vanish" here. No valuables of note.
- **1T20 visitors by day, 1T10 by night.**
- Only real guards: **3 cultists watching the stairs down**.

**Upper-temple map key:** 1 Hall · 2 Antechamber · 3 Priests' Room · 4 Temple Hall · 5 Kitchen & Dining · 6 Privy · 7 Guard Room · 8 Stairs Down. *(scale 2–10 m)*

### The Sewers (*Kloakerna*)
- A vast labyrinth: main channels **3 m wide, 2 m deep** with **1 m ledges**; half-flooded side tunnels **2 m across**.
- Falling in the muck: failed **FYS** → badly nauseous, **–3 to all rolls**.
- Only 1st/2nd-circle cultists know the route. Others need **Spåra / Finna dolda ting / Skugga, ≥4 times**; each failure = lost + an encounter:
  - **Floating corpse** of a thief murdered by greedy comrades — rat-/worm-eaten, face-down; turning it over → **skräckslå 5/–**.
  - **1T2 cultists** with ordinary daggers.
  - **A paranoid fugitive thief** fleeing the Ormtungor (said too much to a bounty-hunter); just wants to be left alone, won't trust the PCs.
  - **Gas pocket:** tunnel dips; gas ignites at the least spark → **1T10 KP**.
  - **Rat swarm** (panicked by the hybrid): hundreds of rats; **STO vs 22 on the resistance table** or be dragged into the water; success = **1T4 dmg**, fail = double; **25% chance of infection**.

### Demon-Hybrid (*Demonhybriden*) — mandatory encounter
- Attacks anyone it doesn't recognize (ignores cultists). Warning: the **water seems to boil with bubbles**, then it lunges out.
- **Everyone skräckslå 15/5**; it **auto-wins initiative on the 1st SR** and tries to devour victims.
- The **chosen PC** recognizes it from his nightmares → **paralyzed by fear 1T6 SR**.

### Demon Temple (*Demontemplet*) — room by room
- **Entry:** a sturdy wooden door with a small grilled slot. (GM sets the cultist count; subtract any already killed.)
- **Vakt (Guard):** small room, 3 chairs + old table; **3 cultists** talking loudly.
- **Fängelseceller (Cells):** corridor of **13 cells**, mostly empty (one holds prisoners' belongings); grilled doors **(tål 110 KP)**, bolt locks **(svårighetsgrad 16)**.
- **Förrum (Antechamber):** where visitors hang clothes/gear.
- **Förråd (Storage):** silver candlesticks, wax candles, linen cloths, ritual daggers, etc.
- **Samlingssal (Assembly Hall):** seats ~50; stone-mosaic **black-dragon** floor, whitewashed walls/ceiling. If alerted, the **big fight happens here**; normally **1T10 cultists** present.
- **Helgedom (Sanctuary):** double doors, **locked (svårighetsgrad 17)**, iron-reinforced wood **(tål 120 KP)**, magically warded — **each KP of damage triggers ELD E1** (green demonic flames). Domed room, whitewashed walls, **mosaic pentagram** floor with mystic symbols; **all magic rituals happen here**.
- **Sovrum (Dormitory):** corridor of **25 sleeping-cells** (bed, table, chair); home of the **2nd circle**.
- **Träningssal (Training Hall):** large; training weapons/tools on walls; stamped-earth floor.
- **Matsal (Dining Hall):** tiny; ~10 people; meals in shifts.
- **Kök (Kitchen):** small, chimney-vented; well-stocked larder; a **lame, injured cultist cook** who attacks intruders with a **big cleaver (köttyxa)**.
- **Den första cirkelns rum (First Circle's Room):** combined sleep/work room for the inner circle; **Prenikaft** usually lives here. Holds the cult's **treasure chest** — combination lock **(svårighetsgrad 19, tål 120 KP)** inside an iron strongbox **(tål 230 KP)**, ~**50 kg**, containing **2000 gm, 10000 sm, 1T100 gems, 1T100 rings & lesser jewelry**.
- **Avträde (Privy):** *"Do you really want a description?"*

**Sewer-temple map key:** 1 Entrance · 2 Guard Room · 3 Cells · 4 Antechamber · 5 Storage · 6 Dining Hall · 7 Kitchen · 8 Assembly Hall · 9 Sanctuary · 10 Dormitory · 11 Training Hall · 12 First Circle's Room · 13 Privies.

## 14. The Cult's Weapons (*Drakens vapen*)

- **Dragon-tooth daggers (draktänder/drakdolkar):** very rare, costly; **1T6 damage**, **BV 25**; inscribed with signs & symbols. The cult owns **16** of them.
- **Fire-spitting:** holding a flammable liquid in the mouth and spitting it past a burning torch → spray fire for **2T6 damage**, **range 2 squares**; victim keeps burning **1T2 SR**.

## 15. NPCs / Stat Guidance (*Spelledarpersoner*)

The author leaves most stats to the GM but gives anchors:

- **1st-circle cultists:** demonologist **Prenikaft** + merchants **Herig, Katugi, Follo** (5th member **Kirisval** = now the hybrid).
  - **Prenikaft:** demon abilities **Demonbesudling (Demon-Defilement)** and **Ökat skydd (Increased Protection, 5-pt absorbing skin)**; **FV 18 Demonologi** + many spells.
  - **The merchants:** stat as an *extraordinary cultist* (see *Tjuvar och Lönnmördare*).
- **2nd-circle cultists:** stat as a *newly created cultist*.
- **3rd-circle cultists:** not real cultists — ordinary deceived people, no special professional skill.
- **The Master:** stat as a *hjältelönnmördare (hero-assassin)*.
- **Demon-Hybrid:** humanoid with an oversized head; limbs become strong **tentacles**; body covered in long black spikes; green slime oozes from ~100 holes. Demon abilities: **Skräckslå 15/5**, **Demonvomering (Demon-Vomit)**, **Förlänga lemmar (Lengthen Limbs)**, **+ one of the GM's choice**.

## 17. Clarifications on the Two Criminal Networks

The text uses several overlapping names; here's how they fit together:

- **Ormtungorna ("the Snake-tongues")** = the **thieves' guild**. They run the **Arkadum** auction house, receive and fence the thugs' loot, and salt the auction room with armed members. The fugitive thief in the sewers is on the run *from* them. (So the "Ormtungor" standing guard at the auction are simply guild members.)
- **Kharynos** = a separate, **exiled criminal organization** (driven out when "Vicotnik" took power). **Ernandhiz**, the boarding-house keeper, is a reformed Kharynos man — which is how he knows the auction-house underworld and can broker information for the party.
- **Tjuvgillet** ("the thieves' guild") in the text simply refers to Ormtungorna.
- These crooks are **not** the cult. The whole plot turns on the **Dragon's Society's brutal ritual murders being mistaken for, or competing with, the assassins' guild's "legitimate" business.**

## 18. GM Running Aids *(organizational layer — synthesized from the text above, not extra source material)*

### The adventure spine (one line per beat)
1. Party can't find lodging → conned by "Gilimart" → **beaten and robbed** in an alley (GM rigs a non-lethal KO).
2. **Mealin & Ernandhiz** rescue them → point them to the **Arkadum auction**.
3. At the auction they must **buy back their own coffer** (+50 gm) and rebuff **Barbana the Fat**.
4. The coffer holds the **demonic dagger**; whoever touches it first is **"chosen"** and begins the **nightmares**.
5. Party investigates; honest merchant **Jargaf** offers to buy it (palace visit, feast, weapon room).
6. **If they keep it** → shadowed → **night ambush by mute, drugged dragon-cultists** who seize the dagger.
7. **Interlude (off-screen):** botched ritual creates the **demon-hybrid** (now in the sewers); cult massacres the assassins, leaving only the **Master**.
8. Cult comes for the **chosen PC** → big street **assault** → the **Master** saves them → **4 weeks of training** at the hidden camp.
9. Cult finds the camp → **counterattack**: storm the decoy temple → down into the **sewers** → fight the **hybrid** → assault the **demon temple** → **Sanctuary** showdown.

### The author's built-in "rails" (where the GM is told to steer outcomes)
- The alley fight: **steer it so the PCs are knocked out without real harm**.
- The night raid: **rig dice so the cult actually takes the dagger** (repeat until they do).
- Jargaf is **functionally invulnerable to fair play** — attacking him just brings down bounty-hunters + watch + cult.
- The **demon-hybrid is a mandatory encounter** in the sewers.

### Who wants what
| Party | Goal | Lever on the PCs |
|---|---|---|
| **Dragon's Society (cult)** | Recover the dagger; complete the ritual using the chosen PC | Theft, ambush, kidnap-the-chosen-one |
