# Melindors Återkomst — Adventure Summary & Reference

**System:** *Drakar och Demoner: Ereb Altor* (Target Games / Äventyrsspel, 1991)
**Series:** *Den nidländska reningen* ("The Nidlandish Cleansing"), Part 2 of 3
**Designer:** Johan Sjöberg
**Timeframe:** Spring, year 613, after Vicotnik the Arch-magician's world-conquest crusade has begun.

---

## Background — What Has Happened (recap of Part 1, *Helvetesfortet*)

The player characters (PCs), with old mercenary friends from Bedorum in the Hynsolgian county of Mergilia, learned that Vicotnik's long-prophesied "cleansing" was finally coming — and soon. They were sent to spy on and sabotage **Helvetesfortet** ("the Hell Fort"), a massive war-fortress on the Nidlandish side of the Grynnerbergen mountains.

On the way they met the elf prince **Talyros** and the pegasus **Enulisor**, whom they rescued. Their dwarf-friend guide **Ehrman Wall** tried to lead them through the dwarven realm of Tårat-Khôn but was refused at the gate; they went through abandoned dwarf mines instead. There they clashed with svartfolk (dark folk) mining iron and forging weapons for Vicotnik. Fleeing, they entered an ancient burial vault of a king from before the age of humans and elves, fought his mummy, and took a ~50 cm staff inscribed "**Portens Väktare**" ("the Gate's Warden"). The staff transformed into a being that promised aid "when all hope is out," then reverted to staff form.

Emerging, they were captured and brought to Helvetesfortet, thrown in a cell, and hauled before its overcommander **Achatoth**. At that moment the entire fortress exploded — right after Enulisor and friends snatched the PCs to safety. The blast also opened the gate for **Bestraffaren** ("the Punisher"), an ancient demigod summoned by the Demon-spire, who entered Altor's dimension to offer Vicotnik his services. The PCs believed Achatoth died in the explosion. **He did not.**

## The Setting / Geography

**Landori** — A vast central-Ereb forest of elves and other fae, ruled by the elf-king **Pargal**. Home to the rare *gyllenlönn* (golden maple), whose sap glows on its own, and the lucky black *Midnattshjorten* (Midnight Stag). The forest has a living soul; anything that happens reaches Pargal instantly. The elves loathe svartfolk but welcome most others, trade with the Nidabergen dwarves, and are arming against Vicotnik.

**Berendien** — On the Tolan peninsula, the country the adventure aims to save. Ruled by the ailing, heirless King **Frekvand** from the capital **Entika**. Cheerful, hospitable people (~170 cm, dark-haired). The king long ago disowned his daughter (Melindor) for associating with commoners. Religion centers on *De unga gudarna* (the Young Gods), headed by the over-god **Sholak**; *Den lysande vägen* ("the Shining Way") is also strong here.

**Hynsolge** — A war-torn land on Krunhalvön, nominally ruled by King Mefimor IV, split into 13 counties. A civil war since year 604 (sparked by a famine and the nobility's refusal to help the peasants) left it too weak to resist Vicotnik. By the adventure's start almost all of it has fallen; only **Anarghel Ståhlkänga**'s county of **Sirigon** (capital: the port **Fervidun**) was holding out — until a week before play, when the **Röda klingan** ("Red Blade") orc mercenaries betrayed it to **Echotun**, Vicotnik's 12th disciple.

**Nidland** — Vicotnik's homeland behind "Den stora muren" (the Great Wall), 100% human, ruled as a dictatorship by **Vicotnik Ärkemagikern** and his 12 disciples who wield the dreaded *mörkermagi* (dark magic). Vicotnik arrived from Akrogal in 468 and prophesied he'd launch his conquest "a dozen dozen years" later (≈612). It came a year late, in 613, with Bestraffaren at his side. His armies can "brainwash" defeated peoples into joining them, so his hordes constantly grow.

## The Plot, in Detail

### The Awakening (Äventyrets början)
The PCs wake in Pargal's living **Trädslott** (tree-castle) in Landori after three months of healing sleep, tended by Talyros and his younger brother **Lilarion**. They're fed in the Hall of Flowers, given enchanted elf cloaks (+10 CL to *Gömma sig* outdoors), and taken through Pargal's magical garden.

**Pargal's Garden** includes a lowerable platform, a living-tree bridge, a waterfall, a chapel of flowers, the grove containing his memory-tarn (a mirror pool in which events are "stored" and replayed; breaking the surface destroys a memory and jolts Pargal psychically), and the **Himmelseken** ("Sky-oak"), a silver-shimmering tree holding a **golden nut**.

### Meeting Pargal
Pargal reveals a prophecy: the PCs are the only ones who can save Ereb. Using his tarn he shows them Helvetesfortet's destruction, Bestraffaren's release, and Vicotnik's conquest of Krun, most of Hynsolge, and southern Cereval in three months. He charges them to fetch the **golden nut** from the Himmelseken; given to King Frekvand, it will instantly cure him. He recites a dwarven prophecy: roughly, *"When the Righteous one returns, the days of Judgment will dawn for those who do not forgive and who close their eyes to the pure."* (This foreshadows the dark ending.)

### Journey to Entika
Talyros escorts them three days through Landori (giving them a flask of luminous golden-maple sap), with possible encounters: playing elves, a flirtatious dryad, an elf village, harassing will-o'-wisps. Then a day's ride into Berendien — a border toll post (5 sm/person, 10 sm/animal), then King's hunting grounds. Optional scenes: bandits sacking a farm, scholars returning from Entika's university, royal foresters, a friendly old woman's cottage, a dangerous bear. Finally they glimpse Entika's copper roofs across the **Opadalen** valley.

### Entika
(≈20,000 inside the walls; famous for university, wine, and trade.) The PCs go to the castle, where the king's advisor **Merkato** (secretly Achatoth in disguise) blocks their audience but smoothly arranges free lodging at the inn **Lammet och Grisen** ("The Lamb and the Pig") — really so he can track them.

### The Nightly Theft
That night Nidlandish spies break in and steal the golden nut no matter what precautions the PCs take. Someone glimpses **Kharman**'s face (a man from the PCs' Nya Armén days). His cloak snags on the window and tears, leaving a scrap bearing the sigil of **Mäster Gulfven**, an exclusive tailor.

### The Investigation
Tracing the sigil to Gulfven's shop, then the university register (or city archive), the PCs learn the cloak's owner consorts with **Harvan Rulhan**, an Akrogalian "scholar" living at **Domaregatan 132**. A neighbor (cabbie Joser Kvint) reports dark-clad figures sneaking in and out at odd hours. Harvan's house is detailed room by room; the nut is hidden in a **secret compartment in the fireplace of Harvan's bedroom**, with gold, silver, and two red gems. Living there: Harvan, Kharman, and spies **Petlon** and **Ytrin**.

### On the Run
Once the PCs investigate (and perhaps recover the nut), Merkato brands them Nidlandish spies. Wanted posters go up (200 gm reward). As the city watch closes in, a wagon driven by a teenager named **Pontar** crashes through and whisks them away to a forest cottage. *(Alternative path: if captured, they're jailed, then freed by an anonymous note and the same wagon.)*

### Hagras's Cottage
The bearded man inside is **Hagras Rådgivaren**, the king's former court magician of 20 years, ousted when Merkato arrived and began having loyal courtiers executed as "spies." Hagras explains Merkato has tricked the dying king into granting Vicotnik's armies "free passage" through northern Berendien — which Hagras is sure is a trap. Berendien's only hope, he says, is **Melindor**, the king's disowned daughter, a brilliant resistance leader last seen near Fervidun in Hynsolge. He sends the PCs to **Hevken** to find his old friend **Ulvan**, who can ferry them across the Echatian Sea.

### To Hevken & Across the Sea
A roughly week-long trek through central Berendien past various nobles' lands. Optional encounters: a jester troupe (with a love-triangle subplot involving a fire-eater and "Strong Adolf"), a battlefield where defeated soldiers shake a magician's hand and receive a **cross-shaped scar** (Vicotnik's mark of coming control), a war camp with iron-collared enslaved dwarves building a giant black machine, an orc/dark-elf roadblock, and a temple of the goddess Delitha that heals those fighting Vicotnik. At Hevken, the unexpectedly **old (65), sour fisherman Ulvan** agrees to sail them once he hears Hagras sent them, dropping them near the burning capital **Orkovia**.

### Ulvan's Death
After landing, Ulvan sails off — and the PCs watch a Nidlandish patrol boat with a ballista impale and kill him, then riddle his corpse with arrows. They must flee inland.

### To Fervidun
A ~3-day march through devastated Hynsolge meant to be portrayed as horrifically as possible: women and children hurled from city walls; jet-black mörkerriddare cutting down fleeing townsfolk; a necromancer's village of zombies and skeletons; their old comrade **Argwin** fighting four Nidlandish soldiers (whom they can rescue and recruit); more handshake-scar battlefields; the dwarf-machine camp.

### The Resistance
Near Fervidun, the PCs are sought out by **Riorik** and others from the resistance, hidden in an improved bear-den HQ (~70 fighters). Riorik reveals Melindor was captured days ago — likely held in Fervidun's castle. He proposes the PCs help free her.

### The Rescue (Fritagningen)
The PCs, Argwin, and Riorik infiltrate **Fervidun** (now mostly ruins; castle intact on its island; river Widja splits Old and New city). Best entry is via the river, or disguised as dark knights/orcs. The castle (two 10 m walls, gatehouse, four towers, two-storey keep with banquet hall, trophy hall, chapel, library, prison, treasury) is detailed extensively. Melindor is **not** in the prison cells — she's in the **catacombs beneath the castle**, reached by a secret door behind the headless **Fingal Orchnackare statue** in the chapel (press the center of the seven-pointed golden star).

### The Catacombs
Generated randomly via a 1T20-meters-per-section corridor table, with a 15% chance per section of 1T6 undead (skeletons and zombies made from the dead). At the end: a **pentagram room** guarded by the demon **Qullog**, then cells — Melindor is in cell (c), broken and terrified, calming only when she recognizes Riorik.

### Flight from Fervidun
Escaping back through the catacombs (exits in the castle, the harbor, or the Fingal temple), pursued by **Echotun and Ruggtash**. Above ground they witness an enormous black flying galleon drawn by shaven-headed silver elves with black wings and horse-shaped brands — a grim sight.

### Back to Entika
Melindor reluctantly agrees to return and rule; Riorik refuses her offer to come with her and stays to lead the resistance (a tearful parting). The skipper **Hjarn** ferries them back. They find Hagras's cottage burned to ash. Melindor reveals a **secret passage** from a hidden garden pavilion (lusthus) outside Entika straight into the king's bedroom.

### Visit to a Dead Man
Through a peephole they see Merkato leaving the king's room. The king lies dead — *Första hjälpen* confirms death, *Läkekonst* says at least three days. The golden nut does nothing; **the king is truly dead.** The PCs realize the horrible truth and chase Merkato to his tower.

### The Final Battle (Slutstriden)
In his black-walled laboratory, Merkato reveals his staff, transforms, and the PCs recognize **Achatoth** — believed dead at Helvetesfortet. He attacks with icy winds and a fireworks-illusion (PCs must pass a PSY roll or be paralyzed). Once he's beaten, **Hagras** arrives with royal soldiers and academics, having organized an inside resistance against Merkato.

### Epilog — The Chilling Twist
Melindor is crowned with great ceremony and thanks the PCs warmly. As she waves goodbye, they see a **cross-shaped scar in her palm** — the same mark Vicotnik's victims bore. Perhaps they have just helped Vicotnik along, after all. *Who knows?*

## Characters (with stats)

### Pargal
Elf-king of Landori, 4,271 years old, a master animist who built much of his tree-castle. Tall, slim, snow-white hair, cat-pupiled blue eyes; once a great archer, now a calm philosopher who only bristles at Vicotnik himself.

> **Magiker (animist).** Långbåge 22, Överlevnad (skog) 35, Botanik 35, Zoologi 35, Drogkunskap 28, Animism 37 (all spells at skill +1T20).

### Kharman
Achatoth's loyal right-hand spy from Akrogal, ~170 cm, agile, turbaned, black-haired, a nasty drunk; would die before betraying his mission.

> STY 15 · SMI 17 · KAR 12 · STO 10 · INT 13 · KP 12 · FYS 13 · PSY 11 · SB 0 · Age 29

### Echotun
Vicotnik's 12th disciple, commander of Fervidun; a "warrior-mage," weakest at magic among the disciples but a superb swordsman and leader of the mörkerriddare; ~2 m, bald, foul-breathed, utterly merciless, a torturer.

### Atachan
Echotun's black unicorn (golden horn/hooves), with him ~20 years, armored in black metal.

### Qullog
A demon from Inferno's 8th circle (Kaos), summoned and bound in the catacombs' pentagram to guard prisoners; now driven mad by confinement. ~210 cm, dark-violet scales, lizard head with a steel-like horn, crab-claws for hands, spiked tail-club.

> STY 25 · SMI 24 · KAR 1 · STO 21 · INT 15 · KP 22 · FYS 23 · PSY 17 · SB +1T6 · fairly old
> Natural armor abs 9. 2 claws (1T8), tail-snap (1T8+1), bite (1T8, ½ SB), gore (1T8), kick (1T6), fire-breath Eldkvast (2T10, up to 8×/day). Two attacks per round; Skräckslå 3/0.

### Melindor
The disowned Berendian princess, 31, a self-confident archer, swordfighter, tactician, and resistance leader; also a flutist. Medium height, brown curly hair, nut-brown eyes. Loves Riorik.

> STY 13 · SMI 15 · KAR 15 · STO 10 · INT 13 · KP 11 · FYS 12 · PSY 12 · SB 0 · Age 31
> **Bard.** Långbåge 18, Bredsvärd 15, Liten sköld 14, Taktik 18, Överklasstil 16, Spela flöjt B3, Rida 16, Överlevnad (skog) 16.
> Has nothing on her when found.

### Merkato / Achatoth
Vicotnik's **5th disciple**, master of dark magic, mentalism, and necromancy; survived Helvetesfortet and replaced the real Merkato to subvert Berendien. As Achatoth: black-bearded, bald, red/black tattoos. As Merkato: clean-shaven with a mustache and shoulder-length hair — an appearance created by a gold **neck-ring** from Vicotnik. Calm and still; only bristles at his subordinates' incompetence.

> STY 10 · SMI 12 · KAR 14 · STO 12 · INT 17 · KP 11 · FYS 9 · PSY 25 · SB 0 · Age 54
> **Mörkermagiker.** Mörkermagi 19, Nekromanti 18, Mentalism 16, Administration/juridik 17. All available spells (Mörkermagi S 1T6+14, Nekromanti S 1T10+5, Mentalism S 1T6+7, General S 1T10+5).
> **Magic item:** a 2 m staff of human bone topped with a one-eyed human skull set with a black gem (**SKYDD E5, LADDNING E5, PERMANENS, NEXUS**); teleports to his hand within 100 m.

## Notable Items

- **The Golden Nut (gyllene nöt)** from the Himmelseken — the quest macguffin; instantly cures King Frekvand if given to him *while alive* (useless once he's dead — the cruel catch).
- **Golden-maple sap (gyllenlönn)** — self-luminous, gifted by Talyros.
- **Enchanted elf cloak** — +10 CL to *Gömma sig* outdoors, one per PC.
- **Gråklinga** — Riorik's master-forged sword (BV 15, 1T10+3).
- **Echotun's enchanted bronze plate** and **point-and-cast war-club**.
- **Merkato/Achatoth's bone staff** (skull with one black-gemmed eye, teleporting).
- **Hagras's lightning-staff** and his book *Vägen till Multiversum*.
- **"Portens Väktare"** — the staff/entity from Part 1 that destroyed Helvetesfortet and freed **Bestraffaren** (the Punisher), Vicotnik's new demigod ally.
