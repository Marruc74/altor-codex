Berendien is a land where life follows the rhythm of the soil. Across its rolling fields, farmers rise with the light and work until the sun sinks low, their labor shaping the harvest that feeds both village and town. The countryside stretches wide, marked by wheat, barley, and the quiet presence of farmhouses that have stood for generations. There is no grandeur here, only endurance and the steady passing of seasons.

Beyond the fields stands Kandra, a place where the quiet of the land gives way to movement and noise. Merchants call out over crowded streets, carts roll through mud and stone, and guards keep watch as trade flows in from distant regions. It is a town built on exchange, where every road leads to another story.

To the north, the land changes. The elfforests of Landori rise in shadow and silence at the border. Few enter, and fewer return with answers. It is said the elves still watch from within, unseen but never absent, keeping their distance as the world of men continues just beyond the trees.

## Geography

Berendien is located in the northern part of Tolanhav Island.  
The realm lies north of Felicien and south of the great elven forests of Landori.

## People

The spoken language is Berendian.  
It is a Joric language, related to others in the region.

## Government

Berendien is a feudal monarchy.

Power is organized roughly as follows:

- King – supreme ruler
- Grand Dukes – govern large regions
- Counts and other nobles – govern local areas

The political system closely resembles those of Zorakin and Kardien.

## Economy

Berendien is primarily an agricultural land.

Common crops include:

- Wheat
- Barley
- Oats
- Rye

Other notes:

- Livestock farming is mainly practiced by wealthy landowners.
- The country has at times been at war with Felicien.

The economy is therefore typically feudal and agrarian.

## Religion

The dominant religion is the worship of the Young Gods.

This places Berendien within the same religious tradition as many other realms in Ereb Altor.
