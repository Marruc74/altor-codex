Jorduashur lies in the far north of Ereb Altor, where the sea is cold and the land resists all comfort. It is a place shaped by wind, salt, and stone, where few gather and fewer remain soft. Along the coasts, longhouses stand against the storms, and ships are as vital as bread. Inland, the Kardforest stretches deep and dangerous, filled with creatures and old powers that keep most folk near the shore.

The land is divided among four jarls, each ruling in their own way, bound loosely under a king whose voice carries only as far as the others allow. In Halle, they meet to settle matters that affect them all, though unity is never certain.

Here, a person belongs first to their kin. Family and clan decide fate, justice, and survival. There is no standing army, but when war comes, the people rise. Their ships travel far, trading, raiding, and returning with stories and wealth. Jorduashur endures not through comfort, but through strength.

## Overview

Jorduashur is a cold and rugged barbarian land in Ereb Altor. The land is sparsely populated, with a harsh climate shaped by warrior culture, trade, and seafaring.

- **Population:** approx. 30,000
- **Population Breakdown:**
  - 50% humans
  - 33% svart and stenfolk
  - 10% elves
  - 7% dwarves
- **Capital:** Halle
- **Head of State:** Elfhelm Korp
- **Government:** Union of four jarldoms
- **Exports:** salt, amber, metals, dwarven craftsmanship
- **Imports:** luxury goods, textiles, plundered goods
- **Religion:** Aesir worship
- **Military:** no standing army, but strong levy (up to 30%)

## Geography

Jorduashur is a peninsula north of Ransard.

### Key Features

- rugged coastlines
- stormy climate
- deep inland forests (the Kardforest)
- very few inland settlements
- most people live along the coast

### The Kardforest

The forest is dangerous and inhabited by:

- elves
- orc tribes
- minotaurs
- giants

## The Four Jarldoms

The land consists of four more or less independent regions.

### 1. Allnemark

- southernmost region
- most densely populated
- capital Halle
- major trade center
- wealthiest area

Halle is also the only major city in Jorduashur.

### 2. Sigsdal

- cold and stormy coastline
- many dwarven settlements
- known for minerals and mining
- frequent conflicts between groups

**Main settlement:** Tarkens Krök

### 3. Nordal

- youngest jarldom
- poor and isolated
- small farms along the coast
- frequent raiding

Clan ties and family bonds are especially important here.

### 4. Krakelid

- founded through conquest
- located along the Drakrännan coast
- city of Krakelid
- strong focus on trade and raiding

## Society

### Clan Society

The most important social unit is:

**kin → family → homestead**

The kin group:

- provides protection
- enforces justice
- resolves conflicts

### Language

The people speak **Jordiska**, a barbaric language related to Ransardian.

### Government

Formally, Jorduashur is a kingdom, but in practice:

- four independent jarls rule
- the king has limited power
- jarls meet in the great assembly in Halle

Major decisions are made there.

## Economy

Jorduashur is a seafaring culture.

### Common Ships

- **Knarr** (merchant vessel)
- **Dragonskepp** (warship and long-distance vessel)

### Trade Partners

- Trakorien
- Felicien
- Soluna
- other barbarian lands

## Military

There is no standing army.

Instead:

- each jarl maintains a **hird** (around 100 warriors)
- a **levy** can be raised when needed (up to 30% of the population)

## Religion

The people worship the Aesir, including:

- **Wodur** – chief of the gods
- **Elokir** – god of the sea
- **Thôrm** – god of war
- **Frajir** – goddess of fertility
- **Grahir** – god of beauty

Offerings are often made in the form of:

- livestock
- war spoils

## History

- Dwarves arrived first for mining around 1500 years before present
- humans settled later
- expansion and colonization followed around 800 years before present
- a golden age of raiding and trade
- later, raiding declined while trade increased

Many Jorduashur people became sailors and merchants in foreign lands.
