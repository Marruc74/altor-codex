Ransard stands in the west where mountains cut the sky and cold winds shape the people as much as the land. The kingdom is held together by clans, each bound by loyalty and pride, yet all sworn to a single king who rules from Kapahlt. Life is harsh and direct. Strength is admired and weakness remembered. Villages cling to ridges and valleys while warriors train from youth, learning the rhythm of shield and spear. The Kardbergen rise like a wall in the east, home to the griffins that have become the symbol of Ransard’s power. From these heights riders take to the skies, turning war into something swift and sudden. Trade exists but raiding defines their presence along distant coasts. Their gods promise rebirth through battle, and many choose the sword over retreat. Ransard is not a land of comfort but of endurance, where honor carries more weight than gold and every generation is shaped by conflict.

## Overview: Kingdom of Ransard

### Basic Facts

Population: approx. 130,000  
Demographics: 85% humans, 15% darkfolk  
Capital: Kapahlt  
Government: Clan-based monarchy  
Ruler: Vortiger (king), at the time Argald II  
Religion: Worship of the Æsir  
Military: Very strong, especially known for flying griffin cavalry

The economy is based on:

Exports: rock salt, metals, furs  
Imports: precious metals, grain, plunder

---

### Geography

Ransard lies in western Ereb. Its core consists of three main regions:

- Nordland
- Ransland
- Sydland

Later additions:

- Timora
- Alerien

Key geographical features:

- The Kardbergen mountains in the east, rising to nearly 4000 meters
- Forested highlands
- Coastlines and peninsulas

Griffins live in the Kardbergen, and the Ransarders later learn to tame and use them in war.

---

### The People: The Ransarders

#### Appearance

- Taller than average (around 180 cm)
- Strongly built
- Light skin
- Often blue eyes
- Men wear thick beards

#### Clothing

- Colorful wool garments
- Each clan has its own patterns

Typical clothing:

- Linen shirt
- Jacket
- Heavy trousers
- Merrlokh, a long cloth wrapped around the body

---

### Weapons

Common weapons:

- Seax (short sword)
- Tarque (round shield)
- Spear
- Sometimes glamorg (two-handed sword)

Archers are also present and considered highly skilled.

---

### Culture and Values

Ransard society is strongly shaped by a warrior ideal.

Typical traits:

- Proud
- Harsh
- Honest
- Honor-bound
- Brave

Key cultural aspects:

- Strength and courage are highly valued
- Cowardice is a great shame
- Skill in battle brings status
- A warrior is often judged by how many enemies they have slain

They tend to view other peoples as weaker or less civilized.

---

### Social Structure

#### Clans

Society is divided into clans of 50 to 500 people.

- Led by a clan chief
- Strong internal loyalty
- Each clan controls its own territory

Clans function almost like small local principalities.

#### The Vortiger (King)

- Supreme war leader
- Highest judge
- Oversees trade and taxation
- Rules from Kapahlt

The Vortiger holds great power but depends on the support of the clan chiefs.

---

### Laws

Ransard laws are considered unusually humane compared to other lands.

Examples:

- Prisoners of war are not tortured
- Defeated enemies are treated with respect
- Slaves may receive acceptable treatment

However, personal offenses are punished severely:

- Rape
- Adultery
- Defamation

Punishment is often death or exile.

---

### Trade and Economy

Ransarders are not known as major traders.

Instead:

- Raiding expeditions are common
- Fast ships are used
- Coastal cities around Ereb are frequent targets

They prefer quick raids rather than prolonged wars of conquest.

---

### Military

#### Tactics

Despite their aggressive culture, Ransard forces fight in a disciplined manner.

Typical formation:

Harhærn

- Wedge-shaped unit
- Flank warriors with two-handed swords
- Archers positioned behind

When the wedge breaks enemy lines, gaps open for additional units to surge through.

#### Griffins

Ransard’s most feared military asset.

- Tamed in the Kardbergen
- Used as flying cavalry
- Extremely fast and difficult to counter

Most clans possess at least one trained griffin.

---

### Religion

The Ransarders worship the Æsir, the same pantheon as in Jorduashur.

A central belief:

- Warriors who die in battle are reborn in the realm of the gods

This often leads them to seek death in battle rather than flee.

---

### History (Brief)

Key periods:

- 9th century  
  Barbarian tribes migrate into the region

- 27 B.O.  
  Ransard is unified under Omric the Great

- 1–10 A.O.  
  Major war of independence in Belgoth

- 176  
  Timolerien founded east of Ransard

- 370  
  Second Ransard War

- 410  
  Crusade against Palinor

- 458–462  
  Ransard controls most of Saphyna

- 497  
  Third Ransard War

- 508  
  The Millennium of Griffins, Argald Omric successfully tames many griffins

- 7th century A.O.  
  Conflicts with Alerien and Trakorien

- 610 A.O.  
  The kingdom stands on the brink of another great war
