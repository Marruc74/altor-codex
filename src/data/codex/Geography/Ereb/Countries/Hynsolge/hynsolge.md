The old roads of Hynsolge do not welcome travelers. They watch them. Stone walls lean inward as if listening, and the air carries the weight of decisions already made. This is a land where loyalty is worn like armor, and suspicion settles in the bones. Men walk with steady steps and heavy gazes, their drooping moustaches marking them as sons of a hard country shaped by wind, war, and quiet endurance. The king still holds his throne, but the realm bends under strain. Noble houses whisper and tighten their grip, while common folk endure in silence, caught between duty and survival. In Orkovia, the heart of the realm, streets are narrow and damp, and nothing is ever fully forgotten. Every stone remembers who stood, who fell, and who chose to remain. Hynsolge does not shout its strength. It endures it.

## Overview

Hynsolge is a kingdom in eastern Ereb, shaped by a long standing civil war between the king and the nobility. The land is deeply divided politically, religiously, and culturally, despite sharing a common historical foundation.

Population: approx. 110,000  
Capital: Orkovia  
Government: Monarchy (King Mefimor IV)  
Religions: Fingal Cult and the Parbagian Faith

## Geography

Hynsolge is a rugged and forested land, with several large rivers and mountain ranges. The forests are dense and at times dangerous, and the varied landscapes have shaped both the mindset and lifestyle of its people. Multiple regions and settlements exist, with Orkovia as the largest center.

## Cities

Orkovia is the capital and long standing seat of power. Other important cities include:

- Brangoria – former capital, heavily fortified
- Gerdlik – known for silver mines
- Krun – fortified settlement
- Fervidun – religious center
- Greyburg – trade city

Cities are often fortified and politically divided.

## The People

The Hynsolgers are generally short, around 165 to 170 cm, with dark hair and sturdy builds. They are known for their temperament, proud, quick to anger, and driven by honor, but also for their cultural depth.

The language is Hynsolgian, closely related to Pranagi.

## Politics

The kingdom is weakened by:

- Central royal authority under pressure
- Rebellious barons
- Ongoing civil war
- A strained economy

The nobility has historically held strong influence. The crown seeks to reclaim control through the New Army.

## Religion

Two main religious traditions exist:

**Fingal Cult**  
The state religion. A pantheon of gods with strong support among the common people.

**Parbagian Faith**  
An alternative pantheon, more popular among the nobility. The religious divide mirrors the political conflict.

## History (brief)

- Origins in Parbagian shamanism
- Rise of the Fingal Cult
- Centuries of power struggles
- Civil war, plague, and uprisings
- Comets and omens interpreted as divine signs
- Establishment of the New Army

The history is violent, marked by religious shifts and constant struggles for power.

## Tone and Theme

Hynsolge feels like a land:

- In constant conflict
- Marked by deep historical wounds
- Where religion and politics are tightly intertwined
- Where honor and pride carry as much weight as law

It is a kingdom that has not fallen, but is always close to the edge.
