# The Hersch

![Fields of hersch on the slopes of Beyural](/codex/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Erebos/Hersch.jpg "Fields of hersch on the slopes of Beyural")

#### Of Its Nature
The hersch is a grain unique to Beyural, growing tall in dense golden fields across the island's fertile slopes. It is related to wheat, yet hardier, shaped by the heavy volcanic soil from which it rises.

#### Of Its Form
Its stalks are thick and resilient, bearing heavy heads of grain that bend low when ripe. The kernels are larger than common wheat, with a pale, warm tone and a firm texture.

#### Of Its Use
Hersch is the foundation of Beyural's harvest. It is ground into flour for bread and porridge, sustaining both towns and travelers. Its yield is steady, and its growth reliable even in difficult seasons.

#### On Its Cultivation
The grain thrives in the island's dark soil, but it demands labor to bring forth. Fields must be turned deep, and harvest comes only after long, careful tending. The work is slow, and often done alongside hornox.

#### Of Quiet Beliefs
Some say hersch draws strength from the land's buried fire, that its roots reach deeper than other crops. It is said that fields left untended too long grow uneven, as if the ground itself shifts beneath them.

#### In the End
The hersch feeds Beyural.

And in its quiet way, it endures as the land does.
