Erebos lies scattered across the Kopparhavet like a chain of quiet promises, each island shaped by wind, water, and the long memory of trade. Here, the sea is not a border but a road, and every harbor is a meeting place for distant voices. Ships come and go with the rhythm of the tides, carrying goods, stories, and the weight of unseen agreements. The people of Erebos live with one eye on the horizon, always aware that fortune may arrive with the next sail or vanish with the last.

The towns rise in pale stone along the coasts, built to endure salt, wind, and time. Markets gather at the heart of daily life, where hands measure grain, weigh metals, and judge the worth of a deal in a single glance. Nothing stands still for long, yet everything follows a pattern shaped by generations who learned to listen to the sea.

There is a quiet order beneath the movement, a balance between risk and reward. In Erebos, survival is not won by strength alone, but by patience, awareness, and the willingness to trust the tide.

## Overview

Erebos is an island realm in the central Copper Sea and functions as a wealthy trade state.  
Its economy and politics are largely controlled by merchant houses rather than nobles or kings.

It is one of Caddo’s greatest rivals in trade and seafaring.

## Geography

Erebos consists of four islands:

### 1. Beyural

- The largest and wealthiest island
- Economic center of the nation
- Contains most cities and harbors
- Capital: Gringul
- Fertile land with some mineral resources

A key export is **sunstones**, a yellow gemstone that contributed greatly to Erebos’ wealth.

### 2. Dakkilo

- The smallest island
- Capital: Nohstril
- Dominated by steppe and grazing land
- Exports wool, meat, and cheese

Nohstril holds over 75% of the island’s population.

### 3. Targero

- The largest but poorest island
- Inhabitants are generally larger and stronger
- Many naval soldiers originate here
- Contains ruins from the old Jorpagna Empire

The mountains are home to orcs, trolls, and other creatures.

### 4. Tolokfe

- A smaller island known as the Island of Cyclopes
- Inhabited by cyclopes
- Extremely dangerous
- Ships avoid the region
- Strong currents and heavy fog make navigation difficult

## Society and Culture

### Population

- Approx. 125,000 inhabitants
- 85% humans
- 15% ducks

### Mentality

Erebosians are often described as:

- Short in stature
- Temperamental
- Talkative
- Strongly trade-oriented

The individual is valued more than lineage or tradition, unlike in feudal societies.

## Politics

Erebos is governed by the **Council of Five Houses**.

This council:

- Represents the five wealthiest merchant houses
- Acts as both government and legislature
- Rules from Nohstril

The system can be described as a **plutocracy**, where wealth determines power.

The nobility lost its influence after a civil war between 512 and 514 FO.

## Military Power

Erebos maintains:

- A small standing army
- A large navy
- Extensive use of mercenaries

The army is expensive but effective.

Strategy focuses on:

- Naval warfare
- Maneuvering
- Sieges

## Religion

The primary religion is the worship of **Fraschikel**.

She is:

- The goddess of justice
- Patron of merchants and craftsmen
- Associated with life, earth, and the sea

Religion is generally tolerant, and the state rarely interferes in matters of faith.

## Language

Most people speak **Erebosian**, a language related to:

- Hynsolgian
- Berendian

Many fishermen also speak local dialects, though most are bilingual.

## History (Brief Overview)

### Early History

- Original inhabitants: Jorers
- Later migration of Massiviners
- Cultures blended over time

### 100s FO – The Age of Kings

- A king attempts to rule Erebos
- Central power remains weak

### 100–300 EO – The Age of Lords

- Several warlords attempt to become kings
- None maintain lasting control

### 400s – The Age of Trade

- Trade expands rapidly
- Merchants become wealthier than nobles

### 500s – The Age of the Five Houses

- Merchants revolt against the nobility

Result:

- Five merchant houses take control
- The country becomes governed by a trade council

## International Relations

Erebos is especially in rivalry with:

- Caddo (main rival)
- Felicien
- Zorakin

Piracy and trade conflicts are common.

## Summary

Erebos is:

- A wealthy maritime trade state
- Governed by merchant houses
- Dependent on trade and seafaring
- Militarily strong at sea
- Politically driven by wealth rather than nobility

It can be described as a medieval trade republic, similar to a fantasy version of Venice or the Hanseatic League.
