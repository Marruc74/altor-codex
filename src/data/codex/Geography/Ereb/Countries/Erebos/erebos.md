Erebos lies scattered across the Kopparhavet like a chain of quiet promises, each island shaped by wind, water, and the long memory of trade. Here, the sea is not a border but a road, and every harbor is a meeting place for distant voices. Ships come and go with the rhythm of the tides, carrying goods, stories, and the weight of unseen agreements. The people of Erebos live with one eye on the horizon, always aware that fortune may arrive with the next sail or vanish with the last.

The towns rise in pale stone along the coasts, built to endure salt, wind, and time. Markets gather at the heart of daily life, where hands measure grain, weigh metals, and judge the worth of a deal in a single glance. Nothing stands still for long, yet everything follows a pattern shaped by generations who learned to listen to the sea.

There is a quiet order beneath the movement, a balance between risk and reward. In Erebos, survival is not won by strength alone, but by patience, awareness, and the willingness to trust the tide.
