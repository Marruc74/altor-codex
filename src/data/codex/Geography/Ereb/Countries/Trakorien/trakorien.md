Trakorien lies far to the north, yet its heart beats with the rhythm of distant seas and southern markets. Ships arrive daily, carrying spices, metals, and whispers from lands most citizens will never see. Along its narrow harbor streets, merchants speak in many tongues, and power is measured not in crowns, but in contracts and alliances.

The realm is divided across four islands, each with its own nature. Fertile Saphyna feeds the population, while rugged Trinsmyra breeds harder folk shaped by conflict and isolation. Palamux holds ancient temples and quiet dangers, and Paratorna stands at the center of it all, where the capital Tricilve gathers wealth, ambition, and intrigue.

Though an emperor rules, true authority is shared with the noble council, where decisions are weighed carefully and influence flows like trade itself. Soldiers fight for coin, fleets guard the lifeblood of the realm, and beyond the cities, old beliefs and strange cults question the very nature of reality.

Trakorien endures because it adapts, balancing north and south, tradition and change, always looking toward the next horizon.

# Overview: Trakorien

## Core Idea

Trakorien is an island realm in Ereb Altor, located relatively far north but strongly connected to trade with southern regions. Its culture is therefore a blend of northern and southern influences, although its climate and environment are distinctly northern.

It is a wealthy trading realm with strong seafaring traditions, political intrigue, and a complex religious landscape.

## Basic Facts

- Population: approx. 160,000
- Majority: humans (80%), with also darkfolk and ankor
- Capital: Tricilve (~9,000 inhabitants)
- Government: empire with a powerful noble council
- Economy: trade and seafaring
- Military: small imperial army plus many mercenaries

## Geography

Trakorien consists of four major islands, each with its own character.

### Palamux

- Largest and most populated island
- Contains important religious sites
- Also holds dangerous areas with monsters and strange phenomena

### Saphyna

- Highly fertile land
- Often called the breadbasket of Trakorien
- Focused on agriculture and food production

### Trinsmyra

- Small, mountainous, and difficult to control
- Home to many strange and dangerous peoples
- Associated with warlike tribes and mysticism

### Paratorna

- Political center
- Location of the capital, Tricilve
- More urban and trade-oriented

## People and Culture

Several peoples live across the islands.

Main groups:

- Palamoxians – from Palamux
- Paratornians – from Paratorna
- Saphyners – primarily farmers
- Trinsmyrans – more warlike and untamed

The language is Trakorian, a West Jorian language.

## Politics

The system of rule is unusual.

Trakorien is governed by:

- The Emperor
- The noble council known as Bodåken

The council is divided into political "hands," each responsible for areas such as:

- Defense
- Law
- Trade
- Foreign affairs
- Seafaring

The system is somewhat oligarchic and politically intricate.

## Military

The military consists of several types of forces:

- Mercenaries (very important)
- The Hird (the emperor’s elite troops)
- Local militias
- Private armies maintained by nobles

The navy is also crucial, as the realm depends entirely on seafaring.

## Trade

Trade is one of the most important activities in Trakorien.

### Exports

- Metals
- Textiles
- Agricultural goods
- Salt

### Imports

- Sulfur
- Timber
- Precious metals
- Spices
- Luxury goods

Trade routes extend toward:

- Caddo
- Erebos
- Eastern lands

## Religion

Trakorien has no unified religion.

Multiple cults exist, including:

### Archetypal Gods

Common deities found across many cultures.

### Shamash

A religion centered on truth and purity.

### Inashtar

Goddess of war and fire, especially important on Trinsmyra.

### Kastyke

Protector deity of the Paratornians.

### Remuntra

A strange cult that believes the world is an illusion.

## History (Brief)

Trakorien’s history consists of several eras:

- Ancient Age – the fall of the Jorpagna empire
- Dark Age – fragmentation and small wars
- Klavyrian Empire – a larger realm emerges
- Religious Awakening – new cults rise
- Trakorien Empire – the current state forms
- Expansion and wars with neighbors

Over time, the realm develops into a powerful trading nation.
