The first telling of Felicien does not begin with its banners or its fleets, but with its harbors. Cities of pale stone rise above the sea, their terraces filled with traders, sailors, and voices from distant lands. Wealth moves constantly through its markets, measured in spice, grain, and amphorae carried across the water. Yet this prosperity is not carried by merchants alone. Much of the labor is done by slaves, taken in war or bought through trade, bound to the docks, the fields, and the oars of the great galleys. Beneath the noise of commerce lies a rigid order that few can escape.

From these shores, Felicien built its strength. Its ships are long and disciplined, its crews trained to move as one. The navy is not only a defense but a tool of control, securing trade routes and enforcing the will of the realm far beyond its coasts. Those who meet its fleets at sea rarely forget them. In Felicien, power flows from the water, but it is held in place by those who command both ships and people alike.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 170,000
- **Capital:** Tyros (approx. 14,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Monarchy with elements of representation
- **Wealth:** Rich nation
- **Economy:** Trade, craftsmanship, gemstones
- **Imports:** Timber, metals, textiles, slaves
- **Religion:** The sea god Philas and the earth god Paridon
- **Society:** Approximately half of the population are slaves

## Geography

Felicien is located in the southern part of the Tolan Peninsula.

The country consists of:

- The peninsula of Kritos
- The southern part of Pelenos
- Coastal regions around the Koalin Bay

They also control:

- Trade colonies in Dermania
- The Hakkai archipelago
- The Gerdikk archipelago

The region lies strategically between the eastern and western parts of the Copper Sea, making it an important trade hub.

## People

Typical traits:

- Tall stature
- Dark hair and dark eyes
- Limited emotional expression
- Highly enduring and strong-willed

They are often seen as hardworking and disciplined, but also arrogant by other peoples.

## Society

A notable feature of Felicien is that:

**Women often hold leading roles in society.**

They work as:

- Politicians
- Bankers
- Craftspeople
- Merchants

This is due to traditions where women are not expected to perform heavy physical labor, which is instead carried out by slaves.

Families are relatively loose.  
Many young people leave home around the age of 20 to pursue careers or military service.

## Language

Most Feliciens speak two languages:

- **Kimzon** – an ancient official language
- **Felicic** – a more common spoken dialect

Kimzon is used in:

- Administration
- Religion
- Higher social classes

## Upbringing

Child upbringing is strict.

Children are trained early in:

- Sailing
- Running
- Reading and writing
- Craft or military skills

At the age of 20, one is considered an adult and expected to support oneself.

## Slavery

Slaves make up nearly 50% of the population.

They are used as:

- Agricultural workers
- Fishermen
- Craft assistants
- Household servants
- Rowers in galleys
- Entertainers (singers, dancers)

Important notes:

- Slaves have no legal rights
- They can be freely bought and sold
- Some may be freed after long service

## Government

The country is ruled by:

**A king and a council of 15 members**

The council represents:

- Regions
- Military
- Craftspeople
- Merchants

The king holds four votes, giving him strong influence.

## Economy

Felicien is one of the richest trading nations in Ereb Altor.

Trade is mainly based on:

- Gemstones
- Wood (cedar)
- Purple dye
- Wine
- Sculptures

The trade center is located around Koalin Bay.

## Military

The military is primarily based on the navy.

The fleet is divided into:

- Marine Corps
- Sea Corps

In wartime, they can mobilize around 50 galleys.

The land army is smaller.

## Technology

Felicien is technologically behind in some areas, but highly skilled in:

- Warfare industries
- Shipbuilding

Their galleys are considered among the fastest in the Copper Sea.

## Religion

Two main gods:

**Philas**

- God of the sea
- Creator of land and sea
- Central to Felicien belief

**Paridon**

- God of the earth
- Creator of humanity

Philas' daughter, **Felis**, is the goddess of mysticism.

## History

The people originate from the Kimzons, who fled the deserts of Akrogal.

After a long journey, they settled around Koalin Bay and founded:

- Tyros
- Sidon
- Elium

Felicien grew into a powerful maritime and trading nation.

They have fought wars against:

- Berendien
- Zorakin
- Dermania

They often succeeded thanks to their strong navy.

## Foreign Relations

Feliciens are known for being:

- Proud
- Arrogant
- Tough negotiators

They sometimes use state-backed privateers to force trade advantages.

This has led to conflicts with several nations around the Copper Sea.
