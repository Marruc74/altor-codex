Klomellien lies along the western coast where the sea meets ambition. It is not a kingdom but a gathering of eleven city states, each guarding its own interests while watching the others closely. Ships arrive daily, carrying timber, salt, iron, and pearls outward, while fabrics and food return in steady flow. Along the coast, markets never rest and agreements are written as quickly as they are broken. Inland, the Melashe Mountains rise with forests, mines, and the deep waters of Jaggarack. Rivers carry wealth toward the harbors, and with it come tension and rivalry. Mercana leads through trade, Hamur through strength, and others through quieter means. Old ruins and mist covered valleys remind travelers that the land itself holds older stories. Here, wealth and hardship exist side by side, and fortune favors those who understand both.

## Overview

Klomellien is a loose union of eleven independent city-states along the western coast of Ereb Altor. It is not a unified realm, but rather a constant political chessboard where trade, intrigue, and balance of power dominate.

- **Population:** approx. 125,000 (70% humans, the rest dwarves, ducks, halflings, and others)
- **Government:** Union of self-governing city-states
- **Exports:** timber, salt, iron, pearls
- **Imports:** textiles, food

## Geography

Klomellien lies along Gryppa Bay.

To the east rise the Melashe Mountains, featuring:

- Forests
- Mines
- Lake Jaggarack
- The Maure River, flowing through the land

The coast is densely populated and heavily trade-focused. Inland resources are transported to the coastal cities for export.

## Government

Each city-state governs itself. Cooperation happens when necessary, but rivalry is constant, and minor conflicts are part of everyday life.

Currently, Mercana is the most powerful city-state.

## Major City-States

### Mercana

- Trade metropolis and economic center of the union
- Ruled by a merchant council
- Trade regulated through tariffs and trade charters
- Majority worship Olem
- Cosmopolitan with powerful merchant families

### Hamur

- Mountain city known for iron and silver
- Harsh rule, leader chosen through brutal combat
- Militarized with a strong warrior tradition

### Addiaska

- City of pearl fishing
- Monarchy
- Known for luxury and pearl craftsmanship

### Yolev

- Fishing city, nearly self-sufficient
- Isolated and peaceful

### Triska

- Oligarchy and agricultural power center
- Can deploy its army to secure food during bad harvests

### Sanzas

- Democracy
- Known for tobacco cultivation

## Notable Locations

- **Kvulm Lake** – A mysterious brown-colored lake known as “the gods’ drain,” with dark folk nearby
- **The Misty Valley** – Dense fog, dangerous and largely unexplored
- **Joraga** – A demon-destroyed ruin island in Lake Jaggarack

## History

Klomellien was originally colonized by the Jorpagnians.

The Conflux struck the region hard but ultimately led to its independence.

It has long remained outside major power politics, yet holds significant economic importance due to its salt production.

There is great wealth here and just as much poverty.
