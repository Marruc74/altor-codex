Ransard stands in the west where mountains cut the sky and cold winds shape the people as much as the land. The kingdom is held together by clans, each bound by loyalty and pride, yet all sworn to a single king who rules from Kapahlt. Life is harsh and direct. Strength is admired and weakness remembered. Villages cling to ridges and valleys while warriors train from youth, learning the rhythm of shield and spear. The Kardbergen rise like a wall in the east, home to the griffins that have become the symbol of Ransard's power. From these heights riders take to the skies, turning war into something swift and sudden. Trade exists but raiding defines their presence along distant coasts. Their gods promise rebirth through battle, and many choose the sword over retreat. Ransard is not a land of comfort but of endurance, where honor carries more weight than gold and every generation is shaped by conflict.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 130,000 (85% humans, 15% darkfolk)
- **Capital:** Kapahlt
- **Government:** Clan-based monarchy
- **Ruler:** Vortiger, at the time Argald II
- **Religion:** Worship of the Æsir
- **Exports:** rock salt, metals, furs
- **Imports:** precious metals, grain, plunder
- **Military:** very strong, especially known for flying griffin cavalry

## Geography

Ransard lies in western Ereb. Its core consists of three main regions: Nordland, Ransland, and Sydland, with the later additions of Timora and Alerien. The Kardbergen mountains in the east rise to nearly 4,000 meters, with forested highlands and coastal peninsulas. Griffins live in the Kardbergen and are later tamed for use in war.

## People

The Ransarders are taller than average at around 180 cm, strongly built, with light skin and often blue eyes. Men typically wear thick beards. Clothing is colorful wool with clan-specific patterns, consisting of a linen shirt, jacket, heavy trousers, and the **merrlokh**, a long cloth wrapped around the body.

## Culture

Ransard society is strongly shaped by a warrior ideal. Strength and courage are highly valued, and cowardice is a great shame. Skill in battle brings status, and a warrior is often judged by how many enemies they have slain. The Ransarders tend to view other peoples as weaker or less civilized.

Common weapons include the **seax** (short sword), the **tarque** (round shield), spear, and occasionally the **glamorg** (two-handed sword). Archers are also present and considered highly skilled.

## Society

Society is divided into clans of 50 to 500 people, each led by a clan chief with strong internal loyalty and control over their own territory. Clans function almost like small local principalities.

Ransard laws are considered unusually humane compared to other lands. Prisoners of war are not tortured, and defeated enemies are treated with respect. Personal offenses such as rape, adultery, and defamation are punished severely, often with death or exile.

## Government

The **Vortiger** is the supreme war leader, highest judge, and overseer of trade and taxation, ruling from Kapahlt. The position holds great power but depends on the support of the clan chiefs.

## Economy

Ransarders are not known as major traders. Raiding expeditions using fast ships are common, with coastal cities around Ereb as frequent targets. They prefer quick raids rather than prolonged wars of conquest.

## Military

Despite their aggressive culture, Ransard forces fight in a disciplined manner. The standard formation is the **harhærn**, a wedge-shaped unit with flank warriors carrying two-handed swords and archers positioned behind. When the wedge breaks enemy lines, gaps open for additional units to surge through.

### Griffins

Ransard's most feared military asset. Griffins are tamed in the Kardbergen and used as flying cavalry: extremely fast and difficult to counter. Most clans possess at least one trained griffin.

## Religion

The Ransarders worship the Æsir, the same pantheon as in Jorduashur. A central belief holds that warriors who die in battle are reborn in the realm of the gods, which often leads them to seek death in battle rather than flee.

## History

- **9th century**: barbarian tribes migrate into the region
- **27 B.O.**: Ransard is unified under Omric the Great
- **1–10 A.O.**: major war of independence in Belgoth
- **176**: Timolerien founded east of Ransard
- **370**: Second Ransard War
- **410**: Crusade against Palinor
- **458–462**: Ransard controls most of Saphyna
- **497**: Third Ransard War
- **508**: the Millennium of Griffins; Argald Omric successfully tames many griffins
- **7th century A.O.**: conflicts with Alerien and Trakorien
- **610 A.O.**: the kingdom stands on the brink of another great war
