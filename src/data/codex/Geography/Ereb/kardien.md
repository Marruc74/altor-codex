Kardien is a land where golden fields stretch beneath a warm southern sun, and stone castles rise over a people divided by birth and duty. In the fertile south, farmers known as faltrakier tend the soil along the Divra River, feeding the realm through steady labor and simple lives. Beyond the Aidne Mountains lies the harsh north, a poorer and wilder land where scattered settlements cling to survival and darkfolk move through the high passes.

Power in Kardien rests with the echther, a proud and honor bound nobility who rule through a strict feudal order. Kings, dukes, counts, and barons each hold their domains, yet none stand entirely above the others, and the balance of power is always uncertain. Cities may serve the nobility or stand free under their own councils, but all remain tied to the crown.

The people follow the Shining Path, a faith that guides their lives without often shaping their politics. From warbands raised by noble houses to quiet villages far from courtly intrigue, Kardien endures as a realm of duty, hierarchy, and quiet resilience.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 160,000
- **Capital:** Ekeborg (~7,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Feudal monarchy
- **Religion:** The Shining Path
- **Economy:** Poor country
- **Exports:** Timber, iron, copper, furs
- **Imports:** Cloth, salt, precious metals
- **Military:** Small fleet, small army, but strong knightly cavalry

Kardien is almost entirely ruled by the nobility (the echther) and is a typical feudal society.

## Geography

### Southern Kardien

- Located on the Aidne Peninsula
- Climate similar to Spain or Italy
- Fertile plains along the Divra River
- Major grain production area

This is the wealthiest part of the country.

### Northern Kardien

- Separated from the south by the Aidne Mountains
- Known as the Inmark
- Sparsely populated and poor
- Many dark folk (orcs) inhabit the mountains

More primitive groups live here, largely untouched by civilization.

- **Mountains**: sparsely populated, dangerous, home to predators and darkfolk
- **Forests**: relatively few, as many have been cut down for shipbuilding
- **Rivers**: important transport routes

## People

### The Echther

The ruling nobility.

- Proud and high-born
- Strong honor culture
- Arrogant toward outsiders
- Skilled warriors and riders

They see themselves as the natural rulers of Kardien.

### The Faltrakier

The common people.

- Farmers and fishermen
- Cheerful and simple
- Live scattered in small villages

They hold no political power.

### Minorities

Several smaller groups also exist:

- **Penter**: native population of Divra
- **Ker-bosh**: native people of Antivin
- Darkfolk in the Aidne Mountains
- Elven folk in the forests
- Some halflings and ducks

## Society

### Feudal System

Kardien is structured as follows:

- King
- 10 Dukes
- Counts
- Barons

Each level governs its own lands and commands vassals below.

The nobility is almost always echther.

### Power of the Nobility

The nobility holds immense power:

- Can maintain their own soldiers
- Govern their lands almost independently
- The king rarely exercises full authority

The balance between king and nobility is delicate.

### Cities

- **Noble Cities**: governed by bailiffs, pay taxes to the nobility
- **Free Cities**: have bought their independence, governed by city councils, pay taxes directly to the king

## Military

The army consists mainly of noble retinues. Typical peacetime forces:

- **King:** ~500 soldiers
- **Dukes:** ~100
- **Counts:** ~50
- **Barons:** ~25

In war, peasants and mercenaries are also mobilized.

The fleet is small and somewhat outdated.

## Religion

All Kardians follow **The Shining Path**.

- Major church center in Ekeborg
- The church is governed from Caddo
- Rarely interferes in politics

## History

After the Conflux, Kardien fell into chaos.

- Civil wars between many leaders
- Valentin Echthe united his people
- The echther seized power and formed a feudal state
- The system was modeled after Zorakin

This laid the foundation for modern Kardien.
