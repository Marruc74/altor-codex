Krun lies south of the Grynner Mountains, where dry hills meet the wide waters of the Golwynda Sea. It is a land that remembers greatness but no longer holds it. Once, its rulers commanded trade and watched ships pass through their waters from distant shores. Now, the empire endures in a quieter form, shaped more by necessity than ambition.

Its people live close to the coast, drawing their survival from the sea. Fishing boats replace the fleets of old, and worn nets hang where banners once stood. Trade still passes through Krun, and with it comes coin, but also dependence. In darker corners, the buying and selling of lives continues, a shadow that lingers from harder times.

The land itself offers little. Fields struggle against the dry soil, and harvests fall short of what is needed. Yet the people remain, bound not only to survival but to belief. In their temples, they worship Mullah, a god seen by outsiders as a distant echo of Etin. Their faith endures, structured and familiar, even as the empire around it has faded.

## Geography

Krun lies south of the Grynner Mountains, in southeastern Ereb.

## Government

Krun is described as an underdeveloped empire.

## Economy

The country sustains itself mainly through three sources:

- Fishing in the Golwynda freshwater sea
- Slave trade
- Toll income from trade between:
  - Soluna
  - Akrogal
  - Ereb

Agriculture is poorly developed and does not provide enough to support the entire population.

## Religion

The population worships Mullah.

Many westerners consider Mullah to be a distortion of the god Etin.

The structure of the church resembles that of the Shining Path.
