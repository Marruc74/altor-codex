Jorpagna was once the heart of an empire that believed itself eternal. Its roads still cut through the land like old veins, carrying farmers, traders, and soldiers between scattered domains that no longer answer to a single crown. The Konflux shattered more than power. It left behind a land that remembers order but lives in division. In every valley a prince rules, in every town a banner rises, and none trust the next.

The people endure. Fields are worked from dawn until dusk, grain flowing upward to halls built on older stone. Markets swell with voices and coin, watched closely by men who claim their share. Along the coasts, nets are cast in waters dominated by distant fleets, while in the mountains, dwarves and humans dig into the bones of the past, pulling wealth from what was left behind.

And far away, above forests and mist, the old capital still stands. Not dead. Not whole. Watching.

## Government

The area is now divided into several hundred small independent principalities, often entangled in internal intrigue and power struggles. No strong central authority has emerged.

## Economy

Agriculture is the dominant livelihood, carried out by serfs.

### Coastal Regions

Some fishing takes place, but it is limited by the vast fishing fleets of Erebos, which seem to dominate the seas.

### Mountain Regions

A small number of miners work alongside dwarves.

## History

Jorpagna was once the central region of the former empire. Around 1000 years ago, the "Konflux" occurred: a major event that shattered the realm. The region has never fully recovered from this.
