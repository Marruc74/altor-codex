Beyond the Gizur Mountains lies Traxilme, a desert that seems alive with color and memory. Once it is said to have been a place of gardens and cities, but now only sand and stone remain. The ground shifts in hues of violet, green, and yellow, and even the sky above is never the same twice, as if the air itself carries the breath of the earth below.

Those who live there do not do so by chance. They are traders, gatherers, and survivors who know the land better than any map could tell. From the sands and the rocks they collect strange minerals, some beautiful, some deadly. It is from these that poisons are made, carried far beyond the desert to places that have never seen Traxilme.

But the land is not gentle. When the winds rise, they carry more than dust. The air thickens, and what seems like mist can choke and kill. Old ruins lie scattered beyond the settlements, half buried and silent, as if waiting. And those who travel too far into Traxilme often speak of things that watch from the shifting colors, where the desert remembers what once was.

## Geography

Traxilme is a vast desert region beyond the passes of the Gizur Mountains from southern Sombatze. In ancient times the area appears to have been rich and flourishing, with gardens, cities, and advanced cultures. For many centuries it has been dry and desolate.

The mountains are made of multicolored stone, causing the entire region to shimmer in shifting hues. The desert plains are covered in violet sand that shifts in tones of green and yellow, and the mountains appear in deep purplish red. Even the air can change color, likely due to magmatic currents lifting mineral particles into the atmosphere.

## Dangers

Toxic minerals are found throughout the region and gathered by poison-makers who carry their wares far beyond the desert. When the winds rise, poisonous clouds can form. The ancient ruined cities are difficult to reach and highly dangerous.

## History

The ruins of Traxilme's ancient cities lie scattered across the landscape, half buried and silent. According to stories from western Soluna, they are said to be haunted by spirits and demons.
