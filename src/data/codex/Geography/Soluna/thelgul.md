# Thelgul

Along the warm coasts and dry inland roads of Soluna lies Thelgul, a land shaped as much by faith as by trade. Its people look not only to the earth beneath their feet, but to the sky above, believing the movements of the heavens guide the fate of all things. From fertile coastal settlements to long caravan routes stretching into the desert interior, Thelgul gathers the wealth of the land and sends it outward.

Across the waters, Niferland commands the northern Orm Sea with its powerful fleet, controlling the flow of ships and goods toward the Copper Sea. Though the two realms have long stood as rivals, neither can truly stand without the other. Thelgul feeds the trade with goods from the inland, while Niferland carries them across the sea.

Even their ancient enemies, the Nivralans, rarely meet them in open war. Distance, trade, and necessity bind these lands together more strongly than hatred can divide them.

## Overview

- **Government:** Priest state
- **Religion:** Worship of celestial bodies: stars, planets, the sun, and the moon

## Geography

Thelgul lies on the mainland of Soluna, at roughly the same latitude as the Nivralans.

## Economy

Thelgul controls key caravan routes from the interior of Soluna. Together with Niferland's galley fleet dominating the northern Orm Sea, the two realms are economically dependent on each other despite their rivalry: Thelgul feeds trade with goods from the inland while Niferland carries them across the sea.

## Religion

Thelgul is a priest state whose people worship celestial bodies: stars, planets, the sun, and the moon. The movements of the heavens are believed to guide the fate of all things.

## Foreign Relations

Thelgul and the Nivralans are hereditary enemies, though they rarely engage in large-scale wars. The conflict is more political, religious, and historical in nature than one of constant military confrontation. Trade in the region creates mutual dependence that limits open warfare.
