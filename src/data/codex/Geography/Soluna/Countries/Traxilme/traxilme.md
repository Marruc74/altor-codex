# Traxilme

Beyond the Gizur Mountains lies Traxilme, a desert that seems alive with color and memory. Once it is said to have been a place of gardens and cities, but now only sand and stone remain. The ground shifts in hues of violet, green, and yellow, and even the sky above is never the same twice, as if the air itself carries the breath of the earth below.

Those who live there do not do so by chance. They are traders, gatherers, and survivors who know the land better than any map could tell. From the sands and the rocks they collect strange minerals, some beautiful, some deadly. It is from these that poisons are made, carried far beyond the desert to places that have never seen Traxilme.

But the land is not gentle. When the winds rise, they carry more than dust. The air thickens, and what seems like mist can choke and kill. Old ruins lie scattered beyond the settlements, half buried and silent, as if waiting. And those who travel too far into Traxilme often speak of things that watch from the shifting colors, where the desert remembers what once was.
