Beyond the Gizur Mountains lies Traxilme, a desert that seems alive with color and memory. Once it is said to have been a place of gardens and cities, but now only sand and stone remain. The ground shifts in hues of violet, green, and yellow, and even the sky above is never the same twice, as if the air itself carries the breath of the earth below.

Those who live there do not do so by chance. They are traders, gatherers, and survivors who know the land better than any map could tell. From the sands and the rocks they collect strange minerals, some beautiful, some deadly. It is from these that poisons are made, carried far beyond the desert to places that have never seen Traxilme.

But the land is not gentle. When the winds rise, they carry more than dust. The air thickens, and what seems like mist can choke and kill. Old ruins lie scattered beyond the settlements, half buried and silent, as if waiting. And those who travel too far into Traxilme often speak of things that watch from the shifting colors, where the desert remembers what once was.

# Traxilme

Traxilme is a vast desert region located beyond the passes of the Gizur Mountains from southern Sombatze. In ancient times, the area appears to have been rich and flourishing, filled with gardens, cities, and advanced cultures. For many hundreds of years, however, the land has been dry and desolate.

## Landscape

Traxilme is described as a strange and vividly colored landscape:

- The mountains are made of multicolored stone, causing the entire region to shimmer in shifting hues.
- The desert plains are covered in violet sand that shifts in tones of green and yellow.
- The mountains appear in deep purplish red and other strong colors.
- Even the air can change color, likely due to magmatic currents lifting particles into the atmosphere, making travelers feel as if they are walking through a dream.

## Dangers

Despite its beauty, Traxilme is extremely dangerous:

- Toxic minerals are found throughout the region and are gathered by poison-makers.
- When the winds rise, poisonous clouds can form, making the landscape deadly.
- The ancient ruined cities are difficult to reach and highly dangerous.

## Legends

According to stories from western Soluna, the ruins of Traxilme are said to be haunted by:

- spirits
- demons

## Summary

Traxilme is a colorful yet deadly desert that was once a thriving land. Today it consists of strange shifting landscapes, toxic minerals, and ruins that, according to legend, are haunted by supernatural beings.
