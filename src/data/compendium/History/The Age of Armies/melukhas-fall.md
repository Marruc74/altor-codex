# The Fall of Melukha

For long ages Melukha was a paradise island, a place of peace and plenty that knew nothing of war. That golden age came to a violent end when the seafarers of Morelvidyn turned their prows toward its shores. In a single stroke of conquest the island's long tranquillity was shattered, and Melukha was plunged into a civil war that would consume it for generations.

## Overview

- **Era:** The Age of Armies
- **Date:** 1040 fO
- **Aggressor:** The seafarers of Morelvidyn
- **Targets:** First Sivoa, then the island of Melukha
- **Outcome:** End of Melukha's golden age; descent into a long civil war

## The Seafarers of Morelvidyn

In 1040 fO the seafarers of Morelvidyn set out raiding. Their first blow fell upon Sivoa, which they struck before pressing on toward a richer prize. These were folk of the sea, and no island was safe from their reach once their sails were sighted on the horizon.

## The Seizure of the Paradise Island

From Sivoa the raiders turned upon Melukha and seized it. The paradise island, which had flourished in peace through its long golden age, fell under the conqueror's hand. The order that had made Melukha a place apart was broken, and the calm that had defined it for ages was gone in the span of a single campaign.

## The End of the Golden Age

The conquest did not merely change Melukha's rulers - it ended a way of life. With its peaceful golden age over, the island was thrown into a long and bitter civil war. What had been a paradise became a battleground, and Melukha would not soon know peace again.
