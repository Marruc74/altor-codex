# The Melukhan Civil War

In the wake of Morelvidyn's conquest, Melukha unravelled into a long and tangled civil war. So knotted and confused was the strife that it earned the name of the noodle-tangle - a snarl of conflict with no clean sides and no swift end. It was the island's share of the Age of Armies, a generation-spanning unrest that filled the years after its golden age was broken.

## Overview

- **Era:** The Age of Armies
- **Region:** The island of Melukha
- **Name:** The noodle-tangle (the Melukhan civil war)
- **Cause:** The conquest of Melukha by Morelvidyn
- **Character:** A tangled, many-sided war of long duration

## The Noodle-Tangle

Once Morelvidyn had seized Melukha and ended its peace, the island fell into a civil war of bewildering complexity. There was no single rebellion to be crushed, no two clean armies to decide the matter on one field. Instead the fighting wound through the island in a tangle of shifting struggles, so confused in its course that it came to be remembered as the noodle-tangle.

## A Long Unrest

The war was less a single event than a prolonged condition of disorder, the long unrest that the Age of Armies brought to Melukha. Where the island had once enjoyed the calm of its golden age, it now endured a drawn-out turmoil that gave its people no rest. The tangle held the island in its grip throughout this troubled age, a lasting mark of the day the seafarers of Morelvidyn came ashore.
