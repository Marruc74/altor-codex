# The Rise

The island of Yndar gave the world one of its proudest and most fearsome high cultures. It did not begin so. In its first age Yndar was home to a peaceful people, but peace was torn from them by invasion, and from that wound grew a civilisation of cold brilliance - cruel, proud and learned beyond measure, a people who gave the world both the foundations of mathematics and the foundations of necromancy.

## Overview

- **Era:** Bronze Age high culture
- **Seat:** the island of Yndar
- **First folk:** peaceful humans
- **Conquerors:** the margyler, come from southeastern Akrogal
- **Character:** cruel, proud and deeply scholarly
- **Bright gifts:** mathematics, abstract writing, the craft of war
- **Dark gifts:** torture, necromancy and spiritism

## The Peaceful Beginning

Before its rise to power, Yndar belonged to a peaceful people. They were humans who had settled the island and lived without great ambition or great cruelty, much as the gentler cultures of the age did elsewhere. Had they been left alone, Yndar might be remembered as a mild and forgotten place. They were not left alone.

## The Coming of the Margyler

From southeastern Akrogal came the margyler, and they fell upon Yndar as conquerors. Their invasion overturned the old peaceful order and remade the island under harder masters. From the meeting of the conquered and the conquerors there emerged a new culture, one that carried the cruelty of its founding in its very bones and that would never again be a gentle people.

## A Culture of Cruelty and Pride

The Yndar that rose from the conquest was a cruel and proud civilisation. It prized mastery, dominion and the cold perfection of knowledge, and it held little of the warmth that marked Melukha or the patient faith of Golwynda. Yet its pride was matched by genius, for the same hard turn of mind that made the Yndarians cruel also made them the most relentless scholars of their age.

## The Bright Foundations

For all their cruelty, the Yndarians were great founders of learning. They laid the foundations of mathematics, teaching the world to reckon and measure with precision. They devised abstract writing, freeing the written word from mere pictures so that thought itself could be set down. And they perfected the craft of war, ordering battle into a discipline as exact as their numbers. These gifts outlasted the culture and passed into the common inheritance of later ages.

## The Dark Foundations

The same restless intellect that gave the world mathematics gave it darker things as well. The Yndarians made an art of torture, a study of pain pursued with the same rigour they brought to their numbers. Worse still, they laid the foundations of necromancy and spiritism, daring to reach past the boundary of death to bend the dead and the disembodied to their will. In these black sciences the cruelty and the brilliance of Yndar became one, and the world has never wholly escaped what they began.
