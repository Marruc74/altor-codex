# Sanithsid

In the cold years of the 600s fO, while the southern empires reeled from raids and rivalries, a strange new power was forming in the frozen north. Dwarves crossed the ice; the ice-barbarians of Orghin made common cause with the Ice Lords; and from these unions arose the Sanithsid, an ice-folk who bound themselves together by a holy oath and then turned their backs upon the world. To this day the peoples of Sanithsid keep to themselves, indifferent to the affairs of their neighbours.

## Overview

- **People:** The Sanithsid ice-folk
- **Era:** The 600s fO
- **Formed from:** Dwarves who crossed the ice, the ice-barbarians of Orghin, and the Ice Lords
- **The Holy Alliance sworn:** 617 fO
- **Disposition:** Inward-looking; they ignore their neighbours

## Across the Ice

The making of Sanithsid was a coming-together of peoples in the harsh latitudes of the far north. In the 600s fO dwarves crossed the ice, leaving older homes behind to seek the cold frontier. There the ice-barbarians of Orghin allied themselves with the Ice Lords, powers of the frozen reaches. From this gathering of dwarf, barbarian and lord the ice-folk of Sanithsid took their shape.

## The Holy Alliance

In 617 fO the peoples of Sanithsid swore what is remembered as their holy alliance - a sacred compact binding the ice-folk into a single people and purpose. Whatever its precise terms, the oath set Sanithsid apart from all the realms around it.

## The Turning Inward

Since swearing that alliance the Sanithsid have turned inward. They take no part in the quarrels and dealings of their neighbours and seem content to be left in their cold isolation, attending to their own concerns and ignoring the wider world. Among the southern peoples little is truly known of them, for they neither seek others out nor welcome their attention. Sanithsid endures behind its wall of ice, a realm apart.
