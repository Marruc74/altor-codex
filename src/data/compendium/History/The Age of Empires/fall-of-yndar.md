# Fall of Yndar

While the swarm out of the marshes was bringing down the Empire of Jorpagna, the island of Yndar was destroying itself by another means entirely: its own magic. The Yndarians wielded sorcery without restraint or caution, and in the end that recklessness consumed them. The ruin they brought upon themselves is remembered across Ereb as the Yndarian Depression - a byword for what becomes of those who trust too far in the arcane arts.

## Overview

- **Event:** The Yndarian Depression
- **Land:** The island of Yndar
- **Era:** The same age of catastrophe that ended the Imperial Age
- **Cause:** Reckless and unrestrained use of magic
- **Outcome:** The self-ruin of Yndar

## A Realm of Sorcery

Yndar was a land steeped in magic, and its people did not shrink from using it. Where wiser realms treated sorcery with fear and restraint, the Yndarians reached for it freely, bending great powers to their purposes without due care for the cost. For a time this made Yndar mighty. In the end it made Yndar a ruin.

## The Yndarian Depression

In the same age of catastrophe that saw the fall of Jorpagna, Yndar undid itself. Its reckless magic turned against it, and the island that had wielded such power was brought low by its own hand. This self-inflicted ruin is what later ages call the Yndarian Depression - not a single disaster but a long unravelling, the slow collapse of a people who had grasped at more than they could hold.

Two episodes of that ruin are remembered with particular dread: the vanishing of the city of Krau-Ki, carried off bodily by the gods, and the calamity of the Slimpaku Expedition, which loosed the slempukar upon Kamsun and Yndar alike. Together they stand as the sharpest wounds of the long decline that swallowed the island.
