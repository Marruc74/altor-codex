# King Targald Hardfoot

If bronze was the dwarves' first great secret, iron was their second, and they guarded it more jealously still. The tale of King Targald Hardfoot - Targald Karskfot in the old tongue - tells how that secret was wrung from the dwarves of Grynnerbergen, and how, even in defeat, they kept the deepest of their crafts hidden from human eyes.

## Overview

- **Iron-smithing in Grynnerbergen:** around 1700 fO
- **Ruler:** Targald Hardfoot (Targald Karskfot), war-prince of the dwarves
- **Seizure of power:** at the age of sixty
- **Deed:** drove the humans out to the plains
- **Fate:** captured and forced to reveal the iron-craft
- **Secret kept:** the dwarven art of alloys (legeringskonst)

## The Smithing of Iron

Around 1700 fO the dwarves of Grynnerbergen smithed iron, mastering a metal far harder than the bronze they had given the world ages before. Iron was the key to a new age of tools and weapons, and the dwarves who first commanded it held an advantage that no human smith could match. As with bronze before it, the craft was kept close within the mountain halls.

## The War-Prince Targald

Into this age came Targald Hardfoot, a war-prince of fierce temper and great ambition. At sixty years of age - no great span for a dwarf - he seized power in Grynnerbergen and turned his strength against the humans who had come to dwell near the dwarven lands. He drove them out from the mountains and down onto the open plains, asserting the dwarves' mastery of their own country by force of arms.

## Capture and the Stolen Craft

Targald's triumph did not endure. In the wars that followed he was taken captive, and his captors used his bondage to pry loose what the dwarves had so long withheld. Under duress Targald was forced to reveal the craft of iron, and so the secret of smithing passed, against the will of the dwarves, into human hands. The proud war-prince who had cast the humans onto the plains became the very means by which they gained the iron to arm themselves.

## The Secret Within the Secret

Yet the dwarves were not wholly undone. Though Targald gave up the working of iron, the dwarves kept their art of alloys - their legeringskonst - hidden still. The deeper mysteries of mixing and tempering metals, the craft that made dwarven steel finer than any other, remained locked behind the captive's silence. So even in their defeat the dwarves held back the heart of their mastery, and to this day the finest secrets of their forges have never been shared with the peoples of the surface.
