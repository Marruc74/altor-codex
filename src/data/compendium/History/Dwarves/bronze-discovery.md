# Bronze Discovery

The casting of metal was no gift of the gods to humankind but a craft wrested from the deep halls of the dwarves. In Grynnerbergen, where the dwarves delved and laboured, the secrets of copper and bronze were first mastered, and it was from there - by theft rather than by teaching - that the knowledge spread into the wider world.

## Overview

- **Discoverers:** the dwarves of Grynnerbergen
- **Copper-casting:** mastered around 3700 fO
- **Bronze-casting:** mastered around 3500 fO
- **Thieves:** the goblins (vattar)
- **Buyers:** the humans of the Golwynda culture

## The Dwarves of Grynnerbergen

The dwarves of Grynnerbergen were the first to truly master the working of metal. Around 3700 fO they learned the casting of copper, drawing the soft red metal from the stone and shaping it to their will. Close students of the mountain and its hidden riches, they guarded the craft as their own, a treasure as precious as any vein of ore.

## The Coming of Bronze

In time the dwarves learned to do better still. Around 3500 fO they mastered the casting of bronze, the harder and more useful alloy that would give its name to a whole age of the world. With bronze the dwarves stood far ahead of every other people, holding a knowledge that could arm, tool and enrich whoever possessed it.

## The Theft of the Vattar

The dwarves did not share their secret willingly, but it did not stay hidden. The goblins, the vattar, stole the craft of metal-casting from the dwarven halls. What the dwarves had won through long labour the vattar took by stealth, and having stolen it they did not keep it - they sold it.

## The Secret Passes to Men

The buyers of the stolen craft were the humans of the Golwynda culture. From the vattar they purchased the knowledge of casting, and so bronze passed at last into human hands. It was no accident that the Golwyndans, already a wealthy farming and trading folk, should be the ones to acquire it, for they had the goods to pay and the cities to put the craft to use. Thus the metal first mastered in the dwarven dark came, by theft and by trade, to kindle the great human cultures of the Bronze Age.
