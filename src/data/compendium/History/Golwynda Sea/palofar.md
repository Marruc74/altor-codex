Among the tales the Golwynda culture told of itself, none was dearer than the legend of Palofar and the water-god Enki. It is a founding story, a tale meant to explain how a simple farming folk came into their great abundance, and it places at the heart of that abundance not cleverness or conquest but faith - the trust of one man in the gift of a god.

## Overview

- **Type:** founding legend of the Golwynda culture
- **Hero:** Palofar
- **God:** Enki, the water-god
- **Gift:** the divine grain
- **Marvel:** a triple harvest from a single sowing

## The Gift of Enki

In the days of beginnings, so the legend tells, the water-god Enki came to the man Palofar and gave into his hands a divine grain. Enki was a god of waters, and from waters comes the life of all growing things, so it was fitting that he should be the one to bestow the seed from which a whole people's fortune would spring.

## Palofar's Choice

The grain that Enki gave was rich and full, and a hungry man might well have ground it and baked it into bread to ease a single day's want. But Palofar did not bake the grain. Instead he carried it to his fields and planted it in the earth, choosing the harvest of seasons to come over the meal of one evening. In this choice the legend sets the whole spirit of the Golwynda culture - patience, husbandry and trust in the slow generosity of the land.

## The Triple Harvest

For his faith Palofar was richly rewarded. The fields he had sown gave back not a single yield but a threefold one, a triple harvest beyond anything that planting should have given. So the abundance of the Golwynda people was born, and so the legend taught every farmer after Palofar that the gods reward those who sow rather than those who merely eat. From this story the culture drew its sense of itself as a blessed and bountiful folk, fed by the favour of Enki and the wisdom of the first who planted his gift.
