# Expansion

Around the great freshwater sea of Golwynda there grew one of the oldest and most learned of the Bronze Age high cultures. Where Melukha turned its plenty toward art, the people of the Golwynda sea turned theirs toward the plough, the trade-road and the contemplation of hidden things. From the rich shores of their inland sea they spread outward, and with them spread the casting of bronze, the first letters of magic and the first stirrings of philosophy.

## Overview

- **Era:** Bronze Age high culture, flourishing around 4000 fO
- **Seat:** the lands ringing Golwynda's great freshwater sea
- **Foundation:** farming and trade
- **Craft:** mastery of bronze-casting
- **Lore:** founders of elemental magic, symbolism and mentalism
- **Mind:** deep interest in mysticism, astrology and philosophy

## The Inland Sea

The culture arose around Golwynda's great freshwater sea, whose broad and gentle shores were well suited to settlement. The waters watered the fields, carried the boats of merchants and bound the scattered communities into one people. From this centre the Golwynda culture grew and pushed its borders ever wider, an expansion built not on the sword but on the wealth of grain and goods.

## A Farming and Trade Culture

At its root the Golwynda culture was a culture of farmers and traders. Its fields fed a growing population, and its surplus flowed along the rivers and shores to be exchanged for the things its own land lacked. Around 4000 fO this farming and trade brought a great flowering, when the settlements swelled into a true civilisation rich enough to spare its best minds for matters beyond the next harvest.

## Masters of Bronze

The people of Golwynda became masters of bronze-casting, and this skill carried their influence far beyond their own shores. To work bronze well was to hold a power that few peoples then possessed, and through their trade the Golwyndans spread both the metal and the knowledge of its making. Their command of the forge set them among the foremost cultures of the age.

## The First Arts of Magic

It was the Golwynda culture that laid the first foundations of the magical arts. Here were founded elemental magic, the symbolism that gives signs their power, and mentalism, the discipline of the mind. These were the seeds from which much later sorcery would grow, and that they were first cultivated on the shores of an inland sea marked the Golwyndans as pioneers of the unseen as well as the seen.

## Mystics and Philosophers

Beyond the workshop and the field, the Golwyndans held a deep interest in mysticism, astrology and philosophy. They studied the wheeling of the stars, sought meaning in the order of the heavens, and reasoned over the nature of the world and the gods. This turn of mind made theirs a thoughtful culture as much as a wealthy one, and the questions they first dared to ask outlived the people who asked them.
