# Furgia Invasion

When the great colonization began after 1500 fO, the crowded high cultures of the old world sent their surplus folk across the seas to seek fresh land. Among the realms born of that exodus was Furgia, raised by colonists upon the continent of Akrogal. Yet the new realm did not find the empty frontier its settlers had hoped for. The lands they claimed were already known to the margyler, and for centuries the young kingdom of Furgia would have to defend its very existence against them.

## Overview

- **Era:** Colonial Era
- **Date:** After 1500 fO until around 800 fO
- **Region:** Furgia, on the continent of Akrogal
- **Belligerents:** The colonial realm of Furgia and the margyler
- **Outcome:** Furgia endured; the margyler were driven out by around 800 fO

## The Colonists of Akrogal

The founders of Furgia were among the many who left the overpopulated high cultures during the long colonization that followed 1500 fO. Pressed by crowded homelands, they crossed to Akrogal and there set down the roots of a new realm. What began as a hopeful settlement soon hardened into a frontier kingdom, for the land they had chosen was contested ground.

## The Wars Against the Margyler

The margyler fell upon Furgia again and again, and for a long age the realm knew little rest. So fierce were these incursions that the margyler seized and held a portion of Furgia for themselves, ruling it as conquered territory while the colonists clung to what remained. Generation after generation, the Furgians fought to keep their borders and to win back what had been taken.

## The Long Recovery

Only around 800 fO did the tide finally turn against the margyler, who at last lost their grip on the lands they had occupied. With the invaders driven back, Furgia could begin to grow in earnest as a settled realm rather than a besieged outpost - a colony that had survived the worst the frontier could send against it.
