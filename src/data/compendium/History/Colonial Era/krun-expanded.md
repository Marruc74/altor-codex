# Krun Expanded

After its hard-won victory over the dwarves, the realm of Krun entered a golden age. Freed from its old rival and flush with confidence, Krun turned outward, sending its people across the waters and along the shores to plant colonies and forge alliances. The settlements it founded in this era would outlast Krun's own glory, growing into realms whose names would echo through the later history of Ereb.

## Overview

- **Era:** Colonial Era
- **Outcome of the Dwarf War:** A golden age for Krun
- **Colonies founded:** Along the north coast of the Copper Sea
- **Successor realms:** Berendien and Erebos
- **Allies:** The northern barbarians, granted the land of Hynsolge

## The Golden Age

Krun's golden age was won upon the field, for it came only after the dwarves had been defeated. With that ancient struggle settled in Krun's favour, the realm rose to a height of strength and prosperity it had never before enjoyed. It was an age of expansion and ambition, when Krun looked beyond its own borders and saw a world open to its reach.

## Colonies on the Copper Sea

Along the northern coast of the Copper Sea, Krun set down a string of colonies. These were no mere trading posts; in time they swelled into realms of their own. From these foundations rose Berendien and Erebos, two of the powers that would shape the lands around the Copper Sea long after Krun's own pre-eminence had faded.

## The Barbarian Alliance and Hynsolge

To secure its growing reach, Krun made common cause with the northern barbarians. In return for their friendship and their arms, Krun granted them the land of Hynsolge to settle and hold. It was a bargain that served Krun well in its golden age - though the patronage Krun extended to these northern folk would, in a later age, be repaid with war.
