# Grey Elves

The grey elves, the graalver, were the great seafarers among elvenkind, and their ships did more to shape the early ages of the world than the histories often remember. It was they, more than any other people, who carried the craft of bronze across the wide waters and bound the scattered cultures of the age together. Yet their place in history is also a tale of slow decline, for the same humans whose rise they helped to spread would in the end wear them down.

## Overview

- **People:** the grey elves (graalver)
- **Calling:** seafarers and voyagers
- **Historical role:** crucial to the spreading of bronze across the world
- **Fate:** worn down by long conflict with humans

## Masters of the Sea

The grey elves were a people of the sea. Where other elves kept to forest or to settled land, the graalver took to ships and made the waters their road, ranging far across the world along coasts and between distant shores. This wandering life set them apart and gave them a reach that few peoples of the age could match.

## Spreaders of Bronze

The seafaring of the grey elves made them the great carriers of the Bronze Age. As bronze passed from its first makers into the wider world, it was the graalver who bore it across the seas, linking distant cultures and spreading both the metal and the knowledge that came with it. Without their ships the casting of bronze might have stayed penned within a few lands, and many of the high cultures that defined the age owed something of their flowering to elven sails.

## The Long Decline

The grey elves did not keep their place forever. Over the long centuries they were worn down by conflict with humans, a struggle drawn out across generations that slowly sapped their strength. The very ages they had helped to set in motion brought forth a human world that crowded and pressed upon them, and the seafaring people who had once knit the world together were ground down by the tides of human history they could not turn back.
