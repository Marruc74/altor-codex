# Grafferburg

The Council of Grafferburg is remembered as one of the blackest acts of treachery in the long history of the Jorpagna Empire. What was called as a parley - a gathering at which the chieftains of the forest-folk came under truce to treat with the Emperor - ended instead in seizure and slaughter. From that betrayal grew a hatred that would not be quenched, and wars that scarred the imperial frontier for generations.

## Overview

- **Event:** The Council of Grafferburg
- **Year:** 912 fO
- **Place:** Grafferburg
- **Convened by:** Emperor Thamsul
- **Those betrayed:** The chieftains of the barbarian forest-folk
- **Outcome:** The chiefs seized and put to death; lasting blood-feud and long wars

## The Council

In 912 fO the barbarian chieftains of the forests came to Grafferburg to treat with Emperor Thamsul. They came as men come to a parley, expecting the protections owed to those who sit at council under truce. Whatever terms they hoped to win, they came in the belief that their persons were safe.

## The Betrayal

Thamsul had no intention of treating. At the council he had the assembled chiefs seized, and then put them to death. In a single stroke the leadership of the forest-folk was destroyed, and the sanctity of the parley with it. The act was meant, perhaps, to break the barbarians' resistance by cutting off their heads of council in one blow.

## Aftermath

It did the opposite. The treachery at Grafferburg deepened the forest-folk's hatred of the Empire beyond any hope of reconciliation. Where there might have been an uneasy peace there was now an undying grudge, and the betrayal fed long and bitter wars against the barbarians along the imperial frontier. The Council of Grafferburg is thus remembered not as the end of a war but as the poisoned root of many.
