# Jorpagna Empire

For some four centuries the Empire of Jorpagna stood as the great power of central Ereb, a realm of paved roads, walled cities and disciplined legions that bound a hundred peoples under a single crown. At its height no other state on the continent could match its reach, and even now, centuries after its ruin, the marks of its passing lie everywhere: in the ruined milestones along old trade routes, in the laws of the lands that rose from its corpse, and above all in the common speech that travellers still use from coast to coast.

The Imperial Age is reckoned roughly from 950 to 600 fO, the long noon of Jorpagnan dominance before the catastrophe that broke it. To the peoples of the present day the Empire is both memory and warning - a vision of order and grandeur, and a lesson in how swiftly such things may be undone.

## Overview

- **Name:** The Empire of Jorpagna
- **Era:** The Imperial Age, roughly 950 to 600 fO
- **Period of expansion:** 1025 to 710 fO
- **Heartland:** Central Ereb
- **Territories at its height:** The lands now called Zorakin, Berendien and Felicien, reaching west toward Mirelien and Kardien
- **Never ruled directly:** Trakorien and Caddo
- **Lasting legacy:** Jori, the common tongue of Ereb, which descends from the imperial language

## Rise and Expansion

The Empire grew outward from its central heartland over the course of three centuries, from 1025 to 710 fO. Legion by legion and province by province, Jorpagna absorbed the lands that the present age knows as Zorakin, Berendien and Felicien, and pressed its frontiers westward toward Mirelien and Kardien. Roads were driven through forest and over mountain, garrisons planted at every river crossing, and the conquered peoples bound into the imperial order whether by treaty or by sword.

Yet the Empire's reach was not without limit. It never brought Trakorien under its rule, nor did it master distant Caddo. These lands remained beyond the imperial frontier throughout the whole of the Imperial Age, rivals and neighbours rather than subjects.

## The Imperial Order

At its height, from roughly 950 to 600 fO, Jorpagna was the unrivalled power of central Ereb. Its strength lay not only in arms but in administration: a common law, a common coin and a common tongue knitting together peoples who had once been strangers and enemies. The cities of the heartland grew rich on the trade that flowed along the imperial roads, and the name of Jorpagna was spoken with awe and resentment in equal measure across the continent.

## Legacy

The Empire fell, but it did not vanish without trace. Its deepest mark upon the world is its language. The common tongue that merchants, pilgrims and wanderers speak across Ereb today - jori - is the worn and weathered descendant of the imperial speech. When the Empire collapsed and its provinces drifted apart, that single language splintered into the many dialects of the successor lands, yet beneath them all the old imperial grammar still endures. To speak jori is, in a sense, to speak the ghost of Jorpagna.
