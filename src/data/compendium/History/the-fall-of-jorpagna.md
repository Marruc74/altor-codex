# The fall of Jorpagna

The Empire of Jorpagna did not fall to an enemy army, nor crumble slowly from within. It was destroyed in a matter of weeks by a plague out of the sky. In the summer of 599 fO, under the malign omen of the Third Conflux, a devouring swarm rose from the southern marshes and darkened the heavens over central Ereb. Within weeks half the people of the heartland were dead, the Empire was broken beyond mending, and the Dark Age had begun.

## Overview

- **Event:** The Fall of Jorpagna
- **Omen:** The Third Conflux, 599 to 598 fO
- **The swarm rose:** Summer of 599 fO
- **Origin of the swarm:** The Samkarna marshes, by way of the insectoid archipelago Maminira me delema
- **The plague:** The koettbitare, flesh-eating grasshoppers
- **Toll:** Roughly half of central Ereb's population dead within weeks
- **Aftermath:** Collapse of the Empire and the onset of the Dark Age

## The Third Conflux

A conflux is a moment when the stars themselves align in ominous array, heralding events that shake the whole world. Such moments are dreaded across all Altor, for they mark the turning-points of history and the unleashing of catastrophe. The Third Conflux, falling across the years 599 to 598 fO, was feared from one end of the world to the other, and with good reason - for it was under its shadow that the Empire of Jorpagna met its end.

## The Devouring Swarm

In the summer of 599 fO the omen bore its terrible fruit. From the marshes of Samkarna there rose a swarm of koettbitare - flesh-eating grasshoppers - coming by way of the insectoid archipelago of Maminira me delema. They climbed into the sky in such numbers that they blotted out the sun, and they fell upon the lands of central Ereb as a living plague. Where they passed they left nothing. Within weeks the swarm had cut down roughly half of all the people of the imperial heartland, a slaughter no army could have wrought.

## The Collapse and the Dark Age

The Empire of Jorpagna, already strained, could not survive a blow that emptied half its lands of the living. It collapsed for good. With its fall came the Dark Age - a long descent in which the arts of the imperial peace were lost. In many lands the knowledge of writing and the working of metal slipped from living memory, and the once-unified imperial tongue, no longer held together by a single state, splintered into the many common dialects that are spoken across Ereb to this day.
