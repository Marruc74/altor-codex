# Corpse Eaters


![Corpse Eaters](/compendium/Creatures/Corporeal Undead/corpse-eaters.png "Corpse Eaters")
Corpse eaters are a special form of corporeal undead that cannot really be counted among the undead at all, since they keep their living vigour. They are a kind of midpoint between undead and living creature, and so retain many of the qualities of life; they must still eat, drink and breathe. Corpse eaters are intelligent humanoids infected by a corpse eater's bite, and so become corpse eaters themselves. They cannot be affected by spells that work only on the undead.

## Appearance

A corpse eater's body changes slowly after infection. The teeth and jaw turn into a predator's, the sharp fangs ideal for tearing flesh, and the black nails become sharp claws that easily strip flesh from old bones. The skin grows grey-white, cracked and foul-smelling, the hair grey and lank, the nails and lips blacken, and the eyes turn lifeless and yellow.

## Behaviour

Corpse eaters are rather unintelligent scavengers that can live only on the corpses of intelligent humanoids. They keep either to burial grounds or to the edges of settlements, where under cover of darkness they may strike down some unfortunate and then devour him. For obvious reasons they try as far as they can to avoid all contact with other folk, since everyone fears and despises them.

## Abilities

Anyone wounded by a corpse eater's natural weapons may become one in turn, the more grievous the wounds the greater the risk. A person who becomes a corpse eater keeps all his old skills but those based on the intellect, and a corpse eater cannot be affected by poison, disease or sleep.
