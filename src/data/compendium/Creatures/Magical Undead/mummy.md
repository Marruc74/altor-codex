# Mummy


![Mummy](/compendium/Creatures/Magical Undead/mummy.png "Mummy")
By treating a corpse with various chemicals and magical spells, one can reanimate it as a mummy. They are often found as guardians of tombs and temples, since they obey blindly and need neither sleep, drink nor food.

## Appearance

The mummy is a corpse preserved with embalming salves and wound about with thick wrappings of linen or cotton cloth. They can be made from the bodies of any intelligent humanoid, and rarely use ranged weapons owing to their poor agility.

## Behaviour

Like all magical undead, mummies keep all the skills of their former life save those based on the intellect, so they cannot cast spells, though they are good at moving stealthily. A mummy's conduct in a fight is as cunning as that of the magician who made it, but it may come unstuck if the fight takes an unexpected turn. It has no will of its own and obeys its maker's commands, given within sight and hearing.

## Abilities

The mummy's wrappings serve as protection but are also flammable. A burning mummy feels no pain and goes on acting as if nothing were amiss for a short while, then collapses into a stinking heap; all who catch that stench must master their stomachs or be left badly sick for some minutes. Like all magical undead it is bloodless and has perfect vision in the dark.
