# Shishi

The shishi has the body of a lion and the blunt, pug-like head of a small dog, and for all its strange look it is a noble, wise and kindly beast. It turns fearsome only against the wicked, or if it is provoked, and then there are few things in Jih-pun more dangerous to face.

## Guardian

The shishi is drawn to goodness. It will attach itself to a holy man or a hermit to walk at his side and guard him, and it keeps watch over temples and sacred places, where its stone likeness is set at the gate in token of the living beast's protection. To the honest traveller it is no threat at all; to the thing that would defile a shrine or harm its charge, it is a wall of teeth and claws.
