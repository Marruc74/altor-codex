The bakemono-sho are the lesser and commonest of the black-folk, the goblin-kin of Jih-pun: bow-legged creatures of the forests and hills who live by waylaying travellers and demanding a toll of gold to pass. They are cowardly by nature. A band that is paid will let a road alone, and a band that is fought will usually break and run rather than die for the takings. Scorned without payment, though, they turn spiteful, dogging a road for days with thrown stones, snares and driven boar.

A bakemono-sho war-band carries spears and bows, its leader marked out by a sabre and shield, and likes to boast that the whole wood is full of its kind when it rarely is. Dirty and malicious, they pick their fights only when the odds are theirs, and some are armoured in a coarse hide of scale or fur.
