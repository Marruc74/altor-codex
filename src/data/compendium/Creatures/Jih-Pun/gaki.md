# Gaki

![Gaki](/compendium/Creatures/Jih-Pun/gaki.jpg "Gaki")

A gaki is a hungry ghost, the spirit of one who died with some craving unsatisfied, now bound restlessly to the place of its death. It passes for an ordinary person but for one mark: a grotesquely swollen belly that no amount of feeding will fill.

## The Hunger

What a gaki craves varies, money, food, drink, the company of others, or life itself, but the want is endless and drives everything it does. It is near impossible to strike, for it can fade from sight and is hurt by nothing but weapons of mulberry-wood, and it drains away the strength of the living a little at a time, each loss gone for good. Beaten down, it only fades for a while, anything from an hour to a year, and then returns to its haunt.

## Laying It to Rest

A gaki is driven off for a time by the rites of exorcism or by the opening of a spirit-gate, but to be rid of one for good its bones must be dug up, burned on a pyre of mulberry-wood, and the ashes scattered while the gaki looks on.
