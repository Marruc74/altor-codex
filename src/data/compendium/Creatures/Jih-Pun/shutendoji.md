# Shutendoji

The shutendoji is the vampire of Jih-pun, and unlike the horrors that haunt the wilds of Ereb it lives openly among ordinary folk. By day it is a person like any other, going about an ordinary life; by night it becomes a bloodthirsty thing with long claws and fangs, swelling to half again a man's height.

## Feeding

It lives on blood, and binds its victims to it as slaves, drawing them in with the dream-trance it pipes on a charmed flute. Its bite drains the spirit a little at a time, and a victim emptied of will becomes a shutendoji in turn, so that one of them unchecked can quietly seed a whole village with its kind.

## Weaknesses

For all its power the shutendoji is undone by its own nature. Faced with a choice it cannot help but falter, made to weigh every danger before it will pass, and a quick foe can use that hesitation. And it cannot abide mi-rin, the sweet rice-wine, whose smell sickens and turns it away.
