# Kappa

![Kappa](/compendium/Creatures/Jih-Pun/kappa.jpg "Kappa")

The kappa is a water-creature of the lakes and slow rivers of Jih-pun, a green and scaly thing the size of a child, with a turtle's shell on its back and a shallow bowl set into the crown of its head. That bowl holds water, and the water is the kappa's strength: spill it and all the creature's power drains away with it.

## Habits

For all its menace the kappa is a dull-witted beast. It lurks at the water's edge and seizes the unwary, dragging them under to drown and tearing out their entrails, and a swimmer caught in its grip is in deadly trouble. Yet it is more easily turned aside than most monsters, for it loves cucumbers above all things and will leave a traveller in peace for the gift of one.

## Weaknesses

Its other weakness is its own stiff courtesy. Bow to a kappa and it is compelled to bow in return, and when it does the water tips from the bowl on its head and its strength runs out with it. A clever traveller who knows the trick can undo the creature with nothing more than good manners.
