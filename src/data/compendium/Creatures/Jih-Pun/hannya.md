The hannya is the nymph of the mountains and the high caves, and the strangest of the three kinds to look upon, for below the waist she has no legs but the coiling tail of a serpent. She lairs among the rocks and the cave-mouths of the ranges.

Like all her sisters she sings, and heals with her song, and charms the wits from any man who meets her eye, so that a climber who follows a voice up into the crags may never come down himself again.
