# Orochi

The orochi is a great serpent of fresh water, a distant kin of the tatsu that keeps to the holy lakes, rivers and marshes of Jih-pun as their guardian. Its scales run from black in the young beast through blue-grey to the grey-white of the very old, and a single horn, half a metre long, juts from its head.

## In the Water

That horn can hole a boat and send it to the bottom, and worked into a spearhead it makes a fearsome weapon. The orochi bites and drags its prey under to drown, and will hurl itself up out of the water to fall upon a small boat and crush it. It hunts by sound, listening for a steady rhythm, so that the fishermen of those waters take care to row out of time when they pass near a serpent's haunt.

## In Human Form

Like the orochi's larger cousins, it can take the shape of a man or woman, though shrunken from its true bulk. The disguise has one flaw: let the soles of its feet grow wet and it cannot hold the form, but slips at once back into the serpent.
