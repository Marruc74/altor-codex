The kumo are the giant spiders of Jih-pun, and they come in four kinds, growing larger and cleverer and more venomous with every year they live, from a young thing newly hatched to an ancient monster of great cunning.

## The Four Kinds

The earth-spider is the swiftest, running down hares and voles and fledgling birds. The tree-spider lives out its whole life high among the branches, where it spins webs strong enough to hold even a mi, and its bite is the more poisonous for it. The water-spider walks upon the surface of pond and stream and dives beneath it through a breathing-tube. Largest and oldest of all is the mountain-spider, which may measure six metres from claw to claw, and lairs in caves and hewn-out galleries underground.

## Prey

The great mountain-spiders are feared above the rest, for they take lost children, wanderers and goats, and even the mountain-orcs they call shikome, dragging them back to the dark to be wrapped and kept.
