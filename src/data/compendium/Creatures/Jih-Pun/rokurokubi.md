# Rokurokubi

![Rokurokubi](/compendium/Creatures/Jih-Pun/rokurokubi.jpg "Rokurokubi")

A rokurokubi passes by day for an ordinary person, indistinguishable from any neighbour. By night the head lifts free of the body and flies off on its own, hunting living prey and carrion alike, and where one goes others follow, for they hunt in flocks of a handful at a time.

## The Severed Head

The body lies as if asleep while the head is abroad, and the two are bound together: should either the head or the abandoned body be slain while they are parted, both vanish at once into nothing. The head must return and rejoin the body by sunrise, and the creature must then sleep or sit in meditation, or it cannot fly again. There is one sure way to know a rokurokubi among the living, for it can never drink sake; forced to, it loses its hold and the head comes away.

## The Hitobe

Rarer still is the hitobe, which looses not only its head but its hands, and these fly with it, as strong again as the living grip. A branch of mulberry-wood laid between a flying head and its hand strikes the hand dead and holds it paralysed until the branch is taken away.
