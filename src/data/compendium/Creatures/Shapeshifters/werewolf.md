# Werewolf


![Werewolf](/compendium/Creatures/Shapeshifters/werewolf.png "Werewolf")
Lycanthropy is a disease in which the infected, at every full moon, lasting three days every fourth week, is transformed into a werewolf, a fearsome beast driven by bloodlust and above all by a hunger for human flesh. The infected may be of any race or sex, though the disease most often strikes human men. The change takes only a moment and comes the instant the moon rises; it cannot be stopped, whether the werewolf sees the moon or not, and when the moon sets again he returns to his human form with no memory whatever of the night's deeds.

## Appearance

The change is real and touches both body and mind, though clothing and gear are unaffected, so it is wise to take off rings, bracelets and chains before it begins. Lycanthropy comes in three forms: mild, grave and complete, according to how strongly one resists the disease. In its mildest form the victim becomes a wholly humanoid werewolf, a man with great fangs, sharp claws, heavy hair and yellow eyes; those of less resistance cannot withstand it at all and are turned entirely into enormously powerful wolves without any human feeling or trait left to them.

## Behaviour

Every werewolf is driven by bloodlust and a craving for human flesh, and must struggle hard to resist that hunger even a single night. How the werewolf behaves can hold the disease back, drive it onward, or even cure it: the more humans it devours, the surer it is to keep the sickness from worsening, yet one must not eat a single human if one hopes ever to defeat it. In a pinch a werewolf can make do with other meat, such as horseflesh, but at a permanent cost to its body and spirit. In its milder states it keeps most of its human skills and some memory, struggling to recall a specific thing such as a comrade's face; in the grave and complete forms it loses all that was human.

## Abilities

A werewolf can be harmed effectively only by silver weapons; all other wounds it regenerates at once, a blow that would take off a head healing before the blade has passed through, an arrow falling out the way it came and leaving no trace. Wounds dealt by silver heal only very slowly, even after the werewolf has returned to human form, and magic and magic weapons do only half harm. Just after the change the beast is at its very weakest until it can throw back its head and howl at the moon, which also restores all the strength it has lost, save to silver. A creature stricken with lycanthropy can never willingly touch silver in any form, for it burns like red-hot iron, so a silver necklace is a most effective weapon against a werewolf, which in nine cases out of ten aims its first blow straight at the throat.
