# Shapeshifter

Shapeshifters are humanoids of any race who have been given the power to turn themselves into a particular animal. The commonest forms are bear, bird of prey, swan, deer, hound, cat and wolf.

## Appearance

In their own form shapeshifters look like any ordinary member of their race. In animal form their body changes wholly into that of the beast, and to all appearances they are a true and complete animal of the chosen kind.

## Behaviour

The origin of the power is unknown; some are born with it, some receive it through the intervention of a god, and others gain it through certain rituals, in which last case the shapeshifter must have an unintelligent mammal as the alternate form. The change is real, neither magical nor an illusion, so a shapeshifter takes on the very physical nature of the animal, yet keeps her own intelligence, her memories and her intelligence-based skills, save that she can never cast spells in animal form and that many of her skills cannot be used, since a beast cannot, for instance, speak well with a throat not made for it.

## Abilities

A shapeshifter takes on the strength, size, toughness and agility of the animal she becomes, together with all the abilities and the skills the beast naturally possesses. If she stays too long in animal form she risks being trapped in it forever: she can remain a beast only so many hours, and for each hour beyond that she must struggle to break free, until at last a failure leaves her able to regain her own form only through death. Every shapeshifter returns at once to her natural form when she dies.
