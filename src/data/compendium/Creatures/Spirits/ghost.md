# Ghost


![Ghost](/compendium/Creatures/Spirits/ghost.png "Ghost")
Ghosts are the spirits of people who died while they still had some important task to perform. The strength of will and the rage at the moment of death bound their spirits to the world of the living, and to escape it they must see that task completed.

## Appearance

A ghost appears as a blue-shimmering, immaterial figure with the same look it had when it died, wearing the same clothes and bearing its old wounds, perhaps a half-severed head and the like.

## Behaviour

A ghost's one wish is usually to be freed from its undead state, which it can be only when its task is done. If the task is something the spirit cannot finish alone, to destroy something, fetch something, free someone, or kill something or someone, it may well help all whom it meets; but if the task is to guard or protect something, it is far crueller, and uses its power to strike terror.

## Abilities

Merely by touching someone a ghost can drain the body's strength, a little each moment, the loss recovered only slowly over the weeks, and a victim wholly drained falls unconscious and dies. A ghost is always bound to the place of its death and cannot stray far from it; like all spirits it passes through solid matter and is harmed only by spells of the mind, though a banishing undoes it entirely.
