# Will-o'-the-Wisp

Will-o'-the-wisps are the ghosts of wicked people who drowned or met a violent death in marshes, swamps or small forest tarns. Like all such spirits they are bound to the place of their death, born of a soul that died full of hatred, and they would sooner lure others to the same fate than save them from it.

## Appearance

They appear as orbs of light that drift freely through the air; within the light one can make out the dead person's features. They can switch the light on and off as they please, or raise and dim its brightness at will.

## Behaviour

The will-o'-the-wisp's aim is to lure unwary travellers off the safe paths of the marsh and out into the bottomless mire, where it can drink in the will of a dying person; for the victim, unconsciousness and death follow, and the wisp's own strength of mind grows.

## Abilities

A will-o'-the-wisp can seize control of a person's mind and entice him off the path, where he is all too likely to sink and drown. The will it spends in doing so it cannot recover by ordinary means, and its only way to gain more is to bring about the drowning of intelligent creatures nearby. It must shine while it works the compulsion, and the will it sucks from a victim, a little each moment, is lost for good. Like all spirits it moves through solid matter as easily as through air, takes no harm from ordinary or even magical weapons, and can be hurt only by spells of the mind or driven off entirely by a banishing.
