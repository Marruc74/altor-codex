# Leather Bird

Leather birds are featherless fliers of wide, leathery wingspan, their bare hide stretched over light bones like a bat's. A single one is a cowardly thing that keeps its distance, but they gather in large flocks, and a flock is another matter: they fall on prey together in a shrieking storm of beaks and claws that can strip a grown animal in moments.

It is the noise as much as the numbers that undoes a victim, the whole flock screaming at once as it dives. Alone, a leather bird flees almost anything; in a flock, it fears almost nothing.
