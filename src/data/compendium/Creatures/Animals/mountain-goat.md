# Mountain Goat

Mountain goats are sure-footed grazers of the high country, picking their way along canyon ledges and cliff faces that no other large animal would attempt. Shaggy-coated against the cold and tipped with short curved horns, they keep to the heights above the trails, where their footing on bare rock is their best defence against wolves and cats.

They are wary but not aggressive, and bolt for steeper ground at the first sign of danger. A billy pressed into a corner will lower its horns and charge, and a kick or a butt from one is enough to send a careless climber off a ledge.
