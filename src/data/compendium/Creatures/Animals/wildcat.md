# Wildcat

The wildcat is a small, fierce hunting cat of wooded hills, ravines and rocky country, no larger than a stout farm cat but a true wild predator with none of the farm cat's tameness. Tawny and faintly striped, it dens among rocks and in the mouths of caves, where a mother will raise a litter of two or three kittens away from the open trails.

A wildcat hunts alone, taking birds, hares and rodents, and keeps well clear of people by choice. Cornered, or with kittens to defend, it is all teeth and claws and quite willing to drive off something many times its size. Travellers who stumble on a denning mother in a cave do best to back out the way they came.
