# Warden

Wardens stand guard over the things the orcs most wish to keep hidden: their important works and the deeper chambers of their lairs. Each is a stone idol with a dreadful spirit bound inside it, and the spirit sees everything that passes before it.

## Appearance

A warden is a stone statue of three bodies grown together, crowned with three vulture heads that face in different directions, so that nothing can approach unseen. It sits upon a throne of stone graven with mighty troll-runes.

## Behaviour

The moment the runes are cut into the stone, they draw a spirit into the idol, and there it stays until it either flees or is beaten, whereupon a fresh spirit takes its place. Let an intruder who has no business there come before a warden, and within moments it raises a scream that brings orcs running; only the sound of a bell from the orcs' guardroom will quiet it. It pays no heed to small creatures slipping past, rousing only to those of real size, but it is not so easily cheated as that, for even one who creeps by unseen is found out if he lingers too long in the room.

## Abilities

Set upon, a warden screams louder still, a din fierce enough to deafen those nearby. The surest way to be rid of one is to destroy the runes that bind its spirit. A flawless strike from a magical sword will drive the spirit out on the instant; but let the blow go wrong, and the stone's own magic shatters the blade.
