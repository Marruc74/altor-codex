# Demons of Inferno

Of all the worlds of Demonicum, Inferno lies nearest to Altor, and it is from here that most of the demons summoned into our world come. It is a cellar-dimension of warring demon-princes, built in nine circles that run deeper and worse the further down you go, and even the demons reckon it a hellish place to live. What makes it dangerous to us is how close it sits, and how badly its inhabitants want out.

## The Ranks of Inferno

Inferno is built like a ladder, and a demon's station is everything. The rank and file are the lesser demons, called into the human world as servants and weapons and set, at home, to torment the damned souls as overseers, torturers and gaolers. Those that survive long enough rise to greater demons, the chancellors, officers, judges and overseers of the infernal machine. Above them stand the demon nobles, who rule some lesser princedom or sphere of torment and have the most dealings with the human world. Higher still are the demon princes, the eight lords who each hold one of Inferno's circles and the millions of subjects within it, and over them all stands Zahmet, ruler of Inferno.

## The Nine Circles

A traveller who means to reach the deeper circles pays dearly for the passage. They run, from the outermost in: Limbo, the threshold where most who cross from Altor first arrive; then the circles of Cold, of the Beast, of Shadows, of Plagues, of Wars, of Pains, and of Chaos; and last the Inner Circle, deepest and most terrible of all. Cutting through them all is the Abyss, the great rift that joins the circles together, home to the war-people who breed in their millions for the endless fighting.

## Why They Covet Altor

A demon that is middling or weak in Inferno becomes something deadly in Altor, where the air itself seems to feed its strength, and that alone is lure enough for many. But the princes have a longer aim: to break the Order the Young Gods laid over creation and bring back the old laws, when gods and demons and the dead walked the world as they pleased. For now the walls hold, and a demon crosses only when a summoner meets the strict conditions of the rite, or when a mightier demon sends it. Slain here it loses only its borrowed body and wakes again in Inferno a few days later; to end one for good it must be hunted down and killed on its own plane.
