# Urbaug the Insane

A guardian demon of the ninth circle, sent to Altor to set right the ruin he himself has caused. Urbaug stands some three meters tall, his black hide rough and crawling with worms, with seven clawed arms, five red and slimy eyes, a fearsome underbite, a long purple tongue and a spined back. The small wings folded at his shoulders are useless for flight, and he is ashamed of them.

## A Demon Brought Low

Urbaug is the thirteenth son of Ursling the Roarer and Baughild the Fair, and he was once a demon-prince. He fell far. First he was broken down to choirmaster in the Choir of Lamentation, where he whipped four hundred and sixty-five singers to death; then he was made a veridit, one of the knowledge-demons; and at the last the demon-lord Shaitan dispatched him to Altor to repair his own disasters, a labour reckoned at twenty years and more. He has begun to grow homesick.

## Temper

For all his terrible shape, Urbaug is rather dull and rather kind. He dares not use his powers freely, for he fears Shaitan above all, and though he is honest he will not refuse a fight - least of all against the Shining Way, the elf-folk, or anyone of a good heart. He is easily befriended and often in need of comfort, and when he tries to frighten anyone he only manages to look ridiculous.

## In Play

Urbaug makes an odd and memorable figure to stumble upon: a demon caught eating a flock of sheep, or hunting a meal at a roadside inn; a shadow out of some hero's past; a creature who might be hired to kill "the demon in the mountains," or who might just as well hire the heroes himself.
