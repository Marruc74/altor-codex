# Servant

![Servant](/compendium/Creatures/Demons/servant.jpg "Servant")

Servants are ordinary humanoids, usually necromancers, who have made a pact with a demon noble, a demon prince, or some other being mighty enough, and have thereby gained certain special powers. They stand in league with that patron and must follow set rules, often given a particular task to perform; succeed, and all but unlimited power and riches await, while failure brings a fitting punishment, most often death in the most agonising way imaginable.

## Appearance

A servant bears nothing outward to betray its purpose.

## Behaviour

Nothing remarkable in itself, for a servant passes as an ordinary person. Yet the presence of one within some hundreds of metres sours milk and cream, turns butter rancid, sets cats in heat out of season, makes every flame burn in a round, clear-blue ball, halts the rising of wine and bread, throws horses into a morning frenzy, stops crops growing, silences the cockerels at dawn, and keeps paint from ever drying.

## Abilities

A servant always has one, often several, of a range of dread powers. It may hurl bolts of raw energy, age or restore the years of a person whose name and belongings it knows, burn out a victim's eyes by meeting his gaze, fly, or wield demonic powers such as fire-breath, the draining of life, mind or strength, and teleportation, and it slays any familiar spirit that comes near. Yet a servant has its weaknesses: holy ground or holy water burns it, it can never pass beneath an upturned horseshoe, an iron nail driven into its footprint wounds the matching limb, salt or garlic in an open wound burns it, and one can ward off any of its powers by biting on a copper coin.
