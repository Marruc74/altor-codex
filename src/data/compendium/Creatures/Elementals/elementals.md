# Elementals

An elemental is an element made flesh, or as near to flesh as fire and water and air can come. They are drawn out of the elemental planes by a magician's elemental magic, one plane for each of the eight elements, and in our world they hold their shape only so long as some power keeps them here. To the peoples of Altor they are less a race than a force: summoned, bound and spent. The mightiest of them, though, need no summoning at all.

## The Three Kinds

Altor's lore divides the elemental beings into three. Lowest are the elemental spirits, mindless incarnations that a mage calls up and must steer with his whole attention, for they have no will of their own. Far above them stand the elemental lords, vast and willful powers who cross between the planes as they please, each bent on drowning, burning or burying every world in its own element. Between the two come the elemental creatures, the native beasts of the planes, which have wits enough to act on their own but none of the spirits' raw elemental force.

## Bound to the Planes

Every elemental belongs first to its plane and only briefly to ours. A spirit lasts as long as the spell that called it and no longer. A creature slain here does not truly die but slips back to its plane. Only the lords come and go at will, and even they are drawn always homeward, and toward the endless work of spreading their element through the worlds.
