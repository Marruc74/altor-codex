# Phantom


![Phantom](/compendium/Creatures/Wraiths & Wights/phantom.png "Phantom")
Phantoms are half-material spirits of people who gave their lives in the search for one particular object, whether something they sought or something they made at the moment of their death. A magician who dies in a backfire while crafting a very powerful magical item may become a phantom; so may a warrior who dies just as he reaches, after a lifelong search, some legendary artefact, bound thereafter to that object. Here lies the great difference from a death wraith: phantoms did not become wraiths willingly, and so their powers are limited.

## Appearance

Phantoms appear in the same shape their body had before death, without the wounds that killed them, but as a faintly blue-shimmering figure. They normally carry the same gear they bore when they died.

## Behaviour

The temperament of phantoms varies greatly, depending on their nature before death; unlike other wraiths it is not evil will that makes a phantom, but rather unhappy circumstance. The only way for a phantom to be freed is for the object it is bound to to be destroyed; should its human body be destroyed it vanishes for a time, only to manifest again later near the object, at a moment of its own choosing. The devilish thing is that a phantom can never willingly let anyone destroy the object, and will do everything to prevent it.

## Abilities

Phantoms are material enough to handle objects, though with limits: they cannot lift anything too heavy, must wield a one-handed weapon with both hands, and cannot use two-handed weapons or full armour. They take only half harm from magical weapons and all spells, and no harm at all from ordinary weapons. The touch of a phantom's weapon or body delivers a jolt like a stroke of lightning, but no other harm. A phantom can move only a few metres from the object it is bound to, and its power to strike terror is a wailing, plaintive cry that fills all who hear it with fear.
