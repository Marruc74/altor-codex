# Elf Eater

Elf-eaters are the name for a kind of carnivorous plant that has specialised in attacking elf-folk and drinking in their blood, though they will gladly drink the blood of any living creature at all.

## Appearance

Elf-eaters look like very large orchids with intricate, violet petals that form the shape of great upside-down pears, almost exactly like the blooms of the lily known as Tevatenus's purple gift, which all elf-kind love and cannot pass without a closer look. Within the sheath of thick, sweet-scented petals, however, lie coiled tentacles lined on the inside with many suckers and tipped with a venomous sting. The tentacles can grow up to three metres long and a decimetre thick.

## Behaviour

When a creature comes within reach of the elf-eater's tentacles, they lash out with blinding speed and try to strike the face with the sting. If this succeeds and the venom takes hold, the tentacles wind round the victim's arms the very next moment and begin to drink blood with their suckers. If the victim wears armour the tentacles seek out the face and hands, or else simply tear the armour from the body.

## Abilities

An elf who sees an elf-eater's blooms, or catches the heavy, sweet scent of one, must master a hard effort of will or be drawn to the plant to breathe in the beguiling scent and meditate for some hours; for some hidden reason elf-kind cannot tell the two flowers apart, while other creatures skilled in botany can. The venom is far stronger against elf-folk than against others, and one who succumbs falls asleep the next moment and slumbers sweetly until death, or until woken by the right spell. A tentacle fastened to bare skin drinks blood steadily, and to tear one off one must overpower the plant's strength and master the pain of it; a tentacle that is hacked off goes on sucking, the blood spurting from the severed stump, until the wound is burned.
