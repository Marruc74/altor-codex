# Spider Vine


![Spider Vine](/compendium/Creatures/Plants/spider-vine.png "Spider Vine")
The spider-vine is a carnivorous relative of the strangler-vine, but considerably more cunning and dangerous. It grows on the border between damp marshland and drier ground, and breeds in the same way as the strangler-vine.

## Appearance

The spider-vine consists of a short, central stalk bearing a mouth like a large knot-hole with jagged edges. On the upper part of the stalk are between three and seven black tentacles that can grow up to three metres long. The tentacles are covered with thousands of hairy outgrowths that wave constantly like spider-legs, which is how the vine comes by its name.

## Behaviour

Once the spider-vine has reached a year of age it digs a large pit, around three metres across, which quickly fills with water flowing in. On the surface settles a green scum that looks like ordinary moss. The vine then lies on the bottom of the pool and waits for someone to tread into it, whereupon the tentacles shoot out with lightning speed to drag the victim down toward the mouth. In the spider-vine's gullet is a very strong secretion that can dissolve practically anything it manages to catch.

## Abilities

A tentacle does no damage in itself, but if its attack succeeds it is taken to have wound itself around some part of the victim. Each moment the vine overpowers its victim's strength it drags him deeper into the water, and within moments he is wholly beneath the surface. A tentacle never lets go of its grip, but must be hacked off, and the vine is entirely immune to every form of fire.
