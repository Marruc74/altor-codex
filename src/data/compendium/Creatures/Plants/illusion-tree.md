# Illusion Tree


![Illusion Tree](/compendium/Creatures/Plants/illusion-tree.png "Illusion Tree")
The illusion-tree is a very nasty plant that lives by catching living creatures and draining out their soul and life-force. The tree has no interest in the undead.

## Appearance

An illusion-tree can look like any other tree. As it grows it takes on the same appearance as the nearest normal trees, and after that the illusion-tree can never change its shape again.

## Behaviour

The illusion-tree catches its prey by using a power resembling the mentalist spell that controls a person. First it casts an illusion that makes the skeletal remains lying at the tree's foot look instead like something of interest, such as food, weapons or pieces of armour, and at the same time it tries to seize control of a victim's mind. If it has several victims to choose from it automatically takes the one with the weakest will, since that one is easiest to ensnare with magic. When the prey comes within two metres it is attacked by the tree's branches.

## Abilities

If one or more of the illusion-tree's branches strike a victim and wound him through whatever armour he wears, the tree drains soul and body from him each moment, and these points are lost permanently, added instead to the tree itself, which slowly loses them again over the weeks. When the tree dies, however it is slain, it sends out a powerful psychic death-scream; every creature for kilometres around feels a strong unease and sickness for as long as the scream lasts, and magicians are struck far harder, with horrible visions, severe pains and sometimes even unconsciousness, unable to use any spells during that time. A small measure of the tree's sap, drunk, heightens body and spirit for a day. Its thick bark cannot be set alight, though the branches burn as normal, and the fire does not spread from one branch to the others.
