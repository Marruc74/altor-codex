# Primeval Tree


![Primeval Tree](/compendium/Creatures/Plants/primeval-tree.png "Primeval Tree")
Primeval trees are mighty forest beings that resemble great oaks with human features. They serve as shepherds and wardens of all the forests' trees, which they love above everything else and protect from every evil. Some primeval trees in turn have a tree master, a guardian who serves, worships and protects the tree so that it does not fall asleep, grow woody in temper and, after a century or so, die like an ordinary tree. The tree master's temperament rubs off on the tree, and if the master is deeply evil the primeval tree is turned into a so-called black tree.

## Appearance

Primeval trees are very impressive in both height and breadth. The trunk is like a stout oak-bole up to two metres across, covered in a thick, rugged bark that wards off both violence and fire. Smooth, branch-like arms jut from the trunk and end in seven-fingered, gnarled hands. The strange head is merely a continuation of the trunk, and at the very top spreads a great crown of foliage, dark green in summer, turning blood-red or blue-violet in autumn and winter; the leaves are thick and fleshy and round in shape. Low on the trunk it divides into two thick legs that end in metre-long feet with seven toes like gnarled, living roots. The large eyes are brown and shimmer with a greenish light.

## Behaviour

Primeval trees are normally harmonious and quiet and make little of their own presence, but should they suffer violence, or meet creatures that harm nature or that the tree judges evil, they can go on the attack with spells, roots and branches alike. Those controlled by a tree master, however, may have their temperament greatly changed. They are highly intelligent and command magic, most often the nature-magic of animism. Truly evil trees, the black trees, can invert the effect of their animism and behave more like a necromancer, with dead nature all around them; such a tree takes the form of a withered tree, a grey-pale skeleton with blood-red or black leaves and a corpse-pale, silvery shimmer about the trunk, and these are enormously dangerous and carefully shunned by all the creatures of the wood.

## Abilities

All primeval trees can use their roots and branches to grip and crush their opponents, and can show unfathomable strength when they choose to; a creature caught in a primeval tree's hand must overpower the tree's strength to break free. The tree's most important trait is its will: the higher it is, the more active the tree, but it loses a measure of will each season, permanently, and a tree of low will is woody in both body and spirit, until at last its will reaches nothing and it falls asleep, becoming a wholly ordinary tree, to be woken again only by a tree master.
