# Nargur Giant Oak

The nargurian giant oak is the greatest living thing in all of Ereb, a tree that climbs to a hundred metres with a trunk fifteen across. It grows only in the forests of Nargur, and the groves it forms are so rare that barely a dozen have ever been found.

## Appearance

The branches are straight and can reach fifty metres from the trunk; the leaves are broad as a giant's hands, and the cones resemble smooth coconuts. The bark is immensely thick and the leaves so full of sap that no ordinary fire will so much as scorch them.

## Growth

Giant oaks grow in stands of five to ten, set about seventy-five metres apart in a ring with the largest tree at its centre, and a single forest may hold half a dozen such rings, often hidden away inside the surrounding pines. For their first two centuries they grow much as common oaks do; after that they simply never stop, adding a hand's width of girth a year for century upon century. Trees more than five miles apart have been found to share a single root system. Their true age no one knows.

## The Gods' Protection

No one has ever felled a giant oak. The bark turns aside axe and flame alike, and the leaves will not burn, so that folk have come to believe the trees magical, or under the protection of the gods themselves; a branch may be lopped for craft or carving, but the tree itself endures. They are not always safe to sit beneath, for blood-elves are fond of nesting in the great trunks and crowns, and most creatures give the oaks a wide berth on that account. A petrified oak or giant oak is a sure sign that blood-elves are near.
