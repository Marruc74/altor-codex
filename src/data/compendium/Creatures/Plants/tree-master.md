# Tree Master

A tree master is a magician who, by giving life-force to a primeval tree, comes to share in the tree's knowledge and wisdom. The magician's own outlook on life rubs off on the tree as well, so that if the magician is evil the tree slowly changes into a black tree. A tree master can be of any race and either sex.

## Appearance

Outwardly a tree master looks like an ordinary humanoid, but one who has served long is marked by the bond: green or brown eyes and brown hair, skin that can be hardened into bark at need, and, in the most devoted, a faintly greenish tinge from the chlorophyll said to flow in their veins, for such a one can live on sunlight and water in place of ordinary food.

## Behaviour

A tree master must command the nature-magic of animism. He must meditate and spend long stretches of time with the tree to share in its magic and powers, and to gain those powers he must give up some of his own will to the tree, a willing act, and the loss is permanent; as he sacrifices, the primeval tree teaches him magic and rewards him with gifts. A tree master is bound to the tree's life: if it dies he dies, and if it suffers violence he loses as much vigour as the tree does. He cannot die of old age, but lives as long as his tree; should the tree fall asleep, his gifts fade one by one and he begins to age normally again. An evil tree master who serves a black tree may, instead of giving up his own will, sacrifice living, intelligent victims to the tree.

## Abilities

For each gift he gains, the tree master must give up a measure of his will, and it takes at least three months of study to learn one. The gifts include: learning a spell of animism; turning his skin to bark for a time; moving through forested country without being slowed; doubled potency when he casts animism near the tree; living by sunlight and water like a plant; drawing fresh strength of body and will from nature; melting into the wild so as to be all but undetectable while he stands still; making saplings spring up wherever he walks barefoot; and even crafting a golem of living wood, a thing only a tree master can do.
