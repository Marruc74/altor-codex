# Grey Mareskunk

The grey mareskunk is a marsh creature unique in that its only weapon is wholly of the mind: it learns the thoughts of others and casts them back at its victim. It is told that some years ago, in one place, a levy of two hundred men was gathered to kill a single mareskunk that had learned a hunter's death-terror as he was being devoured by a strangler-vine; that skunk had burst the hearts of four men and driven three more mad by making them live through the hunter's ordeal.

## Appearance

The mareskunk looks like a half-metre-long skunk with shaggy grey fur. It has a pointed snout, four rather short legs, and two coal-black, unfathomable eyes. It is hard to spot in the dim marshes, for besides its drab colour it is also very still, and one often notices it only when it feels threatened and uses its dreaded defence. Unlike an ordinary skunk it has no weapon of scent.

## Behaviour

The mareskunk is a very shy but somewhat curious creature that wanders slowly about its marsh, rooting in the mire for tasty bog-roots, midge-larvae and other good things in life. It knows nothing of day or night, but sleeps a while whenever it grows tired; asleep it is all but impossible to see, but being sensitive to thought it wakes almost certainly the moment an intelligent creature comes within a dozen metres. Feeling threatened, it at once sends its terrifying thoughts at one victim at a time, and should it still be cornered it bites as best it can. It seeks out a mate and breeds once a year, after which the skunks part again, never to meet; the young are left in an underground burrow to which the mother carries food until they are old enough to leave.

## Abilities

The mareskunk can learn the thoughts and feelings of other creatures. It scarcely grasps their finer meaning, but seems able to tell apart basic patterns such as fear, sleep and confusion, and when threatened it casts these borrowed feelings at its attacker so that the alien thoughts seize his will. Older animals have learned by experience which thoughts most effectively put attackers to flight, and can conjure dreadful nightmares, sudden terror, delusions in which a victim believes himself another person or a beast, or even an echo of some mind-affecting spell it once witnessed a magician cast. Its thoughts reach as far as a hundred metres.
