# Hippogriff


![Hippogriff](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/hippogriff.png "Hippogriff")
The frolickers of the air, the hippogriffs are called in the southern mountains where they are common; further north they grow far rarer, so that a tame hippogriff is there a very rare and costly thing.

## Appearance

A hippogriff's hindquarters are a horse's, while its forequarters recall the eagle-like front of a griffin. The horse part of the body is white as snow, but the bird part is clad in gold-brown and yellow feathers.

## Behaviour

Hippogriffs are fond of pranks and love to play in the warm summer winds. Not so aggressive in temper as their distant kin the griffin, they nonetheless fight savagely if set upon, making diving strikes on the wing. They live on small game and edible plants. Hippogriffs can be tamed, but are not wholly reliable as mounts; among other things they love venison, and a rider must keep a firm hand when his mount catches sight or scent of a deer. They normally avoid humanoids and nest on high cliff-ledges, and now and then a hippogriff is itself hunted by the far wilder griffin, for the sake of its horseflesh.

## Abilities

Hippogriffs have no special powers.
