# Pegasus


![Pegasus](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/pegasus.png "Pegasus")
Pegasi are shy, flying horses that live alone on lonely mountain peaks, grazing the untouched grass of the highlands. Yet should one manage to tame a pegasus and make it a mount, it becomes a most faithful companion for life, for a tamed pegasus lives as long as its master, unless it meets a violent death before then.

## Appearance

Pegasi look like ordinary chalk-white horses with silver tail and mane. On the back, just behind the neck, sit two white, pointed bird-wings that grant very good flight, spanning some ten metres.

## Behaviour

Normally pegasi are very shy loners that flee the moment any other intelligent creature comes near. They have a fine gift for reading minds, and on meeting an extremely good-hearted creature may choose that one as a master, remaining faithful to it for the rest of their lives. Pegasi loathe the dark folk above all else, and would never show the least kindness to a creature of that kind.

## Abilities

A pegasus has extremely keen hearing and can answer its master's whistle from up to a hundred kilometres away, whatever barriers lie between. It shuns the underground, however, where it cannot escape with its great wings, and only with the greatest effort of will can it bring itself to obey a call that would lead it below the earth. Pegasi normally live several hundred years, but a tamed one always dies at the same moment as its master.
