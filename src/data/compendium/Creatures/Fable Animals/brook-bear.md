# Brook Bear


![Brook Bear](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/brook-bear.jpg "Brook Bear")
The brook bear is a good-natured little creature, no taller than a metre when it rears up on its hind legs, and despite the name it puts one more in mind of a monkey than a bear. It lives wild only in lush deciduous woodland, along the shallow streams where it fishes.

## Appearance

A brook bear is covered in short grey-blue fur, with long, supple forelimbs and a shaggy head set with broad, pointed ears. Those ears serve it well, for its hearing is very keen.

## Behaviour

Brook bears are extremely shy and keep to the quiet edges of forest streams, where they catch fish in the shallows. They swim well and climb better still, and at the first hint of danger they are away up a trunk or into the water. For all their wariness in the wild, they take to captivity once tamed, which is why they are prized as pets. Taming one is no easy task, but those who have managed it reckon the trouble well repaid.
