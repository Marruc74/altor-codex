# Forgyor


![Forgyor](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/forgyor.jpg "Forgyor")
The forgyor is a predatory fish of the deep sea, and sailors who have met a hunting flock of them rarely care to speak of it afterwards.

## Appearance

A forgyor is covered in silver scales that catch the light, and its wide belly-fins are a bright blue. Those fins are far larger than an ordinary fish's, and the creature uses them as wings: out of the water it can fly in short bursts, fast in a straight line but heavy and clumsy in a turn.

## Behaviour

For most of its life the forgyor keeps to the deep and hunts alone. When prey worth the trouble comes within reach, though, the solitary fish gather into a flock and strike together, breaking the surface and coming at their quarry through the air. Ships on the long crossings are a favourite target; a flock will rise from the water and throw itself across a deck, snapping at anyone caught in the open before the sailors can drive them back into the sea.

## Abilities

Its silvered scales lie thick enough to turn aside a glancing blow, so a forgyor is harder to kill than its size suggests. The real danger is the bite: rows of small sharp teeth that tear at exposed flesh. A lone fish is little threat, but a whole flock in the air at once can overwhelm an unguarded crew, and only steady spears and a stretch of clear deck will keep them off.
