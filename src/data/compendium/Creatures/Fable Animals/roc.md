# Roc


![Roc](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/roc.jpg "Roc")
A roc is a gigantic bird of prey that lives in great seclusion, far from all that is called civilisation and humankind. They are predators that hunt in southern lands, taking buffalo, rhinoceroses, hippopotami, goats and sometimes even elephants. They may grow up to a thousand years old and live alone all their lives but for a single year, when they seek a mate to raise young; the two eggs are brooded for forty years before they hatch.

## Appearance

A roc can grow up to forty metres between the wingtips and takes the shape of a wholly black eagle. Its talons are very sharp and can lift up to ten tons. The eggs are three metres tall, green-speckled and lightly oval. Its nest is thirty metres across, built of whole trees torn up by the roots and arranged into an eyrie lined with feathers, and at the very bottom the roc stores its hoard, which most often consists of great quantities of gems, chiefly rubies.

## Behaviour

A roc is so mighty that it cares little for other creatures, so long as it is fed. The only beings that can match it in strength and size are the dragons, but the roc avoids them, since it cannot match their gouts of flame, which easily set its plumage alight. When a roc fights creatures without flight it usually contents itself with dropping great boulders from on high. Humans and other humanoids hold too little meat for it to bother hunting them for food, so it attacks them only when its nest is threatened.

## Abilities

A roc has very sharp sight and can easily make out a human at fifty kilometres in clear weather.
