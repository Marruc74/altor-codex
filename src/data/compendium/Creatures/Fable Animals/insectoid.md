# Insectoid

Ant-like beings that raise vast, ordered colonies ruled by a single queen, their society split into rigid castes from scholar-leaders down to mindless workers.

## Appearance

Chitin-shelled and many-limbed, the insectoids range from small, scuttling workers no larger than a child to a queen so swollen she can no longer move from the heart of the nest. Winged messengers are slighter still, while the warriors are armoured and grim.

## Society

The colony is a single mind made of many bodies. The leaders are clever and learned, ordering the nest's affairs; the queen is its mother and memory; the winged messengers, the only flyers among them, carry word between colonies and forget nothing they have seen. Below these the warriors fight with venomous bite and thrown spears, the diggers carve the tunnels, and the great mass of workers labour without thought. The lower castes have little wit, and the race as a whole feels nothing of love or fear, so that no insectoid can ever fully wield magic.

## Behaviour

Insectoids build advanced nests deep in mountains and caves, and some are said to raise floating fortresses of hardened spittle upon the open sea. They speak among themselves by scent, sound and the touch of their antennae. They are not wicked, and do not kill for sport, but they defend their queen and their nest without mercy. Their distant southern kin, the raugoner, prey on shipping in the warm seas, and the insectoid archipelago of Maminira me delema is wholly theirs.

## Abilities

A warrior's bite carries venom, and it casts light spears in a swift volley. The diggers are deadlier to gear than to flesh: their bite drips an acid that eats through armour, shield and weapon alike, though it cannot harm what is enchanted. Against a roused colony numbers count for little, for the nest never tires and never breaks.
