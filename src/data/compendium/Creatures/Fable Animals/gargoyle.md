# Gargoyle

Some believe gargoyles are stone statues perched on temples and old castles, brought to life by mad magicians. This is wrong. Gargoyles are hideous stone-like monsters that inspired the stonemasons to adorn temples and castles with imitations of them.

## Appearance

A gargoyle usually looks like a small humanoid creature with large staring eyes, broad lips around a wide, unpleasant leer full of sharp teeth, and small, blunt horns on its brow. On its back sit two wings, like an angel's. Its hide is grey as stone and very hard, and its hands bear sharp claws that ooze a faint venom.

## Behaviour

Gargoyles are rather unintelligent, but possess an unexpected cunning. They love to torment helpless creatures and are manic collectors of anything that glitters. Usually they nest in tall buildings, though the odd one may also be found in underground caves. Their favourite trick is to sit stock still and pretend to be part of a building's ornament, in which case they can be spotted only by a careful eye or a prickle of danger-sense. If they go unnoticed they plunge silently and lightning-fast upon a victim and try to claw out its eyes.

## Abilities

If a gargoyle dives on a victim unnoticed it has a far better chance of clawing out one or both eyes; a close helmet makes this impossible, and any larger helmet undoes its advantage. The venom on its claws blinds a victim for some days if it takes hold, working in a moment or two.
