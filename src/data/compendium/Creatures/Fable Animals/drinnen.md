# Drinnen

The drinnen is a strange beast of the Aidne marshes, found nowhere else: a low, scaled hunter of the reeds and shallows. It is small but fierce, and one of the few marsh creatures the local folk truly fear to meet, since it gives no ground.

## Appearance

A drinnen stands only seventy-five to a hundred centimetres high. Its hide is black, shot through with silver and red scales that thicken into a partial armour along its back and flanks. Its feet are splayed and can spread wide to carry it lightly over soft ground and floating weed, where a heavier creature would sink. Its tail ends in a flattened, triangular blade.

## Behaviour

Drinnen are aggressive and territorial. They live in pairs that hold a stretch of marsh against all comers and kill or drive off any intruder. Unlike most marsh animals, they do not flee from larger creatures; they turn and fight.

## Abilities

Its splayed hooves let the drinnen move fast across bog that would trap a pursuer, so it fights on ground of its own choosing. Its part-armoured hide turns aside many blows, and it uses its bladed tail as a weapon, lashing and cutting at prey and foe alike.
