# Sand Demon

The sand demon is the dramatic name for a venomous serpent that lives in the southern deserts, though it has also been found in other sandy regions, since it endures any climate free of ground-frost. It has the power to spit a strong venom which, should it strike the victim's eyes, usually leads to an agonising death. Many desert folk always carry a powder made from the ash of certain plants, which is strewn into the struck eye and then rinsed with plenty of liquid, urine if nothing else can be had.

## Appearance

The sand demon looks much as snakes mostly do. It is a metre and a half long, sand-coloured on the back and chalk-white on the belly. Come really close and one can make out the great spit-glands that bulge like half-sized buns on its cheeks.

## Behaviour

The sand demon hunts by lying buried with only its eyes above the surface. When prey, usually small mammals but in principle any creature not too large, comes within five metres, the serpent lifts its head and spits at the eyes with great accuracy; if the eyes are struck the victim is blinded at once and falls into convulsions within a minute, whereupon the snake glides up and gives it the death-blow with its rear fangs. If a sand demon feels threatened, when a larger animal comes within a metre, it attacks with its spit and then tries to slip away, and that spit is very hard to dodge once one is its target.

## Abilities

The sand demon's venom-spit has a range of at most six metres, and grows ever more accurate the closer the target draws. A close helm or a visor halves the serpent's chance to strike home.
