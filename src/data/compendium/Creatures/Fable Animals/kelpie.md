# Kelpie

The kelpie is a treacherous predator of fresh water. There are both kelpie stallions and kelpie mares, but whether they truly breed no one knows, for no one has ever seen a kelpie foal, nor two kelpies together.

## Appearance

A kelpie looks like a large grey or white horse, but study its teeth more closely and you find a predator's fangs in place of an ordinary horse's.

## Behaviour

A kelpie usually dwells in water-filled caves or hollows whose floors are strewn with the bones of its prey, and since it gladly hunts humans and other intelligent creatures, treasure of various kinds often lies scattered there too. Its hunting methods are sly: its favourite is to pose as an ordinary horse so as to approach a person near a watercourse, and it can easily dominate ordinary horses with its strong will, sometimes passing itself off as the lead mare of a wild herd to lure prey within reach.

## Abilities

Within ten metres a kelpie can launch a silent attack of the mind, overpowering its victim's will so that he is enticed to climb onto its back; the beast then bolts out into the water with its helpless rider and drowns him. The compulsion lasts about two minutes, or until the victim is in the water.
