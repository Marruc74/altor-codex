# Murder Vulture


![Murder Vulture](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/murder-vulture.png "Murder Vulture")
The murder vulture is a nightmare creature of the southern deserts that has spread to the wastes and thinly settled regions of the north. Unlike other vultures it does not wait for its prey to die of its own accord, but hastens the end by sapping the victim's mind.

## Appearance

Murder vultures are fairly large and powerfully, though slenderly, built. They have blood-red, faintly hooked beaks, heavy claws, and black pupils set in a black eye with no white at all. In other respects they resemble ordinary vultures.

## Behaviour

The murder vulture lives only on dead creatures, like its cousins the common vultures, the difference being that the murder vultures see to the killing themselves. They keep to lonely country, since they are hunted in more peopled regions, and prefer middling prey, large enough to feed a vulture-family for a few days but not so large that they cannot finish it. Generally they are content to attack from the mind, and only if cornered, or if the prey seems weak enough, do they fight with beak and claw. They nest in tall trees.

## Abilities

The murder vulture can circle over its chosen victim, by day or night, and draw the strength of mind from him a little at a time; while it hunts him he can recover none of that strength, even what he loses for other reasons, and when it is wholly spent he dies of terror and mental exhaustion. Should the vulture break off the attack for any reason, the victim recovers as normal. The vulture must be able to see its prey to begin draining it, and so cannot strike at, say, someone asleep in a tent, and it pursues a victim until he passes from sight under solid cover, such as a deep cave or the lower floor of a tall building, or reaches inhabited country. Its draining power reaches some hundred and fifty metres.
