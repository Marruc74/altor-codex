Sea-dwelling cousins of the insectoids, who raise floating nests of hardened spittle in the warm southern seas and prey on the ships that pass them.

## Appearance

Chitin-plated and many-limbed like their landbound kin, the raugoner are built for the water. The swimmers are lean and paddle-limbed, the workers squat and tireless, the warriors broad and armoured, and the queen a swollen mother who never leaves the heart of the nest. Their shells run to the blue-greens and greys of deep water.

## Society

A raugon nest is a single colony under one queen, who may live a hundred and fifty years and carries the memory of the whole brood. Beneath her the castes keep to their work and never stray from it: the warriors guard and raid, the swimmers range wide to gather and to scout, and the workers build and tend the nest. Her will runs through the colony by scent and touch, and no lower raugon thinks to question it.

## Behaviour

The raugoner build on open water, rafts of spittle set as hard as horn, and drift the trade lanes from the insectoid archipelago of Maminira me delema north as far as the waters off Palamux. They speak among themselves by sound, by scent, and by the touch of their antennae, and forget nothing that passes between them. A ship that strays too near is fair game, for a nest will board a becalmed trader or hole it below the waterline and carry off the crew. They are not blind raiders, though. The grey elves have dealt with them for ages, trading worked metal for the pearls and salvage the nests bring up from the deep.

## Abilities

A warrior's bite carries venom, and though the warriors cannot swim a stroke, they breathe as easily beneath the water as above it and will walk the sea-floor to come at a hull from below. The swimmers are the quick ones, darting and turning where the heavier castes only sink. Against a roused nest a ship's crew is badly outmatched, for the raugoner do not tire and do not break, and there are always more of them under the waves.
