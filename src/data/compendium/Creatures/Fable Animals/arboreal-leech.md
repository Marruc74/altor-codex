# Arboreal Leech


![Arboreal Leech](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/arboreal-leech.png "Arboreal Leech")
Tree-leeches live in the treetops of dark, damp deciduous forests. They are, sadly, rather common, and so pose the greatest danger to adventurers who dare to venture there.

## Appearance

Tree-leeches resemble blood-leeches but grow much larger, up to a metre long, though most never exceed about four decimetres. The body is slimy and pale green, veined with a network of darker lines, so that at a hasty glance, in the dim light they favour, they look exactly like large leaves. They have a mouth at each end of the long, flat body.

## Behaviour

Tree-leeches live on the blood of various mammals, fresh human blood included. They normally lie hidden up in the foliage and then drop straight down onto their intended victims and fasten on. When the victim is drained of blood, or the leeches are sated, they climb back up the trunk and wait for the next.

## Abilities

A person may be set upon by many leeches at once, losing blood steadily for each that fastens, and any that land on the ground crawl to the victim and take hold of the legs. They can be burned loose, but to tear one off by force wounds the victim and demands real strength; and it is no use cutting a leech away, since the mouth stays behind and goes on sucking.
