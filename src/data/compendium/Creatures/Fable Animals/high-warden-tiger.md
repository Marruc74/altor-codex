# High Warden Tiger


![High Warden Tiger](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/high-warden-tiger.png "High Warden Tiger")
The high warden tiger belongs by origin to the deep jungles of the far east. The odd one has sought refuge further west, for the simple reason that it is hunted in most of the lands where it occurs naturally.

## Appearance

It looks like a great chalk-white tiger with black stripes, walking upright on its hind paws. In its large, sharp-toothed maw one can see a forked serpent's tongue, should one have the misfortune to come close enough. The forepaws look like human hands, but tipped with long red claws.

## Behaviour

High warden tigers are intelligent creatures with very black hearts. They worship Khonî, the black tiger-goddess, and are forever seeking fit victims to offer her, young, beautiful, strong humans being her favourites, though the tigers themselves live on ordinary small game with the odd human slipping down too. A high warden tiger dwells in a hut in the densest forest it can find, sparsely furnished at first but appointed ever more luxuriously with the years, until after a century or two it may become a veritable palace in miniature; when it feels its life drawing to a close it tries to return to the jungle where it was born.

## Abilities

The high warden tiger has a hypnotic gaze; staring into a creature's eyes and overcoming its will, it can give the victim telepathic commands, though none that mean obvious certain death, such as leaping from a cliff. A command to fight its own comrades, however, works perfectly well, and the trance lasts some hours, breaking at once if the tiger that cast it dies. It can also send telepathic suggestions across up to a hundred metres, of a general kind that does not run against the victim's nature, and besides its telepathy it can speak the tongues of humans.
