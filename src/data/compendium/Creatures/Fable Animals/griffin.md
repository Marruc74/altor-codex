# Griffin

Griffins are aggressive and dangerous predators. A captured griffin can fetch a tidy sum at market, and griffin eggs or chicks can make a trapper rich.

## Appearance

The griffin has a powerful yet supple golden lion's body, mighty eagle's wings and an eagle's head. Its back is covered in gold-brown feathers.

## Behaviour

Griffins build their nests on inaccessible cliff-ledges. They love horseflesh above all else; come within a kilometre of horses and catch their scent or sight, and they take up the chase at once. Catch a griffin chick and, with much patience and a strong will, it can be tamed and even trained to be ridden, as the warrior-folk of the land of Ransard have managed, which is why young griffins and griffin eggs are so prized. They usually hunt by day, but may be abroad by night as well.

## Abilities

Horses that catch the scent of a griffin flee in blind panic, and only well-trained warhorses have any chance of mastering their terror.
