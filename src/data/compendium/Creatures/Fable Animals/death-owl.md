# Death Owl


![Death Owl](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/death-owl.jpg "Death Owl")
It is said that the death owls' ancestral home lies in the deep forests of the north.

## Appearance

Death owls resemble ordinary owls, but their plumage is chalk-white and they are nearly twice the size of their common cousins. Their eyes are a clear red, and the razor-sharp beak has an uneven, jagged edge that gives the impression of teeth.

## Behaviour

The death owl lives in forest country and hunts within a few kilometres of its nest, which is always set in a tall tree. Its favourite prey is medium mammals and rodents, such as rabbits, badgers and halflings, but now and then it attacks people if they are alone or only a pair. It is an intelligent predator and can be tamed, though only by capturing it and overcoming its will once each day for at least a week; fail even once and its confidence is so strengthened that it becomes impossible for that person ever to tame.

## Abilities

The death owl's saliva is venomous, so all who are wounded by its bite must fight off the poison. It also has a faintly hypnotic gaze it can turn on a victim within a couple of metres; one who cannot steel his mind is struck by a mental numbness that dulls all his quickness and wit for a short while, halving his movement and letting him fight only every other moment.
