# Sphinx


![Sphinx](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/sphinx.png "Sphinx")
When the intelligent races were shaped, some gods clearly worked from ideals of beauty and reason no human mind can follow, and their creations turned out very strange to our eyes. The centaur is one example; the sphinx is another, and to a far greater degree. The learned suspect that some god with a particular fondness for cats made the sphinx, perhaps the same Segorr said to have fashioned the cat-folk, but neither they nor the sphinxes themselves have ever uncovered the truth.

## Appearance

A sphinx has the body of a great cat, most often a lion's sandy brown, though black, spotted and striped sphinxes have also been seen. Upon this sits a woman's head, and from the shoulders rise great, magnificent eagle's wings. Its teeth are a predator's, made to kill and tear prey apart, and its eyes carry a cat's near-elliptical pupils.

## Behaviour

Sphinxes make their lairs in caves in mountainous country. By preference they live off foolish adventurers who cannot answer their riddles, but otherwise on any small game that crosses their path. They keep to no particular rhythm of day or night, sleeping whenever the mood takes them. Above all they are gripped by a strange mania for riddles, and when a sphinx meets travellers she will commonly spare their lives if they can answer one very difficult riddle she sets them.

## Abilities

A sphinx's forepaws can grasp, though nowhere near as deftly as the supple, well-made fingers of the humanoid races. She speaks the tongue of the cat-folk and usually several human languages besides, for the sphinxes' gift for learning new languages is practically unmatched, a talent bound up with their fascination for riddles. A sphinx also has perfect pitch and is usually a highly skilled musician and singer.
