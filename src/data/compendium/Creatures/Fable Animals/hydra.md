# Hydra


![Hydra](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/hydra.jpg "Hydra")
The hydra is a distant relative of the dragon that lacks the power of flight. This many-headed, unintelligent creature is a monster that figures in many a hero-saga, and with which wicked grandmothers frighten their grandchildren by the fireside of an evening.

## Appearance

The hydra's body might be likened to a hugely fat serpent's. It has four legs with clawed feet, and is born with two to five dragon-like heads, though it may grow more in time. It is grey-brown in colour, and the broad lizard-scales that cover it gleam and glitter dully.

## Behaviour

The hydra lives in rocky country or in underground cave-systems. It cannot truly be tamed, but a skilled magician who overcomes it can set it to guard a place, though this demands a will at least three times the hydra's own. Left to itself a hydra shows no truly intelligent behaviour; it is voracious and rapacious, bent on overcoming as many foes as it can.

## Abilities

Cut a hydra deeply enough with an edged weapon and you sever a head, but within moments two new heads grow from the stump unless the wound is seared with fire before they can sprout; several spells of fire and raw energy serve as well. So long as its heads are not burned away, a hydra is all but impossible to kill by the blade alone.
