# Rust Monster


![Rust Monster](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/rust-monster.jpg "Rust Monster")
The rust monster is a destructive creature that springs from the elements of earth and water, and was probably called up by some magician in the beginning of time, but has since bred and so lives on in our human world.

## Appearance

A rust monster is usually about two metres tall, though they come in varying sizes. It has a humanoid body with a reptile-like face and hands. The body is made of a watery, rust-brown sludge that darkens the more metal it has lately absorbed; when hungry it is coloured more like clear water than like iron-scrap.

## Behaviour

Rust monsters live on the nourishment in water and metal, and dwell where there is plenty of both, such as old mines and underground watercourses. They can sense the scent of metal from hundreds of metres off and are drawn to it as bees to honey. Many have mistaken rust monsters for intelligent creatures, since they sometimes set upon knights and soldiers and destroy their weapons, but this is simply natural behaviour. They normally appear alone, though it is rumoured that in rich dwarven mines great bands of them have been met that laid waste whole iron-veins. A rust monster can hide in water by letting its watery body blend with it, giving the water a sickly rust-brown tinge, and now and then flakes of rust float up to the surface.

## Abilities

All corrodible metal a rust monster touches rusts at once: weapons of copper, iron, bronze, brass or steel used against it are spoiled, and metal armour struck by its natural weapons has its protection eaten away. On land it loses substance steadily and leaves a damp trail behind it, but in water it recovers, and can grow larger if it has metal to gnaw at the same time.
