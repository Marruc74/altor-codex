# Phoenix


![Phoenix](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/phoenix.png "Phoenix")
The phoenix is closely bound to the elemental source of fire, and is often used as a symbol by revolutionary movements that hold themselves impossible to crush, forever reborn. It is very rare and found only in desert country, where it builds its nest in clefts of rock.

## Appearance

The phoenix is a large, strongly built bird, not unlike a great peacock, with red, gold and purple plumage. The neck is slender and the head delicate; the long, pointed beak glitters like a mingling of gold and silver, and the small, intelligent eyes are a beautiful red. Its mighty wingspan calls to mind a great eagle.

## Behaviour

The phoenix spends as much time as it can lying in its nest, pondering existence and the meaning of life. It is not averse to approaching strangers at a distance, but avoids direct contact. Anyone who tries to attack it has made an enemy for life. It can be summoned by the nature-spell that calls a phoenix, but only by overcoming the bird's will, and if the summoner is hostile the phoenix resists twice as hard. Curious but cautious by nature, it cannot be tamed.

## Abilities

The phoenix can speak mind to mind across up to two hundred metres. It can also loose a high, whistling shriek that paralyses all within thirty metres who cannot steel their minds, and it can strike at a single foe with its mind, draining the will of any who lose the struggle. If a phoenix dies in its nest a fierce fire springs up and the body burns away; in the ashes is found a phoenix-egg, which hatches within three weeks.
