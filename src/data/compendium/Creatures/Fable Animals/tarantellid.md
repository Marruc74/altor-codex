# Tarantellid

The tarantellids, or spider-men, are a loathsome crossbreed, thought by most to be the get of barbarian men and giant spiders, though nothing has ever proved it. They lair in earth-burrows in the dark forest country near marsh and fen, in families of a handful led by the largest male, or now and then in colonies many dozens strong.

## Appearance

A tarantellid has a hairy, black-blue, roughly man-shaped body, four thread-thin knotted arms and two knotted legs ending in three clawed toes, and a broad, heavy head with four red eyes and powerful jaws. Its hide is tough and leathery, knobbed with growths of bone, and its flesh is poison to eat.

## Behaviour

They are pure predators, fondest of boar-meat, and hunt by preference from the trees, being superb climbers and very swift, though for all their spider blood they cannot spin a web. They are witless beasts that speak only in hisses and the grinding of their jaws.

## Abilities

A tarantellid kills with its bite and with the venomous stings it carries, whose poison leaves the victim paralysed, and tears at whatever it has caught with its clawed hands.
