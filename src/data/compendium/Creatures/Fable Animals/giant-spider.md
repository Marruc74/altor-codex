# Giant Spider


![Giant Spider](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/giant-spider.jpg "Giant Spider")
Giant spiders are fearsome, intelligent predators that live either in hidden underground caves or in the canopy of some unusually dark and unpleasant forest.

## Appearance

A giant spider can grow up to three metres across the outermost legs and a metre high. At the rear of the abdomen sit spinnerets that can weave a very strong, sticky web.

## Behaviour

Giant spiders hunt either by catching creatures in their sticky webs or by leaping on their prey from behind or from a treetop. Once they have caught and poisoned a victim they wrap it in webbing cocoons filled with a fluid that can dissolve almost anything; when the victim and his gear are reduced to liquid, the cocoon is punctured and the spider drinks in the nourishment. It takes some days for the victim to die, and until then he lies unconscious.

## Abilities

Giant spiders are superb leapers, springing up to ten metres at a bound. Their webs are very sticky, and to tear free one must overpower the spider's strength, often more than once depending on how many threads have caught hold; cutting a single thread is hard work, and a severed strand may lash back and stick to the one who cut it. The spider's jaws are coated in a strong paralysing venom, and a victim already snared in the web is bitten automatically, falling unconscious for hours if the poison takes.
