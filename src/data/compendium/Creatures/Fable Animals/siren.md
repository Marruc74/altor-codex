# Siren


![Siren](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/siren.png "Siren")
Sirens may be called the sea's answer to the huldre. They are certainly kin to the harpies, and though far more pleasing and beautiful than those, they are certainly more dangerous too.

## Appearance

A siren has a large, gull-like body, but a very beautiful woman's head and full breast. It is always well-preened and clean. It has a bird of prey's legs and claws, but nothing answering to a human's hands, and its wings are magnificent, glittering white gull-wings with long, well-kept flight-feathers.

## Behaviour

Sirens appear in flocks along coasts of reefs, treacherous shoals, or shores dangerous in some other way. They try to lure male seafarers in among the coast's perils with their beautiful, bewitching songs, for sirens can suck the life-force from drowning creatures and so prolong their own existence. A siren always avoids close combat and flees if threatened, and a dead siren's body dissolves into a cloud of smoke and is gone.

## Abilities

A siren can aim her song at any intelligent humanoid of the male sex who can hear her, and if her charm overcomes his wits the man at once tries to make his way to her, either steering his boat toward her or flinging himself overboard to swim; once the song has taken hold he stays under its sway as long as she sings, though she can hold but one man at a time. The song carries for about a kilometre. The simplest protection is to stop the ears so as to hear nothing at all, and certain spells help as well. If a man charmed by a siren begins to drown, she can draw his very will out of him as he loses his strength to the water, a loss that is permanent should he somehow survive.
