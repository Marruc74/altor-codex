# Cockatrice


![Cockatrice](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/cockatrice.png "Cockatrice")
The basilisk's larger cousin, the cockatrice is often called. Its gaze, however, does not petrify but only briefly paralyses, which makes it far less dangerous than the small, deadly basilisk. The cockatrice was made by a necromancer who took up the nature-magic of animism and went mad in the doing; from that insane project a power-crazed witch-sorcerer later drew inspiration and made the basilisk. The cockatrice may thus be called the basilisk's prototype, and basilisks hate cockatrices.

## Appearance

The cockatrice broadly resembles the basilisk but is considerably larger. It has a rooster's head and legs, a lizard's body and great leathery wings. The long lizard-tail ends in a sharp, spiny point.

## Behaviour

Because the cockatrice was made by a wholly mad wizard it shows an extremely disturbed nature. It cannot abide the nearness of living things, and attacks both large plants and creatures with a violent fury the moment it spies them, though it hates most what gives off warmth and so attacks creatures sooner than plants. One thus usually comes upon a cockatrice in the act of rushing about mowing down grass and bushes, until it catches sight of you and charges. It never tires and never sleeps.

## Abilities

The cockatrice can paralyse a foe with its gaze; each moment it may fix a creature with its eye and try to overcome its will, leaving the victim paralysed for a short while if it succeeds, and nothing happening if it fails. The gaze cuts through magical defences as though it were a weak spell, and being faster than any weapon the cockatrice always strikes first. It is also immune to a basilisk's petrifying gaze. In its madness it can always lash with its tail at the same time as it strikes with beak or claws, and it flies very poorly, far slower than it moves on the ground.
