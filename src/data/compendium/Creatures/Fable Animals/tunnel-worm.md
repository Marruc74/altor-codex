# Tunnel Worm

Tunnel worms are very large underground creatures that live on practically anything. They bore their own tunnels through everything softer than bedrock, swallowing the earth and drawing out the nourishment in it; whatever they cannot or will not eat at once they leave behind in slime-cocoons, things such as metal objects and warriors clad in metal armour.

## Appearance

The tunnel worm can grow up to thirty metres long and three metres across. Its body is brown with black blotches and covered in a foul-smelling slime that keeps it moist. At the front the worm ends in a vast maw that takes up almost its whole width, and at the rear the body narrows to a half-transparent, very mobile spout, through which it extrudes its cocoons and fixes them to the tunnel roof.

## Behaviour

Tunnel worms are wholly without intelligence and bore at random through the ground. The tunnels they leave behind are often put to other uses by other creatures.

## Abilities

All worked metal is poison to the tunnel worm, even though its stomach-acid could dissolve such things without trouble, so it leaves them behind, hung up in the roof and sealed in cocoons of hardened slime. The fumes of the worm's stomach-acids are powerfully soporific, so a victim swallowed by the worm falls asleep within moments. If the worm lands a bite, its great jaws have seized the victim and hold it fast, and a second bite on the same victim swallows it whole and draws it swiftly down inside. The worm's body, though not its head, is immune to every kind of natural fire and to all acid.
