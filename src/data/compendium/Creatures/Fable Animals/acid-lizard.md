# Acid Lizard

Acid lizards are large black predatory lizards that normally live on insects, rodents, fish and bats, but in larger company, more than five of them, will also attack humanoids. At the back of the jaw they have acid-glands that produce a very fiercely corrosive acid, which they use to dig their underground burrows slowly through the earth, and which they can also gather in the mouth and spit at their foes as a ranged weapon.

## Appearance

The acid lizard can grow up to a metre from nose to tail-tip. Its body is black and oily, covered on the upper side with hard, irregular scales, while the underside is faintly greenish and softer. Along the whole back and on top of the head it bears hard, sharp spines. Its eyes are yellow, and on each leg are four sharp, red claws.

## Behaviour

An acid lizard that spots prey at once calls every kindred nearby and waits until they have the numbers to attack, though if the prey is small it takes it alone. They attack first by spitting acid, but against prey close enough they will also use claws or bite.

## Abilities

It takes a little while for an acid lizard to gather enough acid for a single spit, and it can spit only a handful of times a day before the acid is spent; the spit reaches a few metres. Any armour or shield struck by the acid loses a measure of its protection each time it is hit, though magical items take no harm.
