# Elephant Bird


![Elephant Bird](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/elephant-bird.jpg "Elephant Bird")
![Elephant Bird](/compendium/Creatures/Fable Animals/elephant-bird-2.jpg "Elephant Bird")
The elephant bird is the largest bird that has ever lived, standing more than three and a half metres tall and weighing upward of six hundred kilos. It resembles a vast ostrich, and its eggs are so big, well over two kilos apiece, that a single one makes a meal.

## Appearance

In shape the elephant bird is much like an ostrich grown to monstrous size, long-legged and long-necked, with a heavy beak. It is calm and good-natured by temperament, which is part of why several cultures have found it can be tamed.

## Behaviour

Elephant birds live in small flocks in open and rolling country. A grown bird has little to fear: it defends itself and its eggs with well-aimed kicks and jabs of the beak, and few predators can bring one down, though the eggs and chicks are easy prey. Their one great weakness is a terror of anything flying overhead. The sight of a dragon far off sends a whole flock into hiding, and they are skilled at melting into the landscape when they do.

By far their most dangerous enemy is the intelligent hunter. Knowing the birds' fear of flying things, hunters cast great boomerangs over a flock; from a distance the shape passes for a dragon on the wing, and the birds freeze and crouch in cover, where they are easily killed. Hunted this way, the elephant bird has been driven to the edge of extinction in much of its old range. Cereval is one of the few lands where it still lives wild, and elsewhere it survives mainly as a tamed beast.

## Domestication

A bird trained for war is guided by its rider and will strike with beak or feet at whatever target it is set on. Such a mount is rare and costly, worth a small fortune to those who can afford one.
