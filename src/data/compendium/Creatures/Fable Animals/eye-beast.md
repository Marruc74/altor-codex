# Eye Beast

Eye-beasts are very dangerous predators that live in small packs of between four and eight. They can eat practically anything they get hold of: fish, rabbits, deer, birds and wild boar. They never attack creatures as large as humans, however, unless there are several of them together.

## Appearance

These fearsome predators are named for the three pairs of eyes set in their bulldog-like heads. The body looks like a gigantic frog's, but with four forelimbs; the front pair is used only for fighting or eating, while it hops on the rear pair. When it fights it rears up on its hind legs and attacks with all four arms or with its mouth. Eye-beasts stand about a metre tall upright, and half a metre when they go on all fours.

## Behaviour

Eye-beasts attack by hiding up in the foliage of a tree and then flinging themselves down on their unsuspecting foes, trying to fasten their teeth in the throat. If the eye-beasts have hidden themselves well, and the victim fails to sense the danger, the bite lands in the first moment of the fight.

## Abilities

Eye-beasts have perfect vision in the dark.
