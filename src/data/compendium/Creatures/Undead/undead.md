# Undead

The undead are the dead who will not lie still. Some keep the bodies they died in, or take new ones; some walk as bodiless spirits, known only by a chill and a shape in the dark. What they share is a refusal of death's ordinary end, whether by the strength of a soul that would not go, by a curse or an infection, or by the work of a necromancer who drags the dead back to serve him. The elf-folk hold them the worst of all abominations, a defilement of the life-force itself, and count their unmaking a holy task.

## The Kinds of Undead

Altor's lore sorts them by how they endure. The corporeal undead keep a body and, often, a will of their own, from the reasoning revenant to the plague-bearing vampire. The magical undead are corpses woken to unlife by a sorcerer, mindless things bound to a master's command. The spirits are the souls of the dead held to the world by some unfinished business, and the wraiths and wights are the mightiest of those spirits, risen from the few whose strength of soul in life was overwhelming.

## Bound and Unbound

Most undead are tied to something: a place, a grave, a task, a master, and cannot stray far from it or find rest until it is undone. The magic that raises and holds the dead is the work of necromancy, while the calling and binding of spirits belongs to spiritism. Break the tie and many simply fade, but the greatest of them, the death wraiths and their kin, are as hard to end as anything that walks Altor.
