# Revenant

A revenant is among the very few undead that can pass unremarked among the living. It is most often a necromancer who has worked the rite upon himself to cheat death: the body dies in that instant, but the man goes on, deathless, keeping his own mind, his memories and his learning. Unlike the mindless zombie or the bloodthirsty living dead, a revenant reasons and remembers as it did in life, and that is what makes it so hard to know for what it is.

## A Cold Existence

The change is bought dear and can never be undone. The revenant needs no food, drink or sleep, sees in almost any darkness, and is untouched by poison, disease, cold or want of air, though magical cold still bites it, and a killing wound to head, chest or belly unbinds the magic that holds it together and ends it at once. Worse is what it loses: love, joy and warmth fade, all desire dies, and the creature grows selfish, pragmatic and sunk in a mild, settled gloom. Its hurts never heal, and the gifts of a living mage, a familiar and certain workings, slip from its grasp for good.

## Hiding Among the Living

Every priest's follower and all elf-folk hold the undead an abomination to be destroyed without mercy, and the rest of the world loathes them out of fear, so a revenant must keep its nature hidden or face the mob and the fire; it cannot be hanged, having no breath to stop, but it can be burned. The signs are there for a careful eye: it does not breathe, though it can mimic the motion; its wounds are dry and it never bruises; it is cold to the touch and takes the temperature of the air around it, so it offers its hand only in warm weather; horses and hounds grow skittish near it; and another undead knows it on sight. Most carefully of all it treads near any skilled necromancer, for the spell that masters the higher undead can seize a revenant as surely as it seizes any other.
