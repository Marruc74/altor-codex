# Poltergeist

![Poltergeist](/compendium/Creatures/Undead/poltergeist.jpg "Poltergeist")
![Poltergeist](/compendium/Creatures/Undead/poltergeist-2.jpg "Poltergeist")

A poltergeist is a spirit from another dimension. They are fairly easily called up by spiritists, returning whence they came when the spell runs out, but now and then a poltergeist breaks free of the spell, or comes into our human world by some other means, and then it stays until someone slays or banishes it.

## Appearance

A poltergeist can change its shape exactly as it wishes. It is made of an immaterial body and so is quite transparent.

## Behaviour

Poltergeists are violent, savage and very aggressive. A poltergeist cannot itself harm a living creature, however, and so contents itself with creating dreadful chaos: every object within some ten metres is lifted up and flung about at high speed, every sound within that range swells in strength, and so on. When the poltergeist departs all grows calm again, the objects falling to the ground and the noises dying away.

## Abilities

A poltergeist can lift any number of objects weighing up to about twenty-five kilos and juggle them about as it pleases, and should it hurl something at someone, a knife, an axe or a stone, it throws with the force of its own will. Like all spirits it passes through solid matter, takes no harm from ordinary or even magical weapons, and can be undone only by spells of the mind or by a banishing.
