# Corporeal Undead

The corporeal undead are those that keep a body of flesh or bone, and often a mind to go with it. Some are made against their will, and some choose the road themselves: a necromancer works the rite of undeath upon his own body to cheat the grave, a dying man is infected with vampirism, a slain warrior claws his way back for vengeance. What sets them apart from a sorcerer's mindless servants is that many of them reason, remember and scheme as they did in life.

## Among the Living

Because they keep both body and wits, the cleverest of the corporeal undead can pass for living men. A revenant looks like anyone else, and a vampire better than most, which is what makes them so dangerous: the thing may be at your table before you ever guess what it is. Others keep no such disguise, the mara that rides a sleeper to death, the corpse-eaters at their graveyard work, the hell-steed and the ghost-horse, but all of them share the same stubborn hold on a body that the spirits and wraiths have given up.
