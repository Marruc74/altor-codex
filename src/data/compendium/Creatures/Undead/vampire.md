Vampires are ordinary humans infected with vampirism, a mixture of disease, curse and divine gift. Vampirism drastically changes the metabolism and bodily powers of the afflicted, for the better according to some vampires, for the worse according to others.

## Appearance

Vampires look just like beautiful, perfectly ordinary humans, with somewhat paler skin and overgrown, sharp eye-teeth. They age very slowly, one year for every ten ordinary ones, but cannot die a natural death, so that a vampire of some five centuries begins to grow rather feeble with age. Vampires cast no reflection.

## Behaviour

Vampires are very discreet, so as not to give away their identity. It is rare for a vampire to pass on its power, since too many vampires in one place soon stirs suspicion and draws vampire-hunters. They avoid mirrors and almost always wear gloves so as to handle silver. Being unable to show themselves by day, they usually keep to large cities, where there are many destitute folk to feed on, easy places to hide, and ready excuses for moving only by night; they often keep several resting-places so as to shift home frequently.

## Behaviour and Feeding

A vampire must drink the blood of living, intelligent creatures to survive, draining the victim's strength with each moment its teeth are in him until, drained wholly, the victim dies. If a vampire goes without blood for a night its strength of mind wanes until it feeds again. If it wishes, it can pass vampirism to a victim; the change takes three days of high fever and sickness, and the new-made vampire wakes with a fierce thirst for blood, ignorant of what has befallen and of its new powers until taught by another vampire. A vampire can calm a victim with eye-contact, leaving him drugged and dreamlike, unable to resist in body or mind, until the vampire releases its hold.

## Abilities

A vampire cannot be killed by ordinary means, but only by sunlight, which at the least touch consumes its body in a fierce flame that harms nothing else and leaves only clothes and ash; by a wooden stake through the heart as it lies in its coffin; by a silver weapon to the heart; or by silver-dust entering its blood. It takes only half harm from normal weapons and double from silver, but such wounds heal with a day's sleep, and it can never be knocked unconscious or struck down, fleeing automatically when badly hurt. It is immune to all natural fire, heals magical burns in a day, is immune to all disease, and is harmed only by poisons that contain silver.
