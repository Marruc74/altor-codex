The imariots are the walking dead of the Montures, the people whom the demigoddess Imaria turned to living death long ago, and the kind of corporeal undead her curse still makes. They keep the ruined Dead City of the Gideon Chasm, and a remnant of them serves her there yet.

## The Body Without Life

An imariot is a corpse that walks, and is known by what it lacks. It does not breathe, though it can fashion a breath, and it does not bleed; its wounds are dry, and it takes no bruises. It needs no sleep, and most labour day and night without rest. It can neither eat nor drink, for though it may chew it cannot swallow. Above all it is cold: cold-blooded as the dead are, its flesh is "death-cold" to the touch, so that animals grow nervous near it and another of the undead knows it at once.

## The Mind That Remains

What is lost is more than warmth. The feelings of life, love, joy, the warmth of the heart, fade and go out, and the imariot becomes selfish, pragmatic and quietly grim, caring only for use. Drugs, sickness, cold and want of air do it no harm, though magical cold can; it is destroyed only when its head or breast or belly is dealt a mortal wound. Those imariots that wear one of Imaria's rings are bound to her through it, and should such a one lose its ring, it is destroyed on the instant.
