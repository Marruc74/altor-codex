# Spirits

A spirit is the soul of a dead person that never reached the realm of the dead, held instead to the world of the living by the manner of its dying. A task left undone, a rage unspent, a death too sudden to accept: any of these can bind a soul in place, and there it stays, unable to rest until the thing that holds it is settled. Most are bodiless, seen as a cold shape or a wisp of light, and most are tied to a single place or person.

## Restless and Wronged

The ghost is the common sort, bound to some errand it must see finished before it can go. The poltergeist is angrier, flinging furniture and fittings about to make its fury felt; the spectre is colder and more dreadful; the will-o'-the-wisp a false light that leads night travellers astray into bog and mire. None of them can be fought the way a living thing is fought, for steel passes clean through them, and to be rid of one for good you must most often answer whatever it is that keeps it here.
