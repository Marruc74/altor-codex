# Stilakor

At the end of all things ride the four - Death, Pestilence, Famine, War - and the world ends with them. The Riders cannot be summoned, but each sends a forerunner ahead, an old demon that waits out the ages on the Riders' own plane. Stilakor goes before Death. What little anyone knows of the four was copied out of a brittle codex found in the grave of Tul the Blood-Sorcerer.

## Appearance

A body of black mist, and at the heart of it a single humanoid skull, floating. To either side hang bat-wings that end in bony, clawed hands. The scent of roses reaches a man tens of paces before the thing itself does, and its voice is a whisper barely loud enough to hear that still silences the loudest hall.

## Nature

It is among the rarest of sights, for the summoning is murderous work and only a demonologist of real strength can hold it even briefly. Its power is one of those that will, in the end, help to unmake the world, and it is loosed grudgingly and never for long.

## Abilities

Where Stilakor passes, the dead will not lie still. It raises and rules them in their hundreds, lesser and greater alike, calls up death-knights and wraiths, and crews ghost-ships out of the deep. It kills with a touch, and it carries a terror that hollows out the bravest heart.
