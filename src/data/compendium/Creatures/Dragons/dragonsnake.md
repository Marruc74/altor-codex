# Dragonsnake


![Dragonsnake](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/dragonsnake.jpg "Dragonsnake")
Dragonsnakes are only distant kin to the mightiest of all creatures, the dragons, and lack most of the special powers and traits that set true dragons apart. A dragonsnake is neither as intelligent nor as rapacious as a dragon, and cares far less for treasure; instead it is driven by a fierce, gnawing hunger and devours almost anything that crosses its path.

## Appearance

It looks like a gigantic serpent fitted with a dragon's head and two short dragon-arms, each ending in four great black claws. The body is sheathed in scales with the same resilient qualities as true dragon-scale, usually red and faintly metallic in sheen though they may be almost any colour, while the belly bears no scales and is protected instead by tough, golden-yellow hide. A dragonsnake can grow up to six metres long, the body close to a metre thick at the belly.

## Behaviour

Its life is not ruled by the long dragon-sleep; it keeps an ordinary rhythm of waking and rest. Less greedy for treasure than a true dragon but all the hungrier, it will make its den almost anywhere, though it favours cave-systems and old stone strongholds where prey is easy to ambush.

## Abilities

A dragonsnake has no special powers beyond hearing and sight keener than a human's and perfect vision in the dark; its sense of smell, however, is as sharp as a true dragon's, able to pick out the race, age, sex and mood of any creature nearby. Its blood, like a dragon's, is black and burning, and ruins the weapons that draw it.
