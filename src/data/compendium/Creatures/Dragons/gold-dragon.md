# Gold Dragon


![Gold Dragon](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/gold-dragon.jpg "Gold Dragon")
Gold dragons are the most magnificent and imposing of all dragons, and at the same time by far the rarest, for they live far longer than any other kind yet still seek a mate only once in their lives. They dwell even more solitary than fire-dragons and shun both very hot and very cold climates.

## Appearance

They greatly resemble fire-dragons but are larger and heavier, which makes them less swift and supple though stronger of breath. Their scales are a touch lighter in colour and far more lustrous and metallic, the wings seeming almost overlaid with a film of gold-dust; some say a gold dragon flying toward the sunrise on a clear day is the most beautiful sight in all the world.

## Behaviour

For all their might, gold dragons are as a rule less aggressive and quarrelsome than fire-dragons. Vainer and more intelligent than their lesser cousins, they prefer for some reason to snare their prey with magic before eating it rather than grill it or tear it apart; yet the moment they or their hoard are threatened they do not hesitate an instant to use the most terrible means at their command. In legend they have done something to soften the dragons' name for mere bloodthirst: egoists who care little what humans, elves, dwarves or dark folk get up to so long as they are not themselves threatened, they lack the others' extreme hunger, and one is usually allowed at least to state one's business in the cave before being eaten. Keenly aware of their own invincibility, they seek out intellectual challenges even more eagerly than fire-dragons do.

## Abilities

A gold dragon's gout of flame reaches some seventy-five metres, broader and higher at its end than a fire-dragon's. It may live around seven thousand years, full-grown at two hundred and aged only after some five and a half thousand. In all else it is as the fire-dragon, sharing the burning black blood, the paralysing gaze, the power to instil terror, and the dragon-magic akin to mentalism.
