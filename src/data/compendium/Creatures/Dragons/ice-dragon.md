# Ice Dragon


![Ice Dragon](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/ice-dragon.jpg "Ice Dragon")
Ice dragons make their homes in the coldest climates and are happiest amid cold and snow, though they fare perfectly well in any climate, for a dragon's greatest strength is perhaps its hardiness against every element. Unlike other dragons they do not dwell in stone caves but dig out their own caverns in the ice of glaciers and polar floes. They are predators that live on polar bears, walruses, seals, frost-elves and the like.

## Appearance

The ice dragon's scales are bone-white with a glitter like sunlight on fresh-fallen snow. The rather small wings are likewise yellow-white and give a false impression of brittleness in the cold air about them. The extremely sharp, steel-hard claws are near-transparent, like ice, as is the razor-edged ridge along its back. As with fire-dragons its two horns sweep backward, but they are not sharp enough to do much harm and serve mainly for digging in snow. An ice dragon grows to about six metres long and two high.

## Behaviour

Ice dragons are neither as intelligent nor as powerful as their dragon-kin, which paradoxically makes them perhaps the most dangerous foes of all dragonkind, since they do not feel the same overweening superiority as the others. They are not, however, especially unpredictable: one may safely assume they attack any prey they catch sight of. Nor are they as bent on amassing great hoards, though they love gold and jewels well enough, for among the polar glaciers and slushy waters there is little of value to be found; what treasure they own they gather on their raids southward, in remote dwarven colonies or among the northern halls of the ice-barbarians. An ice dragon most often attacks by plunging from a great height onto its prey and seizing it in its very strong jaws.

## Abilities

The ice dragon's cone of cold reaches some fifty metres and chills everything in its path far below freezing. The harm it does is much like a burn; the skin freezes so hard that blisters form, and to grip metal cooled by the cone is to leave the skin frozen fast to it. It is warded off by leather, mithril or dragon-scale armour and by strongly enchanted shields and mail, but bare flesh caught in it risks frostbite so severe the limb must be amputated or be lost to gangrene. An ice dragon may live some three thousand years, full-grown at a hundred and fifty, and its back-spines carry no venom. In all else see the fire-dragon.
