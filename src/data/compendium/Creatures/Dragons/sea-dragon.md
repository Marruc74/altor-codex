# Sea Dragon


![Sea Dragon](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/sea-dragon.png "Sea Dragon")
Sea dragons differ greatly in form from the other dragons, but they share most of the fire-dragon's powers. They make their nests in caves and hollows above or below the water, along coastlines or on remote rocky isles; since they cannot breathe water as fish and sharks do, the lair must hold air and have access to fresh air, though its entrance may well lie below the surface.

## Appearance

The greatest difference between sea dragons and the rest is that they have no wings to fly with. The wings have instead been reshaped into great fins that let the sea dragon move through the water at astonishing speed. Its scales are grey-green, the softer underside of its long, slender, streamlined body a shimmering greenish white. Even the feet, with their fairly short claws, are well fitted to a life in the water, and on its brow it bears two large, straight, blunt horns.

## Behaviour

Sea dragons are predators that live on the larger creatures of the sea, such as whales, sharks, giant squid and sailors. With their powerful horns they can attack and stave in the hull of even a very large vessel, then help themselves to the seamen and treasure aboard. Those that dwell in coastal waters may on rare occasions venture inland, but since they cannot fly and move clumsily on foot this is exceedingly uncommon.

## Abilities

A sea dragon is superbly suited to the element of water and moves far more swiftly and surely in the sea than a fire-dragon ever could in the air. Taking a run-up from fifty metres' depth, it can hurl itself in great leaps high into the air, a favoured tactic against ships too large to break from beneath: it springs aloft, coils its body and breaks the vessel apart by plunging down upon it. It can manage only a very limited gout of flame, reaching barely ten metres and hot enough only to set a ship alight, and underwater this is useless but for the cloud of steam that may briefly disorient a foe. On land or in its lair all its prowess is much diminished.
