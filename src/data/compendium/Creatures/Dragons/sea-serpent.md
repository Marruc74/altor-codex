# Sea Serpent


![Sea Serpent](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/sea-serpent.png "Sea Serpent")
Sea serpents are vast serpent-like monsters found in most seas and in some great lakes and rivers. They are close kin to the dragonsnakes, but lack arms and bear instead a single great, sharp horn in the middle of the brow. They are bloodthirsty predators living chiefly on whales, dolphins, giant squid, sharks and large fish, but should the chance arise to vary the fare with a little human flesh they do not hesitate.

## Appearance

A sea serpent has a long, powerful serpent's body clad in thick, hard scales whose colour ranges from black through blue-grey to grey-white. The head recalls an ordinary dragon's, but in place of ears and the usual horns the sea serpent has a webbed, duck-foot-like growth of bone jutting back on either side of the head. A sea serpent can grow up to forty metres long and two metres thick.

## Behaviour

Sea serpents are drawn to rhythmic sounds, such as the monotonous thud of someone rowing. Exceedingly voracious, they will not scruple to attack small boats, swimming up from the deep to stave in the hull from below. Larger ships they usually leave alone, but may shadow them for days in the hope that someone will tumble overboard. Because a sea serpent sees poorly above the surface, it takes its prey chiefly from creatures already in the water.

## Abilities

Sea serpents see perfectly underwater but far less well above it, and so strike with much less accuracy at targets above the surface. If a sea serpent lands a bite and then overpowers its victim's bulk with its own strength, it can drag the prey down into the depths of the sea to drown.
