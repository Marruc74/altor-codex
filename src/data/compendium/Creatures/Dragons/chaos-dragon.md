# Chaos Dragon


![Chaos Dragon](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/chaos-dragon.jpg "Chaos Dragon")
Chaos dragons are the very opposite of gold dragons in their general bearing. Their name comes from the way the sagas paint them as cunning, unpredictable and malevolent, and their black shape has done much to make people link them with the creatures of Chaos. In truth they are wholly creatures of our own world, with all the strengths and weaknesses that implies.

## Appearance

The colour of a chaos dragon's scales shifts between very dark blue and black; the blue scales recall blued, gleaming metal, while the black are wholly matte and light-drinking. Their bellies are dark grey and rarely grow any treasure-crust, which leaves them more vulnerable than fire-dragons. The wings are like a bat's, covered in a very soft, thin fur. Like fire-dragons they have two curved horns, though these point forward, spiny back-scales like fish-fins, and long sharp claws; behind the head, which is crowned with a mass of little horns, runs a matted black mane all the way to the base of the tail, which ends in a heavy, spiked, club-like knob. They match the fire-dragons in size, and are if anything larger, for they live longer.

## Behaviour

Chaos dragons live much as gold dragons do, but they delight in asserting themselves through violence rather than through their admittedly keen wits. They will not scruple to lie or betray simply to savour the terror on a victim's face when the dreadful truth dawns. Nor are they as susceptible to flattery as other dragons, at least not as they would admit, while they love above all the moment their prey grasps how helpless it is before the dragon's drooling jaws. Creatures of legend and usually masters of dragon-magic, bloodthirsty, hungry and violent, they are the very sort of monster with which parents threaten their children.

## Abilities

Though much smaller than gold dragons, their gouts of flame are a match for them. A chaos dragon may live around five thousand years, full-grown at two hundred and aged after some three and a half thousand. The spines along its back are smeared with a deadly venom. In all else it is as the fire-dragon, sharing the burning black blood, the paralysing gaze, the terror it can instil and the magic akin to mentalism.
