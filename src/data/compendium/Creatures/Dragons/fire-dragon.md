# Fire Dragon


![Fire Dragon](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/fire-dragon.png "Fire Dragon")
Fire-dragons are the commonest kind of dragon and most likely the ancestral stock from which all the other kinds descend. They are no great masters of magic, but make up for that one weakness by being unusually swift and agile, even for dragons. They thrive in every climate but the very coldest, always lairing in remote caves, and burn the land around their lairs at intervals to mark their territory and wipe out any would-be intruder.

## Appearance

Red-gleaming scales cover the whole body save the belly, which is soft and yellowish unless reinforced by a crust of hoarded treasure. A full-grown fire-dragon runs to about ten metres, half of that the heavy tail, with a wingspan of some ten metres. A long line of venom-tipped spines runs down the spine to the very tip of the tail. The teeth are small, yellowed, slanted and razor-hard, the tongue long and narrow; two backswept horns and backswept whiskers frame the head, and the red, almond-pupilled eyes radiate power, certainty and cruelty.

## Behaviour

The fire-dragon is the very archetype of a dragon. Like all its kind it is famed for high intelligence and a love of riddles and wordplay, and flattery pleases it as much as the fear it loves to instil, so a fawning answer always serves a traveller better than silence or, worse, an insult, for a dragon has a fierce temper and repays a slight at once. By human reckoning their outlook is cruel, greedy and wicked: they live above all to amass vast hoards of gold and gems, will lay whole districts to waste for the terror and respect it earns, and sometimes demand great ransoms to spare a land, only to burn and plunder it anyway. With age, like all dragons, a fire-dragon tires of raiding and takes more and more to lying upon its hoard and dreaming. A dragon never surrenders; sensing defeat, it flees rather than stay and be killed.

## Abilities

A fire-dragon's gout of flame reaches some fifty metres and sets alight everything burnable in its path, warded off only by mithril, by armour of dragon-scale, or by strongly enchanted shields and mail. Anyone who looks into its eyes may be left wholly paralysed until the dragon turns its gaze away, and when it rears up to bare its teeth, hiss and smoke it strikes sheer panic into unintelligent creatures, who flee at once. The spines along its back ooze a paralysing venom, so any creature caught by the tail-lash is likely struck by a spine as well, the limb deadening within the hour. Its burning black blood ruins the weapons that pierce it, and a fire-dragon may live some three thousand years, full-grown at a hundred.
