# Lindworm


![Lindworm](/compendium/Creatures/Dragons/lindworm.jpg "Lindworm")
Lindworms are a kind of cross between dragons and dragonsnakes. They have inherited much of a dragon's look but possess few of the traits that make dragons the mightiest creatures in the world: they cannot use dragon-magic, cannot breathe fire, lack the dragon's power to instil terror, and cannot bind a victim with their gaze.

## Appearance

The lindworm has a dragonsnake's body, but fitted with wings and with two legs in place of arms. The body is clad in green-brown dragon-scale save on the underside. On its brow sits a sharp horn smeared with a deadly venom, and the same venom tips the sting at the end of its long tail. The head is rather small for a dragon's, but the manifold rows of small, slanted teeth are no less sharp for that. A lindworm can stand up to four metres high on its legs, its rolling, ungainly gait balanced by the powerful tail.

## Behaviour

The lindworm's manner of life closely resembles the dragonsnake's, and the two compete for suitable lairs, since both favour the same surroundings: cool, sheltered, dim, and within reach of food. It is a hungry predator with little interest in treasure except where it can be traded for a meal. It is fairly intelligent, though not so quick-witted as a human, and it is especially fond of horseflesh.

## Abilities

Lindworms have no special powers beyond hearing and sight keener than a human's and perfect vision in the dark; their sense of smell is as sharp as a true dragon's, reading the race, age, sex and mood of any creature nearby. The horn and tail-sting carry a deadly venom, and their blood, like a dragon's, is black and burning, ruining the weapons that draw it.
