# Swan Maiden


![Swan Maiden](/compendium/Peoples/Shapeshifters/swan-maiden.jpg "Swan Maiden")
One of the elf-kin, a swan-maiden is a graceful woman of the lakes and rivers who can take the shape of a white swan, and who can no more bear to be shut indoors than a wild bird can.

## Appearance

In her woman's form she is slender and fair after the manner of the elf-folk; at will she becomes a great white swan. The change takes only a moment.

## Behaviour

Swan-maidens keep to lakes, rivers and quiet waters and shun walls and roofs, which fill them with a caged dread. Like all elf-folk they are ageless and proof against sickness, needing only a few hours' rest a night. Gentle and solitary, they keep to themselves and never burden themselves with armour heavier than leather.

## Abilities

A swan-maiden can shift between woman and swan in a single moment, and in either shape she swims as readily as she walks. By a silent bond of thought she commands every swan within a hundred paces, and a wild flock will rise to her defence at need. In swan-form she flies swiftly and far.
