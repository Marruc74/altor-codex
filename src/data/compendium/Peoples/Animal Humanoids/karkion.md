# Karkion

The karkions are not of this world. Almost every thinking creature of Altor was made by its gods and set in its place, but the karkions came from another universe entirely, one that collapsed a few thousand years ago in a reversed unmaking. They had grown so deep in magic by then that most fled in time across the spiritual plane to the many worlds of the multiverse, and so came, among many other places, to Altor. They have wandered here for fifteen hundred years and more, named in the old chronicles of Jorpagna, Yndar and Tsunkuo, but they have always been very few and keep to the margins, and so have left little mark on humanity.

## Appearance

Like a human with a panther's head and enormous bat-wings spanning up to four metres, a little over a metre tall, with a golden lion-like pelt over all but the black, hairless wings. They wear only a loincloth and favour light arms they can carry in flight.

## Nature

Highly intelligent, strong of soul and gifted in magic beyond most things that walk, they are thought mysterious and dangerous, and most folk treat a karkion with great respect or avoid it outright. In truth they are gentle and tolerant, going their own way through life, asking respect and granting it, and letting others be. What drives a karkion is the search for wisdom, which it values above all else: it seeks broad understanding and the shape of the whole rather than narrow expertise, seeing the world as one connected web. Material things mean little to it, for they perish, while knowledge can always be carried and never wears out. The same self-possession makes a karkion a poor follower, little inclined to bend to a group's plans unless they suit its own, so that group-minded folk often call it stubborn or wilful. It is, by the same measure, almost impossible to bribe or sway: gold and worldly power leave it cold, while a rare manuscript or talk with a learned mind can hold its whole attention. It is said, too, that karkions understand dragons with surprising ease.

## Abilities

A karkion is of both sexes at once and needs no mate to bear young, though two may sire children on each other and both carry at once; a single tear in either wing robs it of flight entirely. They live very long, past a thousand years and perhaps to two thousand, and being unable to carry many books aloft they commit all they learn to memory, so that few creatures hold as much wisdom. Their own tongue is tonal, like the speech of far Tsunkuo, the pitch of a syllable changing its meaning, and it can hold finer shades of relation than any human language; their writing is built of thousands of ideograms, and no outsider ever masters it fully. A karkion's true name grows with its life and its deeds, and is far too long for others to use, so it takes a short name among the peoples it lives with.

## At the Court of Jorpagna

The best-remembered of them is the karkion the jori called Shirian, "the wise," who came to Grivela in the days of the emperor Aurelion II, around 980 fO. No one in the imperial city had seen such a creature, not even the elf Derion, the emperor's most trusted counsellor, but those who had met him spoke well of his wisdom, and the emperor took him into the palace, gave him that jori name because his own was too long even for so learned a tongue, and sought his counsel on hard decisions. Wicked men thought him a way to sway the emperor and found him impossible to bribe or persuade, caring only for knowledge and the company of the wise. When Aurelion II died, Shirian left Grivela to go on exploring Altor, and no jori ever learned where he went. The tale survives in the *Chronica Augustorum*, the chronicle of the empire written by the philosopher Tuchion and kept at Pendon.
