Lizardfolk are a scaled, lizard-headed people of the marshes and reed-country, leaner and quicker than the heavy crocodile-headed reptile-men they are sometimes taken for. They make their homes in the wetlands and in the towers and ruins that stand among them, and hold their ground with spear, blade and poison.

## Appearance

Slim and cold-eyed, a lizardman stands on two legs with a narrow lizard's head, a long tail for balance, and small hard scales in the greens and browns of the marsh. Clawed hands and feet grip wet stone and reed as easily as dry ground, and they go in little more than harness and belt, with the odd piece of armour stripped from someone who came hunting them.

## In Battle

They fight by ambush and by weight of numbers, lurking in the reeds and the spider-marsh where heavier folk flounder and lose their footing. Spear and blade are smeared with venom, so that even a glancing cut can turn deadly over a long fight, and where they hold a tower or a ruin they garrison it stubbornly. A war-band is patient on its own ground and dangerous well past what its stone-age craft would suggest, which is why a marsh road held by lizardfolk is one most travellers would rather go around.
