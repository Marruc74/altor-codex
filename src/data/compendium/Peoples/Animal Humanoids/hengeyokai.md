# Hengeyokai

The hengeyokai are people born with two forms, one human and one animal, between which they pass at will. Each has only a single beast-shape, settled at birth, be it bird or fish, cat or hound, bear, wolf, ape or rat, and the change is no illusion but a true and complete passing from the one body to the other.

## Two Lives

They are said to arise from a slip in the turning of souls, a single soul somehow seated in two bodies, and the gift is never inherited and never common, fewer than one in a hundred carrying it. A hengeyokai always knows another of its kind on sight, though to ordinary folk the two are indistinguishable, and so they live unseen among their neighbours, keeping their own counsel.

## Among Beasts and Folk

In beast-shape a hengeyokai is a true animal in strength and size, yet keeps its own mind. The wild creatures of its own kind never attack it, sensing one of their own, and only under some unnatural strain, falling senseless or the like, does it risk slipping its chosen form against its will.
