# Goblin


![Goblin](/compendium/Peoples/Dark Folks/goblin.png "Goblin")
Goblins live in tight-knit clans in great underground mining complexes and have reached almost the same level of craft as dwarves, at least in practical function if not in beauty, so they trade their work to the less skilled dark folk. Their cities are well planned and well built and can hold up to three thousand.

## Appearance

About a metre and twenty tall, with grey-green mottled skin that hides them well in the wild, slanted pale-yellow eyes, an oversized almost round head, pointed ears and patchy hair.

## Behaviour

Industrious, diligent and fiercely loyal to their clan, much like dwarves but far greedier and so desperately suspicious, posting guards everywhere out of dread that intruders mean to steal their gold.

## Abilities

Light-sensitive eyes let them see in the dark as well as a man sees by day, though they are blind in total darkness and flee from bright sun. A very few can learn magic, and those become greatly revered.
