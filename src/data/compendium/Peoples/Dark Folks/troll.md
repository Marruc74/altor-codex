# Troll

Trolls were in the world long before either men or elves. The goddess Slergolis made them out of the most enduring and stubborn stuff she could find — stone — and set them to rule the forests and the mountains by night. They began in the high north and spread from there across the world, settling into many kinds as they went: the trolls of the mountains, the forests, the caves, the snows and the swamps. For all that difference, every troll is the same dull, brute-strong, ever-hungry thing at heart, and every one of them turns to stone in the light of day.

## Overview

- **Made by:** Slergolis, the troll-goddess, out of stone
- **Older than:** both men and elves
- **Their bane:** direct sunlight, which turns them back to stone
- **The kinds:** mountain, forest, cave, snow and swamp trolls
- **Their nature:** immensely strong, slow-witted, foul-smelling, and forever eating
- **Their years:** five hundred and more

## Stone and Sunlight

A troll caught in direct sunlight begins to change back into the stone it was made from. The work takes a few minutes, more or less, and once it is done only animism can undo it — the rite that lifts petrification, worked one stripped strength at a time. So trolls sleep through the day and wake at night, and a troll that must be abroad while the sun is up keeps to deep shade and throws a great cloth over itself, and even then has to master its fear before it will go out at all.

## Body and Long Life

A troll's hide is thick and warty and tough, most often brown, sometimes greenish or yellowish, and paler the further south its kind is found. Its hair is never washed nor combed, darkening to grey and at last white with the years, and cut only by hacking it off when it grows too long. Trolls see superbly in the dark and hear and smell keenly. A tail like a cat's grows the whole of a troll's life, so that you can read its age in the length of it: the young are born soft, hairless and tailless, and do not get a tail until about seventy. They run to fat, live past five hundred years, and grow more sluggish as they age, until moss and mushrooms take root on the oldest of them. They breed slowly — seldom more than three young, often many years apart, carried for two years before they are born.

## Ways and Faith

Wherever they live, trolls live much the same: by robbery and a little rough farming, almost never by herding, since beasts bolt from the stink of them. The family is the centre of everything, and holds together through all its squabbling. A young troll is reckoned grown when its tail reaches the ground as it stands, at around a hundred years, and then it leaves to find another troll-family with a daughter of the right age and works a year and more to prove itself. They are omnivores in the fullest sense, eating anything at all, thinking folk included, though they leave ducks alone — too much trouble to pluck — and refuse meats that give them gas. They love spices but are far too lazy to grow them, so saffron and cumin are among the most prized things a troll can be robbed of.

Trolls are not a devout people, and call on Slergolis only when they are in trouble. Their one great festival is midwinter, when they gather to eat and sing: the males snort in time while the females wail the tune, and the songs are mostly boasting, about a troll's looks, its house, its children, until everyone has quarrelled. They feast on raw meat with every spice they can lay hands on, finish with fly-agaric, wash it down with ale and mead and wine, and round off the night by going to let off steam on the nearest village of men or halflings. A tribe will often have a shaman, usually a woman, who keeps the link to the goddess and sits in judgement alongside the chiefs.

## The Kinds

The **cave trolls** are the commonest and the closest to the first stock, sheltering underground by day. The **forest trolls** are the most settled, living in small communities and farming their toads and trollcap mushrooms. **Mountain trolls** are the largest, ranging the highlands; **snow trolls** are the rarest, white and shaggy in the polar wastes; and **swamp trolls** keep to the marshes and the fens.

## Magic and Foes

Magic is all but unknown among trolls — their spirits are too dull for it, and few would trouble to teach them — but the rare troll who masters even a little is prized as a shaman, and the sight of a spell is one of the surest ways to frighten a troll into giving up a captive. Common belief credits trolls with healing as fast as they are hurt, even regrowing what is cut away; the truth is less, for they cannot regrow a lost limb, though uncut wounds knit in less than half the time another creature would need. The elf-folk loathe them for their cruelty and their grotesque appetites, and the stench of them is more than wolf-men, cat-men or rat-men can bear. Men and dwarves leave them alone so long as they keep from the caravans and villages, but a band of elves can wipe out a whole troll-family simply by falling on it in daylight, when the trolls cannot flee.
