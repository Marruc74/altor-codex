# Boggart


![Boggart](/compendium/Peoples/Dark Folks/boggart.jpg "Boggart")
The smallest of the dark folk, yet dangerous in a swarm, falling on passing travellers in a great mob. They prepare for every fight by bathing in a vat of black dye and sharpening their prized battle-forks, and live in big tribes in old badger-setts and mole-runs they have seized and widened.

## Appearance

Small and scrawny with a long narrow head, large faintly red eyes and bodies mostly bone and sinew under dark, leathery skin, with thick nails that curl into claws. They wear little, favouring long necklaces strung with dead enemies' teeth.

## Behaviour

Fizzing with restless energy and stubbornly single-minded, they cannot count past three or four, are endlessly curious, and will rifle a camp for anything useful, metal forks above all, even with the owners standing right there.

## Abilities

Keener sight and hearing than a man and a bloodhound's nose for tracking, with good night vision but poor sight in sun. They can never learn magic.
