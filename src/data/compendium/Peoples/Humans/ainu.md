The Ainu are the first people of Jih-pun, and quite unlike the Jih-mono who came after them: fair of skin, dark of hair, and heavily bearded, where the islanders of the south are black-haired and smooth-faced. They were the hunters and gatherers of the island in the age before rice and before the newcomers crossed from the mainland.

## Driven North

When the Jih-mono came over the sea in their reed boats they pushed the Ainu out of the good southern country, north past the isthmus into the cold, windswept and barren land of Beikuo and up into the mountains. There the old people have lingered ever since, a dwindling and despised remnant on the hard edge of the realm, living by their herds and their fishing and their hunting.

## A Conquered Folk

The Ainu have never been content in their exile, and now and again they have risen against the islanders who took their land, only to be put down with great slaughter. They keep their own ways at the margins of a country that has all but forgotten it was ever theirs.
