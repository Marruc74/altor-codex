# Wolf-Rider

The wolf-riders are a fierce, warlike people of Ereb Altor's forests. Small and gifted at hiding, they have all but escaped the notice of the wider world. They are said to descend from humans who were first twisted to serve some evil purpose, and a wolf-rider lives and dies for one thing above all: the wolves it rides.

## Appearance

A wolf-rider has blond hair, pale eyes and pointed ears, with a skin covered all over in heavy grey fur. They are slim and lithe, standing about a metre and a quarter and weighing little more than a child, and being barbaric by nature they often use their clawed hands as tools.

## Behaviour

Wolf-riders live in the woods, on flat ground that suits their mounts, usually in dens and burrows they dig out themselves. A band numbers fifteen to thirty grown wolf-riders and as many young again, ruled by the strongest among them; the weaker keep the household while the rest are raised hard from infancy to weapons and the saddle. They hunt for their living and keep to themselves, shunning all dealings with other creatures and living as loners. A stranger who blunders across a hunting party risks being taken for game, and since they trade with no one, weapons, armour and gear are all welcome plunder. Only in times of famine or sickness do they raid human settlements, and then as a last resort, for they fear reprisal. Their two mortal enemies are the cat-people and the wood elves. An outcast may try to begin a new life among humans, though its wild nature makes that hard.

## The Wolves

Each wolf-rider lives in close fellowship with its wolf, a breed apart from the common kind, larger, stronger, hardier and cleverer, and able to understand its rider's speech. The bond is for life: a wolf will bear no one but its own rider, and should either the wolf or the rider die, the survivor never takes another partner. Many a capable warrior is left this way to a quieter life, guarding the settlement, when the one creature it would ride is gone.
