The lindskiarn, also called the pharynkier, are a people of unknown origin, and most who have studied them think them magical in some way. They came as conquerors and were broken as conquerors, and where they once raised a dazzling city they now cling to life in a few hidden valleys. They were feared as warriors and admired as craftsmen, but today they are nearly gone, so few and so secret that anyone who stumbles on one usually takes it for a giant or a troll.

## Appearance

The lindskiarn are built for war: large, standing well over a head taller than a man. Their smooth yellow-white skin is almost furred to the touch, softer than leather but tougher and longer-lasting, and it shields them from cold and wind. Their feet and lower legs are like an elephant's, giving a low, steady stance, and a second thumb drawn down from the little finger makes their grip sure. Their eyes sit wide apart, so they see more to the sides than a human, dwarf or elf. At close to three hundred kilos they can match almost any humanoid, and they are strong, quick and agile for their bulk.

## Behaviour

They are fiercely aggressive and worship battle, yet they fight with cold discipline. Because they can speak mind to mind, they need no shouted orders: a battle is planned beforehand and then carried out in silence, every warrior moving as part of one will. They favour the spear or the longsword behind a shield and fight in tight squares, trusting weight and order over arrows, which they almost never use. When they do speak aloud, they speak jori, which has fed an old and false belief that they are some lost kin of men.

## Craft

For all their love of war, the lindskiarn are among the finest artists and craftsmen in the world. Their metalwork rivals the best dwarven forging and their ornament the best elven art, and the splendour of their lost city was still spoken of with wonder long after it fell.

## Pharynx and the Wars

The lindskiarn came down out of the north in the 200s fO and built, in central Zorakin, the brilliant fortress-city of **Pharynx**. From it they conquered widely, fighting always on bronze and shunning iron, until **Valien Rikesbyggare**, the Realm-builder, turned the tide against them. Their wars ended in 55 fO with their defeat, and the people were all but wiped out. Perhaps a hundred survive today, hidden in two secret colonies in the western Aidne fells and the eastern Snoar Forest, where anyone who finds them is killed or enslaved to keep the secret. Mostly they are forgotten, and the few who glimpse one take it for a resar or a troll.

## Abilities

They take no harm from ordinary fire, and only half from other spells, and they speak mind to mind across a whole company at once. Iron, though, is poison to them: its touch alone saps their spirit, and long contact will lay one low, so they handle captured iron weapons only through thick gloves. No iron was ever found in all of Pharynx, and that, more than anything, is why many hold the people to be magical in origin.
