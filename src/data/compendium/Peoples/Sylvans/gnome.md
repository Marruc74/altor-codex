# Gnome

Gnomes are a small and seldom-seen elf-folk, dutiful caretakers of the world's quiet corners. When the world was made, Mother Earth wanted a keeper for its small daily errands, and so shaped the First Gnome, dutiful, stubborn and endlessly pliant. His children took their forms from the places they settled in, and once a gnome's shape was set it could never be made over again, like clay that has been fired. Seven kinds are known today, and there are thought to be more.

## Common Ways

For all their differences the gnomes share much. Save the mountain-gnomes they are pacifists who will not touch a weapon, and only the grave-gnomes work any magic. Their food is mostly green stuff, the shore-gnome alone breaking the rule for fish. Most live solitary lives, and only the mountain-gnomes and the ice-gnomes gather in any number. They are wary of strangers by nature, even hostile, but a gnome who counts you a friend is loyal past question. Like all elf-folk their souls are reborn when they die, and a gnome who has shirked its duties in life comes back as one of the fallen: a dark-gnome if it was male, a rå if it was female.

## The House-Gnome

The commonest kind, found on the greater part of country farms, who keeps the rats and mice from the grain and the cat from the milk so long as he is well treated and left his food a few times a week, with a sweet now and then. He lodges under a threshold, furnishes his home from the farm's cast-offs, and is a stern and pedantic little soul. Peaceable though he is, he deals a magical clout to any who vex him, never a killing blow, and if he is neglected he takes his revenge: at best he drives off the livestock and moves away, at worst he burns the whole farmstead to the ground.

## The Mountain-Gnome

The largest and strongest of the gnomes, who lives much as the dwarves do and counts them great friends, with even finer craft than theirs and a broad, drastic, sometimes deadly sense of humour, so that coming uninvited upon a mountain-gnome hold is a poor idea. They live in clans of many score under a king, who wins his crown in a duelling rite through a roofless maze of pits and traps that the whole clan can watch from above, and who may be challenged once a year. Alone among gnomes the mountain-gnome bears arms, favouring a little morning-star of his own make, and alone among them he makes a fit adventurer.

## The Shore-Gnome

A gnome of the sea-banks, who lines his burrow in the grassy tussocks with wreck-wood and lives on what the sea gives, breaking the gnomes' green diet with fish and a soup that none who taste it forget. He is the most bohemian of the folk, a fine painter and musician, which the other gnomes find faintly suspect, and he loves a board game above most things, so that he and the garden-gnomes are firm friends. Unlike his kin he is not shy of people, and a person may seek a shore-gnome out for a talk.

## The Forest-Gnome

A gnome of well-kept woods, and a wood soon becomes well-kept once he arrives. He lodges beneath an old tree, an oak for choice, lives there with his wife, and his children are always born as twins. He is shy but genial and loyal once known, a true keeper of the balance, friend to halflings, dryads, wood-elves and satyrs and no friend at all to the wood-rå or the orcs. He is very shy of people, and holds the duck to be the finest joke nature ever made.

## The Grave-Gnome

A house-gnome who followed his master into the grave, having been honoured above the common run, and was remade by higher powers into something deathless and able to work spells. He dwells in a quiet corner of a churchyard and grows ever more withdrawn, prone to strange fears and fixed ideas, and with his magic he can make a graveyard very dangerous, above all if he takes it into his head that visitors mean to rob the graves. He is the rarest of all the gnomes, and there is never more than one to a churchyard.

## The Ice-Gnome

A gnome of the far north, on the tundra and the bare fells where the burdus hold sway, who lives a wandering life in bands under a chieftain, most often the eldest whatever their sex, each band holding to its own stretch of country. They dwell in skin tents and travel by sled drawn by the tinnuit, a tame lemming-like beast that they also ride. At the heart of all they do is kinnuicht, their duty to the band, to the ancestors and to the land in their keeping, taught from the cradle, and nothing in the world can make an ice-gnome break it.

## The Garden-Gnome

A gnome of calm, well-tended gardens, who passes his days strolling, smelling the flowers and gathering fallen leaves, and since a tidy garden gives him little to do he runs easily to fat. Three things trouble his peace: the cat, which reckons him a rare delicacy and which he mostly escapes by sheer nimbleness; the shore-gnome, who turns up now and then and draws him into board-game binges that last for weeks; and the kwah, a tugging restlessness that comes upon every garden-gnome at once about every fiftieth year and drives them out to wander until each finds a mate. At the first snowflake he builds himself a nest in a shed or byre and sleeps until the coltsfoot blooms.

## The Fallen

A gnome that neglects its duties is reborn as one of the fallen. The males come back as dark-gnomes, like the black-folk in temper and morals though no kin to them, smaller but craftier, bound to no fixed place, poor warriors who make up for it with wit and can dupe the rå into doing their violence for them. The females come back as the rå, of whom the wood-rå is only the best known; there are mill-rå and mountain-rå and sea-rå besides, and rumour of farm-rå and grave-rå, each bound to her own place as a nymph is bound to hers.

## The Yule-Gnome

Last there is the so-called Yule-Gnome, dismissed by the learned as vulgar fancy that only children believe, a vast, booming-laughed pseudo-gnome of impossible girth said to appear but once a year.
