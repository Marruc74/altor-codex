# Fire Pixie


![Fire Pixie](/compendium/Peoples/Sylvans/fire-pixie.jpg "Fire Pixie")
Fire-pixies are a most peculiar elf-folk that live in deep forests, often together with dryads, najads or wood elves, who provide them with food. They are also called will-o'-the-wisps, or irrbloss - not to be confused with the drowned marsh-ghost that shares the name.

## Origin

A fire-pixie is thought to come into the world when a mighty elf-magician, or one of the Earth Mother's own messengers, is reborn into so small a shape. The soul's great force is too large for the tiny body and spills out around it as a corona of flame, and that fire is the very measure of its life: dimmed, it can be coaxed brighter again, but spent wholly, the pixie dies with it.

## Appearance

A fire-pixie looks like a very small human with pointed ears. It normally drifts about inside a little transparent, faintly glowing ball of fire, but the fire is wholly under the pixie's control, so it can vary its brightness and heat at will, and it can never go out while the pixie lives.

## Behaviour

Practical jokes and general pranks played on strangers in the forest are a fire-pixie's greatest delight, though they are not malicious by it. They never deliberately try to harm anyone or lead them to ruin, and should one of their victims come to real grief they will likely call the wood elves for help.

## Abilities

Fire-pixies are immune to all fire and heat and cannot be blinded by bright light, but they take double harm from water- and cold-attacks. The pixie's ball of fire works much like a fire-spell, and it can make the flame fiercer at the cost of its own strength of will.
