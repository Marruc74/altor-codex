# Nymphs

Nymphs are nature-spirits in the shape of beautiful women, each bound body and soul to a single place in the wild, a tree or a spring or a crag that is at once her home and her life. She is less a guardian of nature than nature itself given a face and a will, and she cannot stray far from the thing she is tied to. Kill her in the flesh and she returns before long; the only way to be truly rid of a nymph is to destroy her home, to fell her tree or divert her stream, and that kills her outright.

Whatever her kind, a nymph shares a few gifts. She can heal a hurt with a touch. She can lay a charm on men, who fall helplessly in love and cannot bring themselves to harm her, which is how the crueller sort lure them to grief. And she can slip between her solid body and a spirit form invisible to ordinary eyes, so that she may only be struck at while she wears flesh.

## The Kinds

Nymphs are told apart by where they dwell. The dryad keeps to a forest glade and the one tree that is her own. The naiad lives in spring, stream, waterfall or pool, and can melt into the water and become it. The oread belongs to the high country, the crags and caves, the meadows and valleys. Their tempers run the whole way from gentle to murderous, often according to how the world has treated the place they guard.
