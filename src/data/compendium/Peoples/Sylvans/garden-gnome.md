# Garden-Gnome

A gnome of calm, well-tended gardens, who passes his days strolling, smelling the flowers and gathering fallen leaves, and since a tidy garden gives him little to do he runs easily to fat. Three things trouble his peace: the cat, which reckons him a rare delicacy and which he mostly escapes by sheer nimbleness; the shore-gnome, who turns up now and then and draws him into board-game binges that last for weeks; and the kwah, a tugging restlessness that comes upon every garden-gnome at once about every fiftieth year and drives them out to wander until each finds a mate. At the first snowflake he builds himself a nest in a shed or byre and sleeps until the coltsfoot blooms.
