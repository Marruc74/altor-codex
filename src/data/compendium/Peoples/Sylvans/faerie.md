Faeries are small, mischievous sprites that live in beautiful, bright forests, gathering berries and nuts. They almost always carry their magical bows, the better to defend themselves.

## Appearance

Faeries are very small and elf-like, with gossamer, transparent insect-wings on their backs. They have pointed ears and almond-shaped pupils, just like elves, and a snub nose, with long, straight hair and pale skin. They rarely wear more than a loincloth.

## Birth

A faerie is not born of any union but springs from a wild-rose bud. A glow gathers about the bud, magic pulses in the air, and the faerie bursts forth already grown and already lovely, where a newborn elf is not. It is said only one new faerie comes into the world in a hundred years. The colour of its hair follows the flower it sprang from - white roses give white hair, red roses fiery red, a yellow water-lily golden hair - and so does its size, most standing between six and ten inches, a few a little taller. A newborn reaches its full height within a week or so, then seeks out a private corner of the forest to call its own.

## The Earth Mother's Charge

The faerie-folk were set on Ereb by the Earth Mother to be the wardens of life and growing things, to guard her creation from harm. Few of them remember it. For all the long years given them, they are mortal as any other living thing, and the weight of such a charge can wear on the soul, so most have laid it aside and live instead to play tricks on passing travellers. No one has yet recorded a single case of a faerie lifting a hand to defend a beast or a tree.

## Behaviour

Faeries of both sexes are a high-spirited folk who love to make sport of any humans and beasts foolish enough to wander into faerie ground. Their pranks and jokes, much like a peerie's, are never cruel in nature, and should anyone come to harm by them they grow at once remorseful and do their utmost to set it right. On rare occasions one or two faeries become faithful companions to a particular person, whom they both help and drive to distraction with their endless mischief.

## The Faerie Rings

In spring and summer the faeries gather in their hundreds to dance hand in hand, ringed in drifting mist, on glades and meadows close to the earth's own power - and as they dance, they feed. The dance must be held on such a place at least once a month through the warm seasons, and at summer's end comes a longer winter-dance that stores up energy for the cold months; a colony that neglects its feasts grows dim and dwindles away.

For a living thing the ring is a perilous blessing. Within ten paces or so the air is charged with a quickening energy that closes wounds, eases small sicknesses, and leaves a pleasant lightness like mild drink. But whoever runs into the very heart of the ring takes far too much, and if he so much as drinks a drop of water in the ten days that follow, he drops dead on the spot. The cure for this faerie-ring sickness is to fast - which is why the common folk, who dose the sufferer with broth and so kill him, hold the faeries in such ill repute.

## Abilities

Faeries can turn invisible at will and can read the thoughts of other intelligent creatures. They usually carry two kinds of magical arrows, one that sends a victim to sleep for some hours and one that makes him forget all that has happened in the last hour; the arrows must pierce any armour to take effect. Beyond these, a faerie never feels the cold, however thin its dress, and seems blessed with an absurd, unfailing luck in all it does - though no one can say whether that is a magic of its own or only good fortune. Rarest of the kindred are the faeries born wreathed in a corona of flame - the fire-pixies, or irrbloss, which are a folk of their own.
