# Najad

Najads, or spring-nymphs, make their home in a spring, a waterfall, a small forest tarn, a rapid or the like, and when in the water can dissolve their bodies and become water themselves. There are really several kinds: nereids of rapids and springs, oceanids of the seas, potameids of rivers and limnads of still pools. Like all nymphs a najad is bound to her home and unkillable except by its destruction, the diverting of her stream.

## Appearance

In her human form the najad takes the shape of a very beautiful, pale-skinned young woman with long black hair and green eyes.

## Behaviour

Najads are perhaps the most malevolent of all nymphs. With a charm and a light flirtation they can lure men out into dangerous currents, and the men can do nothing to resist.

## Abilities

A najad has total command of her home and of the nearest stretch of water around it, where she can wholly govern currents, waves and flows. Like all nymphs she can heal with a touch and turn a charm on men, can shift at will between physical and spirit form, invisible in the latter, and can be hurt only in physical form.
