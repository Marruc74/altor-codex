The wood elves, who call themselves the satari, build their homes in the crowns of great trees. Their magicians can make a tree grow into the shape they want, so they need little else to build with, and the silver-linden is the best wood for it. They live quietly as hunters and gatherers and grow a fine grain called eniri. Their dress and woodcraft let them avoid all contact with other peoples when they choose to, and their villages, set around a central glade with the dwellings hidden in the leaves and reached by tunnels or rope-ladders, are almost impossible to find unless you already know where they are. They are the best archers of all the elf-folk.

## Appearance

A wood elf has blond hair with a faint green tinge, and dresses in the greens and browns of the forest, which makes them very hard to see. On everyday clothes they care little for jewellery or ornament, preferring plain breeches, a short jacket and perhaps a hooded cloak. They almost always carry a bow and a short sword.

## Behaviour

Wood elves are very shy of strangers, and their best defence is simply to go unseen, so they hide rather than fight. If discovered and threatened, a wood elf flees away from its home so that no pursuer is led there. Lost travellers are set right by signs left in nature, the elf never showing itself, while dark folk who enter its woods are killed from ambush with the bow. What a wood elf dreads is close combat. Should it come to that, the elf trusts to its skill at moving fast and unseen through woods it may have known for centuries.

## The Ensouled Forest

The forests the satari live in are not lifeless. Each has a soul, the tivariol or great mother, which knows everything that happens beneath its trees. In every tribe one elder woman, the ivari, speaks with the forest through the tivariol, and she feels it when the woods are hurt. The first wood-elves raised sacred groves called rani, rings of tall silver-lindens set on a hilltop, and the deep magic worked there turns aside any outsider who wanders too close.

## The Amari

The amari are the nature-mystics of the satari, the elves closest to the hidden powers of the wild and the best spell-weavers among their people. They live in small, well-hidden villages and dress in blue, green or white, the newly initiated in white and the eldest in blue. Their warriors, the cerini, are skilled warrior-mages who go to battle with satyrs, centaurs and faeries at their side.

## Abilities

In the forest a wood elf is very hard to spot, all but vanishing into the cover. Like all elves they have keen sight and hearing, see well by night, are immune to disease and do not visibly age.
