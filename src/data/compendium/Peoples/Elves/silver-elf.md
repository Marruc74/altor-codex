# Silver Elf


![Silver Elf](/compendium/Peoples/Elves/silver-elf.jpg "Silver Elf")
The winged silver elves live an airborne, nomadic life as hunters and gatherers. They live in tribes of thirty to fifty and feed chiefly on fruit, berries and birds caught in great nets. Silver elves are not especially enduring fliers, and so raise large herds of pegasi to use as mounts.

## Appearance

A typical silver elf stands about a metre and a half tall, with fair skin, silver-blond hair and pale blue eyes. The most striking thing about them is the great feathered wings on their backs, fully five metres across when spread; the feathers range in colour from bright blue to white, and the pattern is as unique to the individual as a fingerprint.

## Behaviour

Silver elves are a hardy folk, tough against the strains of an airborne life and used to fending for themselves and surviving most situations the wild can throw up. This gives them a fairly high opinion of themselves, and they can at times seem recklessly self-assured; one easily gets the sense that a silver elf is a man of few words and great confidence. Their one great weakness is an almost panic dread of enclosed spaces, so that they can scarcely bring themselves down into cramped underground caves where they cannot trust their wings to bear them clear of danger.

## Abilities

Silver elves can freeze a cloud by an effort of will, hardening all the cloud-stuff within thirty metres to the hardness of wood for a week. Their flight lets them fly at a fair speed, though not for very long before they must rest. Like all elves they are immune to disease and do not visibly age.
