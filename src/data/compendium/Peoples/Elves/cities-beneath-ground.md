# Cities beneath ground

The cave elves made their homes where no other elf-folk would follow, deep under the mountains. Their cities run for miles through natural cave-systems they have widened over the ages, in caverns that rise hundreds of metres overhead and never see the sun. Glowstones, magical fire and ever-burning flame light the halls in place of it.

## Gates and outer wards

A city is reached by one great gate, with hidden back-ways cut and disguised in the rock around it for escape and for surprise. Just inside lie the outer wards, where the warriors are quartered. At the first alarm they pour out to hold the gate and the tunnels, since dark folk and dwarves keep pressing in from the deep in search of new homes.

## The main cavern

The heart of a city is its main cavern, where an underground lake is ringed with dwellings and worked halls. This is where the cave elves live and where their smiths keep their forges, built to stand great heat. Their treasure is held apart, sealed in a vault away from the open halls.

## Mines and the river

Below the halls and the smithies run the mines, watched by the city's elite patrols and lined with cells for those they take. An underground river threads the whole place, carrying water to the halls and giving the cave elves a road through the dark that no surface enemy knows how to follow.
