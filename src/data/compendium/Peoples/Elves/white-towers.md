# White Towers

The high elves keep apart from the world in towers of bone-white stone, raised alone on empty plains where almost nothing else lives. From the outside a tower shows no door and no window, only a smooth pale wall that climbs out of the grass. The great gates answer to no hand. They open to strong magic and to thought alone, and within them a silence holds the halls that has lasted as long as the towers have stood.

## Within the tower

A visitor who gets that far comes first into the antechambers, whose walls carry the tower's history, and, the high elves say, its future as well. Past them the great halls run vast and high, joined by deep shafts and pillared galleries that climb through the whole structure. Near the top lie the gardens, kept far above the plain and grown thick with magical plants that have no business living so high or so far from anywhere.

## The Heart

Far below the tower lies its Heart, the source that feeds it. Two of the high elves give themselves to the Heart and pour their own power into it without rest, and the tower lives only so long as they hold. These two are called the life-givers. When the high elves speak of a tower falling silent for good, this is what they mean.

## The gate

A tower's one entrance is a fortress in itself, built so that forcing the outer door wins nothing. Behind it waits a run of guarded courtyards, narrowing passages and walls that move, and an intruder is led deeper into a trap rather than into the tower proper. The gates are held by the Keepers, the red-clad warrior order that is the only part of high-elf society trained to arms, and the only one that leaves the towers for long.
