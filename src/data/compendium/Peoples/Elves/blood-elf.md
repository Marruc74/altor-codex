Blood elves are an elf-folk that live, among other places, in the forests of Nargur in Ereb Altor, dwelling solely in settlements high in the crowns of giant oaks, and they are very rare. By legend they are an elf-folk abandoned in elder days by Tevatenu for dreadful crimes against their own kin, and so are far worse tainted by the world's evil; they fled into the deep forests of central Ereb and have lived there as long as any can remember. Like all elf-folk they are thought to be immortal, and since few visitors have ever seen a young one, they are believed to breed very slowly. They live in giant oaks quite literally, petrifying the trees and then hewing stairs and rooms into the trunks and building small huts among the branches.

## Appearance

Whether or not blood elves are truly elves, they share many traits with ordinary wood elves: the same shape of ear, the same cat-like pupils, and they move, if anything, even more silently. Yet there are distinct differences: their skin is almost yellowish, the whites of their eyes wholly red, their teeth black and pointed, and their fingers and toes tipped with claw-like nails. Their hair is usually long and black, gathered in a long, well-kept braid down the back.

## Behaviour

In temper and conduct blood elves and ordinary elf-folk are absolute opposites. Blood elves are hunters one and all, living on meat with only the barest supplement of berries and fruit. They are bloodthirsty and seem at times to kill purely for the high pleasure of it, and have been seen to show inhuman cruelty, said to have killed deer with their bare hands and eaten them raw. Strangers are seldom welcome, though now and then received as guests with some suspicion; other elves they loathe above all, a loathing fully returned, and a blood elf will kill a wood elf without hesitation, even from behind at a distance. So few of any people even know blood elves exist that they are met very rarely.

## Abilities

Like all elves they have keen sight and hearing, see well by night, are immune to disease and do not visibly age. Some blood elves, about one in ten, can speak mind to mind and read the thoughts of others.
