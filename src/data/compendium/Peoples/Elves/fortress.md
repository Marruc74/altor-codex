# Fortress

![Dark Elf Fortress](/compendium/Peoples/Elves/fortress.jpg "Dark Elf Fortress")
![Dark Elf Fortress](/compendium/Peoples/Elves/fortress-2.jpg "Dark Elf Fortress")
![Dark Elf Fortress](/compendium/Peoples/Elves/fortress-3.jpg "Dark Elf Fortress")

The dark elves raise their fortresses high in the mountains, wedged between the peaks where the air is thin and the cold bites. Each is crowned with demonic statues, and the bones of beaten enemies hang from the black walls as a warning to anyone who climbs that far.

## The approach

The only road up is watched from the foot. Watchtowers stand along it, each held by a handful of dark elves, and past them wait the Guardians, statues woken by magic that take up swords of judgment against any intruder who reaches them. The gate itself is a great horned skull, climbed by a long stair.

## Temple and keep

At the fortress's centre stands the temple, its floor a pentagram worked in black galvorn, where the lords bind the powers of Chaos to their will. Beyond it rise the baileys and the keep, the main hall with its throne and the palace of the fortress lords. Under all of it lie the hollows, where the dark elves keep their prisons and torture-rooms, their smithies and mines, and the catacombs in which their embalmed rulers are laid.

## The roost

A fortress is also a roost. The trivani, the dragon-lizard riders who are the dark elves' elite, keep their flying mounts in caves cut into the mountainside, and bring them down onto the field when the host rides out to raid and to plunder.
