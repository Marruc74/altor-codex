# Talking Animals

In some corners of the world there are beasts that speak and reason, set apart from the dumb animals around them. The tales say that at the dawn of the world the god who made it gave a few of his creatures the gifts of speech and thought, and their line has carried those gifts ever since.

## Nature

A talking animal is cleverer than the common run of its kind, with a quicker and deeper mind, but in every other way it lives as its kind does — the same haunts, the same shape, the same needs. A talking wolf is still a wolf; a talking raven, still a raven. How many there are, and where they are found, depends entirely on the beast.

## Their Place in the World

They are free folk. A talking animal is never ridden, never yoked, never kept as a beast of burden, and it does not serve as a dumb animal serves. But to those it comes to love it is loyal past any hound, and that loyalty, freely given, is worth more than any harness. Now and then one falls in with adventurers and walks the roads at their side as a companion and an equal.
