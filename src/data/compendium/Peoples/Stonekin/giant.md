# Giant


![Giant](/compendium/Peoples/Stonekin/giant.jpg "Giant")
Giants dwell in remote forest and mountain country where they can be left in peace by other peoples. They lead a simple life as woodcutters, farmers or herders, with a fairly plain culture; they make simple tools themselves and get the rest from the far more advanced gargants. Giants live out in the open, but usually keep some kind of wind- and rain-shelter near at hand. Like many peoples they have spread over much of the world and adapted to different climates, and far in the north one finds the ice-giants, with a shaggy pelt and wholly untroubled by cold.

## Appearance

Giants stand close to six metres tall, but are otherwise much like humans. They tend to grow very stooped with age, and dress mostly in hides, almost never wearing armour. On the rare occasions they are goaded into a fight they use, by preference, whole tree-trunks as a kind of two-handed club.

## Behaviour

Giants are in the main peaceable and friendly toward those who treat them well. Sadly that peaceableness easily turns to hatred of the smaller races when those send adventurers into their lands to kill giants. The few giants one meets in human country are nearly always ones driven from their villages for being too quarrelsome, which is why many believe giants a sour, cruel and aggressive race.

## Abilities

Giants have no special powers, and like all stonekin they are long-lived, two to four centuries, and loathe to fly, sail or ride.
