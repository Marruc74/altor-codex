# Dwarf


![Dwarf](/compendium/Peoples/Stonekin/dwarf.png "Dwarf")
The most civilised of the stonekin, perhaps of all humanoids, are the dwarves. Their underground cities and mining communities are usually far better ordered than those of humans, and without doubt the dwarves are the more industrious, disciplined and loyal of subjects. Dwarves from every corner of the world feel a far greater kinship than, say, humans of different cultures, and with it comes a great need for fellowship; an outcast or solitary dwarf is almost certainly a very unhappy one.

## Appearance

Dwarves are short, around a metre and a quarter on average, and robust, with brown or red hair and beard, the beard a mark of status. At home in their underground forges and mines a dwarf wears very light clothing, but the commonest image of the dwarf is of a well-armoured warrior in an exquisitely smithed coat of mail, with a two-handed axe or war-hammer, his favoured weapons, and an open helm.

## Behaviour

Dwarves hold five basic ideals: solidarity, chiefly toward their own race, firmness of principle, industry, endurance, and stability of both outward and inward calm, this last the very trait that gave the stonekin their name, for a dwarf is very rarely frightened, flustered or unpredictable. They are a very proud people who stand fast by their ideas, which easily leads to conflict with others, the more so as dwarves are rich, well-armed, hard in a bargain, self-assured and easily greedy. For all his long life a dwarf has a good memory and never forgets a wrong or a favour. They are industrious to a fault and suffer when left idle, working chiefly at mining and craft, above all smithing of every kind.

## Abilities

Dwarves see in the dark even where there is no light at all, as far as a human would by torchlight, and like all stonekin they live two to four centuries.
