# Troglodyte

Troglodytes, or cave-men, live mostly underground, sometimes among dwarves or gargants but usually in old abandoned complexes. They shun sunlight and rarely leave their caves, though it does them no real harm. You know you are nearing their lairs by the wall paintings of enemies and prey, for they believe that to paint a thing is to summon it or call its fortune, so a painted hunting scene brings good hunting.

## Appearance

About two metres tall, heavy and long-armed, with sparse black or brown fur, a low brow and a heavy jaw. Poor craftsmen, they dress in stolen clothing and armour and fight with clubs and stones, scalping their enemies and hanging the scalps from their belts.

## Behaviour

They fear all that does not belong to the underworld and often attack intruders without warning, though those living among other underground folk are far calmer and serve as guards, ore-bearers and water-pumpers.

## Abilities

They see in the dark like dwarves, and live some fifty to seventy years.
