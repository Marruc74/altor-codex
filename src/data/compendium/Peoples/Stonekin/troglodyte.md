# Troglodyte


![Troglodyte](/compendium/Peoples/Stonekin/troglodyte.png "Troglodyte")
Troglodytes, or cave-people as they are also called, live mainly underground, sometimes alongside dwarves or gargants but most often in old abandoned complexes. They shun sunlight and so avoid leaving their caves, though they take no real bodily harm from it.

## Appearance

Cave-people are about two metres tall, powerful and long-armed. They have a sparse black or brown pelt over almost the whole body, a low brow and a heavy jaw. They often dress in stolen or captured clothes and armour, since they are no craftsmen at all, and likewise fight with clubs or stones unless they have managed to get hold of some other weapon. They regularly scalp their foes and hang the scalps from their belts.

## Behaviour

Troglodytes fear all that does not belong to the underworld, and creatures that make their way down into their dwellings are often attacked without warning. One can tell a troglodyte dwelling is near by the paintings and inscriptions on the walls, usually of enemies and prey, for the troglodytes believe that to paint a thing is to summon or call it forth, so that painting hunting-scenes brings good hunting. Those that live among other underground stonekin are far calmer, having strangers about them constantly, and often serve as sentries, ore-bearers or water-pumpers.

## Abilities

Troglodytes have the same darkvision as dwarves, but unlike other stonekin are short-lived, fifty to seventy years.
