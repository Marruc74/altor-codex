# Stone Biter


![Stone Biter](/compendium/Peoples/Stonekin/stone-biter.png "Stone Biter")
The stone biters are the stonekin who best earn the name. They not only live in stone and by stone, they are in fact made of stone. They live solely underground, where they work the mines, no heavy task for them, since they can dig through solid bedrock with their bare hands much as a human digs through an earth bank. The other strange thing about them, and what gave them their name, is that they live on stone, eating it, preferring ore-free minerals, so that their mines and their larders often lie in quite separate parts of the mountain, sometimes leagues apart, joined by perfectly dug passages. What they do with all the metals and gems they dig out, no one quite knows; they certainly do not sell them on.

## Appearance

Stone biters are humanoids about two and a half metres tall with an extremely powerful upper body. They have all but no neck, the head resting straight on the shoulders, so that they can scarcely look to the side without turning the whole body. Their hide is most like a suit of thumb-sized, asymmetrical slate plates, black but glittering when the light catches it. They have no body-hair and cover themselves only with a loincloth, and being practically made of stone they never use armour and only rarely weapons, a spear or a poleaxe, preferring to fight with bare hands.

## Behaviour

Like most stonekin the stone biters are shy of strangers, and after the cyclopes they are perhaps the most fiercely set against all visitors. No outsider is willingly let into their cities, which are well barred and guarded by elaborate traps. The few times they leave their caves it is solely to seek new homes, going out in small patrols of a handful, trying to avoid all contact with other intelligent creatures.

## Abilities

Stone biters have perfect vision in the dark, but see everything very blurred in sunlight, like a badly short-sighted human, and they hear very poorly. Like all stonekin they are long-lived.
