## Plot

Beaten and hunted after a spying job gone wrong in the southern province of Sama, a band of masterless sell-swords flees northeast into the mountains of Mawako, travelling by night through one of the last wild forests of the south. On what they hope is their last night on the run they come upon a glimmer of light in a deep ravine, and creeping close they see a rite they were never meant to witness: a priest in white, a seemingly dead girl, a shimmering disc of light, and a horned shadow reaching out of it for the girl's heart.

Then the bank gives way under them. They fall, the rite is broken, and the thing that was summoned, denied its offering, takes the priest himself, driving out his soul and settling into his body. The fugitives wake at the bottom of the gorge with aching heads, stripped of everything but their clothes, and a goatherd looking down at them from the broken edge.

## A Village That Will Not Believe

Led to the nearby village of Matsuka, the strangers find no welcome and no belief. The acting elder takes their story for the excuse of men robbed by bandits, and sets them to draining the rice-fields for their keep. Meanwhile the cattle of the valley are being found dead in the dark, their heads torn off, and the folk mutter about a savage bear, while the truth lairs in a woodcutter's cabin deep in the trees: the possessed priest, hunting and feeding by night, and his eight ninja with him.

An old travelling priest named Ruiyi sees further than the villagers. He has come over the mountains seeking the temple's stolen books, and he takes the strangers out by night to watch the killer at his work, only for the murders to be laid at their door the next morning. A scar-faced "masseur" bears false witness, the council finds the outsiders guilty, and they are condemned to be boiled in oil. Ruiyi vanishes before he can be taken, but in the small hours a bundle is thrown into their cell, and his letter sets them on the only road left to them.

## The Jewel-Heap

The demon, the letter explains, can be undone only by the rite written in the Rui-dien, the Jewel-Heap, a dragon-hide book the creature holds but cannot destroy. The trail runs two days through the monster-haunted Spider-Leg Forest to the temple of Lushin on its high hill above the river, where the stolen girl is held and the rite of sacrifice may yet be tried again on the night of the eleventh moon. There the fugitives must slip past or cut down the eight false priests, find the book among the library's thousand volumes, read it by the strange grace of a dragon's tongue, and either work the cleansing rite on the hollowed-out priest or put silver through him, before he can finish what the broken rite in the ravine began.
