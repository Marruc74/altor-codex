## The Fifth Conflux

The third chapter of the long campaign turns on a single, strange necessity: to save the world, the heroes must first leave it. The hero Great Stonefather - who alone can hold together a war-torn young kingdom and so keep the fifth conflux from falling to the deathless sorcerer Shagul - is held captive in another world entirely. The road to him runs out of Altor, down through a drowned underworld of acid, and across the grey bridge that lies between all worlds.

## Out of the World

The way is barred by gates no force can open, and won only by riddles: a colossus toppled by the dust of a hero's rotted axe; a sea of acid raised into wave upon wave until its wall breaks. Beyond the last gate there is no road at all - only a thread of sand cast out into a red and starless nothing, and at its end a god's invented world building itself up around the travelers as they fall.

## The Crystal Bull

That world is Bythos, made by a failed and exiled god from the stolen life of the living castle - which now lies broken in it as the Crystal Bull, its life split among the written names of seven archons. To make the bull whole again each name must be undone, one by one, until the hero can be carried home. There is no certain victory here, and much that wears a kindly face is not: the surest rule, as a departing friend warns, is to mistrust everything that is met beyond the world.

## The Way Home

The road back leads through the failed god's own hall, where a guest-book waits to enslave any who sign it and a pen of many colors makes real whatever it draws. Carried into Altor at last, the woken hero folds into a great golden crystal - a chrysalis from which, in time and at a song, an unconquerable keep may grow - and the company turns north at last toward the conflux, and the final chapter to come.
