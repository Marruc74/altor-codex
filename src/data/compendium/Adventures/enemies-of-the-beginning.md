## The Missing Son

Groffotor Destanior, son of the commandant of Krilloan, has vanished from his bed without a sign of struggle. His father pays a ransom and keeps the matter quiet to avoid a scandal, but his daughter Yrea is not content to wait, and she hires the band to find her brother, in secret, as her private guests. What begins as a search for a kidnapped boy becomes something far larger, for Groffotor was taken not for ransom but because someone needed him gone.

## The Conspiracy

While the band searches, the city burns. A mob is hunting the necromancers of Krilloan, dragging them from their homes, firing their houses, and the watch cannot stop it. None of it is chance. Behind the riots stands the animist Jesefael Bamilit and a handful of confederates: a harmonist whose music turns a crowd to fury, a councillor-priest who poisons the holy water so the faithful leave church hating the necromancers, an illusionist, a tattoo-witch. For more than fifty years Jesefael has nursed his hatred of Krilloan's necromancers, and now he means to see them destroyed.

## The Cloned Dragon

The heart of his scheme is a dragon. Long ago the beast Morriart was slain at Krilloan; from a single scrap of its hide Jesefael grew it anew, hatched it from a giant egg, and had a witch age it by her art to full and terrible growth. The old dragon's soul was driven back into the new body by a drugged and unwilling spiritist. Armed with a sun-crystal that withers the undead, Jesefael means to ride the dragon against Tannatopol and lead the roused city behind him, and so end the necromancers and their isle together.

## The Dragon Cave

The trail leads from the animist's pillar-tower in the city to a cave beneath the mountains, joined to it by a pair of teleporting stone plates. There the band find the proof of the whole plot in Jesefael's own diary, the caged and mutated beasts of his workshop, the kidnapped Groffotor, and at the last the dragon and its hoard. The dragon may be undone by a gift of Guazzo's that breaks any enchantment, or simply outrun; and if Jesefael is not stopped in his cave, his crystal will betray him in the end, for brought near Tannatopol's well of death-magic it turns on its bearer and destroys him with it.
