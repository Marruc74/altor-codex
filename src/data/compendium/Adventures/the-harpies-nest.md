## The Island

The Harpies' Nest is a jagged volcanic spire some forty kilometers southeast of Hakkai, rising about a hundred and twenty meters straight out of the rust-red water of the western Copper Sea. It takes its name from the harpies that nest in its crater, and it serves the pirate baron Piet Steeljaw as his hidden base.

### The Reefs

Three-quarters of the island falls sheer into the sea, its cliffs guarded by reefs of jagged rock, so that landing by force is near to suicide. A dozen ships have learned this the hard way, and their picked-clean wrecks lie scattered on the reefs as a warning to the next. Only one navigable channel threads the reefs and currents to the inside, leading to a sheltered cove and a soft sand beach where the slopes of half-melted lava have worn down into a livable valley.

### The Green Valley

Underground springs feed fresh water across the valley, and around the dead volcano the growth is thick and green, near to a half-jungle, with abundant game. High on the slope lies the Blood Spring, a tarn of clear red water, ever bubbling, its drink sweet enough to swallow; the pirates flask it and hurl it at the ships they board so the victims think themselves drenched in blood. A red stream runs down from it toward Muck Cove, where the fort stands.

### Those Who Hold It

Steeljaw and his band were driven here one dark winter night in 608 by an Erebos squadron, slipped almost unharmed into the cove, and have used the island as a robber-base ever since. His blockhouse fort stands above the cove, and a tall stone tower keeps watch over the eastern sea. The crater above holds a colony of some thirty harpies under their leader Fghrkahx, who keep an uneasy bargain with the pirates - the fort left alone in return for prisoners - and on the southwest peak a pair of sea griffins nests, made vicious by the harpies that torment them. Somewhere in a hidden glade the baron has buried his treasure, warded by the griffins and reached only with a salve that turns the beasts away.
