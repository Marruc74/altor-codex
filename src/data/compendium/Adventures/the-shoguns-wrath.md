## The Plot Behind the Province

Word reaches the shogun that an unknown organisation is worming its way into the mines of Nara, the iron and gemstones of which arm and enrich the realm, and the daimyo Sorin Arima sets his trusted trouble-shooters to root it out and answer to him alone. What none of them yet knows is that the rot has a single source: Ozuno, an oni-prince who plots from a fortress hidden inside the Fire-Peak volcano, and who means to be rid of the daimyo and rule Nara through a double of his own making.

Ozuno's web runs deep. His agent Akino works the capital as a humble monk; his ninja Hidemasa carries word and bribes through the northern villages; the daimyo's own beloved concubine is his spy; and the proud rival daimyo of Gihata has secretly sworn to him and raises an army for the day the trap is sprung. Around all this turn quarrels the oni did not make and does not wholly control: a ring of bribe-taking merchants, a pragmatic yakuza clan, and an apostate forest-witch who has gone into business for herself.

## Three Threads

The module draws together three investigations. In **Shadow of a Murder** the adventurers are guests at a jizamurai's estate when their host is shot dead on a morning ride, and they must untangle a starving village, a sacrifice-cult and the killer-oni sent to silence them, in a hunt built to be hard to solve and harder to close. In **The Golden Ring** a merchant is knifed in his bed the very hour they come to question him, and the threads of his death lead through a tea-house, a warehouse and an astrologer into a ring of bribery feeding two hidden masters who do not know they are enemies. Neither tale ends neatly, by design, for they are pieces of a longer game.

## The Burning Mountain

Only in **The Final Reckoning** does the source come clear, when a monk's dreams, sent by a troubled spirit of the borderland, point past the infiltrated temples to a power in the Fire-Peak. Armed with the temple's blessing and a healing wand of white ivory, and aided if they are kind by a fur-hunter who takes a hawk's shape, the adventurers climb into the lawless southern mountains and storm the oni-prince's fortress above its lake of fire, to cut Ozuno's web off at its root before the false daimyo can ever take the Moon Castle.
