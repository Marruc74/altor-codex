## Plot

To those who make camp one evening in a quiet forest glade, a shimmering figure steps from the tree line after supper: a fair-haired man in light-blue robes who names himself the wraith of sir Valland de Klochard. Four hundred years ago, he tells them, the land had no king and great families schemed for the throne. Valland hid the royal regalia to keep them from the usurper Aldorg Zeen, and for his trouble was ambushed, killed, and cursed by a necromancer's staff to wander the earth without rest. The staff was then broken into three pieces and hidden away.

Weary of his long centuries, the ghost has reasoned that joining the three pieces at the exact spot where he died, the very glade where he now appears, will at last break the curse. But bound to his grave, he cannot fetch them himself. In return for the king's lost treasure, he asks the travelers before him to gather them.

## The Three Roads

The pieces lie scattered in three distant places: the orc-held ruin of Castle El Nhassiv in the contested borderlands; the island castle of the necromancer brothers Zeen, a week's sail past pirates and sea-beasts; and the Kings' Grave-Temple, a cursed royal necropolis where the usurper Aldorg himself lies entombed with the last piece driven through his heart.

## Let Me Die

When the three pieces are joined again at the glade, thunder rolls, lightning leaps from the wraith into both ends of the staff, and a final bolt from the sky undoes the ghost at last, leaving silence, an icy and indestructible staff, and the buried regalia for the brave to claim.
