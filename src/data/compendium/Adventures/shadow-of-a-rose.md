## The Chalice

Late one evening, on the way home through the pleasure-quarter of Krilloan, the adventurers cross paths with a man running for his life. He is Auxin Mendel, a courier, and the killers at his back catch him in the alley and put a knife in his spine. Dying, he thrusts a small nailed-up box into the nearest hand and is gone. Inside is a chalice of old silver, ringed with grinning skulls, and the band has just become the keepers of the holiest relic of the cult of Imária, the goddess of the undead. Mendel had brought it to the city to have it destroyed; instead it is now theirs, and everyone who wants it knows where it went.

## The Brotherhood's Hunt

First come the offers, then the thefts. The Black Rose Brotherhood, a fellowship of witches centred on the isle of Tannatopol, tries to buy the cup, then to steal it, then to take it by trickery. Among their tools is Hilja Adriagoles, a paralysed medium who can leave her body and slip her spirit into another, and who rides unseen inside one of the band, turning their own number against them. None of it works. The band holds the chalice, and the Brotherhood's patience runs thin.

## When the Dead Walk Openly

It is the vampire Lerajie who breaks the stalemate, and the city with it. Tired of subtlety, he raises a dead man into a death-knight and sets it to murder everyone who looks like the adventurers. For the first time in living memory the people of tolerant Krilloan see the dead killing openly in their streets, and the long peace between the living and the necromancers shatters in a single night. Lynchings begin; the necromancers, demonologers and witches who were tolerated yesterday are hunted today, and the city slides toward riot.

## Rodomelantos and the Black Library

The Brotherhood is as furious as the band, for Lerajie's recklessness threatens them all, and so the two old enemies make their peace. Here the tale turns: those who set out to destroy a coven of witches end by fighting at its side. The witches open the way to Rodomelantos, the demon-lord Hemaquiel's own world, where the band must answer to the goat-footed prince himself before he sends them on into the Black Library. There, in a labyrinth that shifts behind them and borders a hundred worlds, guided by a riddling librarian and a dagger that scents the dead, they run the vampire to ground at last, and learn that the line they thought ran between good and evil was never where they believed.
