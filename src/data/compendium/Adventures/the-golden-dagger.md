## The Killings

A cold autumn night in Kitteln, and a man lies dying in a back-alley with a gold dagger between his shoulders. His last words are that the killer came from above. He is Sibraid, the mapmaker who charted the district's sewers, and he is the third to die this way in three months. Each victim was left with a blade worth a small fortune, each killing fell in the last week of its month, and no one ever saw it done.

## The Trail Below

The clues all point downward. Sibraid's clothes are filthy from wading the drains, and on his body is his own map, one section freshly inked, ending at the very alley where he fell - a dead-end, which means the killer came from behind it or down through a street-grate. Search that stretch of sewer with enough patience and a forgotten passage opens beneath Atur Kastelli, the castle, climbing by a winch-hung shaft to an overgrown corner of the garden. There the way is barred by the guard, and the only other course is to watch the tunnels and wait for the last week of the next month, when the killer comes again.

## The Vizier's Secret

The murderer is Arkondor II, grand vizier of Krilloan. Years ago he found the old shaft out of the castle, and ever since he has slipped into the city by night in careless disguise, hunting Kitteln for the thrill of it. The three dead had the bad luck to know his face. Naming him is the danger: kill him and the court will have the truth out of the heroes and see them hang; denounce him openly and the scandal buys them nothing but a knife in the dark. Carried quietly to Ordo Magica it might earn a small reward - and the memory wiped clean afterward. Pressed in private, the vizier will grant almost anything to keep his secret, which is the most dangerous road of all, and perhaps the most rewarding.
