## The Sorceress Who Would Not Die

In the cold far north of Barbia, the wind-priestesses of Fadarim read in old legends what they had long forgotten: the immortal sorceress Siratsia, believed slain seven centuries ago, still lives. Born the last of a dead tribe and raised by a hermit magician, she banished her own aging into a timeless dimension and set herself a vast and terrible purpose - to scour all evil from the world and raise a paradise of her own, in defiance of the gods. Now she is but a month from completing the work of seven hundred years, and the priestesses recruit the heroes to stop her.

## The Long Trail

The hunt runs through truths and lies in the temple's great library, and on through the sorceress's old haunts: her razed first fortress and its walled-up dead, her overgrown childhood home with its deathless rose, a decoy fortress built only to waste their days, and the cyclone where a bitter old mage keeps the blinded east winds. It is no simple thing, for she has bewitched spies across the land, and her true home is an illusion so near to real that it leaps to a new place every midnight.

## The Book and the Fire

The goal is not to kill her - she is too easily underestimated, and another might find the book - but to seize the white spellbook and destroy it, and no power but dragon-fire can. So the heroes must reach the ancient white dragon Alad-Arch, who has waited since the dawn of time for exactly this task. Whether they tear the book from a monster or from the world's one hope of a paradise without evil, the dragon's fire is the same: a scream that echoes forever, and nothing left to win but honor.
