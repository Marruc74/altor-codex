## The Dark Beneath Gripeborg

Gripeborg had stood empty for more than thirty decades, shunned by the village at its foot, until Marquis Thorvald Grip took back his ancestral seat and set a stonemason to restoring it. Within weeks the old dark woke. After an inspection of the wine-cellar the marquis cried out, fell white and ice-cold, and soon vanished from his locked room; a laughter "as from a man without a throat" rose from the cellar stair, a window shattered, the torches went out, and the servants fled. By the time adventurers arrive, drawn by an invitation to a feast that will never be held, by an old half-burned warning, or simply by a terrified village, nothing has been heard from the castle in a week, and Master Runek can only point them at the barred cellar door from which the scraping and thudding still comes.

## The Crypt of the Priests

Below the wine-cellar lies what the castle was raised over: an ancient burial-crypt of high priests three hundred years dead and more, and the demon that has made it its lair. The moment the adventurers step inside, the cellar door seals itself shut behind them by a power no force will break while the demon lives, and the only way out is down and through. The crypt is a locked chain of trials, each leading to the next: a rat-haunted passage where a dead man's bones hold a riddling verse, a hall of giant spiders among the skulls, a pit of fire that cannot be leapt and will not be quenched by magic, a room of skeletons that wake at a touch, and the chamber of the Heart of Darkness, a bat-headed onaqui held there by the demon.

## The Sword and the Demon

The crypt is built as a single puzzle. A serpent from the skeleton-room, turned to stone at the onaqui's black pillar, becomes the key that opens the iron chest where Whaladrugh lies; a pair of giant iron-shod boots from the same chamber lets the adventurers cross a trap of petrifying clay to reach it. And the opening of that chest wakes the master of the castle: a three-horned demon the size of a house, the laughter from the stair, which holds the marquis chained at its side and is proof against every weapon save the one just drawn from the chest. Only Whaladrugh can cut it down, and only with the demon dead does the sealed door above open and the marquis, healed of his deathlike sleep, come home.
