## Plot

It is told that travelers passing through a forest were once stopped by cave elves with a plea. A wizard named Telach had appeared in Syrta Bay and conquered it with orcs and ogres, and he meant to raise warriors enough to crush the elves at last. Most of his troops were away raiding to the south, so a small band might slip onto his island of Syrtan where the elves' army could not. The elves lent the adventurers a small boat and asked one thing in return: that their stolen rings and the Great Book of Wisdom be brought back undamaged.

Landing past the shark-haunted reef, the adventurers crossed the island - a burned and haunted village, a riddling sphinx at the river, a guardhouse crowded with orcs - and found at its heart a five-sided black tower. The tower was no mere keep but a sentient, evil artifact that had corrupted master after master. Hours before the travelers arrived, Telach had botched a ritual and loosed a demon, Whaisszg, which now raged trapped on the upper floors while Telach hid in the caves below.

## The Black Duel

The trail led down into Telach's cave-stronghold for the final reckoning. He was a cunning, slippery foe: he fought only until half his strength was spent, then fled through a secret passage to the harbor and sailed off, raising the alarm so the corridors filled with guards - unless he judged he could win, in which case he rallied his orcs and ogres for a fresh attack. When at last both Telach and the demon were slain and word carried back, an envoy of Mother Earth came to break the tower's evil soul, the elves' blessing tearing the building apart. Yet those who covet such a tower and keep it for their own find its evil working on them in turn - the seed of a darker tale still to come.
