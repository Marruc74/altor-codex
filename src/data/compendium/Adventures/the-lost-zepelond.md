## The Wall and the Poison

Phantango daMelasti is the worst sort of noble - rich, idle and hungry for a name - and when a lucky wind carried his airship clear across the Trakorian Sea to HOXOH, the praise went to his head and he flew on, over the dark land of Kargom, and was not seen again. Word comes back from the spies that his zepelond has fallen to the drudd'hocider, the foul poison-brewers of that country, and that they see in it a weapon: a way to carry aloft and rain down a brew that would kill the Eniaken, the vast living fungus-wall that alone keeps Kargom's horrors penned away from the rest of the islands. The heroes must go into the swamp to stop it, with a half-wild turncoat for a guide and, if they have the sense to ask, the silent monks of the Hypherer order at their side - for the Hypherers walk noiseless among the wall's great threads, and know its moods as no one else does.
