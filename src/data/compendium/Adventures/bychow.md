Bychow is an out-of-the-way fishing village clinging to the inaccessible western coast of Elmor, home to some four hundred and fifty souls. To a visitor it seems poor and unremarkable, but its people guard a secret worth killing for: every one of them is a shark-man.

## The Secret

The villagers are gill-throated, sharp-toothed shapeshifters who endure cold water and take a shark's shape at will; in the old tongue they are the bychowites, "those who never smile." Their fishing boats are empty decoys, for they have no need of them. Their true trade is salvage: they dive the wrecks dragged into the Gideon Chasm and strip them of their cargo, which has made Bychow one of the richer villages of the isles, though it hides its wealth carefully behind a show of want.

## The Squid's Den

The village inn, the Squid's Den, is kept by Antor Grimtooth, a hard and hostile man who watches every stranger that comes. Any traveller who guesses too much of the truth is quietly murdered and offered up to Shranck, the giant shark-god worshipped at the reef, where the people make their sacrifices, and not always of fish.
