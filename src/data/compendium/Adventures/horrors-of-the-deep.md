## Plot

Hard-luck adventurers take on poorly paid work on the frozen northern frontier and sign on as sled-drivers to haul the herders' annual reindeer-meat taxes south for the king. At the herders' camp they hear of Dracht, a dwarven mining village sacked by dark folk two months past, its fabled riches perhaps still buried in the ash. Tempted, they desert the expedition and climb to the ruins - and find only burned halls and the frozen, mutilated dead.

Deep in the mine a bound spirit offers them the dwarves' treasure if only every villager's corpse is gathered before its stone face. Two bodies are missing, lost down an old shaft, and the search for them draws the adventurers out of the mine entirely - down into a vast primordial cave system that is the only trade road between coast and mountains. There, beneath the starving cavern-city of Nordsal, they uncover the truth: the "robbers" choking off the caravans are not bandits but the walking dead, led by a baron who had himself remade into a death knight.

## A Curse Beneath the Mountain

Seven centuries ago the dwarves of Dracht bound an offended mountain spirit into the carved face on their treasure door, to serve as its guardian. The binding hides a trap: lay all the villagers' corpses before the face within two months of their death and the spirit goes free - but the face bursts and the whole mine comes down in a killing earthquake. The spirit, wanting only its freedom, leads any who find it straight into that snare.

Nearer in time, a baron built a castle in a deep cavern so his sun-shy daughter could live out of the light; she died young in an underground stream, and the grieving family left it empty. A century later a necromancer academy took the castle, and their leader returned from the south with a ritual to raise a death knight. Their patron, the ambitious Baron Boltshield, had himself transformed - and the thing that rose kept his will, grew far stronger than the mages feared, and butchered them all, raising the dead as his servants.

## The Caravan Crisis

Boltshield means to conquer the whole underworld. His method is patient and grim: ambush the caravans in the lightless Margoroth cavern, drain the guards and raise them as skilled, near-lifelike zombies, and with that growing army fall upon a starving, isolated Nordsal. By the time outsiders descend that far, no caravan has reached the city in over a month, the food and fuel are running out, and the goblins are being blamed for it.

## Facing the Dead

There is no single road to victory, and meeting the undead head-on is deadly, for every one of them sees perfectly in the dark. The dead are undone only by patience and cunning: by the cave-elf Envoy's Curse Undead laid upon the death knight, by iron filings flung over the bane-men to silence their magic, by the stolen ring of keys that locks the dead inside their own vault, or by picking them off one at a time. Even triumph is perilous, for though the death knight and his bane-men fall, the zombies carry on their last standing order - to kill every living thing in sight.
