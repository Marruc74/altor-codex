> *A note on this adventure: "The Sea Wolves" is one of the oldest Drakar och Demoner scenarios, written before the Ereb Altor setting existed and first set in our own Viking-age Europe, sailing from Norway to raid the coast of Spain. It has been placed in Ereb here: the searovers' homeland stands in for Jorduashur, the wild coast they raid is the lawless north of Kardien beyond the Aidne-bergen, and the old world's peoples and tongues are recast as their Ereb kin.*

## The Raid

The tale begins in Halle, the great port of Jorduashur, where a sea-captain named Erik Järnnäve, son of Styrbjörn the Strong, is gathering rowers for a long raid south. The prize is the coastal stronghold of a robber chieftain, Luis Candelas, far away on the wild northern shore of Kardien. Erik can pay in silver up front and a share of the plunder on the way home, and he has one thing no one else does: a guide. El Jabato, a Kardener whom Candelas once took captive and sold into slavery, was bought back by Erik in the north, and will lead the raiders to the fortress in return for the means to get home to the south of Kardien once the work is done.

## The Voyage South

It is three hundred miles of open water from Jorduashur to the Kardien coast, and the sea sets its own terms. Wind and weather decide how fast the ship runs and how hard the crew must pull at the oars, and the provisions will only just last a careful crossing. Other ships share the water: merchantmen bound north, who would rather trade than fight; and now and then a rival raider, Torbjörn Starke, whose dragon-ship is the twin of Erik's and whose hold is worth taking if the grappling-irons can be made to catch. Worse than any ship is the horned sea-serpent that may rise alongside, the water boiling before it rears at the rail to pull men under. All the while the crew trade rumours of the fortress ahead, some true and some only meant to unnerve.

## The Overland March

The landing puts the band three days' march from the fortress through the harsh north country of Kardien, beyond the Aidne-bergen, where the law does not reach and the dark folk move freely. The road holds its own dangers by day and night: territorial bears in their mountain dens, a sounder of wild boar in the glades, a barrow-ghost that demands gold and magic as the price of passage, and trolls that storm the camp after dark to put out the fires. A party of svartalfer carrying a wounded comrade may beg for healing, and how the band answers decides whether they walk away with an enchanted sword or with ten vengeful dark folk on their trail by night. Off the road lies an optional warren of cave-orcs under the chieftain Tammam, who guards a true hoard behind a false one and a poisoned throne.

## The Robber's Fortress

The stronghold is a timber hall ringed by a palisade of oak, its gates barred from the inside, so the raiders must go over the wall or find the hidden tunnel that climbs up into the lowest caves. Within are two-score brigands, their horses and plunder, and a handful of ill-used slaves and prisoners who will help the band rather than their captors. Magic guards the deeper rooms: stone statues with dragon bodies and twin raptor-heads, each bound with a spell that strikes or seizes anyone who tries to pass, until a dispelling quiets it. The vault holds the real wealth, silver and gold behind a knife-trapped chest, with potions and an alchemist's dragon-blood and dragon-hide besides. Below it all spread the half-dug caves the brigands never finished, where a corpse-eater waits at the bottom of a dry well, giant spiders lair among broken arms, and a Daegurth lies still as a pool, waiting for a hand to reach for the ring at its heart.

## After the Raid

What the band carries home depends on how much they can move and how they treated those they freed. The captured slaves and prisoners can be set loose with horse and arms or sold in Halle; El Jabato, if he lives, must be sent south to Kardien as promised; and the loot, the silver, the fine arms, even a rival's whole dragon-ship taken on tow, has to be sailed back across the same hard sea. The pay is shared out at journey's end, with the captain and his steersman taking the larger portions and anyone who claimed a magic prize taking less of the coin. And there is always another hook waiting in Halle for a crew that has proven it can raid and live.
