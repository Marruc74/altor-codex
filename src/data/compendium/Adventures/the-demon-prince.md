## Into Inferno

The trail that began with a merchant's lie ends by leaving the world behind. Chasing the fugitive temple lord Luciano through the black portal in the slain dragon's cave, the heroes fall out of Altor and into Inferno - the infinite plane of the damned, nine circles strung along a cleft of fire and ice. They come down on an icy ledge above the Abyss with the Sword of Truth still in hand, and that blade is the one thing that keeps them whole, for the very air here works to turn the living cruel. From the first hour they are strangers in a country where every road is owned, every kindness is a bargain, and the only constant is the war of all against all.

## The Prince's Game

What the heroes cannot see is that they have been pieces on a board since the day they were first hired. Two demon-princes have warred for ages through mortal proxies in Altor: Târg of the Shadows and Malkûth of Chaos. The merchant houses, the assassin brotherhood, the death-cult in the church, even Luciano himself - a servant of Malkûth only feigning service to the dragon - were all moves in that long game, and the heroes were Târg's unwitting hand throughout. Now the prince means to spend them on one last move. Every patron and rescuer they meet in Inferno, from the necromancer Videric in the Abyss to the strategist Akaron at Târg's court, is steering them toward a single act: the death of Malkûth, so that Târg may seize the Circle of Chaos and set his own heir upon its throne.

## The Throne Over the Vortex

The road runs down through the Abyss and the dead circles - plague, war, and pain - to the heart of Chaos, where Malkûth's palace drifts on the storms about the Vortex. A discontented commander smuggles the heroes into a hall floored with glass above the churning red light, and there the Prince of Chaos rises to fight them, and Luciano with him. Only the arms Târg forged of his own blood can bite a prince, and when Malkûth falls at last the throne shatters and the Vortex darkens. Then Târg arrives to set his heir Târgkrizt in the dead prince's place, dissolves the borrowed weapons, and frees the heroes from their bond. They are returned to the dragon's empty cave with its hoard for a reward - older, a little wiser, and having won a demon-prince's war for him without ever once being asked.
