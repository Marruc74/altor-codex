_Chapter 2 of the Altor Codex chronicle._

## Plot

It began at the inn in the village of Outskirt, where three dwarves burst in one evening with a strange tale. Crossing the Mirror Lake two days before, they had seen the sky turn red, the mists part, and an island appear, crowned by a great tower atop which a towering, fire-licked creature roared until the ground shook. The dwarves wanted nothing more to do with it, but a ragged villager named Sirter was quick to peddle an old map of the island, spinning tales of a wizard's hoard gathered three centuries ago.

The truth was that the island had for four hundred years been a haunt of power-hungry magicians, who prized it for summoning demons because it lay under a mist that never lifts and was a wellspring of magical power. Its last master, Sith, had died only two days earlier, struck down with his apprentice when they called up a death demon to ask the secret of eternal life. Those who crossed to the island climbed its sacrificial tower and dived into the central lake to enter Sith's flooded stronghold, where his demons and guardians still waited among the traps.

### The Misty Island

For four centuries the island in the Mirror Lake has drawn power-hungry magicians, who prize its never-lifting mist and its deep well of magic for calling up demons and elementals. Its last lord, Sith, perished but two days before the dwarves crossed the lake, struck down with his apprentice Verkon when they summoned a death demon and asked it the secret of eternal life. On the island's stony northern end stands a twenty-metre white marble tower topped by a pentagram of five blood-channelled altars, where their charred bodies still lie.

### The Village of Outskirt

Outskirt is a remote forest village of some fifty folk, three days from its nearest neighbour, supplied with metal by three travelling dwarves and watched over in matters of law by its bailiff. It is here the dwarves' tale is told and Sirter's map changes hands. Beneath the village's stone chapel the priest Rabindranath guards a buried alf-forged sword, the Dragonslayer.

### Families of Outskirt

Some fifty villagers live in Outskirt, most of them in a handful of households. At the smithy are the smith Vagn and his wife Belinda, with their son Jore, who helps at the forge, and their daughter Elusia, reckoned the loveliest maiden in the village. The largest holding is the great farm of Warge and his wife Luvia, home to their children Biorn, Vagnhild and red-haired Rosiall, along with their livestock and the watchdogs Bitske and Gråpäls. The mill is kept by the miller Fyndve and his wife Velinda, who have no children of their own.

Among the other farming families are Evke and Yvalinda with their two small ones; the pious Ikald and Ävyn, whose son Boltar is the terror of the village young; Anbel and Lycinda, with a brood of six; and Baliol, father of five daughters, who keeps so stern an eye on their company that the village lads are forever gathering at his gate. Living alone are the priest Rabindranath, the hermit-alchemist Mifalodor, the hunter Hårde, and old Castellan, who at eighty fills his days scolding the children. The inn, the Three Stags, is run by the two former outlaws Bryte and Ulvar, while the bailiff Fogden represents what little authority reaches so remote a place.

### The Underground Complex

Sith's stronghold lies beneath the island's central lake, reached through a flooded cave mouth. Its upper level is a maze of narrow, water-logged cracks where the death demon Akrae stands bound to a pentagram, the ghost of the warrior Cadal lingers, and the old cave troll Okvir hoards the gear of those he has killed. The lower level is the magicians' own work, a place of black and white marble, pentagram-sealed doors, deadly traps and a hidden library, guarded by the riddling fire demon Khaa.
