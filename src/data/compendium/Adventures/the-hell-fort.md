## The Cleansing That Was Never Late

A band of mercenaries, just dismissed from the rebel army of a war-torn land, gather for a farewell feast - and over the talk and the beer they catch a thread that will not let go. A hundred years past, the archmage Vicotnik sealed the land of Nidland behind its great wall and foretold that in a dozen dozen years he would loose a crusade to cleanse the whole world. That span is reckoned to have passed with no crusade, and the world breathes easy. But Nidland's years run three days longer than the rest, so a year fewer has passed within its walls than without: the Cleansing has not failed. It is almost here. To learn it, and one day to break it, is the heroes' fated charge.

## The Road to the Wall

The road north runs through ravaged country and a robber-haunted forest, where a courteous elf-prince beset by dark elves repays his rescue with a boon that will matter more than any blade; through the magic-muddling Grynner mountains and an oracle's fiery prophecy; to a dwarf-realm whose one open gate is barred to men. Turned away there, the heroes must pass instead through Ghan-toth - an abandoned dwarf-city now swarming with orcs - and down into its mines, where a runaway ore-wagon casts them into the forgotten tomb of a king older than the elder races, and into the keeping of the relic that will decide everything.

## The Hell Fort

Over the Great Wall and into Nidland, the heroes are taken - as the prophecy meant them to be - and marched to the Hell Fort, a black fortress of ten thousand soldiers where the disciple Achatoth waits to offer them up so his masters may seize the power to begin the Cleansing. Their one escape is the thing they carried out of the tomb: the imprisoned Punisher, who will free them at the last - and then loose itself upon the fortress in a fire that levels everything for a quarter-mile, while a summoned pegasus bears them clear and the demon turns inward, toward the archmage, and the tale to come.
