## The Stolen Blade

Three hundred years ago the archmage Tatarus forged himself a black sword that left him almost impossible to kill, and led an army of dark folk and the dead to within a stride of taking all of Lamania. The mages of Zorla answered it with a sword of noble silver, the hero Matiros called Tatarus out to single combat, and the duel had barely begun when the old dragon Haraxidix fell on them both, burned the two men to nothing, and carried off both blades to his cave. Dwarves later walled the dragon in and stole the swords back, and the crown locked them away in the royal vault. There they sat, side by side, until the day the black one was simply gone.

The heroes are hired to find it and to drag the thief to Zorla. What no one at court knows is that Tatarus never truly died. He lingered as a death-wraith, hid himself away in the north, and two weeks before the hunt began he slipped into the vault and took back his own sword. He could not touch the silver one, which is warded against him, and that is the heroes' single advantage - and the reason they are sent carrying it.

## Four Trails

There is no straight path here, and the order is the heroes' to choose. One suspect is the dragon, and that trail ends in a sealed cave near Bergborg at a heap of bones, useful only for the people met along the way. The duck merchants of Wissi rose so fast that the council suspects them, and a night in their counting-house turns up the best clue of all: the mage they are hunting is north, in Lövlän. In a southern marsh, three murdered brothers who once paid for the silver sword's forging lie sealed in their grave, and if they are handled gently they will admit the thing that matters - that Tatarus walks. And in a lonely tower a chivalrous knight keeps a duke's daughter prisoner and will say only that she is evil, which happens to be true: she is the archmage's creature, raised since childhood to be his queen.

All the while the land itself feels wrong. The sky stays grey, the people are sour and wary, the beasts are too bold or too shy, and two ravens are never far off. It is Tatarus pressing down on everything, and it does not lift until he falls.

## The Keep in Lövlän

The road ends north of the Norrflod, on a withered plain under a rain that never stops, where a ruined keep stands on a ridge and a weather-spell has killed the grass for a mile around. Inside, the archmage is racing a clock the heroes cannot see: the black sword is weak, and he needs weeks of ritual before it can serve him and march the four hundred skeletons sleeping in his cellar. He keeps duck mages to work the spells he cannot, twin strategists to plan the war, and his ravens to tell him the moment his hunters arrive.

It comes down to the two swords in the bare pillar hall, silver against black, and the duel is more even than it should be only because Tatarus ran out of time. Win, and the evil blade goes home to Zorla, the sky clears, and a king thanks his heroes from the balcony. Lose, and the wraith takes their left ears to listen through, sends them home beaten, and finishes what he started.
