## The Quest of the Golden Nut

Three months after the Hell Fort fell, the heroes wake from a healing sleep in the living tree-castle of the elf-king Pargal, while the archmage's crusade rolls over kingdom after kingdom outside the forest. In his memory-pool the old king shows them the ruin to come and sets them a single errand: carry a golden nut from his silver sky-oak to the dying, heirless king of Berendien, that he might be cured and his land hold against the storm. He recites a dwarven prophecy of a righteous one's return and a day of judgment for those who will not forgive - words whose weight will only be felt at the end.

## Spies, Flight, and the Disowned Daughter

In the capital a smooth royal advisor blocks every door, and that night the nut is stolen by Nidlandish spies. The trail of a torn cloak leads to their nest - and brands the heroes as spies in turn, to be spirited out of the city and on to the king's ousted magician. He names the kingdom's only hope: Melindor, the king's disowned daughter and a resistance leader, last seen in the war-torn ruin of Hynsolge. The road there crosses a land of horrors and a sea that claims the boatman who carried them, to a captured princess held in catacombs beneath a betrayed city, behind a maddened demon.

## The Crown and the Curse

The princess freed and won back to her duty, the heroes return to crown her - only to find the old king already dead, the golden nut useless, and the smooth advisor unmasked at last as the disciple thought slain at the Hell Fort. He is beaten, and Melindor is crowned amid great joy and warm thanks. And then, as she waves them farewell, they see it: a cross-shaped scar in her palm, the very mark the archmage's broken victims bear. Whether they have saved Berendien, or only delivered it, no one can say.
