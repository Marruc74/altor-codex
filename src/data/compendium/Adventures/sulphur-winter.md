## Plot

The island of Marjura lies in the northern ice sea as Trakorien's most distant outpost, long a refuge for the political and religious freethinkers who fled the kingdom's rigid hierarchy. That fragile peace breaks when Trakorien invades to control the sulphur trade flowing from the island's largest city, Arhem. Among occupying troops, native islanders, dwarves and giants, old grudges and quiet schemes pull in every direction, while the necromancer sect of Shagul steers events from its hidden Iron Tower. Into this tangle of feuding factions wander strangers from across the seas, in a place where almost no one can be fully trusted.

### Factions

The island is pulled between several powers: the occupying Trakorians under the bailiff Gottard; the necromantic shagulite sect in its hidden Iron Tower; the discontented Marjuran islanders, some in a secret rebellion; the immigrant dwarves and ice-giants of the mountains; the kvurs, the shy native folk who hate the shagulites; the peaceable ice-druids of Kmorda; and the dragon Blatifagus, squatting in the dwarves' mercury mine. Any of the sect's agents may wear a friendly face, and a hidden assassin watches over its interests in the town.

### The Trials of Marjura

Beneath the intrigue run several perils, each its own tale: the recovery of the mercury from the dragon-held mine for the dwarf Gwellmyn; the long crossing of the ice to the ice-druid Perrima's sacked temple on Biletand; the descent into the graves of old Cruri after their mighty weapons; and, at the last, the reckoning with the sect in the Iron Tower. All these threads draw toward a final confrontation with the sect and its founder, Shagul, whose grave hides a clone of the true necromancer and a design to make himself master of the world.
