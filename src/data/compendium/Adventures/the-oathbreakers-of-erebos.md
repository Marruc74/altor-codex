## A Drowning Man

The heroes are walking the streets of Nohstril when they see a richly dressed easterner climb over a bridge rail to throw himself into the river. He is Erbolsus, a small merchant of weapons, and once he is talked down he pours out the most wretched story in the world. A shipment of dwarf-forged swords he had traded for at great cost, and meant to sell on to a Zorakin duke, never reached port. The trading house that escorted it told him the ship had been taken by Felician pirates. His insurer denied he had ever paid the security fee that would have covered the loss, and when he went for his own copy of the papers he found his office burned and his savings gone. Then his younger son was kidnapped, his wife left him for his best friend, and the two of them sailed off with the last of his gold. He has nothing left but the clothes and jewels on his back and his two loyal slaves, and he begs the heroes to clear his name and find the men who broke him.

The truth is worse than Erbolsus knows. The house of Hinkare sold his entire cargo of weapons to Felicien, a trade flatly forbidden with a country Erebos is sliding toward war against. To keep it quiet they forced his insurer to burn the proof of his payment, sent thugs to torch his office, and took his son when he would not stop digging. His friend and his wife never betrayed him at all. They were seized and locked in the house's cellar, and the wife made to write the farewell letter that broke him.

Following the thread leads through the Hinkare office, a murdered clerk, a pair of harbour toughs wearing the merchant's stolen rings, and at last to the harbour itself, where Erbolsus's own lost ship rides at anchor freshly painted and renamed, crewed by men who only pretend to be captive pirates. The boldest course is to seize the youngest Hinkare brother and wring the truth from him, though he holds back the one secret he will die before sharing: the arms-trade with the enemy, the proof of which lies hidden in his office.

In the end the surest way to break the house is to let Erbolsus, a fine speaker, stand in the city square and tell the crowd what was done to him. The mob turns, the house's guilt is laid bare, and the rival concerns of the Council move to cast the Hinkares out of Erebos. For their trouble the heroes win the reward Erbolsus promised, five hundred gold apiece, and the offer of a place in the realm's own security service.
