## The Job

It begins in the dark month of 615 eO, with the Felician Pirate War at its height and Nohstril packed with the soldiers and warships of the alliance. Over a drink at the Sunmill, a smooth man named Lupullus offers the heroes a clean job: break into a merchant's house, copy two pages of cipher, leave no trace. The merchant is a Felician spy, the cipher a prize that could lay the enemy's code bare, and Lupullus serves Ocula Negra, the spymasters of Erebos - though he says none of that. The break-in itself is the easy part. On the way out, the heroes blunder into a fresh corpse and a third murder, and the night turns from burglary to a hunt with the city watch and a tower of grieving magicians at their heels.

## The Murders

Two masters of the Elementary Academy have been killed with their eyes put out and their hearts cut away, and now a third. Sinwaldor, who heads the academy, takes the heroes on to find the killer he fears is one of his own. He is right. The murderer is Marcus Tullius, master of shadow-magic and a Felician spy, and the murders are no madness but a harvest: seven eyes and three hearts are wanted for a rite, gathered from the bodies of his colleagues. The trail of forged messages, a Felician contact with a birthmarked palm, and a dark-handed symbolist who proves only a red herring all lead down at last into the catacombs beneath the north city.

## The Demon in the Grave

Beneath Nohstril lies the grave of Echram Schroedel, a war-demon the merchant house Festglade hired a century ago to slaughter a rival's cyclops guards, then cheated, killed and dismembered, chaining his soul in the ground and selling off his sword. Bound there, he scratched the rite to wake him onto his own grave-lid, and Tullius has read it: three waters, three hearts, seven eyes, an anointed blade, and a charming man's body to wear - the bard Eleidos. What Tullius does not know is that Echram is owed a debt of some thirty-six thousand lives, and means to collect it by laying the city waste. The heroes can follow the spies down to grave 712 for the reckoning, or end it sooner - and if the rite is not stopped, Nohstril learns what it chained beneath its streets.
