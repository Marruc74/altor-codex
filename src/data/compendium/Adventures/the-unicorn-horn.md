## Plot

The healing Delitha order keeps the Temple of the Life-giving Spring outside a village, where its gatekeeper Liéthon disarms visitors and brings worthy seekers before The Sanctified, the order's aged leader. She explains that some forty years ago one of the order's temples was destroyed by orcs led by black priests of Zeeris, the Left-Hand Path, who were hunting the temple's unicorn relics. The relics were carried to safety and hidden by an adventurer named Larm, and the order now believes this was Larm Legast, who later settled in the city of Kandra.

The Sanctified hires the party to slip into Kandra, recover the relics and bring them back, handing them a unicorn-horn amulet to prove their errand. She also warns them that months ago the black priests abducted one of the order's young novices, a girl descended from the foundress and bound to the relics, and asks them to watch for her. To draw the enemy away she makes a show of riding toward Karan-nur-Alemin with a large retinue, putting it about that she is chasing the man under his other name, Dwarffriend, while the party quietly follows the real trail to Kandra.

In Kandra the trail runs through Larm Legast's daughter Liva and leads at last to an old corner house, number 247, kept by the alchemist Gallahan. He and his wife Moira are in truth shadow-priests of the Zeeris order, and their shop is a front for the local black-temple cell. In its crypt the party finds the prisoners, among them a merchant taken for ransom and the missing novice herself, a twelve-year-old girl named Liella, marked on the shoulder with a rearing-unicorn birthmark and kept for sacrifice on the crypt altar.

The party must free Liella and recover the unicorn horn and its stone. Brought together, the relics pour the foundress's indestructible soul into the child and place her in contact with the unicorn itself, which appears and bows to her, healing every wound and scattering nearby enemies. Only the highest black priests have the will to stand and fight once it manifests.

### The Delitha Order

A small and little-regarded order of healers founded long ago by a woman called in a vision to care for the weak and sick of every race. They serve Delitha, a half-sister of the goddess Ninnave whom many in Kandra do not accept as a goddess at all. The order takes both women and men, though only women rise past the rank of initiate, and it teaches magic alongside its healing. Its priestesses wear white robes whose coloured bands mark their grade, climbing from novice up to the Sanctified.

### The Zeeris Order

The black priesthood of Zeeris, the Left-Hand Path, named for a half-brother of Delitha. They are the healers' oldest enemies, and in these days their followers outnumber the Delitha order. Their servants deal in necromancy and darker rites, and it was they who destroyed the unicorn temple long ago and who now hold Liella for sacrifice.

### The Unicorn Horn

The relic the whole adventure turns on, a small spiralled horn, faintly pink and giving off a silver-white light. Near it a Delitha or Ninnave worshipper finds their will and presence doubled, and a senior priestess can heal others with it. Its true purpose, though, is to pour the indestructible soul of the order's foundress into her young descendant and call the unicorn back into the world.
