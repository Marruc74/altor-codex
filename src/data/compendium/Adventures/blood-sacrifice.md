## The Charge from the Lake

It is told that the brothers of a Wegil monastery, set high and alone in the Nida Mountains above the Great Lake, ceased one season to send their messengers down to the wooden town of Torsborg. The silence stretched until the church of Wegil in the town grew uneasy, and the priesthood laid a charge upon a hero born of that country - to climb the mountain road and learn what had befallen their distant fellows.

What the climber found was no living house but a burned ruin. Its reception hall had been hung with the bodies of the slain, its cells stood empty and despoiled, and a winged demon of dark-red hide and gold eyes - Kanicaar-an-Cosh, set there to kill the curious - haunted the ashes. Yet in the broken prayer hall, beneath the toppled statue of Wegil, the dying abbot Karn Lendur still drew breath. He had marked black-robed men passing into a hidden cave behind a bronze door wrought as a demon's face; his brothers had crept in to learn the truth and been slaughtered or dragged away alive. With his last strength he gave over a parchment - his account and a map to the temple - and begged that the captives be saved before the sacrificial knife found them.

## The Temple of the Dark God

The map led to Kashim's Temple, an ancient hall of the dark god hidden away for ages and woken anew eight years past by the necromancer Kati Lom. She had learned the black worship from old books and gathered about her a cult of some fifty fanatics, with twelve war-priests beneath her - ten of whom wandered the wide world spreading the faith, while two deathless vampires, Palimas Carnor and Dori Thalik, kept her hall by night in the shapes of bat, rat, wolf and smoke. Past the bronze demon-door lay the guard rooms, a great library of forbidden lore, and the sacrifice hall, where a black idol stood ringed by ten fire-pits and the captive brothers waited their turn upon the knife.

To pass the guardians, free the brothers and break the worship of Kashim was the heart of the deed. Yet willful Kati Lom kept ever a secret tunnel behind her treasury, and cornered she would rather snatch up her spellbooks and flee to wake another hidden temple far away than die in this one.

## The Cold Reward

If the cult was broken and the brothers led down out of the mountains, the church of Wegil in Torsborg gave its praise for the deed - but no reward, for it holds that virtue is its own reward, and so the heroes kept whatever treasure they had carried out of the temple for their own. There remained one shadow on the victory. Should Kati Lom have slipped away down her tunnel with her books of dark lore, she might one day wake another temple of Kashim and return for her revenge - for to have crossed the cult of the dark god is to have made a patient and far-reaching enemy.
