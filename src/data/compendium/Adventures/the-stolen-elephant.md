## Plot

The cavern-city of Nordsal sits crammed full: a carnival has come, the inns are taken, and into this crush walks trouble. The master thief Naurudun has stolen a dwarven blueprint, the drawing of some new fire-wagon, for the necromancer and collector Camulius Martein. Forced by the crowds into a humble new tavern called the Rearing Bärbagge, Naurudun loses the parchment on his very first night and cannot find it again. What he does not know is that the landlady, Melina, has found it, thought it looked fine and important, and dropped it into her green elephant savings-box.

When Camulius arrives and learns the blueprint is gone, he sends Naurudun to steal the whole statue. The thief throws it out of a window into the street, the circus triplets find it and carry it off, and on the morning the players wake at the Bärbagge the hunt is on, with everyone in Nordsal somehow getting in the way of everyone else.

## A Thief-Hunt, Not a Riot

This is a chase dressed up as chaos, and the trick of running it is to keep the town whole. The adventurers must find the culprits without tearing Nordsal apart, and for much of the adventure they are chasing the deranged Mad Palle on his wild progress through the streets. There is no shortage of cheerful brawling for those who want it, between the robbers' alley, the drunken giant Bachtrecht and the loud, blameless circus folk, but the answer is run down by listening, not by swinging swords.

## The Knight Beneath the Fool

Mad Palle, the simpleton who challenges the players to help him, is in truth the noble Knight Paladin of Tirisholm, in Nordsal on a secret errand for his order. Playing the village idiot is his cover: no one minds what a madman does, so he and his squire Farre can come and go unwatched. The errand is the gamemaster's to invent, but the suggested one ties Nordsal's two tales together, for Paladin is on the trail of the necromancers deeper in the cave system, the same evil that haunts *Horrors of the Deep*. Once the elephant is recovered he may ask the adventurers' help, which makes this whole affair an alternate way into that darker adventure, by the front door rather than the back.

Paladin bears no great hatred for Camulius. He wants the landlady's stolen savings restored, he wants to know why the necromancer is in Nordsal, and he half-hopes a man so capable might even be turned against the worse things in the deep. So he means to take Camulius alive, and will stop the players from killing him.

## The Moon Monastery

Camulius runs at last to the Eilana monastery on the city's edge, the silver-domed house of the cult of the moon-goddess, the same faith set out in *Kandra*. Eilana is the goddess of the moon and the night, of silence and of secret things, who counts the dark a friend and rewards finesse and beauty over brute force; her monks keep black panthers and her temple holds an illusory moon that shows the true phase of the sky. Having hypnotised the curious Brother Botvid, Camulius has hidden among the monks with a handful of goblin zombies, raising a teleport-gate in the cellars to slip away through. The end of the hunt turns on whether the adventurers reach him before that gate opens, and on their not plundering the cloister's treasures, which would set every soul in Nordsal, and the goddess herself, against them.
