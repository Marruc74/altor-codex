## The Legend of Salagir

At the inn of the frontier village Utmir, an old man by the fire tells how, at the dawn of the world, the gods made war and mortals lived in terror. His ancestor Salagir chanced upon a council of the evil gods and overheard their plot to ambush the good; he carried the warning to the high god Org, who sprang a counter-trap on the great plain and raised a cliff to mark the victory - the very cliff on which a baron's castle now stands. For his service Salagir was given the Nova Stone, an eight-sided ruby that grants great magic to his bloodline alone, and is useless to any other hand.

The old man is Glockmar, and he is that bloodline: a demigod fallen into failing human form because the ruby that is his power has been stolen. Poisoned and confused, he believes a long-dead king's ghost took it. The truth is otherwise.

## The Three Trails

Three rumors run through Utmir, and only one is true. One says the ghost of King Tortumkan bore the stone to his barrow in the fallen north; another that the dragon Gasp hoards it in his lair. Both are dead ends, though each is a deadly and golden detour. The honest innkeeper Bork knows the third and true road: the ruby went south, to the castle of the tyrant Dargomor.

## The Hidden Hand

Yet even Dargomor does not have it. A month before the tale begins, the wizard Dingheimul came into the stone and carried it to his mountain caves to master its power - and Dargomor's own physician, Domiral, conspired in secret to steal it for him. The baron keeps only a decoy, and is dragged through ambush and siege before he can be made to confess how little he truly holds.

## The Mountain Caves

The last road climbs through the Org mountains to Dingheimul's warren, past a half-giant gatekeeper, the roused dead, a chained demon and a maze of one-way mirrors, to the laboratory where the Nova Stone sits beneath a glass dome. When the wizard falls the mountain comes down, and only the swift escape. Carried home to Utmir and restored to Glockmar, the ruby makes him a demigod once more - and he grants a single wish before he is gone.
