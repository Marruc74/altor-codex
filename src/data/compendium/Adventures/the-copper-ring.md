## The Bargain Behind It

Raigo lies under the heel of Tsun-kuo, and among its nobles the secret society of the Divine Wind has sworn to drive the invaders out. Its leader, the apostate priest Arima, has found a way no sane man would take: a pact with Aneki, a being from another dimension that names itself a demon-prince and means to rule the ruins of the world. Arima has his necromancy and his demon-servants; in return he must bring Aneki across, and for that the demon needs twenty-five souls, each torn from its body and trapped screaming in its own severed head, the suffering feeding the demon and the power gathering in a ring of copper on the floor of his workroom. One-and-twenty have already been given, and their living heads line a hidden temple beneath a drug-den. Only the breaking of the ring will shut the door.

## A Death Among Teachers

The thread the adventurers catch is the murder of their old teacher, the magister Hirkita Golsai, who grew suspicious of the disappearances and asked one question too many. They reach his rooms minutes after the demon has taken his head, and find what he left behind: a scrap of parchment with four names, a tobacco-pouch from the den called Tersi, and his ancestral sword, Dragon's Claw, a god-forged blade that does threefold harm to demons. From there the trail runs through the capital, Yakhama, to a smuggler of the drug kachejima, a frightened historian, a watch sergeant, and at last the den itself.

## Down to the Temple, Out to the Villa

Beneath Tersi, under a trapdoor, lies the cult's temple, the bloody altar and the wall of screaming heads, and a demon set to guard them. The den's papers carry the cult-leader's name, and beyond it his villa outside the city, where the copper ring waits with its guardian. To cross its threshold is to call up a horned demon out of the smoke; to break the ring is to free the trapped souls and banish the demon-prince for nine years, before the twenty-fifth and last sacrifice can ever be made.
