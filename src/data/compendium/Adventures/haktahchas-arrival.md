## A Tale from Both Sides

This is an aftermath to the Cleansing, and it is meant to be lived from both ends at once: one band the faithful of Nidland, freeing a prisoner who can call a mighty ally into the world, and the other the occupiers' spies, racing to stop them. What each band does bends the road of the other, so the same villages and the same dead are met twice over, once from each side.

Nidland has lain under occupation half a year, and the hatred of strangers runs hot. The faithful's one hope is Judge Isthan, an old dark-mage who alone knows the rite to summon Haktahcha - and Isthan has been taken, held in a temple at the village of Khemen. The faithful are sent to free him; the occupiers' spies are sent to carry him off to a surer prison first. The faithful reach him a little ahead, and the chase is on.

## The Two Ingredients

Freed, Isthan needs two things for the rite, and his magic is bound for most of the road, so those who freed him must keep him alive. The first is the Demon's Heart, a fist-sized red gem in a dwarf-hold deep in the Grynner Mountains, where three bands of dark folk feud beside the dwarves and a hidden vault keeps the stone behind passwords and a crossbow trap. The second is the heart of an ancient silver elf, sheltered in the city of Besporho - cut out with a black dagger and sealed in a black casket so that it stays pure. At each turn the spies arrive a step too late, left to pick up the trail from whoever the faithful leave behind.

## The Halls Below

With both hearts in hand the road runs to Khaanikel, the capital, and down into the Labraatrium, the buried dark academy beneath it. There Isthan works the long rite while the spies close the distance. If they come in time, the last battle is fought in those halls, and they may yet strike the judge down before he finishes. If they come too late, the rite is done: Haktahcha crosses into Altor, and Isthan gives it room in his own body, growing half a meter as it takes him. One of the two bands will not walk out of the dark - and if it is the faithful who hold the floor, Nidland has its new Punisher, and within the year the occupiers may well be driven out and a fresh crusade made ready.
