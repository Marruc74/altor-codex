Moril Dilaz is the great canal-city of Magilre and one of the richest in all of Ereb. It was founded ages ago by the Dyler as Jicon, on the only break in the cliffs of the West Sea, but the marshy ground slowly sank until, after a century, it was unliveable, just as Joric settlers arrived with their legions and drove the Dyler out. The subsidence later stilled, and the Jorers rebuilt the place as Moril Dilaz.

## The Canal-City

Water flows between the buildings now, and folk travel by boat: short rowboats, or dolphin-drawn taxis for a coin or two. The houses are of stone and brick under tiled roofs, and terraces serve as walkways, joined by little bridges of stone and wood, the wider gaps spanned by ornate pillared crossings. The water runs so clear that on a sunny day one can look down and glimpse old Jicon, the sunken city, shimmering far below. Just offshore, beneath the surface of the bay, sits a small but active volcano.

## The City Watch

Three hundred uniformed soldiers keep order in patrols of five to seven, well enough paid to be hard to bribe, though they walk only the Harbour and Gamla Jicon; Gunsten has the duke's own guard, and the Poor Quarters have none at all. Refuse goes into the canals everywhere but Gunsten, yet the water stays clean, carried off by the flow and the fish. To own a boat is a mark of having arrived, and a visiting ship pays a steep daily fee to lie in the harbour.

## The Legend of Rhys

Long ago, sorcerers were said to dwell beneath the bay, mistaken for gods, until the Dyler drove off all but the young Rhys, who took his revenge by slowly sinking their city. When the Jorers came they struck a pact: leave Rhys in peace, and he would guard the city against any great threat. To this day the common folk of Moril Dilaz look kindly on magicians for his sake, and trust that whatever comes, Rhys is watching from below.
