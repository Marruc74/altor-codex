The Academy at Grynnerbergen is a magicians' school perched on a steep crag among the Grynnerbergen of south-eastern Ereb, ruled with a single hand by the elemental magician Zvuldo of Mountain and Cold. It is the gathering-point for the magicians of all eastern Ereb, where every school of magic is represented, even necromancy, though the necromancers are watched closely and only a few may teach the art at once.

## The Castle and the Village

Some two hundred and thirty souls live in the castle, among them around forty teachers, and below it sits a village of perhaps a hundred farmers and craftsmen who supply the academy and whose ancestors were the folk once driven from this land. The only road up ends at a double bridge: the first span is narrow and polished glass-slick, slathered with an alchemist's soap, so that every visitor must wear a personal bronze amulet whose lasting magic keeps him upright; the second is mined, with a destroying spell hung ready above it. Beneath the central tower that Zvuldo raised, his laboratory has been dug down twelve levels into the rock, and the castle holds one of the largest libraries in all of Ereb, its alchemical learning especially far advanced.

## History

The stronghold was built centuries ago by the warlord Nemedat, who came with an army of orcs, svartalfs and a few ogres. When the nobles of Hynsolge shattered that dark-folk host, Nemedat gathered the survivors into the castle, and Hynsolge hired Zvuldo to be rid of him; the magician broke Nemedat with fire and lightning and was granted the region as a barony, with freedom from taxes for life. That was two hundred and sixty-three years ago, and the promise has never been broken.
