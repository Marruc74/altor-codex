## The Final Conflux

This is the end of the long road. The heroes return from the made-world with the god-hero Great Stonefather - now a giant glowing crystal seed - and the divine rainbow pen, and the elf-seer Melealina names where the fated point will fall: the tiny holm of Clusta Noba, near the cursed island of Marjura. But she fears the conflux has split into two points, one on the holm and one far out in the Trakorian Sea, and a split conflux would be catastrophe. The whole campaign turns on one brutal rule: whoever is closest to the conflux when it falls wins, and all the rest is the hard road to get there.

## Four Evils to Undo

By the seer's reckoning there are four great evils to undo before the point can be kept safe: heal the wound in the earth from which the dead draw their half-life; lay King Ottar's undead host back in the grave; banish the demon Ebharing from the world; and end the deathless sorcerer's clones - all while the stone-hero is planted, grown, and made to enclose the conflux itself.

## The Race to the Holm

Six powers race north for Clusta Noba: the heroes aboard their living ship, the fleets of Trakorien, Ransard and Stegos, the lone king Arn Dunkelbrink, and the undead crawling across a stone bridge. To win, the heroes cannot fight; they must quietly find the exact point and grow the stone-hero over it before any rival arrives. Beneath the holm a titan must be freed to drag Marjura south and mend the split - and his lurch begins a countdown, and cracks open the ravine that leads down to the conflux grotto.

## The Queen's Gambit

The demon is undone not by force but by sacrifice. Melealina merges herself with a monstrous fused immortal, then lets the sorcerer take her and drive the demon-sword into her chest - and the demon recoils in horror from what she has become, tears free, drags a clone of the sorcerer away to eternal torment, and abandons the world. The seer frees her companions, sends her love to the king, and steps through a mirror, gone.

## The Eight Fates

The climax falls in the steam-filled grotto, where time is measured in paces and the one standing closest in space and in time prevails. Eight fates wait on who wins: the happy peace of King Arn restored; an unforeseen hero crowned by chance; the nightmare of the sorcerer and his mask-plague; the witch Rirba reborn to rule a realm of darkness; the insectoid Keriaka's world-eating swarm; the holy state of Manzonla; the just new empire of Praanz da Kaelve at his new capital; or the undead realm of King Ottar and his naiad. As the titan rams Marjura into the mainland, the old capital Tricilve drowns beneath the wave, and the saga closes.
