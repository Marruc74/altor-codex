On the southern shore of Lake Sango, set on either side of the river Miseva, stand the two fishing villages of Sharfal and Dudhil, their names meaning west and east in the old tongue. For a hundred years the families Sirtam of Sharfal and Lambager of Dudhil have been locked in a blood-feud whose first cause no one now remembers, a quarrel that has cost eight lives and runs on still in nightly sabotage and worse.

## The Lovers

Across that hatred a forbidden love has grown. Docined Sirtam, a gentle potter of the western village, and Huirel Lambager, youngest daughter of the eastern family, meet in secret at the old graveyard between the villages and plan to run away together. They have reason to hurry, for Bremed Sirtam, Sharfal's most dangerous man, trains daily for blood and has begun building firebombs in secret, and should he have his way the feud will end not in a wedding but in fire.

## The Old Graveyard

The meeting-place of the lovers is no safe ground. Some five hundred paces south of Sharfal lie a hundred imariot graves, their lead coffins holding twenty-eight of the dread imariot rings, and the place is watched over by barrow-wights that stir against any who trouble the dead. What the young pair take for a lovers' refuge sits atop a far older and darker thing.
