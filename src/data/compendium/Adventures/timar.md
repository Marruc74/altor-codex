Timar stands upon a rocky headland on the east coast of Elmor, where the river Trangor runs down through it to the sea. Its name means clifftop-fortress in the old tongue, and it is ringed by a high, thick wall set with watchtowers. Within them the city is divided as sharply by rank as by district.

## The Quarters

At the gates lie the garrison and customs-house, where every good is taxed, and crowded behind them the close-built workers' quarters and the squalid harbour. The merchant and craft quarters make the orderly, paved heart of the city, and apart from all the rest, behind their own walls, stand the stone houses of the nobility and the richest merchants. Above it all rises the duke's marble castle, linked in secret beneath the streets to the court-house and the tax-office.

## Trade and Pleasure

The city keeps its higher life: an academy of learned men and women, a theatre that seats a thousand where the operas of Narge Gyldenharpa draw the crowd, and four great festivals a year. Its wealth runs through the great merchant houses, which bring in cloth, spices, silver and luxuries and send out glass, wine, silverwork and the famed Trangorian spice-cheese; one house, behind a false name, runs slaves against the law. By night the harbour turns violent, and the watch goes in fours behind an officer with a whistle.
