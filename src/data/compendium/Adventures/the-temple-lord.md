## The Cult in the Robes of the Church

The southern isles of Pandarealia are ruled by the church of Iao, a strict and lawful faith of eight virtues and spiraled neck-rings of rank. Something has gone wrong inside it. Trade with Agriea has stopped, ships sit held in harbor, and the people have grown frightened and silent. The clans of the north, who count such things in coin, hire the heroes to find out why - and the answer, when it comes, is worse than a trade dispute. A cult that worships death and rot has grown up inside the church like a canker, and it wants a holy war that will set the islands against each other and leave the cult to pick up the pieces.

Behind it stands the temple lord Luciano della Peredio, and behind him a thing far older. The trail begins with a merchant contact in San Paulo named Verdi, who is courteous, dismissive, and a cultist of ten years' standing. He betrays the heroes the moment they leave his door, and from there the city shows its true face: dark wagons leaving by night, captives marched through the streets, an old house full of penned slaves, and a black cathedral where eight prisoners are killed in mockery of the eight virtues.

## Across to Materé

Breaking that rite, and keeping one terrified priest alive to talk, gives the heroes the names they need and a hard choice of how to use them. Win over the First Lawreader with the bodies and the freed slaves, and a righteous priest will sail with them and a company at his back. Move too slowly, and the cult turns the city's fear against them and brands them the murderers - in a place this suspicious of strangers, an easy lie to sell. Either way the road leads on to Materé and the church's holiest house, the Golden Temple on its cliff, now defiled to the Winged Death. There a greater sacrifice is underway, and there the temple lord himself is found at the altar - only to make a sign, call his master's name, and vanish before any hand can close on him.

## The Mountain of Gold

He flees to a ruined castle in the forest, and from its cellars a secret passage runs down through a valley where the trees rot and the birds have fled, to a cave that stinks of the grave. On a heap of gold and silver waits Arraquwâin, the undead dragon - thirteen thousand years old, caught in death and hating everything that still breathes, working a slow ritual to tear open a permanent gate to the land of the dead. The fight is meant to be terrible and very nearly hopeless, and it turns on a blade the heroes may have learned of along the way, a dwarven dragon-slayer that bites three times as deep through a dragon's hide. Beyond the dragon a black portal turns in the far wall, and the temple lord, cornered at last, does not stay to die. He lets it swallow him - and the way it opens leads on, out of the isles, to the part of the tale still to come.
