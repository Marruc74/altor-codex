## Robbed in a Corrupt City

The heroes come to a large, crooked city carrying something they must not lose - a magic item lent by a master, with a warning ringing in their ears - and lose it almost at once. A friendly young man offers to walk the lodging-less party to his cousin's inn; there is no cousin and no inn, only a dark quarter and a gang of thugs who beat them down and take their packs. They wake in the care of a boarding-house couple, the husband a reformed man of an exiled crime-ring, who tells them their goods will surface at the Arkadum auction house. The watch is no help - the watch drinks at Arkadum - so there is nothing for it but to go and buy back what was stolen.

At the auction, in a room thick with armed thieves and silent whispered bids, the heroes watch their own worn coffer sold off cheap, and must pay it back with interest at the pickup counter. As they leave, a vastly fat merchant accosts them, frantic to buy the "worthless" box and flashing a purse far too heavy for the price. That is the first sign that the coffer holds more than junk.

## The Dagger That Chooses

Inside, among a dead necromancer's curiosities, lies a ritual dagger - a flame-shaped black blade with runes that burn red under the moon. The first hero to touch it is bound to the demon sealed within, and the nightmares start: a black dragon hunting through a misty wood, a knife raised over a cold altar, yellow eyes watching from rising water. The cult that wants it is the Dragon's Society, a demon order wearing the mask of a charity, three circles deep, that means to drive the dagger into the chosen one's chest so a demon prince's child can grow in the body and be loosed on the world.

There is, for a while, an honest way out: a genuinely decent merchant named Jargaf, an outer-circle dupe who knows nothing of the truth, will pay a small fortune for the dagger and feast the heroes royally to get it. Sell it or keep it, the cult is coming either way - and if they keep it, mute, drugged cultists with dragon-daggers in both hands come through the roof in the night to take it by force.

## Knives in the Dark

The cult's ritual fails the first time and makes a horror of it: one of its own masters is twisted into a tentacled demon-hybrid that escapes into the sewers, leaving the true temple reachable only through the muck. The cult buys its secrecy by massacring the city's assassins' guild, whose murders it had been blamed for all along - and the guild's lone surviving Master, black-clad and hung with knives, takes up the heroes' cause. He pulls them out of a street ambush, trains them hard at a hidden camp, and leads the strike when the cult finds them: down through a sham charitable temple, into the sewer labyrinth and its lurking hybrid, and at last to the dragon-floored sanctuary below, where the dagger, the demonologist, and the demon at the door all come to their reckoning.
