## The House of the Eightfold Way

The grandest, largest and fairest building in Nohstril is the House of the Eightfold Way, raised in 545 eO by the dwarf master-architect Ikarin the Anglemeasurer, and added to over the years with its garden, its water-play and its museum. It has housed the Elementary Academy for close on three hundred years - founded in 320 eO by Br'jandik Smallog, a water-elementalist out of Beyural - and within it some ninety students learn the eight elements from a dozen masters. Three parts make up the house: the central tower of twenty-five meters, the plainer appendix of reading-halls and student rooms, and the long pillared Boulevard.

## The Mirrors

In the Hall of Mirrors hang three glasses that are no ordinary mirrors. The Dark Mirror, kept veiled, throws back a beam fierce enough to blind when strong light strikes it, and the sun finds it at every clear sunset. The Power Mirror drinks the strength of mind from any living thing that gazes into it, a little at a time, until the gazer tears his eyes away; what it gathers a master may draw out as his own. And the Soul-Teller is no single glass but a net of small mirrors that takes a soul into a waking sleep and shows, in five reflections, its childhood and its dreams, its nightmares, the face it wears for others, and the truth of what it is.

## The Library and the Pendulum

The library holds seven thousand books in eight sectors, one for each element, kept by the bookkeeper Terion and warded by two cold glacials that put out any flame. Higher up hangs Llewh's Pendulum, known to every magician in Ereb, which answers to the power-flowing through the world; a bell on its cord rings when it swings, and the masters take it as a duty to come and read it. Below, in the cellar, the Treasury keeps a share of Ereb's magical wealth, and the garden and its herb-beds feed the alchemists their rarer ingredients.
