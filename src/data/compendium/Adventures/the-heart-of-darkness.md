## Plot

The third and final part of the Path of Glory trilogy is an epic rather than a treasure-hunt: a struggle between light and darkness in which what the party does decides the future of the whole Eastlands. It takes up the moment the Gates of Power left off, as the heroes wash out of the dwarven realm along an underground stream and into a land torn by a merciless three-way war between the armies of Dakoth, the priesthood of Sathmog and the small, skilled knighthood of Eledain.

Long ago the gods of light and darkness fought a war of three hundred years. They bound the defeated lord of darkness, Herz the Witherer, into a great red ruby, the Heart of Darkness, and buried it far in the north under the watch of four of his servants. Through the ages Herz stayed awake and his hatred grew, until five hundred years ago a mighty magician named Dakoth passed the grave; Herz seized his will, taught him dark magic over decades, and set him to raise the god anew and remake the world as a realm of darkness. Foreseeing this, the gods of light forged a weapon to undo it, the Lightbringer, a mithril war-hammer made to shatter the Heart of Darkness, wieldable only by one of pure mind, and hid it in the catacombs beneath their old fortress of Otag. The Day of Darkness, when Herz may be reborn, draws swiftly near.

### The Road East

Tefalas leads the party from the underground river toward Eledain's mountain temple, across a war-ravaged land of burned villages and retreating soldiers. If the undead knight Mogdath survived the Gates of Power, he pursues them still, swimming after them, and attacks along the way, a last chance to bind him in the mithril dagger. On the road they find a temple of Sathmog, sacked and all within murdered; its one survivor, the high priest Mordag, returns, takes them for the killers, and thereafter hunts them for revenge. At the mountain temple the magic-master Godvindel heals them, destroys any undead bound in the dagger, tells the legend of the Lightbringer, and sends them to find it in Otag.

### Otag and the Lightbringer

Through a secret way Tefalas knows, the party enters the captured fortress of Otag unseen, passes its catacombs and the Guardian set over the weapon, and wins the Lightbringer. Slipping out, they overhear Dakoth speaking with the death wraith that now holds Otag, and learn where the Heart of Darkness lies, that the city around it is almost built, and that the Day of Darkness is almost upon them.

### Into the Dark Lord's Realm

The road to Dakoth's fortress runs through the dead heart of his realm, scorched and frozen under a soot-grey sky, thick with orcs and patrols. The only way in is to take service in his mercenary host; the party and Tefalas pass the trials, are placed in the Elite Guard, and are brought up to meet Dakoth himself. Within the keep they find a map onward to the temple of darkness, but a death wraith discovers them; Tefalas roars at them to run, and they escape in their stolen uniforms while he is left behind and killed.

### The March and the Village of the Dead

Back at the now-besieged mountain temple, Godvindel reads the map and gives them a last company for the end: four of Eledain's finest knights and a band of Sathmog's priests, a fresh truce having been struck between the two. But one knight, Tyger, is a traitor in Dakoth's pay, and the priests' leader is Mordag, who still believes the party destroyed his temple. On the march Mordag drugs them all and confronts them, and they likely talk their way clear, only for Tyger to lead them to the Village of the Dead, where a death knight has slain every villager and raised them as banemen for an ambush.

### The Heart of Darkness

At last the company reaches the temple of pure darkness, where slaves under orcs, ogres and mercenaries are raising a fortified city around it. Inside waits a desperate battle against the powers of darkness, until one of the heroes reaches the Heart of Darkness and, by giving up their own life, shatters the ruby with the Lightbringer, destroying Herz forever and ending Dakoth's design. Afterward Godvindel and a representative of Sathmog hold a great ceremony to honour those who stopped the darkness.
