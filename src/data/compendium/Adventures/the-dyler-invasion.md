## The Warning

After a week or two in Moril Dilaz, the party is visited one night by a masked, sword-bearing nobleman, the anonymous envoy of Duke Marcaril Marguz. The Dyler, he warns, the city's first founders, mean to return and destroy it, and he hires the party to find out how and to stop it.

## The Truth

Only a small band of Dyler is actually in the city; the rest are coming down from the Klomeller Mountains. But they march for no warlord. Behind them stands a respected merchant, Miril Gereid, fronting a Munzga conspiracy, the Consortium for Moril's Destruction, that wants the canal-city ruined out of pure commercial rivalry. The plan is to wake the volcano beneath the bay before its time, loose a great wave to flood the streets, and send fighters in amid the chaos. To do it, Miril has dealt with the raugoner, the insectoid colonies of the sea, and through them a harebir, and hired a magician and a pirate to lure the beast onto the volcano with a giant bait. The reward promised the raugoner is the run of whatever is left of the city.

## The Trap

The plotters soon realise the party is the duke's answer to them, and move to be rid of it. A shark-man, posing as a wounded sea-captain, lures the party out onto the water, where he and his fellows throw off their disguise and fall on the boat. They are cowards at heart, and flee once they are hurt. Other threads run alongside the main one: mangled corpses turning up in the canals, taken for the work of natural sharks; a pale "ghost" haunting the Sea-Cow inn; and a true ghost in the palace attic. Should the party fail to stop the plot by any other means, Rhys himself rises from the bay to save the city.
