## The Road to the Dragon

A companion is dead, and the adventurers have read of a way to win them back: a flute, kept by the black dragon Oolong on his mountain far out in the eastern wilds, that opens a road into the land of the dead. Those who played through the haunting of Sugimoto carry the very book and map that name it, taken from the ruined temple on Iko; others may come by the legend some other way. The road to the mountain runs through the Boar Forest, where bow-legged bakemono keep a toll and make the rest of the wood miserable for any who refuse it, across the cold ford of the river Archi, where kappa wait for stragglers, and up into the storm-wracked highlands, where a bald old pelt-hunter named Sulimono offers shelter in his cabin and tries to drink the life from whoever sleeps unwatched.

## Oolong's Bargain

Oolong is one of the lung, the dragons of the east, and for all his size and age he is no mere brute. He will lend his flute, but at a price. A pearl was stolen from his hoard and carried down among the dead, and in a strong wizard's hands it could bind the dragon to another's will, so he wants it back and cannot go down himself, the gate being too narrow for him. He sends the adventurers in his stead, arms them with a sword that cleaves demon-hide, and warns that if they come back without the pearl he will seal the road and leave them shut in with the dead. To find their friend below, he tells them, they must call their name aloud, for it is easy to go down into death and hard to come up again.

## Down Among the Dead

Beyond the flute's portal lies a sloping passage of fitted blue stone and then a realm of grey mist, deathly quiet, where double rows of standing stones mark the way to a wide, still river that the dead are ferried across. The longer the living stay, the greyer and more transparent they grow, until they lose all wish to return and become part of the mist. The companion, called by name, rises half-seen out of the grey and warns that there is no going back, but will follow if they insist. The way up is not the way down: the stones now lead only back to the river, and the adventurers must have marked their road, or find it by craft, or call on a spirit who knew it in life.

## The Gate-Demon

At the portal stands the worst of it. The way out is barred by gates of black metal and the Yakitori, a four-armed, steel-skinned demon with the dragon's stolen pearl at its throat, who will not let a soul be carried back unless one of the living stays in its place. There is no bargain to be made, only the demon to be beaten, and behind the adventurers wait the oni in their thousands. When the demon falls the oni surge forward, and the living must throw themselves through the gate and flee up the passage with the horde at their heels, out into the dragon's hall. So long as the road stands open the oni keep pouring out toward the living lands, and the one sure way to shut it is to destroy the flute, for when it breaks every oni that came through it is undone and the dead realm is closed again. That done, and the pearl restored, the adventurers may snatch what they can of the dragon's hoard before Oolong returns within the hour to claim his own and drive them out, back toward the living lands and the next road.
