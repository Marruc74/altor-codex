## The Hook

In a tavern of Moril Dilaz the party meets Silazion Gyllensvärd, a knight of the old Kabrinzi order, and his companion Magot Magul, a wise and talkative monk who keeps their funds. They hire the party to help recover a lost holy relic, an amulet called the Holy God-Wine, from a crypt beneath a ruined Kabrinzi temple a few days into the swamp of Efronzink.

## The Twist

What the two do not say at first is that they are Seekers, bound by the Seekers' curse and already dying; the amulet is the last of the order's three scattered relics, and time is very short. Before the end the party is likely to find itself drawn into the Seekers' own fate.

## The Crypt

The journey runs by wagon and tramway and a last stretch on foot, to a temple all but fallen into the cliff, its tower ruined and its well still dark. The crypt below has been sealed for ages and is now infested by insectoids. The amulet was once worn by a statue, but the insectoid queen took a liking to it and wears it now in her own chamber. Her brood are not evil: they defend her without killing for sport, and the relic is far more likely to be won by cunning than by the sword.
