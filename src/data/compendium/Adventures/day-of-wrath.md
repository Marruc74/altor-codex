## The Forty-Nine

People are dying strangely in Krilloan. They fall from a great height onto the streets; they burn where they stand; they turn to pillars of salt, or drown on dry land, or waste away, or rot with plague, or simply stop, dead of terror. Seven die each way, in seven sevens, and a band of adventurers is set on the trail after the first dozen. The dead seem to share nothing, until the pattern shows itself: every one of them had sinned, against the law or against the commandments, and the killings are counting toward some end.

## The Mad Prophecy

The thread runs back to an old foretelling. Four hundred years ago a prophet founded an order that took it to heart: when the wickedness of mankind is full, the archangel Angarion will come down from the sun to put forty-nine sinners to death, and then the world will end and the righteous inherit a paradise. The order came to Krilloan and died to the last man in a fire, and their cloister has stood empty since. Now the angel has come after all, wearing the body of the cloister's last mad hermit, and by the order's grim reading a sinner is anyone who ever broke a single commandment. By that measure, the count of forty-nine is only the beginning.

## The Cloister

The hunt leads at last to St Kleol's cloister, high on the Forithos plateau above a sheer drop. The hermit Ulrik Oddbratt keeps to its tower by day, in the sun's strength; below the chapel lie the tombs of the thirteen dead monks, whose ghosts chose him for the angel's vessel and who rise against any who break in upon them. In the crypt is the ritual that called the angel down, and the dawning understanding that a creature of the sun, who can be neither found nor fought by ordinary means, will not be stopped by ordinary means either.

## A Devil to Fight an Angel

The answer, when it comes, comes from the wrong side. The Black Rose Brotherhood, who serve the demon-lord Hemaquiel, hold that an angel can be matched only by its opposite: the abyssal champion Teitan, the dark mirror of Angarion. With a stolen prayer-scroll and a rite worked beneath the old town, one of the band, marked from birth with a rose, is unmade and remade into the devil himself. The tale ends in a duel in the night sky over Krilloan, angel against devil, with the whole city watching, and whichever falls, the world is spared, for the victor's borrowed power dies with the loser and leaves only a mortal behind.
