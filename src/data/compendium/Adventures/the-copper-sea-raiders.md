## The Black Flag

The western Copper Sea has a master no king acknowledges. Baron Piet Steeljaw was a Zorakin lord, cast out by the church and condemned by his own king for cruelty, and he answered by turning pirate. His galleon, the Wave-Crusher, is built to look like any other trader, so that by the time a victim sees the black flag run up it is already too late - and a wind-weaver aboard fills the sails when the chase is on. There is three thousand silver on the baron's head, and no one has yet collected it.

## The Island

Steeljaw's lair is the Harpies' Nest, a sheer volcanic crag ringed with reefs and the wrecks of ships that tried to take it the hard way. One hidden channel leads to a sheltered cove and a green valley fed by springs, among them the Blood Spring, whose red water the raiders fling at the ships they board. Above the cove stands the fort, and higher still the crater, where a colony of harpies keeps an uneasy bargain with the pirates, and a pair of soured sea griffins nests on the southwest peak. The baron drove in here one winter night, found the way back out, and has held the island ever since.

## Four Adrift

The richest prize on these waters is Zorakin's yearly copper convoy, four caravels guarded by the greatest warship ever built in Ereb, the carrack Valien's Light. But the great ship is a clumsy sailer, and when a storm scatters the fleet between Caddo and Morélvidyn, the morning finds the four traders becalmed miles from their escort. The black flag comes down on them out of the east, the Wave-Crusher and two longships of a second band closing while the heavy warships struggle to claw their way back through dead air - and the convoy must hold until the wind returns, or be taken.
