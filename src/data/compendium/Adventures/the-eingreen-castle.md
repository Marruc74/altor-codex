## The Haunting of the Valley

A chaos-gate has been left ajar in the old Eingreen Castle, and chaos is seeping, slow and sure, into the world. The gate was opened long ago, about five centuries back, by the demonologer Duke Jargal of Eingreen, who beat the demon Okko'ggh'kth and sealed it again, but died two years later of the effort. Hidden in his study lie his notes on the gate and how it may be destroyed.

Some three months ago a raiding band of orcs out of the Aidne Mountains blundered into the castle and jarred the gate open a crack. Chaos washed over them, and they were changed into warped and grotesque things; most fled in panic, but one by one they have been drawn back toward the castle by a pull they cannot name. Their leader has made the best of it, and now leads what is left of the band in raids on the villages around the town of Steengrå, which has so far been spared. Through the gate the demon Okko'ggh'kth has gained its half-foothold and reaches for more, and more than one person has fallen to its call, among them a knight and his squire.

## A Free Ride Gone Wrong

The strangers come into it by chance. Travellers of modest means win the grand prize in a touring company's lottery, a free ride around the castles of Zorakin, and set off by coach. A week into the journey, near Steengrå, the coach breaks down, and they must wait out the repairs at the town's inn. They find a place dark with fear and suspicion: three outlying villages burned with no survivors, and the townsfolk certain that the colony of gnomes to the west is to blame. The watch-captain, Falter Raed, has held off the mob and demands proof, and offers the strangers the chance to find it.

## The Innocent and the Guilty

The gnomes are guiltless. They are a small mining people who struck a rich vein of silver, and it was their new wealth, and the lynching of their folk at market, that turned the valley against them and drove them into hiding. The real raiders are the chaos-warped orcs of the castle. To save the gnomes, the strangers must follow the raiders' trail to the Eingreen Castle, learn the truth of the gate and the demon behind it, and bring back proof before the town marches west, while a murdered mayor and a hate-blind guide pull the suspicion in every wrong direction.

## The Gate

The chaos-gate cannot be broken by force; it is held to the wall by magic, and to strike it is only to risk opening it wider. It can be unmade in one way alone: by setting the four-and-twenty small black mirrors that belong to it on the castle's old frames, and turning sunlight through them onto the great mirror, which then bursts and brings the ruin down. The mirrors lie buried at an abandoned monastery to the east, and the Red Knight has already dug them up, meaning to do the very opposite, to turn moonlight through them and throw the gate wide for his master. If he is stopped and the gate destroyed, the valley is saved; if he is not, Okko'ggh'kth comes through, and that is an ending no one survives to tell.
