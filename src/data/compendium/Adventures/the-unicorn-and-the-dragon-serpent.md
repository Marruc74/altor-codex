## The Rebellion

The adventure opens in any harbour town on the Copper Sea, where a band of adventurers down on their luck answer a notice calling for fighters to take the mercenary's shilling in Hynsolge. They are signed onto the rolls by Serter Dubbelhand, given numbered lead tokens and a kit with a seven-pointed star sewn to the jacket, and shipped across to the Hynsolge coast. A two-week march through a country burned and broken by civil war brings them to Gråborg, where they join the rebel New Army of Abrahim Stenklo, a former merchant's son turned freedom-fighter who has sworn to throw down the barons who killed his father.

The war is the smaller story. Baron Riikov of Brangoria keeps an adviser named Parikila, who is in truth the half-demon son of the demon Onaabys, near two hundred years old. He has spent generations amusing himself with cruelty among the peasants, and means in time to wipe out humankind with a comet that fell long ago in Gormowac's cursed forest. Now the rebellion threatens to undo him along with the baron, so Parikila and his Star Lore cult mean to carry the comet to an old temple in Gråborg, work a ritual there, and teleport both the stone and himself away to his son in Soluna.

The heroes stumble onto the thread at the inn called the Dead Rooster, where a nervous cultist leaves a sealed message under a wine cup and is thrown out before he can collect it. The note, under the baron's dragon-serpent seal, points to a ritual at the dream-goddess's graveyard. Following it draws the heroes through Gråborg's temples and back alleys, into dealings with a thieves' ring and a possessed priestess, and onto the trail of poisoned wells, sabotaged beer-casks and a buried comet smuggled into town in a barrel.

By the night of the ritual the heroes may have learned enough to lay their proof before Abrahim and turn the war, or they may arrive too late as orcs march to the baron's aid. Either way the reckoning comes in the lemon groves between Gråborg and the baron's castle, the rebels under their white-unicorn banner against the baron's red dragon-serpent, while beneath the town Parikila stands at his altar with the Red Stone, waiting for midnight.
