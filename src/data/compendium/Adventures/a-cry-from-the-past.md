## The Haunting

Gråhall stood empty and crumbling for the better part of two hundred years before its new lord came to rebuild it, cut the taxes, and call in settlers to work the fief. The work was going well until a cold autumn night, when the howl of the storm turned, for a moment, into a cry that no wind makes. A ghost had begun to walk the cellars, and she had already taken the soul of one of the house's own knights, who now lay breathing but empty in his bed. The household swears it will scatter if nothing is done, and so the lord sends for adventurers willing to hunt a ghost.

It is worth knowing from the start that this ghost is not like others. She follows none of the rules the wise know for the unquiet dead. She cannot be laid, bound, burned or banished by any art known or unknown. She can only be helped - and anyone who tries to fight her learns quickly, and at someone else's cost, that she cannot be beaten.

## The Sorrowful Past

Dorindalil was the daughter of Baron Amadus Odarion of Gråhall and his wife Nira - or so it was believed. She was not his child. While Amadus was away on a long hunt, Nira had loved a stately stranger who, in secret, was a lord of the forest elves wearing a man's shape. He gave the girl her name: Dorindalil, "daughter of the forest" in the elven tongue, plain enough that anyone with a little of the speech would catch its meaning.

The child grew up dark-haired and beautiful, glad and fiery and strong of will, and every man who saw her loved her. Sent to stay with family friends in Kandra, she found over the year she lived there that she had magic in her, her father's blood waking, and she began quietly to study it. While she was away the forest stranger came to Gråhall to ask after his daughter, and Amadus learned the truth. A prophecy from his own youth came back to him then: that a maiden who was not his daughter, though she claimed to be, would be his death.

When Dorindalil came home and was found still at her studies, Amadus's rage swallowed him. He had her walled alive into a hidden room in the cellar and left her to die there - for her mother's faithlessness, for being a witch, for being a stranger's child, and to cheat the prophecy. The story put about was that she had caught a deadly plague on her travels and died of it, and some hailed the baron a hero for shutting the sickness away. In the dark of her sealed room, dying, Dorindalil made a last desperate working and touched a power that would let her leave the land of the dead - but only on a condition. Her hidden grave, with four other unquiet graves, had to stand at the points of a pentagram. Not until that shape was complete could her spirit return to take its revenge.

Nira died soon after of grief, and Amadus of his own dread and guilt. The barony fell back to the crown, and Gråhall stood empty until now.

## What She Wants

The pentagram closed the night the Blåskimmer went down in the storm, and all five graves woke at once. Dorindalil took shape in her sealed room, saw the bones that had been her body, and understood that she was dead. She went looking through the cellars for her father, and when the knight Alserban told her Amadus had been gone two hundred years, the last of her hope turned to rage and she took his soul.

She does not truly want the knight, or anyone's soul. She wants her death undone. As she is now she can move through time, back to the very night she was sealed away, but she can only watch it happen, again and again, never reaching into it. What she needs is someone living, from the present, to be sent back in her place to break that night. If they go back and simply hold the baron and his knights long enough that she can flee the house, the night is changed: her haunting ends, every unquiet spirit in the district is laid to rest as the pentagram breaks, and every stolen soul returns at once to the body it was taken from. Whether the baron himself lives or dies in the doing of it does not matter at all.

She is a hard creature to deal with. She is newly dead and already thinks in strange ways, she speaks an old dialect that almost no one now living can follow, and she gives her meaning out slowly, in fragments and riddles. Win her trust over several meetings and she will name her terms. Refuse them, or simply fail to listen, and she takes another soul every third night until someone does.

## Afterward

Those sent into the past come back the only ones who remember it clearly. Everyone else at Gråhall believes they dreamed the whole affair, and that they all dreamed the same thing. The old legends of the house quietly change to a tale of one night when demons harried Gråhall, struck down its baron and his knights, and carried off the fair Dorindalil as their prize. The rest of the region's history runs on unaltered.

What becomes of Dorindalil herself, freed at last, is left untold but for one thing. In all the years since, she has never once stopped searching for the strangers who came out of nowhere and saved her.
