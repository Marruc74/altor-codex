## The Fall of the Jeraz

Five hundred years ago a hot, dry peninsula of hills and olive groves was ruled by the family Jeraz, who held many castles and, among them, the remote hunting-lodge of Asiki. Their last lord, Aram Jeraz, set aside the warrior ways of his line for the study of magic, and reaching past his skill he sought to call up a single demon - and summoned a whole horde instead. The demons killed all at Asiki and for a day's march around before returning whence they came. The land slowly healed, but a half-open gate to Chaos was left in the ruins, leaking its slow evil ever since.

## The Hostile Road

The road to Asiki begins at the poor village of Kharan and its inn, the Red Horse, where rumor, a hunter's knowledge and a rival nobleman's expedition all point toward the cursed ruin. The way there is no gentler than the ruin itself: the parched high plains and their wolfmen, a fallen bridge, a hidden valley of monks who are werewolves at the full moon, and a ford held by a toll-taking ogre family - and over it all, the killing heat and thirst.

## The Gate to Chaos

Within a few miles of Asiki the land sickens, grass gone blue and nothing growing near the walls. Below the broken stubs lies a black-stone cellar where demons materialize again and again, rising from a gate held ajar by a small crystal pyramid. Only the shattering of that crystal closes the gate - and seals whatever has already come through on this side, to be faced in the dark.
