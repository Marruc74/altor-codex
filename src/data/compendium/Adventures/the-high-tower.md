## The Burning Tower

The heroes are travellers caught in the high mountains when they come on the Stonebuck, a walled stone inn kept by an old warrior named Ranald, who lets them in past a wary challenge from the wall and sits them by his fire. The evening is ordinary - other guests keep to themselves, a serving woman lays the table - until the innkeeper's son runs in shouting that the tower is burning. On a far peak a lonely tower stands wrapped in fire, light climbing to a starry sky, and out of it comes a distant cry of pain and then a roar of disappointment before the flames die.

Ranald tells them the tower belongs to Torvalg, the mage he once adventured beside, and asks them to climb to it and find out what has happened - and to help his friend, for pay, if help is what is needed. He would go himself, but for his family. The heroes take the work, rest the night, and set out at dawn with a rough map and the innkeeper's worry going with them.

## The Road Up

The way runs a full day through hard country, and the land tells them something is wrong long before they arrive. They come on a band of mountain orcs fighting among themselves - struck in the night by something that slipped out of the tower, half of them now wanting only to flee while their chief holds the rest by force. Among the dead the heroes find bodies frozen from the inside, eyes turned to ice: the mark of the thing that killed them, and a warning of what waits ahead.

It waits at the bridge to the peak, where the air has turned to winter and the span is sheathed in ice. A cloud of cold gathers into a man-shaped figure that beckons with soft, luring voices, then attacks - an ice demon that freezes the blood of any it grips, and that only fire will drive back across the worlds. Past it, the storm at its worst, the tower itself rises four storeys into a white chill, its windows flickering between colours and its torn door banging in the wind.

## The Gate in the Ceiling

Inside, the tower has been given over to demons. Most are petty things loosed to make mischief - jamming doors, rolling apples down the stairs, wrecking the kitchen - while the whispering dark plucks at the heroes' clothes and turns the shifting light against their aim. Up through halls of imps, animated bones and a hulking guardian, the heroes climb to the conjuring hall, where Torvalg kneels with his arms flung up at a gate torn in the ceiling, holding it half-shut against a monstrous prince that strains to force it wide. He has nothing left to give but words: the staff in his treasury, the key in the hollow globe, and a plea to be quick.

The treasury opens only with his leave and the hidden key, and as the staff is carried back a black-mailed warrior steps through the gate to take it by force. Put the staff in the old mage's hand and the deadlock breaks - the demons are torn back through the gate, the door slams shut, and the hall is whole and silent again. Torvalg thanks his rescuers, pays them from his hoard if they have not already helped themselves to it, and sends them back down to the Stonebuck with a letter for Ranald and a standing welcome at his fire - and, perhaps, the first thread of a longer road in a brooch broken cleanly in two.
