## The Marshland

In the far north of Kardien spreads the delta of the river Orquin: the Marshland, a trackless maze of reed-beds, mud-flats, hidden channels and some eighty islands. Invaded over the ages by elves, svartalfs, an aboriginal people and human settlers, and ever a refuge for pirates, it answers to the kings of Kardien in name alone. Folk fend for themselves here, frightful tales keep the curious away from buried caches, and boats that stray from the main channel are not always seen again. Almost the whole of it is crossed by canoe, for the winds are too weak to sail.

## The Hunt for the Crystal Orb

The through-line of all this wandering is a single errand. The old animist mage Serego has spent his life seeking the Crystal Orb, an artifact of power beyond reckoning that rests unguarded on a ruined island where an order of silversmiths once worked their silver-magic before plague and their enemies destroyed them. Serego would study the orb and then destroy it, knowing that in the wrong hands it would be ruin. He offers coin, training in the ways of the marsh, an old and partly-mistaken map, and a warning: the orb enslaves any bare hand that holds it, and part of his notes have already been stolen by a rival who covets it too.

## A Delta of Tales

Around that hunt the delta opens into a hundred smaller stories: a remote school of magic and the demon a student's folly looses; a music-loving elf tribe and the maiden stolen from them; a cold trading village hiding a dying child; a doorless cursed tower; a temple of bloody sacrifice; a dragon brooding flightless on his hoard; naiads, a knowledge-hungry karkion, and a drowned swan-maiden whose book ties the threads together. There is no promised victory at the orb's resting place, where trials and traps have turned back better wanderers - and an honorable failure may be worth more than a death in the dark.
