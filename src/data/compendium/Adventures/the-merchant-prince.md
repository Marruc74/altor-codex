## Pandarealia

Far out in the seas west of Ereb lies Pandarealia, a rich archipelago of black volcano-isles settled five centuries ago by clans fleeing an old feud on Caddo. Its people, the pandars, are masked folk: every soul wears an animal mask of precious metal in public, marking clan and standing, and an unmasked stranger is not counted a person at all, to be ignored or killed as suits the finder. They trust family first, clan second, faith last, and they are merchants to the bone, reckoning every newcomer as goods to be priced. Beneath them survive the febraker, the copper-skinned natives the settlers all but wiped out, and over them the three great trading clans scheme without end. It is a world made for an intrigue, and an intrigue is what the band walks into.

## The Offer

In Ereb the adventurers are hired by a smooth, maskless broker, Luciano Morte, on behalf of the merchant Guillano DiMercio, who complains that a rival house, the Machiavellios, is ruining him with sabotage and piracy. The work is simple: sail to Pandarealia and worm their way into the Machiavellio house to spy. The pay is a thousand gold in advance, and DiMercio, when they meet him, is so charming and so plainly wronged that no one thinks to doubt him. That is his whole art, for nothing about the man should ever seem the work of a villain.

## The Betrayal

The plan unfolds like clockwork, and every turn of it is a lie. Pirates take the ship on the crossing, and Morte is run through at the mast, killed by his own side to make the ambush look real. Cast ashore, the band stages the rescue of the merchant's son Mario from a street-attack, and so are welcomed into the Machiavellio house, first as guests, then as bodyguards, mapping its guards and its magician's wards all the while. On a moonless night DiMercio's men come in by the way the band has opened, and butcher every soul in the house. Only the adventurers are spared, stripped of arms and masks, and named to all the isles as the murderers. They flee into the mountains and starve out a winter in a cave, the prey of a whole city.

## The Avenger

In the spring soldiers ring them in, led by a limping woman in a silver bull-mask: Lucretia Machiavellio, the one survivor of the slaughter. She carries the Sword of Truth, which takes the head from any who lies before it, and by it she learns what the band could not have known, that they were tools and not traitors. She does not kill them. Instead she arms them from her family's ruin and gives them a single charge and a single chance: destroy the house DiMercio within three moons, or answer to her blade.

## The Brotherhood

The hunt for proof turns up worse than a greedy merchant. DiMercio's letters and false ledgers reveal a slave-trade run by code, scores of people vanished, and a master DiMercio himself serves: the Brotherhood, an assassin-cult of the dark god Vakrath, exiled out of Krun to a fog-wrapped volcano isle no pandar knows exists. They move their cargo on flying ships, weightless vessels left by a vanished race, and the band must stow away aboard one to reach the cult's hidden harbour. There, among the assassins and their slaves, a blue-robed priest of the god Iao haggles for a shipment of the doomed, and when the band is found the reckoning comes as a duel with the Grandmaster and his masters. The priest escapes by a teleport beyond his strength, dying for it, and yet his god and his shipment of slaves are the thread that draws the adventurers on into the tale of the Temple Lord.
