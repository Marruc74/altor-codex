# Ransard Prepares

Across its borders Ransard sharpens its blades, and every smith and quartermaster in the realm knows for whom. The old wound dealt by Trakorien has never closed, and now a hard and warlike Ransard means to repay it. Its neighbour Alerien has already been swallowed whole, and the armies that did it are not being disbanded but enlarged. Trakorien, watching from across the water, knows that the question is no longer whether the storm will break but where, and how soon.

## Overview

- **Sides:** Ransard against Trakorien
- **Cause:** Ransard's long-nursed thirst for revenge upon Trakorien
- **Status:** Open preparation for an offensive war - Alerien already annexed
- **Flashpoint:** Ransard's continued arming, set against the looming Palinor crusade of 610 eO

## The Hunger for Revenge

Ransard's grievance against Trakorien is old and deep, and it has festered rather than faded. The annexation of neighbouring Alerien was the first answer to that grievance, a swift and total conquest that left no doubt about Ransard's appetite or its capacity. With Alerien folded into the realm, Ransard now turns its full attention westward toward Trakorien, arming not for defence but for an offensive war of its own choosing.

## The Army of Ransard

Ransard has few friends, but it scarcely seems to need them. Its army is superb - well drilled, well equipped, and led by men who know their trade. Most fearsome of all are its flying griffins, whose sudden descent throws horse and foot alike into panic; cavalry breaks and infantry scatters before the beasts have struck a single blow. An enemy who has never faced griffins in the field will learn the lesson at a terrible price.

## The Threat of Three Fronts

Fate has set a cruel trap of timing. The crusade out of Palinor comes once in a hundred years, and the next is due in 610 eO - this very year. Should it march while Ransard strikes, Trakorien could find itself pressed on three sides at once: Ransard from the west, the elves from the north, and Palinor from the east. No realm could hold easily against such a vice, and Trakorien is not a realm well placed to try.

## Trakorien Unready

For all the danger bearing down upon it, Trakorien is poorly prepared. Its state of readiness is low and its army largely untrained, ill-suited to meet the seasoned legions of Ransard. Should war come, Trakorien may have little choice but to open its coffers to mercenaries and hope they prove loyal enough. It can count on the moral support of its neighbours among the Copper-Sea lands, and the elves to the north have no love of offensive war and may stay their hand - but goodwill and reluctance are thin armour against griffins.
