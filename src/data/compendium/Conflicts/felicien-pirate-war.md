# Felicien Pirate War

What began as a quarrel over cargo and harbour dues has hardened, year upon year, into open war at sea. The shipowners of Felicien have learned that there is more gold to be had in plunder than in honest freight, and their king has been only too willing to sell them the parchment that makes plunder legal. Today no merchant of Erebos dares cross the open water alone, and the lanes that once carried wine, cloth and grain now carry armed men who watch the horizon for sails.

## Overview

- **Sides:** Felicien and Hynsolge against Erebos, Berendien and Caddo
- **Cause:** Felician shipowners purchase royal letters of marque, allowing them to lawfully raid the merchant vessels of "enemy" realms
- **Status:** Active and escalating - Erebos now sails only in armed convoys
- **Flashpoint:** King Kuthegil Halfbeard's continued issuing of ever more letters of marque against the ships of Erebos

## Letters of Marque

The heart of the conflict is a single sheet of sealed parchment. A letter of marque, bought from the crown, declares its bearer no common pirate but a privateer in the king's name, free to seize the goods and ships of declared enemies and keep a share of the spoils. The shipowners of Felicien have made a thriving trade of it, and King Kuthegil Halfbeard issues the letters faster than ever, each one aimed squarely at the vessels of Erebos. What is robbery to the plundered is lawful war to the plunderer, and so the two sides will never agree even on what crime has been committed.

## The Armed Convoys

Erebos has answered the only way it can. Its merchantmen no longer sail singly but gather into convoys, ringed by ships of war and crowded with fighting men. The convoys keep the cargoes safe, more or less, but they serve a second purpose that the rulers of Erebos do not trouble to hide: under the honest excuse of protecting trade, Erebos rearms openly, building and manning warships it could not otherwise justify. Each convoy that puts to sea is both a shield and a quiet threat.

## The Wider Alliance

The war has drawn in more than the two who began it. Hynsolge stands with Felicien, while Berendien and Caddo have thrown their weight behind Erebos. Yet the alliance against the pirates is far from sound. Caddo and Erebos are bitter rivals of long standing, bound together now only by common enemies, and few believe the friendship will outlast its convenience. Should the winds of advantage shift, Caddo might well turn its hulls against Erebos overnight.

## Those Who Stay Ashore

Erebos counts Kardien and Zorakin among its friends, but neither will be of much use at sea. Both possess fleets too weak to matter in a war of convoys and privateers, and so both have chosen to keep clear of the fighting. Their absence leaves Erebos to carry the weight of the war on its own decks, and sharpens the danger should Caddo ever change its mind.
