# Nidland Purification

In the cold lands of Nidland a darkness gathers under one man's will. The realm is ruled by the evil magician Vicotnik, and his people are bound to him in fanatic obedience, their thoughts and their faith bent wholly to his purpose. Year by year Nidland grows more hostile to the world beyond its borders, and those who read the old prophecies count the seasons with dread. A crusade is coming out of the north, and it will not come to convert but to cleanse.

## Overview

- **Sides:** Nidland, under the magician Vicotnik, against the lands to its south - chiefly Cereval
- **Cause:** Vicotnik's prophesied crusade of purification against the unfaithful
- **Status:** Arming and gathering - the crusade is foretold to set out in 612 eO
- **Flashpoint:** A prophecy holding that the crusade comes "a dozen-dozen years" after Vicotnik's coming

## The Magician's Realm

Vicotnik rules Nidland not as a king among subjects but as an idol among the devoted. His people obey him with a fervour that brooks no question, their will subsumed into his, and under that obedience the whole realm has turned ever more hostile toward its neighbours. What Vicotnik commands, Nidland does, and what Vicotnik intends is a war of purification - a crusade to scour the lands beyond his borders of all that he names impure.

## The Prophecy

The timing of the crusade is no secret, for the prophecy itself names the hour. A dozen-dozen years after Vicotnik's coming, it holds, the crusade will set out - and that reckoning falls in the year 612 eO. The realms to the south have been warned, then, and yet warning has bought them little. The march is barely two years off, and Nidland's preparations are already well advanced.

## The Want of Iron

Nidland's one great weakness is its poverty in iron and weapons. The land yields too little of either to arm a crusade on its own, and so Vicotnik must buy what his smiths cannot make - most likely from Akrogal, whose merchants ask no questions a heavy purse cannot answer. Cut that supply, and the crusade falters before it begins; leave it open, and Nidland's hosts will march well armed despite the barren ground that bred them.

## The Likely Target

All signs point to Cereval as the object of the coming crusade. Yet even Vicotnik will choose his path with care. He will probably steer clear of the trolls that haunt the Cer Forest and give a wide berth to the dragon masters, neither being foes worth the cost of provoking. To the south of Nidland lies Krun, which keeps no readiness at all and would make easy prey, while Hynsolge - which might otherwise lend a hand against the purge - is tangled in a civil war of its own and can spare no eyes for the north. This is the war the Nidlandish Cleansing has long foreshadowed, and its hour draws near.
