# Gadirm

Gadirm, called the Death-Lord and the Quick-witted, is the necromancer-prince who holds the title of Dragon-Master, won by slaying the great dragon Garaum and so earning the name Garaum's-bane. He rules a small independent realm where nearly all the labour is done by the undead, and there leads an academy of a dozen skilled wizards in his pay, given over to research that is mostly magical but reaches also into astrology, the lore of drugs, strategy and mathematics. He is reckoned among the Dimension Wardens, hunting down the hidden doors through which demons creep into Altor, and is said to walk the Guardian's Path, though scarcely halfway along it.

## Manner

He is eloquent, patient and calm, self-assured, suspicious and tireless in his work, talkative and yet somehow reserved, an idealist who believes in knowledge and intelligence in all their forms. Above all he distrusts feeling, holding that emotion clouds reason, and so allows himself none, which gives his manner an eerie, machine-like coldness. He keeps one safety-valve: each evening he sits at the spinet and plays out the day's stored-up feelings, and only there lets them loose. Through long dimension-travel he has lived more years than have passed in the world since his birth, and life-extension magic has aged his body slower still, so that he is far older within than he looks.

## The Slaying of Garaum

To be made Dragon-Master a mage must compel the respect of even the mightiest dragons, and Gadirm set himself to take that respect from Garaum the Ancient, the City-Render and Mountain-King, a beast of near twelve thousand years and father of nine old dragons. He did not face the dragon in open battle. Over months of study he wove an elaborate deception, coming to Garaum's hoard with a false tale of a treasure-laden queen ripe for the taking in the eastern city of Kandor, and offering to ward the dragon's own hoard while it was away on the raid. Trust so bought, he hid his trap in the very gifts and wards he laid: spells concealed in disguised silver, and a scrap of the dragon's own hide worked into a ritual of distant harm. When it was sprung, Garaum was struck down far from his cave, and Gadirm's death-wraiths, clad in dragon-hide and so untouched by the fire, finished what the trap began. Afterward he called up the dead dragon's spirit and questioned it at length.

It was a victory not of strength but of cunning, and that is the heart of why the dragons accepted him. Where the knights prize a "noble battle" of brute force, which dragons reckon a fools' contest won by the strongest arm, Gadirm and the dragons alike hold the true noble battle to be a battle of minds, won by the cleverest. By besting Garaum at that game he won the dragons' regard as no Dragon-Master before him had, taking the title directly from the beasts rather than from the dragon-lords' tests.

## The Dragon-Master

The dragon-lords were uneasy at a master they had not chosen, until Gadirm sought them out in secret and promised to carry on the Encyclopedia Draconicus. Where earlier masters disarmed the dragons' deadly game of honour by being worthless to kill, he warns instead that to slay him is to raise him as a death-wraith of threefold power. He has proved less a gatherer of lore than a questioner of old certainties, and counts dragon-magic among the few arts a necromancer's mind can also turn to; he has set down a plain-language primer of dragon-lore for adventurers, drawn from the great Encyclopedia, and now plans an expedition into the far east to study the lung, the eastern dragons of whom the masters know so little.
