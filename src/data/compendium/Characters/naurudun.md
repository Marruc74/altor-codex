# Naurudun

Mikal Naurudun Darius was the sixteenth son of the Hynsolgian baron Moshe, with fifteen brothers standing between him and any hope of the title, and he was bored to the bone. He could keep accounts and handle a weapon and drift from one feast and hunt to the next, which is exactly the sort of restless young noble the Brotherhood of the Red Fish likes to recruit, and so the Watcher Akane Ironbearer found him, sounded him out, and adopted him into the octopus.

## The Making of a Thief

His first task was a kidnapping. He wormed his way into the court of Count Hamfa, an old friend of his father, and drew the count's gentle daughter Malmolina out to a late ride by the dark forest with anonymous love-letters. When her riderless horse came back at midnight he played the gallant, led the search, "found" the spot and a planted letter, and galloped to the count with the news, all the while serving as the family's own go-between with the kidnappers he worked for. The thing ended happily and very expensively, and Akane's commendation followed him to Baron Vityxne's hunting-lodge in the dark forest, which is no hunting-lodge at all but one of the brotherhood's branches, where he was schooled for a year and came away with its highest marks.

After that he made his name as a knight-errant, a charming diplomat who rode out against thieves and bandits and brought them to heel with words before swords, beloved even by older heads. That courts he visited tended to suffer thefts while he was there, and that the thieves were caught quickly, often by Naurudun himself, no one thought to connect.

## The Burning

In time the duet that watched his arm marked him out, and the Council judged him worthy to be made one of the Burned, knowing as he did that one chosen in three burns to ash in the ritual rather than rises from it. A player by nature, he took the wager, sold his soul to the fire-demon bound above his heart, and shed even his given names for the single one the brotherhood knew him by ever after. He need never creep through the dark like a common thief again.

He was not always so exalted. In the cavern-city of Nordsal, charged with stealing a dwarven blueprint for the necromancer Camulius Martein, he was still only a recruit, nervy and distracted and desperate for the promotion he could feel coming, which is how he came to lose the parchment in the crowds and set the whole farce of the stolen elephant in motion.
