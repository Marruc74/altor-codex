# Kenvadsin Lao'Geraftjan

Kenvadsin Lao'Geraftjan is a necromancer of nine-and-twenty, a Krunian burgher's son out of Erebos, and about as far from the legend of the skull-faced grave-robber as a man of his trade can be. He is good-looking in a soft way, with a neat moustache, blue eyes, close-cropped fair hair and large ears, and he has had no shortage of mistresses. He goes about as a travelling interpreter, for languages are his particular study, and wears a soft black suit and a mantle sewn with red stars, circles and sigils on the inside, where no one sees them.

## The Quiet Sort

He is not a violent man and knows no true battle-magic. When a fight finds him he talks his way clear of it, or falls back on a spell of pain and on the spider-boil, which he learned from the third disciple of Farengadd of Helsing. He is friendly, makes few enemies, and saves his temper for the uneducated, whom he cannot abide. Two things drive him: a hunger for fame, and a long-nursed thirst for justice, or revenge, depending on who tells it.

The art has begun to mark him even so. His hands have grown claws, which he keeps hidden in black leather-backed gloves, and his voice has been choked down to a whisper. He hides his familiar from strangers, and few who deal with the pleasant interpreter ever guess what he is.

## Faldos

His familiar is Faldos, an animated rat-skeleton raised long ago by Kenvadsin's own master and passed down to him. The little thing creeps and hides and listens on his behalf, and still gnaws at hazelnuts out of old habit, never having understood that it died.
