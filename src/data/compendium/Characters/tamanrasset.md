# Tamanrasset

Tamanrasset, "Taman the Mighty," is a goblin who rules a community of some two thousand of his kind by the power of stolen magic. He is unusual among goblins for his sharp, logical mind, and prizes books and study above all the riches he has gathered.

## The Bookish Goblin

He was born in 527 in a goblin community in the Grynnerbergen of eastern Ereb, the son of a respected shaman who could read and write and taught his son to do the same. The father kept a collection of books that Taman pored over instead of joining the fist-fights of his fellows, and when the boy refused to work the mine the worried father burned the books as an offering. Taman fled home, taking what valuables he could carry.

## The Long Study

He wandered west to Kandra in Berendien, crept into the magicians' house and stole books of mentalism and one of elemental magic, then spent years in a forest hut in the north of that land, studying. After that he travelled on, buying and robbing more books as he went; by some accounts he reached as far west as Pendon and dwelt for a time in Fristaden. Where he came by his necromancy is a mystery, though it was most likely at the hidden necromancers' academy in the Vidvind forest of Jorpagna.

## The Coup

At last he fell in with goblins from the Nidabergen north of Berendien and, working on the whole community with his magic, seized it in a bloodless coup. He rules them now in full, his warriors known far and wide as the skull-crushers for the spiked clubs they bring down on their enemies' heads. He keeps a personal treasury rich in silver and gems, for the settlement sits on a fine lode, and a small store of magical items besides. Selfish and given to cruelty, careful of his own skin yet trusting his magic utterly, he is known by his thin black hair bound in a knot, his wide and evil smile, and the black silk robe with a silver pentagram on the back, a rat's skull hanging at his breast.
