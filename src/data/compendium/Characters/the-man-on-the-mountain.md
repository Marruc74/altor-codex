# The Man on the Mountain

High in the mountains west of Ransard sits a very old man who is said to know everything, and to answer any question put to him, for a price. His face is marked by the suffering of centuries and by a wisdom that seems to have no bottom.

## The Climb

Long ago he wandered these mountains like any other traveller. One day he turned and began to climb toward the highest peak, and when he reached it he sat down, and he has sat there ever since, drawing his sustenance from the energies of the earth itself. He sits naked and beardless on the bare stone, and the few who have raised a hand against him he has put down with a plain wooden staff; his power is very great. Those who climb up merely to disturb his rest with idle questions he sends back to the foot of the mountain with a single gesture.

## The Price

The price of an answer is always something of true value to the one who asks, weighed against the gravity of the question, and it may be anything at all. Ask him the truth of life, of the universe, of everything, and the price is everything: body and soul together.

## Sikanin

Legend tells that he was once an ordinary man, a gardener by the name of Sikanin, who in order to win his answers mortified himself and meditated for three hundred years, solving his own troubles one by one as he went. Whether any of this is true, no one can say, for the one question he will not answer is the question of himself.
