# Serek the Dark

Serek the Dark is perhaps the most feared mercenary in all of Ereb. He takes no pay, but appears wherever war is brewing and offers his services to one side, with a marked tendency to favour the crueller of the two. He first made his name at the battle of Bedorum in 580, riding down the Hynsolge champion Denat Urira and all three of his companions single-handed; at Cormwak in 603, the deed for which he is best remembered, he is said to have slain nineteen griffin-riders and their mounts in a single hour of berserk fury.

## Blood

His origins are dark. He was born more than fifty years ago in the eastern Sivoa forests of northern Samkarna, the son of a wood elf and an ogre who had served a local chieftain and forced himself upon her; when Serek was born, his mother took her own life. He was raised among her elven kin, whom he came to admire deeply, but the ogre's blood ran too thick in him for the elves ever to count him their own. Grown to manhood, he went out into the world with a single purpose: to avenge his mother.

## The Veiled Killer

Serek is hideous to look on and goes always with a dark veil over his face, with holes cut for the eyes; he is an albino, and beneath the veil his eyes burn an ominous red. From his father he has an ogre's strength and frame, but his mother's elven blood has left him far sharper of mind than any ogre, and gifted with an uncanny sense for violence: he feels, without fail, in which direction blood is about to be spilled, and goes there.

He holds that all who fight and make war are evil and ought to be destroyed by the sword, himself included, which is why the world reckons him evil for all his honestly twisted ideals. He will not strike unprovoked save in a pitched battle, and under no circumstances will he raise a hand against elves; armed elves he counts as brothers-in-arms, and is wounded afresh each time they refuse his service, as they always do. But should he come upon elves at war with one another, he falls into a killing madness and tries to slay them all.
