# Houd Istimam

Houd Istimam is a wandering necromancer who has never once cast a spell. Most magicians of southern Ereb know the gaunt, ragged old man by sight and pay him no mind, taking him for a harmless madman; few suspect that he may be a fully trained sorcerer who simply refuses to prove it.

## The Runaway

He was born in 563 of a mining town in the mountains of Klomellien, the son of a prominent priest. Drawn to sorcery as a boy, he turned his back on his father's strict creed, which forbade all magic but the animist's, and ran away east. In Jorgund he fell in with the master-thief Gwynton Featherfoot, who promised to smuggle books out of the magicians' academy and win him a place there. Houd kept his side of the bargain; Gwynton was knifed before he could keep his.

## The Self-Taught Necromancer

Houd fled and built himself a hut in the forests of Landori, where he taught himself in secret from the stolen books. Most of them dealt with necromancy, and he studied them so feverishly that in the end he knew them forwards and back. After years of this he reckoned himself fully learned and set out into the world, by now a gaunt, limping figure in rags, his beard long and his old speech half forgotten. Everywhere he went he was mocked and driven off.

## The Spell He Will Not Cast

For all the threats he has met on the road, Houd has never once used a spell, and for two reasons. He is not certain his magic would work at all, having never tested it, and dreads to learn that all his study was for nothing. And he has heard of a necromancers' academy hidden in the Vidvind forest of Jorpagna, the finest and mightiest in Ereb, which takes only novices who have never cast a spell. He has spent more than ten years searching the forests of Jorpagna for it, after combing every library and academy in Zorakin, Kardien, Berendien and Erebos, and he wanders still, never staying anywhere more than a few days. Sharp of eye and ear despite his age, he lets every insult pass without a word, and remembers each one against the day he may repay it.
