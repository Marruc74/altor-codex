# Arn Dunkelbrink

Arn Dunkelbrink da Kharasme was the heir to Kharasma, the largest principality of Lasemos, and from a young age was known as a peerless swordsman and war-leader, strict in his faith and forever reading the gods' will in sheep's entrails and fate-cards before any undertaking. He made a poorer showing at court, lost among the ladies, without humour or small talk, caring nothing for music or learning, yet he was loved by his people and might well have united the principalities of Lasemos under one crown had fate not willed otherwise. He is a knight of the ancient Banzikan, the fighting-school of the war-god Trocuspa, whose symbols are the oak, the stone and the seed.

## The Dragon-Hunt

It was his younger brother Ambrion Blue-snout, hungry for the throne, who goaded Arn into the wager that undid him: to face a living dragon alone and prove himself worthy of the princely title. In 608 eO Arn dressed plainly, swore not to return until he had bested a dragon single-handed, and set out. After half a year following only misty rumour he tracked the middle-aged dragon Blatifagus to the dark mountains north of ancient Jorpagna and walked into its cave without fear, as a knight of the Banzikan does. He hurled his spear into the soft flesh beside the dragon's right eye even as its fire bloomed; the flame burned the very flesh from his face, and only his clothes of phoenix-skin saved his life. Blinded, half-mad with pain, he hacked the tip from the beast's tail, and Blatifagus screamed in agony in the forbidden tongue of the world before the first conflux, a speech only dragons know and the gods have forbidden them to use, until the cliffs themselves warped at the words and the dragon fled.

## The Forbidden Words

Arn took only the severed tail-tip, still living, and carried it away in a sack, jabbing it with needles to ease his rage at the memory of the fight. He had scribes set down piecemeal the unmaking words the dragon had screamed, and left the parchments in the keeping of a law-speaker and friend on the island of Bhannavil, for he knew the words were wrong and should never have been spoken. Among them, he came to understand, was the dragon's true name, a thing that could be read aloud to bind the beast utterly.

## The Pilgrim

Bitter and grave, shunning company, Arn travelled on in a pilgrim's habit, a mail-shirt graven with the Banzikan's symbols beneath the cope, presenting himself as the weapon-master Onedom of Bhannavil. His face and hands are wholly ruined by Blatifagus's fire, though his mountain-grey eyes have healed and look out at the world with an unshakeable strength. He thinks of nothing but the dragon until he finds it; only then will he go home to his inheritance, untroubled that Ambrion has had himself proclaimed prince. He worries, sometimes, that he hears voices, and drives both the worry and the voices off with hard exercise and stern devotions, not knowing that the moment he heard the gods' first tongue he lost a mortal's innocence and became a thing apart, marked out for some fate the gods have not yet shown.

## The Binding

Blatifagus, half-blind and tormented by the needles in its captured tail, fled north and at last recalled the dwarves' quicksilver mine on Marjura, where a healer had told it quicksilver was good for aching flesh, and lairing there on the cold northern island. By the time Arn ran the dragon to ground in that mine, in the summer of 613 eO, the dead king Ottar had woken in Cruri and his undead host had driven the Trakorians from Marjura, though the dragon cared little, having only grown sick of mutton in any case. This time Arn demanded not the beast's life but its obedience, for he held its true name ready on Bhannavil to be read out to all the world should it refuse. Together they left Marjura and flew south through heavy fog, the scarred knight on the dragon's back, until after many days over open water an unknown land came in sight, and Arn bade the dragon go down to drink. What befell them there is another tale.
