# The Demon Sledge

A black-hafted sledgehammer with a rune-graven metal head, forged in 610 eO at the order of the over-inquisitor Yrvein of Krerex. The work was done by the exiled dwarf weaponsmith Kvakin Salvus, the same hand that forged the balrog-slayer for King Valien of Zorakin, and the hammer is a masterpiece of the craft. It is also a fraud.

## The Claim

Yrvein puts it about that the sledge both kills demons and unmasks them. The test is simple: strike a suspect hard across the head. If he dies, he was no demon, and the matter is settled; if he lives, he is plainly a demon, so strike him again. By this reasoning no one the hammer touches comes out of it well.

## The Truth

There is no magic in it at all. The Demon Sledge is an ordinary, if beautifully made, sledgehammer, and any demonologer sees through it in a moment. Kvakin has since gone to live in Krilloan. Yrvein, who has tried the hammer once - on a Caddish priest, who was no demon - has grasped how useful it is for ridding himself of inconvenient people, and means to carry it on his next circuit through Kardien and Zorakin.
