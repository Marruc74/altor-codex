# The Crown Jewels

A king's regalia are five: mantle, orb, sceptre, sword and crown, together the perfect mark of a sovereign. These are no mere ornaments but magical things of the intelligent kind, holy objects that bend their bearer toward certain ends, and the more of them a ruler wears at once the more of their power he can hold. There are two sets in the world, one good and one evil, and a king is known by which he wears.

## The Good Set

The good regalia are wrought of gold inlaid with mithril and gems, softly curved and shining, and they give off a faint light; each piece carries a blessing, and the more worn together the brighter that grace shines.

- **The crown Shenovio** lifts the wearer's bearing and wit, shields his head, steadies his nerve against fear, and weaves about him a working of peace.
- **The mantle Heroid** guards like a full suit of plate, muffles the wearer's step, turns aside hostile magic, and holds him at an even warmth whatever the weather.
- **The sceptre Larima** lays bare every lie and illusion within sight or hearing, and lets its holder read magic, sway minds, dispel and unlock.
- **The orb Midasor** feeds the whole set with power; year by year it can stave off the king's ageing until at last he no longer dies of old age, and it turns aside poison and sickness and mends his wounds, though should he lose it the lost years come crashing back.
- **The sword Navadar** strikes mightily and brighter still in sunlight, wounds the unholy worse than the living, can be called back to the king's hand across a good distance, glows in warning when danger nears, and points the way to anything it has cut that still lives.

## The Evil Set

The evil regalia are forged of black steel set with silver and gems, their patterns twisted and grotesque, and a dark shadow clings to them; the aura they give off leaves all who see it uneasy.

- **The crown Shadovio** lends the same gifts of bearing and protection but turns them to mastery over the dead, over spirits and over demons, and crowns the wearer in dread.
- **The mantle Nidzorid** guards like plate, cloaks its bearer in shadow, and turns aside magic.
- **The sceptre Cahn** unmasks lies and illusions as Larima does, but wields the death-hand, terror, dispelling and sealing.
- **The orb Tiguzor** feeds the dark set and, year by year, works its bearer into an undead wraith rather than sparing him from age; it turns aside charms and love-spells, but lose it and the holder withers away.
- **The sword Shebsadar** strikes harder in darkness, wounds the holy worse than others, and lays a curse on those it cuts that festers like a wasting sickness; it too returns to the hand and points to its living victims.

## A Duel of Kings

Should two rulers ever meet in single combat bearing these regalia, the one wrapped in light and the other in shadow, a strange thing happens where their powers touch: everything turns to its opposite, the bright going black and the dark going pale, as though the whole scene were a photographic negative.
