# Neverind's Magic Sack

Neverind's sack looks like any other sack from the outside, but it opens onto a whole empty dimension. It was made by the necromancer Neverind, a wicked and grasping man of the middle 400s, and it has outlived him in the strangest way.

## The Maker's Fate

For his crimes the higher gods passed a peculiar sentence on Neverind: they cast him into a newborn, empty dimension where time does not run. There he has not aged, and there he remains to this day, twisted by the long ages into something close to a demon, mad and hateful toward everything living and divine.

## The Sack

The sack is of middling size and weighs almost nothing, no matter how much is stuffed into it, for what it holds is held in that other dimension. Reach inside and there is only darkness and a clammy, clinging cold. The space within is all but endless, so it can swallow as much as one cares to put in; but no living creature can pass through, willing or not. To draw something out, one thrusts in a hand and thinks of the thing wanted, and it comes to the grasp at no cost of effort. Since nothing inside can be seen, a wise owner keeps a written list, for grope about blindly and one is as likely to come up with the wrong thing as the right. The sack is proof against fire of every kind.

## The Danger

There is a peril in it. Each time something is drawn out by chance, there is the faintest risk that the act has loosened the door of Neverind's prison a little, and magical things put into the sack may likewise help him claw his way back. The sack is thought to rest these days with some old adventurer, or forgotten in the hoard of a dragon.
