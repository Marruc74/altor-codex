# Soul-Bound Weapons

Among the rarest and most coveted arms in Ereb are those into which a living spirit has been bound: a captured elemental, a demon, or even a dragon, sealed into blade or armour so that the thing becomes half-alive, with a will and a cunning of its own. Such weapons cannot be forged of common steel; only cold-hammered iron, bronze, silver or mithril will hold a soul, and the binding is the work of high ritual magic.

## The Ensouled Blade

A soul-bound weapon is no dead thing. It has a measure of strength, quickness, wit and will, and it lends these to the hand that wields it — striking harder, parrying faster, sometimes carrying a spell bound within it that the bearer may loose by spending a little of his own spirit. But every such weapon carries the weakness of the thing sealed inside it: a blade housing a fire-spirit fears the cold, one housing a water-spirit fears the flame, and a wise enemy learns which is which.

## Weapons of the Elements

The most common ensouled arms hold an elemental of fire, air, water or earth, and strike with the fury of that element. Stranger and rarer are their cousins: the **umbror**, weapons of darkness; the **luminals**, weapons of light; the **glacials**, weapons of cold; and the **thermers**, weapons of heat. Each can do more than wound, loosing a burning or freezing stroke, a blinding flash, a wall of ice or of fire raised between the bearer and his foes, and each can unmake a weapon of its opposing element at a touch.

## Dragon-Soul Weapons

Rarest of all are the weapons that hold a dragon's soul. They lend the bearer a dragon's terror and a paralysing presence, blood that eats through armour and steel, and a breath of fire. But the price is heavy. Every dragon that lives hates and hunts the one who carries such a blade, and the bearer slowly takes on the dragon's own nature, its greed and its quick, burning temper, until little of the man is left. And should the bound dragon's true body die somewhere in the world, the weapon's power wanes away with it.

## Divine Arms

Apart from all these stand the divine weapons and armour, which no mortal binds: a god or an arch-demon makes them of its own will, for itself or for a chosen champion. None but the maker, or the one it expressly permits, may wield them at all, and they wax and wane in power with the fortunes of the god whose strength they carry.

## A Famous Example

The most infamous soul-bound weapon in all Ereb is **Atrax the Mankiller**, a two-handed sword into which the demonologist Altor of Arvorn Gildarion chained the soul of the demon prince Atrax. It lends its bearer terrible strength and speed, strikes with the force of ten ordinary blows, and is all but indestructible. But the bound prince has never forgotten his captivity, and now and again the sword's soul simply refuses to fight, and leaves its wielder stripped of every gift at the very worst of moments.
