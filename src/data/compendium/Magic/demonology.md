# Demonology

Demonology is the art of trafficking with the demons of Demonicum — of calling them across the dimension walls, binding them to one's will, and driving them back whence they came. It is the most perilous of all the magical disciplines, for its every working deals with a being that hates the magician and seeks his ruin, and it is feared and hunted across nearly every land of Ereb. Yet for all that, there has never been a shortage of those willing to pay its price.

## Overview

- **What it is:** The art of summoning, binding and banishing demons
- **Its practitioners:** Demonologists, trained twenty or thirty years under a master
- **Its sister art:** Portal magic, the craft of opening ways between the worlds
- **Its two branches:** The subjugators, who capture and exploit demons; the banishers, who drive them off and hunt those who summon them
- **Its tools:** Protection circles, pentagrams, true names, and the lintropic arts of the demon-hunter
- **Its danger:** A summoned demon is furious, faithless, and bent on the summoner's destruction

## Names and Summoning

The whole craft turns upon names. Every demon bears a usual name, by which it may be called across the walls of the Multiverse, found in old books or sensed by a minor working when the demon is near. But every demon also bears a true name, given it by the Light to hold it in check: spoken in the demon's presence, the true name binds it utterly to the speaker's will, and a demon can do no harm to one who knows it. To summon, the demonologist draws his circles and his pentagram, calls the demon by its usual name into the trap he has prepared, and there masters it, binds it, or shapes it to his purpose — and if he is wise, sends it home again before his wards can fail.

## Circles and Pentagrams

A demonologist's life hangs upon the lines he draws. A protection circle keeps a summoned thing from reaching those within it; a prison-pentagram, drawn the other way about, pens the demon in and saps its strength. Such figures may be strengthened with costly materials worked into the lines — silver and mithril, the blood of dragons and of demons — or, for a moment's need, by the sacrifice of the magician's own spirit. But every circle is only as strong as its weakest stroke: a single broken line, and the whole ward fails at once, with the demon loosed inside it.

## The Faithless Servant

No demon serves willingly. A summoned demon is wild with fury, and spends its cunning trying to provoke the magician into sending it back or breaking his own protections, that it may take its revenge. It obeys an order to the letter or in its widest possible sense, twisting a careless command into years of ruin; it never tells a useful truth unless the truth serves it, and speaks always in riddles and half-meanings. Slain in our world, it loses only its body and returns to its plane to take another; it can be destroyed for good only at home. For this reason the banishers hold that the safest demon is the one never called at all, and they make it their work to hunt down the subjugators who think otherwise.

## The Sister Art

Close beside demonology stands portal magic, the craft of opening and closing the ways between worlds. By it a mage seals or hides or destroys a portal, opens a window to speak across the planes, or tears a gate into the Grey Halls to walk the grey path himself. The two arts are studied together, for the demonologist who would call a demon must first understand the doors through which it comes.
