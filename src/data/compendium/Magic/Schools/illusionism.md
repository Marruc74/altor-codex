# Illusionism

Illusionism is the art of the senses and of deceit, the weaving of sights, sounds and feelings that are not there. With mentalism and symbolism it is one of the arts of the mind, all three descended from the old dragon magic, and of them it is the most given to trickery.

## Overview

- **Domain:** the senses and their deception
- **Aspect:** consciousness, descended from dragon magic
- **Strength:** a lie the eye, ear and mind cannot tell from the truth
- **Limit:** pure illusion has no substance of its own

## Phantoms and Wounds

A true illusion has no body and can lift no weight, yet a victim who believes in it can still be hurt, for a convinced mind will wound its own flesh. Such hurts leave no real injury and mend swiftly, but a man sure that he has been slain may die of it, his heart simply stopping. The more senses an illusion must fool, sight first, then sound, then smell and touch together, the harder it is to weave. The illusionist always perceives his own conjurings, but is never himself deceived by them.
