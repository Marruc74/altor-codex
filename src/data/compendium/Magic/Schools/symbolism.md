# Symbolism

Symbolism is the art of signs, worked through symbols drawn in the old magical tongue of the dragons. Where other mages cast their power upon the air, the symbolist binds it into a mark, set down in advance and waiting to be loosed. With mentalism and illusionism it is one of the arts of the mind grown from dragon magic, and of the three it keeps closest to the mentalist's craft.

## Overview

- **Worked through:** a physical symbol, drawn in the dragon-tongue
- **Aspect:** consciousness, descended from dragon magic
- **Requires:** a complete sign in lasting material, at least a hand's-breadth across
- **Limit:** strikes only intelligent creatures, and is mostly spent in a single use

## The Drawn Word

A symbol must be whole and made in durable material, and the larger the sign the farther its power reaches. Drawn with care beforehand and triggered later, it looses its magic once and then is spent, falling only upon a thinking mind. Because his power waits in the mark rather than in himself, a symbolist can lay a working long before it is needed; but he can never also take up the staff-mage's path, for the two disciplines will not sit in one mage.
