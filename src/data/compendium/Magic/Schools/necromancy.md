# Necromancy

Necromancy is the magic of death, the art of the corpse, the curse and the grave. It is the grimmest of the workings that grew out of mentalism, the school of body and soul, and the one men shun most. The necromancer does not heal nor build; he raises the dead, calls down plague and curse, and wastes the living away, and the dead-house and the battlefield are his proper haunts.

## Overview

- **Art:** The magic of death and of the dead
- **Root:** The death-magic branch of mentalism, grown into a discipline of its own
- **Domain:** Corpses and the undead, plague, curse, and the borderland of life and death
- **The necromancer's gift:** He feels the presence of death about him, and senses the undead at a distance
- **A known codex:** *Nekron's Book*, cursed by the god of death himself
- **Disposition:** Shunned and feared, close kin to the dark art

## The Raising of the Dead

The first and best-known work of the art is the giving of unlife to a corpse, to make a zombie bound to the necromancer's will. The mightiest do not stop at one: they raise whole armies of the dead, from a score of shambling servants to many hundreds. And the necromancer who goes furthest of all works the rite of the Lord of the Undead, by which he ceases to be a living man and becomes himself a deathless thing, vampire- or mummy-like, beyond the reach of age.

## Curse, Plague and Wasting

Against the living the necromancer turns a colder craft. He bursts the blood within a body, rots a limb to ruin, withers strength and health away, and binds a death-link so that a wound meant for him falls instead upon another. He can become a walking pestilence, breathing plague upon all near him, and lay a curse upon a single person, a whole bloodline, or an entire city. By aging a stolen victim he can even renew his own failing years.

## The Necromancer

A necromancer is marked by his trade. He feels where death has been and senses the undead within reach, and he studies the "animal magnetism" of the borderland, that grey country between the living and the dead. Foremost among the books of the art is *Nekron's Book*, a codex of death-magic so steeped in power that the death-god laid his own curse upon it: whoever owns it prospers in his craft and meets a terrible end.

## Kin to the Dark Art

Necromancy is the old magic of death, known here and there across Ereb wherever a mentalist follows his art to its darkest end. It should not be confused with the dark magic of Vicotnik, the grimmer discipline carried out of the east and drawing its strength from the moon - though the two are close cousins, and a land that fears the one has good reason to fear the other.
