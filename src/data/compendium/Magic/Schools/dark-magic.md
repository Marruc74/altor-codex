# Dark Magic

Of all the arts of sorcery known in Ereb, none is so feared - nor so reviled - as dark magic. It is a craft turned wholly inward upon ruin: a magic that does not build, nor heal, nor illuminate, but rots, curses and unmakes. Those who wield it walk apart from other men, marked by the moon and shunned by the living, and the realm they raised in the north stands as a warning to all who would bargain with such power.

## Overview

- **Brought to Ereb by:** Vicotnik the Archmage
- **Year of arrival:** 468 eO
- **Place of origin within Ereb:** Nidland, just south of Cereval
- **Seat of power:** The Academy in Morke
- **Source of power:** Luna, the moon - strongest at the full moon, near to nothing at the new
- **Disposition:** Wholly given to ruin, withering and curse
- **Mark upon its wielders:** Night-sight; a creature of the night, shunned and rumored to be a vampire

## Origin

Dark magic is not native to Ereb. It came out of the east, from the distant lands of Akrogal, carried by a single man of terrible learning. Before his coming the sorcerers of the western kingdoms knew nothing of it, and after his coming they wished they had remained in ignorance. What he brought was not a new spell or two but an entire discipline of the art, ordered and complete, founded upon principles no Ereban mage had ever dared to follow to their end.

## Vicotnik and the Academy

In the year 468 eO there came out of the eastern lands of Akrogal a sorcerer who called himself Vicotnik the Archmage. He crossed into the north and settled in Nidland, the bleak country lying just south of Cereval, and there he set about raising a realm in his own image.

He founded a magocracy - a state ruled by magicians and by magic alone - and at its heart he built the Academy in the city of Morke. From this seat he ruled as Grand-Emperor, and about him gathered some twenty other dark mages whom he named his "judges." Within his borders he forbade the practice of all other magic; only the dark art was to be studied, only his discipline permitted. Two grim sayings are graven upon his rule, and men still repeat them with a shudder of dread: "You shall die!" and "He shall die!" In them is the whole temper of the man and his craft.

## The Nature of Dark Magic

To learn dark magic is to be changed by it. Every dark mage gains the gift of night-sight, seeing clearly where other men are blind, and in return becomes a creature of the night - one who shuns the day, is shunned in turn by ordinary folk, and is whispered after as a kind of vampire. Whether the rumor is true or no, the dark mages do nothing to dispel it, and it serves their dread well enough.

The power of the art is drawn not from within the mage but from Luna, the moon. As the moon waxes, so the dark magic grows strong; at the full moon it stands at its height and its workings are most terrible. As the moon wanes, the power ebbs, until at the new moon it fades to almost nothing and the dark mage is left near as powerless as any common man. By this the wise may reckon when such a sorcerer is most to be feared, and when he may be safely defied.

## The Dark Spells

The spells of the dark art are turned, every one, toward ruin. The mage learns no working that mends or makes; all his craft bends to harm.

- **The evil eye** - a sense by which the mage perceives the magic worked in his presence and reads its very nature, so that no spell may be cast near him unseen.
- **Curses** - tailored by the mage to the particular victim. Some strike blind any who look upon the cursed; some fill all beholders with sudden panic, so that they flee in terror; some erase a face so wholly that none who knew the victim can recognize him; some weaken the legs and the bones beneath the flesh; and some fill the victim with a deathly terror of water.
- **Plagues** - sicknesses called down upon a victim, ranging from no more than a sour and griping stomach to true and deadly pestilence.
- **The crumbling to dust** - a working that turns stone and all dead matter to powder, undoing walls and worked things alike.
- **The withering** - the slow shrivelling and severing of a living limb, drawn out across many days until the member dies and falls away.
- **The grinding-down** - the wearing away of a victim's mind and body together, sapping the wit, the will and the strength until little of the person remains.

## Mage-storms

No magician of Ereb, dark or otherwise, may forget the mage-storms - the magistormar. These are periodic discharges of magical power that gather and sweep across the whole of Ereb in the guise of violent thunderstorms. Within such a storm no spell of any kind may be cast, and the very enchantments bound into magic items are at risk of being stripped away, leaving them dead and ordinary forever after.

For the dark mage, drawing as he does upon the moon, and for every other worker of magic besides, the mage-storms are a peril and a reckoning that no art can master. When the sky turns to one of these storms, even Vicotnik's judges fall silent, and the dark magic - like all magic in Ereb - is for a time undone.
