# Alchemy

Alchemy stands apart from every other art of magic in Ereb, for the alchemist works no spells and spends none of his own psyche. Instead he draws the hidden powers out of things themselves and brews them into elixirs that anyone may drink. It is the one magical craft open to all: it asks no inborn gift and no academy's blessing, only patience, a well-stocked laboratory, and a strong stomach for failure.

## Overview

- **Nature:** the brewing of elixirs from the powers inherent in materials, not the casting of spells
- **Open to:** anyone, with no test of the gift required
- **Draws upon:** the virtues of its ingredients rather than the alchemist's own psyche
- **Needs:** a stone-built laboratory, rare ingredients, and the right hour
- **Risk:** a botched brew can sicken, poison or kill the one who makes or drinks it

## The Four Ingredients

Every elixir is built from four kinds of matter. A base, almost always blood, gives it body. An effector lends the magic its effect, so that an eagle's feather grants flight or a unicorn's horn heals. A strength component governs how the elixir is triggered and how long it holds. And a catalyst, the rarest part, unlocks the power; the stronger the catalyst, the mightier the brew, ranging from a silver-elf's tears to the shell of a dragon's egg.

## The Alchemist's Work

The known elixirs run from flight, healing and invisibility to quickness, heroism, the sharpening of a blade, command over the undead, sight in the dark and the kindling of love. The hour of the working matters as much as the recipe: a full moon or an eclipse strengthens it, while a new moon or a conflux weakens it. The finest ingredients, a dragon's eye or a vampire's blood, can be bought at no market and must be won on dangerous expeditions, which is often adventure enough in itself.
