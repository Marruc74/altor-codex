# The Underworld Guilds

Every great city of Ereb has its underworld, and the underworld has its guilds - organizations as orderly, in their own way, as any merchant house, with their masters and their tithes and their apprentices. Three are found in almost every large town; where they are not, some private syndicate or murder-cult has taken their place.

## The Thieves' Guild

Found in the larger cities and trade-towns, the thieves' guild gathers the thieves, bullies, extortionists, smugglers and confidence-men of a place under one roof. A hidden master - known only as the old man, or the boss - rules it through a treasurer and a ring of area sub-bosses, and takes a cut of every theft, anything from a tenth to a third. To steal in the guild's city without paying that cut is to earn first a warning, then a beating and a brand, and at the third offence the hire of an assassin.

## The Assassins' Guild

Small but mighty, and found only in the greatest cities, the assassins' guild deals in murder, spying and sabotage and in nothing else. Its ladder climbs from apprentices and lookouts up through cut-throats and full assassins to the teachers and masters, and at its summit sits the Alderman, a paranoid figure ringed by fanatically loyal guards. It trains spies and poisoners as well as killers, and thinks nothing of feeding false information to those it does not trust - even, at times, to its own.

## The Beggars' Guild

The loosest of the three is the beggars' guild: the lame, the maimed, the crippled and the small-time thief, ruled by a cunning Beggar-master who deals in rumor and knows everything that passes in the streets. Its members surrender their goods and a tenth of their alms to the guild, and a beggar who works the city outside it is not long for the world.

## The Murder-Cults

Apart from the guilds stand the sects - the murder-cults that worship some forbidden and bloodthirsty god, ruled by a prophet who speaks the god's will and keeps a black list of those marked to die. The best known of them is **RhobdoRana**, the brotherhood of the striped frog. Greater than any guild, meanwhile, is the southern crime-family **Kharynos**, which blends honest trade with every illegal art, and among the duck-folk the dreaded **Blood-spattered Feather** keeps its own grim trade.
