# Craft Guilds

In the jori lands of Ereb the crafts are not left to free enterprise. Every trade has its guild, and the guild rules it closely: it guards its members' interests, tests their skill, and sets the standards of quality, the limits of competition, the levels of production and the prices that may be asked. A craftsman pays his guild a tenth of what he earns, and in return the guild is the thing that makes his trade possible at all. Each city keeps its own guilds, answerable to no other city's, but the guilds of one trade all recognise one another across the jori world and honour each other's ranks, so that a craftsman known in one city is known in them all.

## Overview

- **Where:** the jori lands of Ereb, in the towns and cities
- **What:** one guild per trade, regulating quality, prices, competition and production
- **Dues:** a tenth of a member's income
- **Ranks:** apprentice, journeyman, master
- **Reach:** city guilds are independent, but recognise one another's ranks across the jori world

## The Three Ranks

A craftsman rises through three ranks. The **apprentice** is a master's pupil, learning the grounding of the trade until skill enough and a passed test make a journeyman of him. The **journeyman** is a craftsman in his own right, free to hire himself to a master for wages but not yet to set up on his own; most serve under many masters over the years, in many cities, to learn all they can. The **master** has passed the master's test and may keep his own workshop in a city, taking on apprentices and journeymen of his own.

## The Master's Test

To become a master a craftsman must be a citizen of the realm, born in the city or settled there at least five years, and skilled enough to be reckoned among the best of his trade. He then asks his guild's leave to attempt the master's test, a single work or a few of the very highest quality that can take the better part of a year to make, with no income the while, which is why many journeymen never trouble to attempt it. A new master must also stand the whole guild a feast of ale and wine at his investiture, no small expense, before he may rent a hall and open his workshop.

## The Wandering Journeyman

It is the journeyman, not the master, who takes to the road, and the road suits him. A master is settled and burdened with the care of his workshop and his people, but a journeyman wanders from master to master and city to city in search of knowledge, and no one thinks the worse of him for moving on. The wandering is easiest in the south and west of Ereb, where most realms speak close dialects of the one jori tongue, so a journeyman can find work in a strange city readily enough, stay a few months, and drift on with no eyebrow raised. Some hold the rank in more than one craft at once, though that is uncommon.
