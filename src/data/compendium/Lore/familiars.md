# Familiars

A familiar is a spirit taken into a mage's service and bound into the body of some dumb beast, a companion and a tool in one. Almost any magician can call one, but the bond is not lightly made, for a part of the mage's own strength must be given up for good to forge it.

## The Spiritus Familiaris

By a quiet ceremony, burning certain herbs in a vessel of silver, a mage summons a spirit and seats it in the body of an unthinking animal, most often a falcon, a cat, a crow, a rat, an owl or a snake. The bound spirit keeps the beast's shape and skills but gains a wit of its own, though it can never itself learn magic. Master and familiar share their thoughts whenever their eyes meet, and the creature can manage a halting echo of its master's speech. A familiar's temper comes in time to mirror the one who called it, so that a gentle mage keeps a gentle beast and a cruel one a cruel beast; and the price of the bond, a sliver of the mage's own psyche, is paid once and never won back.

## The Witch's Bjära

Witches keep no animal familiar but make instead a bjära, a small servant shaped from yarn and herbs and quickened under a full moon at the cost of two measures of the witch's strength. The rite is said never to fail, for the gods themselves are held to lend a hand to it. A bjära has no span of years; it serves until the witch dies, and then falls lifeless apart. Witches use these little creatures to carry spells from hand to hand, to steal, and to bear word secretly between one witch and another.
