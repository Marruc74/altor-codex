# Dwarven Architecture

The dwarves hold themselves the finest builders in all Ereb Altor, and whether or not the claim is just, their work is unmistakable. As the elves favour the circle, the dome and the triangle, the dwarves keep to a settled body of form that runs through everything they raise, and three principles above all give a dwarven building its character: the robust, the octagon and the pentagon.

## The Robust

A dwarf builds to last. He prizes quality and stability over grace, sets stout walls of close-fitted blocks, and has no patience for upstart towers or graceful fairy-castles reaching for the sky. Such things he will raise only on commission, the famous example being King Ludowi's improbable "cream-cake of a castle" in Bavari. A free-standing dwarven house is anchored deep and firmly into the ground.

## The Octagon

The octagon is the eight-cornered room, a rectangle with its corners cut away, shaped like a cut emerald seen from above. It is the plainest mark of dwarven work and the commonest form in the underground cities hewn from the living rock. A simple square or rectangle the dwarves despise, and will sniff at.

## The Pentagon

At the harmonious centre of a building, though not always on its middle floor, sits the pentagon, a five-cornered room shaped like a cut diamond seen from the side. The diamond is the highest of spiritual forms to a dwarf, and so it is here that secrets are most often kept. On the floors above and below the true pentagon stand false ones, their points cut away to make hexagons of varying size.

## The Embassy at Entika

The clearest showcase of the art outside the mountains is the dwarven embassy at Entika in Berendien, raised to better watch over dwarven interests abroad as their trade with the kingdom grew. Its walls are two metres thick and it has no windows; as the price of building a fortress so near the city, the dwarves agreed to raise it with no gates at all, and its great ceremony hall stands open to the weather through an octagonal opening in the roof. It is a half-scale copy of the middle floor of the Great Palace below the mountains, and among its treasures are a parade suit of mithril and the Mountain-Spirit, a flawless diamond set in gold that sounds faint harmonies near other flawless gems.
