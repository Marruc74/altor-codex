# Hersch

![Fields of hersch on the slopes of Beyural](/compendium/Lore/Beyural/hersch.jpg "Fields of hersch on the slopes of Beyural")

## Of Its Nature

The hersch is a grain unique to Beyural, growing tall in dense golden fields across the island's fertile slopes. It is related to wheat, yet hardier, shaped by the heavy volcanic soil from which it rises.

## Of Its Form

Its stalks are thick and resilient, bearing heavy heads of grain that bend low when ripe. The kernels are larger than common wheat, pale and warm in tone, with a firm texture.

## Of Its Use

Hersch is the foundation of Beyural's harvest. It is ground into flour for bread and porridge, and it feeds both townsfolk and travellers. Its yield is steady, and its growth reliable even in difficult seasons.

## On Its Cultivation

The grain thrives in the island's dark soil, but it demands labour to bring forth. Fields must be turned deep, and the harvest comes only after long, careful tending. The work is slow, and often done alongside the hornox.

## Of Quiet Beliefs

Some say hersch draws strength from the land's buried fire, that its roots reach deeper than other crops. It is said that fields left untended too long grow uneven, as if the ground itself shifts beneath them.

## In the End

The hersch feeds Beyural, and in its quiet way it endures as the land does.

*Expanded from the brief official lore of Beyural in Ereb Altor.*
