# Lullad

![Lullad trees on the dry slopes of Beyural](/compendium/Lore/Beyural/lullad.jpg "Lullad trees on the dry slopes of Beyural")

## Of Its Nature

The lullad is a small stonefruit grown across the dry slopes of Beyural. Its trees are low and gnarled, shaped by wind and poor soil, yet they bear fruit in steady abundance.

## Of Its Form

The fruit is round and firm, with a skin that shifts from muted green to darkened purple as it ripens. Beneath the surface lies dense flesh around a hard pit, heavier than its size would suggest.

## Of Its Use

Lullad is seldom eaten fresh. Instead it is pressed for its oil, which is used in cooking, trade and preservation. The oil is thick and pale, valued for its stability and long keeping.

## On Its Cultivation

The trees need little water and grow where other crops would fail. They are planted wide apart and endure long seasons of heat. The harvest is done by hand, the fruit gathered into baskets and pressed soon after.

## Of Quiet Beliefs

Some say the lullad tree takes its strength slowly, drawing from stone as much as soil. Old growers claim the oldest trees yield the richest oil, though no one has ever measured it.

## In the End

The lullad draws no attention to itself. Yet much of Beyural rests upon it.
