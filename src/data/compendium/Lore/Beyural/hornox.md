# Hornox

![A hornox on a field in Beyural](/compendium/Lore/Beyural/hornox.jpg "A hornox on a field in Beyural")

## Of Its Nature

The hornox is a great beast of Beyural, broad and heavy, built for endurance rather than speed. Its thick hide bears the marks of labour, and its single forward horn is worn from use. It resembles cattle, yet carries itself with a quiet weight that sets it apart.

## On Its Temperament

It is neither aggressive nor truly docile. The hornox works, but not out of obedience alone. It learns the people who guide it and settles into a steady rhythm beside them. When mistreated it does not lash out. It simply refuses to move.

## Of Its Use

In Beyural's dark volcanic soil the hornox is essential. It pulls plows through heavy ground, hauls stone, and labours where other animals fail. Fields are shaped by its slow, deliberate strength.

## On Its Bond with Man

No one fully owns a hornox. Trust is earned over time, and even then it is never complete. There are moments when it resists for no reason anyone can name, and the wiser farmer does not press the matter.

## Of Quiet Beliefs

It is said the hornox senses what lies beneath the earth. Some claim it feels tremors before they come, or shies from ground once touched by old fire. These things are not proven, only accepted.

## In the End

The hornox does not hurry, nor does it falter. It endures, and in Beyural that is enough.

*Expanded from the brief official lore of Beyural in Ereb Altor.*
