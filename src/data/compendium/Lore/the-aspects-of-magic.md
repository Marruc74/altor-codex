# The Aspects of Magic

Every working of magic leaves an indelible mark upon the soul of the one who works it, and by these marks the scholars of Ereb divide the many arts into four great aspects. The three great schools, animism, elemental magic and mentalism, remain the chief and most widely practised of all magic; but each belongs to one of these deeper aspects, and around them the lesser arts are ranged.

## The Four Aspects

- **Order** — elemental magic, harmonism and voice magic, the arts of pattern and law.
- **Chaos** — spiritism and demonology, the arts that reach beyond the ordered world.
- **Life** — animism and necromancy, the arts of the quick and the dead, with witchcraft standing alongside.
- **Consciousness** — mentalism, illusionism and symbolism, the arts of the mind, all grown from the old dragon magic.

## Opposites and Barriers

Order stands against Chaos, and Life against Consciousness, and a mage steeped in one aspect can scarcely cross over to its opposite; the road from Order to Chaos is closed outright, and the others are crossed only at great cost. Two arts stand apart from the scheme altogether: alchemy, which is a method rather than an aspect, and staff magic, a medium that can reach toward them all.

## Beyond the World

The aspects are bound up with the shape of the world itself. An endless number of parallel worlds are joined together by the spirit plane, and every school teaches a ritual for crossing between them. Eight elemental sources are known to feed the art of the elementalist, while the Realm of Death and Chaos itself are held to lie outside the multiverse altogether.
