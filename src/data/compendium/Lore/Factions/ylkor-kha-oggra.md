# Ylkor kha oggra

Ylkor kha oggra, the Mad Dogs, is a clan of svartalf wolf-riders that ranges the highland of the Mörkerbergen in the wild north, near the Aidne Mountains. The elves call them the Skeleton Riders, in plain translation, and few clans of the dark folk have made so feared a name. Out of the mist they come on their great wolves, their banners hung with bones and skulls and knotted scraps of cloth, their faces painted in stark white — the death-colour of the svartfolk — and the bones of fallen enemies lashed wherever they will sit on their gear. The sight alone has broken armies. Though the Burned Earth Clan of the northern Aidne counts the wolf-riders among its own, Ylkor kha oggra is a great power in its own right.

## History

The clan was sworn together in the year 544 before Odo, when six feared svartalf tribes that wandered the Mörkerbergen highland made common cause against the growing traffic of men in their country. The first human force sent to make an example of them was cut to pieces in a string of ambushes; the army raised afterward from the levies of a dozen holds and towns was shattered in a single furious charge of mounted wolves. After that, men wove legends about the unbeatable riders of the highland and learned to leave the country alone. In 255 eO the clan made its pact with the Burned Earth Clan, and the two swore to make no war on one another and to stand as brothers. When they gather, spontaneous campaigns tend to follow, and the elves keep a close watch on them both.

## The Six Tribes

The clan is still built of its six founding tribes, who keep to their own separate ranges and gather as one only seldom. They are **Yrnokh**, the Hungry; **Dogh**, the Berserk; **Lunorgh Dogh**, the Moon-mad; **Karhh**, the Renders; **Ugnobb**, the Avengers; and **Elf Kharhh**, the Elf-butchers. All their settled camps lie on the south side of the highland, with a few temporary ones around the mountains; the heart of the range itself they shun, with what looks like real dread.

## The Four-Legged Brothers

What sets the clan apart is its wolves. Svartalfs often keep wolves, but no one keeps them in the numbers Ylkor kha oggra does: nearly every warrior rides one, which puts the clan's full muster at something like a thousand to thirteen hundred mounted fighters. Such a host has to feed its mounts, and the hunting carries the packs far from home. The wolves often range off on their own, hunting better alone than alongside their riders, and serve as scouts and trackers that make it almost impossible to enter the clan's land unseen. A warrior dresses his spear with wolf-tails below the head and sets his small parrying shield with wolf-teeth, and the finest rider of them all is Utugg the Mad, a raider no wolf has ever thrown.

## Faith and the Bone Men

The clan worships Baawk, the wolf-god, and the moon from which he watches over them, and its devotion is fanatical. Every third year, at the first full moon of autumn, the whole horde gathers at Baawk-Thungh, a vast black monolith, for three nights of sacrifice and riot in the god's honour, and that rite holds the clan together more than anything else. The clan has no chieftains. Each tribe is led instead by its Bone Men, and the leading Bone Men rule the whole clan through a council, which governs — by the measure of the dark folk — remarkably well, walking a careful line between robbing the borderlands of the neighbouring realms and provoking the open war it would rather avoid. Their war-cry is "Eff grobb nichh yngrykk": the last to arrive gets no ears to eat.

## Friends and Foes

Men go to war against the clan only reluctantly, overawed by the legend of those early massacres. The elves are its oldest enemies, forever waging small wars against the clan and its single tribes, for the wolves raid as far as the elven forests and the svartalfs make a tradition of fouling the elves' holy places; the elves answer by sending their best warriors to punish them. Most feared of all are the satyrs, who strike deep into the clan's land and hurt it badly — so that to kill a satyr earns a svartalf the highest standing the clan can give. A skull fixed to the helm is held to bring luck in battle, and so nearly every helm wears one.
