# The Black Rose Brotherhood

A fellowship of witches on the fog-bound isle of Tannatopol, off the jungle port of Krilloan, who keep the worship of the demon-prince Hemaquiel. To the Shining Way he is the Tempter, an old enemy of the light. The Brotherhood keep him for what he was before that name, a lord of fertility, wisdom and joy, and they serve him not with blood and butchery but with patience and quiet magic meant to wear faith down from the inside.

## Hemaquiel's Keepers

Hemaquiel is horned and goat-footed, a prince of the Oktagon who prizes wit and delight and rarely strikes at anything directly, and his Brotherhood works the same way. They almost never raise a hand against the Shining Way in the open. Instead they spread doubt, buy silence, and let a well-placed rumour or a season of bad luck do what a knife cannot. They are not the only ones who keep his name. Other covens hold to him elsewhere, such as the Cnoth in the Nida mountains, each working alone and often unaware the others exist, but the Brotherhood of Tannatopol is the one whose shadow falls across Krilloan.

## The Black Book and the Black Rose

Their lore is gathered in the Black Book, a grimoire bound in black leather over a silver pentagram, a single pressed black rose laid between its boards, written in a hand no outsider can read. The rose is more than a badge. The Brotherhood grow a black rose of their own making, sweet and hallucinogenic, and burn its petals in their censers; the smoke opens the mind and lends strength to any rite worked in it. Their sabbaths are held in secret, an initiate led to them blindfolded, and their sign is pressed into the wax of every letter they send.

## The Highest

At the top sit the Highest, the elders who hold the deepest secrets and the sternest discipline. A brother or sister who fails the Brotherhood may be called to answer to them, and would sooner bare their throat to a friend than face it. Two of their number cross the trio's road. Mildred Yeovil, Krilloan's foremost herb-mistress, is pale and lovely and does not age, for she holds a pact with Hemaquiel himself, and behind the healer stands one of the coven's leaders. Her finest instrument is Hilja Adriagoles, a paralysed medium who cannot rise from her bed but can send her soul out of it, riding unseen in another body to watch and listen for weeks at a stretch.

## Enemy, Then Ally

The Brotherhood first meets the trio as a foe. When a silver chalice holy to the death-goddess Imária falls into their hands, the coven wants it, and tries in turn to buy it, steal it, and trick it away. What breaks the standoff is treachery from outside: the vampire Lerajie, of Imária's own cult, turns on everyone, and the Brotherhood, wronged and endangered, sues for peace and sets the trio loose on him instead. They keep their word at the close, clearing the trio's name and deeding them a house. Later, when an archangel's wrath threatens to scour Krilloan, it is the Brotherhood that finds the counter in its Black Book and raises the abyss-being Teitan to meet the angel in the sky, a devil called up to save a city.
