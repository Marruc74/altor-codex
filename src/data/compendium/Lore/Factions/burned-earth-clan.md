# Burned Earth Clan

![Likhh Drogh](/compendium/Lore/Factions/burned-earth-clan-likhh-drogh.jpg "Likhh Drogh")

The Burned Earth Clan, in the black tongue Ignarth Bogglf, is a great orc power of the northern Aidne Mountains, some two thousand fighting warriors strong. It takes its name from the way it makes war: it burns everything it cannot carry off, leaving scorched fields and dead villages behind it. The clan is mostly kraukh-orcs and mountain-orcs, with a tribe of svartalfs and a small band of svartnissar trailing along.

## Tribes

The clan is built of several tribes, each with its own chieftain under the clan as a whole. Its head tribe is **Lunorgh Kah**, the Mad Moon. **Rulgh Borgnag**, the Bloody Claw, and **Urgh Grobb**, the Swine-eaters, are its other great kraukh-orc tribes, with **Gylk Lobbnack**, the Howling Skulls, beside them; the mountain-orcs field **Grogol Gribb**, the Dwarf-stranglers, and the svartalfs **Ylkor kha oggra**, the Mad Dogs. A swarm of svartnissar, **Kallakadak Yldrokk**, attaches itself to the Bloody Claw, and a small group called **Grokashak Oggra**, the Sick Eye, mixes kraukh- and ylk-orcs with a lone rese. The band of resar known as **Dekkadorel Gnubbt** keeps company with the clan as hired muscle without truly belonging to it.

## History

The clan was formed in 110 eO, when it carved up the northern Aidne Mountains for itself. In 255 eO it made a pact with the Moon-wolves clan. Its great rival has long been the Black Tower: in 479 eO a horde from the Tower tried to draw the clan into its service and failed, and a few years later, in 484–486 eO, the clan laid siege to the Black Tower itself. The clan's second age of strength is reckoned from a famous blood-duel won by the warrior Gunhk Orrgh, who killed his last opponent by biting through the hamstrings; the fight left thirty-six dead and a hundred and fifty-one hurt.

## Relics

The clan's pride is the **Death-bringer** (Ilkhh Drogh), a huge three-storey battering-ram built by orc smith-masters and captured dwarves, hauled into battle by hundreds of orcs. It also keeps **Brogh**, "the Roar," a war-drum so large it takes sixteen orcs to work and can be heard for miles, and **Droll's Skull**, the cranium of a giant rese, carried as a holy thing to lift the warriors' battle-morale.
