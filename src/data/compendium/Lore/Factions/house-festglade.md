# House Festglade

House Festglade is one of the five great merchant houses that rule Erebos from its capital, Nohstril, and it claims, against the doubt of the other four, to be the oldest trading house of the islands. Whatever the truth of that, it has always sat near the centre of Erebos's trade and politics, helped by a line of heads who were one and all charismatic, headstrong eccentrics. The house works by reaching its ends with the least friction it can manage, forever the one to offer the accommodating proposal and broker the compromise, and that habit has bought Erebos an unusual stability and the house a broad respect.

## Overview

- **What it is:** One of the five merchant houses of the Council of Five Houses that rule Erebos
- **Seat:** Nohstril, the Erebos capital
- **Claim:** to be the first true trading house of the islands
- **Its heads:** charismatic eccentrics who sleep little and work all hours
- **Its dark habit:** a long and dangerous dabbling in demons
- **Its present head:** Serafim Festglade, a secret and inept demonologist

## The House and Its Heads

Every head of the house has been a forceful, eccentric man with the will to drive his ideas through, and the young heirs are schooled early from the many-volumed family book, in which each past head set down his counsel. The heads share two odd traits: they need very little sleep, working at every hour and turning up in person at their warehouses and docks by night, and they throw legendary feasts, with fine food and drink, song, music and jesters, and guests drawn from every rung of the city. The house keeps clear of the squabbles that do not touch its interests, and seeks neither glory nor a crown, only advantage.

## The House and Its Demons

For all its respectability, House Festglade has a long and ugly history with demons. A century ago it hired the war-demon Echram Schroedel to butcher a rival's cyclops guards, then cheated, killed and dismembered him and chained his soul beneath the city - a betrayal that still waits, buried, to be answered. The house's present head, Serafim, carries the habit on. A brilliant trader and political mind but hopeless at any practical task, he was forced to take up his father's seat too young and broke off his training under the demonologer-master Qaterman, returning to Nohstril half-taught. Determined to finish his schooling anyway, he built a demonological laboratory into the top floor of the residence and works there alone, and his carelessness has more than once let demons slip loose - which is why Nohstril is troubled, again and again, by demons no one can account for. Worse, he means to call up an ancient demon, Nimum, a working almost certain to fail and to kill him, and his death would shatter the balance of the Five Houses, for the house has no ready heir.

## Those Who Have Come for Him

Two strangers have followed the rumours to Nohstril. The first is Morarin, a binder: a mage who walks the second road of demonology, the one that despises demons and drives them out, and who looks barely thirty though he is more than a hundred and twenty, his life and his ageing both frozen by his magic. He is known by the claw-scar down his left cheek and his white dress, and he means, if he can, to talk the hidden demonologer to his senses before he turns to harsher methods. The second is Tonopaz Demonbane, a famed and now cynical hero, sick with suspicion and sure the gods chose him to scour the world of evil, who carries the rune-graven greatsword Drakeld and has killed a mighty demon, many small ones, and more than one innocent he wrongly took for possessed.
