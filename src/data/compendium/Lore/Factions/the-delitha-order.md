A small and little-regarded order of healers, founded long ago by a woman who was called in a vision to care for the weak and the sick of every race. They serve Delitha, a half-sister of the goddess Ninnave whom many will not grant the name of goddess at all, and they keep her worship alive in a handful of quiet temples where the door stands open to anyone who comes in need.

## The Healing Faith

The order takes in both women and men, and teaches its magic alongside its healing, so that a priestess is as much a worker of animism as a nurse. Its great relics are the horns of unicorns, freely given, through which a trained hand can pour healing strength into another body. The most famous of these temples is the Temple of the Life-giving Spring, where the order's aged head, called the Sanctified, still longs to see the lost unicorn relics carried home.

For all their gentleness they are not without teeth. The higher grades are taught to see through the disguises of the dark priests of Zeeris, the Left-Hand Path, who are the healers' oldest enemies and who in these days far outnumber them. It was black priests of Zeeris who burned one of the order's temples long ago while hunting its relics, and the feud between the two has never cooled.

## The Grades

Every priestess wears a white robe, and the colour of the band at its edge marks her grade. The order takes men as well as women, but only a woman may rise past the rank of Initiate.

- **Novice** - the entry grade, marked by a yellow band, schooled in the first arts of animism and healing.
- **Initiate** - marked by a blue band. This is the highest a man of the order may reach.
- **Life-giver** - marked by a green band, able to channel healing into others through the unicorn horn.
- **Priestess** - the full priestess, marked by a red band, who learns the art of revealing the hidden dark priests of Zeeris.
- **Sanctified** - the order's highest grade, marked by a purple band. Only one holds it at a time, the elder who leads the order and guards its deepest charge.
