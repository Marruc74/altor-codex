The black priesthood of Zeeris, the Left-Hand Path, named for a half-brother of Delitha. They are the oldest enemies of the Delitha healers, and in these days their followers far outnumber them. Where the healers give life, the servants of Zeeris deal in necromancy and darker rites, and they work as often through poison, fear and a quiet knife as through open force.

## The Left-Hand Path

The order keeps to the shadows, running its cells behind honest-seeming trades. An alchemist's shop in a corner house, a temple-school in a river town, a laboratory over a hidden crypt: each is a face the black temple wears. Its priests raise the dead as guards and servants, hold prisoners for ransom and for sacrifice, and answer to masters the outside world never sees. It was they who destroyed a Delitha temple long ago in their hunt for the unicorn relics, and they have never given up the chase.

## The Grades

Each grade adds a mark to the plain black cloak of the priesthood. The order takes women as well as men, but only a man may rise past the rank of Shadow Priest.

- **Novice** - the first rank, in a plain black cloak with no other mark.
- **Initiate** - who adds a silver ring through the left nostril.
- **Shadow Priest** - who adds a brow-band of black steel. This is the highest a woman of the order may reach.
- **Dark Priest** - who shaves a part through the hair.
- **High Priest** - whose shaved part is painted blood-red, and who keeps a familiar at his side.
- **The Perfected** - the sixth and highest rank, marked by a blood-drop tattooed on the left palm and a thick gold ring about the neck.

## The Left Hand

Beside the priesthood rides its martial arm, the black knights called the Left Hand. They are landless men with shaved heads and white shields, who wield enchanted broadswords that bite doubly deep into elf-folk. Each is served by squires who bear the same gear but are forbidden to shave their heads until the day they are knighted.
