# Galvorn

Galvorn is a rare and precious metal, a refinement of the already mighty dwarf-steel, black as jet but bright rather than dull, so that it shines like polished coal and is now and then set in jewellery for its lustre. It is as hard as dwarf-steel, yet far more pliant under the smith's hammer, so that the rings of a mail-coat may be drawn finer and lighter and still turn aside every point and arrow. It is very hard to come by.

## The First Armour

The first armour of galvorn was forged by an elf, and forged in one of the dwarves' own strongest holds, in the days before the two peoples became strangers to one another. Smiths of later ages take it for a sign that the old enmity of elves and dwarves was no natural thing, born when the dwarves first won their steel, but a wound worked later by evil, the same hand that set elves and men at odds.

## A Metal for Mages

Like meteor-iron, galvorn does not hinder magic as common iron and steel do, for both metals have been used by elves and magicians since the elder days. A mage or an elf may wear a coat of galvorn and still work spells freely, or stand near another's working without smothering it, which makes the rare metal prized far beyond even its worth as armour.
