# Heroes

A hero is one who has raised himself above the common run by his deeds, and won a name for it. The heroes of Ereb are brave, strong and principled, and they do not hesitate; they spend their lives on the road, called to feasts and dragged into tournaments to keep the renown they have earned. But behind the songs lies a deeper truth, for most heroes are not their own masters at all: they are pieces in the endless game the gods play across the face of the world.

## The Gods' Instruments

Bound from acting directly upon Altor, the gods reach into it through chosen mortals, and a hero is one of the surest of their hands. Such fate-burdened heroes come in three kinds. The **chance heroes** were snatched up on the spur of the moment, the first tool that came to a god's hand, unreliable, and the weakest of the three. The **true heroes** were marked at their very first breath, bound close to their god yet keeping some will of their own. And the **god-born** are the sons and daughters of gods themselves, the mightiest, and wholly their parent's creatures. Apart from these stand the heroes driven by their own ambition alone: less potent than the chosen, but freer than any of them.

## Oaths and Fates

An oath sworn at a charged moment, over the body of a dying friend, or in the hour of greatest need, may be overheard by a higher power and taken up. When that happens the oath becomes a fate, and it drives the hero as surely as any doom the gods could lay on him, until the very moment it is fulfilled, at which it is broken. The oath-bound hero is apt to grow single-minded, even manic, in its pursuit. Only the pull of true love is reckoned strong enough to excuse the breaking of such a vow.

## Curses

What one god grants, another may blight. The gods curse the heroes of rival powers, and a curse is most often bound to an object the hero carries, so that it lifts only when the thing is willingly cast away, sold, or stolen. The curses are of many kinds: a hero forever misunderstood, one whose wounds will not heal, one who reeks or freezes or draws biting insects, one whose every foe somehow finds his weak point.

## Dark Heroes

Not all who rise above the common run serve the light. The dark heroes are as skilled as any champion and far more feared, and they keep company with demons and the dark folk. Dread goes before them, so that their fame counts for more than an honest hero's; and the worst of them, the dark-princes, rule by terror alone. Some seal a pact with the powers of darkness and are promised that, slain, they will rise again as a death-gast to hound whoever struck them down. Most carry a hidden flaw for all their power, a blood-lust or a vanity or a greed, by which a clever enemy may yet undo them.
