# Magical Symbols

Every magician knows that magic is not only waving hands and muttered chants. There is a graphic side to the art - a body of signs, marks and ideograms that can be painted, carved, hewn into stone, or scratched in haste into the sand. Only a few true symbolists ever master the writing of them in earnest, but a great many of the signs are common knowledge among magicians of every school, drawn under concentration and woken with the right word of power. Most look plain enough; not just anyone can make them work.

## Signs of Power and Protection

The best known of all is the classic pentagram, the five-pointed star of high magic, used to summon powerful demons and to break the wall between worlds - but also, in humbler hands, daubed on a hermit's door against trolls and oknytt, or kept as a quiet recognition mark among occult circles. A perfect one, the old books claim, can only be drawn by marking the wandering star Durval's place on a star-chart five years running and joining the points, which is why pentagrams and that star are forever bound together in an astrologer's mind. Turned upside down, the same star becomes the sign of Prince Shaitan, Etin's foremost enemy, and on a level floor it calls up the servants of darkness.

Other signs ward and exalt. A many-cornered figure of two crossed squares, said to come from Akrogal, is cut on the gates of strong castles to guard them; the octogram of creation protects a house against tomtar and lesser spirits, and is the alpha and omega of those rash enough to try making beings of their own. Kossel's staff, named for a legendary arch-magician, stands for raised-up power, and a warlord will keep it painted bright upon his throne.

## Signs of Time, Fate and Flow

Some signs reach after the great forces. There is a sign for Time itself - which Gadirm Dragon-master wished to count as the eighth element - said to let a master bend time's flow within a narrow space. There is the infinity cross, marking the endless and eternal, which sets a tremor in the world-flow because it defies the law of transience that holds nothing can last forever, and so is used with care. And there is the grim sign sometimes called Ptelemos's lament, after the Krun philosopher who shook his fist at fate; drawn with two crossbars it stands for death and the reaper, and is chalked white on the doors of the plague-struck. A sign of flows marks how power should be channelled in a conjuration - the magician Rimbald is said to have carved it on river-ice to melt it under his pursuers as he fled with a stolen treasure.

## Signs of Life and Death

A seed-of-life sign, said to have come to Ereb from far Jih-Pun across the Western Sea, is used to help a barren woman to a child or to hasten the growth of crops and trees; its companion, a spiral, is carved on a cradle to grant the child a great life, and is whispered to appear as a birthmark on those who one day overturn their world. Against these stand the necromancer's sign of putrefactio, which speeds the rot of all things, and the spirit-tamer's sign, worn against ghosts and wraiths - the wandering exorcist Perokytalar keeps it stitched on his midnight cloak. A sign of healing speeds the mending of wounds and, it is said, can even cure deadly sickness or restore a severed limb; another, of life, rebirth and the journey of the soul, a magician may have cut on his own grave to win a kinder afterlife.

## The Unsolved Signs

A few signs keep their secrets. One ideogram is held to be one of the thousand eyes of the Seer, a demigod of Golwyndian myth, by which a mighty magician can stand far off and watch a place as though his back were to its wall. And on the road between Smyrrha and Arkhaz stands the philosopher's stone, graven with a sign that no living magician or scholar can read - which does not stop them coming from far and wide to puzzle at it, hoping for a share of the old ones' power.

## The Thirty Signs

The full reckoning of the common signs, as a magician learns them:

1. **Spiritus clarus** - the pure and spiritual; a marker between magicians for holy groves, healing springs and stores, and the recognition sign of the kafriler monks of Mereld.
2. **Keep and preserve** - a barbarian rune from Nargur's forests, carved on jars and jugs to keep their contents long fresh.
3. **Quinta essentia** - the ether, the lost fifth element; used by those who study the make of the universe and reach toward other worlds, as a faction in Arkhaz does.
4. **The classic pentagram** - high magic and protection alike, from summoning demons to warding a door against trolls; bound to the wandering star Durval.
5. **The reversed pentagram** - the sign of Prince Shaitan; drawn on a level surface it breaks the wall between worlds and calls up the servants of darkness.
6. **The infinity cross** - the endless and eternal, which defies the law of transience and is used only with great care.
7. **The polygon** - two crossed squares for fortification, cut on castle gates against enemies; said to come from Akrogal.
8. **Creation's octogram** - creation, alpha and omega, for those who would make beings of their own; also a ward against tomtar and oknytt.
9. **The sign of Time** - the would-be eighth element of Gadirm Dragon-master, said to bend time's flow within a narrow space.
10. **Ptelemos's lament** - inexorable fate, death and the reaper; cut for curses and chalked white on the doors of the plague-struck.
11. **Expansion** - authority, governance and faith; drawn in law-books and guild statutes for luck, and favoured by imperial factions in Trakorien.
12. **Putrefactio** - the rot of all things, used by necromancers to hasten decay; the undoing of the keep-and-preserve sign.
13. **The crescent moon** - insight, the subconscious and the heart; meditated upon for balance and fresh ideas.
14. **The thunder sign** - invokes the thunder god and his lightning.
15. **The seed of life** - fertility, come to Ereb from far Jih-Pun; aids a barren woman to a child and hastens crops and trees.
16. **The spiral of potential power** - the life-seed turning in the spiral; carved on a cradle for a great life, and seen as the birthmark of those who overturn their world.
17. **The all-round occult sign** - death, rebirth and the hidden depths, said to watch over those who work underground, whether miners or thieves.
18. **Matter over spirit** - chaos and disharmony, linked to the chaotic moon Raukhra and the third conflux.
19. **Spirit over matter** - the opposite of the last, where spirit rules matter and harmony arises.
20. **Perz** - secrets and mysteries; it garbles a writing against unwanted eyes and conceals hidden doors.
21. **The sign of flows** - power-flows and their channelling; it can also mean "melt," as the magician Rimbald used it on river-ice.
22. **The anti-magic sign** - a small anti-magic that disperses conjurations in its reach, even the caster's own, weakening with distance.
23. **The spirit-tamer's sign** - a ward against ghosts and the undead, worn by the wandering exorcist Perokytalar.
24. **The Seer's eye** - one of the thousand eyes of the Seer, by which a mighty magician watches a distant place.
25. **Kossel's staff** - elevated power, named for the arch-magician, kept painted on a ruler's throne.
26. **The staff of healing** - hastens healing, and is said to cure deadly sickness and restore a severed limb.
27. **The four season signs** - winter solstice, spring equinox, summer solstice and autumn equinox, found on the calendar rune-staffs kept at Entika.
28. **The alchemical sign** - the alchemical art, painted in the bottoms of glass vessels and retorts.
29. **Life, rebirth and transmigration** - the soul's journey, cut on a grave to win a kinder afterlife.
30. **The philosopher's-stone sign** - an unknown ancient magic graven on the lapis philosophorum, on the road between Smyrrha and Arkhaz.
