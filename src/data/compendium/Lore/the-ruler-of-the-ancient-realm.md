# The Ruler of the Ancient Realm

There is an old legend in Ereb of Meh-Zadria, "the abode of the gods", a kingdom that stood in the Kard mountains of the north-west before history began and has lain in ruins ever since. The legend says the abode of the gods will one day be restored, to herald a god's coming into the world, and that two powers, one good and one dark, each set a chosen champion to win the ruined realm and name it in their god's sign. It is less a tale of two people than of good and evil contending for the same ancient crown, and it has been used as the spine of a long campaign wandering half of Ereb.

## Overview

- **What it is:** an old Ereb legend and prophecy about the ruined divine kingdom of Meh-Zadria and its foretold ruler
- **The realm:** Meh-Zadria, "the abode of the gods", once a kingdom in the Kard mountains of north-western Ereb, now buried ruins
- **Its marker:** the Pillar of Meh-Zadria, three kilometres high, the one thing left standing when the kingdom fell
- **The prophecy:** that the abode of the gods will be restored to prepare for a god's coming into the world
- **The wager:** a good power and a dark one, each backing a champion to be crowned ruler in that god's sign
- **The keys:** two matching sets of crown jewels, scattered across Ereb, and the pillar itself, needed before the reckoning

## The Ancient Realm

Meh-Zadria was a kingdom of the elder gods, their demigods and dragon-guardians, until the mightier younger gods drove them out and cast the whole realm down. Since then it has lain in ruins in the Kard mountains, its treasures buried under fallen stone. The only piece of it still standing is a single pillar three kilometres high, the Pillar of Meh-Zadria, untouched from the day the building that was the kingdom came apart around it. The mountains about it are near empty of people, wild country that has kept the ruin's secrets for longer than any recorded age.

## The Prophecy

It was said that the abode of the gods would one day be raised again, and that its restoration would prepare the world for the coming of a god. Which god that would be, no telling agreed. The dispute settled into a struggle between two, one good and one evil, each meaning the prophecy for itself.

## The Wager of the Gods

The rule between the two gods is simple. Whichever sees its chosen one crowned ruler of Meh-Zadria, in the sign of what that god stands for, wins the realm. Each god may help its own champion, and the help is balanced: whatever gift or aid one grants, the other may answer with its like, so neither champion is ever left hopelessly behind. Whether the gods truly care once a ruler has named himself over the old kingdom, the legend does not say. Perhaps it was only a wager that amused them. Perhaps the land really is being made ready for a god to walk it. Perhaps the winning god means to lead its ruler on into the endless struggle for mastery over every living thing in Altor.

## The Two Champions

Two figures strive for the old kingdom, one carrying the good power and one the dark, though each is certain his own is the true power and the other the false. Neither can simply put an end to the other, for the powers that back them forbid it and press them into a wary alliance: the reckoning is foretold to fall on the pillar, and should one champion die too soon the other loses his claim, and the gods turn their wager to some other pair in some other place. So the two circle each other through the years, winning allies with promises and gifts and quiet bargains, until the time and the conditions are ripe.

## The Crown Jewels

The contest cannot reach its end until each champion has gathered his own set of the crown jewels, two matching sets broken up and hidden across Ereb long ago. Because each needs a full set of his own, the search keeps the rivals bound to the same roads even as they mean to destroy each other. Fragments are rumoured in far and dangerous places: some of the dark set deep in Khab-Hemi, the Cauldron of Bitterness, some of the good set beneath the rainbow. The royal swords that belong with the jewels are caught up in the same long hunt.

## The Reckoning

When the conditions are met the two come at last to the Pillar of Meh-Zadria, and the matter is settled there. The victor takes the mastery of the abode of the gods, and then faces the harder task of making his prize a true kingdom, for the mountains around the pillar are all but empty and must somehow be peopled. A fair ruler might look to the gods to turn crag into fertile land; a dark one might raise svartfolk and the walking dead out of the mountains' ancient graves. What the neighbouring realms do about a new power rising in the north, and what becomes of the beaten champion if he lives, the legend leaves for the telling.
