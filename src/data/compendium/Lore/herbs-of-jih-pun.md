# Herbs of Jih-pun

The folk of Jih-pun have raised the use of herbs to an art unlike any in the rest of Ereb. The title of herb-master is old, named already in the chronicles of Hokera's deeds in the centuries before the empire, and was made a high imperial office a few years after the empire's founding. The herb-masters reckon their plants in three kinds: the taka-ku, the healing herbs, which are the commonest; the kago-we-ku, the everyday herbs, which run from the strengthening to the intoxicating; and the mana-ku, the poisons, which are mighty weapons. The whole of this lore they call yogen, and the ninja keep a set of herbs of their own that no herb-master will teach.

## The Everyday Herbs

The kago-we-ku serve a hundred ordinary needs. Some lend the body strength or speed: forte, brewed warm from the leaf, briefly doubles a man's strength; hiroshi, a boiled mountain lichen, quickens the limbs; ikkyo lets the one who drinks it move at twice the speed; warejawo sharpens the aim for a moment; galena gives sight in the dark; gakkeno, a moss chewed raw, drives out fear; and domaka, a root boiled in cow's milk, restores a man's vigour. Others serve the traveller: isasa berries still hunger for hours, a single chewed nagasa stalk gives the rest of a whole night's sleep, hikaka salve guards the skin against the sun, and wodejima, a boiled coast-weed, lets one breathe underwater for a short while.

Some touch the mind and the senses. Ikowara sharpens smell and taste; titikaka root fixes everything seen and heard under it in the memory forever; kagemuri, smoked, lets one reach the mind of a friend nearby and send a short word; and kachejima, brewed from the leaf, is simply intoxicating, the same drug the lowlands of Ereb call opiander. A few are made for magicians: adonai, a powdered dead bark, wards off magic as the antimagic working does; materasu sharpens spellcraft while dulling all other skill; and ronemaka, dried and chewed, lets a mage cast at half the usual cost in power. Garekone is a sleeping-draught, and kageruwa, stirred into a healing brew, makes it work on beasts as well as men.

## The Healing Herbs

The taka-ku are the herb-master's chief trade. Many simply mend wounds, from chu, dogake and kagewari for small hurts, up through kamika, suba and kerisu, to the potent harade and menigi that close grievous wounds at once; mirena berries and rodin root do the like, and yotesu works slowly over an hour. Aketawa and kagi stop bleeding; banza and kagemasu knit broken bones and ease sprains, and hon mends a strained muscle. Against poison there are gadesowu and the stronger yawodo, and zakeshazu, which dissolves clotted blood and is the one cure for the poison izume. Gemari and romeju guard a wound against rot and gangrene, isatwa strengthens the body against sickness, karewo eases burns, and the two kinds of karesi treat frostbite and shield against heat. For the smaller miseries there are mato and yama for fever and headache, hita for a sore belly, the inhaled steam of tageo for a cold, doware and manuge for cuts and insect-bites, and kawasa to take away a bruise.

## The Poisons

The mana-ku are reckoned weapons, and the herb-masters know a great many. The deadliest kill outright; izume clots the blood to bring death within hours. Others maim without killing: hotesie withers the muscles and leaves a man crippled for life, takiza costs the stricken limb outright, kuchiku and suzu paralyse a limb. Some take the senses, hokkie and kase-domo stealing the sight, masurati the smell and hearing. Some sicken or weaken: gekko and donute turn the stomach, toweki and zazo sap the strength, ayowasi brings three days of fever and sweats, wazika the bleeder's sickness, and jinguro a leaden dullness. A few work stranger harms, kichiware sinking the victim into a coma, okari driving him into a berserk fury against the nearest man, and kodewara loosing his bowels for hours. The simplest, akewato, merely makes a wound fester.

## The Ninja's Herbs

The ninja keep herbs no herb-master will teach, and many are crueller than any poison. Adome, burned to smoke and breathed in, leaves a victim zombie-like and obedient for an hour or two; amekiwa's smoke brings senseless sleep, foul dreams and the loss of the hours before it; kuto, a powder drawn up the nose, rots away the face and the brain in agony; ashiga kills at a touch; jizure ruins the nerves so the limb must be cut away; ikone swells a limb to twice its size; koshie stiffens the joints; and ezie makes a wound fester and turn black. Toshi raises the body's strength at the cost of the wits. A handful serve the ninja himself rather than his mark: burojuzi root stands in for a meal and a night's sleep, usake gives a glimpse of the future as the divining working does, and uzawu and uziko close his wounds.
