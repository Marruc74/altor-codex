# The Heavenly Bodies

Twelve sphere-shaped bodies wheel through the heavens above Altor, and all of them may be seen, in one fashion or another, from the surface of the world. There are nine small moons, the sun, the dread body Raukhra, and at the edge of all things the sphere of fixed stars. Astronomers chart their courses, priests read their omens, and sailors steer by their light. Each has its own nature and its own place in the great clockwork of the sky.

## Luna

Luna, called Lunia in the old tongue, is the largest of the moons and the only one to be seen the whole year round. She circles Altor in about a single day, her face turning always toward the world so that one side shines while the other lies forever dark. Her light is cold and gives no warmth, a pale silver glow that falls upon the night without comfort. Her circuit is not quite constant - it slips by about an hour for reasons the wise have never fathomed - and the bright face she turns to the world is held by many to be a god itself, riding an errant orbit and lending the dark its light. The cold-gods are said to guard her, and her shining face is their watchtower over the sleeping world.

## The Other Moons

Besides Luna there are eight smaller moons, and not all may be seen with the naked eye - several are glimpsed only through the astronomer's telescope. A number of them turn in time with Altor's own motion, keeping pace so that they cast permanent shadow-eclipses upon fixed regions of the world below.

Three among them are the dreaded black moons, dark bodies that drink the light rather than give it. One hangs over southern Soluna, one over the far western sea, and one over easternmost Akrogal, where no Erebite has ever set foot. Beneath each lies a place forever touched by their shadow, shunned by living things and all but unknown - and some hold that the mystic Krindenmaar in Trakorien was once such a place, before whatever shadowed it passed from the sky.

## The Sun

The sun is the Life-giver, the source of all warmth and growth upon Altor. It circles the world just outside Luna's path, completing its journey in exactly a day. The sun is the great prize of the gods' endless contest: the heat-gods strive forever to push it northward, to scorch and burn, while the cold-gods strain to drive it south, to freeze and still. Where it rides in the heavens, there the kindly band of the world is set, and so the warmth of every land hangs upon a war that never ends.

## The Fixed Stars

At the very edge of the universe stands the sphere of fixed stars, a vast shell of black material studded with countless points of light. It encloses all the heavens and marks the boundary of the cosmos. From time to time pieces break loose and fall toward the world as shooting stars, blazing briefly across the night. It is said that the greatest of heroes, when their work on Altor is done, are raised to join the fixed stars and shine forever among them.

## Raukhra

Raukhra is the evil body, and its coming is feared above all others. It sheds a blood-red, cold light upon the world and follows a wildly eccentric orbit, swinging far out into the dark and returning to near Altor only a few times in every thousand years. Raukhra is a power of chaos, and when it draws close, ruin follows.

During the Third Conflux it nearly struck the world itself, looming in the sky as the Red Omen, and the memory of that terror has never faded. Raukhra is worshipped by none but dark cultists, who alone welcome its blood-red return.

## Shakhora

Shakhora is a hidden black moon, seen by none save its own cultists. The learned believe it circles just outside the path of the sun, but it shows itself to no telescope and no honest stargazer. Only those who serve it claim to behold it, and what they see they keep to themselves.

## Comets

Comets are dreaded omens, blazing visitors that streak across the heavens and then are gone. Unlike the moons and the sun, they do not return - each comet is seen but once and never again. To the folk of Ereb their coming foretells calamity, and their passing leaves only dread behind.

## Azuria, the Blue Moon

In the myths of sailors there is told of Azuria, the Blue Moon, that once lit the night with a gentle blue radiance. But Azuria shines no longer. The old tales say it was destroyed in the First Conflux, shattered in that ancient catastrophe, so that now only its memory remains - a lost light that sailors still mourn in their songs.
