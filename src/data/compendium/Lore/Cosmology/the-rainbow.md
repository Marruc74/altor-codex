# The Rainbow

The rainbow of Altor is no mere trick of light but a springboard to the heavens, a source of luck and life. The good gods raised it in the dim past, after the great divine wrath, as a foretaste of paradise, and it can be walked by those worthy to find it.

## The Worthy and the Unicorn

Only the pure of motive may reach the rainbow: there is no place on it for selfishness, for the lust of vengeance, or for malice, though a true act of penance can atone for old wrongs. It helps to have met someone who has been there before and taken a liking to the seeker, but such people are very hard to find. The one creature most bound up with the rainbow is the unicorn, its guardian and constant companion; stand on good terms with a unicorn and one may count on leave to enter, and to ride one is to reach the rainbow far faster than by any other means.

## The Long Seeking

The rainbow shows itself in rain with the sun at one's back, best of all at dawn or dusk. A seeker pursues it in half-circles, the sun always behind, drawn ever northward toward the lands of the midnight sun, where the longer the day the longer the chase. It is a perilous pursuit, for the rainbow's lure can take hold of a soul: those who cannot master it can never again leave off seeking, and must reach the rainbow or die in the trying. Many wander further and further north and never return, and some are found at last in the far reaches of Ereb, frozen into statues with dead, far-gazing eyes.

## Paradise

To reach the rainbow's land is to come into a true idyll: golden-leaved forests, a green and watered valley, falls and fresh summer grass, with unicorns wading in the streams. It is a sanctuary for the good, and the evil can scarcely set foot there, for the place is warded by inexhaustible magic and the unicorns sense any hostile heart at once and gather to drive the threat away.

Those who are welcome find every hurt undone here, sickness and poison, curses, lost limbs and failing wits all made whole, and their spirit and insight lifted besides. At one end stands a cauldron brimming with the brightest gold, enough that the finder need never again think of money; at the other, the Spring of Life, whose water halts a drinker's ageing for some years, though it loses that virtue if carried away and is then no more than holy water. A magician standing in the rainbow's light may work magic at no cost to himself at all.

## The Bridge to the Heavens

The rainbow is more than a refuge. One who stands in its light may be carried bodily through time and space, to other worlds and dimensions; those who travel this way often enough are marked by it, the whites of their eyes shimmering in rainbow colours, the skin taking on a faint, misty hue. And one who can walk the rainbow itself may climb it to the clouds and the Cloud Realm beyond, and, walking further still, come at last to the gate of the gods, for the rainbow is the bridge from Ereb to their home.
