# The Polar Regions

At the two poles of Altor dwell powers older and stranger than any kingdom of men. They are not gods of any temple, yet whole peoples bow to them, and their endless war governs the weather of the whole world. At the north pole stand the Ice Lords; at the south the Salamanders. Between them stretches every land that men have ever known, and across it surges the tide of their unending struggle.

## Overview

- **Northern power:** The Ice Lords (Isherrarna), at the north pole
- **Southern power:** The Salamanders (Salamandrarna), at the south pole
- **Nature:** Beings of condensed energy
- **Northern worshippers:** The Orghin and Sanithsid ice-barbarians
- **Southern worshippers:** Desert nomads and the dinosaur-folk of Samkarna, among others
- **Their war:** The Eternal Struggle, which drives the climate of all Altor

## The Ice Lords

The Ice Lords - the Isherrarna - dwell at the north pole. They are beings of condensed energy, some of them ten metres tall, blue and shimmering, and shaped roughly in the likeness of men. They take no food as living things do but feed only upon the energy of the poles. To the cold barbarian peoples of the north, the Orghin and the Sanithsid, the Ice Lords are objects of worship.

## The Salamanders

The Salamanders - the Salamandrarna - dwell at the south pole. They too are beings of energy, but reptilian in form, and they wield powers of telekinesis, of levitation, and of telepathy. They are worshipped by the nomads of the deserts and by the dinosaur-folk of Samkarna, among others who hold to the warmth of the south.

## The Eternal Struggle

Between the two poles rages a war without end. It is no mere quarrel of distant powers, for the tide of their struggle drives the climate of all Altor. When the Ice Lords gain the upper hand, the ice spreads and the world grows cold. When the Salamanders prevail, the heat rises and the world is parched. The same tug-of-war is mirrored in the heavens above, where the cold-gods and the heat-gods contend to push the sun southward or northward across the sky. Thus the seasons of every land, and the slow shifting of the great ice itself, are but the visible signs of a battle that has neither beginning nor end.
