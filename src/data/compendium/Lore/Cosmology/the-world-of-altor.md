# The World of Altor

For as long as scholars have raised their eyes to the heavens, they have argued over the shape of the world beneath their feet. The dispute was settled - to the satisfaction of the learned, if not always the common folk - by the Furgian astronomer Dykos Barrha, who in the year 612 fO set down the world-picture that still bears his name. The Dykian model is the accepted cosmology across the civilised lands of Ereb, taught in the academies and trusted by navigators and stargazers alike.

According to Barrha, Altor is a perfect sphere resting at the very centre of the universe, with all the heavenly bodies wheeling around it. The world is no flat plate nor endless plain, but a great ball roughly a thousand mil across - some four thousand kilometres from edge to edge - upon whose curved skin the seas and continents are spread.

## Overview

- **Shape:** A perfect sphere at the centre of the universe
- **Diameter:** Roughly 1,000 mil (about 4,000 km)
- **North pole:** A place of paralysing, killing cold
- **South pole:** A waste of murderous, unbearable heat
- **Ereb:** Just north of the equator, in a mild temperate band with a yearly mean near zero
- **Far side:** The distant land of Jih-pun, at the same latitude as Ereb
- **Cosmology proposed by:** Dykos Barrha, Furgian astronomer, in 612 fO

## The Poles and the Temperate Band

The two poles of Altor are realms no living thing may endure. At the north lies a place of paralysing cold, where the air itself seems to freeze and no warmth from the sun can reach. At the south burns a waste of murderous heat, so fierce that nothing may cross it and live. Between these two deaths stretches the bearable world, and the gods contend endlessly to push the kindly band toward one pole or the other.

Ereb lies just north of the equator, within a mild temperate band where the yearly mean hovers near zero. It is this gentle balance that has allowed the kingdoms of men, elves and dwarves to flourish here, neither scorched nor frozen.

## The Far Side of the Sphere

Directly opposite Ereb, at the same latitude on the far curve of the world, lies the distant land of Jih-pun. Few in the west have ever travelled there, and fewer still returned, so that to most Erebites Jih-pun is a name from sailors' tales rather than a place upon any honest map. Yet by Barrha's reckoning it must share Ereb's temperate fortune, and so be a land as fit for life as our own.

## The Reaches of the Air

The air that clothes Altor grows thinner the higher one climbs. Mountaineers and those who have ascended the great peaks tell of breath that comes short and hard, and the learned hold that near fifteen thousand metres the air becomes barely fit to breathe at all. Above twenty thousand metres breathing is all but impossible, and only the thinnest, coldest air remains.

It is high in that rarefied upper air, far above the loftiest mountain, that Caranor drifts - the floating home-island of the silver elves. There, where no ordinary lungs may draw breath, the silver elves keep their ancient seat, sailing the thin heights of the world beyond the reach of mortal climbers.
