# The Gods

The gods made Altor, and the gods play it. To them the world is an eternal game of chess, its peoples and powers the pieces they move across the board, each god straining to claim a little more of the world for its own. Yet for all their might, the gods do not stride openly among mortals. They are bound, as surely as any pawn, by a law older than the game itself.

## The Law of Transience

The first and greatest law that governs the gods is the Law of Transience: nothing upon Altor may last forever. All things must pass - the mountains, the seas, the kingdoms of men, even the works of the gods themselves. This is no mere custom but the very condition of the world's existence.

For if anything eternal were ever to touch the surface of Altor, the Law would break, and the world would end. An undying thing set upon the transient world would be a contradiction the cosmos could not bear. And so the gods, who are themselves eternal, dare not act directly upon Altor. To set even a finger upon the world would be to shatter it.

## How the Gods Work

Bound from direct action, the gods must reach into the world by other means. They work through demigods, beings of divine blood yet bound to the world's transience, who may act where the gods cannot. They work through god-gifts, the blessings and cursed boons granted to mortals who serve them. And they work through envoys, messengers and chosen servants who carry the divine will into the affairs of the living.

By such indirect hands the gods move their pieces, never touching the board themselves, forever playing through intermediaries who may rise and fall as the Law demands.

## Why Even the Cruel Uphold the Law

It might be thought that the cruellest gods, those of cold and violence, would gladly break the Law and bring the world to ruin. Yet even they uphold it, and for good reason: the Law keeps an outlet open for their evil. A world that endured forever would be a world in which their cruelty had at last spent itself or been wholly defeated. But a transient world is a world always in flux, always offering fresh ground to be claimed, fresh peoples to corrupt, fresh battles to be fought. The Law that forbids the dark gods from ending the world is also the Law that grants them an eternity in which to work their malice.

## Light and Darkness

The struggle of the gods is endless, and at its heart lies the contest between Light and Darkness. Light is the power of good, of warmth and growth and order. Darkness is the power of cold and violence, of ruin and despair. Each strains to claim as much of the world as it can hold, and the borders between them shift with every age.

It is said that the gods are infinite in number, beyond all counting. No single faith has ever grasped them whole. Every religion of Ereb, however ancient or proud, worships only partial images of the true gods - shadows and reflections glimpsed through mortal eyes. The priest who names his god and the heretic who curses it may both be reaching, blindly, toward the same vast and unknowable power.
