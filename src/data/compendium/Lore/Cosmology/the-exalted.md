# The Exalted

Beyond the gods and the common dead stand the Exalted, also called the Immortals: beings who were once mortal and who, by deeds near the edge of the impossible, won themselves a place among the deathless. What an Exalted becomes depends on how a land understands its gods. To a shamanic people they are mighty spirits; to a polytheistic one, lesser gods who may yet rise in the divine order; where all things are held to be one, they melt into the world-soul as a new facet of it; and under a single god they become his envoys and closest servants. However they are named, all of them were once mortal, and all reached their station the same way.

## The Hiding of the Secret

The path to immortality is guarded as jealously as it is sought, for the higher powers will not have their door thrown open. An aspirant who lets his knowledge spread is punished, and finds the way grown harder and the powers' favour withdrawn, so the wise keep silent. Most never learn that the Exalted were once mortals at all, still less that seven roads lead to them.

## The Seven Paths

Each of the seven paths asks the work of a lifetime, and none can be walked without leading into trouble, for every demand stands at the edge of the possible.

- **The Prophet's Path** rises to immortality by becoming a god's foremost servant and envoy, reckoned the chief of his church and tested by his god's enemies with the offer of the world, with cunning lies, and with the loss of all he holds dear.
- **The Guardian's Path** is won by defending the balance between the dimensions, turning back invasion and chaos and the waking of elder powers across many worlds. Gadirm, who holds the title of Dragon-Master, is said to walk this path, though scarcely halfway along it.
- **The Truth-seeker's Path** reaches immortality by grasping the innermost secrets of the multiverse, gathering a hoard of knowledge greater than any on the continent and answering at last the seven great questions of life that only the gods can answer.
- **The Archmage's Path** belongs to one who becomes the mightiest and most inventive mage of the age, master of his guild and victor in every magical duel, who leaves new workings of his own devising to those who come after.
- **The Nature-warden's Path** is for one who becomes nature's keeper, warding the wild from ruin again and again and teaching its ways to chosen pupils, until he is counted among Mother Earth's foremost.
- **The Hero's Path** is the road of great deeds, walked by one whose feats win him renown as the foremost hero of three continents, who throws down every challenger and leaves monuments to outlast him by five hundred years.
- **The Ruler's Path** ascends by founding and holding a realm so great it dominates a whole continent, set on so sure a footing that it can be warded against every threat for five hundred years after the ruler's reign.

## The Taking Up

Every path asks more than great deeds: lasting works left to posterity, a spirit wholly given to the road, dread trials and temptations that ruin those who fall to them, the keeping of mysteries no mortal may hear, and decades of faithful service. When at last every demand is met, the aspirant is told by some omen or revelation, and given a year to settle all his earthly cares and give away everything he owns. Then he withdraws to a lonely place, leaves the world, and the Immortals take him up among them.
