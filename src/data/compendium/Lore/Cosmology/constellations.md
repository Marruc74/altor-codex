# Constellations

When night falls over Ereb and the heavens wheel above, the folk of the land trace patterns among the fixed stars and tell the tales that go with them. Each constellation carries a myth, handed down from the elder days - of heroes and monsters, of gods and their gifts, of love won and hubris punished. The stargazers of Ereb know them all, and by them they reckon the seasons and recall the old stories. Here are the chief constellations of the Ereban sky, each with the legend it bears.

## Orcherion the Warrior and Charcheloch

The figure of Orcherion the Warrior stands among the brightest of the heavens, and beneath his feet coils Charcheloch, the two-headed serpent. The myth tells that Orcherion slew the great serpent that writhed below him, setting his heel upon its twin heads, and so warrior and beast were raised together into the sky, victor and vanquished bound forever.

## The Golden Sickle, King Gesnus and Medusa's Hair

Three constellations belong to one tale. King Gesnus saw his beloved carried off into the realm of the Death-prince, and to ransom her he was set a terrible task. With the Golden Sickle he cut three serpents from the writhing locks of Medusa's Hair, and by this deed he won his love back from death. The sickle, the king and the serpent-hair shine together as a testament to his courage.

## Harablos the Dragon and Hagros the Ship

The hero Akkillo hunted the dragon Harablos across sky and sea, sailing in his ship Hagros. When at last the dragon was slain, its vast body fell and became the island of Caddo, which sailors still know to this day. Dragon and ship were lifted into the heavens, the hunt frozen forever among the stars.

## The Lute

The Lute recalls the magic instrument Alofon, which belonged to the Empress Farimah. With its enchanted music she freed the Emperor Leodorn from the clutches of a giant, charming the brute or breaking its will, and so the lute was honoured with a place in the sky.

## Turingwil the Wolf and Vilevin the Rider

Turingwil was the giant wolf, father of all wolves, a beast so great that no blade could pierce its hide. The hero Vilevin slew it not from without but from within, and for this feat both the monstrous wolf and its slayer were set among the stars, hunter and prey side by side.

## The Staff

The Staff belonged to the archmage Kossel, mightiest of his age. But Kossel grew proud, and for his hubris the gods took his staff from him and cast it into the heavens, where it hangs as a warning to all who would set themselves above the divine.

## The Sword, the Demon and Elfhelm the Demon's Bane

In the first days there was no day, only the reign of the Demon of Darkness. The boy Elfhelm took up the sword Yrbad and went against the Demon, and though he was but a child he struck it down. With the Demon slain, the light came forth and the first day dawned. The Sword, the Demon and Elfhelm the Demon's Bane shine together to mark how day itself was won.

## Ghazil the Dwarf

Ghazil the Dwarf is honoured as the forefather of all dwarves, the first of his kind, from whom every dwarf of the deep halls is descended. His figure stands sturdy and broad among the stars, a patriarch watching over his children below.

## The Gods' Helm

The barbarians tell the saga of Ilfdanur, who stole the golden helm of the gods. From this bold theft, they say, comes the fair hair of the barbarian peoples, for the gold of the helm passed into their heads. The tale also explains their deep fear of magic, for they learned what comes of meddling with the powers of the divine. The Gods' Helm shines as the emblem of that ancient saga.

## Aruros the Goat

Aruros the Goat belongs to the smith-god Mingar and the hero Horgar-Nir. The myth tells of a wondrous goat forged of six metals, a creature of the smith's art made living, which served the hero in his deeds. The goat was raised to the heavens, gleaming with its many metals among the stars.

## The Harp

The Harp is Belerofon, the harp god-smithed for Parikila, the mother of birds. From its strings came the first birdsong, and all the singing of birds since flows from that god-made music. The Harp hangs in the sky as the wellspring of every song the birds have ever sung.

## The Sun

The Sun, among the constellations, is the symbol of the Shining Way - the faith of light and warmth. It stands in the heavens not only as the Life-giver of the world but as the sign of those who walk the path of the Light, a beacon for the devout to follow.
