# The Shaul Deck

The Shaul deck is a set of twenty graven stone tiles used for divination, among the most trusted means of foretelling known in Ereb. The cards are said never to lie. The whole difficulty of the art lies in reading them, for they answer truly but only ever in riddles, and a careless seer may be led as far astray by a true answer as by a false one.

## Origin

The deck was discovered by the abbot Klausul Kovermikus in the year 389 eO. From him it passed to the monk Alfons Benevox and to the wizard Shaza Sahivann of Ordo Magica, and through their hands its use spread among the learned, until the Shaul cards became the diviner's tool of choice across much of the civilised world.

## The Twenty Cards

The deck holds twenty cards, each a knot of meanings the seer must untangle: Death, Chaos, the Tempter, Love, Hatred, the Dragon, Man, the Unicorn, the Knight, the Servant, the Ant, the Beggar, the Teacher, the Prophet, the Meditator, the Firmament, Luna, the Sun, the Key, and the Pendulum. Each carries its own web of associations, from the renewal hidden in Death and the unconquered chance of Chaos to the good fortune of the Unicorn, the wisdom of the Teacher, and the divine perfection of the Pendulum. The cards give true counsel; whether the seer is wise enough to follow it is another matter.
