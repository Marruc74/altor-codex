# Dimension Travel

To cross from Altor into Inferno is the demonologist's most dangerous art. The mage opens a portal on the spot where he stands — a circle, most often chalked on the ground with a pentagram drawn inside it — and that portal is his one tie to home for as long as he walks the demon-world. While it stands he can step back through it in a moment. Should so much as a single line of the pentagram be broken, he is stranded in Inferno, and feels the loss the instant it happens, wherever he is.

## Portals

A portal need not look like a chalked circle. Left to settle, it tends to take the shape of some natural feature of its surroundings, and small still pools and mirrors are the commonest forms of all, the more so for the permanent portals that stand open for years. Permanent portals are rare and jealously guarded; across the whole of Ereb there are reckoned to be no more than ten. The cunning hide and ward theirs, against demons that would slip through and against rivals who would steal or break them.

## The Borderland

Between the two worlds lies a borderland, a great dark space ringed by a pentagram far larger than the one that opens it, that can be seen from both Altor and Inferno but touched from neither. When two beings meet at a guarded portal, one may be caught there, and any who pass after must reckon with it; the trapped thing may let them by or force a fight. A duel fought in that borderland is watched from both worlds and meddled with from neither, and mighty mages seek out one another's portals on purpose for exactly such duels — the Circle Masters on the island of Sanritra are known for it. Most arts fail there, and a demon that dies in the borderland is not merely sent home but cast into the Outermost Darkness, to wait out the ages with the Old Gods until Doomsday.

## The Dimension Wardens

Worst of all the portals are the hidden doors that open into Altor from the Inferno side, warded against discovery from our world but not from the demons' — for they let the demons creep through at will, and the country around such a door soon crawls with demonic things. These are hunted down by the Dimension Wardens, Gadirm chief among them, who find and unmake them before they can do much harm. Few last long.

## Known Portals in Ereb

Two of Ereb's permanent portals are spoken of more than the rest.

The first lies in the Darbon mountains on the island of Sanritra, where the river Stycos, the Abyss River, plunges into the greatest waterfall in all Ereb. The river is said to run on through the Realm of the Dead under the name Styx, and a traveller who follows the ravine down far enough comes at last to a portal that opens into the Realm of the Dead itself. None who has gone through has come back.

The second is the grave-chamber of Arkhaz, where the magician Kachzebraud was laid to rest. His power at death was too great for the demons of the dead to drag his soul away, and so it hangs torn between Altor and Inferno to this day. Lift the hatch of his tomb and you look straight into a dimension portal; step through, and you meet Kachzebraud's withered soul waiting in the borderland.

Among living dimension-walkers, none is better remembered than Zalgi of Teng-Vezhor, a short-tempered dwarf demonologist who, at the Seekers' twenty-third meeting on Mereld, called up the ancient demon Garwaxxthogg in under ten seconds — purely because he was sour at having been made to come by boat.
