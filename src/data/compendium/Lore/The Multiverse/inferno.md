# Inferno

Of all the worlds of Demonicum, Inferno is the one that lies nearest to Altor, and the one from which most of the demons that trouble our world come. It is a cellar-dimension of warring demon-princes, where intrigue never sleeps and open war is an ordinary thing. Even the demons reckon it a hellish place to live; what makes it dangerous to us is how close it sits to Altor, and how badly its inhabitants want out.

## Overview

- **What it is:** The demon-world closest to Altor, source of nearly all the demons summoned here
- **Its shape:** Nine circles, each deeper and worse than the last
- **Its rulers:** Demon-princes, forever scheming and at war with one another
- **What its demons want:** To break into Altor and bring on its Doomsday
- **Why they are barred:** Demons are immortal, and no immortal thing may walk Altor unless the Thirteen Conditions are met

## The Nine Circles

Inferno is built in nine circles, and a traveller who means to reach the deeper ones must pay dearly for the passage. They run, from the outermost in:

- **Limbo**, the threshold, where most who cross from Altor first arrive
- **The Circle of Cold**
- **The Circle of the Beast**
- **The Circle of Shadows**
- **The Circle of Plagues**
- **The Circle of Wars**
- **The Circle of Pains**
- **The Circle of Chaos**
- **The Inner Circle**, deepest and most terrible of all

The wise open their portals in Limbo and on some lonely spot where demons seldom go. To arrive deeper, or worse, in the middle of a demon's lair, is to arrive already in trouble.

## Why the Demons Covet Altor

A demon that is middling or weak in Inferno becomes something deadly in Altor, where the air itself seems to feed its strength, and that alone is lure enough for many. But the demons have a longer aim. They mean to break through the Order that the Young Gods laid over creation and restore the old laws of the Old Gods, when gods and demons and the dead alike walked the world as they pleased. Should they ever manage it, that would be the end of the world as its peoples know it — what men call Altor's Doomsday.

For now the dimension walls hold, and the demons come only one at a time and only when called. An immortal cannot truly die in our world, and so cannot be suffered in it freely; a demon is let through only when a summoner meets the Thirteen Conditions, and a demon slain here merely loses its body and wakes again in Inferno a few days later. To end one for good, it must be sent home and killed there.
