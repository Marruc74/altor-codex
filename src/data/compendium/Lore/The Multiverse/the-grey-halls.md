# The Grey Halls

Between the walled worlds of the Multiverse lies a country that belongs to none of them: the Grey Halls, the astral plane, an endless reach of pillared halls where no time passes and all colour is leached away. It is by the Grey Halls that mages most often travel from world to world, for it touches them all; but it is a road that has swallowed more travellers than it has carried home.

## Overview

- **What it is:** The astral plane, a timeless grey country touching every world
- **What enters it:** Not the body but the astral form — a grey, colourless mirror of the traveller
- **Its gates:** Each closed portal appears as a locked and rune-graven iron gate, beside a stone bearing the rune of the world it opens upon
- **Its danger:** To lose one's way is to forget one's own body, perhaps forever
- **Its dwellers:** Lost mages, demons and wanderers, ready to help or to prey

## The Colourless Country

To step through a portal into the Grey Halls is to leave the body behind; only the astral form crosses over, a grey reflection of the traveller stripped of all hue. What lies beyond is hall upon hall without end, ranked with pillars and crowded with statues and old memories, lit by no sun. Each gateway out stands as a locked ancient door of iron, graven with runes, a stone plate beside it carved with the sign of the world to which it leads. The mage who knows the runes may find his way; the one who does not may wander until he forgets even the shape of the flesh he left behind, and so is lost beyond recall, unless he can seize some new body to wear.

## Traces and Wanderers

Every being that passes through the Halls leaves a fading astral trail behind it, and the demon-hunters who have learned the lintropic art can read and follow these traces from world to world. The Halls are not empty: demons, sorcerers and adventurers who lost their direction haunt the grey corridors, some seeking only a portal that will give them back a body, others ready to fall upon any traveller they meet. The most pitiable are those who have forgotten their own form entirely, and will try to take by force the first body they find.

## A Refuge for the Exiled

Because no time touches the Grey Halls, they have served as a prison and a place of exile. It is told that a failed god, cast out by his own kind, was banished into the grey country, and there built a red world of his own and worked his spite upon those who wandered in. Such tales are not idle: the Halls are the back-passage of the Multiverse, and what is shut out of every honest world may yet be met with there.
