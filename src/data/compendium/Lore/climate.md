# Climate

From the ice-locked crowns of the north to the sun-warmed shores of the southern seas, Altor is a world of many weathers. A traveller who sets out from the frozen wastes where the ice-barbarians hunt and walks the long road south will pass through every kind of country the world holds, until at last the air turns soft and dry and the nights grow cold beneath a clear sky. Sailors and merchants know these zones as well as they know the stars, for a man's fortune and often his life depend on knowing what skies and seas await him.

## The Arctic North

In the far reaches of Orghin and Sanithsid lies a land of eternal, biting cold. Here the snow and ice never wholly relent, and the wind cuts like a flensing knife. No ordinary folk can live in such a place without shelter and aid, and only the ice-barbarians make their home upon these wastes, wrapped in furs and bred to the cold across countless hard generations. To outsiders it is a country of white silence, beautiful and pitiless, where a careless step or a single unsheltered night means death.

## The Subarctic Lands

South of the true ice lie the cold lands of Jorduashur, Barbia, and the straits that part Ereb from Orghin. The climate here is harsh and windswept, and the snow-free months can be counted on a single hand. Winters are long and dark, summers brief and grudging. The peoples of these lands are hardy and weathered, used to wresting a living from grudging soil and stormy waters. Though the worst of the arctic chill is spared them, few who are born to warmer climes would call this country gentle.

## The Temperate Coasts

Along the seaboards of Trakorien and Ransard the weather is ruled by the sea. Winters come cold, wet, and windy, with gales driving in off the water and rain more common than snow. Summers, by contrast, are mild and at times even hot, when the clouds break and the sun lies warm upon the harbours. It is a changeable, restless climate, well suited to seafaring folk who have learned to read the moods of wind and water and to make their living upon them.

## The Temperate Inland

Across the heart of Ereb stretches the dominant climate of the world - the temperate inland country. Here the yearly mean hangs only a few degrees above freezing, and the rains fall in moderation through the turning seasons. Spring and autumn are long and gentle, summers warm without cruelty, winters cold without the savagery of the north. It is in these lands that the great kingdoms of men have flourished, for the soil yields well and the seasons keep their faithful round, and a careful farmer may look to bring in a harvest year upon year.

## The Copper Sea

Of all the climates of Altor, none is more pleasant than that of the Copper Sea. Its summers are warm and bright with long sunlit days, and its winters are short and mild, bringing more rain and sleet than snow. The waters lie calm and inviting for much of the year, and it is no wonder that this is the busiest shipping country in all the world. Here the great trade-routes meet, here the rich port-cities raise their towers, and here a sailor may pass his days under kinder skies than anywhere else beneath the sun.

## The Subtropical South

In the southern reaches of Soluna and Samkarna the land turns warm and dry. Long spells of cloudless heat are broken only now and again by the coming of the rains, and the yearly mean climbs to around ten degrees above freezing. Yet for all the warmth of the days, the nights can turn surprisingly cold, the heat of the sun fleeing swiftly once it has set. It is a country of bright distances and parched earth, where water is precious and the rhythm of life follows the long alternation of drought and downpour.
