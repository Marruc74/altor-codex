# Remuntra

Strangest and most dreaded of all the faiths of Trakorien, Remuntra is no religion at all but a contagious madness - a parasite of the mind that crept into the world through the world-seams of Krindenland. It does not spread by touch. It spreads by being thought of, catching in the mind like a tune that will not leave, so that even to dwell on it is to risk taking it. It arose in Krindenmaar, and the kettle-mender Klastobrest Dimvett, the first to be seized by it, carried it abroad: he came preaching to Lasemos in 349 eO, then sailed away westward with half the city's fleet, and what became of him no one knows.

## The Teaching

Learn the world and all that is in it, Remuntra teaches, so that at life's end you may leave it with a laugh. The world is a prison for free souls at the deepest level, and the surest road to freedom is to laugh, to sing, and to do the unthinkable. Its followers shave the head to five tufts - one over each ear, one at the brow, one at the nape, and one standing straight up from the crown - and wrap themselves in a plain cloth hung with odd ornaments. Whoever kills a worshipper of Remuntra is apt to catch the teaching himself; so when its devotees appear, the wise give them quickly whatever they want and see them gone.

## What It Does to the Mind

The change Remuntra works is no god's gift but something stranger. A worshipper's mind can no longer be touched by fear or by magic; he is wholly beyond reach, withstanding any assault - or else undone all at once, as a single whole, when the flame of his fate finally gutters out.
