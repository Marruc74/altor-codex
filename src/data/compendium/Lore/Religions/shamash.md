# Shamash

Shamash is the god of truth, and once he was only a minor figure in the old classical pantheon of Jorpagna. He rose to power when the lands of Moskorien and Fokale were salted and ruined and the hearts of their people fell into darkness; out of that grief a piece of southern Moskorien broke away to become a religious state, the Holy Kishatet, founded in 311 eO at Frand.

## The Teaching

The world, Shamash teaches, is full of false images, and the righteous soul holds to sincere faith and guards itself against hypocrisy, vanity, seduction and lies. His holy writ is the Nomona veritra, "the truth's name-rolls," set beside the Grubha veritres, the commentaries of learned men - and both are given over chiefly to rules for shunning the temptations and illusions of life. His symbols are the circle, the clear crystal and the water-drop; his priests go in brown and white. Mirrors, images and ornament are forbidden, and at the borders of the Kishatet such things are seized or painted over with tar.

## The Truth-Throne

The faith is ruled from the truth-throne in Albarunzia by the Shar Kishati, "the high dream-slayer," who reigns with thirteen crystal-lords, the krystalokrater. A tithe of a tenth to a third is taken of the faithful, according to their standing. At the faith's grim edge stands the Joobalasher sect of Frand, who hold that the world is wholly without meaning.

## The God's Gifts

To his devoted priests Shamash grants uncanny powers against falsehood. Such a priest can tell every lie that is spoken to him, and can see through any disguise - illusion, invisibility, a changed shape, all of it laid bare. Greatest of all, he can speak the binding word, by which a man swears an oath before Shamash himself; should the oath be broken, the penalty the swearer named falls upon him of its own accord - though if the word is spoken wrongly, it recoils upon the priest instead.
