# Wegil

Wegil, the Lord of Light, is a god of old Jorpagna, worshipped above all by the Jorer and reckoned by most a good god. He is the patron and protector of Torsborg, where his temple is the grandest building in the town, and the old tales are full of his long war against the dark god Kashim.

## The Lord of Light

Wegil is shown as a middle-aged man of vast build and noble features, long-bearded and piercing of eye, armed with a war-club and a long spear and clad in a short shirt of mithril mail. To him are given the powers to change his shape, to wake and speak with the dead, to sway the weather, and to read the thoughts of men. What he wills is the spread of civilisation, of law and good order, and his servants are bidden to stand brave against darkness, barbarism and chaos wherever they find it.

## The Faith and Its Forms

Wegil's worship takes three forms. Commonest by far is the church, whose priests go in light-blue robes and dark-blue cloaks. In remoter places stand the monasteries, where brothers in dark-blue habits fill their days with prayer, meditation, the copying of books, and the labour that keeps them fed. And there are the knightly orders, some forty or fifty sworn knights to a castle, who ride in heavy armour beneath light-blue mantles.

## The Rule of Life

Those who follow Wegil are bound to a rule: to help the weak and the needy; to shun needless violence and killing, though self-defence and the hunt for food are allowed; and to uphold law and order, breaking no worldly law save such as run contrary to Wegil's own teaching. To break the rule is to be gnawed by guilt until a deed of penance sets it right.
