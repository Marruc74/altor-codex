Kashim, the Ruling Flame, also called the Lord of Life, is worshipped by some few humans and held by everyone else to be an evil god. Where he came from no one knows, only that it was not Jorpagna. He is the eternal foe of Wegil, the Lord of Light, and where Wegil's faith stands open in its grand temples, Kashim's is a thing of secret cellars and hidden altars.

## The Ruling Flame

Kashim is shown as a mighty, powerful being with glowing red eyes, clawed hands, and charred, scaly skin, bearing a burning sword and a bronze round-shield. He can wake and speak with the dead, hurl balls of fire, and loose deadly sickness among those who do not worship him.

## The Right of the Strong

His teaching is simple and brutal: the right of the strong. A society that lasts, it holds, can be built only when the weak have been culled out - and among the weak it counts the elves and the crippled. By worshipping and appeasing Kashim a man may win for himself the strength to become one of the Strong. Until that day the Light is the enemy, for the Light shelters the weak; but when Kashim rules at last, the teaching promises, he will clothe himself in white and light the world himself.

## The Cult

His worshippers are ill-regarded, and so they bind themselves into small, secret societies. By day they dress as anyone might; at their ceremonies they put on long black robes lined with wine-red, and gather in remote temples or underground crypts, often heavily guarded. Their worship takes blood: a living victim is brought to an altar that holds a small image of Kashim, killed with a single quick stroke of the dagger, the blood caught in a bronze bowl and poured over the statue, and the body burned in a fire-pit. The image is decked with stolen jewels, both to mock Wegil and to bind the members more tightly to the society.

## The Rule of Life

A follower of Kashim must visit a temple and bring an offering at least four times a year, and one of those offerings must be a "faithless" human; must report any spy, intruder or traitor to the priesthood at once; and must shun all close contact with the Light and the Weak, undergoing rites of strengthening when such contact cannot be avoided. For this reason a worshipper of Kashim and a worshipper of Wegil can never long keep the same company - sooner or later each learns what the other is, and they come to blows.
