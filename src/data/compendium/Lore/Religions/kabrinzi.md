# Kabrinzi

Kabrinzi is a fallen faith of a single god, and the half-secret order that still keeps it. The name is Joric for "the greatness of thought," and the religion began not in revelation but in study, the work of a club of learned men who set out to reason their way to the one true god.

## The Founding

In the town of Kaibab, now Kabiona, some twenty intellectuals, authors, scholars and magicians, formed a club to discover the one true and unassailable religion. They spent half a century at it, in research, divination and travel to other worlds, and came at last to a single god, Kabrinzi, who governs the laws of nature and the fate of every living thing. Their conclusions they set down in the Book of the Eternal Songs.

## Zumlit Zaar

The order's ruin was a man recruited into it by mistake: Zumlit Zaar, an alchemist of Krun, sodden with drink, drugs and ill-used magic. Convinced the doctrine had to be spread at any cost, he murdered his fellow founders, burning them alive in a magical "firework" that left a crater still visible today, and then preached the faith himself, drawing converts by the force of a terrible charisma. He vanished without trace on a missionary journey at sixty-five, and is said to live yet, kept going for five centuries by a drug, a wretched addict hidden away near the swamp of Efron's pool.

## The Robber-Knights and the Fall

After Zumlit vanished, a curia of priests founded a knightly order to carry the faith abroad. It soon rotted into an ungodly robber-band that burned fields and killed peasants in Kabrinzi's name, spreading the religion and ruining its name together. The order's true strength lay in three relics that could sway a whole crowd with a single word, kept under heavy guard and used only with great care. When the rulers of the land came to favour the Shining Way as the people's religion, bold nobles broke into the vault, stole the three relics, and murdered the entire Kabrinzi leadership. The order collapsed, its temples thrown down or left to rot, and the relics were scattered and hidden, for no one living knew how to wield them.

## The Seekers

A remnant survived in an unreachable corner of Efronzink, sworn to restore the faith by recovering the lost relics. Two of the faithful at a time undergo a rite in which they drink the holy water Mindusvinet; if they fail to find all three relics within twenty years, the Seekers' curse strikes them dead, though before dying each may name two new Seekers, each granted twenty years of their own, and every Kabrinzi adherent is bound by oath to aid them. Two of the three relics have already been recovered, and the third has lately been found.

## The Three Branches

Perhaps three thousand adherents remain, in three kinds. The Monks keep to themselves in the mountains near Klomellien, at their prayers and ritual objects and their own provision. The Underground, the largest group by far, are ordinary folk of every trade who meet rarely and in secret. And the Wandering Knights are armoured messengers, spies and enforcers who bear no heraldry at all, known only by the one thing they share: every last one of them rides a pure white horse.
