# Imária

Imária is the goddess of the undead and of eternal torment, and the mightiest of the eight demon-princes of the Oktagon, who are bound to Raukhra, the evil red moon. Hers is a worship of the grave and of suffering made sacrament, kept in secret wherever the dead are made to serve and pain is offered up as a gift.

## The Chained Goddess

It is told that Imária does not range free, but lies chained upon the Montures, bound in a buried chamber by fetters that none of her own kind can loosen. Her cults labour toward the day of her release, and reckon the slow torment of the living and the raising of the dead as works pleasing to her. Among the demon-princes of the Oktagon she is held the greatest, and the necromancers and torturers who serve the red moon name her first.

## The Curse of the Montures

Long ago the demigoddess was loosed once already, when Queen Karaleia VII of the Montures, granted a vision of her, freed her from her mountain prison and so unknowingly sold her soul. Imária turned the whole people of the islands into walking dead, the imariots, and raised an underground city, until swarms of flesh-eating locusts and the Third Conflux together broke the land and sent the city into the depths of the Gideon Chasm. There she was chained anew, and there she waits.

Should she ever be set free again, the weakest souls on the isles would drop at once into walking death, and the rest she would claim one by one, manifesting as a dark mist or a pale young woman, seizing living bodies and commanding the dead for leagues around. Her reach already runs through the imariot rings, silver bands imprinted with her evil through which she may borrow a wearer's senses, seize his body by night, and at last draw him wholly into her grip; only the elf-folk are proof against them. Her freedom rests now with her chained servant Karaleia, and no power known to the islands can destroy her.
