# The Shining Way

The Shining Way - in the old tongue Den Lysande Vagen - is the dominant faith of the lands about the Copper Sea. It teaches that there is but one true God, the sun-god Etin, whose light fills all creation and whose all-seeing eye weighs the deeds of every living soul. From a single revelation granted to a potter's son some six hundred years past, the faith has grown into the mightiest and wealthiest institution of the civilised West, crowning kings and condemning heretics alike.

## Overview

- **Name:** The Shining Way (Den Lysande Vagen)
- **Deity:** Etin, the one true God, the all-seeing sun-god
- **Founder:** Odo, son of a potter of Arno in Caddo
- **Holy text:** The Golden Book, recorded in Dalkish
- **Symbol:** The sun
- **Seat:** Ekeborg, residence of the Exarch
- **Strongholds:** Zorakin and Kardien; official but weaker in Erebos and Berendien
- **Greatest festival:** The Sun Dance, at the summer solstice

## History

About six hundred years ago a young man named Odo, the son of a potter in the town of Arno in Caddo, received a revelation from the sun-god Etin. He was then twenty-seven years of age. From that day he began to preach the Shining Way, and his teaching spread across the lands of the Copper Sea. By the 100s eO the faith had taken firm root in Zorakin and Kardien, where it remains strongest to this day.

The unity of the church did not endure. In 278 eO a bitter dispute arose over whether the working of magic could be reconciled with Odo's teaching. The quarrel could not be healed, and in 281 eO the exarchs of Arno and of Ekeborg condemned one another. This is remembered as the Great Schism. It left behind two rival organisations and a faith much weakened by the division.

## Doctrine

Etin is the one true God, the all-seeing sun-god whose light reaches every corner of creation. When a soul departs the body, Etin himself sits in judgement. The best of the dead are taken to serve at his side in light everlasting. The worst are cast down into a hell of chaos and darkness. The great mass of ordinary folk, neither wholly good nor wholly wicked, must endure a long purgatory before they may be cleansed.

## Creation

In the beginning there was only the void. Etin filled it with his light and from that light shaped Altor and all that is upon it. He made humankind in his own image. The lesser god-beings that move through the world possess no such power. They may alter and reshape what already exists, but they can never truly create, for creation belongs to Etin alone.

## The Six Commandments

The faithful are bound by six commandments:

1. Etin is greatest.
2. Do not misuse the Teaching.
3. Honour your family.
4. Commit no cruel deeds.
5. Do not steal.
6. Keep your word.

## Customs

The words of Odo are preserved in the Golden Book, written in the Dalkish tongue. The symbol of the faith is the sun, displayed upon temples, vestments and banners throughout the believing lands. The greatest of all festivals is the Sun Dance, held at the summer solstice, when the God's light is at its fullest. A person is brought into the faith through the Sun Ceremony.

## Organization

The church is ruled by the Exarch, a figure much like a pope, who resides in Ekeborg. About him stands a curia of six high clerics. Beneath the Exarch the realm of the faithful is divided into archbishoprics, each under an archbishop; these in turn are divided into bishoprics under bishops; and these into parishes, each tended by its parish-priest.

The church is supported by a tithe of one tenth, drawn from the faithful, and it pays no tax. Over the centuries this has made the church enormously rich. Its cathedrals are lavish and its leaders live in great luxury - a wealth so conspicuous that it has eroded the trust of common folk and given heretics and reformers much to point at.

## Power and Wealth

The Shining Way is strongest in Zorakin and Kardien, where kings and nobles alike are counted among its members. It is the official faith of Erebos and Berendien also, though its hold there is weaker. By law the four kings of these realms must profess the faith. Through this bond of crown and altar the church wields influence over the affairs of nations as surely as over the souls of men.

## The Bright Hand

The Bright Hand - Den Ljusa Handen - is the inquisition of the church. It was instituted by the exarch Zegedorn III in 314 eO. Its office is to police doctrine and to hunt out heresy wherever it takes root. The Bright Hand does not operate upon Caddo, the very land of Odo's birth, which lies beyond its reach since the Schism.

## Heresies

The church names several heresies and tolerates none of them gladly:

**Caddism** holds that all magic is ungodly and must be banned outright. Since the Great Schism it has been forbidden upon the mainland, though it endures upon Caddo.

**Illusism** comes out of Trakorien and teaches that the whole of the world is a grand illusion. It was condemned in Kardien by the exarch Pergon IV in 403 eO, and its illusionists were driven into exile in Trakorien.

**The Simple Ones** - De Enkla - preach that no church ought to exist at all. They live strictly by the Golden Book and are the loudest of all critics of the church's luxury and wealth.

Beyond these, the church holds the undead to be an abomination that must be destroyed wherever it is found, and all necromancy is forbidden outright.
