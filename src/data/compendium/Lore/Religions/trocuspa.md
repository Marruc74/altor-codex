# Trocuspa

Trocuspa is the war-god, and his purpose is a strange one: to raise a mighty host against the last battle with chaos at the end of time. That army waits in a vast bronze fortress beyond the rim-mountains of the world, and Trocuspa gathers it soul by soul. His rule of faith is the Blodsboken, the Blood Book, which calls the world "a cirkhantisk world" - and since *cirkhantisk* in the old tongue can mean both circular and spherical, his followers take it to mean the world is flat.

## The God Without Morals

Trocuspa lays down no rules of right and wrong. The one thing that interests him is skill at arms, and the right bearing toward oneself, one's enemy, the fight, and life and death alike. That bearing is called banzikan.

## Banzikan

Banzikan is the war-art and the discipline of the mind that goes with it. Its three symbols are the states a novice must reach: the oak, which walls its rot away from its core; the stone, which rests untouched on the bottom of the current; and the seed, which gathers its whole being into a sealed chamber. A master is not to be swayed, worn down or reshaped: he splits his mind into parts that work apart from one another, sealing panic, pain and fear away from his resolve and loosing the stored feeling only when it suits him. The classic schools of the art are Bhannavil and Clusta Noba, on eastern Marjura. To its finest students the god grants a deadly skill at arms, an endurance and fearlessness that no terror can break, and the power to pass at will between cold calm and a battle-fury.
