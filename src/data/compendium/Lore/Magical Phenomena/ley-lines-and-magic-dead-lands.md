# Ley Lines and Magic-Dead Lands

Magic does not lie evenly upon the world. It runs in hidden rivers and pools in hidden wells, and in some places it fails altogether. The mage who knows where it gathers and where it dies holds an advantage worth more than any spell, and the siting of temples, academies and strongholds has turned on that knowledge since the art was young.

## Overview

- **Ley lines:** Hidden currents of magical force; a mage near one finds his power swollen
- **Magic-nodes:** Where the lines cross or well up, the strongest places of all
- **Magic-dead lands:** Tracts where all magic fails, and a mage ages as a common man
- **Mage-storms:** Periodic discharges that sweep all Ereb and undo magic for a time
- **Why it matters:** Mages seek the nodes and shun the dead lands when they choose where to build

## The Hidden Currents

Across the face of Altor run the ley lines, unseen rivers of magical force. A magician who works his art close to one finds his power grown of itself, and where several lines run near together the swelling is greater still. Greatest of all are the magic-nodes, the points where the currents cross or rise to the surface, and at such a place even a modest mage may work wonders far beyond his ordinary strength. The wise have always sought them out, and the oldest temples and academies stand where they do for that reason and no other.

## The Dead Lands

Against the nodes stand their opposites: the magic-dead lands, tracts of country where the currents do not reach and all magic simply ceases. No spell will take there, no enchantment hold; a magician in a dead land is no more than any other traveller, and ages year for year as common folk do. Such places range from a few hundred paces across to whole districts, and a mage caught unawares in one has lost his every advantage at a stroke.

## The Mage-Storms

Over and above the fixed geography of magic come the mage-storms, the magistormar - periodic discharges of magical power that gather and sweep across the whole of Ereb in the guise of violent thunderstorms. Within such a storm no spell of any kind may be cast, and the enchantments bound into magic items are at risk of being stripped away forever. Against the mage-storm neither node nor study avails; for as long as it rages, all magic in Ereb is undone alike.
