# The Black Water

Far in the north of the world lies a stretch of water unlike any other: dark and heavy and oil-like, neither freezing in the cold nor moving with the wind, so black that it swallows the light of day. Sailors give it a wide berth, and mages give it wider still, for of all who venture onto the Black Water it is the worker of magic who is least likely to come home.

## Overview

- **What it is:** A dark, oil-like stretch of water in the far north
- **Reputation:** Deadly, and deadliest of all to magicians
- **What it is said to be:** An ill-willed water elemental, or a shadow-beast wearing the seeming of a sea
- **Disposition:** Wholly malevolent toward the living

## The Sea That Hates

The learned cannot agree what the Black Water truly is. Some hold it for an ancient water elemental turned to malice, a single vast and hating thing that has taken the shape of a sea. Others name it a shadow-beast, a creature of the dark places that lies still and patient beneath a skin of black water and waits for prey to cross it. Either way the tales agree on the end: those who sail upon it are dragged down and drowned, and a magician aboard is taken first of all, as though the thing knows its enemy by the magic in his blood.
