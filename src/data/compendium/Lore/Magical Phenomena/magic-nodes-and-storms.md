# Magic Nodes and Storms

Magic does not lie evenly upon Ereb. In some places it runs strong and in others weak, and now and then it fails altogether. Two peculiarities mark this above all: the magic nodes, where the art waxes or wanes with the land, and the mage-storms, in which no magic can be worked at all.

## Magic Nodes

A node is a place where the matter that makes magical currents usable gathers thick, its strength shaped by the make of the earth's crust and by the climate above it. Within a favourable node a mage casts more easily, spends less of himself, and recovers his strength faster; in an unfavourable one the reverse is true. The land itself leans toward certain arts. Animism comes easiest by the sea, necromancy in the heights and the deeps, and mentalism among rock and stone, while spells of cold falter in hot country and spells of heat in the cold.

## Mage-storms

The mage-storms, the magistormar, are periodic discharges of magical power that gather and sweep across the whole of Ereb in the guise of violent thunderstorms. Within such a storm no spell of any kind may be cast, and the enchantments bound into magic items are at risk of being stripped away, leaving them dead and ordinary forever after. They can be foreseen only by the art of weather-prediction, and a true thunderstorm follows close behind. Against them no sorcery avails, and for a time all magic in Ereb is undone.
