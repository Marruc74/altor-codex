# Khab-Hemi

![Khab-Hemi](/compendium/Lore/Magical Phenomena/khab-hemi.jpg "Khab-Hemi")

Khab-Hemi, the Cauldron of Bitterness, is a shadow-shrouded valley in the wilds of Ereb where darkness and bitterness thrive. It is wrong to call the place evil, for it was not so at the beginning, and a person of clean conscience and strong soul might even find it beautiful for all its bitter weight. But darkness and sorrow keep close company with evil, and over the years the valley has drawn to itself every kind of light-shy creature and every magician with a destructive plan, until it has become, in the world's telling, the abode of wickedness. No one knows its true origin: some say a magical experiment gone to ruin, some a grave at its heart whose dead one's grief and anguish leak out as this, some a thing the gods cast down from heaven, a foretaste of hell.

## Overview

- **What it is:** a shadow-shrouded valley in Ereb, called the Cauldron of Bitterness
- **Its shape:** a circular valley three kilometres across, ringed by black-crystal cliffs three hundred metres high and lidded by black cloud
- **Its dark:** a magical darkness that deepens the further down one goes, and preys on weak souls
- **Its people:** outcasts and schemers, the light-shy and the hiding, who prize magical light above gold
- **Its heart:** a black swamp with an island, and on the island a tower built for one purpose, the summoning of demons

## The Valley

Khab-Hemi is reached only by climbing harsh upland country until, at the lip of what seems a wide plateau, the land falls suddenly away around the valley like the inside of a funnel, its centre a dark stain. Black clouds hide it from above, a fearful sight from afar, and a sheer cliff of black crystal three hundred metres high rings it within the cloud. Only three hidden paths lead down to the curved floor. Inside, hills and crags of black crystal rise from black earth, black streams run down to a black swamp at the centre, dark forests crowd the open ground, and on an island in the swamp stands a ruin. At the edge between forest and plain lies a ruined city that has never seen daylight.

## The Darkness

The black cloud-lid lays a strange grip on the soul. The mist feels cold and the air wet and hard to breathe, and to all who enter it the dark seems alive, wrapping and snaring them with a will of its own that grows heavier the further down they go, held in place by sorrow and anguish until it feels like smothering. The strong of soul shrug it off; the weak do not, and those given to anguish, vengeance, envy, hunger for power, or a cruel nature feel it worst of all. One it overcomes is called dark-taken: the mist seeps into them and lets their worst sides master their will, so that they grow sunk in despair, speak little, shun the light, and drift away from any company toward their own bitter thoughts, sure the answers lie deeper in Khab-Hemi. A cruel soul, by contrast, finds it all exhilarating, a seed given the spark to grow, and feels itself drawing near the dark powers it longs to serve.

## The Inhabitants

Here live svartfolk and humans, shadow-beasts, gasts and ghosts, night-wolves and vampires, all of them come to flee something, or fallen here against their will, or lying low for a time. They keep little company with one another, though they trade in food, tools and one thing above all: magical light. Common flame cannot be seen in the magical dark, only felt and warmed by, so a magician's light is worth more than gold, and there are those who will kill for a handful of it. In the lightless ruin-city a thread of magical glow shows now and then from a window, where some light-shy thief hiding from a hanging lives wall to wall with a vampire or a shadow-beast, each minding their own and troubling no one, for they came here to be left alone.

## The Island and the Tower

In the swamp's heart stands an island of black crystal, thirty metres across, faintly lit from within by slow-drifting motes like stars. A ruined tower rises at its centre, habitable in part and used at times by a magician, and a hidden stair descends to a chamber built for one purpose alone: the summoning of demons, its black floor set with a great pentagram of mithril. Whether the hall was raised in recent years or has stood as long as Khab-Hemi itself, no one can say.

## Strange Properties

The valley bends the world around it. Time runs slow within: those who leave find only a third as much has passed outside, so that it is a place to study undisturbed. The mind sharpens there, and prophecies and foretelling-spells come truer than they would anywhere else. But all the black arts, necromancy and dark magic and witchcraft and the rest, come at half their usual cost to learn, and everything that stays in the valley turns black, a stain that only hours in strong sunlight will lift. Worst of all, one who has been taken by the mist and harbours evil designs finds it very hard to leave at all, held by the bitterness until some new purpose pulls them out. It is said that a few of the lost crown jewels of the old kingdom lie hidden on the island, for any with the will to brave the cauldron for them.
