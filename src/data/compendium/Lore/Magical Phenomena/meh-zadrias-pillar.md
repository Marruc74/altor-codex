# Meh-Zadria's Pillar

![Meh-Zadria's Pillar](/compendium/Lore/Magical Phenomena/meh-zadrias-pillar.jpg "Meh-Zadria's Pillar")

A single gigantic pillar of grey marble stands in the wilds among the peaks of the Kardbergen, six metres across and rising a full three kilometres into the sky, its top often lost in cloud. A spiral stair winds the whole of its height to a flat summit ringed by a low parapet, and only the durability magic worked into it holds the impossible thing together.

## The Pillars of Heaven

The travel-notes of Sadaram ta-Podiam name it as one of the pillars that once held up the heavens and parted the earth from the sky. Legend goes further: that it is all that stands of an enormous temple and palace from before Ereb's time, a dwelling of the gods called Meh-Zadria, and that the mountains all around are the rest of those ruins, with incredible treasures buried among them. The dragons, the story says, were the wardens of that treasury, and when the new age came their minds were sealed against the wisdom of the elder world, leaving behind only the lust for hoarded gold that has driven them ever since.

## The Magician's Secret

For a magician the pillar holds a stranger power still. One who stands upon its summit and works his spells finds their reach extended enormously, so that what would carry a few paces carries for kilometres, and sight itself can be cast across miles of country. This makes the pillar a prize of the first rank wherever war-magic is in play. A prophet foretold that it would one day be the central fortress of a kingdom that grows with its magician's knowledge, and that a great reckoning would be settled there as a duel upon the top, for the one who stands on the summit is the rightful ruler of all the land he can see and reach.
