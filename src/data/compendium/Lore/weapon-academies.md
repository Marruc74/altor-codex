# Weapon Academies

A warrior may learn his trade in the field or from a hired master, but the finest learn it at one of the great weapon-academies — schools where a single art of arms is studied to a depth no common soldier ever reaches. A handful are famous across Ereb, each built around a single founder and a single family of weapons, with its own hard road to mastery. The teaching is dear and the training long, three years and more, but a master of an academy is worth a company of ordinary swords.

## Eledain's Fencing School

In the city of Gringul stands Eledain's school, the home of the duellist's art. Here a student learns the long-sword and broadsword, the rapier and the thrusting-blade, each paired with the dagger, the kidney-dagger, the stiletto or the parrying-dagger held in the off hand. It takes some hundred students a year, with its fees softened for the truly gifted, and its graduates are the duellists and swashbucklers who make their name on the point of a sword.

## Kane's Order

Hidden somewhere in the Cer Mountains of Cereval lies the most storied school in Ereb, the order founded by the legendary elven war-monk Kane. Its place cannot be bought, only earned: only the worthy ever find the way to its gate, and most who seek it never do, for a warrior may make the attempt but once in his life. Those who are admitted learn the one-handed sword and every kind of shield, and pay nothing for it; but the final trial is a battle against ice demons, and not all who begin it walk away. It is whispered that dragons hate the disciples of Kane above all other warriors.

## Hauksheim

In Sigsdale, in the land of Jorduashur, stands Hauksheim, the academy of the axe. It was founded by the barbarian Hauk, who lost his left arm and built a whole school of one-armed axe-craft around the loss. Its students learn the broad-axe, the hand-axe, the battle-axe and the bearded axe, and its teaching is all attack and no caution. Barbarians are given pride of place there, as is only fitting in a house founded by one of their own.

## The Schools of the East

Far across the western sea, in the island realm of Jih-Pun, the warriors keep martial schools of their own: the empty-handed arts of Karate and Kenpo, the throws and locks of Ju-jutsu, and the secret Ninjutsu, the ninja's way, taught only within the hidden clans.
