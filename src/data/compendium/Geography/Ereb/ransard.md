# Ransard

![Ransard](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Ransard/Ransard1.jpg "Ransard")
![Ransard male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Ransard/Male1.jpg "Ransard male")
![Ransard female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Ransard/Female1.jpg "Ransard female")
![Griffinrider](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Ransard/Griffinrider1.jpg "Griffinrider")

Ransard stands in the west where mountains cut the sky and cold winds shape the people as much as the land. To the soft realms of the south it is a place of barbarians and evildoers, but that judgement is unfair and born of fear, for the ransarder are aristocratic warriors bound by a strict honor-code and known above all else for their griffin cavalry. The kingdom is held together by clans, each one bound by loyalty and pride, yet all sworn to a single king who rules from Kapalht. Life is harsh and direct. Strength is admired and weakness remembered. Villages cling to ridges and valleys while warriors train from youth, learning the rhythm of shield and spear. The Kardbergen rise like a wall in the east, home to the griffins that have become the symbol of Ransard's power. From these heights riders take to the skies, turning war into something swift and sudden. Trade exists but raiding defines their presence along distant coasts. Their gods promise rebirth through battle, and many choose the sword over retreat. Ransard is not a land of comfort but of endurance, where honor carries more weight than gold and every generation is shaped by conflict. Now, in the year 610, the kingdom mobilizes for a final reckoning with hated Trakorien.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 130,000 (85% humans, 15% dark folk)
- **Capital:** Kapalht (approx. 4,500)
- **Government:** Clan-based monarchy
- **Ruler:** The vortiger, at this time Argald the Younger (Argald II)
- **Religion:** Worship of the Æsir
- **Exports:** Rock-salt, metals, furs
- **Imports:** Precious metals, grain, plunder-loot
- **Military:** Very strong, especially the flying griffin cavalry

## Geography

Ransard lies in western Ereb, south of Jorduashur and north of the Ljusna peninsula. Its core is the three Ransardian peninsulas - Nordland, Ransland and Sydland - together with the recently subjected kingdoms of Timora and Alerien, which remain nominally independent under vortiger-kings of Ransardian blood. Between Nordland, Ransland and Timora lies Gauland, independent in name yet paying tribute and answering to the same governance.

The realm is bounded in the east by the Kardbergen, a range that rises to nearly 4,000 meters and shelters several strong orc tribes, chief among them the Red Moon's orcs. Below the peaks a partly forested highland descends to the coast, though much of the woodland has been burned away to make farmland. High in the Kardbergen lie many griffin nests, and it was here, long ago, that the ransarder first tamed the young griffins and built from them the cavalry that makes their name feared across Ereb.

## Climate

Cold winds sweep down from the mountains across the peninsulas, and the people weave thick wind-proof cloth to endure them. The highland is bleak and the winters hard, qualities that have shaped a folk of endurance rather than ease.

## People

The ransarder stand well above medium height at around 180 cm, powerfully built, fair-skinned, often blue-eyed, and heavily bearded. They weave thick wind-proof wool dyed in bright colors, and each clan keeps its own pattern, so that a ransarder's clan can be read at a glance. Everyday dress is a white linen shirt, a long-sleeved jacket, heavy hose, and the **merrlokh**, a long cloth wound round the body and left loose on the left side so the spare length can be used to parry a blow.

For battle they don a mail shirt and metal-reinforced leather limb-guards, with an open conical helm and a mail aventail. Promising young warriors are given intensive training in weapons, unarmed combat, field survival, drill and command, and the finest of them are raised to the elite of griffin-riders, mighty foes upon their great winged mounts.

## Culture

Ransard society is shaped by a warrior ideal. Strength and courage are prized above all, and cowardice is a great shame. Skill in battle brings status, and a warrior is often judged by how many enemies he has slain, sometimes notching a stick for each foe felled. The ransarder consider themselves superior to all other folk, regarding other peoples as uncivilized rabble, and they reserve a particular contempt for the ankor, for halflings and for dark folk. They bear no ill will toward elves, yet a widespread belief that elf-blood grants immortality leads the elves to avoid them.

Their favorite arms are the **seax**, a blade falling between short- and broadsword, and the **tarque**, a small round shield with a central grip used not only to guard but to strike, knocking an opponent aside like a boxer's jab. To these they add a long spear of some 2.5 meters, fit for mounted use, melee or throwing, and a long dagger. Some warriors instead carry the **glamorg**, a huge two-handed sword up to 2 meters in length. Griffin-borne archers are uncommon but extremely accurate, their iron-hard bows piercing both shield and steel helm in a single shot.

## Language

The ransarder speak **Ransardian**, a west-Nargurian dialect close to *Jordian* and to the tongues of Timora and Alerien.

## Society

Life is lived in clans of fifty to five hundred souls, very tightly bound, each led by its eldest able man, who may name another in his place should he judge himself unworthy. Each clan holds a small territory and its chieftain acts as a petty lord, with no fixed rank set among the clans.

For all their love of battle the ransarder are reasonable and just among themselves, and an offender will stand by his crime without complaint. A ransarder burdened by an inferiority complex usually leaves his clan to seek fortune abroad, hiding his origin, for the ransarder are not a popular folk beyond their own borders.

### Laws

Ransardian law is unusually enlightened and humane for Ereb, for a people of such high self-esteem find harsh penalties unnecessary, and they live by the rule of treating others as they wish to be treated. Prisoners are never tortured, a beaten foe is met with respect, and slaves are given tolerant if impersonal treatment.

### Feuds

Clan feuds are common, arising over disputed land or slain kin, for a fallen clansman must be avenged or the whole clan is dishonored. Such feuds are often settled by **holmgang**, a single combat between the clans' best champions. Crimes against personal honor such as rape, adultery and slander are punished hard, usually by death if the offended kin so decide, or by exile if the vortiger judges the matter.

## Government

Ransard is ruled by a king, the **vortiger**, who is at once war-leader, supreme judge, administrator of trade and treasurer. He holds absolute power, and disobedient chieftains defy him at their peril. He sits for five years at a time, after which he must be re-approved by the chieftains, and he names his own successor, usually his eldest son. The capital Kapalht and its surrounding lands always belong to the sitting vortiger.

## Economy

The ransarder are not truly traders, though they pretend to be, for in truth they take what they need by force. Their coastal raids on Magilre, Trakorien, Klomellien and even Kardien are widely feared. Fast, seaworthy ships range far in search of gold, silver, spices, fabrics and jewels. Griffins seldom come on these raids, being hard to carry by sea and great eaters, so the raiding sticks to the coasts.

## Military

Despite their love of battle the ransarder fight with discipline and method. Their favorite formation is the **haphener**, a wedge of twenty-five men six ranks wide, with two flankers to each side and archers set behind the gap. The flankers, often bearing two-handers, keep the enemy from the wedge's sides, while the archers shoot through the gap, aiming first at the enemy's horses. On contact the wedge spreads into a line, and once it reaches some fifteen men wide it opens to launch a fresh wedge.

### Griffins

The griffins of the Kardbergen are Ransard's mightiest allies and its greatest offensive weapon. Their hard temperament allows the ransarder to tame them as mounts, and nearly every self-respecting clan owns at least one. A griffin bonds intimately with its tamer and is unlikely to bear any other rider. In battle they wear a breastplate that gives some protection against arrows, but they rely most on swift evasions, for their riders are masters of control and the beasts themselves are skilled flyers.

## Religion

The ransarder worship the **Æsir**, a god-family of very many members, the same pantheon honored in Jorduashur. They believe that one slain in battle is reborn in the realm of the gods, and from this faith comes their fearlessness, leading many to seek death in battle rather than flee.

## History

### Founding and the western migrations

- **900s B.O.**: barbarians arrive from the west, some passing on to Jorduashur
- **400s B.O.**: dark folk, mainly orcs, reach the Kardbergen
- **27 B.O.**: Ransard is united under **Omric the Great**

### Belgoth and the wars of freedom

- **2 B.O.**: **Hanric Silvertongue**, Omric's son, weds Princess **Timrin of Belgoth** under a treaty granting Belgoth's crown to Hanric should its king die heirless
- **1 B.O.–10 A.O.**: the Great War of Freedom, in which Belgoth's chieftains revolt and are crushed, sowing many later feuds
- **12 A.O.**: Belgoth is absorbed into Ransard
- **18–24 A.O.**: western Belgoth clans revolt; Hanric makes peace and forces them to emigrate south of the Ljusna peninsula

### The Saphyna colony and the Ransarder Wars

- **50 A.O.**: the ransarder found a colony on the Trakorian isle of **Saphyna**
- **129 A.O.**: the Brothers' War in Ransard
- **176 A.O.**: the kingdom of **Timolerien** is founded east of Ransard by southern refugees
- **181–191 A.O. (First Ransarder War)**: the klavyker, forerunners of the Trakorians, attack Saphyna; after years of war the Ransardian fleet is destroyed at Rubakin's Cove (187) and the colonists crushed (191); the ruling clan **Omric** falls and clan **Amron** takes power
- **204 A.O.**: the theocracy **Palinor** is founded east of the Kardbergen by the fanatical **Gheril sect**, a front for the Lord of Evil **Gheeril** masked behind a false good god, **Hjallbrand**
- **210, 310, 410, 510 A.O.**: Palinor launches four crusades against the heathens, all beaten back, often with Ransardian aid; the **Brotherhood of the Cross** is founded in Alerien (411) in defense
- **277–287 A.O.**: Ransard allies with Milacke and Magilre to crush the klavyk realm, then falls out with Magilre over Saphyna, and both withdraw to the mainland
- **370–385 A.O. (Second Ransarder War)**: **Edorl the Young** tries to retake Saphyna but splits his army; the Paratorna landing fails, Edorl falls, and his brother **Margon the Wise** organizes the retreat
- **378 A.O.**: Margon of clan Amron is deposed; **Teldoric** of clan Omric becomes vortiger
- **387–392 A.O.**: Timolerien's king dies without an heir and the realm splits into **Timoria** and **Alerien**, with Teldoric brokering a tense peace

### The Millennium of the Griffins

- **488 A.O.**: **Argald Omric** is born, foretold to be Ransard's greatest vortiger
- **497–507 A.O. (Third Ransarder War)**: Trakorian troops land on Saphyna; vortiger **Gorm the Old** and the eleven-year-old Argald sail to reinforce, but Trakorian numbers prevail; Gorm gathers the survivors and sails home (507), pursued, with half the fleeing ships sunk or burned
- **508 A.O.**: the **Millennium of the Griffins** begins as Argald Omric tames a king-griffin; titled **the Griffin-tamer**, he weds Princess **Eowen of Narim** and is crowned vortiger, and many Kardbergen griffins are tamed
- **510 A.O.**: Palinor's fourth crusade is easily repelled with the new griffins, and Argald the Griffin-tamer personally slays Palinor's war-leader, the mage **Krarr**
- **513 A.O.**: **Joran the Golden**, Argald's son, is born; in the **Wolf-winter**, ice-barbarians from Orghin invade from east and west, and Ransard sends aid to poor Narim
- **515 A.O.**: Narim joins Ransard; **Nerol**, Joran's son, is born

### The Omric vortigers and the road to war

- **532 A.O.**: Argald the Griffin-tamer is murdered by Trakorian spies; Joran the Golden is crowned
- **540 A.O.**: Timora is invaded
- **549 A.O.**: Joran is killed in an ambush before the Battle of Heron; **Nerol Omric** is crowned and ends the Timorian war
- **559 A.O.**: **Caorm Omric**, Nerol's fourth son, is born
- **565–572 A.O.**: the Red Moon orcs raid Narim repeatedly
- **573 A.O.**: Nerol's sons die one by one and the king grows paranoid and unpopular; Caorm travels Ereb learning foreign war-craft and becomes **Caorm Elf-friend**, blood-brother to a grey-elf companion
- **582 A.O.**: the chieftains depose Nerol; Caorm Elf-friend returns and, with the griffins, crushes the rebels in a short and bloody civil war
- **583 A.O.**: tentative contact is made with the Kardskogen elves
- **584 A.O.**: **Argald the Younger**, Caorm's son, is born
- **599 A.O.**: the theocracy Alerien attacks Narim, and the Red Moon orcs, under a spell, ravage the Kardskogen elves; Ransard cannot help, for the Alerien army stands between them, and the elves break off contact
- **600–605 A.O.**: Ransard counterattacks and conquers Alerien under Caorm Elf-friend, who is then murdered (605) by his Timorian mistress, again with Trakorian fingerprints; Argald the Younger swears at his coronation to avenge his father
- **606–609 A.O.**: Argald plots and mobilizes for a final confrontation with Trakorien, and many young griffins are tamed
- **610 A.O.**: the kingdom stands on the brink of its greatest war

## Foreign Relations

Ransard's history is a long quarrel with Trakorien, whose spies and assassins have struck down two vortigers and whose grip on Saphyna has cost the ransarder three wars. The subject realms of Timora, Alerien and Narim are bound to Ransard under vortiger-kings of its own blood, while neighboring Gauland pays tribute though it keeps its name. To the east the theocracy of Palinor has launched four crusades against the ransarder and been thrown back each time, and the Red Moon orcs of the Kardbergen remain a standing threat to the northern lands of Narim. With the elves of the Kardskogen relations are uneasy and now broken, soured by the orcs' assault and by the elves' own fear of a folk that covets their immortal blood.

## Heraldry

The arms of Ransard bear a single rampant griffin, reared upon its hind legs - the beast of the Kardbergen heights from which the realm built the flying cavalry that makes its name feared across all Ereb.
