# Montures

![Montures](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Montures/Montures1.jpg "Montures")
![Montures male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Montures/Male1.jpg "Montures male")
![Montures female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Montures/Female1.jpg "Montures female")

Montures lies scattered across the warm waters of the Golwynda Sea, a chain of islands where life follows the rhythm of tides and trade. The high cliffs of Gogero stand quiet and harsh, while Elmor carries the weight of power through the city of Timar, where merchants, nobles and sailors shape the fate of the duchy. Across the smaller islands, fishing villages cling to the coast, their people bound to the sea that feeds them.

The land is warm and forgiving, with olive groves and low woods covering the hills, yet beyond the fertile ground lie mountains where fewer dare to settle, and a great fog-filled chasm that the islanders speak of only in fear. The history of Montures is marked by ducal rule, shifting alliances and the constant pressure of pirates and rebellion. Trade flows through its harbours, bringing wealth, ambition and conflict in equal measure.

In Montures, power is negotiated in quiet halls, fortunes are carried on ships, and every village holds its own struggles beneath the same sun - while far beneath the mountains an older darkness lies sleeping, waiting for the day it wakes.

## Overview

- **Type:** An island duchy in the Golwynda Sea, some eight hundred miles northwest of Melukha's northern cape
- **Capital:** Timar, on the island of Elmor
- **Government:** A duke who reigns as the puppet of a seven-member Council
- **Peoples:** The Montureans, the hidden shark-men of the western coast, and the old undead horrors that dwell beneath the isles
- **Faith:** The state Church of Sbintor, set against the true forest-god Mokylider
- **Exports:** Silver, glass, wine and crafts
- **Climate:** Warm and subtropical, with hot summers, mild wet winters and sudden autumn storms

## Geography

Montures is a scattering of islands in the Golwynda Sea, two of them great and the rest small, divided one from another by warm and treacherous waters.

The northern island is **Gogero**, a harsh and barren land of black cliffs that rise sheer from the sea, in places to the height of fifty paces, so that no ordinary landing can be made upon its shores. Some ten peaks crown it, and the greatest of them is **Oredraki**, a mountain near four thousand paces high that stands almost always wrapped in mist, ringed by a score of geysers and many hundred hot springs. Its rivers run hot and reeking with sulphur, and no fish live in them.

The southern island is **Elmor**, the fertile heart of the duchy, long and tapering toward the south, half of it given over to mountains and half to good farmland. Across its north runs the **Agino Mountains**, a broad range whose silver has been dug since the days of the imariots. To the south spreads the **Vergili Forest**, a great wood thick with game, ash and pine on the high ground and oak, chestnut and cypress below. There too lies **Lake Sango**, a deep and miry water famed for its fishing and feared for the monster said to dwell in it. Elmor's chief rivers, the **Miseva** and the **Trangor**, run sweet and full of fish and carry the trade of the inland down to the coast.

Between and beyond the great islands lie the smaller isles and one dread feature above all. The **Gideon Chasm** is a ravine two hundred miles long and half a mile deep, narrowing in places to a few dozen paces, forever filled with fog and the roar of the wind and the wreckage of lost ships. The sea around the isles is easy to reach from the east, but its autumn winds are treacherous, and vessels wrecked east of the islands are dragged by undersea currents toward the chasm's eastern mouth. Off to the southwest lies little **Jalujak**, a pasture-isle where the wild dwarf-horses run.

## Climate, Flora and Fauna

The islands enjoy a warm subtropical climate, with hot summers and mild, rain-heavy winters. But the autumn brings sudden storms that can rise from a calm sea to a full gale in the space of half an hour, and sailors who do not heed the sky pay for it dearly.

The forests of Elmor hold one treasure above all others: **knotgran**, a stone-hard evergreen seldom taller than a man, whose wood is prized far above any common timber for fine carving. So scarce and costly has it become that the law now protects it, and to fell it without leave is punished as theft. From the wild herbs of the isles the islanders draw their drugs. **Sgucco**, a bitter root-drink, will nourish a person for a whole day on a single draught, though long use loosens the bowels; and **ponuvera**, a smoking-drug drawn from a rare herb, brings hallucination and deep sleep and is so dangerously habit-forming that its making and keeping are outlawed.

The wildlife of the islands runs from the curious to the deadly. The thieving **magpie** has a sharp eye for hidden glittering things and will seek out buried treasure, which makes it both prized and hunted. In the warm seas swim the cowardly **silver-sharks**, which gather in numbers and turn savage at the scent of blood, their tough hides cut into boots and gloves. Larger and far more dreadful is the **giant squid** that troubles the divers of Zoduda and lurks also in the drowned lagoon beneath the Dead City. And on the little isle of Jalujak run the **wild dwarf-horses**, caught and broken to draw the ore-carts of the mountain mines.

## People and Society

The folk of Montures live within a rigid feudal order, each rank set firmly above the next. At the summit stands the duke, and below him the seven Councillors who rule in his name. Beneath them come the nobles and the great-farmers, many of them noble themselves and free of all tax, who hold the land and lease it out to the smallholders and tenant-farmers who work it. Below these stands the great mass of common folk, the farmers, labourers and soldiers, and lowest of all an underclass of sailors, wanderers and the workless who hold no standing at all.

Justice in Montures is weighed by rank as much as by deed: the punishment for a crime is scaled by the standing of the offender against that of his victim, the duke and the Councillors are never punished at all, and each fresh offence drags a man's status lower. The penalties themselves are harsh, running from fines and the lash and the brand through the loss of a hand or the tongue, up to death at the stake or being cast living into the Gideon Chasm.

The land is dotted with villages every few miles, each clustered about its fields and orchards, where peach, melon, apricot, plum, fig and olive grow alongside the grain. The islanders are proud of their table, and prize above all the famed **Trangorian spice-cheese**, made to secret family recipes and sold even to the mainland. But not all of Montures lives so well. High in the **Agino Mountains** lie the harsh mining camps, labour-gangs of men alone, where women and children are forbidden, the work is brutal and dangerous, and newcomers are bullied without mercy.

## Government

In name, Montures is ruled by **Duke Mauritz VI Bergenswaard**, a young man tall and slender and fond of silk, who has the look of a thinker but little of the substance, and who passes his weeks in feasting. In truth he is a marionette, and the strings are pulled by the **Council**.

The Council was founded long ago as a body of seven advisers to the duke, but it has grown into something far greater and far worse. Its seven members are appointed for life and cannot in practice be removed; they pay no taxes and answer to no one. Over the years the body has hardened into a nest of conservatism, greed and intrigue, and it is the Council, not the duke, that truly governs the realm. Its chairman is **Frithiof Greyhand**, head of the Church of Sbintor, and among its number sit some of the most powerful merchants of Timar.

## The Capital Timar

**Timar** stands upon a rocky headland on the east coast of Elmor, where the river Trangor runs down through it to the sea. It is ringed by a high, thick wall set with watchtowers, and within those walls the city is sharply divided by district and by rank.

At the gates lie the **garrison and the customs-house**, with their barracks, their toll-offices and the city prison, where every good brought in is taxed. Crowded behind them are the **workers' quarters**, a tangle of close-built houses where pickpockets ply their trade, and the **harbour quarter**, sunk into squalor, with its sailors' bars, its pleasure-houses, its drug-sellers and its outlaws. The **merchant and craft quarters** make up the prosperous heart of the city, paved and orderly, where shopkeepers dwell above their stores and the workshops, taverns and bakeries of the middle sort do their business. Apart from these, behind their own walls, stand the stone houses of the **noble quarter**, home to the nobility and the richest merchants.

The city's higher life has its own seats. The **academy** gathers learned men and women of language, history, botany, zoology, geology and astrology, and keeps a library open to any who pay the fee. The **theatre** seats a thousand, and the operas of Narge Gyldenharpa are the favourite of the crowd. Above all rises the duke's **castle**, a three-storey hall faced in marble, with pleasure-grounds driven by water-power, and beneath it a **hidden underground complex** that links the castle in secret to the city's court-house and its tax-office.

The trade of Timar runs through its **great merchant houses**, which import fine cloth, spices, silver, weapons and luxuries and send out in return glass, wine, silverwork, crafts, grain and the famed Trangorian spice-cheese. One house, hidden behind a false name, deals in a darker cargo still, and runs slaves against the law.

## Villages and Country

Beyond the capital, the life of Montures is the life of its villages, each with its own trade and its own quarrels.

**Zukos** is a large farming village east of the Trangor, known across the isles for its **Trangorian spice-cheese**, made to a secret recipe, and its dark bread. By the southern shore of Lake Sango lie the two fisher-villages of **Sharfal** and **Dudhil**, set on either side of the Miseva and locked for a hundred years in a blood-feud whose first cause no one now remembers, their men trading sabotage and worse in the dark. Yet across that hatred a forbidden love has grown, two young folk of the warring families who meet in secret at an old graveyard and dream of running away together.

Along the coasts the sea rules all. **Zoduda**, on Elmor's southern shore, lives by pearl-diving, drawing pearls, purple-shells and fish from the water, and pays for it twice a year when the giant squid comes. North of Lake Sango stands **Andadax**, a cooperative forest-village of hunters and woodsmen who hold hard work sacred, share what they gather, and quietly shelter the rebels of the Vergili Forest. On the inaccessible western coast lies **Bychow**, whose four hundred and fifty folk are secretly **shark-men**, gill-throated and sharp-toothed shapeshifters who keep empty decoy boats and grow rich by plundering the wrecks of the Gideon Chasm, hiding their wealth and murdering any who guess their secret. And high in the Agino Mountains stand the mining camps of **Nakab**, **Hiradu** and **Owitudu**, grim labour-gangs that send their silver down the Trangor to Timar.

## Religion

Two gods divide the worship of Montures, and only one of them is real.

The **Church of Sbintor** is the faith of the state, raised up around a sun-god whom the rulers themselves invented to unite the people and to draw money from them. Every grown islander is bound by law to make a yearly offering at the great temple in Timar, a windowless hall of marble crowned with a silver dome. The ceremony at its heart is a fraud: the priests' prayers in the old tongue trigger old enchantments hidden in the altar, conjuring light, a blinding flame and a sudden fog, while a trapdoor drops the offerings into a vault below. Only a handful of high priests and the Councillors know the truth. Those who fail to pay their tithe are visited by the church's secret "wrath", and burn in their homes without witnesses.

Against this stands **Mokylider**, the true forest-god, the protector of the woods, worshipped by the hunters and woodsmen who know the worth of the wild. He punishes any who harm forest or beast without need, and his temples of timber stand among the trees of the Vergili Forest. His last known priest is the aged **Attar Sparrowheart**, who seeks an heir to his dying faith, and his power is felt in the forest through a shapeshifting guardian known only as **the Hunter**, a pale horned figure who can take any animal's shape, command the trees and beasts, and turn those who break the wood's law into trees themselves.

## The Hidden Darkness

Beneath the duchy's quarrels lies a far older peril, the demigoddess **Imaria**, the goddess of the undead and of eternal torment. She lies bound in a buried chamber by fetters that none of her own kind can loosen, and for now her power is held in check. But she was not always so confined: long ago she turned the whole island's people into walking dead, and should she ever be set free again, every living soul upon the isles would fall under her shadow, the weakest dropping at once into walking death and the rest claimed by her one by one. No power known to the islands can destroy her.

Her freedom rests with her servant **Queen Karaleia**, the ancient monturean queen who first loosed her and was made an undying vampire for it, now sealed in an obsidian sarcophagus in the Agino Mountains, a vampire unlike the old tales, who casts a reflection, walks in sunlight and fears no cross. Imaria's reach also runs through the **imariot rings**, silver bands imprinted with her evil that hold no magic of their own, yet through which she may borrow a wearer's senses, seize his body in the night, work her curses, and at the last draw him wholly into her grip as one of the walking dead. And in the wall of the Gideon Chasm hangs the **Dead City**, the ruined hold of the imariots, where the undead still keep their dark vigil and bring living captives down to lay before the goddess.

## History

The tale of Montures is reckoned in the years *fO*, before the coming of Odo, and *eO*, after.

- **c.1300 fO** - Seafarers out of Krun discover the isles, and some two hundred colonists are sent to settle them, building roads, harbours and a city, and growing rich on silver and trade.
- **1117 fO** - Montures breaks free of Krun and stands on its own.
- **1022 fO** - Queen Karaleia VII, granted a vision of Imaria, frees the demigoddess from her mountain prison, and so unknowingly sells her soul.
- **1018 fO** - Imaria turns the whole people into walking dead; all contact with the world beyond is cut off, Karaleia becomes a vampire, and an underground city is raised.
- **598 fO** - Swarms of flesh-eating locusts and the Third Conflux together break the island; the city falls into the depths, opening the Gideon Chasm, and Karaleia flees to a crypt where she is sealed in.
- **370-373 eO** - Mauritz I Bergenswaard, stripped of his title in Kardien, sails out, rediscovers the isles, and raises a fortress and the city of Timar with some five hundred followers, reclaiming the ducal title.
- **373-380 eO** - Mauritz I is murdered by the anarchist Gideon Barktongue, who is cast into the chasm that takes his name; the Bergenswaard line endures through the centuries that follow.
- **434 eO** - The Church of Sbintor is founded, and its worship later made obligatory by law.
- **607 eO** - Mauritz VI takes the ducal seat, last of his line and wholly a creature of the Council.

## Pirates and Rebels

Montures is a land on the brink, beset from without and from within.

Off its waters lurk the **pirates of Morelis**, a mixed company of men and stranger folk who strike swift and hard at the shipping of the isles, buy slaves in Timar by night, and keep beasts and worse in their island lair. Through the countryside move the **outlaws**, wanted men with prices on their heads, who drift naturally into the cause of the rebels. And that cause grows stronger by the season: an anarchist **rebel movement** has risen against the oppression of the Council and the duke, sheltering in the Vergili Forest under Mokylider's protection and fed by the village of Andadax, led from Timar's harbour by **Palina Livillion** and meeting in secret in a sailors' tavern called the Narwhal's Horn. The rebels are hanged without pardon where they are caught, yet still they grow, and the whole duchy now stands upon the edge of open civil war.
