# Torsheim

Torsheim lies in the heart of Ereb, a wilderness of deep forests and rushing rivers where the woods press up against the rising white walls of the Nida Mountains. It is a land shaped by old paths and older grudges. The howather clans move through the trees with quiet authority, hunters and warriors who answer to their own elders, while the jorer till their fields at the forest's edge. High in the mountains the Kvizur dwarves carve halls from the living stone and trade iron and gems for grain and timber.

Two faiths divide those who pray. Wegil, the Lord of Light, brings law and the works of civilization, while Kashim, the Ruling Flame, calls for strength and dominance and is worshipped only in secret with blood. Their followers rarely agree, and their houses rarely stand close together.

At the center of it all stands Torsborg, a wooden town on the shore of the Great Lake where clans, merchants and dwarves bargain under watchful eyes, and where no blade may be drawn nor spell spoken within the walls. Beyond the roads the forests remain vast and untamed - wolves hunt in the dark, the troll lands are shunned, and not all who enter return. Torsheim is a place of opportunity, but also of tension, where every path leads to a story waiting to unfold.

## Overview

- **Type:** A wilderness region of central Ereb gathered around the town of Torsborg
- **Chief town:** Torsborg
- **Government:** None over the region as a whole. Torsborg lies under a town-master and a merchant council, while the howather answer to their clan-elders and a chief called a *herse*
- **Peoples:** Howather forest- and mountain-clans, jorer farmers, Kvizur dwarves, the Nekhi wolfmen, scattered elf-kin, and svartfolk in the deep wilds
- **Faiths:** Wegil the Lord of Light, and the secret cult of Kashim the Ruling Flame
- **Trade:** Dwarf metalwork and jewellery, furs, timber, and fish
- **Tongue:** Howathic, with *jori* widely spoken as the language of trade

## Geography

Torsheim is a broad forested land lying between the mountains and the lake, never ruled as a single realm but bound together by its rivers and its woods.

To the north rise the **Nida Mountains**, a vast snow-capped range rich in ore and gems where the dwarves keep their halls. The peaks are crossed only in the height of summer by the **Nida Pass**, a single thread of a road that closes again with the first snows. From these mountains run the rivers that give the region its life: the **Gipul**, the **Hrid** and the **Hronn** spill down from the high country, and all of them feed at last into the broad **Leipter**, the great river that carries trade, travelers and trouble toward the wider world.

Three great woods shape the land between the waters. The **Iron Forest** clings to the mountain skirts where the dwarves cut their timber and the ore-roads run. The **Old Forest** is the ancient heart of the howather hunting-grounds, deep and green and laced with the paths only the clans know. Darkest of all is the **Troll Forest**, a trackless tangle of black pines and thorns where trolls, orcs, dark-elves and ogres make their lairs, and where treasure is rumored to lie unclaimed because none who seek it come back to tell.

At the center of the region lies the **Great Lake**, a wide grey water on whose eastern shore stands Torsborg. Beech, oak and maple crowd its banks, opening here and there into meadows and grazing-land, and through the woods move deer, bears, wolves and wild boar, so that hunting is the common work of the wilderness folk.

## Climate

Summer in Torsheim is mild and steady, kind to the herds and the high passes alike. Winter comes hard, with cold and deep snow that seals the Nida Pass and quiets the woods. Spring and autumn are seasons of heavy rain, and the weather can turn from clear to storm in the space of an afternoon.

## People

No single crown rules Torsheim. Its peoples live side by side in wary balance, each keeping its own law and its own ground.

### The Howather

The **howather** are the warrior folk of the woods, divided into mountain-clans and forest-clans that live by hunting, herding and the strength of kin. Each clan answers to its elders, called **wagas** (the senior heads of a clan), and above the clans stands a single chief, the **herse** (the war-leader and ruler of all the clans), who bears the bronze short-sword Stigis as the mark of his office. Beside the herse stands the **volva** (a female shaman and seeress), keeper of law, history and the spirit-world, whose counsel even a proud chief dares not ignore. The howather prize loyalty, courage and the old ways above all else.

### The Jorer

The **jorer** are farmers, a more settled and townward folk than the howather, who clear the forest's edge for their fields and bring grain to the markets of Torsborg. Their tongue, *jori*, has become the common speech of trade across the whole region.

### The Kvizur Dwarves

In the Nida Mountains dwell the **Kvizur** dwarves, master smiths and jewellers whose ironwork and gemcraft are famed far beyond the region. They keep mostly to their tunnel-halls and deal with the surface folk through Torsborg, where dwarf metal is the heart of all trade.

### The Nekhi Wolfmen

The **Nekhi** are wolfmen, fierce wolf-like folk who came late to Torsheim and swore an oath of eternal friendship with the howather. They run in packs through the wilds and trade now and again with the human clans, though their loyalty is increasingly questioned by those who fear what they may become.

### The Wild Folk

Harpies haunt the lonelier crags, and shy elf-kin keep to the deep woods, seldom seen and seldom troubling the affairs of men. In the darkest forests and the eastern mountains lurk the **svartfolk** - the trolls, orcs, ogres and dark-elves whose lairs are shunned by all sensible folk.

## Religion

Two gods contend for the souls of Torsheim, and their rivalry is older than any clan or town.

### Wegil

**Wegil** is the Lord of Light, a good god of civilization, law and order. His worshippers labor to spread learning and justice and to push back darkness, barbarism and chaos. His faith favors discipline, mercy and the protection of the weak, and once kept a monastery in the region before ruin took it.

### Kashim

**Kashim** is the Ruling Flame, the eternal foe of Wegil and an evil god of strength and dominance who demands that all bow before him. His worship is outlawed and practiced only in secret, in hidden temples where his cult offers blood sacrifice. Where Wegil teaches restraint, Kashim teaches that the strong are made to rule the weak.

## History

The story of Torsheim is told in two great ages, the years *fO* before the founding of the old realms and the years *eO* after, and it is a story of migration, war and uneasy peace.

- **c.1500 fO** - The howather come down out of the north and take the forests and mountains for their own.
- **912 fO** - After the Council of Grafferburg, long wars break out between the wilderness folk and the realm of Jorpagna to the south.
- **599 fO** - A conflux of dark powers ushers in the Dark Time, a long age of fear and disorder across the region.
- **100s-200s eO** - Jori settlers push up from the south into the forest's edge, founding farms and the first lasting market on the Great Lake.
- **212-213 eO** - War flares between the settlers and the clans; the peace that ends it walls the town of Torsborg and binds the howather to the old treaty that the herse still keeps.
- **300s eO** - Elves come into the woods, and the great animist Eloaner founds Santono among the high branches of a giant beech.
- **335 eO** - The Karsakker attack falls upon the region, and the common danger turns the old enmity between clans and townsfolk into a true alliance.
- **487-488 eO** - The Reser War, against the ogres of the wilds, tests the alliance and is remembered in song.
- **590s eO** - The Nekhi wolfmen arrive and swear their oath of eternal friendship with the howather.
- **609 eO** - The Herse Feud splits the mountain clans; out of it Tagaswether rises to power, his rule won but not unchallenged.

## The Town of Torsborg

**Torsborg** is the chief town of Torsheim and its only true settlement of size, a sprawl of timber halls and wharves on the east shore of the Great Lake where howather, jorer and dwarves come together to trade. It is governed by a **town-master** and a council of merchants, and kept in order by a watch of some thirty guards who close the gates at dusk. Within the walls both weapons and magic are forbidden, that the meeting-ground may stay neutral and peaceful for all who come to bargain.

The life of the town turns on its great merchant houses, and chief among them stand two. **House Sagbon** holds the town-mastership by the ancient right of its eldest, though it is the bold Elissa Sagbon, not her indecisive brother Laberkus, who truly runs its affairs. Against them rises **House Vedort**, whose smiling Vidar Vedort schemes to seize the town and the dwarves' trade for himself. Between these two houses the quiet struggle for Torsborg is fought.

## Brewing Conflicts

For all its wary peace, Torsheim is a land where old tensions are coming to a head.

The deepest rift is between **settlement and tradition** - the spreading farms and the growing town press ever harder against the howather's ancient hunting-grounds and the old ways the clans hold sacred. In Torsborg itself the **Vedort-Sagbon rivalry** sharpens by the season, as Vidar Vedort maneuvers to break House Sagbon's hold and master the dwarves' trade. Beyond the walls, **the hunger for farmland** drives the landless to clear ever more forest, breeding resentment among both clansfolk and the poor who own nothing.

Two darker fears gnaw at the region. The **King's Barrows** have grown foul, their sealed mounds now infested by corpse-eaters that prowl the surrounding woods. And a **rising suspicion of the wolfmen** sets clan against ally, as some among the howather begin to wonder whether the Nekhi's oath will hold, or whether the wolf will turn at last upon those it swore to befriend.

## Notable Locations

- **The Wegil Monastery** - The ruined house of the Lord of Light's faith, fallen long ago and now a haunt of memory and danger rather than worship.
- **Kashim's Temple** - The hidden temple to the dark god, reawakened by the necromancer Kati Lom, its sacrifice-hall ringed with fire-pits where her fifty fanatics gather.
- **The King's Barrows** - Three magically sealed mounds holding the heroes of the Village Time and their grave-treasures, among them the Frost Sword and the Night-helm, now overrun by corpse-eaters.
- **The Dark-elf Lair** - The hidden hold of Svirk Langskagg's raiding tribe in the eastern Nida Mountains, ruled by a chief whose fits of madness his folk take for spirit-possession.
- **The Troll Lair** - The stronghold of Gomol Skalle's forest-trolls deep in the Troll Forest, where a cruel and oversized chief hoards stolen jewellery.
- **The Castle Ruin** - The broken keep of the wizard Losether, who vanished five centuries ago, still guarded by his deathless skeletons and said to hide a forgotten library.
- **The Thing-place** - The sacred hill of Thiwosgos where the howather clans gather each spring for a five-day assembly to judge their herse and uphold the law.
- **Santono** - The elf-kin haven and animist academy founded by Eloaner around a giant beech, its tree-houses set high in the branches, a place of healing and learning respected by all.
