# Kardien

Kardien is a land where golden fields stretch beneath a warm southern sun, and stone castles rise over a people divided by birth and duty. It is a feudal crusader kingdom upon the Aidne peninsula, ruled without exception by the proud nobility of the echther, devoted body and soul to the Shining Way, and bound to neighbouring Zorakin by an ancient alliance. In the fertile south, farmers known as faltrakier tend the soil along the great rivers, feeding the realm through steady labour and simple lives. Beyond the towering Aidne-bergen lies the harsh north, a poorer and wilder country where scattered settlements cling to survival and dark folk move through the high passes.

Power in Kardien rests with the echther, an honour-bound caste who rule through a strict feudal order. Kings, dukes, counts and barons each hold their domains, yet none stand entirely above the others, and the balance of power is forever uncertain. Cities may serve a noble house or stand free under their own councils, but all alike remain tied to the crown.

The people follow the Shining Way, a faith so woven into daily life that the church now stands nearly as mighty as the nobility itself. From war-retinues raised by noble houses to quiet villages far from courtly intrigue, Kardien endures as a realm of duty, hierarchy and quiet resilience.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 160,000 (humans 90%, ankor 7%, halflings 2%, elf-folk 1%)
- **Capital:** Ekeborg (~7,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Feudal monarchy
- **Ruler:** King Vidar VI
- **Religion:** The Shining Way
- **Exports:** Timber, iron, copper, furs
- **Imports:** Fabrics, salt, precious metals
- **Military:** Weak fleet, small army, strong knightly cavalry

Kardien is almost entirely ruled by the nobility, the echther, whose grip on the realm is far tighter than that of the lords of any neighbouring land. It is counted a poor country, though its southern fields are rich.

## Geography

Kardien sits upon the Aidne peninsula, gathered around the towering Aidne-bergen that split the realm in two. The land is subtropical, its warmth and dry summers recalling Spain or Italy. Forests are scarce, for the great woods were long ago felled to build ships, and only two of note remain.

### Southern Kardien

The river Stirpaz marks the border with Zorakin, and west of it lies the heart of the kingdom. The fertile Divra plain yields wheat and rye, worked by serfs under their noble masters, with the Insakullarna rising on the headland of Faltrax Ness. The broad, slow Gridefloden is the realm's chief artery, carrying trade from village to village and binding the inland to the cities of Arntuna and Ekeborg. This is the wealthiest and most settled part of the country.

### Northern Kardien

North of the mountains lies the Inmark, sparse and hard, harried by mountain dark folk who descend from the heights. Beyond it stretch the remote and poor northern lands, the duchies of Videlrike and Nordmark, where royal control has grown weak since the rise of the elf-realm of Goiana to the east. Dark Folk hold the north coast of Antivin, and there too dwells the ker-bosh, a native people who still live as in the stone age.

- **The Aidne-bergen**: sparsely peopled and perilous, home to cliff-goats, predators and dark folk, and to old legends of cyclopes and giants
- **Gladaskogen**: the surviving woodland of the west
- **Brynhildur's Forest**: the eastern forest, largely inhabited by elf-folk, whose trees are protected from the touch of steel

## People

### The Echther

The ruling people of the Divra and the only caste from which nobles may be drawn.

- Proud, high-born and intensely honour-bound
- Would sooner die than break a sworn word
- Arrogant toward all other folk
- Suspicious of strangers
- Nobles must marry within the echther

They count themselves the natural and rightful rulers of Kardien.

### The Faltrakier

The common people of the realm, Kardien's counterpart to Zorakin's legerdier.

- Cheerful, good-natured farmers and fishers
- A cowed people, scattered in small villages
- Easily governed and held down by the nobility

They hold no political power whatsoever.

### Minorities

Several smaller peoples share the land:

- **Penterna**: the natives of Divra, granted an autonomous province whose chieftains swear a special oath, rank as underdrots, and owe only a yearly tribute to Ekeborg
- **Ker-bosh**: the stone-age natives of Antivin, left to themselves
- **Dark Folk**: dwelling unsubdued in the Aidne-bergen and on Antivin
- **Elf-folk**: roughly a thousand souls in Goiana's forests, left in peace as the king's personal subjects
- **Halflings and ankor**: full members of Kardian society, found mostly in the free cities

## Language

The tongue of Kardien is Kardiska, close kin to Zorakian yet far nearer to the old imperial jori. Zorakier find Kardier old-fashioned and stilted in their speech, while Kardier in turn judge Zorakian sloppy and unrefined.

## Society

### The Feudal Order

Kardien is bound in a strict hierarchy:

- King
- Dukes (hertigar), ten in number
- Counts (grevar)
- Barons

Each rank governs its own lands and commands the vassals below it, and every level save the lowest is filled by echther alone. Other folk may rise only as priests, burghers or farmers, never as lords. The eldest son inherits an estate undivided, which leaves many landless nobles to seek their fortune as mercenaries, order-knights or priests.

### Power of the Nobility

Because every lord is echther and no other may hold land, the nobility of Kardien wields far greater power than its counterpart in Zorakin:

- Each lord maintains his own soldiers and rules his lands almost as he pleases
- Vassals owe military service rather than tax, each duke and count keeping part of his fief as personal land
- Castles and walls may be raised only with royal permission, so that even a wealthy city stands defenceless before a noble's troops
- The king's authority, though vast, is rarely used, lest the careful balance break

### Cities

- **Noble cities**: owned by a lord, taxed, and run by a bailiff (fogde)
- **Free cities**: which have bought their freedom and a royal charter to build walls and levy tolls, ruled by a council of guild councilmen sworn directly to the king

## Government

Kardien is an absolute monarchy resting upon a feudal pyramid. Each vassal swears two oaths, one to his feudal lord and one to the king, and the king's oath always takes precedence over the other. Ten dukes form the Council, which advises the crown, while the barons are smaller and weaker than those of Zorakin.

The royal domain of Ekenfara is the king's personal land, governed by appointed officials whose offices do not pass to their heirs. Its income, together with the fees of the free cities, keeps the royal treasury normally at least five times richer than that of the wealthiest noble, who is usually the duke of Satgherin or of Ridfara. The king's power is immense yet sparingly used, for any ruler who has abused it has tended to end his days "with a dagger between the shoulder blades."

## Military

The strength of Kardien lies in the war-retinues of its nobles. The realm sets strict caps on how many soldiers each lord may keep in peacetime:

- **King:** 500 soldiers
- **Duke:** 100
- **Count:** 50
- **Baron:** 25
- **Free city:** 25
- **Exark:** 300
- **Tots-kerigass:** 50
- **Kerisgass:** 50
- **Bontisål:** 10

The military orders of the church are counted apart from these limits. In time of war the host swells with conscripted peasants, hired mercenaries and untrained city levies. The war-fleet is small and old, and should the realm need a navy it would lean upon the warships of the sun-knights, the military order of the Shining Way.

## Religion

All Kardier follow the Shining Way with great devotion. The capital Ekeborg, whose name is a softened form of "Echtherborg," holds the great exark palace, and from it the whole church of the Shining Way is governed, save only for Caddo. So deeply does the faith run through Kardien that the church stands nearly as mighty as the nobility.

Heresy and slackness of faith are hunted down by the **Bright Hand**, the church's inquisition, whose courts sit across the mainland under its grand inquisitor, the tots-kerigass. Its reach runs into Zorakin as well, and under a zealous head it has lately pressed a reformation upon the whole mainland church.

## History

The Conflux shattered the old jorisk culture. Kardien fared a little better than the rest of Jorpagna, yet all its administration collapsed all the same, and 650 years of chaos followed before a new realm took shape.

- **The gathering**: a small civil war struck the south, and the people drew together into villages under the leader Valentin Echthe, who died of illness soon after; his followers took his name and became the echther, refusing all dealings with other folk
- **The northern realm**: northern Kardien and Faltrax Ness were long a separate kingdom that sought to expand, nominally part of the realm yet never truly controlled, until the elf-realm of Goiana declared its independence in the east
- **The great malaise**: around that time a strange listlessness, blamed by some on the sorcerer of the Black Tower, robbed the rulers of all will to govern, as though "in eternity's perspective it makes no difference"
- **The echther accept the crown**: with no one willing to lead, the people offered rule to the prosperous echther, who reluctantly accepted, divided the realm into duchies and counties after the manner of Zorakin, bound the faltrakier down in law, and carried the Shining Way into the north

## Foreign Relations

Kardien is joined to Zorakin by an alliance of great age, forged when the echther chieftain Vidar Odise and Zorakin's King Valien swore a bond in the year 56 fO that binds the two realms still. To the east the elf-realm of Goiana broke away and now stands free, and its rise has loosened the crown's hold upon the northern duchies.

## Heraldry

The arms of Kardien bear three crowns, one for each of the dynasties that have held the realm, set upon a field crossed by a silver chevron and a black bar inherited from King Vidar II, who reigned from 400 to 464 eO. Together the charges proclaim the unbroken descent of the crown through its royal houses.
