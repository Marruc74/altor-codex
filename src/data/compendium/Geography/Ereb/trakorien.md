# Trakorien

![Trakorien](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Trakorien1.jpg "Trakorien")
![Trakorien male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Male1.jpg "Trakorien male")
![Trakorien female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Female1.jpg "Trakorien female")

Trakorien lies far to the north, yet its heart beats with the rhythm of distant seas and southern markets. Ships arrive daily, carrying spices, metals, and whispers from lands most citizens will never see. Along its narrow harbor streets, merchants speak in many tongues, and power is measured not in crowns, but in contracts and alliances.

The realm is a loosely held maritime empire spread across four islands, each with its own nature. Fertile **Saphyna** feeds the population, while rugged **Trinsmyra** breeds harder folk shaped by conflict and isolation. **Palamux** holds ancient temples and quiet dangers, and **Paratorna** stands at the center of it all, where the capital **Tricilve** gathers wealth, ambition, and intrigue.

Though an emperor rules, true authority is shared with the great noble council, where decisions are weighed carefully and influence flows like trade itself. Soldiers fight for coin, fleets guard the lifeblood of the realm, and beyond the cities, old beliefs and stranger cults question the very nature of reality - none more dreaded than the contagious cult of **Remuntra**.

Trakorien endures because it adapts, balancing north and south, tradition and change, always looking toward the next horizon.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 160,000 (80% humans, 10% svartfolk, 10% ankor and halflings)
- **Capital:** Tricilve (~9,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Empire with a strong elected emperor and the great noble council, the Bodåken
- **Ruler:** Emperor Radassabar den Store (the Great)
- **Religion:** Several tolerated faiths; no single dominant church
- **Exports:** Metals, fabrics, farm produce, salt
- **Imports:** Sulphur, timber, precious metals, spices, luxuries
- **Military:** Small but strong imperial army and a powerful war-fleet; mercenaries make up roughly 75 percent of the total force

## Geography

Trakorien consists of four major islands, each with its own distinct character.

### Palamux

The largest and most populous island. It holds the **Holy Kishate**, the god **Shamash**'s strongest seat. The **Fokaler Plain** is insanely hot, baked by a mirroring wall that rings it, and long salt-water irrigation has ruined much of its soil. The fierce **kargomiter** of the northwest are feared by all; the rest of the island is shielded from them by the **Eniaken**, a wall grown of unknown fungus. In the south lies the elf-realm **Gwondel Leigå** by the **Thimassergapet**, and nearby the giant-folk **tolgulderna** guard the Fokaler Plain from the horrors of the far west.

### Saphyna

Often called Trakorien's granary. The very fertile **Frimboliner Plain** is tended by the good-natured, simple **saphyner** farmers. The island is split north and south by the snowy **Matagdebergen**, home to evil svartfolk. The eastern cape **Tocme** holds the impassable **Tocmeskogen**, while north of the mountains lies pleasant **Korjulme** with its fruit orchards. The whole western part is **Krindenland**, a feared zone of strange events - possibly a seam where Ereb Altor presses against a parallel universe, causing violent overnight changes in the very geography. It remains too unknown to truly describe.

### Trinsmyra

The smallest and harshest island, rocky and never fully subjugated, yet rich in tin, lead, copper, and silver shipped out from **Vumbra**. It is home to the **wolfmen** of Vulferveden, the dancing **milacker**, the emotional **karker**, and the warlike, rebellious **rungler** who ride **skolopoder** (centipede-like insectoids) over the steep mountains. Deeper caves hold goblins and other vermin.

### Paratorna

The main island and the political center of the empire, ruled from the capital **Tricilve**. The northern province **Ilibaurien** is enslaved to the narcotic spice **opianderkrydda**. **Kryfferskogen** in the east is choked by the thorny **kryffermåra** vine, and only the spotted-skinned dog-folk **skecker** live there. **the Swamp of Shadows** at the island's center holds creatures whose psychic powers drive even the toughest mad. The south is pleasant, with golden grain and lush pasture.

## People

Several peoples share the islands. The **palamoxierna** of Palamux and the **paratornierna** of Paratorna, reputed proud to the grave, are the two largest. The **saphyner** of Saphyna are good-natured and simple, and constantly raided for their wealth. They generally speak **trakoriska**, a west-jorisk dialect.

## Government

Trakorien is governed by an elected **emperor** and the great noble council known as the **Bodåken**. The form is borrowed from old Palamux but is far less rigid, and it grew out of three phases of rule.

- **Feudal Lasemos**: proud noble houses with no king, each area its own principality.
- **The Klavykiska riket's empire**: founded with its capital at **Fontra Cilor** by refugees from the wars of Lasemos, who chose a life-emperor aided by the noble council.
- **The Trakoriska riket's oligarchy**: born of the **Kastyke Conversion**, which swept away the old morals. The thinker **Pisarus** taught, "Strike at everything! Crush the brittle, that only the durable may endure," and the profanet **Makulon Nihivelli** read this as "nothing is holy, only more or less expedient."

The emperor is elected for life, for the title is not hereditary - and his life may prove short if discontent rises. From the council, smaller working groups called **limbes** (limbs; singular *limba*) are detached, each representing one of the many-armed **Kastyke**'s hands and feet.

- **Mana Xifa** (Sword-hand): defense
- **Mana Friga** (Cold-hand): law and justice
- **Poda Arux** (Gold-foot): finances
- **Mana Extenda** (the Realm's Long Arm): foreign policy
- **Mana Phena** (Sea-hand): shipping and fleet
- **Poda Snica** (Fore-foot): trade

Sub-divisions are called **digeter** and **falaner**, fingers and toes. The emperor sits on every limba for unique insight and holds a fivefold vote in the Bodåken. There is also a folk-assembly of rich burghers, which wields a limited veto over the council's decisions.

## Economy

Trade is the lifeblood of Trakorien. Lively island traffic links **Magilre**, **Bzegusta**, and the city **O**, and beyond them convoys guard against pirates, monsters, and storms.

- **Exports:** metals, fabrics, farm produce, salt
- **Imports:** sulphur, timber, precious metals, spices, luxuries

### Convoys and Trade Routes

The **sulphur convoy** sails to Marjura each spring and fall. Roughly three convoys a year go to Melukha, and one, the **Kopparkonvojen**, continues on into the Copper Sea toward Caddo, Erebos, and beyond. About every other year a dangerous but very profitable **demontrade** runs east through barbarian waters to Demontungan, returning with eastern spices and Kamsun paper.

## Military

The army is a patchwork of mercenary forces, hirds, roteknektar, folk-militia, and private armies.

### Mercenary Companies

The mercenary companies are hired out by a **condottiere** who contracts to the best offer and pays his own men, who own their gear. The advantages are clear: the people stay productive, and a disarmed populace rebels less. The drawbacks are loyalty - mercenaries may switch sides if unpaid or over-endangered, though a disloyal condottiere soon wins no contracts. Trakorien hires many small armies rather than a few large ones, so no condottiere grows too powerful, and seeds them with spies from the feared secret police, the **Digeta Longa**, the law's long finger. Masterless mercenary companies can turn dangerous, raiding villages and ransoming towns; some condottieres have set themselves up as petty princes.

### Hirder

The realm's own professional troops serve under the Bodåken and emperor, used where mercenaries are not trusted, such as defense of the capital. They are led by a **hirdman** or **storhirdman**, and only nobles from Paratorna and Palamux may hold the rank.

### Roteknektar

Local men maintained by a *rote*, a group of households. They keep village order, town-watch, and tax-collection under the local *fogde*.

### Folkmilis

Common folk trained quickly in emergencies, often used by condottieres to soak up the enemy's worst missiles and spells - a tactic called the "black opening," considered dishonorable.

### Private Armies

Nobles with their own estates may keep a small private army, earning tax breaks if they lend it to the realm. Such forces are often well equipped but poorly trained, and unpopular.

### Flotta

The war-fleet is mostly state-owned under **Mana Phena**, with condottiere-style units common as privateers and pirate-hunters.

## Religion

No single religion has ever dominated Trakorien, so most are tolerated. The great theological debate is whether the world truly exists or is mere illusion.

### Arketypiska gudar

The archetypal gods, standard deities under various names inherited from Jorpagna. They include **Enki**, Lord of Freshwater, wisdom, and tradition; **Anxalis**, the fickle Queen of the Sea-depths, served by the **Ambaqua** order of wandering monks; **Tiamat** of primal chaos; **Marduk** of storms and weather; **Ezgela** of clouds; **Luvena** of the moon, unfairly tied to disease and creeping death, with secret moon-cults of necromancy, human sacrifice, and cannibalism especially on Palamux; **Ereshkigal** of the death-realm; **Valliman & Drigel**, guardians of the sun; and **Tigwalwan** of light, protector of the sick.

### Shamash

The god of truth, once a minor Jorpagna god, now central to a religious revival on Palamux after the salting of Moskorien and Fokale and the wars that followed. A stretch of south Moskorien became the religious state of the **Heliga Kishatet**. Priests teach that the world is full of false images, and the righteous keep to sincere faith while shunning hypocrisy, vanity, seduction, and lies. Worship is ascetic, built on study, meditation, and tithes of ten to thirty percent. Its symbols are the circle, the clear crystal, and the water-drop, and its priests wear brown-and-white robes. In the Kishatet, travelers meet endless rules: no public laughter, and mirrors, images, and decorations are banned, confiscated or tarred over at the border.

### Inashtar

Originally from the northeastern ice-barbarians, Inashtar has long been the special goddess of the **rungler** and all of Trinsmyra. She is goddess of fire, war, and unbridled lusts, depicted as a devastatingly beautiful armored woman amid wild flames, her symbols a hawk-headed dove and a battle-horn strung with harp-strings. She is worshipped ecstatically with huge bonfires, wild music and dance, orgies, and fighting-games, and has no organized church, for all women are held to share **Inashavi**, part of the goddess herself - hence the strong position of women on Trinsmyra. Splinter sects include the dreaded **RhabdoRana**, the brotherhood of the striped frog, assassins worshipping the demon **Ghumgakk**, the Avenger with the Black Lips, a professional murderer-guild that has terrorized northern Ereb since around 150 eO. Gentler by far is the cult of the **little leather-fairy**, queen of the **astrilder**, the tiny and mostly invisible love-fairies.

### Kastyke

Paratorna's official patron, found among the native **skecker** as a huntress-goddess. Her tenet is that life is a struggle and one must never give up: "Kastyke loves the one who loves himself!" From the start the merchants made her cult their own, and her priests are really business-agents who rattle spirit-rattles to keep up appearances, many having bought their priestly rights with gold.

### Remuntra

The strangest and most feared faith of all. It arose in **Krindenmaar** on Saphyna, where a tinker named **Klastobrest Dimvett** was seized by an irresistible calling to preach. He reached Lasemos in 349 eO, gathered an inexplicably large following of nobles and learned men, and sailed west "as a tribute to the god Remuntra" - half of Lasemos's fleet followed him, and none ever returned. Remuntra's only intelligible command is to learn to distrust the world and all within it, so that one may leave it laughing at life's end, an idea that questions itself and tangles the logic, for "logic is a prison for the inner self." A worshipper shaves the head to five tufts, one over each ear, one at the forehead, one at the nape, and one upright on the crown, then wraps the body in cloth hung with junk: rope-ends, fish-heads, a mug of mud, a broken wheel, a reed. All Trakorien dreads the cult, for it spreads like a disease and its god is never named. If Remuntra-worshippers appear, you give them what they want and get rid of them quickly.

## History

The story begins with the Third Conflux of 599 to 598 fO and the fall of Jorpagna, which had just colonized **Lasemos** and **Laabne** on west Palamux.

- **The Dark Age (550-250 fO)**: the colonists warred constantly. Mages seized Laabne around 380 fO and founded Trakorien's first academy in **Voag Aspede**, which drained Lasemos like a leech, until **Baskimer Brushane** overthrew the "grey eminences" and banned all magic in Lasemos.
- **The Klavyker (from c. 290 fO)**: friendship with the Gwondel Leigå elves let colonists reach the Moskoriska plain. They named themselves **klavyker**, from *klavys*, key, for they had unlocked a promised land.
- **The Klavykiska krigen (130-68 fO)**: the wars over Moskorien forged a lasting alliance with the giant tolgulderna, and the Klavykiska riket was born in 68 fO.
- **Kristallens sekler (50 fO-150 eO)**: failed efforts to cleanse **Kargom** of moon-worshippers, necromancy, and svartfolk. Colonies spread to **Stegos**, **Paratorna**, **Bzegusta**, **Torilia**, and the city O. When the holy **Kornella** lost O fighting Ransard, a revelation from Anxalis told the klavyker to stay on the islands - the origin of Trakorian reluctance to settle the mainland.
- **The salting of Palamux**: irrigation left salt and silt that ruined Fokale and Moskorien, the palamoxer's great sorrow.
- **The First Ransarder War (180-200 eO)**: ended in klavyker victory at Rubakins vik in 199.
- **The galna milackerna (220-300)**: the mad milacker of Trinsmyra nearly overran Palamux, their fast **katremasser** burning the heavy fleet with **vildeld**, before being beaten back.
- **The Kastyke Conversion (304 eO)**: freed Paratorna from the weakened Klavykiska riket. The new nation took the name **Trakorien**, from *traekor*, "to breathe freely," and installed Kastyke as its patron.
- **The Heliga Kishatet founded (311)**.
- **The Second Ransarder War (370-385)**: repelled with the new Kishatet's help. Trakorien declared itself heir to the Klavykiska riket, the **Trakoriska riket**, cleared Paratorna of svartfolk by around 460, and conquered **Ilibaurien** in 460 without battle, using the alchemist **Astrumal diFoldt**'s opianderkrydda. The old klavyker colonies were absorbed.
- **The Third Ransarder War (497-507)**: drove Ransard off Saphyna.
- **The Saphyna revolt (520-525)**: fought with drug-zombified troops, the **zombin**, who obeyed any order, including the enemy's. Trakorien won through the loud-voiced herald **Skrynk** at "Ylarnas afton," then annexed all of Saphyna.
- **The invasion of Kark (530)**: broke south Trinsmyra. The ruling **Eel-skulls** fled into the unconquerable **Rung** mountains, while Milacke kept its independence.
- **The occupation of Arhem on Marjura (595)**: secured the realm's sulphur.
- **610 eO**: the sulphur-winter ends and the kingdom **Cruri** can emerge from the ice once more - how this will end remains to be seen.

## Foreign Relations

Trakorien guards its islands jealously and remains famously reluctant to settle the mainland, a caution rooted in the revelation of Anxalis that bid the klavyker stay on their shores. Its reach abroad is carried by trade and treaty rather than conquest. The **Mana Extenda**, the Realm's Long Arm, steers foreign policy, while convoys, privateers, and the long-running rivalry with Ransard shape relations across the seas. Eastern voyages of the demontrade and the copper-runs of the Kopparkonvojen bind Trakorien to distant Demontungan, Melukha, and the powers of the Copper Sea.
