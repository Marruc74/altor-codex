# Trakorien

![Trakorien](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Trakorien1.jpg "Trakorien")
![Trakorien male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Male1.jpg "Trakorien male")
![Trakorien female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Trakorien/Female1.jpg "Trakorien female")

Trakorien lies far to the north, yet its heart beats with the rhythm of distant seas and southern markets. Ships arrive daily, carrying spices, metals, and whispers from lands most citizens will never see. Along its narrow harbor streets, merchants speak in many tongues, and power is measured not in crowns, but in contracts and alliances.

The realm is a loosely held maritime empire spread across four islands, each with its own nature. Fertile **Saphyna** feeds the population, while rugged **Trinsmyra** breeds harder folk shaped by conflict and isolation. **Palamux** holds ancient temples and quiet dangers, and **Paratorna** stands at the center of it all, where the capital **Tricilve** gathers wealth, ambition, and intrigue.

Though an emperor rules, true authority is shared with the great noble council, where decisions are weighed carefully and influence flows like trade itself. Soldiers fight for coin, fleets guard the lifeblood of the realm, and beyond the cities, old beliefs and stranger cults question the very nature of reality - none more dreaded than the contagious cult of **Remuntra**.

Trakorien endures because it adapts, balancing north and south, tradition and change, always looking toward the next horizon.

## Overview

A maritime merchant-oligarchy of four islands, bound together by trade, treaty, and a shared dread of the open sea's many enemies. Wealth is its language and its army, and few realms in Ereb are richer.

- **Capital:** Tricilve (~9,000 inhabitants), on Paratorna
- **Islands:** Paratorna, Palamux, Saphyna, and Trinsmyra
- **Government:** Elective empire; a life-elected emperor shares power with the great noble council, the **Bodaken**
- **Ruler:** An emperor chosen for life, never by blood
- **Peoples:** Trakorians of jori stock, with barbosker, tolgulder giant-folk, dwarves, elves, seal-folk, bird-men, and dark folk of the wilds
- **Religion:** Five great faiths and many common gods; no single church dominates
- **Exports:** sulfur, sylvoleum, metals, cloth, salt, farm produce
- **Imports:** timber, precious metals, spices, luxuries
- **Tongues:** the island-jori dialects **Muskari** (Palamux) and **Bralgiska** (Paratorna), plus **Karkiska** (Trinsmyra)

## The Four Islands

Trakorien is built upon four great islands, each older than the empire that binds them and each holding fast to its own temper. Each has a page of its own, listed under Trakorien in the codex and as cards below.

- **Paratorna** - the heart of the realm and the seat of its power, ruled from the capital Tricilve.
- **Palamux** - the oldest and most populous island, the mother island, where statecraft is preferred to the sword and the Holy Kishatet keeps its truth-god's law.
- **Saphyna** - the southernmost island and the realm's breadbasket, fertile and fairly peaceful, with the warped land of Krindenland along its western reach.
- **Trinsmyra** - the northernmost island, rocky, music-loving and never wholly subdued, rich in metals and proud of its independence.

## People

Many peoples share the four islands, woven together under the empire's law.

- **Trakorians**: the jori-descended majority, merchants and seafarers; the proud palamoxer and paratorner are the largest stocks, the good-natured saphyner the most often raided.
- **The barbosker**: the proud, hairy, bearded barbarian-kin of Ziddis on Palamux, hardy and quarrelsome.
- **The tolgulder**: a giant human-kin who worship the sun and the earth-spirit, travel only barefoot and on foot, and dread enclosed spaces; they ride to war with the giant-folk sabre.
- **The dwarves**: who fled slavery to found their own realm in the Copper Mountains, and who dwell in friendship with the warlike rungler at Ard Gachlaf on Trinsmyra.
- **The elves of Gwondel Leigu**: an ancient folk of the oak forests of Palamux, long allied to the klavyker colonists.
- **The seal-folk**: a shapeshifting elven kind of the coasts and isles of Paratorna, who take the shape of seals upon the water.
- **The bird-men**: intelligent feathered humanoids of the Ooram forest, who cannot work metal and dread enclosed spaces, fighting with knife and spear.
- **The dark folk of the wilds**: orcs, dark elves, and goblins of the mountains and the ill-famed north, hunted from the settled lands but never wholly destroyed.

## Government

Trakorien is governed by an emperor chosen for life and the great noble council, the **Bodaken**. The form was borrowed from old Palamux but is far less rigid, and it grew out of three phases of rule: the feudal principalities of Lasemos, the life-emperor's rule under the klavyker realm, and at last the merchant oligarchy born of the Kastykan Conversion, shaped by cold thinkers such as **Makulon Nihivelli**, who held that nothing is holy, only more or less expedient.

The emperor is elected for life, for the title is never inherited, and his life may prove short if discontent rises. He sits on every working group of the council and holds a fivefold vote in the Bodaken. A folk-assembly of rich burghers wields a limited veto over the council's decisions.

From the council are detached smaller working groups, the **limbs** of the many-armed goddess, each governing one sphere of the realm.

- **Mana Xifa** (the Sword-hand): defence
- **Mana Friga** (the Cold-hand): law and justice
- **Poda Arux** (the Gold-foot): finances
- **Mana Extenda** (the Realm's Long Arm): foreign policy
- **Mana Phena** (the Sea-hand): the navy
- **Poda Snica** (the Fore-foot): trade

Sub-divisions of these limbs are called fingers and toes. Provinces descend from a chief procurator down through procurators, provincial lords, and local reeves. Order is kept by a patchwork of armed forces: **mercenary legions** hired through a kondottiar, the realm's own professional **state hirds** under the council and emperor, the local **levies** of village and town, and the **private armies** of the great nobles. Threading through them all is the secret police, the **Digeta Longa**, the long finger of the law, whose severed-finger token is a warning every Trakorian understands.

## Religion

No single faith has ever dominated Trakorien, so most are tolerated, and the great theological quarrel is whether the world truly exists or is mere illusion. God-granted powers are believed real, and temples are served by an elaborate order of priests, singers, dream-readers, and temple courtesans.

### Shamash

The god of truth, central to the ascetic theocracy of the **Holy Kishatet** on Palamux, ruled by the god-king **Shar Kishati'n** and his thirteen crystal-lords. The faithful shun hypocrisy, vanity, seduction, and lies, keeping to study, meditation, and heavy tithes. Mirrors, images, and decorations are forbidden across the Kishatet, confiscated or tarred over at its border, and even public laughter is banned. Its symbols are the circle, the clear crystal, and the water-drop.

### Inashtar

The goddess of fire, war, and unbridled lust, special patron of **Trinsmyra**, depicted as a beautiful armored woman amid wild flames. She is worshipped ecstatically with bonfires, music, dance, and fighting-games, and has no organized church, for all women are held to share in the goddess herself, which gives women a strong standing on Trinsmyra. Her splinter sects include a dreaded brotherhood of assassins and the gentle cult of the little leather-fairy, queen of the tiny love-spirits.

### Kastyke

The self-loving huntress, official patron of **Paratorna** and protector of the whole realm, whose tenet is that one must never give up: Kastyke loves the one who loves himself. From the first the merchants made her cult their own, and her priests are in truth business-agents who keep up the trappings of faith for appearance's sake.

### Remuntra

The strangest and most feared faith of all, which arose in warped Krindenland and was carried into the world by the prophet **Klastobrest Dimvett**. It spreads like a contagion of the mind, and its only clear command is to learn to distrust the world so wholly that one may leave it laughing at life's end. Worshippers shave the head to five tufts and hang themselves with junk and refuse. All Trakorien dreads them, for the faith catches like a disease, and the wise give a Remuntra-worshipper what he wants and send him swiftly on his way.

### Trocuspa

The war-god whose army waits for the final battle against chaos beyond the world's edge. His worship is bound up with the martial art **banzikan**, the correct warrior's mindset, taught at the temple-schools and symbolized by oak, stone, and seed.

Beneath the five great faiths thrive a host of common gods inherited from old Jorpagna: **Enki**, lord of fresh water and wisdom; **Anxalis**, fickle queen of the sea-depths; **Marduk** of storms; **Luvena**, the moon-goddess shadowed by secret cannibal cults; and many more besides.

## Languages

The everyday tongues of the realm are the island-jori dialects, descended from the imperial **jori** once spoken across central Ereb and now the common trade-tongue of the seas. On Palamux they speak **Muskari**, on Paratorna **Bralgiska**, while Trinsmyra keeps **Karkiska**, a blend of jori with the older highland speech. Mutual understanding follows how close two tongues sit on the family tree. Beyond these are the barbarian tongues of Ziddis and the northern ice-folk, and the elvish **satenu**, which elves the world over understand alike and which learned men use as a lingua franca.

## Calendar and Climate

The Trakorian year is reckoned in twelve months: Persil, Gyrsag, Fateska, Iskamsi, Misiska, Minde, Melendo, Byrdo, Giren, Taragan, Nusin, and Tannan. The four islands span four land-climates, from the parched steppe of inner Fokale, through the temperate inland of Paratorna and Saphyna, to the wet coasts of Trinsmyra and southwest Palamux, and at last the wild storm-belt of the northwest. Three great wind-zones govern the surrounding seas.

## Money and Trade

Trade is the lifeblood of Trakorien, and its coin is trusted across the seas. The gold **trakin** is struck by the empire, by the guilds, and by private houses alike, each with its own purity and reputation; below it run the silver **decaur**, a tenth of a gold trakin, and the shapeless copper **marackel**. Documents are sealed in red-clay boxes or written on parchment and witnessed by a notary, and the great trading houses issue their own banknotes for a fee.

The realm's wealth rides in great convoys. The **sulfur convoy** sails to the colony of Marjura each spring and autumn; three convoys a year run to faithful **Melukha**; the **Copper convoy** presses on into the Copper Sea; and every other year the perilous **demon-trade** ventures east through barbarian waters, returning with eastern spices for great profit and greater risk.

These convoys are carried by many kinds of ship: swift **catamaran raiders**, weak in heavy seas; the **floating fortresses** built for sea-battle; the oared **war-galleys** rowed by convicts; and the common **merchantman** that does the everyday work of the trade. Above them all drift the **airships**, the zepelonder, their envelopes lifted by sylvoleum burners and towed by trained seabirds and winged beasts, an expensive pastime of the Paratornian nobility.

## Colonies and Neighbours

Trakorien guards its islands jealously and is famously reluctant to settle the mainland, holding only a handful of footholds abroad. Its colonies are the ice-bound sulfur isle of **Marjura**, the freed land of **Bzegusta**, and the small mainland holding of **Torilia**.

Across the seas lie many powers. **Mereld** is a free republic of elemental magicians; **Melukha** is the realm's oldest and most reliable ally, bound by an ancient rescue at sea; **Magilre** is a feudal land of skilled diplomats; and **Klomellien** is a league of free craft-cities. The kingdom of **Ransard** is the realm's bitter arch-enemy, against whom three great wars have been fought. The lawless free city of **Staden O** trades with all and answers to none, and far in the cold north dwell the blue-skinned ice-folk of **Sanithsid**, who sell winter furs and the noble ice that cools the tables of Tricilve.

## Creatures

The seas and wilds of Trakorien hold many strange beasts.

- **Raugoner**: sea-dwelling insectoids that build floating saliva-nests and live in castes of queens, venomous warriors, swimmers, and workers; they sink and board ships and speak through sound, scent, and antennae.
- **Mustelon**: a huge golden-brown marten-predator that kills for pleasure, swift and stealthy, a terror of the eastern mountains.
- **Skolopod**: a giant millipede up to thirty metres long, ridden by the folk of Rung from saddles set along its length, which lays its eggs in the carcasses of the fjords.
- **The six-legged battle-rhino**: a great six-legged beast bred as a draught-animal for the heavy war-chariots of Moskorien.
- **The monsters of the Swamp of Shadows**: among them a giant blood-fly whose bite breeds creeping madness, a grey telepath that forces nightmares upon its prey, and a grasping spider-vine that snares and poisons the unwary.

## History

> Dates are reckoned in **fO** (before Odo) and **eO** (after Odo). The present day is **610 eO**, when the eight-hundred-year sulfur winter ends.

- **The Third Conflux (599-598 fO)**: the fall of the empire Jorpagna, which had just colonized Lasemos and Laabne on west Palamux.
- **The kingdom of Cruri (598-250 fO)**: a realm of necromancy and demon-worship on Marjura, cast there long before as punishment for ungodly magic, which enslaved the wild tribes and spread its colonies across the northern isles.
- **The Dark Age (550-250 fO)**: the jori colonists of Lasemos warred without cease.
- **The grey eminences (310-250 fO)**: magicians seized Laabne and built the realm's first academy at Voag Aspede, draining Lasemos like a leech, until **Baskimer Brushane** overthrew them, united Lasemos, and banned all magic there.
- **The fall of Cruri (~200 fO)**: the gods doomed the demon-kingdom and sealed its island beneath the ice, beginning the eight-hundred-year sulfur winter.
- **The klavyker wars (130-68 fO)**: colonists, allied with the Gwondel Leigu elves and at last the giant tolgulder, won the plains of Moskorien and named themselves klavyker, the key-folk, for they had unlocked a promised land.
- **The klavyker realm (from 68 fO)**: born when an illusionist hid an army at the festival of HOXOH and the old nobility of Nastrol were cut down; the new empire rose with its capital at Fontra Cilor and its council, the Bodaken.
- **The Crystal Centuries (50 fO-150 eO)**: long and failing efforts to cleanse the ill-famed north of necromancy and dark folk, even as colonies spread to Stegos, Paratorna, Bzegusta, Torilia, and the city O.
- **The Voyage of the Holy Swans (year 0, eO)**: the klavyker rescued thousands from sinking Kymm, forging an eternal friendship with Melukha.
- **The First Ransard War (180-200 eO)**: ended in klavyker victory at the sea-battle of Rubakin's bay.
- **The Mad Milacker (220-300 eO)**: Trinsmyra under the dictator **Hiatus Neffro** nearly overran Palamux, its swift catamaran raiders burning the heavy fleet with the dreaded incendiary **vildeld**, before it was beaten back.
- **The Kastykan Conversion (304 eO)**: Paratorna threw off the weakened klavyker realm and took the name **Trakorien**, "to breathe freely," with Kastyke as its patron and protector.
- **The Holy Kishatet founded (311 eO)**: the truth-god Shamash's theocracy took root on Palamux.
- **The coming of Remuntra (349-350 eO)**: Klastobrest Dimvett preached the contagious faith and sailed west with half of Lasemos's fleet, never to return.
- **The Second Ransard War (370-385 eO)**: repelled with the new Kishatet's help; Soblak was ceded to Trakorien.
- **The development age (385-450 eO)**: the **Trakoriska riket** was founded as heir to the klavyker realm, waging a war of extermination against the dark folk; Paratorna was cleared and Ilibaurien conquered without a battle through Astrumal diFolt's drug opiander.
- **The Aalskalle line (405-530 eO)**: at the council of Vumbra **Ganfryd Aalskalle** declared himself king of Trinsmyra, founding a royal house that would resist the empire to the end.
- **The eclipse of Stegos (440 eO)**: in punishment for betraying the Kishatet, Shamash cast the island of Stegos into permanent darkness.
- **The Third Ransard War (497-507 eO)**: drove Ransard from Saphyna, incorporating the Gudiena plain.
- **The Saphyna revolt (520-525 eO)**: fought with troops drugged on zombin who obeyed any order given them, and won at last through a loud-voiced herald at the battle of Ylarna's eve, after which all Saphyna was annexed.
- **The invasion of Kark (530 eO)**: broke southern Trinsmyra; the Aalskalle line fled into the unconquerable mountain-land of Rung, while Milacke kept its freedom.
- **The occupation of Arhem (595 eO)**: secured the realm's sulfur on Marjura.
- **The end of the sulfur winter (610 eO)**: the eight-hundred-year winter lifts and the buried kingdom of Cruri may rise once more from the ice; how it will end, none can yet say.

## Culture and Curiosities

For all its talk of contracts and convoys, Trakorien is a land of strong appetites and stranger pastimes.

The realm's favourite game is **smickelbräde**, a board-game that mixes cool tactics with sudden violence: by the rule called *hoppsmickel*, a player whose piece is captured may keep half of it if he can cleave it with his sword before the points are counted - "he who has no fingers shall not stay in the game." Among the white ducks of Ruhl the street-theatre **kommedia dell'anco** is played with its fixed cast - the verse-spouting old scholar Coprostomba, the cunning hero Kvalbak, the villainous black duck Yaggamuk, the lovers Mesurinn and Nindada, the fool Prolzo, and the wolf-man Okrof. Cruelest of the public shows is the **Fate Lottery**, in which Kastyke's Pillar-Father draws the name of a noble who is then made an outlaw - a lesson, it is said, in the caprice of the gods, though a wise man may quietly buy his name out of the urn. And in Tricilve the **Freedom Carnival** stages old battles in miniature, gladiator-fights, ballads and the great ritual dramas.

Trakorian tables are as particular as its games. Through the winters of Saphyna and Paratorna folk drink **coffa**, a stimulating red-brown brew from the dried berries of the coffa-bush, and on the Frimboliner Plain they favour a thick, malty black beer. The boldest gourmands eat the **tetroderkarp**, a delicious fish of the Trakorian Sea whose innards are deadly poison - a true connoisseur, they say, treats himself to it once in his life and dies happy. The hideous **isterulk** must be soured before it can be stomached, but one fish will feed three for a week. The grimmest fare of all is the "Shamash-cooking" of the truth-cult: tough old ox-meat watered with tears, by design never quite enough to fill the belly.

Among the realm's prized goods are **algsilke**, a cloth spun from cultured algae in the dam-farms of Bralorge; **ziddisgranit**, a green-blue stone that breaks in clean right angles and graced the proud buildings of the great age; and **kamsunsk papir**, a paper-cloth so fragile it allows nothing but idleness, sewn into throwaway finery and worn only by the very rich. Even the coinage carries scent: the **skråtrakiner** struck by the guilds are perfumed by secret arts with the smell of their trade, a fragrance that lingers for years and that a sharp tongue can taste out.

The four islands hold one another in cheerful contempt, as a Tricilve saying has it: "when the grain runs short, send nuts to Saphyna and soldiers to Trinsmyra, but to Palamux a thinker, for the palamoxer can chew on his brooding and be fed by it." The earthy, patient **saphyners** are looked down on by their neighbours, yet bear more than their share of **silverbarn**, uncanny wonder-children. The high-born **paratorner** make an art of the insult, scorn modesty as weakness, and drape themselves in impractical kamsunsk papir. The **palamoxer** are brooders and thinkers, their tempers darkened by the slow salting of their soil; the music-loving **trinsmyrer** distrust all overlords on principle; and the **karker** are famous for foul moods and a speech so rich in curses it is said to suit poetry and damnation alike.

## Heraldry

The arms of Trakorien bear a ship, a tower, and a gold coin. The ship stands for the trade that is the lifeblood of the island empire, the tower for its strength, and the gold coin for the prosperity that flows from its convoys and markets.
