# Caddo

![Caddo](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Caddo/Caddo1.jpg "Caddo")
![Caddo male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Caddo/Male1.jpg "Caddo male")
![Caddo female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Caddo/Female1.jpg "Caddo female")

Caddo lies along the bright waters of the Copper Sea, a long volcanic island shaped by trade, wind, and a way of seeing the world that few outsiders understand. Its cities rise from pale volcanic stone, strong and practical, built for merchants who value coin above crown. Ships arrive daily, heavy with goods, and leave just as full, carrying the island's influence far beyond its shores.

What makes Caddo truly strange is not its wealth, but its people. The **dalker**, who make up nearly all of the population, are born without the ability to see colour as others do. Over generations they built their world without regard for harmony of colour, choosing fabrics, banners, and patterns for form and meaning rather than appearance. To them, their cities are ordered and beautiful. To visitors, the garish combinations are overwhelming and disorienting.

Yet beneath this confusion lies precision. Trade is measured carefully, agreements are honoured, and every citizen is expected to defend the island if needed. In Caddo, perception differs, but purpose remains clear.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 100,000 (humans 97%, *ankor* duckfolk 3%)
- **Capital:** Arno (~7,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** council republic of 37 cantons
- **Ruler:** none - no head of state
- **Religion:** a conservative branch of the **Shining Way**
- **Exports:** ships, grain, wine, handicrafts, leather and jewel work
- **Imports:** timber, metals, salt
- **Military:** small standing army, very strong militia levy, strong navy

## Geography

A long island split lengthwise by the **Appobergen**, a range rising up to about 3,000 metres and studded with many extinct volcanic cones. The mountains catch the sea winds, watering the slopes and keeping them fertile, while their interior holds the only untouched wilderness on the island.

- The mountain regions are barren and sparsely peopled, acting as a rain barrier
- Fertile, gently rolling coastal plains lie to the north and south, where nearly everyone lives
- The plains are near overpopulated, intensively farmed for wheat and olives
- Only the high mountains remain wild

## People

Caddo is almost entirely inhabited by the **dalker** (dalcoi, "island folk"). Tall and fair-haired, blue or grey-eyed, and deeply sun-tanned beneath their island sun, they are an energetic people, industrious almost to a fault, and superb craftsmen, merchants and shipbuilders. Of unknown origin, they are unrelated to the *jorer*. They prize equality above all, judging a person by conduct and office rather than inherited rank - a habit that irritates visiting nobles. They dress mostly in light hose and a short, long-sleeved jacket of velvet or silk.

Every dalk is wholly colour-blind. They are well adapted to their sunny climate, and their world is built without thought for the colour pairings that visitors find nauseating.

## Culture

Hard-working to the point of being exhaustingly energetic, the dalker fill their days with craft, commerce and shipbuilding. Archery is the great national sport, pursued with such devotion that Caddish longbowmen are prized as mercenaries across the Copper Sea. They value freedom and self-governance, and stand ready to defend their land.

## Language

The dalker speak **dalkiska**, a unique language isolate unrelated to any other tongue in the region. Most inhabitants speak only dalkiska, and few foreigners ever learn it, which keeps the island culturally insular even as its ships range across all of Ereb.

## Government

Caddo is a council republic with no head of state, born from the civil war *the Fate-Feud* (104-111 eO) and stable and popular for centuries since. The island is divided into 37 cantons, each governed by its own assembly.

- Each canton holds a *canton assembly*, a gathering of all adult taxpaying men and women, roughly 35% of the population
- The **land council**, a national council of elected canton representatives, controls foreign policy, the standing army, great public works, the courier service and inter-canton disputes
- Laws passed by the land council must be ratified by two-thirds of the canton assemblies

A military coup is essentially impossible under this system. The chief tension lies between the 12 merchant-city cantons and the rural cantons - farmers dominate the council, while the cities enjoy strong self-rule.

## Economy

Caddo is a major trading nation dealing with all corners of Ereb. It imports salt, timber, grain and metals, and exports ships, grain, wine, handicrafts, leather and fine jewel work.

Its banks are the most trusted in all Ereb, owing to the island's political stability, its isolation and safety from raids, and the dalkers' reputation for honesty - though they charge high interest. The leading banking families are the **Medicini** clan and the duck-house **Hugger**, who together hold great sway over Copper Sea trade.

## Military

The army has three traditional parts. It is weak in cavalry but its archers are superb and sought after as mercenaries.

- **levy** - the general folk-muster; every adult dalk man must own weapons and armour and train yearly, carrying standardized arms of two throwing spears and a one-handed spear, or a bow and battleaxe, plus a shield
- **land-watch** - professional patrol troops armed with battleaxe, composite bow and medium shield, in mail or scale
- **realm-host** and **realm-fleet** - the national army and fleet, defending Caddo and its colonies

## Religion

The **Shining Way** wholly dominates the island and is the only permitted faith, here in a conservative form. The church holds strong influence in society and forbids all magic as contrary to the faith. Magic is therefore completely outlawed within the realm, though it is still practiced in secret.

## History

- **312 fO**: **Vasker Bruushane**, a descendant of the *jorer*, unites the dalker tribes and is made king
- **102 eO**: the Bruushane line dies out, plunging the island into civil war
- **111 eO**: the warlord **Gerlo the Tall** gathers the tribes at the **Council of the Long Noses**; the **Arno Oath**, swearing never again to take up arms against kin, founds the republic
- **200s eO**: trade with the Eastlands makes Caddo a great power; it joins the Shining Way's crusades against **Morälvidyn**, helping to begin the Great Schism

## Foreign Relations

Caddo lives in chronic rivalry with the other trading states, chiefly **Erebos**, over mainland trade. It has long felt friction with **Zorakin** over that realm's conquest of *Fristaden*, which disrupted dwarven commerce and led Caddo and the dwarves to found the city **Ereno** further north. At present the island is drawn into conflict against **Felicien** over masked piracy, allied with **Berendien** and, reluctantly, with **Erebos**.
