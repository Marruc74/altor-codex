Cereval is a land shaped by distance and survival, where wind moves freely across dry plains and every road carries both trade and risk. Caravans travel slowly through the savanna, guided by those who know the land well, bringing goods between scattered settlements of stone and canvas. These travelers are not wanderers but lifelines, connecting people across a harsh and unforgiving region.

Beyond the open plains rise forests where trolls live openly as part of the land's balance, powerful and watchful. Deeper still, the mountains hold veins of moonsilver, a metal prized and feared in equal measure. High above all this, the **dragonmasters** move along the peaks and skies, distant yet ever present, a reminder that Cereval is never ruled by one force alone.

Known to its neighbours as "the Dragons' Realm", Cereval holds the largest concentration of dragons anywhere in Ereb, yet its people live in relative safety because those dragons are reined in by the dragonmasters and held in check by moonsilver. It is not a true state but a loose web of city-states, free heath-farmers and nomads, all bound together by the **Moon Law**. Here, humans endure, trolls belong, and dragons remain.

## Overview

- **Population:** approximately 80,000 (75% humans, 11% trolls, 14% svartfolk)
- **Capital:** none - the three city-states of Cerevallis, Drakonia and Lirvella each stand independent
- **Government:** independent city-states with no obvious ruling class, all subject to the Moon Law
- **Ruler:** none - final authority rests with the dragonmasters through the Moon Law
- **Religion:** several faiths; extensive nature worship, with a moon goddess and druids honoured in most towns
- **Exports:** fish, meat, alchemical items and elixirs
- **Imports:** timber and metals
- **Military:** no standing army, but a well-trained levy; the nomads and trolls are formidable warriors

Cereval is a poor land in coin, yet rich in the one substance no other realm possesses, and what makes it unusual is that its trolls hold real political weight despite being a minority.

## Geography

Cereval stretches from the **Cer-floden** in the north to the **Grynnerpasset** in the south, and from the **Cerevalbergen** in the west to **Masevabukten** in the east. Running from the sea inland, the land rises through lowlands, then forest, then mountains. The lowland itself is split by the deep **Troll Firth** into the **Dän Plain** in the north and the **Lir Plain** in the south.

### The Coastal Heath

- The coastal heath of open grass and scrub, over a thin and precious layer of topsoil
- Destroying this fragile soil is among the gravest of crimes, punished by blood sacrifice - usually of the offender
- The three true towns and city-states sit here: **Cerevallis**, **Drakonia** and **Lirvella**

### The Savanna and the Stone Deserts

- A dry plateau rising to roughly 500 metres
- Hunted by wild dogs, cliff lizards, sand demons and jaguars
- Home to cliff rabbits, cereval antelopes, rhinos and the great **elephant-birds**

### Tarassusravinen

- A ravine some twenty kilometres long, choked with poisonous thornbushes
- Holds very ancient ruins from the world's elder ages

### The Cer Forest

- A near-jungle of moss-covered ground, kept in twilight even at midday
- Home of the **trolls**, who cultivate forest ponds and pan its streams for moonsilver

### Cerevalbergen

- A high range whose peaks rise beyond 6,000 metres
- The dwelling place of the dragons and the mining ground of moonsilver
- By legend the range formed when a star fell to Altor; the star's heart still lies beneath the roots of the mountains, while its shattered shell now surfaces as moonsilver
- The dragonmasters dwell upon the very highest peaks

## Economy

Cereval is poor in the wealth other realms count, trading fish, meat and alchemical elixirs outward and drawing timber and metals in. Its true treasure is moonsilver, which by the Moon Law may never leave the land. Within Cereval the metal is worth twice its weight in gold, yet no one parts with it willingly - it changes hands only through barter, fines, or the moonsilver tax.

### Moonsilver

- A semi-magical metal found nowhere but Cereval, lying in lumps within soil and rock
- On the heath it is turned up by the plough; on the highland it glows faintly under moonlight; in the forest it is panned from streams
- Each lump is matte and bluish, ranging from the size of a pea to blocks of many kilos, the largest now rare and costly
- Easily worked, it makes tools that render heavy labour effortless, weapons lighter, sharper and harder, and armour stronger at half the weight
- In full moonlight, moonsilver weapons and armour become nearly invisible
- Heavy use weakens the metal, leaving it heavier, more brittle and dimmer; left in moonlight it recovers its strength
- Beyond Cereval's borders moonsilver slowly dies, turning blue-black and at last to dead, magicless powder, so it is worthless abroad
- The Moon Law forbids its export; smugglers are killed outright or handed to the dragonmasters

## Society

Cereval society is divided among three great groups of people, together with the trolls of the forest and the svartfolk of the mountain roots. Despite their differences, all are bound by the Moon Law, and open violence is rare - the chief exception being the endless feuding between the city-states.

### The Cerevalier

The three human groups make up some three-quarters of the realm's people. They tend to be below medium height, dark-haired and light-skinned, and are by nature quiet, honest and peaceful, for all of them respect the Moon Law.

### Free Heath-Farmers and Ranchers

- Tend the fragile coastal soil almost as an act of worship, since to destroy it means immediate execution
- Ranchers raise bison-like cattle and surgically implant **silver poison pouches** beneath the animals' hides, so that any dragon which roasts one is poisoned
- United on three things: care of the soil, their view of the dragons, and freedom from the city-states

### Nomads

- The original immigrants to Cereval, living in clans of thirty to sixty adults under a chieftain of absolute power
- Gather each year for clan-meetings of weddings, markets and trials, where no fighting is permitted
- Keep tame antelopes and elephant-birds, the latter used as dragon-lookouts, mounts, and for eggs and down
- Each clan keeps a moonsilver-smith and a shaman; the clan hunter carries a moonsilver sabre, a long side-spiked pike and a bola, and camps are guarded with slings that hurl two stones joined by tarred rope to entangle a dragon's wings

### City Dwellers

- Craftsmen, merchants, scholars and outlaws, mostly of barbarian descent
- The three great cities also hold small communities of dwarves, elves, ankor and halflings
- Outlaws may include those of svartfolk blood, but full-blooded orcs and reptile-men are killed or driven off

## Government

The Moon Law stands above all local law, and beneath it the peoples of Cereval govern themselves through their own councils and assemblies.

### The Moon Law

- The Moon Law, also called the Dragon Law, supreme over every local custom
- Its first purpose is that no moonsilver ever leaves the world
- It governs the storage, charging, export and taxing of moonsilver, the conduct between people and communities, the fines for breaches, and the surrender of criminals to the dragonmasters
- A guest in a strange land may swear the Oath of the Moon to gain safe passage, water and shelter, in return for helping defend the host if attacked
- Breaking the Law most often means a dishonourable execution in which even the offender's friends take part

### The Moon Moot

- The Moon Moot, held when the dragonmasters come to collect the moonsilver tax every one or two years
- Offenders may appeal here; a master reads their mind and judges finally, the losers usually taken away into the mountains
- Whole communities may be heavily fined in moonsilver, which the masters use to slowly reassemble the fallen star

### Jehennin

- An advisory council formed by the peoples to soften the dragonmasters' harsh fines
- Its rulings carry no binding force, but its counsel is heeded
- Led by a law-speaker, it gathers observers from all the peoples, even troll changelings

### The City-States

- Each city stands fully independent, ruled by a council of its most successful merchants and craftsmen
- Every council must include a moonsilver-smith and an expert in the Moon Law
- A council is led by a burgomaster, village elder or law-speaker

## Religion

Faith plays only a small part in Cereval life, and what worship exists is varied and generally tolerant, though dark temples are actively driven out.

- The dragonmasters are revered, regarded by some as gods and by others as immortals
- A moon goddess and her druids are honoured in most of the towns
- The trolls worship **the Wanderer**, seen in the sky as the constellation that humans call **Orcherion the Warrior**, believing they are reborn into it as they await the return of a missing star
- The nomads hold that trolls are the souls of nomads eaten by dragons, passing through the forest in troll-shape before they are reborn

## History

The history of Cereval runs back to a fallen star and the long contest of elves, trolls, dragons and men for its silver.

- **Legend**: A star fell to Altor; its forsaken people were bound to matter and charged to guard the star's heart, and so became the dragonmasters
- **3600 to 1700 fO**: The **moon-elves** build a glittering high culture upon moonsilver, driving off every rival
- **1700 to 1100 fO**: The trolls rise to a high culture of their own; neither side can drive out the other
- **From 1600 fO**: Dragons begin to gather and the dragonmasters appear; dragons by day and trolls by night wear down the proud moon-elves
- **1070 to 1040 fO**: The last moon-elf falls and their final hidden city vanishes from the world, a lost paradise its traps and the dragons still guard
- **950 to 600 fO**: The dragonmasters consolidate their power, the blue halo settles on the peaks, and the Moon Law is imposed
- **599 to 598 fO**: The Third Conflux ravages even Cereval
- **100 fO to 287 eO**: The Great Disorder, in which humans and trolls war until the dragonmasters intervene and all human groups accept the Moon Law
- **287 to 456 eO**: The human cities arise - Drakonia founded in 294 with its university in 304, Cerevallis in 357, Lirvella in 378; in 408 the Battle of the Troll Firth closes the fjord to humans forever; in 456 Jehennin is formed
- **530 to 550 eO**: The era of **Dird Ligamester** of Cerevallis, marked by great orc and barbarian invasions, the sieges of the cities, the relief of Drakonia by sea, and reconciliation over orc-looted moonsilver; in 549 the sea trolls destroy Lirvella's harbour
- **604 to 610 eO**: Huge moonsilver finds in the Tarassusravinen spark a silver rush, ancient moon-elf artifacts revive the legends of the lost city, and tension mounts anew between trolls, nomads and city-states

## The Trolls

The trolls of Cereval are far more advanced than the degenerate trolls found elsewhere in Ereb, likely from their constant contact with moonsilver. They are smaller and nimbler, with higher intelligence and richer social life. They do not dwell in cities but in the forests and caves, organised in communities and clans under their own law, and where the forest rules, troll law prevails. Humans both respect and fear them, and trade passes steadily between the two peoples.

### Forest Trolls

- The forest trolls, some four in five of all Cereval trolls
- Live in caves in communities of fifteen to forty
- Cultivate forest ponds, grow the white-green poisonous mushroom *trollhatt* for food, medicine, flour and a ceremonial drink, and raise giant toads, water-snakes and spiders
- Ruled by a ruler-troll and use svartfolk slaves to handle the fire they themselves fear

### Ruler-Trolls

- The ruler trolls, the crown of their kind - larger, stronger and smarter
- Born of ordinary trolls but lavishly educated, then choosing a calling as scholar, teacher, mage, warleader or ruler
- A ruler founds a new community that grows until the ruler dies, after which it dissolves

### Warrior Trolls

- The warrior trolls, bred by a dark-mage cult as a super-warrior race - huge and strong but dull of mind and weak in magic
- Lured over to the forest trolls, they form the core of troll armies under a ruler-troll warleader and forest-troll lieutenants

### Lesser Trolls

- The small trolls, halted in their growth by a strange condition, tinier than gnomes or elves
- Playful and mischievous, with stronger magic, living for pranks against gnomes, elves, travellers and even dignified dragons
- A dragon humiliated by a lesser troll may stay in a foul mood for years, and they are best handled with song, shiny things and food

### Sea Trolls

- The sea trolls, descended from lesser trolls that adapted to the water
- They grow throughout their lives, some sighted at around twenty metres long, and are formidable in their element
- Since 408 eO the Troll Firth has been forbidden to all human ships on their account

### Cave Trolls

- A few genuine cave trolls still linger in the mountains, much as they do elsewhere in the world

### Changelings

Changelings are human children swapped by troll magic for troll young and imprinted to look identical to the child they replace. They gain partial resistance to sun, fire and steel, grow larger and stronger, hold a natural talent for magic and far-reaching dark-vision, but can never father or bear children with humans, and after about a century they slowly take on troll traits. Raised in upper-class human families they may rise to fine careers as warriors, craftsmen or mages; raised poor they are often bullied into the slums. Those raised by trolls are cherished, educated, trained in magic, and serve as the trolls' loyal representatives, some even studying at the university of Drakonia.

## The Svartfolk

- An underground orc realm lies at the northern tip of the Cerevalbergen, its leaders orcs, with ogres and dark elves often kept as slaves
- Since the Moon Council of 401 eO, all svartfolk save Cereval's own trolls are outlawed and may be killed without penalty

## Dragons and Dragon Masters

The Cerevalbergen are the realm of the dragons and the strange humanoids who keep them, the foundation on which all of Cereval's uneasy peace rests.

### The Dragon Masters

- Humans call them the dragonmasters; the trolls name them roughly "the Forsaken People"
- Tall and sooty-furred, with long blond hair, pupil-less light-blue eyes, a prehensile gripping tail and retractable claws as hard as a dragon's own
- They dwell above the snowline among white trees that grow from the bare rock, into which criminals are transformed as punishment, meditating until their souls are purified and released
- They speak mind to mind, and are immortal in material form, though they can be slain by violence; when one dies, four masters create a new body from their own flesh, so their number never changes
- Masters of animism, elemental magic and symbolism, they fight chiefly with their spells, and it is they who keep and train the dragons

### The Dragons

- Kept in great caves lower on the mountains, where the young live and train together in combat and magic under a master, much as hounds with their hunter, the bond willing on both sides
- As a dragon nears maturity it seeks out its own cave and hoard, and most die in territorial fights with their kind
- A few bond truly with their master and learn to bear him aloft in flight, while many remain too independent and in time rebel
- Cereval's dragons are less long-lived than those elsewhere but develop twice as swiftly

## Heraldry

The arms of Cereval bear black crosses, set to stand for the unity of its many city-states - a single device drawing the independent towns of the heath together beneath one sign.
