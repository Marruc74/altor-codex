# Zorakin

Zorakin lies in the southern reaches of the Aidne Peninsula, where golden fields stretch beneath a steady sun and pale stone villages stand between forest and farmland. It is a land shaped by soil and sword alike, a fairly prosperous feudal kingdom descended from old Jorpagna and devoted body and soul to the Shining Way. Most people live simple lives tied to the harvest, yet above them stands a rigid order of nobles, each ruling their lands with near independence under a distant king. The common folk have little say in the running of the realm.

In Grindanu, the Toril Forest looms, changed by an ancient disaster, its depths feared and avoided. To the east, Indar feeds the kingdom with vast grain fields, making Zorakin one of the wealthier realms of the Copper Sea. Knights ride these lands as both protectors and symbols of power, their heavy armour and great war-stallions making them a formidable shock force ready to answer when war calls.

The people are divided. The Penner, strong and ambitious, dominate trade and rule. The Legerdier work the land, bound to it and to those above them. Faith in the Shining Way guides daily life, though it has not always united the kingdom. Old conflicts and religious division still echo beneath the surface.

Zorakin endures through balance. Between wealth and hardship, order and unrest, it remains a kingdom rooted in earth, tradition, and steel.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 250,000 (90% humans, 5% ankor, 4% elf-folk, 1% halflings)
- **Capital:** Pendon (~7,500 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Feudal monarchy, hereditary since 156 eO
- **Ruler:** Valien VII the Just Council, "the Just-counsel"
- **Religion:** The Shining Way
- **Exports:** grain, iron, timber, furs
- **Imports:** textiles, salt, precious metals
- **Military:** very strong knightly cavalry, strong church troops, weak fleet

## Geography

Zorakin lies in the southern part of the Aidne Peninsula and is a land of great regional contrasts. To the north rise the barren high fells of the Aidne Mountains, while in the south fertile plains roll down to the shores of the Copper Sea. Kardien borders the realm to the west, and the inlets of the Dakkilo Bay cut into the coast to the east.

The kingdom hangs upon two great peninsulas, Grindanu in the west and Indar in the east, joined only by a narrow coastal strip. Both are fertile, flat, and fairly populous, and together they form the "real" Zorakin. The northern reaches are too forested and mountainous for farming, so the folk there live instead by mining, hunting, and forestry.

### Grindanu

Roughly half forest and half cultivated land. The Toril Forest covers about half the peninsula. Once a bright, leafy wood, it turned dark and evil after dark folk came into it in the early 100s eO, and its depths are now feared and avoided. Grindanu's only great river is the cold, swift Jöstel, which empties into the vast Grimmani Marsh. About half of all Zorakin lives here, and the peninsula is more urban than Indar.

### Indar

Fertile, flat, and three-quarters under the plough. The slow-moving Sola and the Granström, together with a web of ditches, give Indar the largest harvests of any land on the Copper Sea. The over-worked western part has turned to cattle-raising, and windmills dot the windy plain. These mills serve a double purpose, for they are also semaphore towers, their sails set to signal across the open country and warn of invasion, of coming rain, and of the approach of tax-collectors and press-gangs alike. North of Indar, along the foot of the Aidne fells, lies the great Nordberg Forest, home mostly to forest-elves alongside humans, satyrs, and shy swan-maidens. Its eastern part, the duchy of Salamora, is held wholly by elves, who trade furs and herbs in exchange for being left in peace.

## Pendon

Pendon, the royal capital, is the largest city in either Zorakin or Kardien and the fifth-largest on all the Copper Sea. It began in 367 fO not as a town but as a monastery and lyceum, founded by **Leon Inergryd** of the **Mandelorden**, the scholar-monks of **Ingeril**, goddess of learning. Its fine harbour drew trade, the trade drew people, and by the time the kingdom of Zorakin was proclaimed the settlement had grown great enough to be made its capital.

The city is crown-land, so that the king owns its ground and takes its taxes, though its day-to-day governance falls to a city-marshal, a council, and six city secretaries. Alone among the realm's cities, the folk of Pendon hold the right to propose new laws, a privilege granted them by King Ragnvald the Fair. At its gates stands the **muraskatt**, a toll of about a silver mark on every traveller and beast that enters, from which only farmers and royal officials go free. Roughly a third of its people are merchants and a third craftsmen, and though its trade has slipped since the lost Sympathy War against Felicien and the rising boldness of the dark folk, it remains among the richest cities of the region.

### The Lyceum and the Library

Pendon is the seat of learning for the whole Copper Sea. Its royal library holds some ten thousand volumes and is the oldest of its kind anywhere on that sea, and the lyceum of the Mandelorden gathers about two hundred scholars and eight hundred students, who study history, languages, geography, magic, alchemy, and medicine.

### Defence and Layout

The city is built of stone and brick, its houses mostly two storeys high, and a great castle stands upon Kastelludden, the headland that once held the lyceum until pirate-ankor burned it in 55 fO. Long sandy shallows bar the harbour, forcing all cargo onto flat-bottomed barges, which helps the customs-men and frustrates smugglers. A city guard of four hundred, in four companies, keeps order, and two hundred picked men of the royal garde hold the king's palace. Weapons must be given up at the gate weapon-houses, and carrying arms inside the walls needs a royal writ, so crime is rare. At the King's Square stands the **Kora Cathedral**, nearly a hundred and thirty metres tall, the greatest Shining Way cathedral in Zorakin and the fourth-largest in the world, its doors set to face Arno, where Odo was born.

## People

Two peoples make up roughly three-quarters of the population, both of Joric descent, and together they are the "typical zorakier."

### Penner

A Joric folk of southern Grindanu, heirs of the pre-conflux inhabitants and of old Jorpagna culture. They speak a dialect close to the original Joric tongue. Since Valien Rikesbyggare, the Realm-builder, was himself a penner, his folk are heavily over-represented among the nobility, and most of Zorakin's upper class are of penner stock. Traditionally warriors and merchants, they are more cultivated than most Aidne folk and possess a singular sense for tactics and organisation. They are deeply devoted to the Shining Way, and Joric artifacts and architecture carry very high status among them.

### Legerdier

The folk of Indar, pre-conflux farmers struck hard by the cataclysm. They first formed a kind of collective farming, but each farm soon became a tiny isolated realm, leaving them terribly vulnerable in the lindskiarner wars. Those wars methodically devastated Indar, and afterward each farmer rebuilt his own land alone, binding him ever more tightly to it. This was the root of the serfdom that arose among them. Isolated and easily ruled, they fell wholly under the control of the penner nobility. Their farming is underdeveloped, saved only by extraordinarily fertile soil. They are a hard-cowed, deeply religious people, their priests entirely under noble control, and they speak a distinctive dialect with almost no short vowels.

## Government

Zorakin is a feudal society headed by a king, hereditary since 156 eO though originally an elective kingdom. In practice the realm is ruled by the nobles who once received their land from the crown. The population falls into three groups: about three-quarters live under a chain of baron, count, duke, and king; about a quarter are "crown-folk" under a markis answering directly to the king; and roughly one in twenty dwell in free cities under a burgomaster and city council. The Shining Way church owns about a tenth of all the land, most of it gifted by dying barons in exchange for masses.

### The Nobility

At the Great Assembly of 55 fO, King Valien rewarded the heavy cavalry who had fought the lindskiarner. Knights, the riddare, became barons each holding a few square kilometres; their better aides became counts holding five to twenty baronies; and his ten closest men became dukes holding three to five counties. Within his own fief a noble could make laws and levy taxes, but a lord who starved his people through over-taxation would be stripped of his fief and declared "the basest and most outlawed of creatures." A baron holds near-absolute power in his own land, setting laws and taxing some five to fifteen gold a year per farmer, plus tolls on his bridges, mills, wine-presses, and roads.

Fiefs were at first non-hereditary, the superior choosing each successor, but by tradition the eldest son usually took over, and after 100 eO the inheritance became near-automatic. Each new lord swears to "aid with counsel and deed," furnishing a set number of armoured warriors on demand, so that the king could once call on nearly a thousand knights besides levies and mercenaries. As fiefs grew hereditary the crown lost its grip: nobles took liberties, fought private wars, and increasingly bought out of muster with coin rather than send troops. This swelled the use of mercenaries and shrank the importance of the knights, so that by the 600s the king can count on only about half the realm's knights at muster.

### Crown-lands and Markiser

King Valien set aside the most profitable baronies as crown-land, about a fifth of the realm's area, whose income funds the king. A baron could, for a great sum, buy free of his count and make his barony crown-land, becoming a markis. Such a lord is largely his own master, yet swears fealty to the king each year.

### The Free Cities

Of Zorakin's roughly hundred larger cities, about twenty have bought their freedom and become a kind of crown-land under special laws, largely self-governing under royal jurisdiction. Each is ruled by a city council, an odd number of eleven to twenty-one members who sit for life and co-opt their successors, headed by a burgomaster who is a royal official. In diocesan capitals the bishops, the bontisåler, hold a guaranteed council seat. Cities tax in money rather than produce, some twenty to thirty gold a year on craftsmen and merchants, of which about ten go to the king. Some cities were bought free by a single group, such as the church or a guild, and are dominated by it. Corruption is common.

### The Realm's Council and the Estates

The Realm's Council of fourteen members and the king executes the king's decisions, each member heading an area such as the fleet, army, tolls, taxes, or minting. The mightiest are the drots, head of the estates, the kansler or chancellor of finances, and the marsk who commands the army. The Council may propose laws and offer counsel; under weak kings it has held great power, under strong kings none, for the king need not heed it now. The estates coordinate all royal lands, and every burgomaster and markis may sit among them. They were meant to meet yearly to vote on proposals, but royal measures no longer need their approval and so they are rarely called, gathering only twice in the whole 300s. Once all dukes, counts, and barons sat in the estates and a majority of both Council and estates was needed for any royal measure, until the cunning King Karolu XI abolished that law and made Zorakin hereditary in 156 eO.

## Economy

Zorakin is no trading state, yet it is one of the wealthier realms around the Copper Sea and trades richly with the other lands of that sea.

- **Exports:** grain, iron, timber, furs
- **Imports:** textiles, salt, precious metals

## Military

The military rests mainly on the knights and their retinues, who usually serve as fortress garrisons, so any offensive war demands conscripted peasants and mercenaries, while city levies remain untrained. The knights themselves are very well trained, and their heavy armour and strong war-stallions make them a formidable shock force. The war-fleet is small and old, and in any real naval contest the church's warships would be the main strength of the realm at sea.

## Religion

The Shining Way is the only religion of Zorakin, and faith in it guides daily life across the kingdom.

## History

The history of Zorakin is marked by two great conflicts: the lindskiarner wars of the 50s fO and the Great Schism of the 350s eO.

### The Lindskiarner Wars

From the 180s fO an aggressive man-like people called the lindskiarner migrated in from the north. They founded the city-state of Pharynx in central Zorakin and conquered nearly all of Grindanu, then, with a squadron of ogres, overran most of the north, west, and centre, until peace in 66 fO ceded those lands to them. Valien Valiensson, new leader of the southern Grindanu penner, sought aid from Caddo merchants and again and again from Kardien. With their eventual help the lindskiarner were barely beaten back. A pact was sworn between Valien and Vidar Odise, the Kardier leader, to aid one another forever. At midsummer of 55 fO the kingdom of Zorakin was proclaimed, Valien Valiensson becoming King Valien I, and at the Great Assembly his foremost warriors were raised to dukes, counts, and barons.

### The Great Schism

The teachings of Odo won a great response in Zorakin from the year 0. By 278 a rift had opened between the Caddo branch of the Shining Way, which held that magic is purely destructive and must be banned, and the mainland branch, which held that magic is neutral and its purpose set by the mage who wields it. The split soured relations between Zorakin and Caddo, and the bitterness has never wholly faded.

## Foreign Relations

Zorakin has long been a fairly strong realm, strong enough to deter attack. It fought in Berendien's war against Felicien from 370 to 394, until its fleet was crushed and it sued for peace. Relations with Caddo have been strained ever since the Great Schism, marked by trade blockades and crusades in Morälvidyn. The kingdom enjoys good and profitable relations with Erebos, and for six centuries with Kardien, its sworn ally. Berendien too is an old friend, with good relations kept despite the distance between them.

## Heraldry

The arms of Zorakin are a composite drawn from the arms of its various duchies, the charges of each gathered into a single shield - a heraldic emblem of the feudal realm bound together beneath one crown.
