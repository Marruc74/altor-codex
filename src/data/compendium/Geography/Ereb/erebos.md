# Erebos

![Erebos](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Erebos/Erebos1.jpg "Erebos")
![Erebos male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Erebos/Male1.jpg "Erebos male")
![Erebos female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Erebos/Female1.jpg "Erebos female")

Erebos lies scattered across the Kopparhavet like a chain of quiet promises, each island shaped by wind, water, and the long memory of trade. Here, the sea is not a border but a road, and every harbor is a meeting place for distant voices. Ships come and go with the rhythm of the tides, carrying goods, stories, and the weight of unseen agreements. The people of Erebos live with one eye on the horizon, always aware that fortune may arrive with the next sail or vanish with the last.

The towns rise in pale stone along the coasts, built to endure salt, wind, and time. Markets gather at the heart of daily life, where hands measure grain, weigh metals, and judge the worth of a deal in a single glance. Nothing stands still for long, yet everything follows a pattern shaped by generations who learned to listen to the sea.

There is a quiet order beneath the movement, a balance between risk and reward. In Erebos, survival is not won by strength alone, but by patience, awareness, and the willingness to trust the tide. It is one of the foremost trading states of the Copper Sea, ruled by the heads of its five greatest merchant houses, and the long-standing rival of **Caddo**.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 125,000 (85% humans, 15% ankor)
- **Capital:** Nohstril (approx. 9,000), on the island of Dakkilo
- **Government:** Plutocracy, ruled by the council of the five largest merchant houses
- **Ruler:** The Council of Five Houses
- **Religion:** Worship of Fraschikel, protector of all things; the Shining Way is also strong
- **Exports:** Shellfish, gemstones, fabrics
- **Imports:** Timber, grain
- **Military:** A small national army and fleet, bolstered by professional mercenaries; no levy

## Geography

Erebos consists of three rocky volcanic islands lying roughly thirty mil northeast of Caddo, together with two further isles. The land is poor in farmland but rich in harbors, and every island turns its face toward the sea.

### Beyural

- The largest and most fertile island, lying to the southwest, and the realm's unofficial heart
- Holds most of the people, most of the towns, and most of the harbors
- Largest city: **Gringul**, a place of palaces and canals at the mouth of the river **Elspet**
- Other towns include Jachindo, Behrill, Josch, and Yrrtik

Serfs here raise **entorningar**, one-horned cattle, grow wine, the giant wheat called **hersch**, and **lullader**, a small bitter stone-fruit pressed for spice and cooking oil. Remnant forests of **trill** and **iron book** trees are protected by law. In the **Bey-bergen** lie the only sunstones found anywhere in Ereb: yellow gems that glow with their own warm light. Immensely valuable, they were the foundation of the wealth Erebos amassed in the 400s eO. They are rumored, without proof, to ward off dragons, dark folk, demons, undead, magic, and disease, and they do at least instill courage in those who carry them.

### Dakkilo

- The smallest island
- Capital: **Nohstril**, which is essentially the whole of the island's life
- The remainder is poor pasture for steppe-goats and long-wooled sheep

The wool of Dakkilo's sheep is woven into prized warm fabric, lately grown fashionable in Kardien and Felicien.

### Targero

- The largest but poorest island
- Its people are bigger and tougher, and most of the fleet's soldiers are recruited here
- Lively fishing sustains its coasts
- Old **Jorpagna ruins** lie in its mountains and draw treasure-hunters

Targero is the only Erebos island troubled by dark folk, both orcs and trolls. There are rumors of perytons, hydras, basilisks, and even dragon-serpents lurking in its high places.

### Tolokfe

- A smaller island called the Cyclops' Isle, shunned by all
- By legend the cyclopes were cursed there in ancient times
- No trees grow upon it, so the shipwrecked have no means of escape

Treacherous currents, the **Megalossundet** and the **Gensnuporgraven**, together with perpetual fog, keep Tolokfe almost unexplored. The very few who return come back mad. One invasion of some five hundred men vanished entirely. The cyclopes dwell in vast cave-cities and raise steppe-goats; they are not evil, only hungry, and wish above all to be left alone.

## People

Erebosians, the erebosier, are of jorisk descent. The original inhabitants, the **massiver**, were quickly driven into the mountains in the early days. The erebosier are reputed lazy in all things except the making of money. They are short, hot-tempered, and talkative, admiring wealth and skill in negotiation above all, and judging one another by their own deeds rather than by ancestry. They tend to have dark hair, brown eyes, and somewhat dark skin, and favour close-fitting silk or wool hose, white shirts, and short jackets.

## Culture

In so mercantile a land the skilled negotiator is admired while the warrior is seen as little more than a tool in the merchant's hand. Eloquence, rhetoric, and learning are held in high regard, and skalds and bards are prized. The erebosier are individualists, valuing the worth a person earns over the name they inherit.

## Language

Most people speak **erebosiska**, an East-jorisk tongue mixed with the old **massi**. It is related to:

- Hynsolgian
- Berendian

Coastal fishermen often speak **nyjori**, though most are bilingual.

## Society

Beneath the merchant houses, the old order has all but dissolved. Lacking rich farmland, Erebos never grew a true feudal nobility; its old chieftains held power through the ships they controlled. Those chieftains now command only the coastal fishing, while the great houses control more than half the realm's resources. Some two dozen lesser houses remain below the five.

## Government

Erebos is governed by the **Council of Five Houses**, ruling from **Nohstril**. For roughly a century the five richest merchant houses have held power, each seating a single representative on the council. Every decision must be reached unanimously, for each house holds a veto, and this keeps a careful balance of power between them. The system is a plutocracy, where wealth determines who may rule.

The houses came to power after the civil war of 512 to 514, when the old nobility tried to crush the merchants and lost.

## Economy

Erebos is a rich realm, built entirely upon trade and the sea. It exports shellfish, gemstones, and fine fabrics, and imports the timber and grain its poor soil cannot provide. The sunstones of the Bey-bergen, the woven wool of Dakkilo, and the produce of Beyural's serfs all feed a merchant fleet that reaches across the Copper Sea and beyond.

## Military

Erebos funds a national army and fleet through taxation, and in addition each merchant house maintains its own fleet and army. All forces are paid professional mercenaries, mobile and hard-hitting, though fewer in number than the levied hosts of rivals such as Caddo. Because every campaign must be carried by ship, the forces are almost entirely heavy infantry and archers, with cavalry rare. They excel at marine operations and sieges but fare poorly against mainland knight-armies in open field. The Five Houses prefer diplomacy to war, for war is bad for business.

## Religion

The chief faith is the worship of **Fraschikel**, a survival from old **Jorpagna**. She is the blind goddess of justice, who weighs good deeds against evil, and she is besides the patron of all craftsmen and merchants and a goddess of creation, death, and the sea. Worship is casual, invoked when needed, and small cloisters dot the islands. The realm is tolerant in matters of faith. The **Shining Way** is fairly strong, though merchants distrust it as "Caddo's long arm."

## History

Little is known of Erebos before Jorpagna. The original **massiver** likely arrived after the second conflux.

### The Days of Jorpagna

As Jorpagna expanded in the 700s fO, settlers came to Targero, then more fertile and a favored haunt of mages. After the conflux the survivors fell into a viking-like barbarism, their ships both raiding and trading along the coasts.

### The Age of Kings

The scattered tribes were united under a single king near the start of the year-reckoning. Lacking rich farmland, no true feudalism took root; the so-called nobility were chieftains who controlled the ships, and over time they were overtaken by the families who controlled the trade-routes and grew ever richer.

### The Age of Trade

Through the 400s eO trade expanded swiftly, and the sunstones of Beyural made certain houses wealthier than any lord. Merchants came to hold more true power than the old nobility.

### The Age of the Five Houses

When the old nobility moved to crush the merchants, civil war broke out from 512 to 514, and the nobility lost. The five richest houses formed the Council of Five Houses in the new capital of Nohstril, and Erebos has been governed by their trade council ever since.

## Foreign Relations

Erebos and **Caddo** are arch-rivals, contending over sea trade through intrigue, diplomacy, and at times open violence. The quarrel is truly house against house, yet it spills onto the national stage. The two now share a common enemy in **Felicien**, whose pirates seize Erebos, Caddo, and Berendian ships alike, and both realms are rearming in answer. Trade disputes with **Zorakin** are likewise common.

## Heraldry

The arms of Erebos bear grain-barrels and a merchant ship, the plain marks of a realm whose prosperity is built upon trade. The barrels speak of the goods that fill its warehouses, and the ship of the merchant fleet that carries them across the Copper Sea.
