# Nidland

Nidland stands on a high plateau in easternmost Ereb, enclosed by a vast stone wall that marks both its border and its will. The land is dry and sparse, shaped more by discipline than abundance. At its center rules **Vicotnik the Archmage**, a dark-magician who walked in from the east and bound the country to his vision of order. His word is law, carried through a strict hierarchy of judges, disciples, and local masters who govern every settlement.

The people live under constant structure. They work, obey, and endure, shaped by a system that allows little deviation. Magic exists, but only in the hands of those who rule. Religion reinforces this order, teaching that the people of Nidland are chosen and destined to rise above all others.

Trade flows in and out through guarded gates and floating bazaars, while the army gathers strength in quiet preparation. To outsiders, Nidland appears sealed and controlled, yet steady. It is a land that does not bend easily, and one that watches the world beyond its walls with intent - or, as some fear, with the patience of one preparing to march upon it.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 240,000 (100% humans)
- **Capital:** Khaanikel (~18,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Magocratic dictatorship
- **Ruler:** Vicotnik the Archmage
- **Religion:** Kuzherism
- **Exports:** silk fabrics, precious metals, spices, handicrafts
- **Imports:** iron, slaves, weapons, timber, horses
- **Military:** Strength unknown, but believed considerable and growing

## Geography

Nidland lies in easternmost Ereb, between the **Grynnerbergen** (the Grynner Mountains) and the **Demontungan** (the Demon Tongue). The land is mostly a single highland, roughly 400 meters above the sea, with thin soil bound together by shrubs and low trees. The plateau is meager, fit only for grazing sheep and cliff-goats.

Toward the coast the land slopes away steeply, and the narrow coastal strips are richly cultivated. Here the people grow **alkhéri**, a grass whose ears are milled into a green flour that forms the staple of the Nidland table.

## Climate

The highland is dry and exposed, offering little shelter and little bounty. The sun bakes the open plateau, while the soils hold scarcely enough moisture for shrubs and stunted trees. Only along the lower coastal strips does the land grow generous enough to feed those who tend it.

## People

The **Nidlanders** are tall, sun-tanned, and black-haired, and the men wear large, bushy full beards. Their dress is strikingly uniform: very wide trousers called **puffader**, a cloth vest known as the **khresh** or a long robe called the **thogu** worn over a light shirt, and the **burtan**, a long strip of cloth wound into a ball and worn upon the head.

Once a proud people, the Nidlanders have grown mentally dulled and weak beneath Vicotnik's rule. They obey orders without thinking, and whatever the Archmage or his judges declare is taken as law without question. Much of this is no accident of temperament but the deliberate work of dark magic.

## Language

The Nidlanders speak **Nidlandish**. It began as a dialect of Akrogal, but over generations it has mixed so thoroughly with *jorisk* and various barbarian tongues that even other Akrogalers can no longer understand it. The realm's isolation behind the wall has only deepened this drift.

## Society

Beneath Vicotnik's law every community answers for itself through an **elder-master**, usually the oldest man of a town or village. He knows everyone within his reach and carries unofficial authority by any means short of breaking the Archmage's laws. His age is itself part of the design, for should he be lost to punishment or experiment, little of value is thought to be lost with him.

The fabric of Nidland society has been reshaped to serve a single end. Vicotnik has improved the lot of the lowest classes so that they make good and grateful soldiers, indebted and loyal to the man who raised them. More chilling still, he has used spells to erase the past from people's memories, severing them from history so that Nidland is the only world they have ever known.

## Government

Vicotnik rules absolutely, governing with an iron hand through a strict hierarchy of mages he has trained in his Eastern magic.

- **Vicotnik the Archmage**: the Archmage, whose word is the final law
- **The judges**: 20 pupils and dark-mages who use mind-reading to determine guilt; civil cases follow an 800-chapter book of precedent called *The Eight Hundred Steps*
- **The disciples**: 50 to 100 promising young mages studying the innermost secrets of dark magic, each hoping to rise to the rank of judge
- **Elder-masters**: the senior men who answer for each town and village

Disciples who fail to become judges are made mountain hermits, sworn never again to use magic. Vicotnik ensures by his own means that the oath is kept.

### Crimes and Punishments

- **Foreigners on Nidland soil**: a permit costs roughly 10 gold marks per day; trespass brings one month of forced labor for each illegal day, plus a fine of 10 gold marks per day
- **Killing a Nidlander**: the **Labraatriet**
- **Theft**: one day of forced labor for each silver mark of the stolen goods' value
- **Preaching any creed but Kuzher's**: the **Labraatriet**
- **Using magic**: the **Labraatriet**
- **Begging**: forced conscription into the army

## Economy

Nidland is a poor land, its meager highland yielding little beyond wool and the green flour of the coastal **alkhéri**. Yet the Nidlanders are known across the east as honest traders who pay well for quality goods.

Because no foreigner may set foot on Nidland soil without a permit, trade is conducted at great floating bazaars anchored outside the harbors. This arrangement gives Vicotnik's officials a clear oversight of everything that enters or leaves the realm. The country sends out silk fabrics, gold, and silver, and brings in spices from Akrogal, timber from Barbia, iron and weapons from the Grynnerbergen dwarves, and horses from Hynsolge.

## Military

Little is known of the true strength of Nidland's army, for the wall hides its musters as it hides all else. Yet much suggests that vast forces are being gathered for **the Great Cleansing**, a crusade meant to sweep across all of Ereb.

When Vicotnik first came to Ereb in 468 eO, he prophesied that the world had "a dozen dozen years" - one hundred and forty-four - to submit to him. With only two of those years remaining, no land has so much as spoken to him, and no one beyond the wall knows what armies wait behind it.

## Religion

The only permitted faith is **Kuzherism**, almost certainly devised by Vicotnik himself to suit his ends. Travelers report fragments of its doctrine scattered among various Akrogal cultures, which suggests he stitched the creed together from older beliefs.

At its heart, the Nidlanders are taught that they are the **Kuzhers**, the chosen people of the One God, destined to rule over Ereb while all other peoples remain inferior. With the One's prophet, Vicotnik, they shall crush every false faith and reclaim the dominion that was always meant to be theirs. An invented ancient history explains their fallen state, telling how they betrayed Kuzher again and again for other gods. This story leaves many Nidlanders with a deep shame that feeds their fanaticism.

The **elf-folk** are loathed above all others. They are named the eternal enemies of the master-race, treacherous and demonic, and their dark-vision, immortality, and sharp hearing are offered as proof of an evil origin. Their oval pupils, the Kuzhers say, show that they were shaped by the same evil god who made the serpent and the wildcat. By this teaching, Ereb's peoples must be enlightened through either mission or war, and those who refuse to convert are said to have chosen war themselves.

## History

The region long belonged to **Hynsolge**, but it was cut off from that realm after the Dwarves' March in the early 400s eO. Then, in **468 eO**, a short and stout man in a black-brown silk robe walked in from the east, over the Demontungan, calling himself Vicotnik the Archmage. He wielded a mighty and evil Eastern magic unknown to any of Ereb's scholars.

Vicotnik gathered followers swiftly, drove out the weak local rulers, and ruled through pupils he taught his new magic, punishing the disobedient in horrible ways. Exiled nobles gave the land the unflattering name **Nidland**. He banned the old Hynsolgian gods, introduced Kuzherism, sharpened every punishment, barred foreigners, and forbade all magic but that of his own pupils. He doubled the taxes upon the rich and set the poor to paid forced labor.

In **502 eO** the **Great Wall** was begun, meant to turn the whole of Nidland into a single fortress, though shortages of labor left it incomplete along the coasts. In the seventy or so years since its completion, few reports have escaped the stone curtain. The most reliable of them say that the Archmage is preparing for an enormous war - a warning taken seriously by few, but watched with deep unease by those who hear it.

## Foreign Relations

Nidland holds itself apart from the world. No foreign embassy is received, no foreigner may cross its soil unbidden, and what dealings it has with its neighbors happen only at the floating bazaars beyond the harbors. To the lands of Ereb it is a closed and silent country, its intentions unknown.

Vicotnik's standing prophecy hangs over all of it. Having given the world one hundred and forty-four years to bow to him, he has been answered with silence, and that silence may yet be repaid with the Great Cleansing.

## The Great Wall

**The Great Wall** runs along the entire border of Nidland, drawn even against the sea. It stands 4 meters high and 4 meters thick, with towers of 6 meters rising every 50 meters along its length. It was built between 502 and 541 eO, largely by the labor of convicts, criminals, and bought slaves.

Heavily fortified gates stand at every hundredth tower, one every 5 kilometers, and they are guarded day and night. It is said to be impossible to enter Nidland except through these gates, though in truth the wall remains incomplete in places, most notably along the coasts where labor ran short.

## Vicotnik and His Disciples

**Vicotnik the Archmage** rules Nidland not merely as a tyrant but as a near-god, and he has taken deliberate steps to make himself the people's natural leader. He raised up the lowest of society so they would become loyal soldiers, grateful to the man who lifted them. He used his spells to erase the people's memory of the past, cutting them off from all history so that his realm became the only world they know. And through his harsh laws and his dark-magic mind-reading he made himself appear a just leader, until, joined with the teachings of Kuzherism, the people came half to regard him as a god.

Beneath him stand his **20 judges**, the judge-mages who read minds to weigh guilt, and below them the **disciples**, who study the innermost secrets of his dark art. Together they form both his government and his school, each rank trained and bound by the magic he carried out of the east.

## The Dark Magic

The Archmage's power is a foreign and evil sorcery, unknown to the scholars of Ereb. Its darkest expression is the **Labraatriet**, Vicotnik's own experiment-chamber, where he and his pupils refine their magic and search for new uses for it.

The horrors told of the Labraatriet are many: bone-marrow turned to boiling lead, skeletons dissolved until only a quivering lump remains, a head set upon the belly, curses that leave a victim trembling at the touch of sunlight. No one leaves the chamber without grave deformity or shattered mind, and such ruined victims are most often cast out and killed. To be sentenced to the Labraatriet is, in every meaning, the end.

## Heraldry

The arms of Nidland are the same as those borne by Vicotnik himself - an armored gauntlet gripping three lightning-bolts, the sign of the Archmage's iron rule and the dark power he wields over the whole sealed realm.
