# Marjura

Far in the cold north-west, at the very edge of the polar ice, lies the island of Marjura: a bleak, volcanic land of reeking sulphur swamps, frozen mountains and a buried evil older than any kingdom now standing. It is counted among the colonies of distant Trakorien, which holds the harbour town of Arhem and the sulphur fields by force of arms, a month's hard sailing from Tricilve. But Trakorien holds only the edges. The interior belongs to the native kvur tribes, to dwarves and to giants, and beneath all of it, locked under eight hundred years of ice, to the dead kingdom of Cruri.

Marjura is a place where the skin of the world feels thin. Its sulphur is prized across the south and carried out twice a year by armed convoy; its cold is worshipped as a god; and its history is a warning, for the gods once made the island a prison, casting down a whole city of necromancers upon it. Now, after eight centuries, the long winter is lifting, and what was buried may be waking.

## Overview

- **Population:** scattered - native kvur tribes in the wilds, a few thousand colonists and convict-labourers about Arhem, with dwarves and giants in the mountains
- **Chief town:** Arhem, held by Trakorien
- **Government:** no single rule; Trakorien occupies Arhem and the sulphur swamps, the kvur tribes are stateless, and the monks of Clusta Noba keep to themselves
- **Held by:** the realm of Trakorien, through its bailiff and garrison at Arhem
- **Religion:** Kmorda, the god of cold; the war-art of Trocuspa at the eastern schools; the buried necromancy of old Cruri, kept alive by the hidden shagulite sect
- **Exports:** sulphur, and quicksilver from the dwarven mine
- **Imports:** provisions and trade goods, brought twice a year by the sulphur convoy
- **Sailing time:** about a month from Tricilve, by way of Vumbra and the isles of Trinsmyra

## Geography

Marjura is a volcanic island at the edge of the polar ice, far to the north-west of the Trakorian heartland and the isles of Trinsmyra. Its coasts are cold and its interior colder, broken by frozen mountains where the giants dwell and by the broad sulphur swamps that are the island's one source of wealth. Off the eastern shore lie lesser islets, one of them home to the monk-colony of Clusta Noba. Most of the island has never been mapped by southern hands: Trakorien holds Arhem and the sulphur fields, and little else.

## The Peoples

The oldest people of Marjura are the kvurer, primitive tribes whom the necromancer-kings of Cruri once enslaved, and who still keep to the wild interior. To them were added, over the centuries, waves of outsiders. Freethinkers who fled the Trakorian isle of Trinsmyra crossed the sea to found Arhem and other colonies; the shagulites, a necromantic sect driven out of the south, fled here after them; dwarves came to work the mountains and a mine of quicksilver; and giants hold the high frozen peaks. Last of all came the Trakorians themselves - soldiers, convict-labourers and merchants - who hold Arhem and are cordially hated by the islanders born there, a hatred that simmers into a quiet movement for freedom.

## Language

The colonists speak a northern dialect of the Trakorian tongue, and the kvurer their own speech. Beneath both lies Ithilgrom, the dead language once spoken in Cruri, which survives now only in scattered words and in the formulas of necromancers.

## History

In the deeps of time the gods lifted a whole city, Krau-Ki, out of the high culture of Yndar on the far side of the world, and dropped it upon Marjura in punishment for ungodly magic. From Krau-Ki, by way of Kru-Hri, grew Cruri, an evil kingdom of necromancy and demon-worship that ruled the island from 598 to 250 fO, enslaving the kvur tribes and spreading colonies across the northern isles.

Around 200 fO the gods doomed Cruri as they had doomed Krau-Ki before it, and sealed the island beneath the ice. So began the eight-hundred-year sulphur winter, with the demon-kingdom frozen beneath it. In 405 eO freethinkers fleeing a tyrant's rise on Trinsmyra crossed the sea and founded Arhem and other colonies on the island's thawed margins, and in 510 eO the shagulites, a necromantic sect driven out of Kargom in the south, fled here as well. In 595 eO the realm of Trakorien, hungry for the island's sulphur, seized Arhem and the sulphur swamps by force.

And in 610 eO, after eight centuries, the long winter at last begins to lift, and the buried kingdom of Cruri may rise once more from the ice. How that will end, none can yet say.

## The Sulphur Trade

Marjura's worth to the south is its sulphur, dug from the reeking swamps around Arhem and prized for the keeping of wine and for a hundred uses besides. Trakorien holds the trade by holding the town, and works the fields with convict labour under the eye of its garrison. Twice a year, in spring and autumn, the sulphur convoy makes the month-long voyage from Tricilve to Arhem and back, unloading provisions and loading sulphur for the journey home. The dwarves of the mountains add a second prize, a mine of quicksilver, and their smiths do work that ranges from common iron up to true mithril.

## Religion

The cold itself is worshipped here as Kmorda, the god of cold, who keeps a chapel in Arhem's marketplace. On the eastern islets the monks of Clusta Noba and the schools of Bhannavil teach banzikan, the war-art of the god Trocuspa, the disciplined and fearless mind fit for battle. And beneath everything lies the island's older and darker faith, the necromancy of Cruri and the formulas of its dead tongue, kept alive by the hidden shagulite sect.

## The Buried Kingdom

The heart of Marjura's dread is Cruri. For eight hundred years the necromantic kingdom has lain locked beneath the ice while the island above it lived its hard, cold life around the edges. The shagulites who fled here came seeking it; the Trakorians who hold Arhem barely credit the old tales. But the winter that pinned Cruri down is ending, and the strangers and wanderers the sulphur isle draws to itself may find that the dead kingdom is the true reason any of them came.

## Heraldry

Marjura is a colony, not a crowned land, and bears no arms of its own. The banner that flies over Arhem is Trakorien's.
