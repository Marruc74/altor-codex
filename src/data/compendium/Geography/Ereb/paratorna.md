# Paratorna

Paratorna is the heart of Trakorien and the seat of its power, ruled from the capital Tricilve. Its high-born paratorner make an art of the insult, scorn modesty as weakness, and drape themselves in throwaway finery of fragile kamsunsk papir. It was here that the realm threw off old Palamux and took the name Trakorien, and here that its wealth and intrigue gather.

## Tricilve

The capital holds the academy and its library-park, and the great folly-clock the Kronolabe. Though it is the seat of the realm it has no deep harbour of its own and is served by the port of Okruzande, where the noble ice of Sanithsid is landed to cool the tables of the rich. Each year the city keeps the Freedom Carnival in memory of its liberation from Palamux, with mock battles, gladiator-fights and ritual dramas, and at the games are held the cruel Fate Lottery and the svartjakten. The goddess Kastyke is honoured above all here, she who loves the one who loves himself.

## Kamest and the Swamp of Shadows

The trade-town of Kamest deals in sylvoleum, the oil pressed from the oil-willow forests and burned in lamps, in the incendiary vildeld, and in the gas-bags of the airships. Nearby spreads the vast Swamp of Shadows, breeding nightmares of its own: a giant blood-fly whose bite sows creeping madness, a grey telepath that forces its dreams upon sleepers, and a grasping spider-vine that snares the unwary.

## Fam Kvalvi, Siola and the holy coast

The holy city Fam Kvalvi is the seat of Kastyke's church and its Pillar-Father, crowned by a colossal six-armed statue of the goddess that doubles as a lighthouse. Nearby Siola spins wool and houses an imperial arsenal. To the east lies Kryfferskogen, choked by the thorny kryffermara vine and home only to the spotted, dog-headed skecker.

## Ilibaurien

The northern province of Ilibaurien was once a freedom-loving land, broken not by arms but by the addictive drug opiander, loosed upon it by the sorcerer Astrumal diFolt, the demon from Melse, until the whole country fell under the rule of the drug.

## The airships and the sea-folk

Above the island drift the zepelonder, the airships of the Paratornian nobility, their envelopes lifted by sylvoleum burners and towed by trained seabirds and winged beasts, an expensive pastime. Along the coasts and isles dwell the seal-folk, a shapeshifting elven kind who take the shape of seals upon the water.
