# Nohstril

Nohstril stands on the island of Dakkilo, off the Copper Sea, the capital of Erebos and the seat of its Council of Five Houses. It is above all an administration-city, a place of clerks and contracts and customs-men, grown in a single century into one of the great ports of the Copper Sea. Its guiding truth is plainly spoken: all is for sale in Erebos. With a full purse and a little patience a person may push through almost anything here, so long as it does not threaten the power of the Five Houses, and the corruption that follows from it runs through everything, the church and the city watch not least.

A traveller comes in by sea, under the fire-basket of the great bronze colossus of Sagrin the Conqueror that lights the harbour, and is met at once by the customs and, soon enough, by someone with a hand out. Some nine thousand souls live here, humans for the most part, with dwarves in their warren below the streets, ducks in their tidy walled quarter, and halflings between.

## Overview

- **Realm:** capital of Erebos
- **Where:** the island of Dakkilo, on the Copper Sea
- **People:** about 9,000
- **Seat of:** the Council of Five Houses, the rulers of Erebos
- **Government:** a governor, appointed by the Council, with wide powers and command of the watch
- **Became capital:** in 515 eO, after the merchants won the civil war
- **Landmark:** the thirty-metre bronze colossus of Sagrin the Conqueror in the harbour

## Government

Erebos is a rare thing in Ereb, a centrally governed realm ruled outright by its business, with none of the scattered feudal power of a Zorakin or a Berendien. Its master is the Council of Five Houses, on which each of the five great merchant houses, Hinkare, Ermini, Gararin, Festglade and Hypon, seats one representative, and which decides only by unanimous vote, every member holding a veto. The Council appoints and dismisses every official, makes the realm's only law, handles its foreign affairs, and sits as its highest court, all of it set down in the State Law, the written constitution drawn up after the merchants' victory.

Nohstril is reckoned one of the realm's twenty provinces, but as capital and seat of the Council it answers to a governor instead, a hard post that wears most who hold it down to nothing within a year or two, for the governor must forever balance the Council against the lesser houses and the unofficial powers, the thieves' guilds and the assassins among them. Beneath all of it the officials are loyal in practice to the house that placed them, and bribery is everywhere.

## Ocula Negra

Behind the dull name of the Welfare Office sits Ocula Negra, the Black Eye, the realm's secret police and intelligence service in one. Its head is anonymous, meets those who hire it in secret, and answers to no one but the Council; its work is to hunt down the enemies of the state and destroy them. Its present worries are three: the discontent of the poor, stirred by reform-minded Fraschikel preachers who talk of justice and freedom; the dalkers of Caddo, and the Caddo branch of the Shining Way it counts subversive; and, most pressing of all, the Felicians, who have lately grown bold and threatening.

## Society

Below the house-owners of the overclass stand the craftsmen, mostly bound to one house or another on long hours and low pay, with heavy fines for changing masters; the seasonal land-workers on the houses' estates; and the fisherfolk, who keep to their old tribal ways in the coast villages, organised in boat-crews and paying their taxes in fish. The gulf between rich and poor is wide, and a bad harvest or a high price of bread brings riots into the streets often enough.

## Religion

The favoured faith is Fraschikel, goddess of justice, maker of life, lady of the sea and keeper of the realm of the dead, a good but uninvolved goddess who gave the world a few plain rules, to respect another's life and keep one's given word and make one's own fortune, and then left it alone. That suits Erebos exactly, and the Council backs the church in all weathers. But the alliance has made the church pliant and corrupt, even selling its offices, and it neglects the poor, which has opened the door to the Shining Way. That faith comes in two branches: the dalkish, out of Caddo, which forbids magic and is watched as a nest of spies, and the kardish, out of Zorakin and Kardien, which tolerates magic and is treated far better. The order of Saint Gorge's Hospital, robed in red, tends the sick and the poor of every kind and stands quietly against the church's worldly leaders.

## The Powers in the Shadows

For all the talk of law, much of Nohstril runs in the dark. The thieves' guilds are organised like trading companies, ruled by an elder and his captains, and Ocula Negra knows of at least five families that carve the city into districts to avoid a war among themselves; the watch is often bought, and the Council seems content to tolerate a certain level of it. Deadlier is the Black Hand, a very secret guild of assassins that Ocula Negra suffers because it can be turned to the state's use, whose price starts at a hundred gold and climbs steeply, and which will not kill a member of the Council or of Ocula Negra itself; its killers worship a long-dead master-murderer raised to a demigod, and strive to be perfect and pitiless. Against all this stand kinder powers: Saint Gorge's Hospital, and Vidfara, an association of merchants trading with far and little-known lands, forever in need of adventurers for distant and dangerous journeys.

## The City

Nohstril is built in three parts: the North City of the great houses and the academies, the South City of the harbour and the common folk, and Cloister Isle, also called Sagrin City, where the Council sits. Most houses are two-storey stone; a few towers rise above them, the governor's spire, the elemental academy's Tower of Knowledge, and the symbolists' cone-topped tower, whose summit a botched spell once turned into something indescribable that flapped away into the night, souring the two academies' dealings ever since. The Great Library holds ten thousand books, and the Golden Cathedral the bones of Saint Gorge. Below the streets lies the dwarf city, entered by a windowless eight-sided hall near the South Gate; the ducks keep their own neat walled quarter, the Duck Town, and the halflings a quarter of half-scale houses between. Over the harbour the colossus of Sagrin holds up its fire, and around midsummer the festival of Fraschikel fills the theatre and draws crowds from across the realm.

## History

In the days of Jorpagna there was likely no city here, only a village of the massiver, the islands' first people; the name is first written down in 63 fO, as the market-town of Nostrili. In 37 fO a war-host out of Beyural was thrown back by a folk-levy under Sebast Bullhorn, master of the fishing fleet, and his line ruled the island after. Sagrin the Conqueror seized Dakkilo briefly in 369 eO and drew it into the Erebos world, and when the merchants and the old chieftains came at last to civil war in 512 to 514, the merchants won. Nohstril, which had stayed neutral, was made the capital and seat of the new Council of Five Houses in 515 eO, and grew swiftly through the rest of the century into one of the metropolises of the Copper Sea.
