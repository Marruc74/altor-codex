# Saphyna

Saphyna is the southernmost of Trakorien's four islands and the realm's breadbasket, fertile and, by the harsh standards of the empire, fairly peaceful. Trakorians call it the realm's hairy footsole, the warm and simple south that feeds everyone else and is raided for its trouble. Its folk are good-natured and earthy, looked down on by their prouder neighbours, yet they bear more than their share of the silverbarn, the uncanny wonder-children born across the islands.

## The Frimboline Plain

The very rich Frimboliner Plain is the heart of Saphyna, tended by patient farmers and dotted with villages such as Guffi, Possi, Mullo and Kvoppe. Its harbour town Frimbole Olvi gives its name to the famous black beer brewed here, thick and malty, and the plain is known for its herb-gardens and for the coffa-bush, whose dried berries make the stimulating red-brown brew that warms the southern winters.

## The Matagde Mountains and the Tocmer Forest

The snowy Matagde Mountains split the island north from south, and evil dark folk lair in their heights. The river Rubakin runs from them, and at the eastern cape stands the impassable Tocmer Forest, choked with the thorny mispel. Halflings keep to the quieter valleys. North of the mountains lies pleasant Korjulme, a pastoral province famed for its fruit orchards and its noble estates.

## Gaaz Ulul and Gudiena

The cliff-city of Gaaz Ulul is built into the rock, a base for the army and a strategic lock between southern and northern Saphyna, where cliff-goats and hippogriffs are kept. On the Gudiena plain lies the drug-town of the same name, home to skilled herbalists and drug-makers; here the alchemist Harlevold Hakeborst first distilled the numbing drug zombin, and here the battle of Ylarna's eve was won.

## Krindenland and the cult of Remuntra

The whole western reach of the island is Krindenland, a magically warped land of light-tremors and shifting geography, thought to be a seam where the world presses against a parallel universe. From this strangeness the dreaded faith of Remuntra first arose, the contagious cult that spreads like a sickness of the mind. The harbour of Krindenmaar lies on its edge, and here the sun-god Valliman is mourned in the old poem of his drowned forest.
