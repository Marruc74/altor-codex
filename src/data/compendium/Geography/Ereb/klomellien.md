# Klomellien

![Klomellien](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Klomellien/Klomellien1.jpg "Klomellien")
![Klomellien male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Klomellien/Male1.jpg "Klomellien male")
![Klomellien female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Klomellien/Female1.jpg "Klomellien female")

Klomellien lies along the western coast where the sea meets ambition. It is not a kingdom but a loosely joined union of eleven independent city-states gathered around **Gryppa Bay**, each guarding its own interests while watching the others closely. The land takes its name from the legendary hero **Klomul** and his giant turtles, the **klomuller**. Ships arrive daily, carrying timber, salt, iron, and pearls outward, while fabrics and food return in steady flow. Along the coast, markets never rest and agreements are written as quickly as they are broken. Inland, the **Melashe Mountains** rise with forests, mines, and the deep waters of **Lake Jaggarack**. Rivers carry wealth toward the harbors, and with it come tension and rivalry. Mercana leads through trade, Hamur through strength, and others through quieter means. Old ruins and mist-covered valleys remind travelers that the land itself holds older stories. Here, wealth and hardship exist side by side, and fortune favors those who understand both.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 125,000 (70% humans, 15% halflings, 8% ankor, 5% dwarves, 2% wolfmen)
- **Capital:** none
- **Government:** union of eleven self-governing city-states
- **Ruler:** none; each city-state rules itself
- **Religion:** several, including the Shining Way, the worship of Olem, and the Æsir
- **Exports:** timber, salt, iron, handicrafts, pearls
- **Imports:** fabrics, foodstuffs
- **Military:** varies between city-states

## Geography

Klomellien sits on **Gryppa Bay**, bounded to the east by the low **Melashe Mountains**. To the north lies the bare plain of **Ishna**, where poor soil keeps settlement thin. East of the plain stretches the hilly **Abzural**, and along the eastern peaks rises the great forest of **Ize**.

At the center, **Lake Jaggarack** gathers the mountain rivers behind steep cliffs and drains through the deep river **Maure** to the sea. The coast is far more fertile and densely settled than the interior, and most of the city-states cluster here, living on trade, pearl and sea fishing, and a little farming. Inland resources are carried down the rivers to the coastal cities for export.

## Economy

Wealth varies sharply between the city-states. The land prospers most from its **salt**, which gives a broad, modest comfort to all who live here - anyone willing to work can extract a few kilos of valuable salt in a matter of days. Timber, iron, copper, silver, pearls, and handicrafts flow outward, while the union depends on fabrics and foodstuffs brought in from abroad.

Mercana stands as the economic heart, drawing goods down the Maure and sending them onward to Trakorien and Magilre through the **Trade-Road Company**, whose routes reach as far as the great trading city of **O**.

## Government

Klomellien has no capital and no head of state. Each city-state governs itself in its own fashion - merchant councils, monarchies, oligarchies, democracies, and crowns won in blood. Cooperation happens only when necessity demands it. Rivalry is constant, and minor conflicts are an ordinary part of daily life. At present, Mercana is the most powerful of the eleven.

## The Klomuller

The land is named for the hero **Klomul**, who once hid from his enemies beneath the shell of a giant water-turtle. These **giant turtles**, the klomuller, are still used today to propel river-barges and to wage war. The city-state of **Triska** sometimes mounts a fighting superstructure on the largest of them, turning the beasts into floating battle-platforms. The older "north-klomuller" are calmer by temperament and bear such towers more steadily.

## Major City-States

Of the eleven, seven are well known to outsiders.

### Mercana

The largest and most important city-state, set at the mouth of the Maure where it borders **Magilre**. It is often confused with the city of **Cvyrdo** across the river, which belongs to Magilre - long ago the pirate **Gryppa Gultand** seized the river-city and sold one half to Magilre's dwarves and the other to Klomellien, and Mercana now regards Cvyrdo as little more than its slum.

Mercana is ruled by a **Trade Council** of the nine richest merchant families, balanced by two annually elected **city-masters** (mayors) who check one another. All its wealth rests on trade. Bulk goods passing through pay a toll of ten percent, recorded on a goods-letter listing the taxed wares; forgeries of these letters are common. Some 85% of its people are human, with halflings and **ankor** making up the rest, the ankor confined to a walled quarter.

Most here worship **Olem den Late**, "the Lazy," who is said to have created Altor out of boredom, then regretted it and now ponders its destruction. His worshippers beg his mercy and entertain him with monthly plays and song-contests on the open stage of his temple. A few priests of **Remuntra** also dwell in the city.

### Hamur

A harsh mountain city in the south of the Melashe range, fourth-largest in the union, grown from a fort that once guarded a silver vein found by the geologist **Niel Littlepick**. Its ruler is chosen by a death-match of eight armed candidates on a round stage; the last left alive is crowned for a single year. The people name the victors as legends - **Mantar Sharpknife**, **Brodwer Skullcrusher**, **Ghulon Bloodjaw** - and once an exhausted champion, **Humnur Crushing-Axe**, fell to a mere teenage boy.

Silver, iron, and copper come down from the mountains on rail-carts to the river **Wemer**, and Mercana's houses keep metal-buying offices in the city. The people worship the **Æsir** under other names. Roughly 60% are human, 30% dwarves, and 10% **wolfmen**. Might makes right here - the royal guard numbers only about fifty, and every man over fourteen must serve in the army. Hamur is famed for its smithing and craft, having forged the brass globes for the renowned **kronolab in Tricilve**. Its folk descend from barbarians mixed with Jorduashur colonists.

### Nya Arno

A small oligarchy of around 4,500 souls, founded by shipwrecked **Caddo** survivors who keep their old customs strictly. It follows the Caddo branch of the **Shining Way** and maintains good ties with distant Caddo. It lives on farming and fishing and is too small to oppose the other states' use of magic.

### Addiaska

A young monarchy of some 10,000, founded on pearl-rich reefs and ruled by an absolute king. It follows the Kardian branch of the Shining Way and is famed for its pearl-jewelry, including miniatures carved from a single pearl.

### Yolev

A monarchy of about 7,000, the first of all the city-states to be founded, settled by fishermen exiled from **Jorpagna**. It follows the Kardian branch of the Shining Way and lives wholly on fishing, neither importing nor exporting. Alone among the states it is at peace with all its neighbors, and its sailors are so skilled that many of Mercana's captains hail from Yolev.

### Triska

An aggressive oligarchy of around 20,000 and the largest state by area, ruled by its three mightiest families - **Cudda**, **Wildgraze**, and **Peffomir** - who war among themselves and pause only to seize their neighbors' land. The people worship the Æsir and live by farming, though in lean years the army turns to plunder raids, sometimes aboard the klomull fighting-platforms.

### Sanzas

A democracy of roughly 12,000, founded by halflings fleeing the dark folk. It grows fine tobacco for export and sprawls like an overgrown village, ruled by a mayor and a **village council** elected every five years. Its halflings endure constant insults from their larger neighbors. The folk here keep no religion.

## History

Klomellien was settled late, long a haunt of the dark folk. Colonists from **Jorpagna** first took the northern coast of the Copper Sea, then Magilre, and only around 620 fO did they cross the mountains into Klomellien. The Third Conflux struck the region only lightly, and when Jorpagna fell, its Klomellien colonies became independent.

Since then the land has remained obscure in the histories of Ereb - too internally divided and too far out of the way to matter to the great powers. Yet it holds real economic weight through its salt. There is great wealth here, and just as much poverty.

## Foreign Relations

Klomellien's chief enemy is **Magilre**, which covets its raw materials. It keeps good relations with **Kardien**, trading salt in exchange for food and metals.

## Heraldry

The arms of Klomellien bear towers and a turtle. The towers stand for the power and cohesion of the union's many city-states, while the turtle - the giant klomull of the hero Klomul - serves as the national symbol of the whole land.
