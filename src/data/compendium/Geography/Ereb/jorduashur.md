# Jorduashur

![Jorduashur](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Jorduashur/Jorduashur1.jpg "Jorduashur")
![Jorduashur male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Jorduashur/Male1.jpg "Jorduashur male")
![Jorduashur female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Jorduashur/Female1.jpg "Jorduashur female")

Jorduashur lies in the far north of Ereb Altor, where the sea is cold and the land resists all comfort. It is a place shaped by wind, salt, and stone, where few gather and fewer remain soft. Along the coasts, longhouses stand against the storms, and ships are as vital as bread. Inland, the **Kardskogen** stretches deep and dangerous, filled with orcs, minotaurs, ogres, and old elven powers that keep most folk near the shore.

The land is divided among four jarls, each ruling in their own way, bound loosely under a king whose voice carries only as far as the others allow. In **Halle** they meet at the great assembly to settle matters that affect them all, though unity is never certain - there has been no true king for some hundred and fifty years.

Here, a person belongs first to their kin. The **clan** and the family decide fate, justice, and survival. There is no standing army, but when war comes, the people rise, for a slain warrior goes to **Æsgar** to feast with the gods. Their dragon-ships travel far, trading, raiding, and returning with stories and wealth. Jorduashur endures not through comfort, but through strength. The land was settled in earnest only about a century and a half after **Ibram Shahalat** sailed around the world.

## Overview

Jorduashur is a cold and rugged barbarian land on a great northern peninsula, sparsely settled and poor in coin, but rich in iron, salt, amber, and the courage of its people.

- **Population:** approx. 30,000 (50% humans, 33% svartfolk and stenfolk, 10% elves, 7% dwarves)
- **Capital:** Halle (approx. 1,000)
- **Government:** union of four jarldoms
- **Ruler:** Elfhelm Korp, jarl of Allnemark, seen abroad as king
- **Religion:** worship of the Æsir, a family of gods
- **Exports:** salt, amber, metals, dwarven handicrafts
- **Imports:** luxury goods, fabrics, plundered loot
- **Military:** no standing army, but an unusually strong levy of up to 30% of the population

## Geography

Jorduashur is a great peninsula north of Ransard and northwest of Nargur. Only the coasts hold human settlement; the interior is wild orc and minotaur country or the mysterious realms of the elves. Travel is almost entirely by boat, for there are next to no roads.

### Key Features

- rugged, storm-lashed coastlines
- a cold northern climate
- the deep inland forest of the Kardskogen
- the highland **Ederls huvud**, holding the orc realm **Orxygg**
- the **Dragon Run**, the "Dragon Channel," said to be the death-furrow of an enormous fallen dragon

### The Kardskogen

All timber for boats and houses must come from this forest, and it is a perilous trade. The southwest is **Alvskogen**, held by a tribe of elves who forbid free logging but will sell wood; travel there is safe. Elsewhere the forest belongs to others, and is best avoided:

- elves in the southwest (the safe Alvskogen)
- orc bands of twenty to thirty roving the timberlands
- minotaurs
- ogres

## Climate

The peninsula is wind-bitten and cold the year round, its northern and eastern coasts especially storm-lashed. The harshness keeps the population small and the people hardy; soft folk do not last in Jorduashur.

## People

The **jordukerlar** are large and powerful, averaging well over 180 centimetres, bearded and shaggy-maned against the cold. They are distant kin to the barbarians of Barbia and Nargur, but taller and fairer of feature. They speak **jordiska**, a barbarian tongue related to Ransardian.

## Society

### Clan Society

The key social unit is the clan or kin, then the **family** - meaning all who live on a single farm, not only blood-kin. A farm of turf- and stone-walled longhouses often holds several households gathered under the chief family. The kin group provides protection, enforces justice, and resolves conflict. Below the jarls sit rich great-farmers whose land others work, bound to one another in a complex web of loyalties and alliances that is the heart of daily life. Equality between free men and free women is nearly total.

## Government

Formally Jorduashur is one realm of four jarldoms, but in practice there has been no king for about a hundred and fifty years, since the death of **Harald Krokig**. A king needs the votes of at least half the chieftains, and none has held them in ages. **Elfhelm Korp**, jarl of Allnemark, is named king abroad, yet holds no power over the others; each jordukerl regards his own jarl as king.

Each jarldom is independent under its hereditary jarl, now chosen in practice by the assembly. The **storting**, held in Halle's king's-hall at least once a year, settles matters too great for a single jarl; any free person may bring an issue, and may even demand a jarl's removal, though this rarely succeeds. Each jarldom keeps its own **ting**, led by a life-appointed law-man, often a blind or injured man; difficult cases are judged by a **council of elders** of eight to twelve men.

## The Four Jarldoms

The land is one realm in name and four near-independent regions in fact.

### Allnemark

The southernmost and most populous jarldom, with some six thousand people in a string of roughly two hundred farms down a fertile coast. It holds the city of **Halle** and the **Sval Isles**, often counted a fifth jarldom and home to some of the realm's finest sailors. The young jarl **Sigurd Brandsvedd** rules from Halle, which boasts the realm's only multi-story wooden houses, its largest court, and its greatest number of thieves and warriors. On an islet stands **Allneborg**, the realm's chief stone fortress, seat of the storting and of the gilded **kungasal**, the king's hall. Trade is guarded by the **Hallefred**, the Halle-peace: anyone bound for market under a solid-red banner is safe from attack, and to break that peace is to be outlawed for a year and a day.

### Sigsdal

North of Allnemark on the cold, stormy **Sigsudd** peninsula, with about four thousand five hundred people in scattered fjord-farms. Its name means "victory," for it was won from the orcs. The land is rich in minerals, with mines worked by men, dwarves, orcs, and ogres alike; folk here sometimes pay tax to both a local orc chieftain and their jarl. The jarl **Hjörleif Shield-cleaver** rules from **Tarkens Krök**, a fortified village built on a clifftop above the river Tarken and nearly impregnable. Within Sigsdal lies the dwarf realm of **Kward'zak**, more than a thousand dwarves in the cities of **Kward'korzub** and the secret **Gerlwerd**; relations with the dwarves are good, and vital.

### Nordal

The youngest jarldom, formed about twenty-five years ago, with somewhat over two thousand people along a long, storm-lashed northern coast. Its folk long for solitude yet bind tightly into great families, their farms set far apart. They live mostly by raiding, against the barbarian lands or south into Magilre and Klomellien. There is no real capital, only the jarl **Håkon Smed**'s own farm, the **Hearth-sure**. The jarl holds little power, and feuds between farms and families are common.

### Krakelid

The last jarldom, with some three thousand people and a capital-town of about five hundred, founded by King **Harald Krokig** as an outpost in conquered barbarian provinces. It holds the realm's second fortress and lies on the **Dragon Run** coast. It trades the area's great **rock-salt** and is ruled with a loose hand by **Ulf Stupa**, a descendant of Harald Krokig. Eastern barbarians - everything east of the border, Jordukerl-like folk and *jorisk* colonists - raid it often, which unites its people.

## Economy

Jorduashur is a seafaring culture, poor in coin but bold on the water. Two main trade routes carry its goods. The **western** route follows the Fyrsneca current by way of the Sval Isles to Trakorien and beyond, the cargo often sold at a Trakorian-island harbor. The **eastern** route deals with the primitive northern coast cultures, such as Barbia's horse-folk, and on rare and bloody occasions runs down Akrogal's rivers past Demontungan to Berendian and Felician harbors, or south to Soluna. The chief goods are **amber** from Orghin's coasts, **salt** from Krakelid, and **metal** from Sigsdal.

### Common Ships

- **Knarr** - a robust merchant vessel of about fifteen tons cargo, one mast, fifteen to twenty-five crew, and slow under sail
- **Drakskepp** - the dragon-ship: shallow-draft, fast, flat-bottomed, and war-suited, carrying four to eight tons and up to fifty or sixty passengers, its prow carved with the gods **Wödur** and **Elokir** and a dragon figurehead, each ship named and decorated by its owner

### Trade Partners

- Trakorien
- Felicien
- Soluna
- the barbarian lands of the northern coasts

## Military

There is no standing army save the jarls' **hirds**, up to about a hundred men each, sworn to blind loyalty in exchange for their keep. Most of the people, though, are battle-trained, and the realm can field a levy of up to thirty percent of its population.

### Weapons and Armor

The jordukerlar arm themselves to suit their land and their faith. The **Nordyxa**, the north-axe, is a two-handed weapon so heavy it demands a special technique; without the skill a warrior may strike only every other round and cannot parry between blows. The **Hylsspjut**, the sheath-spear, is a short-spear disguised as a walking-staff, its head hidden in a wooden or bone sheath; it cannot be thrown. Shields are large round wooden affairs, rimmed and bossed with metal; the central boss is the **fala**, and the painted band between rim and boss, the **rit**, shows scenes from the life of the war god **Thörm** or his children. The **Thörm-helm**, named for that god, is a metal skullcap fitted with eye- and nose-guards of metal bars, worn plain, lined with leather, or backed by mail.

## Religion

The jordukerlar worship many gods. The good ones form the family of the **Æsir**, at feud with the evil **Venir**. A slain warrior goes to **Æsgar** to join the gods' drinking-feasts, and so the people are fearless in battle. Worship is by **blot**, the sacrifice of livestock and war-loot but never of humans, made twice a year at midwinter and midsummer and on great occasions. The faith is spreading among most of the western barbarian peoples.

The chief gods are:

- **Wodur** - chief of the gods, lord of wisdom
- **Elokir** - god of the sea
- **Thörm** - god of war
- **Frajir** - goddess of fertility
- **Grabir** - god of the fine arts

## History

Around 1500 fO the dwarves came for the rich iron of what would become Sigsdal, living peacefully with the svartfolk. After the fall of **Melukha** in 1040 fO a small band of elves settled the southwest of the Kardskogen, seeking something there, perhaps a place holy to Mother Earth. Around 800 fO barbarians migrated north from Nargur.

The Conflux barely touched Jorduashur, and so began a long age of raiding the ravaged southern lands, with colonies planted in today's Klomellien and Magilre and a heyday of loot shipped north and sold on to the dwarves, until the plundered lands recovered. The last of the forts fell at the death of King **Harald Krokig** in 456 eO, though the blended state of jordukerlar and barbarians called **Hamur**, in south Klomellien, still survives.

Afterward came a long quiet age of yearly raids on Trakorien, Klomellien, Ransard, and Kardien, until those lands grew into organized states. Now trade often replaces raiding, though the dragon-ships still come.

## Foreign Relations

Abroad, Elfhelm Korp is treated as king of all Jorduashur, though at home he commands only Allnemark. Relations with the dwarves of Kward'zak are good and vital to Sigsdal's mines. With the elves of Alvskogen the realm keeps a wary trade for timber. Trakorien, Klomellien, Ransard, and Kardien, once the targets of raids, are now as often trading partners, while the eastern barbarians remain enemies whose raids on Krakelid bind its people together.
