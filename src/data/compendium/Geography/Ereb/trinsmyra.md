# Trinsmyra

Trinsmyra is the northernmost of Trakorien's islands, rocky and never wholly subdued, and the most musical of the four. It is rich in tin, lead, copper and silver, and its people, the karker, are famous for foul moods and a speech so thick with curses that it is said to suit poetry and damnation alike. They distrust all overlords on principle, and much of the island holds its independence to this day.

## Vumbra and the worship of Inashtar

The music-loving capital Vumbra stands on the southern plain of Kark and ships out the island's metals. Its patron is Inashtar, goddess of fire, war and unbridled lust, worshipped ecstatically with bonfires, music, dance and fighting-games. She has no organised church, for all women are held to share in the goddess herself, which gives the women of Trinsmyra a strong standing. The island guards the dreaded incendiary weapon vildeld, and the old royal house of Åålskalle once ruled here.

## Kark and the Vulferveden

The plains of Kark hold cliff-climbing livestock that haul carts up the crags, and the dark Vulferveden forest is ruled by Ao'ya, the Evil Bite, the legendary wolf-queen. The metal mines of Hymbergond are worked by slaves under the mercenary captain Cymba.

## Milacke and the highlands

The beautiful region of Milacke keeps its independence, its spear-peak mountains topped with castles and its ancient burial dolmens hung with curses; its chief city is Rakma, once the seat of the mad milackers. In the highlands the rebellious rungler ride the giant millipede skolopod over the steeps, and at Ard Gachlaf dwarves dwell in friendship with them. The folk of the Pillar Mountains hunt the leather-bat with golden pollen.

## Isakra

One of the realm's oldest cities, Isakra, also called Esach-Rha, was founded long ago by colonists of doomed Cruri. It holds the ever-growing temple Etemenanki, tended by its own order, and is famous for the sirens that lure whales to the shore by song.
