# Caranor

Caranor is the greatest of the flying islands, the ancestral home and holy place of the silver elves. Like all the flying islands it was set adrift in the sky in the primordial age when Altor was formed, a core of bedrock grown over with hardy plants; but where the rest range from a dozen metres to a few hundred across, Caranor spans some thirty-five kilometres, and it is no dead rock but a living thing, ensouled by the spirit Caran, whose island it is.

## Geography

The island is steeply hilled, its highest mountains ringing the rim and its centre milder and gentler, and at its heart lies Lake Saran, on whose red water-lilies the bards have made their songs. The climate is mild the year round, for Caran governs the weather and lets the rain fall mostly by night. Birds are everywhere among the birch and juniper groves, and the greatest beasts are the pegasi, which graze in herds and serve the silver elves as tame mounts.

## Caran

Caran is the spirit that gives the island both life and motion; "Caranor" means simply Caran's flying island. It steers the island where it will, though it usually lets the winds carry it slowly over Ereb, and it senses everything that happens within a good distance of the island's edge, taking the wild beasts there under its control as a guard against predators. It commands the weather and speaks mind to mind with the silver elves across many leagues, so that they always know where their island drifts. Necromancy comes hard on Caranor, and any undead thing that so much as touches the island is at once undone in a cloud of dust.

## People

Some eight hundred silver elves live on Caranor, with a few hundred nomads besides, and the island holds a scattering of other elf-folk: a handful of eagle-men and swan-maidens, dozens of pixies, and will-o-wisps in the birch woods. Since 531 there have also been rat-men, the Idri tribe, freed from slavery in a magician's fortress and given leave to settle here; they built the village of Idrit and now number around a hundred, living by hunting and farming. The silver elves themselves dwell in groups of thirty to fifty in villages of thatched wooden houses set in concentric rings, while the nomads pitch tents woven from the leaves of the net-plant.

## Government

Caranor is ruled by an autarch, elected to serve until they choose to step down, who governs in counsel with Caran and is aided by ministers called demiarchs. Since 512 the autarch has been the elf-woman Alena. Defence rests on a general levy: the militia, drawn up and led by the eagle-man Acwi, fields companies of light elven infantry alongside a disciplined band of rat-men, and in battle the elves fight with the edge that Caran's all-seeing eye gives them.

## Sights

Scattered across the island are its holy and storied places. At Lisia's Spring the swan-maiden Lisia keeps a pool in a birch wood and tends her healing herbs. At Casiri, the academy founded by Caran, the animist mistress Eseli teaches some forty or fifty pupils. Alatan, a birch grove on an open field, is the silver elves' chief shrine to the goddess and their gathering-place at the three festivals of the year, kept by the ancient elf Ledo. And in the Song Grove, where the wind through the birches by the brook becomes music, the harmonist-magician Deono has lived for ages among the instruments he collects.
