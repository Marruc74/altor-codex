# Felicien

![Felicien](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Felicien/Felicien1.jpg "Felicien")
![Felicien male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Felicien/Male1.jpg "Felicien male")
![Felicien female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Felicien/Female1.jpg "Felicien female")

The first telling of Felicien does not begin with its banners or its fleets, but with its harbors. Cities of pale stone rise above the sea, their terraces filled with traders, sailors, and voices from distant lands. Wealth moves constantly through its markets, measured in spice, grain, and amphorae carried across the water. Yet this prosperity is not carried by merchants alone. Much of the labor is done by slaves, taken in war or bought through trade, bound to the docks, the fields, and the oars of the great galleys. Beneath the noise of commerce lies a rigid order that few can escape.

From these shores, Felicien built its strength. Its ships are long and disciplined, its crews trained to move as one. The navy is not only a defense but a tool of control, securing trade routes and enforcing the will of the realm far beyond its coasts. Those who meet its fleets at sea rarely forget them. In Felicien, power flows from the water, but it is held in place by those who command both ships and people alike.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 170,000 (humans 100%; roughly half are slaves)
- **Capital:** Tyros (approx. 14,000 inhabitants)
- **Government:** Monarchy with elements of popular rule
- **Ruler:** King Kuthegil Halfbeard
- **Religion:** Worship of Paridon, god of the sea, and Philas, god of the land
- **Exports:** Handicrafts, gemstones, pearls, wine, stone
- **Imports:** Timber, fabrics, metals, slaves
- **Military:** A very strong fleet backed by a small, well-trained army

## Geography

Felicien occupies the southern part of the Tolan peninsula, claiming all land south of the Erbulasbergen. The realm is split in two by the Koalintviken, the Koalint Bay, along whose shores stand its three great cities. The northern half is called Kritos and the southern half the Pelenos Peninsula. The bay lies strategically between the eastern and western reaches of the Copper Sea, making Felicien one of its most important trade hubs.

Beyond the homeland, Felicien holds a scatter of colonies:

- **Bylos and Caraidon**: Slave-trading cities in old Dermanien.
- **The Hakkai islands**: An archipelago off the coast of Soluna.
- **The Gerdikk archipelago**: A mussel and pearl-fishing chain centered on the isle of Kea.

## People

The felicier are tall but slim, with long black hair, dark eyes, and sharp features that rarely betray emotion. They are enduring, strong-willed, and determined, and other peoples often judge them arrogant. They prize strength, resolve, cunning, pride, and a hard realism. For dress they favour light, airy clothing suited to the warm Copper Sea, and are fond of jewellery, above all bracelets, headbands, and brooches of gold and silver.

## Culture

Felicien despises manual labour, which by long custom is left entirely to slaves. Almost no free felicier farms; that work belongs to the unfree. Free men typically go as skippers, merchants, craftsmen, politicians, or soldiers, while free women turn to trade, banking, the crafts, and the army.

Child-rearing is famously strict, and the slaves who raise children may discipline them. Training begins early: at five a child learns sailing and running, at seven arithmetic, reading aloud, and writing, and at eleven chooses a future trade. The young are then trained until twenty in combat, crafts, seamanship, commerce, law, or, rarely, magic. At twenty a felicier is reckoned an adult and must support themselves; those who cannot must become farmers or fishermen.

## Language

The felicier speak two tongues, and guard the older as their most distinctive cultural mark:

- **Kimzon**: The old, elaborate language of officialdom and the upper classes, written in modified jorisk runes.
- **Feliciska**: The common spoken dialect, a form of jorisk understood by Kardier, Klomellier, and Zorakier - though its nautical and military terms are all drawn from kimzon.

## Society

Women are the central figures of Felician society. By ancient tradition free women do no hard physical labor, and so they long ago became its politicians, bankers, and craftsmen. Because the abundance of slaves frees them even from housework, women are nearly as common as men in trade, the crafts, and the army, and there is great equality between the sexes.

Families are relatively loose, and many young people leave home around the age of twenty to pursue a trade or military service.

### Slavery

Slaves make up roughly half of the population and fall into three groups:

- **Workers**: The majority, set to the fields, the fishing boats, the oars of the galleys, and any other heavy labor.
- **Servants**: Mostly women and eunuchs who keep households and raise children; trusted ones may serve as armed bodyguards, but only with a permit.
- **Courtesans**: Young, beautiful entertainers and companions.

Slaves go unpaid, though they are fed and housed, and have no legal protection, being equated with outlaws. Even so, killing a slave is risky, for it angers the owner and good slaves are scarce, and most felicier treat their slaves mildly, holding it bad luck to do otherwise. Freed slaves often stay on as paid servants, and to keep a staff of such freedmen is a mark of high status.

Elf slaves are the most prized of all, being hardworking, obedient, and long-lived, yet only the richest can afford them. This trade has bred a lasting enmity between the elves and the felicier. Several slave revolts have been crushed with great bloodshed.

## Government

Felicien is ruled by a king together with a council of fifteen. The council draws three members from each of the five great regions: one military man, one craftsman or merchant, and one from the council of the regional capital. Each of the fifteen holds a single vote, but the king alone holds four. A measure passes when the king wins the support of two whole regions, or of one entire profession plus one further vote. The kingship is hereditary, and the king may levy taxes on trade and the crafts.

## Economy

Despite few natural resources, Felicien is one of the richest lands in Ereb. Most felicier live by trade and craft around the Koalintviken, home to some of the richest men in the world. What the land does yield, it yields richly:

- **Cedar**: Worked into luxury furniture and the hulls of ships.
- **Gemstones**: The yellow oposer and green smalugger, mined in southern Pelenos - their glitter sometimes luring golden dragons from Cereval.
- **Limestone and marble**: An inexhaustible white stone from northern Pelenos that built the famous white cities and feeds the work of its sculptors.
- **Pearls**: Drawn from the eastern archipelago.
- **Purple dye**: A newly prized colour pressed from the purfuro shell around Kea.

Farming, fishing, and mining concentrate in the south, while Kritos yields fruit and grain. The national drink is chyros, a light sparkling wine.

## Technology

Felicien is underdeveloped in most crafts but masterful in war. Its weapons and armor are of the highest quality, their export forbidden and so widely smuggled. Its shipbuilding is a showpiece: sleek grey ships, fast and maneuverable, fitted with massive steel rams. Its sculptors and winemakers are excellent, but in farming and ordinary craft methods the felicier lag behind, a thing they will never admit.

## Military

Felicien relies above all on its navy, divided into two corps:

- **The marine corps**: Marines, two parts in five of the fleet, trained to fight on land and at sea alike.
- **The sea corps**: Trained for sea-warfare only.

A single galley carries some 300 galley-slaves at 200 oars, 100 soldiers, 25 catapult-shooters, 25 crew, and about 10 officers. On a war footing the marines field some 50 battle-galleys and the sea corps 75; in peacetime each holds 25. Sea ranks run from captain (one ship) to commodore (five), rear admiral (a flotilla of 25 galleys, 2,500 soldiers and 7,500 galley-slaves), and admiral (a whole corps).

The land army numbers only about 2,000 in peacetime, spread thin across the realm, with some 500 always guarding the Erbulas passes against Berendien and pirate raids. It also serves as police, and is ordered into ten corps under a general, with the ranks of colonel, captain, and lieutenant.

## Religion

The felicier are a sea-loving people, and their faith begins in the primal sea Zykidos, from which rose two opposed gods. Paridon ruled the depths; Philas dwelt near the surface and made the land and sky, becoming protector of all land, freshwater, and animals. Angered, Paridon created humans to be intelligent and superior to the work of Philas - which is why the felicier hold humans above all other folk, counting the halflings, elves, ankor, and dwarves of Philas's later making as lesser. Paridon is thus the chief god, with temples everywhere, and a small offering is made before every sea voyage. Philas is mild and wise, worshipped chiefly by vintners and hunters, his priests old farmers.

Philas's daughter by a cat is Felis, goddess of darkness and mystery, honoured by alchemists, astrologers, soothsayers, thieves, and some mages. She is shown as a beautiful dark woman who becomes a cat each night; cats are sacred to her, and dark-skinned folk fall under her protection. The felicier take their name from Felis.

The war god Galmon, a giant bearing a trident, was added during the Berendian wars of the 300s eO. He bears those who die with his name on their lips to the Halls of Honor, and the promise makes Felician soldiers fearless to the point of recklessness.

## History

The warrior-king Kiolomon the Seafarer once ruled the kimzoner on the great isle of Garumos off Akrogal, until the necromancer Valada Isle drove him out. With three ships under Kalameikos - whence the kimzoner are called the Kalameikiden, "Kalameikos' children," in their founding saga - they reached Tiron Cruvi, the Green Land, and the Copper Sea, settling Koalint Bay. There they raised the white cities: Tyros the Golden, their capital; Sidon the Dreaming; and Elikon the Pearl-shimmering. They named themselves felicier after Felis, and grew rich on trade with all peoples but the elves, who foresaw their doom.

Under Cleriomon II the realm expanded onto the Pelenos Peninsula and toward Soluna, until checked by the brown ankor. In 310 eO Dermanien invaded; the southern colonies fell, but the fleet stopped a full conquest, and King Kiomolon III became a Dermanian vassal. The vassalage held until 343, when the Dermanians set their general Assarhaddon on the throne as king. The insult provoked a revolt that drove Dermanien out, and the felicier kept the colonies of Bylos and Caraidon for their own.

The Berendian wars of 344 to 411 failed on land, for the felicier hate the mountains, but were won at sea. At the Battle of Selim in 392 the Felician fleet crushed the Zorakin fleet that had aided Berendien, and in 394 Zorakin ceded the Hakkai islands; peace came in 411 and has held since. The gravest trial came in the Sombatzian invasion of 499 eO, when the new slave-market of Sombatze armed slaves it had smuggled into Felicien and timed a fleet invasion with the worst slave revolt in the realm's history. Tyros and Sidon were besieged for two months, until King Karmos destroyed the relief squadron and crushed the revolt. The reprisal was savage: mass executions, the rebels cast into the sea as offerings to Paridon, and Sombatze made to pay vast reparations.

Now King Kuthegil Halfbeard, ruling an overpopulated land with a strong army, judges the time ripe to dominate the whole of the Copper Sea.

## Foreign Relations

The king issues letters of marque that take pirates into Felician service. A privateer so licensed may plunder ships sailing under certain flags, keeping half the loot and enjoying Felician protection - a lucrative trade, and a tool to force reluctant neighbors into dealing with Felicien.

Of late this has badly angered Erebos, whose merchants have suffered most. King Kuthegil denies that he protects the pirates, though every sign suggests he lies. Erebos has answered with armed escorts and by refusing to renew its trade deals, turning instead to Felicien's arch-enemy Berendien. The tension is rising, and observers call the Copper Sea a powder keg.

## Heraldry

The arms of Felicien bear a trireme, weapons, and a cluster of grapes. The trireme proclaims its mastery of the sea, the weapons its strength upon the land, and the grape-cluster the art and culture that flourish in its white cities.
