# Hynsolge

![Hynsolge](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Hynsolge/Hynsolge1.jpg "Hynsolge")
![Hynsolge male](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Hynsolge/Male1.jpg "Hynsolge male")
![Hynsolge female](/compendium/Geography/Ereb/Countries/Hynsolge/Female1.jpg "Hynsolge female")

The old roads of Hynsolge do not welcome travelers. They watch them. Stone walls lean inward as if listening, and the air carries the weight of decisions already made. This is a land where loyalty is worn like armor, and suspicion settles in the bones. Men walk with steady steps and heavy gazes, their drooping moustaches marking them as sons of a hard country shaped by wind, war, and quiet endurance. The king still holds his throne, but the realm bends under strain. Noble houses whisper and tighten their grip, while common folk endure in silence, caught between duty and survival. In Orkovia, the heart of the realm, streets are narrow and damp, and nothing is ever fully forgotten. Every stone remembers who stood, who fell, and who chose to remain. Hynsolge does not shout its strength. It endures it.

It is a beautiful eastern land torn by a long civil war that sets the feudal nobility against the king and the common folk who stand with him. The deified hero Fingal is its patron, the older Parbager pantheon holds the countryside, and beneath everything lies a hidden demon-cult whose roots reach back centuries. After a decade of burning farms and besieged barons, the war may at last be drawing toward its end.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 110,000, almost entirely human
- **Capital:** Orkovia (approx. 1,500)
- **Government:** Feudal monarchy
- **Ruler:** King Mefimor IV
- **Religion:** Fingal is the realm's patron; the Parbag cult is strong in the countryside; ancestor and spirit worship persists
- **Exports:** Livestock and breeding stallions, handicrafts
- **Imports:** Metals, luxuries, fabrics, slaves
- **Military:** A small but strong king-loyal army, supplemented heavily by mercenaries fielded in the civil war

The realm is poor, its wealth drained by years of fighting. It is presently divided into thirteen provinces, or counties, only one of which the king still rules outright.

## Geography

Hynsolge sits in the east of Ereb, bordered by mountains to the north and east, the Copper Sea to the west, the forests of Landori to the northwest, and Krun to the south. Its Copper Sea coasts are even and fine, good for fishing and bathing but poor for shipping, save for the great harbor of Fervidun in the county of Sirigon. Inland lie broad farmlands where maize, sugar beet, and a few lemon groves grow, while the less fertile north is given over to horse-breeding.

Many deep rivers cut the land, calm enough for riverboats and thick with fish and the small dragon-shaped crayfish prized at every table. Two forests are named with caution. The Hyllmar Forest teems with game and shelters the rare brook-bear. The dreaded Gormowac's Forest is dense and ill-famed, choked with the strangling strangler-vine, home to monstrous boars and blood-leeching tree-leeches, and rumored to hide great cats. The civil war has scarred the countryside, leaving burned farmsteads and the flooded course of the Gridevanda River.

## People

The hynsolger are short, between 165 and 170 centimeters, lean and big-footed, with lightly tanned skin and black or dark-brown hair. The men wear theirs long and often braided, beneath great drooping moustaches, and all of them carry strong, high cheekbones. Country folk favor light, hard-wearing work clothes against the heat. Formal dress is far stricter: for men the tuxan, a black short-jacketed suit with a wide red silk sash, and for women a voluminous gown of white and red.

At heart they remain barbarians, hot-tempered and quick to arms. A young man must pass his manhood-trials before he is reckoned grown, proving himself at riding, at the spear, throwing-spear, shortbow, and bola, in unarmed combat, and in the hunt. In the countryside they are deeply superstitious, hanging iron nails over their doors and herb-pouches against ghosts. A thunderstorm is read as an evil omen, and anyone caught outdoors during one may be suspected of witchcraft or Julsuian worship. Lynchings are not unheard of.

## Language

The hynsolger once spoke prangti, a gesture-heavy barbarian tongue with no written form, now all but extinct. Today they speak hynsolgiska, a jorisk dialect close to Zorakian and Kardian.

## Society

The burghers exist only in Orkovia and perhaps Greyburg, and they back the Uprising; some among them favor a bloodless "silent revolution," after the manner of the author Egilor Featherpen and his book *The Silent Revolution*. Beneath the nobility, the common folk have labored under growing oppression for generations. Three provinces, Brangoria, Mergilia, and Ansorvia, have held their peasants in serfdom since 381 eO.

The thirteen provinces and their seats stand in very different states:

- **Hamellien** (Orkovia, approx. 1,500): the royal province, ruled directly by King Mefimor IV
- **Baldor** (Olsehem, approx. 300): its baron forced to join the royalists
- **Kunhaga** (Ghamburg, approx. 500): now held by royalists
- **Jamarund** (Sabarun, approx. 400): its baron besieged by rebels
- **Varpodia** (Bolbosa, approx. 300): its baron besieged
- **Sirigon** (Fervidun, approx. 3,000): at open war with the king
- **Brangoria** (Greyburg, approx. 1,250): a politically free city since 538 eO
- **Mergilia** (Bedorum, approx. 500): under Abrahim Stenklo
- **Metipov** (Orlangin, approx. 250): forced into a pact with the New Army
- **Galdinor** (Arbine, approx. 300): under Abrahim Stenklo
- **Ansorvia** (Kandor, approx. 600): ruled by Abrahim Stenklo as its new baron
- **Pendor** (Wolkor): destroyed, washed away in the flood of 597
- **Olakia** (Bemir): destroyed, washed away in the flood of 597

## Government

For two centuries the monarchy was strong, but it has since lost its grip to the feudal nobility. The king now rules in full only his own province of Hamellien, where he keeps legislative and judicial power, appoints officials, and leads the army, while a council of twenty learned men handles taxation. Of late he shares his rule in Orkovia with a loyal council of twenty merchants and priests.

Each of the other provinces is ruled by a hereditary baron whose eldest son inherits and who wields total power over his land. Beneath each baron stand some ten marquesses, who collect taxes, raise warriors, and may dub knights, while the baron himself keeps up to a dozen knights of his own.

## Economy

Hynsolge lives off its land and its horses. The fertile interior yields maize, sugar beet, and lemons, while the north breeds the prized stallions sold abroad. Craftsmen produce fine handicrafts, and the realm imports the metals, luxuries, fabrics, and slaves it cannot make for itself. Almost all of this passes through Fervidun, the only true harbor, which makes the feudal nobility of Sirigon enormously rich on the port fees the other provinces must pay. The war has otherwise left the realm poor.

## Military

The king's forces answer to his seven generals, the Mactubve, and consist chiefly of Fingal-loyal infantry stiffened by cavalry. The feudal armies differ greatly. Sirigon fields mostly mercenaries, orcs and felicier, backed by siege engines, while Brangoria keeps an all-human force. The New Army of the common folk, led by Abrahim Stenklo, is drawn from peasants and is mostly infantry, armed with everything from pitchforks to true weapons.

## Religion

Faith in Hynsolge runs along the same fault line as its politics, and the priesthoods have taken sides in the war.

### The Fingal Cult

The realm's official patron is the deified hero Fingal Orchnackare, the "Orc-cleaver," also called Fingal Gorrond, god of military strength and of the realm's unification. He is depicted as a tall, muscular, bearded man in mail, bearing a huge greatsword upon a giant black mail-clad stallion. His symbol is a seven-pointed yellow star on dark blue, the star that appeared at his death in the autumn of 142 eO. His clergy and temple-knights are trained in both arms and learning; they swear to obey their superiors, to oppose the realm's fragmentation, and never to flee from svartfolk. The cult is wholly royalist, and so unloved by the nobility.

### The Parbager, the Urgods

Older than the Fingal cult and traced back to about 900 fO, the Parbager faith holds the countryside. It is a divine family of nine, headed by Anyak the Light-giver and his wife Angusia, Warder of the Gates. A worshipper usually devotes himself to one god while honoring the rest, all save Julsuian.

- **Anyak**: the Light-giver, god of light and friend of the sun and salamanders, strongest in the east; his symbol a yellow four-rayed sun, his white-marble temples kept by an eternal flame and priests in white tunics who never cover their heads
- **Angusia**: Anyak's wife, goddess of wisdom and justice, who guards the gates to Bolgove's silent halls; her symbol a golden key on a black hexagon, her great temple Pervusib-Angusia the seat of the feudal-friendly Angusia Order
- **Ulvar**: the Dark-bringer, god of darkness, moon, and night-cold, son of Anyak yet no rival to him; a small cult with no temples, his full-moon symbol hung over many a bed
- **Grilovana**: Queen of the Springs, Ulvar's wife and youngest daughter of the Urfathers, weather-goddess of running water surrounded by birds; her symbol a white dove on light blue, popular with farmers and fishers, with neither temple nor priesthood
- **Samyyd**: Mistress of the Meadows, a beloved fertility goddess with a small temple in every village; her symbol the circle of eternal rebirth, her priestesses given to charity and poor-relief
- **Reogil**: Lord of the Beasts, god of forests and wild animals, whose servants never kill for sport and offer a slain beast's heart to him; his symbol a stag's head, his antler-helmed priests and followers wearing no metal armor
- **Bolgove**: Mistress of Silence, goddess of sleep, dream, mystery, and death, daughter of Ulvar and Grilovana; she bears no known symbol, her priestesses favoring black and often versed in Spiritism and Illusionism
- **Bolthar**: the Axe-bearer, war god and son of Samyyd and Reogil, whose followers prize strength and weapon-skill, favor the two-handed axe, and wear no metal armor in battle; his symbol a stylized two-handed axe
- **Julsuian**: Slave of Frivolity, the family's black sheep, god of chaos, drugs, drink, sloth, and carnal lust, worshipped in secret and strongest in the underworld of prostitutes, smugglers, and addicts; his symbol two linked rings threaded by an arrow, often tattooed

The Angusia followers favor the feudal nobility and are funded by them, their justicer advising barons on law and administration, and they are strong in the north and in Sirigon. Worshippers of Samyyd, Grilovana, and Reogil are mostly common folk who lean royalist, while Julsuian cultists are usually apolitical anarchists, grown numerous in Olakia and south Pendor since the flood. The other ancestor-cults stay neutral.

### The Star Doctrine

Preached from 421 eO by Mustansir Omurtaag and rooted among the nobility of Brangoria and Mergilia, the Star Doctrine worships not a god but the universe and the heavenly bodies, and busies itself with magic, divination, alchemy, astrology, and the occult sciences. It is a secret society of four circles. The Outer circle, some eighty-five in the hundred, provides the income; the Second, a tenth, are brainwashed cultists; the Third, a scant few, are occult scholars; and the Focal circle are the leaders, schooled in spiritism and necromancy and topped by the high priest known as Onaabys's servant, who takes his orders from Parikila. The sect is behind a string of kidnappings carried out to feed its hidden master.

### Shamans

Older even than the Parbager cult and nearly vanished, the shamans linger chiefly in Olakia, where the bandit-bands have revived tribal society and ancestor-worship.

## History

- **~1350 to 1065 fO**: Krun grants Hynsolge to allied barbarian tribes; the Krun War breaks out in 1080, and in 1065 the hynsolger beat Krun at Grivela, founding the empire Jorpagna; wars with Efaro follow
- **~900 fO**: Jorpagna conquers Hynsolge; the jorisk upper class flourishes while the locals are impoverished or enslaved, and jori becomes the common tongue
- **~800 fO**: the province Lasutyp, later Nidland, is made a sub-province of Hynsolge
- **~750 fO**: Akrogal nomads on hippogrifs attack Krun, and Hynsolge hides its great war-treasures
- **599 fO**: the Third Conflux, a three-tailed comet likely from the red moon Raukhra, strikes Hynsolge; plague kills a fifth of the people and another fifth flee
- **412 fO**: the warrior Gordil Mauroc unites the realm and founds the capital Fervidun; he is murdered in 402, and the realm collapses into oligarchy
- **~50 to 124 eO**: orc raids culminate in an invasion under the blood-elf Korumil Gormowac; Fingal Gorrond crushes the orc army on the future site of Orkovia in 124, is made king, and founds Orkovia in 127; Lasutyp joins voluntarily in 139
- **142 eO**: the childless king dies, the astrologer Elias the Far-seeing sees a new star, and Fingal is deified as patron god
- **312 eO**: the weak king Tanbul loses power to the nobility as oppression of the people grows
- **375 to 381 eO**: a peasant revolt in Ansorvia and Mergilia, aided by dwarves, is crushed, and those provinces are bound to serfdom
- **419 eO**: the Dwarves' March toward Cereval forces Hynsolge to halt them, further weakening the king
- **421 eO**: Mustansir Omurtaag, advisor to baron Aurolf Ironfist of Brangoria, founds the Star Doctrine after a vision
- **468 eO**: the eastern province Lasutyp falls to Vicotnik the Archmage and becomes Nidland
- **538 eO**: Greyburg buys its freedom and becomes a refuge for political fugitives
- **543 eO**: Brangoria's nobles begin slave-trading, and the slaves begin to vanish without trace
- **580 to 587 eO**: Agila Dubbelhand leads a small revolt in Mergilia and is imprisoned, smuggling out poems that spread among the people
- **596 to 606 eO**: flood in the southwest brings famine; the people besiege the nobles of Mergilia and Ansorvia, and the Uprising begins in 604
- **604 to 610 eO**: Abrahim Stenklo, ennobled by Mefimor IV, leads the New Army; the king declares war on Sirigon in 606 and subdues Baldor, Kunhaga, Galdinor, north Pendor, and Mergilia, where the rebels free Agila Dubbelhand. The year is now 610 eO

## The Uprising

Centuries of growing noble tyranny finally broke into open revolt, and the common folk now vent their long-stored anger against the nobility. The king stands on the people's side, which lends the rebellion a moral weight no peasant rising usually enjoys, and the imprisoned poet-hero Agila Dubbelhand has become its martyr and rallying cry. His smuggled lines pass from mouth to mouth: "The twisted horn we follow, the time of slavery is over."

The war has raged for roughly a decade and may at last be ending. King Mefimor IV has subdued Baldor and Kunhaga, and Abrahim Stenklo leads the New Army out of Ansorvia, having just beaten Mergilia and won peace in Galdinor and Pendor, though bandits still roam the south. His army is weakened and awaits foreign mercenaries, even as it besieges Brangoria and seems poised to strike at its baron, Rigurt Riikov. Two strongholds hold out above the rest: Brangoria, and Sirigon with its harbor able to import fresh mercenaries. Should the royalists win, the king will regain his full power, abolish the hereditary nobility, and reward the Uprising's champions with the empty provinces.

## Sirigon and Fervidun

The county of Sirigon is the realm's great holdout against the crown, and the seat of its wealth. Its capital, Fervidun, was founded by Gordil Mauroc in 412 fO and was once the capital of all Hynsolge. It is the realm's only true harbor and trade city, which lets its feudal nobility profit hugely while the other provinces pay heavy port fees and royalists are barred outright. The city guard is the Angusia Order's magistrates, who dispense a justice weighted by social strata and tilted toward the nobility, and a large slum sprawls beneath the wealth.

Sirigon's wealthy baron, Anargel Steelboot, has hired orc and Felician mercenaries and holds off the king with siege engines. So long as Fervidun's quays remain open, the county can buy the soldiers it needs to keep the crown at bay, and it remains, with Brangoria, the strongest of the rebel-held lands.

## The Comet's Dark Secret

Beneath the realm's wars runs an older corruption. The comet that struck in 599 fO came from Raukhra, the Red Moon, and radiates pure evil and chaos; it lay underwater most of the time, which dampened its power, but through it the demon Onaabys, the Soul-eater, sent a vision to Mustansir Omurtaag in 421 eO and so founded the Star Doctrine. In 425 the Focal circle drained the comet's crater, summoned Onaabys, and had him sire a child upon Mustansir's wife, an act whose aftermath killed most of the cultists in a great epidemic.

The boy, Tervel, was sickly until they learned he must devour one soul each week to survive, his inheritance from the demon. He then grew explosively, reaching full adulthood in eight years, killed his stepfather, took his post as advisor to Brangoria's baron, and took the name Parikila, the "father-slayer," turning to dark magic. To secure a victim each week without rousing suspicion, Parikila had the baron let Greyburg buy its freedom, drawing in more people to take, and begin the slave-trade that explains the slaves who never stop vanishing.

## Heraldry

The arms of Hynsolge bear a silver seven-pointed star, raised as the sign of the realm's unity - the same star that blazed at the death of the hero Fingal, drawing the once-fractured land together under one crown.
