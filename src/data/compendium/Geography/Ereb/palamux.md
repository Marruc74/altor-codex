# Palamux

Palamux is the oldest and most populous of Trakorien's islands, the realm's mother island, where statecraft is prized over the sword and the people are brooders and thinkers, their tempers darkened by the slow salting of their soil. The cradle of the whole realm lies here, and so do its strangest holy places and its most dangerous wilds.

## The Holy Kishatet

The strongest seat of the truth-god Shamash is the Holy Kishatet, an ascetic theocracy ruled from the holy city Albarunzia by the god-king Shar Kishati'n and his thirteen crystal-lords. The faithful shun hypocrisy, vanity and lies, keeping to study, meditation and heavy tithes. Mirrors, images and ornament are forbidden across its lands, confiscated or tarred over at the border, and even public laughter is banned.

## Fokale and the Kargomväggen

The inner plain about Fokale is scorched almost beyond habitation, ruined by centuries of salt-water irrigation and ringed by the Kargomväggen, a wall of mirror-glazed rock a thousand metres high that bakes the basin within to a furnace heat. Near it stand the ancient city Yoh Niur and its salt mines, and the oasis of Na Yoh.

## Nastrol and the Eniaken

In the devotion-land of Nastrol stand the cities of HOXOH, the city of illusions, and Balgumel, served by four orders of monks. Across the north of the island runs the Eniaken, a colossal living fungus-wall that drifted in long ago and was planted as a barrier; the monks of the Hypherer order tend and guard it from their fortress, for behind it lies ill-famed Kargom, a land of moon-worship, necromancy and cannibalism.

## Lasemos, Laabne and the grey eminences

Feud-ridden Lasemos, a clutch of quarrelling principalities, is the cradle of the Trakorian realm. Neighbouring Laabne is a land of ash-valleys, once drained like a leech by the grey eminences, the magician-tyrants who built the realm's first academy at Voag Aspede, until the hero Baskimer Brushane overthrew them, united Lasemos and banned all magic there.

## Moskorien and the western marches

The clay plains of Moskorien are holy to the white tapir and field the heavy war-chariots drawn by six-legged battle-rhinos. The giant-folk tolgulder hold the western marches against the horrors beyond, travelling only barefoot and on foot. In the south lie the oak-forests of Gwondel Leigu, home to an ancient elf-folk long allied to the klavyker colonists, and the proud, bearded barbosker of Ziddisbar, who quarry the green-blue ziddisgranit.

## The Copper Mountains

The reclusive dwarf realm of the Copper Mountains was settled by dwarves who fled slavery in the Kishatet's copper-pits and carved out a kingdom of their own, where they dwell apart from the affairs of men.

## Stegos

Off the island lies Stegos, an island cast into permanent darkness under Shamash's eternal wrath for its ancient betrayal of the Kishatet. Its capital and its only sound bear names of divine vengeance, and for two hundred years no daylight touched it.
