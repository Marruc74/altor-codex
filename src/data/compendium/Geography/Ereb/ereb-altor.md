# Ereb Altor

Ereb is the great central continent of the world of Altor, and the heart of the known world for most of the peoples of this compendium. It is a land of many realms and many tongues, ringed by water and by neighbouring continents, and its long history of risen and fallen powers underlies nearly every story told here.

## The Lay of the Land

To the south Ereb meets the bright Copper Sea, about which most of its great trading nations are gathered. Inland lies the strange Golwynda Sea, a vast body of fresh water that bulges upward higher than the seas around it. Beyond Ereb's borders lie the other continents of Altor: Soluna, Akrogal and, across the western mountains, Samkarna, whose Dragon-Back range holds the highest peaks in all the world.

## The Ages of Ereb

Ereb is old. Dragons are the only thinking creatures left from the world's prehistory, before the gods wiped the slate clean; the elves, humans and dwarves came into being some nine thousand years before Odo. In the Bronze Age three high cultures rose and fell, Melukha, Golwynda and Yndar, and from them came music and architecture, elemental magic and mentalism, and the darker arts of necromancy and spiritism. When the dwarves learned to smith iron in their mountains, the iron age began. Later still the Empire of Jorpagna grew to rule the heart of Ereb, until it was broken in the catastrophe remembered as the Third Conflux, around 599 before Odo, after which civilisation sank into a long Dark Age. Out of that decline rose the realms of the present day.

## The Peoples

Most of Ereb's folk are humans, and most of its nations about the Copper Sea, among them Zorakin, Kardien, Berendien and Erebos, trace their speech to the New-jori tongue descended from fallen Jorpagna. Elves and dwarves keep to their own holds and forests, the dark folk to the wild and broken country, and a hundred lesser peoples fill the spaces between. It is a crowded, quarrelsome, much-storied continent, and the stage on which the chronicles of the codex play out.
