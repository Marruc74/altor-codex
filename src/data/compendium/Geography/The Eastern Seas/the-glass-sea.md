# The Glass Sea

The Glass Sea, known to the old mariners as Glashavet, is among the most feared of all the waters east of Altor. Its name is no poet's flourish but a plain warning, for the sea is choked with reefs of clear, glassy stone that lie hidden beneath the surface. These reefs are translucent and edged like broken crystal, and they cut hull and flesh alike with the ease of a honed blade.

Few captains will attempt the crossing, and fewer still return to speak of it. The waters are reckoned all but impassable, and the wrecks that litter the shallows stand as silent counsel to any who would test them. To sail the Glass Sea is to trust to fortune over seamanship, for no chart can mark every reef, and the clearest water often hides the sharpest stone.

## Overview

- **Region:** The Eastern Seas
- **Hazard:** Translucent, razor-sharp reefs; nearly impassable to ships

## Waters

The sea takes its name from the reefs that fill it: spans of clear, glasslike stone that rise unseen toward the surface. Their edges are as keen as flensing knives, and a vessel driven against them is opened from stem to stern. Because the reefs share the colour of the water itself, even a careful lookout may sight them only when it is too late to come about. The whole of the Glass Sea is held to be nearly impossible to navigate, and it is shunned by all but the most desperate or foolhardy of seafarers.
