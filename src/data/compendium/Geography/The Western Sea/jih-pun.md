Far to the west, beyond seas that few dare cross, lies Jih-Pun, a land spoken of more in stories than in maps. It is a realm of steep mountains and mist filled forests where cedar trees rise like silent watchers and ancient shrines cling to cliffs and valleys. Its people live in careful balance between discipline and fear, for the world they walk is not theirs alone.

Samurai serve with unwavering loyalty, bound by oath and tradition, while shinobi move unseen through the hidden spaces between power and secrecy. Yet steel alone does not rule this land. Spirits linger in rivers and forests, demons are said to sleep beneath stone and fire, and the dead are not always willing to depart.

By day, Jih Pun is a place of order and beauty, of courts, poetry, and quiet ritual. By night, it becomes something else, where lantern light flickers and the unseen draws close. Few from Ereb ever reach its shores, and those who do return with stories that sound like dreams or warnings.

## Society

Jih Pun is a proud and ancient civilization ruled by nobles, warlords, and an imperial divine court. Honor matters deeply, but so do appearances and obligation. A smile may hide a vendetta three generations old.

## The Warrior's Way

In Jih Pun it is duty and honor, not the gods, that bind a warrior, and a debt of obligation may outlast a lifetime. The arts of war are prized above raw strength: a blade here is finer and more elegant than any forged in Ereb, and skill and timing count for more than brute force. A warrior's profession is his rank, and to bear arms at all is to hold a place in the order of things.

## Social Order

Society is a strict ladder, and every soul knows its rung. Lowest are the outcast Eta, the unclean, to whom the shadow-walking shinobi themselves belong; above them rise the commoners of the under- and middle classes, then the landless and the lesser nobility, and at the summit the high nobility and the imperial court. Birth sets the rung, but a warrior's profession fixes his standing within it.

## People

### Samurai

Not only soldiers, but retainers, duelists, magistrates, monster-slayers, and guardians of sacred places. Bound by oath and tradition.

### Shinobi

Clans that move unseen through the hidden spaces between power and secrecy. They may serve lords, temples, or secret pacts with spirits.

### Ronin

A samurai without a lord is a ronin, masterless and adrift, his honor his own to keep or to lose. Some sell their swords as they may; others wander in search of a cause worthy of their blade.

### Kensei

The kensei is the aged sword-master, a warrior who has given a lifetime to a single blade and passed beyond mere skill into something near to art.

### Budoka

Below the sword-bearing classes stand the budoka, masters of the empty-handed fighting arts, who carry no sword and need none.

## Arms and Armour

The samurai bears the dai-sho, the paired long and short blades, the katana and the wakizashi, that none below his class may carry. The high nobility alone wear the o-yoroi, the great laced armour, light and beautiful and proof against the cut, while lesser warriors make do with plainer harness. In all things the smiths of Jih Pun favour craft over weight, and their work is the envy of any Ereban armourer who has seen it.

## Martial Schools

The warriors of Jih Pun train in schools as the swordsmen of Ereb train in their academies, but here the arts of the empty hand are held in equal honor with the blade.

- **Karate, the open hand** - the most widespread, a pure fighting art that uses no weapons at all.
- **Kenpo** - given more to kicks and evasions, and to the nunchaku and the staff.
- **Ju-jutsu, the soft school** - the art of self-defence, of throws and locks that turn an attacker's own strength against him.
- **Ninjutsu, the ninja's way** - secret, taught only within the hidden clans, joining the blade and the shadow-warrior's strange weapons to the techniques of war.

## Religion

Shrines, ancestor rites, purification, sacred mountains, and temple orders maintain the balance between mortals and the spirits that dwell beyond. Cursed battlefields and ancient seals are part of daily spiritual life.

## The Spirit World

The unseen world is unusually close in Jih Pun. Many beings share the land with humans:

- **Fox spirits**: charming and deceptive, sometimes protective
- **Oni**: horned beings haunting ruined strongholds
- **River spirits**: territorial and demanding of offerings
- **Tengu**: proud and dangerous, dwelling in the high peaks
- **The dead**: some linger where duty, grief, or vengeance remain unfinished
- **Ancient demons**: sealed beneath temples, caves, or volcanic stone
