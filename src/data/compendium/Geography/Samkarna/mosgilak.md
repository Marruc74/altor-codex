Mosgilak is a realm of the reptile-men, set within the wet and teeming expanse of Samkarna. The reptile-men who hold it are a people far advanced beyond the crude lizard-folk known in Ereb - builders of a true civilisation, ordered and old, with knowledge and craft that the swamp-dwelling lizards of the north could never match.

Theirs is a faith turned toward the south, for they worship the great salamanders of the south pole and look to them as their gods. Toward all others they show neither mercy nor kinship. Outsiders who come within their reach are taken as slaves, and in time they are eaten. To stray into Mosgilak is to be counted not as a guest nor even as an enemy, but as meat.

## Overview

- **Type:** Realm of the reptile-men
- **Religion:** Worship of the south-pole salamanders
- **People:** Reptile-men, more advanced than Ereb's lizard-folk

## Geography

Mosgilak lies within Samkarna, the great southern continent of wet forests and endless swamp. Like the rest of that land it is warm and rain-soaked, teeming with deadly life, and it grows wetter still toward the south where the salamander-gods are said to dwell.

## The Reptile-Men

The reptile-men of Mosgilak are markedly more advanced than the lizard-folk of Ereb, with a settled and sophisticated society of their own. They are masters of their drowned country and suffer no rivals within it.

## Religion

Their worship is given to the salamanders of the south pole, vast beings they hold to be divine. The whole turning of their faith is southward, toward the cold ends of the world where these creatures are believed to abide.

## Outsiders

Strangers are not welcomed in Mosgilak. Those who fall into the hands of the reptile-men are enslaved, set to labour, and at last devoured. No bargain or appeal is known to soften this fate.
