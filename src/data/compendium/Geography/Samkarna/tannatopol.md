# Tannatopol

Tannatopol is the necromancer-isle that lies some three kilometres south-east of Krilloan, a narrow volcanic island wrapped in a grey fog that never lifts. It rose from the sea in 366 eO, and the necromancer Vlad Kamantur founded a school of his art upon it almost at once. Around two thousand living souls dwell there now, and close to a thousand of the dead.

## Overview

- **What it is:** A fog-bound volcanic island of necromancers, off the coast of Krilloan
- **Risen:** From the sea in 366 eO
- **Founder:** The necromancer Vlad Kamantur
- **At its heart:** A necromantic meganode, one of the great death-magic nodes of the world
- **Its orders:** Ordo Corporem, Ordo Demonicum, and the Black Rose Brotherhood

## The Meganode

The island sits upon one of the strongest nodes of death-magic anywhere in the world, and the node bends everything around it. Within its reach a death-spell costs half its usual strength, and a necromancer's spent power returns to him twice as fast. Any who die on the isle rise again as ghosts; those buried there walk as zombies; food rots and water fouls within days; and an animist's magic fails him utterly, his charms turning useless or bursting in his hands. Children born on Tannatopol are said to come into the world with death-magic already in the blood.

## The Black City and its Guardians

The chief settlement is the Black City, some five hundred buildings raised over older temples that run down into the rock. The node is strongest at the burial-ground; nearby lie the Well of Death, whose water carries sickness, and the black cliff where the witches hold their masses. The isle is guarded by skeletons and night-wolves, by perytons and pillars that paralyse the unwary, and by Morriart, the dragon that once terrorised Krilloan, slain there long ago and raised again here as a thing of the grave.

## The Orders

Three orders share the isle. Ordo Corporem, founded in 381 eO, gathers its necromancers. Ordo Demonicum holds its demonologers. And the Black Rose Brotherhood is the order of its witches, bound to a demon of their own.
