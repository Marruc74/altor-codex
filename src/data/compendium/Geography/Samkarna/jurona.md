# Jurona

Jurona is the great southern swamp of Samkarna, a drowned country where the land and the water are never wholly apart. Toward its northern edge it begins as tangled mangrove, the roots wading out into brackish shallows, and as one travels south the mangroves give way to forests of giant ferns that close overhead and shut out the sky. This is the wettest end of a wet continent, where the rains scarcely cease and the heat hangs heavy over still black water.

The swamp teems with monstrous life. Huge amphibians and lizards move through the shallows and the fern-shadow, grown to sizes unknown in gentler lands, and the deep interior is whispered of as the home of hidden realms where the dinosaurs still live. The great rainforest of Morginnerskogen does not so much end as drown, its margins dissolving into the Jurona swamps.

Beyond the swamps lies Blindhavet, the Blind Sea, a fog-bound water where vision fails and no horizon can be found. Its grey shroud hides a worse danger than the mist alone, for the sea is choked with man-eating algae that take the unwary who drift too far into the murk.

## Overview

- **Type:** Southern swamp and fog-bound sea
- **Terrain:** Mangrove shading into giant ferns
- **Hazards:** Giant amphibians and lizards; man-eating algae of Blindhavet

## Geography

Jurona sprawls across the southern reaches of Samkarna, a subtropical land of long dry spells broken by heavy rains, with a yearly mean of around plus ten degrees and cold nights. It is wet and swampy throughout, growing ever rainier toward the south. The mangrove coasts of the north shade gradually into stands of giant fern, and the rainforest of Morginnerskogen fades away into the swamp at its borders.

## Wildlife

The swamps are alive with deadly creatures. Huge amphibians and lizards thrive in the warmth and water, and rumour holds that hidden among the ferns lie lost realms where the dinosaurs survive to this day.

## Blindhavet

Blindhavet, the Blind Sea, lies beyond the swamps under a permanent shroud of fog. Sailors who venture into it lose all sight of land and sky. Worse still, its waters carry man-eating algae, so that the mist conceals not only the way but the death waiting within it.
