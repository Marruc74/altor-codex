# Krilloan

Krilloan is the great port-city of the Sanritra peninsula, in the north-east of the jungle continent of Samkarna, a little south of the equator. Some eighty thousand souls live there, on a narrow strip of coast caught between the high plateau of Forithos and the sea, with the rainforest pressing in from the west.

It is a city-republic in name, ruled by a council and a Grand Vizier. In truth it is ruled by the magicians of Ordo Magica, who hold the council in their hand and enforce their will through a guard of seventeen swords. Above the lawful faiths run the demon-cults of the Oktagon; beneath the canals lie the sewers where the hunted hide; and out in the bay, behind its grey fog, the necromancer-isle of Tannatopol watches the coast.

Krilloan lives by trade, and by one wind. The strait that links it to the wider world runs only one way, and a city of merchants must wait, sometimes for years, on the rare southerly that lets a fleet sail home laden. When that wind is due the place fills to half again its number, and every other purse in the markets belongs to a stranger.

## Overview

- **Location:** The north-east of the Sanritra peninsula, in north-east Samkarna, just south of the equator
- **People:** About 80,000, swelling toward 120,000 when the RhabdoRhinn wind is due
- **Government:** A city-republic; the Grand Vizier Arkondor II reigns as a figurehead
- **Real power:** Ordo Magica, the order of magicians
- **Religion:** Complete freedom of worship, with no dominant faith
- **Coin:** The Krilloan vesir, in copper, silver and gold
- **Reached by:** The Way Out, the one-way strait, on the rare RhabdoRhinn wind
- **Exports:** gems, precious metals and woods, drugs, spices, cloth, slaves, worked magic
- **Imports:** iron, hides, wine, salt, weapons

## Geography and Surroundings

Krilloan sits on a thin shelf of coast at the foot of Forithos, a plateau of two hundred and fifty kilometres by a hundred that rises more than two hundred metres above the lowland. The folk call it the Fault, and it is the city's shield: it walls Krilloan off from the north and east, so that the only easy approach is by sea or up the coast. To the west and south-west the rainforest closes in, a green country of heavy rain and dangerous beasts where a traveller makes half his usual pace. The one road inland is the Old Stone Road, laid over forty years in the third century to join Krilloan with the lesser port of Kiorsham, on the Copper Sea beyond the plateau.

The coast about the city is full of strange things. The blue Crystal Mountain, the Krillghin, is the cooled heart of a dead underground volcano. Out of the shallows rise the stone pillars of Meh-Sylfhin, six and twenty of them, some two hundred metres tall, all that is left of an ancient river that once ran here. Far to the north-east, in the hills, the cloister of St Kleol stands empty but for one mad hermit. And three kilometres out to the south-east lies Tannatopol, the necromancer-isle, wrapped in a fog that never lifts.

The seas around Krilloan are ruled by wind and current. The Way Out, the strait between the Copper Sea and the Serpent Lake, runs one way only, southward, so that a ship may slip down it with ease but cannot beat back up against the flow. The single thing that opens the strait to northbound ships is the RhabdoRhinn, "the venom-frog's breath," a hard southerly that blows perhaps once or twice in five years and lasts about two months. Twice each spring and twice each autumn come the Dragon Winds, the Angyrth Kallabja, storms so fierce that roughly one ship in five is lost to them.

## The City and its Districts

Canals cut Krilloan into ten districts and the citadel, and the canals are as much its streets as any paved road. A few are clean enough to drink; most are not.

- **Meh-Mitzur** is the rich heart of the city, gathered about the Great Square, where the council-house and the harbour-court stand among the mansions of the merchant princes.
- **Meh-Dachaur** is the great southern slum, the most crowded district of all, holding the harbour, the slave-market, and the Thieves' Square where executions are held.
- **Meh-Sylfhin**, raised on the tall stone pillars, is a wealthy and guarded quarter, and the tower of Ordo Magica rises here, seventy-six floors of it.
- **Meh-Taupour** is the sinking city, settling about a hand's breadth into the sea each year, a haunt of criminals; behind its wall lies the Plague-town.
- **Meh-Krillghin**, beneath the Crystal Mountain, is the city's pleasure-quarter, thick with temples, taverns and houses of joy, and home to the guild-palace of the Althair.
- **Meh-Achladi**, the tile-roofed district of the middle sort, holds the Arena.
- **Meh-Phoe** is the crumbling old town, half-fallen and prone to collapse, left to artists and magicians.
- **Meh-Vesali**, the western district, is another quarter of craftsmen and the middling sort.
- **Meh-Esali** is the poor, overlooked east, and **Meh-Nyur** the raw new town up on the plateau, already grown slumlike.
- **Atur Kastelli**, the Crystal Castle, is the palace of the Grand Vizier and the seat of the council's high chamber.

## Government

Krilloan is ruled by a council, the Trakilis ni Botiwani, "the Ever-Enlightened and Honoured," of twenty-six votes. They are shared among the three great temples, the three trade-houses, the three guilds and three private citizens, the high judge, the commandant of the army with his three, the watch-chief with two, the grand admiral with two, and Ordo Magica with five. The Grand Vizier holds a single vote and is consulted only to break a tie, though he must sign every decree the council passes.

In practice the order of magicians holds fourteen of the twenty-six. Through the commandant, the watch-chief and others bought or cowed, Ordo Magica can carry whatever measure it likes, and the present Grand Vizier, Arkondor II, signs what he is given and turns back to his music. He holds his office for life and keeps some ten "antigrafs," doubles who sit in his place to take petitions and complaints. The ambassadors of the foreign realms sit among the council's business too, marked by their fur-brimmed three-cornered hats; by old law they may never bear a weapon within the city.

## Justice and the Arena

The law is kept by the city watch, the filakas, four hundred strong, of whom two hundred walk the streets at a time in eighteen patrols. Above and apart from them is the Sabertooth Guard, the Ereth Zabelas, seventeen elite soldiers in helms wrought like a sabertooth's skull, each sworn to a single master of Ordo Magica, and it is by these that the order's true will is done.

Punishment is harsh. Sacrilege and murder bring death, an assault that draws blood is answered eye for eye, and theft is paid back with a fine to match its worth. A condemned prisoner may sometimes choose the Arena over the gallows, and win his freedom if he lives, or sell himself instead to the magical research of Ordo Magica, from which few return whole. The Arena itself stands in Meh-Achladi and seats twenty-five thousand. Its gladiators are trained by the master Håschir Steel-eye, and among the champions are the minotaur Macorog the Brain-eater and the warrior-woman Nitafia; the beasts set against them range from a great ape to a giant spider and stranger things besides.

## Religion

Krilloan keeps complete freedom of worship and has no dominant faith, a legacy of Emperor Kain. Three open religions are reckoned the city's own.

- **The cult of Paridon** is the largest, with some twenty-five thousand faithful. It honours four gods together: Paridon of the sea, Philas of the land, Felis of darkness, and Galmon of war.
- **The cult of Angusia**, about twelve thousand strong, worships the goddess who guards the gates and the thresholds of the world.
- **The Shining Way**, smaller and mostly of foreigners, follows the sun-god Etin by the teaching of Odo and its six commandments. Its Caddo branch forbids all magic; the mainland branch allows most arts but forbids necromancy.

Beneath the open faiths run the demon-cults of the Oktagon, the eight demon-princes of the red moon Raukhra, brought chiefly by the Soluni. The Black Rose Brotherhood and the secret Star Doctrine serve among them, and a pact with such a power is bought with years of service and, at the last, the servant's soul.

## Peoples

Krilloan is a city of many bloods, drawn from across the southern seas.

- **The Samkarnier** are the great majority, better than three in five, and most of these are native Krilloaners; they are the city's craftsmen, fishers and warriors.
- **The Soluni**, from across the Serpent Lake, are the largest of the incomers, merchants and slaves and fighting-men, and it is they who brought the demon-cults.
- **The Erebians**, from far Ereb in the north, are merchants, craftsmen and bankers.
- **The ducks** make a small but noted community of warriors, ships' captains and traders.
- **The dwarves** keep to their crafts and their counting-houses.
- **The elves** are very few, a bare hundred in all the city, and almost all of them magicians who shun the necromancers' isle.

## Trade and Money

Krilloan is a merchant city before all else, and its coin, the vesir, is struck in copper, silver and gold. It sends out gems and precious metals and woods, drugs and spices and cloth, slaves and worked magic, and takes in iron, hides, wine, salt and weapons. Its drugs are famous and infamous alike: rhas the mild opiate, the chewed leaf koka, the dream-bringing ponuvera, and the hallucinogens that the demon-cults prize.

The city's fortune rests on the armada that rides the RhabdoRhinn wind up the Way Out. The strait runs one way, and that wind is the only road north, so the wealth carried in a single passage must serve for the years until the wind blows again. No merchant of Krilloan can afford to miss it, and the city swells with crews and cargo whenever the southern sky promises its coming.

## Travel and Communication

A ship out of Krilloan reaches the nearer ports of Efaro in nine days and Felician Tyros in a fortnight; the far cities of Ereb lie weeks away, and remote Cerevallis the better part of half a year. For those who can pay, the city offers swifter roads. The flying-beasts of the trade carry passenger and cargo above the jungle, from the heavy flying lizard to the hippogriff and the pegasus, and the great airships, the zeppelonds, bear the heaviest loads of all. The Transporters' Guild will move a traveller from one warded circle to another in an instant, between Krilloan and a handful of distant cities, for a high fee. Messages travel by the trained pigeons of the animists, guided unerringly home.

## The City Itself

The great canals run some twenty metres wide and ten deep, and a gondola may be hired by the half-hour. The eastern canal, the Algur-Ostromos, carries clean water up toward Ordo Magica; the western runs foul and undrinkable. Beneath the streets lie the arched sewers, and from the hills two aqueducts, one of thirty kilometres and one of near sixty, bring down more than half the city's fresh water. Four gates pierce the walls: the Castle Gate for royal visitors alone, the busy North Gate, the seldom-used Old Gate, and the West Gate toward the sinking city.

The speech of Krilloan is a mongrel tongue, forever taking in new words and shedding old ones, and it changes so fast that a document more than sixty years old can no longer be read. For writing that must last, the city keeps to Felician, the official script of its records.

## The Underworld

Crime in Krilloan is as organised as its trade. The thieves' guild, under the master Hiskel Trååne, holds the lesser theft and smuggling of the city. The drug trade answers to the gangster Hazrab El-Mahzud. Murder for hire is bought at the Black Stallion in Meh-Dachaur, where two rival bands keep their trade: the Skulls under Atle the Bonebreaker, and the Slayers under Abel the Magpie, a killer who is said to keep to certain limits. The sewers and the sinking city give all of them their boltholes, and the watch ventures into neither lightly.

## Kitteln

Kitteln is the old thieves' quarter of Meh-Phoe, sunk in a stone hollow that was deepened long ago when the stone for the city wall was cut from it. Once the haunt of the city's burglars, it is now where fortune-seekers and sharpers gather to trade a rumour, a treasure-map, or stolen goods too hot to move anywhere else. Few of its two hundred and fifty people are settled folk, and the watch long since gave up keeping order there. Its grimy houses of stone and timber run as far below the street as above, joined by tunnels that break here and there into the dense, separate sewer-net of Meh-Phoe and drain at last into the old moat, so that a careful traveller can cross half the quarter unseen.

The Golden Portal, an opium den, is the nearest thing Kitteln has to a ruling power: the seat of the gangster Hazrab El-Mahzud and his trade in drugs and slaves. For drink and rumour there is the Cracked Wine-Jug, kept by the old adventurers Mart and Dunn; for a wager, the cockfights of the Bloody Cockerel; and when a point needs making, the bruisers Helbrik and Mundir will break an arm to order. Beneath the Rose and the Cup, a cheap tavern with worse beer, a secret brotherhood keeps its cult.

## History

> Dates are reckoned in **fO** (before Odo) and **eO** (after Odo).

- **The founding (204 eO):** the admiral Krillo DiCastran founded the city after a maelstrom rose in the Way Out, and gave it his name.
- **The earthquake, RhabdinRhaluk (248 eO):** the ground heaved up some twenty-five metres and half the city's people died; there followed the long Age of the Sea-ray.
- **The Dermanian invasion (310–311 eO):** Krilloan was bombarded into ruins.
- **Emperor Kain (311–377 eO):** the pirate-captain Kain Urtus rebuilt the city from the rubble and founded the tolerant culture it keeps to this day.
- **The rise of Tannatopol (366 eO):** the necromancer-isle rose from the sea, and Vlad Kamantur founded his school of death-magic upon it.
- **Ordo Magica founded (374 eO):** Vlad Kamantur, Shaza Sahivann and Toalpios T'ahhah founded the order of magicians.
- **The magocracy (377–486 eO):** at Kain's death a council of nineteen magicians took the city, and Ordo Magica grew with it; Ordo Corporem, the necromancers' order, was founded in 381.
- **The dragon Morriart (~470 eO):** the dragon terrorised Krilloan until Duke Arneval slew it, only to be murdered soon after; the beast was later raised again on Tannatopol.
- **The Great Fire (486 eO):** nine-tenths of the city burned, the magicians' council was thrown down, and the age of the Grand Viziers began with Ragrozukh Kiramilion.
- **The Plague (608 eO):** a pestilence swept Krilloan, and the stricken quarter, Mehir Natzack, was walled off as the Plague-town and given over to undead guards.
- **The present day:** Ordo Magica has quietly taken back the reins through its Sabertooth Guard, while Arkondor II, vizier since 603, reigns and signs and suspects nothing.

## Honours

The city grants a handful of orders to those it would reward. The finest is the Order of the Three-headed Dragon-serpent, held by only four living souls. The Glass Bell is given for faithful service, the Silver Hand of the Elf-princess to those who help the watch take a murderer, and the Golden Key of the Althair to those who serve the guilds, of which only seven have ever been bestowed.
