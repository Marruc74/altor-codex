The Dragonback, called Drakryggen by those who dwell in its shadow, is the highest mountain chain in all of Altor. Its peaks rise beyond fifteen thousand metres, a wall of stone and ice flung across the western reaches of the great southern continent of Samkarna. No range in the known world stands taller, and from a distance its ridged silhouette truly suggests the spine of some vast sleeping beast.

The chain does more than dominate the horizon. It stands across the path of the western winds and wrings them dry, so that the rain falls upon its slopes and never reaches the lands beyond. For this reason the Serpent Lake and the western parts of Soluna lie parched in the rain-shadow of the Dragonback, while the rest of Samkarna drowns in wet and swamp. A single breach pierces the whole length of the wall - Geardon's Gap - and beyond it the western slopes fall away into isolated, alien realms few outsiders have ever crossed.

Among those hidden lands lie the Krystalerna, lakes so perfectly clear that a traveller standing at the shore may look straight down and see every stone upon the bottom, however deep the water runs.

## Overview

- **Type:** Mountain range
- **Distinction:** The highest mountain chain in Altor, with peaks above 15,000 metres
- **Notable feature:** Geardon's Gap, its only pass

## Geography

The Dragonback runs along the western edge of Samkarna, a continent that lies far to the southwest of Ereb. Its peaks climb higher than any others in the world, well past fifteen thousand metres, and remain wrapped in ice and storm the year round. The western slopes descend into remote and little-known country, where the land takes on a strange and alien character unlike anything in the east.

## The Rain-Shadow

By its sheer height the chain blocks the western winds and forces them to shed their moisture upon its own flanks. The lands shielded behind it stay dry - chiefly the Serpent Lake and the western tracts of Soluna - in stark contrast to the rest of Samkarna, which grows ever wetter and swampier toward the south.

## The Krystalerna

On the western slopes lie the Krystalerna, a scatter of lakes renowned for water of impossible clarity. So clear are they that the bottom is visible in full from the shore, every feature of the depths laid bare to the eye.
