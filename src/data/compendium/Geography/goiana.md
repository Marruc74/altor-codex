# Goiana

Goiana is a small forest-realm of the elf-folk, set in the hilly country between the rivers Goio and Firkax on the eastern edge of the Aidne peninsula. It was once a corner of Kardien before it broke away to stand on its own, and its people live with the woods rather than ruling over them. Oak and pine cover the low hills, and beneath them live some ten thousand souls, almost all of them wood-elves, who govern themselves without king or noble and ask only to be left in peace.

One thing sets Goiana apart from every other realm of the Copper Sea: the forest itself is awake. Since the great meditation at Golena, the woods have been bound to a living spirit, and land and people are joined together in a way no army has managed to break.

## Overview

- **Population:** approx. 10,000 (almost entirely wood-elves, with other elf-folk among them)
- **Capital:** Golena, the realm's seat of learning
- **Government:** self-ruling fellowship of the elf-folk, without king or nobility
- **Religion:** the worship of Tevatenu, Mother Earth
- **Foundation:** declared a free elf-realm in 424 eO

## Geography

Goiana lies in the east of the Aidne peninsula, a country of low hills under oak and pine, bounded on either side by the rivers Goio and Firkax. It is gentle, green land, neither cleared for the plough nor cut for timber, because its people take from the forest only what it gives freely. It borders Kardien, the kingdom it once belonged to, and its woods run on into the wider forests of the eastern peninsula.

## People

Goiana is a realm of the elf-folk, most of them wood-elves, who live as hunters and gatherers in the crowns of the great trees and move through their woods all but unseen. They keep no lords and no serfs, and they treat the forest not as property but as kin. Strangers are rarely welcomed, and rarely even noticed, since a wood-elf would far sooner hide than fight.

## Government

Goiana has no king, no noble, and no feudal order. The elf-folk rule themselves in common, held together by their shared life in the forest and by the bond of the great meditation. Matters of weight are settled at Golena, the sacred glade, where the will of the people and the will of the forest are taken to be one.

## The Ensouled Forest

At the heart of Goiana is **Enlil Talannen**, a forest-spirit driven from her own home long ago by necromancers. At the great Meditation in Golena, in the year 429 eO, the elf **Telidor** made contact with her, and she chose to bind herself to the land and its people for as long as Goiana should last.

Because of that bond, the forest is no longer mindless. Enlil Talannen sees and knows all that happens beneath her trees. To those who took part in the meditation she speaks in the rustle of the leaves; to anyone else that rustle is only wind. She makes the magic of those who keep faith with her come easier and their strength return faster, and she can just as readily withhold both from any who displease her. She rules the weather within Goiana's bounds, so that hostile armies always end up fighting in the worst of it, and she can put out a wildfire before it spreads. To those who were not at the meditation she gives only omens, and against her own woods she is well guarded, for within Goiana no spell can bend her to its will.

## Silver-lindens

Here and there in Goiana grow the **silver-lindens**, rare and sacred trees with silver-grey bark, held to be a gift of **Tevatenu**, Mother Earth herself. An elf who rests in a grove of them heals faster in body and spirit, and from their leaves the elves make a paste that closes wounds. Their wood is prized above almost any other: it takes enchantment readily, it makes the finest composite bows, and arrows of silver-linden, treated by the elven oil-makers, fly true and wound cleanly with no iron tip at all, which makes them treasured by mages who shun iron.

## The Dance Academy of Golena

After the meditation, Golena grew into the learning-seat of the realm, and in 457 eO a school was founded there with no equal anywhere in Ereb. Its scholars study the lore of living things, the arts of the animist, and above all dance and music. From his contact with Enlil Talannen, Telidor drew the idea that the elements of the world move together in a dance, and that a dancer can learn to move in step with them.

These are the **elemental dances**, seven of them, each a discipline of its own, and only Golena teaches them. A dancer of the water dance can walk on a still pool as if it were a floor; a dancer of the fire dance, worked out after fire ravaged the eastern woods, can pass through flame unharmed. The greatest masters take the title of dance-master and wear a robe of flame-yellow silk, and it is said the highest of them can summon or banish the elementals themselves.

## History

- **c. 410 eO**: wood-elves begin to settle the forests between the Goio and the Firkax
- **424 eO**: Goiana declares itself a free realm of the elf-folk, breaking from Kardien
- **429 eO**: the Meditation in Golena; Telidor's pact with the spirit Enlil Talannen binds the forest to its people
- **457 eO**: the dance academy is founded at Golena, the only school of the elemental dances in Ereb
- **536–540 eO**: an orc host, set on by the Black Tower, makes war on Goiana and is beaten

## Foreign Relations

Goiana won its freedom from Kardien, and its rise loosened that kingdom's grip on the northern duchies. The elf-folk keep to themselves and look for no quarrels, but they can defend their woods: when the Black Tower loosed an orc host on them in the 530s eO, the forest and its people threw the invaders back.
