# Furgia

Furgia lies in Akrogal, the great continent east of Ereb, a land mostly given over to nomads and wandering hunters where few realms have ever taken firm root. Against that open country Furgia stands apart as an old and settled kingdom. It rises south of the border river Harytis, where the lands of Altor give way to the steppe. It is a realm shaped by war and memory, and long bound in rivalry with Kalmurri, its sworn archenemy. For centuries it has stood as a shield against the peoples of the east, holding the frontier with stone walls and riders alike.

Above all, Furgia has kept faith with the old ideals of knighthood and chivalry, which here have endured long after they faded elsewhere. Honour, the sworn oath, and service to one's lord are not relics in Furgia but the living measure of a worthy life.

Within its borders, ancient noble houses still endure. Their halls are built of stone, but their traditions are carried on horseback. In Furgia, the ideals of knighthood have not replaced the steppe, but grown from it. Lords ride as easily as they rule, and the bond between rider and horse is as strong as any oath.

At the mouth of the Harytis stands Ketoriam, a city of wealth and strength. Its walls have endured for generations, though not without trial. Nomadic riders from the northeast have breached them in rare but hard-fought invasions, reminders that the steppe is never truly quiet.

In ages past, crusading knights came from western Ereb, seeking to convert Furgia and reshape it in their own faith. They found a land that would not yield. The conversion crusades were broken, and Furgia remained its own.

An older shadow lies behind those wars. Long before the crusaders came, Furgia fought to throw off the invading margyler, who held a portion of the realm for generations. Not until around the year 800 before-Odo were the last of them finally driven out, and the memory of that long struggle still runs deep in Furgian song.

## Overview

- **Capital:** Ketoriam
- **Military:** strong cavalry combining knights and steppe riders

## Geography

Furgia lies in Akrogal, the continent east of Ereb, a land otherwise dominated by nomads and wandering hunters with little settled rule. The realm itself rises south of the Harytis river, on the frontier between Altor and the eastern steppe, and serves as a defensive border kingdom and buffer against the eastern peoples. Its capital, Ketoriam, stands at the mouth of the Harytis, where the river meets the sea.

## People

Ancient noble houses dominate Furgian society. Their traditions are carried on horseback, and the ideals of knighthood have grown from the steppe rather than replacing it. Lords ride as easily as they rule, and the bond between rider and horse is as strong as any oath. Chivalry, the keeping of one's word, and loyalty to one's liege are held as the highest virtues, a code preserved here while it has been forgotten in many western lands.

## Military

Furgia maintains a strong cavalry tradition that combines feudal knights with steppe riders.

## History

For long ages Furgia fought to free itself from the invading margyler, who occupied part of the realm until around 800 before-Odo, when they were at last driven out. In later centuries, nomadic riders from the northeast breached the walls of Ketoriam in rare but hard-fought invasions. Crusading knights from western Ereb came to convert the realm and reshape it in their own faith, but those crusades were broken and Furgia remained its own.

## Foreign Relations

Kalmurri is Furgia's archenemy, and the two realms are bound in a longstanding rivalry and conflict. Furgia has also been the target of conversion crusades launched from western Ereb, which sought to bring the kingdom under their faith but failed to break it.
