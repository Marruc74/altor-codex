# The Way Out

![The Way Out](/compendium/Geography/Serpent Lake/The Way Out/The Way Out1.jpg "The Way Out")
![The Way Out](/compendium/Geography/Serpent Lake/The Way Out/The Way Out2.jpg "The Way Out")

The Way Out is the northern inlet of the Serpent Lake, the great sea that lies between Samkarna and Soluna. It is the one place along the whole length of that water where the sea is still fit to drink, a fact that has shaped every story told about it. From here the water runs strongly from the north down to the south, a steady current that carries a ship southward with little effort but resists any vessel that would go against it.

To sail north out of the Serpent Lake is therefore a thing of patience and luck. The lake's winds are feeble, and crews must bend their backs at the twin banks of oars in the slave-galleys that ply these waters. Against the southward flow even the strongest galley makes little headway, and so the inlet keeps its name for those who finally find the way through.

## Overview

- **Type:** Northern inlet of the Serpent Lake
- **Waters:** Still drinkable, unlike the rest of the lake
- **Current:** Flows strongly from north to south

## The Current and the Wind

The water of the Way Out runs ever from north to south, and no oarsmen alone can drive a galley up against it for long. The only thing that opens the inlet to northbound ships is a rare, hard southerly known as the RhabdoRhinn wind. It is a wind that comes seldom, blowing perhaps once in five years, and sailors watch the southern sky for its coming the way farmers watch for rain in a drought.

## The Armada to Krilloan

When the RhabdoRhinn does blow, the inlet wakes. An armada gathers and rides the wind up the Way Out, its holds heavy with drugs, poisons, and gems, all bound for the town of Krilloan at the head of the inlet. For the few years between one RhabdoRhinn and the next, the wealth carried in that single passage must serve, and so the coming of the wind is both a season of fortune and a gamble no merchant of Krilloan can afford to miss.
