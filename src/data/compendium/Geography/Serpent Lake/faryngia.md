# Faryngia

![Faryngia](/compendium/Geography/Serpent Lake/Faryngia/Faryngia1.jpg "Faryngia")

Faryngia, which sailors name "the World's Throat," is a vast whirlpool that turns without rest in the western reaches of the Serpent Lake. It is not the work of tide or sea alone but of the winds that come roaring out of Geardons gap, pouring down upon the water and spinning it into a maw that swallows whatever drifts too near. To the mariners of the Serpent Lake it is among the most feared of all dangers, a place spoken of in warning rather than wonder.

The lake itself gives little help against it. Its winds are weak, and ships must crawl across it by the labor of oars at the twin banks of the slave-galleys. A careless steersman, a current misjudged, a crew too slow at the oars, and a galley can be drawn into the turning throat from which nothing returns.

## Overview

- **Type:** Giant whirlpool
- **Byname:** The World's Throat
- **Cause:** Winds pouring out of Geardons gap
- **Reputation:** A killer of careless sailors

## The World's Throat

The whirlpool earns its byname honestly, for it is a throat that drinks the sea. The winds spilling from Geardons gap fall steadily on the same waters, and over ages they have wound them into a turning gulf that never stills. Those who have looked upon it and lived speak of a sound like a great drawing breath, and of water sliding down into a darkness no rope has ever measured.

## Danger to Mariners

For the galleys that cross the western Serpent Lake, Faryngia is a constant reckoning. The current near its rim runs faster than oars can fight, and a ship that wanders into that pull is lost. Pilots who know these waters give the World's Throat a wide berth, steering by its distant roar, for it has taken many who trusted to luck or to the strength of their slaves at the oar.
