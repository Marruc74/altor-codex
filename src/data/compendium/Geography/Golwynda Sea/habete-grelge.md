# Habete Grelge

Habete Grelge is an ancient island principality that sits within the eastern outflow of the Golwynda Sea, amid the falls known as Grelgifallen. Where Raas Narram pours down in one great cataract and the Demon Tongue bores through stone, the eastern falls take a gentler but grander shape - they are lower than the others, yet far broader, a wide curtain of falling water rather than a single plunging column. In the midst of this broad descent lies the island, set like a stone in the very throat of the sea's eastward escape.

The principality is old, and it is ruled by magicians. Cut off by the falls that surround it and guarded by the arts of its sorcerer-rulers, Habete Grelge has kept its independence and its mysteries through long ages. Few outsiders pass its waters, for the same broad falls that cradle the island make any approach a perilous thing.

## Overview

- **Type:** Island principality within Grelgifallen, the eastern outflow of the Golwynda Sea
- **Falls:** Grelgifallen - lower than the western falls but far broader
- **Government:** Ancient principality ruled by magicians

## Grelgifallen

Grelgifallen is the easternmost of the three mouths by which the Golwynda drains its great height. It lacks the sheer drop of Raas Narram, but it makes up in width what it wants in height, spreading its waters across a broad front as the sea spills toward the lands below. The island of Habete Grelge sits within this expanse, and the falls serve it as both a moat and a wall, sealing the principality away behind a barrier of moving water that no ordinary vessel can cross.

## The Magician Princes

Habete Grelge is held by magicians, and has been so since old days. Their rule has given the island its long endurance and its reputation for secrecy, for behind the curtain of the falls the sorcerer-princes keep their own counsel and answer to no power on the surrounding shores. What learning and what designs they pursue in their isolation is little known beyond the falls, and that ignorance is much to the liking of those who rule there.
